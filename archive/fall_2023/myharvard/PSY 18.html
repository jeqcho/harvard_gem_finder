Description:

Why do our minds overreact to bad things? How do people think about the future? Does sex follow economic principles of supply and demand? Can we develop a scientific theory of free will? Philosophers have grappled with big questions for centuries, and now, social scientists have a new way to address them. In this special edition course, which will be offered for the first and only time in Fall 2023, we will pull together research and data from psychology and other areas of the social sciences to bear on these and other enduring questions. Additional topics covered will include the self, social acceptance and rejection, emotions, sexuality and relationships, self-destructive behaviors, and consciousness. The course is taught by visiting Professor Roy Baumeister, world-famous social scientist and author of multiple books which explore these complicated and interesting questions. 

Course Notes:

The instructor is visiting Professor Roy Baumeister, r.baumeister@psy.uq.edu.au

Recommended Prep:

The Psychology Department requires completion of Science of Living Systems 20 or Psychology 1 or the equivalent of introductory psychology (e.g. Psych AP=5 or IB =7 or Psyc S-1) and at least one foundational course from PSY 14, PSY 15, PSY 16, and PSY 18 before enrolling in this course; or permission of instructor.
