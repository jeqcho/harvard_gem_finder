Description:

Communication is essential to our everyday lives, yet we often donâ€™t consider the complexity involved in understanding and being understood by others. In this course we will explore how language allows us to exchange ideas and the dynamics of human conversation. Topics covered in the course will include the psychology of verbal and non-verbal communication, how children learn to be conversational partners, leadership and influence, and research on communication disorders. 

Recommended Prep:

The Psychology Department requires completion of Science of Living Systems 20 or Psychology 1 or the equivalent of introductory psychology (e.g. Psych AP=5 or IB =7 or Psyc S-1) and at least one foundational course from PSY 14, PSY 15, PSY 16, and PSY 18 before enrolling in this course; or permission of instructor.
