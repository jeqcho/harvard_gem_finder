Description:

How do cells communicate and respond to their environment? MCB 175 explores foundational principles in cell physiology and membrane biophysics, including ion channel structure and function; transport mechanisms; electrical signaling in the brain and nonexcitatory cells; second messengers; organellar signaling. We delve into these core concepts through examples of signaling mechanisms in specialized cell types, disease states, and organismal adaptations. Through reading and discussing primary literature and scientific writing assignments, students strengthen skills in critical thinking, interpretation of data, and experimental strategy. Students also give presentations and design a research project based on course topics.
