Description:

How could it be that paving a new road might increase congestion for all drivers? Why would a professional sports team ever try *not* to score in a game that it wants to win? Why would any student rank highschools not in their order of preference when applying? And what are some incentive pitfalls that the designer of a cryptocurrency system should be aware of? In this course, we will examine seemingly strange social phenomena, use mathematical tools to model them and to analyze how and why distorted incentives give rise to them, and explore mechanisms to eliminate such phenomena.

Recommended Prep:

We will assume mathematical proficiency consistent with having already taken Stat 110 and having already taken or concurrently taking Math 21a.
