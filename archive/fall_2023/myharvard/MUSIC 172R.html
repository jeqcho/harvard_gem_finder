Description:

The Harvard New Music Ensemble (Music 180) is a course for instrumentalists, vocalists and electronic musicians interested in the performance of contemporary ensemble music. The ensemble will be led by Professor Chase, and a wide range of guest artists in the fields of improvisation, composition, and "conduction" (conducted improvisation) will be invited to play with the ensemble over the course of the semester. This is a performance-based course for students who are committed to tackling challenging new works and learning new forms of collaboration, cooperation and multigenre music-making. 

Existing repertoire of the 20th and 21st centuries will be rehearsed and performed alongside new works expressly written for the ensemble, including new commissions written expressly for the ensemble. Regular concerts will be given by HNME throughout the semester in both traditional and non-traditional venues. 

The Harvard New Music Ensemble is a member of New Music Equity Action, a national coalition of artists, ensembles and administrators who are committed to dismantling inequity in our field.
