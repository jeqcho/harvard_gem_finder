Description:

In this seminar we will explore the work of the philosopher Bernard Williams (1929 – 2003), with special attention to one of his major works Ethics and the Limits of Philosophy (Harvard, 1985). We will also read widely in other, shorter works of his and take up such questions as morality and the emotions; action, responsibility, and blame; moral luck, the idea of moral obligation, realism and relativism in ethics, egoism and altruism. We will also take up Williams’ thoughts on philosophy and its relation to its history, including both historical figures and the nature of history as a form of humanistic understanding. Additional readings from McDowell, A. W. Moore, Raz, Scanlon, Wolf, and others.
