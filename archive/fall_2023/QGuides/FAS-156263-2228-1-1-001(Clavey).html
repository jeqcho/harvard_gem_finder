Honestly pretty simple calculation on this one, if you enjoy critical theory and the frankfurt school, it’s a great class! if you don’t enjoy critical theory, you probably won’t love it. if you enjoy critical theory but are skeptical of the frankfurt school (me!), it’s also great! charles is very knowledgeable and leads great discussions. the material is very difficult but he leads you through it well. only warning is that, as to be expected with a class on mid century critical theory, the vibes can be a bit ‘overly erudite,’ so just be prepared for that.
This is one of the better junior tutorials. The readings are –incredibly– difficult, but also some of the most interesting and eye–opening I have had the pleasure of studying. Charles is a really fantastic teacher, so there were days I came in having read 0 words of the assigned readings and still was able to learn and participate. This is because he provides a handout where he identifies short passages with key takeaways, so whenever the reading is too difficult or people don't have a lot to say, he'll just direct us to close read the key passages together.

Every tutorial should be run this way, but I find other tutors in 10 and 98 have this complex about not "giving students the answers" which just makes it awkward and useless when no one has read or the reading was too hard. I find that the approach where there is always close reading to fall back on makes every minute of class actually worthwhile and actually incentivizes students to read more. When we get to see how generative a discussion can be when we are unprepared, we are inspired by that to dig deeper for the next week.

Another thing I loved about this course is that Charles cut out all the unnecessary assignments from the syllabus, so the only thing you really need to do throughout the semester is show up and think about your term paper. Could not recommend this course with more enthusiasm.
If you're at all interested in Critical Theory, TAKE THIS CLASS. CHARLES IS A M'FN GEM WHO WILL LISTEN TO ANY WACKY IDEAS THAT YOU HAVE AND EXPLAIN HOW IT CAN ALL FIT TOGETHER.

DO IT.
Really engage with the readings or else discussion won't be interesting. It will pay off.
