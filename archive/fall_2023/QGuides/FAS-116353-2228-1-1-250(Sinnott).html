It's a solid expos if you're interested in the topic, but somewhat hard grading.
The readings can be lengthy and complex, but the professor is very nice and understanding. She will answer your questions, enhance your understanding, and clarify whatever is necessary to ensure that everyone is on the same page.

Try your best on the draft essays so that you can get the best feedback.
This is a good Expos section with an interesting topic. The professor is passionate about the subject and it is well structured.
If you are interested in privacy issues, you should absolutely consider this course! Dr. Sinnott is a wonderful instructor. I found the grading very fair, and the feedback was excellent. I would not go in with the attitude of this course being an "easy A," but if you enjoy the course you'll put in the effort and succeed grade–wise and gain a lot in terms of writing ability. Ultimately pick your Expos with your interests in mind and how they align with the course topic.

Also consider that this course deals with U.S. legal considerations, so international students may find that there is a little bit of a learning curve (e.g., what is the Constitution/Bill of Rights/Supreme Court) that domestic students don't have to deal with. That being said, the course does mention European laws as well, and if you're interested in the material don't let this deter you.

Dr. Sinnott's background makes her course a bit more targeted to straightforward writing (versus more artsy constructions favored in disciplines like English), which I certainly found to be a positive. She teaches you to write clearly.
