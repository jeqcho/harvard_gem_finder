What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you speak a foreign language and have ever been interested in the process of translation, with all of its nuances and complexity, take this course! You get to take on a project that is (hopefully) really meaningful and interesting to you, and it's really satisfying to have a polished, finished product at the end. Prof. Naddaff is truly a joy, she will guide you through the process and make it fun. You also get to learn about the works that your classmates are translating, which is really fun and enriching as well.
Take this class! I entered "On Translation" without any major interest or experience in translation, but now I am fascinated by it! The readings are challenging but in a good way, and the discussion sessions are super interesting because the students came from very different backgrounds (grad/undergrad, languages, nationalities, interests...) and worked on a project that meant a lot to them. I translated poetry for mine, and that project was what I wanted to be working on all the time during the semester – it was both fun and a great learning opportunity. Professor Naddaff is the kindest person, she is so encouraging and even brought chocolate to each seminar! I'd recommend this class to anyone interested in language, literature, and culture!
I would encourage students who are maybe vaguely interested in translation (and perhaps not literary translation) to take this course. Even if you don't see yourself becoming a translator, it's a chance to study definitions of language, human relations, cross–cultural understanding, the value of literature, the history of communication, etc.
Take this course! It is a great opportunity to learn about theories in translation and of notable translators of the past. It is a fundamental course for anyone wishing to pursue translation as a career, side–job, or personal passion!
One of the more rewarding and enjoyable courses I've ever taken.
Strongly recommend this class! Really interesting theoretical questions, and gives you the tools and frameworks for starting to translate stuff on your own. Also really great to learn from/work with other students' projects and languages. One of my favorite classes I've taken, and certainly one of the ones I've gotten the most out of. Especially essential for those working with translated texts in general.
