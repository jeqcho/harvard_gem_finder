This class is HARD. I lost countless hours of sleep and wanted to rip my hair out so many times. Problem sets can take 20+ hours each week, and the average exam scores were in the low 60s.

But here's what I'll also say: you'll learn some really valuable stuff. Signals and systems are super relevant as an engineer, and when all is said and done, you'll know how to do things like smooth ECG data, filter out background noise, or increase contrast in your MRI images. The problem sets are also designed specifically to reinforce the material covered in lectures, and the Canvas site has all the exams from the past 5+ years as well as handwritten lecture notes for each lecture.

Maurice is also a great lecturer. Sure, he has a quiet voice and small handwriting so it helps to sit in the front of the classroom, but he breaks down these really challenging concepts and actually makes them understandable. Throughout the semester, I searched online for YouTube videos, textbooks, or tutorials to understand convolutions, Fourier Transforms, Laplace Transforms, but nothing compares to how simple Maurice makes it. He really tries to make sure you understand the intuition behind things rather than just memorizing them. At the end of the day, you're often still going to leave each lecture confused because that's how difficult the material is, but just know that Maurice isn't the cause of that confusion.
