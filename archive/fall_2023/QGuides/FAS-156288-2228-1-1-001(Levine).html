This course does a great job building up optimization from first principles. I felt like, from beginning to end, each class made a reasonable jump that pushed the course content forward while also building on foundations from previous classes. Because of this, I felt like I really understood the content by the end! I also thought that the workload was very well distributed across the semester. Quizzes and pset due dates alternate weeks, so each week feels like it has about the same amount of work. In general the workload was on par with a good pset class–– enough to do sufficient practice with the material but certainly still manageable. It was really nice that quizzes were distributed throughout the semester as this made each one feel much more manageable and less stressful than a big midterm and final. If you're interested at all in the topic, I definitely recommend this class! I came away feeling like I could genuinely set up and solve real–word problems with optimization, which feels like a super useful and applicable skill.

It is important to think about the format of the class, which is somewhat flipped classroom. We had readings that we were expected to do before each class along with a brief reading check. We would then do problems as a group in class. Personally, I found it most useful to skim the readings and do enough to answer the reading check, then go to class and work through the problems (which were useful in going through the content), and then return to the reading after to actually take comprehensive notes.

Finally, I just wanted to say how lovely Margo is! You could tell that she genuinely cares about the students. Throughout the class, she made a few adjustments to the schedule to better tune it to the group and make sure our workload was at a good level, something that I always really appreciate about a professor. I definitely enjoyed taking this class.
