What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You get out of this class what you put in. It's really easy to sit in the lecture on your laptop and pay no attention all class and still pass, but if you are engaged with the material you will manage to learn a ton (especially compared to how little work you need to do outside of class). Even if you are only maybe potentially mildly interested in Chinese Linguistics, Professor Huang is a gem who could not be more passionate about his subject material, and if you're open to learning, it's infectious.
This freshman seminar is very interesting and welcomes students of all interests. Taking a linguistic–based class like this not only teaches you about language, but it also teaches you about the world filled with humans who interact and how to appreciate the small things we often overlook.
This is a great course if you're interested in learning more about linguistics.
This course is perfect for people who might not study linguistics but still want to learn about the field from a top linguistics professor or are interested in learning more about Chinese language and history. The workload is really manageable, and Professor Huang is super supportive and approachable. You really learn a lot about Chinese linguistics and get to know your classmates well; Professor Huang invited us out for Chinese/Taiwanese food, and we learned a lot about our classmates and teacher beyond the class.
