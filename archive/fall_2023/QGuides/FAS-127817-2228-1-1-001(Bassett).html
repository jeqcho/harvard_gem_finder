This class is great if you're interested in a theoretical approach to inequality. I will say, it does start from a "Is inequality that bad?" perspective, so as an FGLI student that aspect of the course was challenging, though I'd still recommend taking it. Classes like these are important because of the subject matter, but when they are so personal it can be hard not feel like the stakes are high in class discussion.
Anya is a fantastic professor & a great relationship to have in the Social Studies department (literally a Social Studies superstar). She is a bit of a tough grader but means well & wants to push us to get better. Definitely try and choose a research topic as fast as you can as the prospectus really sneaks up on you. Highly recommend making this tutorial your strong first choice!!
This is a very good junior tutorial. Interesting material and conversations. Anya has so much experience teaching Social Studies students, and she scaffolds the class well to prepare you for your final paper. She is very knowledgeable and gives thoughtful feedback. As Anya says, you can choose to write your final paper about anything related to inequality, social mobility, or America –– which gives you a lot of latitude to write about anything that interests you. I recommend this class.
Anya runs her classes really well. The course readings are set up thoughtfully, assignments are structured to allow for iteration and multiple feedback cycles, and discussions are smooth. Anya also does a good job making the classroom feel like a community.
