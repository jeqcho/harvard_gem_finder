What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Professor Madi is very committed to the course and the learning process. This class can be overwhelming at times, but it is also very rewarding to see yourself advance in your skills. Be prepared to devote a significant amount of time to the class, including going to office hours. Additionally, the sense of camaraderie formed in this class can be very special and it is great to work alongside peers.
I would really encourage them to dispel with the notion that Arabic is a hard language. I would tell them that the class actually becomes so much more enjoyable and fulfilling for you once you try to stay on top of the work and always come prepared to class. The feeling of understanding more and more of what the teacher is saying and the feeling of being able to speak with more and more fluency is unmatched. Please, don't make the mistake I made at the beginning of my language studies and just treat this as an "extra class" where you don't need to put in that much work –– you will become miserable. I strongly encourage you to approach each assignment not as an assignment, but as a genuine chance for you to engage with the language more and become more proficient in it. In summary, PLEASE –– for your own mental health but also for your own enjoyment –– try to stay on top of your work and try to approach this class with a positive mindset.
If you want to improve your Arabic, you should definitely take this course. Amr is a wonderful and caring professor and makes the language learning experience enjoyable and relevant to everyday life. The class requires a pretty significant investment of time outside of class, but it's worth it if you want to be a better speaker.
You will learn a lot in this class, but you will also have to work hard to get to that level learning throughout.
