Comments
Although a lot of people may take this course for its light workload, the material is genuinely really interesting. The class does suffer a bit from a general lack of enthusiasm and effort put forth by its students, and lecture can be a bit dry and repetitive, but if you are interested in learning about Harvard Square's past and future, I recommend this class! Also I would note that the weekly blog posts are pretty involved, and can take anywhere between 1–4 hours to complete.
Great way to learn about Cambridge civic happenings, Harvard's history, from both the teacher, TF, and peers.
This is a really interesting class that brings together multiple sections of history and architecture as well as activism. It is not necessary to have a background in these subjects but it is helpful when completing assignments. The final project allows for creativity and application of the material learned throughout the course.
While I had some reservations about the format, Trevor the TF was incredible. He was always empathetic and enthusiastic when interacting with everyone.
If you need to fulfill a requirement go for it, but not the best run class. Would regularly go over the already 2 hour long slot and there’s no feedback on assignments unless you explicitly ask for it
