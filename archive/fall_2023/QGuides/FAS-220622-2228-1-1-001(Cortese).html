What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is great to take orchestra as an extracurricular and also get credit + a letter grade for it. Strongly recommend.
If you're in HRO, you might as well take MUSIC107. It's not a lot of work plus you get the credits.
Great course to take. It kept me accountable to go to all the HRO rehearsals (our time is limited in college + playing with an excellent orchestra like HRO so don't waste it please!). I personally was interested in learning more about the pieces we were playing, so the class was perfect for me. I understand it's the first time they're offering this, and I'm looking forward to seeing this course evolve and become more structured with time!
If you've played music a long time and are looking for a music community on campus (instrumental), definitely audition for HRO! You'll make some of your best friends here.
Music 107 is still new, but it's a great way to get letter–grade credit for HRO!
HRO is great. It's quite a bit of practice time outside of rehearsal, but most musicians are used to that so it's nothing out of the ordinary. Fed is incredibly passionate and fantastic at what he does. Rehearsals are a big time commitment, and be prepared to show up to all rehearsals as attendance is required.
Music 107 is a great option for HRO members who want to receive credit for the time they invest in HRO. The course is designed so that the additional commitment beyond full rehearsals is quite small. Most weeks there are just a few readings that relate to the composers and works that are being played in HRO. After completing the readings, you simply post some thoughts on the discussion board and see what other members thought as well. HRO is a large commitment, so I think the small additional commitment each week is definitely worthwhile for the credit.
Taking Music 107 makes you more motivated to go to rehearsal, which increases your buy–in and makes you more enthusiastic about orchestra in general. If you're doing HRO already, I see no reason not to take the class.
HRO remains one of my favorite parts of Harvard. When I arrived on campus my freshman year, filled with anxiety and raging imposter syndrome, HRO was the first home I found. I knew I wanted to join, so I auditioned and joined. When we went on retreat that first Labor Day weekend, I suddenly felt at home. I loved getting to know everyone in the orchestra, and sitting in the orchestra is one of the few places at Harvard where I feel I truly have a way to give back to this university that gives me so much. A year and a half later, HRO has only grown more important to me. Many of my favorite memories so far at Harvard have been made through HRO; unforgettable concerts, fun rehearsal memories, and wonderful times with friends at HRO social functions outside of rehearsals. Many of my closest friends have been made through HRO. It's the most important community to me at Harvard and the extracurricular to which I am most devoted.
Take it! The assignments beyond going to HRO rehearsal (which you have to do even when you're not taking it as a class) are quite minimal, and you get 2 credits from it, which is a nice acknowledgement of effort that you have to expend anyway and can only help your GPA.
HRO already rehearses quite a bit (7.5 hrs/wk) and taking this course binds you (by grades) to attendance, which is sometimes inconvenient.
