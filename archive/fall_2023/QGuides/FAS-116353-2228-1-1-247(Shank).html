Ian is an exceptional professor who will make Expos 20 fun and interesting. If you're a STEM kid like me, you probably won't be looking forward to taking any expos course nor will you think it's necessary. The fact of the matter is, you have to take it anyways and any expos course will be a challenge, otherwise it is a waste of time. With that being said, this course takes a course that is challenging by nature, and makes it fun and interesting. Ian does a great job of creating a warm and welcome environment that make students feel comfortable in asking him questions, as well as asking our peers questions. In fact, Ian creates an environment where not only is peer feedback and constructive criticism welcome, but looked forward to. Furthermore, Ian provides in–depth and concise feedback on all draft assignments which he meets with you to go over. After these meetings you will have a better understanding of what was taught in class and how to apply it to your paper, as well as how to improve your writing. Overall, Ian will take you at whatever writing level you are at and improve your skill set in a way that you can't help but become a better writer afterwards. He is enthusiastic about teaching and genuinely cares that you improve your skill set and writing techniques.

Don't feel intimidated by the first essay. While the prompt was much more nuanced than anything I've ever written before, it's a good challenge that helps you grow as a writer. This class can definitely feel challenging as paper standards are high, but the content is truly super engaging and interesting––you read magazine articles, watch films, read novels, and even have the chance to read scholarly journal articles for a topic of your choosing. I feel like there's a lot of creativity and freedom associated with this class, which I've heard from my peers is not always the case with other Expos courses. I also feel like it's an ideal class to take as a freshman because of how Prof Shank tries to cultivate a close–knit, fun environment––particularly with fun icebreaker questions at the start of every class (I know how painful that sounds, but I promise that these questions are actually very interesting and you learn so many random, hilarious things about everyone's lives). I would definitely recommend this class but be prepared to put in a lot of work into your papers going in.
