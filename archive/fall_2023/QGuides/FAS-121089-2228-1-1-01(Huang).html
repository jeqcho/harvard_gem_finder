Before taking the class, make sure to know at least a little bit about constituency, X' theory, and binding theory. These are some of the most foundational concepts introduced in the class, so not being comfortable with these can be detrimental. Considering the fast pace of the class, sometimes to the point where topics are glossed over or skipped entirely due to time constraints, do yourself a favor and read up on these in the textbook (or watch a few videos online). The class is not a lot of work, and psets aren't super challenging, but the midterm and final can seem much more involved. Have a solid conceptual grasp and ask the TF questions during section so they can go into the concepts detail.
You get what you put into this class!! It’s fairly easy to skate by without a lot of work, but if you take the time and pay attention, Prof Huang has a lot to offer. This was my first linguistics class and I learned a lot.
This class is extremely specific and quite niche. Only take if you are REALLY interested in syntax of if it’s a concentration requirement. It was a little boring, and it’s hard to explain to non–linguistics people what you learned in this class. Not one of my favorites from the linguistics department, but was still interesting to learn about.
Professor is very sweet and knowledgeable about the course material, but the lectures often felt like he was just reading from a textbook. Section was very helpful in clearing up questions. Psets were completion–based which was super clutch, as the second half of the psets were quite hard. Interesting content and good class overall
