Lunbeck is the goat, the readings are super interesting, and the vibes are great. and don’t worry, this isn’t the kind of class that’s gonna skip over what makes certain thinkers problematic. really just a great class.
This is an incredibly unique course and one of the best I have taken at Harvard. While knowing the psychoanalytic canon is helpful in many fields, from English to clinical psychology and beyond, a course on its comprehensive history is rare. The reading is not light, but if you have an interest in the material, it is easy to get absorbed and it stops feeling like work. Active participation is expected, and the environment is extremely encouraging, but this means that you do need to do the readings carefully. Additionally, Professor Lunbeck is quite a famous historian of psychoanalysis and has an encyclopedic knowledge and virtuosic understanding of some of the more difficult material. She also has strong footing in sociological theory and incorporates it skillfully into the syllabus; social studies concentrators seemed to gravitate toward this course to great avail. I really recommend this course to anyone in any field who is interested in psychoanalysis, as a variety of perspectives is actively encouraged (everyone from college juniors to post–docs, in fields from medicine to philosophy to architecture) and this variety made for one of the absolute best experiences I have had at this school.
