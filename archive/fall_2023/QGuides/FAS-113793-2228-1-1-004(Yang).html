What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You do not have to handwrite anything in this class, which is a huge plus. Buy the book! Do well in your dictations, since they help you in the long run.
If you turn up to all the classes, you are set. Lots of of in class time, but very little outside of that. Passionate and kind teachers, you develop almost a familial environment within the small sections.
Do cumulative review now and then of grammar & vocab (including learning the assigned vocab, previewing the grammar, & practicing reading––which tends to help with both, as well as with intuition for constructions––before class); really take advantage of the lecture slides when preparing for assignments like tests & exams; try to come to class with a bold & gregarious attitude – this is the culture & what may be necessary to feel comfortable speaking
It's definitely worth it to take Chinese at Harvard–––the department is wonderful and they make you feel really supported throughout the class and after it.
