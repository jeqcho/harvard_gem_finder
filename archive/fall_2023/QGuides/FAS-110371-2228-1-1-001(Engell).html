This class puts humanity into economics, something I find the subject often lacks, and data into morality. Take this class if you truly want to learn and understand the problems we face in society. It pushes your thinking to the next level and highlights just how interrelated social issues are. A wonderful way to explore important topics from several different perspectives. You can decide how much effort you want to put into this class, but you'll gain so much from reading and thinking about the class content.
exams are not telling of how much you have learned, simply how many authors you have memorized.
This is a fabulous class content–wise, but be prepared for a strange and potentially very tough midterm and final! Do the readings for the exams, otherwise it is worthwhile to watch the films and read the books for discussions throughout the semester!
i really liked having both a humanities and economics element to this class, but the material was not novel. if you have taken economics classes before and are a well–read citizen, a lot of this class will be semi–repetitive. it did cover important topics and would therefore be a great survey class (and perhaps a great gened), but the assignments had very little to do with what we did in class or the exams, and required you to know very niche readings (out of a unreasonably long syllabus). a few of the guest lecturers were great but lecture itself was slow and hard to engage with.

Take this course!! It will change and challenge your world view, and you will take it with you no matter what you do in life. The entire course is truly amazing!
This class is amazing! There are a lot of readings and the course covers a great deal of content, but it's worth it – attend lectures, do the readings, and really appreciate Profs. Engell and Friedman.
This is one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. The structure and reading assignments were excellent and incredibly well thought out. This course helped me refine my thinking on a number of subjects that I have already studied extensively, which was very rewarding. The interdisciplinary approach taken by the course is unique among classes I have taken thus far and really added depth to each topic. Professors Friedman and Engel both delivered very compelling lectures. I looked forward to this class every week.
Take this class! The dynamic between Professor Friedman and Professor Engell is great. They hold different opinions about many of the readings and love poking fun at each other. Lecture is always entertaining. Plus, the workload is light.
This has been my favorite course at Harvard, hands–down.
