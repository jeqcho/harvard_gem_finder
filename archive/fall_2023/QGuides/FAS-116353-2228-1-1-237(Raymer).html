What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a really interesting class if you are interested in biology and biomedicine/biotechnology. While the course material could be rather dense and not very engaging at times, Dr. Raymer has always made it a priority to make sure we understand course concepts and literature within the class. Dr. Raymer grades fairly and not overly critically. She gives very thorough feedback to improve upon your essays. I wouldn't say its an easy class, but if you like the material and use feedback effectively, I think this is a rewarding and (relatively) low–stress class.
This class is very good to take and have conversations with about the materials. For a science focused person it allows you to grow your writing skills while still being interested in the topic.
This class was absolutely wonderful, and I highly recommend taking this Expos course. The readings/topics that we discuss are really fascinating, and the way that Emilie guides our discussions is very helpful in terms of posting thoughtful questions for the class to further think and discuss. She is also extremely kind in terms of accommodating schedules when deciding deadlines, and it's clear that she cares for the students in her class. I can't recommend this course enough!
Take the class! I am(was?) pre–med, interested in medicine, and wanted to try a writing style I wasn't accustomed to (scientific writing, that is). I loved all of my high school english classes, especially close reading, and while this course didn't do as much of it, Dr. Raymer does an incredible job of helping you transition out of the "five paragraph" structure and into reading research papers and mediating debates. If you have an interest in modern medicine and have heard that it's the professor that makes the class, Dr. Raymer is that professor!
This is an amazing Expos section! If you are interested in biology, technology, philosophy, and/or ethics, this expos will have something to pique your interest. The readings are really well curated (even if a bit dense) and the class discussions are lively, so if you do the readings and participate, you will have a great time. And, Dr. Raymer is an amazing preceptor: she is expertly knowledgeable about biology, ethics, and writing, gives excellent feedback to help you in your writing process, and is a very nice person (and pretty lenient in class)!
This is definitely one of the strongest Expos choices one can take. The workload is not heavy (especially when papers are not close to being due), and the readings are both interesting and palatable. An A is certainly attainable in this course if you write well and execute (there is also a draft before every essay, which you maximize as much as you see fit).
Writing is important! This class is a great way to learn how to be better at it. Frontiers of Biomedicine has really engaging content and it's rare to find someone who gives feedback that is as thorough and personalized as Emilie. This class is definitely more straightforward than other Expos courses but you'll doubtlessly come out of it a better writer. Emilie finds a way to balance support and criticism in a very encouraging way, and it's possible to glean a lot from this experience (and get a good grade).
You will have to take this course anyway, but if you carefully select your topic of interest and are lucky enough to have as amazing an instructor as I did, you will not regret it and will at times forget that this is a requirement. There are ways to make this an extremely productive and exciting experience. I was very lucky to have mine turn out the way it did and I have Dr. Raymer to thank for this
