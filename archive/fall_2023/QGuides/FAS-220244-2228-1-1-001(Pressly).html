This class is a terrific way to critically about everything you take for granted, from your phone to your alone time. Professor Pressly is a great professor who always invites open dialogue and makes you feel heard and respected!
Emmmmmm.... do you want to read a lot and feel that you should've joined the Expos 20 about the exact same topic? If that's the case, save yourself some time and take the Expos 20. Lowry is a great professor that makes interesting comments, but the class itself won't be as good as you think is going to be. That is partly my problem, because I didn't do the readings, but if you do them, you'll have better debates––only ifyour classmates also read. At the end of the course it was evident that many people were bored, but please stay for the museum because it is actually so so cool. The problem with this seminar is the fact that it is a seminar, because you won't prioritize it as much as your other courses.
Substantial readings but they are all super interesting and are summaries in class. HIGHLY recommend doing them because it adds to discussion but not quizzed on them.
Discussions in class are a ton of fun. Everyone in this class is super different and has wildly different perspectives on matters of privacy. This was probably my most diverse class here.
Every time I leave class feeling like my brain and broken and been put back together again in the way possible way.
Not all readings are directly related to privacy, this class approaches privacy from many different angles, but they tie together during discussion.
Also, the prof. sometimes brings up other relevant opportunities that students can check out.

Oh and I can’t forget! One of the best parts of this class was a personal museum visit with artworks specifically selected and pulled out of storage for our class purposes.

Highly recommend the class to everyone.
Even though it is a freshman seminar, I recommend taking it very seriously. Doing the readings, paying close attention to the discussions during class and engaging in discussions with your peers will make your experience much better and more learnable.
If you're interested in digital privacy, or even just philosophy in general, this is a great Freshman Seminar! Lowry is incredibly smart–it shows that this is his area of expertise–and great at kickstarting class discussions. Some of the readings are a little dense and daunting, but Lowry is good at breaking them down during class time. I think this class is a little less demanding than some other Freshman Seminars–no essays, just short weekly discussion posts–but I still feel like I learned a lot. The quality of the class would improve a lot if everyone did the readings.
Lowry is a really good teacher and I would definitely take this seminar again. It has a really light course–load
