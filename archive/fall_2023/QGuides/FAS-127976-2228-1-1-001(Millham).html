This course is a fantastic intro to the history of surgery, from its very beginnings to modern techniques. Dr. Millham is an incredible teacher and takes pride in teaching us about his craft (he is an incredibly accomplished trauma surgeon in Boston.) He teaches the class masterfully, with many field trips that help explain subjects (including one to the HMS Anatomy lab). With his personal anecdotes and love for teaching, I highly recommend taking A brief history of surgery with Dr. Millham.
Make the best of the amount of knowledge you can get from Dr. Milham. You will get an experience unlike any other in your freshman year and learn things that not many people at all are exposed to. If you have any general interest in learning the progression that led to the medical practice we have today, then I highly recommend this course.
If you are interested in pursuing medicine, but you have no clue as to what type of medicine you would like to pursue, I think this is a very good course to take. Personally, I didn't know if I would be interested in surgery but this course gives you a good idea of what the profession is like. Honestly, I am very glad I took this course.
This class is amazing! The professor is great, and the content is really interesting. The trips are also a lot of fun.
Take the course –– it is intriguing even for those who are not pre–med and prompts additional avenues of exploration in terms of research and other opportunities for careers in healthcare or other fields
This class is amazing and interesting, you should definitely take it.
Take the class! You'll learn not only about surgery but about medicine as a whole. The perspectives explored across the lectures and readings are valuable and intentionally included
TAKE THE CLASSS! We go to Hospitals and learn about medicine from various perspectives: good, bad, dark, doctors, hospitals. Dr.Millham is also super cool; he even brings food for us. You won't regret taking the course! You will also learn medical practices!!
If you are interested in biology or history or just want to expand your horizon in general, this class is a great fit for you. It is not a class for pre–meds only, it will be really interesting for anyone.
If you have any interest in the history of science, medicine, surgery, or just social history this is a great class. Dr. Millham is a very good professor and wields a very large wealth of knowledge.
Dr. Millham is incredible, funny, and insightful. He incites a lot of great discussion in class and wishes the best for all of his students, both academically and in well–being.
If you are interested in surgery/medicine, please do take this class as it will expand your horizons on the progression into modern medicine. Field trips were true Harvard moments as well (you get first–hand experience in cadaver lab, see actual medical records from hundreds of years ago, and the ether dome).
Take this class! You do not have to be pre–med, and it's a low stakes, fun way to learn about something you'll probably otherwise never get an opportunity to explore. There are a few field trips that make the class more worth it, too!
