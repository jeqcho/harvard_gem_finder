This is a great class, and you will learn a lot. However, do not expect to get an A if you put a reasonable amount of time into the class. I learned a lot, but only a certain number of people get As and you compete against grad students.
This class teaches a lot of useful technical skills and is very useful for students in both STEM and the humanities. However, if you're looking for an easy A, this is not the class you're looking for. A's are only reserved for the top students in the class (even though the assignments ask students to do basically the same thing) and the result is that you're constantly comparing your work to other students' in the class and trying to one–up them. It's an incredibly unpleasant way to work and hindered my enjoyment of the material. I do think these skills are really useful and the final project was a great way to apply what I had learned and investigate something that I was really interested in. I did end up enjoying the course, but it was not without its frustrating moments.
Lot of self–learning involved, and you might struggle with some of the concepts, especially when you need to work with difficult–to–mine data for your own project at the end of the term. But if you aren't daunted by that and truly committed to learning about remote sensing (including by reading independently), I would highly recommend this course! The professor regularly shares useful resources that you can dive into to expand your knowledge of the subject beyond what is covered in class.
"Remote Sensing" should probably be the real title of this class. The content is very specific – it covers basic ArcGIS and Google Earth Engine skills for manipulating raster data, focusing on images collected by satellites. If you are interested in spatial data / GIS, but not interested in those exact topics, I would recommend taking one of Jill's other classes on ArcGIS instead of this one.

That being said, Jill is a helpful instructor who gives interesting lectures, and the class is relatively relaxed. So if you are new to ArcGIS and want to learn about remoting sensing as well, I would recommend taking this class!
Try to make a big effort not to fall behind. The lack of an attendance requirement + no late penalty can be dangerous as far as getting off track.
Know why you need to take this course before you do.
Jill is a queen. This class is also just a low–stakes fun course (I took this as a 5th course at the college) that is helpful if you're even remotely interested in the subject! You also look at pretty maps alot.
