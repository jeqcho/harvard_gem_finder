Great setting to learn about the meta aspects of research and PhD life that are often not clearly exposed. Offers a open setting to talk about these challenges and provides some basic tips and skills to overcome them. Not everything is useful for everyone, but most students will find some part of the course useful. Most importantly, a great way to know your cohort and build connections! I am very glad Harvard has a course like this.
please be very open to learning and leverage the course staff for your learning. Yaniv and John are incredible people and amazing resources here at Harvard.
It may seem annoying that this class is a requirement, but it's great at bringing the cohort together and validating everyone's experience, so I'm grateful for the class!
This class is worth showing up for. You get to establish a social foundation at Harvard as well as understanding the things your advisor won't tell you. Not because they withhold important knowledge about how to be a grad student, but sometimes they forget to mention or assume you already have the soft skills required to be successful.
Please take it! This class was amazing – it really helps with transitioning into grad school, making friends, and establishing a support system!
This is a really awesome class! Even though it's technically required lol, I still would highly recommend. You'll get a lot out of it and it's a great way to meet new people :).
Highly recommend!!! This course was amazing. Engage with the course material and get the most out of it, because these lessons are super useful throughout the rest of your PhD.
I think this is a good course for getting to know that feeling anxiety during PhD is a very normal thing, almost everyone goes through it some time.
You should definitely take this class during your first year. It will prepare you for your PhD journey and you will make lots of valuable friends.

I think (and hope) this course is required, but I suggest, as much as possible, actually engaging with your peers and the material. This class is meant to be very easy and that makes it possible to slide by without ever really engaging, but I think there is real personal value in choosing to engage and doing so gives you a chance to create the culture and community that you want to see for your cohort. Go to office hours, talk to people, it's a great chance to form relationships that can give you a support system outside of your little academic corner.
It's a great class. Just come in with an open mind.
