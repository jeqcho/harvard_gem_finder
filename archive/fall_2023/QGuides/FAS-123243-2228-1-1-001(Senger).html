What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is hard, but it's quite rewarding. Learning (co)homology is really important, and it's fun to see the topological fruit that comes from lots of algebraic labor. The material is difficult but not overwhelmingly so, and Andy is a great lecturer and makes everything easy to follow.
Take this class if you like topological invariants and working with exact sequences.
the course introduces concepts in algebraic topology which are absolutely essential for a working mathematician... why this is so is not demonstrated very well, and much of the material (definitions, theorems, techniques etc) feels unmotivated by broader questions or connections with other fields
Good class! Miller's notes and Hatcher's textbook pair well as resources.
Andy's office hours are really great for clarifying concepts that might have been gone over too quickly in class
Homework is very frequent & does require some thought. However, spending the extra time slogging through the problems is incredibly useful in grasping the core concepts of the subject.
This is not an easy course, but it's not super difficult as far as graduate math goes. Homology is a beautiful subject, and you will learn some very powerful tools to handle topological spaces in this course. Be prepared for some difficult concepts and lots of category theory, however, and also don't let yourself fall too behind! (the homeworks can be on the longer side, and there's one every week, on top of fast–paced lectures).
