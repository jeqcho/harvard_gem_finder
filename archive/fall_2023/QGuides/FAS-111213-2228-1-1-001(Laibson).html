What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you want to do macro, investing time into coding problems is very worth it. Algebra not so much, given reasonable effort.
Laibson and Bilal are both fantastic professors! The material is relevant whether or not you go into macro (e.g. dynamic optimization) so go into the course with an open mind.
The methodological tools are very useful regardless ones future research area. I believe this is the most important aspect of this course.
grad macro is incredibly different from undergrad macro, and in a way, I like it. 2010c is significantly more microfounded than 101xb, and there are zero traces of solow/is–lm. that's because so much of frontier macro involves massive undertakings that require a lot of mathematical priors – microfounding gives rise to distributional concerns, which then involves integrals and markov chains and transition functions; thinking rigorously about 'evolution across time' gives rise to dynamic programming and intertemporal optimization. my sense is that 2010c (both David and Adrien's part) focus on developing these concepts and share landmark models through the lens of these tools. I enjoyed this very much, because it means that you are not simply rote memorizing the outcomes of the permanent income hypothesis, you are internalizing all the assumptions that are made in the model and understand how to derive it on pen and paper. it means that you get to learn a set of skills that you can use on any inter–temporal optimization problem, and can even extend it to situations with many individuals making personal decisions that aggregate in some way to give solutions that are not immediately apparent. it also means that it's much easier to accept the assumptions as compared to undergrad macro – but beginning at a lower level of aggregation also means a lot more work to get the aggregate results you desire.

and 2010c is a lot of work! at the same time, taking it as an undergrad, I never particularly felt at any point that it was inaccessible to me, it's just that the tone of grad classes is just really different than undergrad classes. eg. grad classes like 2010c just keep on going and the pace is frenetic, so you have to be able to devote time and energy to keep up with that
The class provides a great introduction to dynamic programming and some of the computational methods in macro.
