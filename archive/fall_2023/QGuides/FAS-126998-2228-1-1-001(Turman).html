What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a required course in the French language series. On the whole, it was helpful for fluency and my writing, but I’m not sure how much my spoken French improved over the semester.
I would like to tell future students that exploring a topic as relevant as social justice writing in any language can not only change the way you speak that language for the better, but also the way you think about your words and constructing them in your favor.
Would highly recommend this class and Dr. Turman! This is the first French course I have taken at Harvard in person (took 2 on zoom), and it was very enjoyable. Class time has a very relaxed feel, mostly focusing on conversation with other students about various aspects of writing or pieces that we were reading. I definitely improved my speaking and listening skills because of this. The most stressful aspects of the course were vocabulary quizzes which were very short and easy (just know the words) and in–class essays. However, Dr. Turman is very fair in grading in–class essays, and they are a smaller part of your grade than the final product. Additionally, it was very helpful to have a template with feedback when you go to write the second draft! In short, would highly recommend! I learned a lot from this class without being too stressed about it.
