This course is a lot of work and time but I think its a worthy investment. If you have a choice in taking it I would say make sure you are ready for the work. I sometimes had to spend more than 25 hours a week working on the homework but in the end it really does pay off. I feel much more confident in my understanding of the concepts covered by the course than I did at the beginning.

I do think if you've never seen Bayesian statistics before it would help to review that. This class seems to assume a fair amount of knowledge of Bayesian statistics which was frustrating and another obstacle to overcome. But if you haven't seen it before it is not insurmountable, it will just require more time.

Great class; Lucas is a great instructor. The lectures and lecture notes are clear and is easy to follow. The homework problems can occasionally be tricky, but is not too bad compared to math classes, for example.
Arguably Prof. Janson went a bit easier this semester—the midterm and final exams were only two questions, not three. But this course is still HARD. Maybe deceptively so: you could be in a situation where you attend lecture, catch up on the notes, and feel like you have a good command of the material. But then you see a homework problem and just have no idea how to even start it. And when you do figure it out, it's like two pages of bashing. Biweekly problem sets help you manage the workload, but this also creates a dual threat: you get less chances to review the material through solving problems. Anyway, I cannot do the difficulty justice so I'll instead just point to past reviews.

Lucas is a terrific lecturer in this course and delivers the material with fluency, seeking to build deeper statistical intuition through the material. This same ethos is reflected in the excellent course notes. Consequently I actually thought the material was more engaging and interesting than Stat 210 and even Stat 111; I definitely left this course having not just learned lots, but also with a better statistical intuition. But, still. Should you take on this course, do NOT underestimate the difficulty.

Stat 211 was a GREAT course. I think Lucas read the Q guide reviews from last year, really slowed the course down from last year (I personally would have preferred he went through the material faster at times), with emphasis on feedback + clarifying confusing points. The midterm and final were also much more manageable than expected (shortened from 3 questions to 2), and took only about ~4–5 hours to finish solving. This course was excellent and Lucas was an incredible lecturer. His examples in class were extremely clear and useful for understanding the subject material. In addition, the way he ran class with the inclusion of pencil problems was fantastic for facilitating learning. I learned a lot from our problem sets, which were very well structured and always released on time. Feedback was timely and helpful, and the exams were well calibrated to the material. All in all, this was the best class I have taken so far at Harvard.

The most challenging course I've taken. You have to be VERY good to be able to do the homework assignments alone, i.e., it's possible that applies to you but I wouldn't count on it. What I would count on, then, is every assignment taking 20–30 hours of hard work and pain even with a good group of people working together. There is certainly a big feeling of accomplishment after these assignments, and they are very good at forcing you to learn the material, but don't underestimate the pain and toughness needed to get there.
Really challenging course, and the psets and exams are quite stressful, but it's a really rewarding experience coming out of it!
I think last year's q–guide overhyped the difficulty of this course. The difficulty of the exams was definitely decreased significantly this year compared to last year, but the psets were the same, and they are reasonable and quite interesting. I think what differs between 211 and 210/212 is that the problems you work on are a lot closer to intuition, and feel more intricate rather than complicated. There is less machinery, and a lot more reasoning. Janson is a really good lecturer who gives a lot of intuition for the material really well, and I really enjoyed this course because the problems are easy to understand, even if their solutions were more complicated.
