Really interesting class that goes in–depth on the concept of exoplanets. Not too difficult, and group work is encouraged
I really enjoyed this class! You get a good foundation in exoplanet detection methods, although there may be some review depending on your background. A lot of class time is spent working through problems with other students.
Seriously take this class if you re fascinated about exoplanets. You get a lot out of it.
Take this class if you have the chance! It is the perfect mix of interesting, challenging, and fun. Professor Johnson is awesome and amazing at teaching this course. He is one of the best in the field and you won't regret taking it. I learned a lot in an environment that really focuses on learning and collaborative work rather than pressure to memorize or be evaluated.
This class is based around worksheets that you complete in groups in class. You average around one worksheet a week, and the entire grading for the class is based around write–ups of your worksheet solutions on Blogger. You have to use basic LATEX to do so, and some of the worksheets require Python. Class participation is absolutely necessary.
Take this course! It is not a lot of out of class work (basically just writing up what you did in class) and the material you learn in class is very exciting!
This is a chill way to become much more acclimated to the field of exoplanets! Classes are really interesting and collaborative, and the assignments were low stress. Highly recommend to anyone with a bit of a background in astrophysics who wants to get a cohesive but low stress foundation in this subject.
This was definitely much more math and physics based than the mind–blowing philosophical type of class you might expect with such a new field as exoplanets. So just be aware that you will be doing lots of math going into it, like mathematically deriving Kepler's Laws, etc.
Professor Johnson is my favorite astro professor at Harvard, and 110 is a great example of why. He really cares about learning, and that goal is really emphasized throughout the course. The in–class "quizzes" are meant to make sure that students are keeping pace with the rest of the class and I never felt overwhelmed by the course content. The class is also pretty dependent on the students, so it's best to come in with a good faith attitude to the course.
John is one of the best and most supportive professors I've ever had, and the course is an excellent introduction to exoplanets, detection methods, and planetary science. It is neither incredibly difficult nor has a burdensome workload.
