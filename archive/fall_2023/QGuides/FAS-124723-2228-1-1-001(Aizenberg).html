Be prepared to spend a lot of time on work if you want to succeed.
This course covers a lot of interesting chemistry material. At least two years of chemistry would be useful to fully understand the class.
This course is intellectually rigorous, but designed intelligently with students in mind. The course load felt just right to me and I loved that nothing was obvious on homework and quizzes. I felt challenged to think and supported by the learning in class. I took away a lot of great information and intuition from the course. Dr. Aizenberg is a supportive mentor and excellent professor.
TAKE THIS CLASS especially if you have any interest in applying chemistry in your work as an engineer across any discipline!! Prof Aizenberg is one of the best professors I've ever had!! She is such an engaging lecturer and cares SO much about her students. You learn so much. Problem sets and quizzes can be difficult but you learn a lot from them, especially if you go to office hours. Don't be afraid to ask questions––Prof Aizenberg wants you to succeed!!
This is a fantastic course to come to have an understanding of how to design materials from a chemical background. Having a background in chemistry was definitely helpful, though not entirely necessary (you will have to do more catchup / learning if you have less chemistry background). The workload is heavier when there are weeks with both quizzes and pset, but tapers off at the end.
It's a really great class for engineers interested in chemistry topics.
Professor Aizenberg is a great teacher and makes herself available for questions and concerns.
