If you have a remote interest in climate change or clean energy you should apply for this freshman seminar you won't be disappointed.
The people are what really makes this class! The central focus is on discussion and the sharing of knowledge through presentations and guest speakers, and you get the opportunity to really hone in one specific topic in any given session. The reading assignments are great in terms in variety (podcasts, reports, news articles, etc.) but keeping track of what you have to do can get chaotic especially later in the term. The most important part of the class is coming reading to learn and having done the proper research if you are the one presenting.
It's definitely not a "for fun" seminar, but it is definitely still worth your time and attention! I really liked how I gained something valuable yet also didn't have a ton of work.
It is very chill and informative, this is a good freshmen seminar to take if you want a light course load and are interested in the climate crisis.
Take this seminar!
If you are interested in energy and climate policy, you NEED to take this class. Professor Stock is such an accomplished economist and policymaker (former Chair of the Harvard Econ Dept, Chief Economist on Obama's Clean Power Plan, and now executive director of Harvard's new Salata Institute). I would repeat advice that was passed down to me – you make the most out of freshman seminars when you want to learn from the professor, not necessarily the content. Getting to know Prof Stock is such an honor as a freshman. Moreover, he brings in incredible speakers every week – this year, it included a co–founder of the Sunrise Movement, the chief economist at GM, and a Massachusetts state senator among others – who are so valuable to learn from about their expertise and lived experiences. The workload is very manageable – it's essentially just one presentation during the entire semester – along with readings, and you'll gain so much from this class. Especially if you have previous experience/demonstrated interest in climate and energy topics, this freshman seminar is a must–take. It has been such a formative class for me.
This is a great freshman seminar! Professor Stock is amazing, and the guest speakers he brings in are really a great way to see what some paths in the climate space might look like. My peers in the class were all awesome as well! Apply to take this class if you're interested in and have a background in some area of climate.
I had an excellent experience in this seminar with Prof. Stock and encourage anyone who is interested and engaged in the fight against climate change to take it. Over the course of the semester, we learned about policy initiatives and private–sector approaches to solving climate change. Plus, we heard first–hand from many guest speakers, who joined our class most weeks. You will gain an appreciation for the complexity of environmental policy in todays partisan system, an economic background on carbon emissions reduction policy strategies, and an understanding of various types of businesses working to help fight climate change. Readings are assigned weekly, and while some of them are a bit long and technical, most of them are interesting and useful for that weeks class. We had only two assignments all semester: one solo 30–minute presentation that explores one weeks topic, and one final 10–minute group presentation. This first assignment is especially long, but besides the two or three weeks with additional work on these presentations, the workload is fairly limited (only a couple hours of reading that can be skimmed if needed). Prof. Stock is a major player in the environmental economics and policy world, and the opportunity to get to know him and learn from him was incredible. The community of students in the class was also amazing and probably my favorite part of the course. Im leaving this seminar with eleven friends with so many shared interests who I know are going to change the world. I only wish he facilitated more open discussions for us to exchange our perspectives on the material these were some of my favorite moments from the course, but they were rather few and far between. Besides that, I really enjoyed taking this course and recommend it wholeheartedly.
Take it! It'll be a lot to absorb, but you'll love it.
