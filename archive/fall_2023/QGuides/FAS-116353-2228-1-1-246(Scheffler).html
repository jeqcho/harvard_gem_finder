This is a fun and engaging Expos class to take, especially if you're interested in Underworld themed writing.
Very interesting topic and readings, weird and quirky but nice professor, low workload for expos (mainly the three long papers)
I loved this class. Adam was a great professor and was really helpful in the different essay assignments. The readings and films that we worked through were all very engaging and interesting. And for the final project you have complete freedom over your source which is really fun.
This is an excellent but challenging course. Overtime the subject matter grows more and more interesting and Adam does a great job generating interesting discussions and facilitating classroom bonds. You will get out of this what you put in. Theoretically, you could get away with not doing the readings, but it just won't be as fun or interesting. Adam is very accessible and clearly cares about the running of the course. Everything he does feels intentional and there is no busy work.
Be prepared for lots of homework.
It is definitely a great class but the grading can be a little harsh.
This class is very interesting as assignments and class discussions truly make you think about hell, religion, and society in a new way. However, the essay grading is unnecessarily harsh at times and the feedback you receive is often too vague to help you improve your grade. I often compare my paper with other people in different Expos classes that are receiving better grades for papers that aren't better than mine. Adam is overall a very nice person, but sometimes his explanations leave you more confused than you were initially. This is not a bad expos class to take at all, but there are definitely ones that are more reasonably graded.
