TAKE THIS CLASS! This is one of the best courses I've ever taken. Dilek is amazing and it was incredibly performing chemistry in such a low–stakes environment.
This class is awesome. 10 hours a week seems like a lot but they are the best 10 hours if you have a fun group of people to work with in the lab. Definitely recommend!
This is definitely an extremely fun and rewarding class to take! We learned both theoretical and experimental concepts in a very clear and thorough way and the teaching staff always goes out of their way to make the class very fun and engaging.
Dilek and Nejc (tf for spring 2023) are truly some of the sweetest, most caring individuals I've ever met. If I ever had a question or problem, I knew I could turn to them for help without judgment. 10 hours/ week in one classic is extremely intimidating, but for the most part, the time flew by (the only exception would be when we were writing the paper on our research). Additionally, Dilek and Nejc created an environment where we all became extremely close and had a lot of fun together. Very minimal work outside of class, and overall, the class seems to be a lot more relaxed than chem 100r. My only small critique is that it's easy to get used to doing lab procedures without understanding the chemistry behind what's going on — make sure to ask, because otherwise it won't be noticed by the teaching staff. Overall, one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard, highly recommend.
Chem 145 is so great!! If you need to take an experimental lab course, I could not recommend this class enough. Dilek is super great (she honestly feels like our lab mom most of the time) and she makes the 5 hours as painless as she can. She understands that we dedicate a lot of time to this class, so post–labs are super short and simple, and she always checks in to make sure we get sufficient breaks. The actual lab part was also really fun because we got to do a lot of these experiments on our own terms without having to worry about "messing up" in fears of grade repercussions (at least, no major mess ups). I learned a lot of new research techniques and this class made me appreciate chemistry again!
