I would recommend taking this course! You assign yourself your own grade (though you must be very honest with it). With that being said, how much you get out of this course is a function of how much time you put in. Out of the AM distribution courses, this has been my favorite class. There are 2 quizzes and they are exactly like the class worksheets/psets. So as long as you review and fully understand the worksheets and psets, you will be TOTALLY OKAY (there’s also a cheat sheet). The psets aren’t the hardest, just go to OH and work with a partner and you will be fine, the skill checks are super easy and make sure you are keeping up with course material (you can retake the skills checks and Sarah gives you example questions – so you are guaranteed a 100%). The final project was honestly a grind, but that’s because it was hard to reproduce a paper – make sure you choose a good group and good paper/project (and START EARLY – you have an 8 page individual paper). Overall i’d definitely recommend taking this class :) it was great :)if you put in your 100% you will definitely get a good grade. but if you don’t, then that’s up to you
This is a very flexible course that emphasizes your own well–being over simply focusing on grades. It is remarkable how much the course staff poured into this course to give a wider range of options for grading, scheduling, and more. The topics learned in the course are also very helpful in personal projects and other classes. If this course seems interesting to you, you should take it.
Excellent course! While it is self graded, don't expect it to be a gem or an easy A. The course structure means you have to actually understand the concepts and be able to solve problems in order to maintain your self grading privilege. However, this is done in a way that isn't super stressful and ensures you learn at the same time, which is a balance not many courses manage to get right!
This course tried to jam in too many subjects for its own good and the PSETs were designed to be veryy challenging and intentionally vague unless you went to OH's. I just think that the course, while covering a useful topic, tried to jam in too much and the PSETs were unsolvable with just class material. I had to use the internet to basically teach myself the entire course.
Be wary of which instructor is teaching since the class workload/requirements change a lot with the prof. Sarah is very nice and is pretty flexible with grading, but problem sets can be a lot of work, especially if you aren't strong in coding (think 8–10 hours per week). We covered a lot of material and often moved extremely fast, meaning that moving from the class "exercises" to the problem sets was a jump. It's helpful to have friends in the class so you can pset with them. Overall definitely doable, not a lot of grading pressure, but still a solid amount of work.
This class is a relatively good class to take for an applied math requirement. It's not too stressful and the work is manageable as long as you have some problem set partners. The self–graded aspect is a little unclear, but you do need to keep up with the work with the skill checks and the quizzes. There's an incentive to do well on the quizzes because the quiz corrections are pretty tedious. Knowing Python is basically necessary coming into this class because coding is not taught but is needed on all of the problem sets and the final project.
This is an enjoyable class. The grading system takes the focus off of your scores on individual assignments, so you can stress less about that and just focus on learning. The psets were interesting and made me better at coding, the final project was spread out over enough time that it was not overwhelming, and the teaching staff was great.
If you are looking for an introduction to scientific computing that can be applied to essentially all disciplines in STEM, this is the course to take! Most of the topics in this class build on previous math knowledge you probably learned in intro courses and the homework assignments help you gain experience with Python coding.
