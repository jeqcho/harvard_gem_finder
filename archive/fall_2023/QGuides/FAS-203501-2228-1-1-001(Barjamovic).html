What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I really enjoyed this freshman seminar. Gojko is an amazing instructor and class was always interesting. The readings can be challenging at times due to length or difficulty of the subject depth, but I'd recommend to do all of them to maximize your experience from this course. Other than the homework readings, there's basically no other assignments involved which takes away a lot of stress, especially if you have a busy schedule in your other classes. You'll meet super cool guest speakers, which is a great component of the course. Overall, through this course you'll learn about the scholarship of analyzing ancient economic practices and perhaps some connections to modern day business practices, and this class is set up in a way that is low–stress, and I always looked forward to it each week. This is a wonderful gem of a freshman seminar.
If you have the option to take this course, please take it. I think it's only offered every 4 years or so, but if it is, please take it. I don't think that there is a better freshman seminar out there (tbh It is a lot more history than the name implies). Gojko is so chill and creates an environment that is conducive to learning. He's amazing and cares about how our freshman year is going. It doesn't really feel like a class as much as it is a learning experience and I think it embodies what a Harvard liberal arts education can be. The only assignment the entire semester is a 3 minute presentation at the end and we don't normally meet for the whole time period. It almost feels as if you're not taking a 4th class but that doesn't mean you don't learn. The readings are belong (but you don't really need to read them). You get cool trips to museums where you can touch 5000 year old writing tablets or to the Business School and we get cool guest lecturers like the founder of Planet Money, people who worked on the Big Short, or HBS professors who talk about everything from how the 2008 crisis actually happened to white collar criminals and scammers.
I would absolutely take this class!
