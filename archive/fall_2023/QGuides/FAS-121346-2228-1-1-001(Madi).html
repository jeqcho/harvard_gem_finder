Definitely take this class if you're invested in Arabic language studies. Professor Madi is a really excellent professor!
I would tell future students to not go into this course expecting it to be easy. Yes, you will learn a ton don't get me wrong but it is A LOT of work so just be ready for it.
While this class is very difficult, it is also very rewarding. You will learn and improve a lot after taking this class, and you learn things that are genuinely useful and practical. This class will take a lot of time, especially in the beginning but you will slowly get used to it and improve in the language. Ustaadh Amr is a fantastic teacher who cares about his students and he is a very skilled language teacher. If you are willing to put in the time and work for this class, I think that it would be worth it.
This class is A LOT OF WORK but you learn A LOT OF ARABIC. You must know walking in that the nightly homework, biweekly tests, and biweekly essays make this class very demanding––your week may revolve around it like mine did. That being said, its excellently designed, Madi is an amazing teacher, and you learn a lot. But it is very very difficult and requires a high level of buy–in. You have to really love the language and be committed to learning it because again this is a tough class that will ask a lot of you. That being said, you get a lot out of it. Be ready to spend 10–15+ hours a week learning vocab, writing essays, studying grammar, in office hours, etc. but know you'll come out better, the department really cares about you, and the small–class environment cultivates a fun community.
Learning Arabic will be one of the most rewarding experiences in your academic life. On an academic level, because you will see the benefits in knowing a new language for your school– and career–related goals. But especially on a personal level, because you will expand your human knowledge and get to know more people, cultures, and ideas.
Take this course! You'll improve your Arabic speaking and writing skills tremendously
