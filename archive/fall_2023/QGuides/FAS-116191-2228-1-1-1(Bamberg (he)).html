If Janna is still around as a TF, this class will be very doable. If Janna is not, then carefully think before taking this course. Janna was the reason why this class was doable because of how kind and amazing of a teacher she was. If you are a CS concentrator, CS 20 will be more applicable than Math 152. But, if you don't take CS20 in the spring, this is the only option as a fall discrete math class. If you have lots of background in math, you'll be fine. If you don't and are looking for a discrete class, make sure there is a good TF. Otherwise, you will find this course very difficult, as you have to teach yourself at many times.
This course is certainly not easy content–wise – good course to take if you're like me, and forgot to take CS20. Will prepare you for proofs, and hopefully graph theory and whatnot for 124 in the spring. Taken concurrently with CS61 and 2 GenEds, and my courseload is manageable, if a bit easy.

I'd 100% recommend taking this course if you're interested in a unique experience and don't care about the grade. Paul is an awesome teacher, with lots of funny stories, and he's a super kind dude. If Janna is still TF'ing, 1000% take this course, it will change your life and make you realize how awesome TFs can be.

Also, don't worry about the grades. if you just do your PSETs and programming assignments, it will be impossible for you to get below a B in the class.
The class is not easy and requires a good amount of work outside of class, especially if this is a first proof class or first abstract algebra class (which it most likely is). Paul and Janna (who will not be a TF next year because she's graduating, but I'll speak to her awesome TF–ing here) were always very available by email or meeting and are great at explaining and breaking down difficult proofs and topics. You will be amazed at how much more you like math after taking math 152 and seeing how what you originally thought was "pure, abstract" math, is actually applicable to the immediate realms of econ, computer science, and how much of it we can illustrate using code (specifically RShiny).
classes are mostly presented by students; take if you have an amazing TF for the year!! otherwise, be more careful
PROS:
– Really passionate and talented instructor (be sure to find his slides about himself online)
– Unique math, some of which isn't being taught anywhere else
– Small class size (we got pretty close over the semester)
– Reasonable, fair workload
– Really flexible with extensions

CONS:
– You'll probably hate the coding assignments
– We had a godly TF, but this class would not survive in the absence of one
– It's pretty hard to find online resources for a lot of the curriculum since it's so rare
– Learning via class presentations can make it pretty tough to keep up, especially if you check out in class

Don't sweat the presentations! Spend 15–20 minutes preparing–– Paul will help you out if you get stuck.
Buy the textbook early on–– it's not a lifeline per–se, but I tried to avoid buying it and ended up getting it halfway through the semester anyways.
If you're taking this to avoid CS20, don't.
