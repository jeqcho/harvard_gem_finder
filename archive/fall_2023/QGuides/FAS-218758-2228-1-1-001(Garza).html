This is such an amazing and important class to take. You'll learn about various topics, theories, and themes relating to race, gender, colonialism, and solidarity. This class would provide you with an in–depth understanding of the past and of the stories of people coming from marginalized communities that often go unheard.
This is the best class I have ever taken at Harvard.
This is a wonderful class, and you should definitely take it! This class was so life–changing for me, and I really, really loved it.
This is potentially one of the best, if not the best, discussion–based seminars I've had (and I've taken ten at the College). The time flew by in each 2 hr 45 min session. Joy is an excellent facilitator and the students in this class seemed to have done the reading and came to each class engaged. Each class had a good combination of activities (warm–up questions, background information and summary of the main arguments of the texts so everyone is on the same page, solo reflection / partner discussion, structured group discussion based on student–written discussion posts, student presentations on related texts) so things never felt dull. The readings are manageable and interesting. The only assignments are weekly Canvas posts and short writing assignments that build towards your final paper / project (which she is very flexible on).

Overall, one of the best classes I've taken. Given the general dearth of ethnic studies courses here, I am very glad I got the chance to take this while Prof. Garza was still here.
This class is amazing!!! Absolutely take it.

Joy is an incredible instructor who really cares about students, and literally every reading & class assignment is so well thought out (never feels like busywork or unmanageable) to help you think about these important issues and reflect on your own identity/positionality!
