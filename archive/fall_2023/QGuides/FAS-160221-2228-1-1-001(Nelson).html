Dr. Nelson is great and extremely knowledgeable about the subject. Student presentations are very interesting and over highly diverse subject matter. Definitely be prepared for the amount of work required for presentations, reaction papers, and the final paper though.
I loved this class! I gained more empathy and understanding of the diverse experiences that children can have growing up, which is especially valuable when you go to such a privileged institution. I recommend if you are interested in psychology and neuroscience (and you can decide how in depth you want to go with each) as well as even education (it counts for credit in the education secondary). The class also became really close because of how serious the topics were, but we were all engaged and put a lot of care into these discussions. The only thing I would say is that, although the grading is super easy (you'll pass don't you even worry) there is quite a bit more work than most freshman seminars. It was fine for me because I spread it out throughout the semester (I recommend this!!) but I know a lot saved everything to the last minute. For full transparency (I don't know what it will be next year) but we had 5 short short reaction papers, two 45–min lectures to give, and a final research paper.
This is truly a fantastic course with an intelligent professor. You will gain an invaluable contact in Professor Nelson and grow incredibly close with your classmates. However, do try and keep on top of the work for this course because it can build up especially if you wait until the end of the semester,
You will likely spend a large portion of class time (80%+) listening to other students lecture the class. This class fulfills one of the 5 course requirements of the GHHP (Global Health and Health Policy Secondary). If you are taking the class because you want to concentrate in neuroscience, I imagine there may some more explorative classes.
If you are interested in taking a class that emphasizes the connections of biology and psychology, take this class! Dr. Nelson is incredibly knowledgable and I learned so much about child development both from a biological and psychological perspective. I really valued that Dr. Nelson reads every application for this Freshman Seminar and picks a really great group of people to work together. You learn so much about so many people's perspectives and it is very enlightening. I could not recommend this class more!
This is an amazing course to take! It is definitely not as easy as other freshman seminars, but that should not make you stay away. Professor Nelson is such a kind, intelligent mentor and teacher. As well, you will have the time and means to delve deeply into two different topics in early childhood development which if you're interested in global health will make you more informed and give you research ideas. Overall, amazing course to take even if the workload is a bit more than other seminars.
