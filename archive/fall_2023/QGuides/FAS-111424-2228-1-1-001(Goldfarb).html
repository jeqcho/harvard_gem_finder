Comments
Prof. Goldfarb is a fantastic professor and really manages to make the material clear! It is also fascinating to see how the more technical questions in logic relate back to questions in philosophy.
Take this course! It is fascinating and extremely rewarding, and while it is difficult it is not overly time consuming.
Not an easy elective
I really enjoyed this class. I would highly recommend taking it (with one caveat; see below). The material improves your ability to make well–reasoned inferences in real life, and it's also an interesting puzzle by itself. The material is presented in a very intuitive and easy to follow way (although it moves quickly, so you have to make sure you're paying attention), and abstract logical concepts were almost always explained using concrete examples, which helped a lot. I thought Prof Goldfarb was a great teacher, though apparently he won't be teaching Phil 140 anymore.

I would say that this class requires some appreciation for pure math and some skill with mathematical reasoning. If you are only interested in philosophy and not math, then maybe you should think twice about taking this class.
If you take this course, read the textbook, go to every section, take notes in lectures, and prepare to feel incredibly lost. This class is super difficult, even for students with extensive math backgrounds. There is little feedback on assignments, and while the beginning of the course feels super easy, it gets incredibly difficult very quickly. I would only recommend this class if you have an otherwise average course schedule. If you are only taking it for your QRD, take something else.
The class gets a lot harder in the final third
I highly recommend against taking this class unless you are a pure math concentrator. Otherwise, you will find yourself very lost amongst useless lectures and difficult–to–understand textbook chapters.
This is ultimately a course that is fulfilling and worth your while. If you've done some formal maths, the preliminary content of the course may be a slow progression. But, the content leading up to 144 is very cool.
If you enjoy abstract reasoning and solving interesting puzzles, you should take this class! This course will urge you to think deeply about simple things, which in my opinion is a critical component of higher education.
Study hard. Read the TB, take notes on the TB.
Take this course please! I was someone who was very steeped in the continental tradition and this course made me lose sleep but also made my thinking a lot clearer. Also, you come to appreciate the foundation of mathematics. Analytic philosophy is not BS as I thought previously
