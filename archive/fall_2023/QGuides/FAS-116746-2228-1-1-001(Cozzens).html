If you are willing to put in the time and effort this class demands, you should take it. Otherwise, it will be a class you might end up wrongfully resenting. It is a difficult language, but the professors do a vey through job of covering the material and making sure you understand with a simply " mafahempt".
Homework in this course is frequent but manageable, and is really meant to help you make Arabic a part of your daily life. Lessons are engaging, and Richard is very receptive to questions–––he makes sure no one is left behind. My Arabic progressed far more in one semester than I had anticipated it would. It is also helpful that this is a small class, so you have a community of speakers who are at the same level that you are–––this helps with the language–learning process. For example, I would frequently exchange Arabic greetings with my classmates whenever I saw them outside of class. If there is one thing that I would say that this class falters in, it is that it has made me a far better writer than speaker. However, that is partially due to the nature of being in an intro–level class–––it is difficult to have high–level conversations without first understanding grammatical rules, which are learned through writing.
