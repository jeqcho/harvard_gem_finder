This class was a solid introduction to abstract math and it covered groups and rings. But it mostly focused on the fundamental notions and ideas and did not offer too much depth into group theory or ring theory. It exposed us to a good amount of abstract algebra. I can say that the course content is good, the lecture notes taken were amazing, but the psets were a bit grindy at times. They can require a lot of casework and proof by exhaustion. Also, a lot of times, the concepts we learned weren't motivated very well, so it felt a bit irrelevant and boring. However, it all makes sense at the end.

I think Myrto is a pretty good lecturer, and most of the time lectures were pretty clear and approachable. I personally enjoyed group theory more than ring theory, but I found stuff related to number theory quite interesting at the end. I can say that this course is slightly slow if you previously took math 25 (or similar level of math). For me that was good, because it was an easy course. So, if you're looking for a nice math course to take, I would say you learn a good amount of material in the course but it is not overly challenging, and does not consume all your free time.
Abstract algebra is just the best. It's a form of wizardry, man. I wish the lectures had been somewhat clearer, and the lecturer more responsive to questions about the material, but if you liked the more conceptual parts of a class like 22a, 122 is a great next step.
Although the material is quite interesting, the instruction was not the most ideal. That being said, the TFs Hahn and Eliot were absolutely amazing! Their sections were so constructive, and their office hours and feedback were very fleshed out. Hahn's notes, too, were absolutely amazing – truly a life saver.

This class is a fairly typical upper–level math class in terms of difficulty. It is well paced and there is not too much work per week. The lectures were not bad, but also not exceptional. The most helpful part of the course were the lecture notes taken by the CAs.
Take it. It is a good class and I learned a lot. I would recommend it if you are interested in math
The course provided an in–depth exploration into groups and rings. I highly recommend looking at the course’s quite exceptional lecture notes to get an idea of what material the class covers, and during the class for review. It is a foundational course in pure math, and I really learned to think abstractly in terms of the structural and functional characteristics of mathematical objects. It gives you the confidence to think about invented mathematical objects that you probably aren’t familiar with and have to rely on examples to ground your intuition. Go into the class with a friend – it will help clarify concepts with each other. The beginning of the class is very very slow. But in the second half of the semester, things pick up wildly and the class becomes very fast, so be warned.
