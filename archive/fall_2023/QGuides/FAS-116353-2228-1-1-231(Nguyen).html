Was a great course as an introduction to philosophy and completing the Expos requirement. The workload is very manageable and not time–consuming at all. The preceptor Jen is very dedicated to her job and holds great respect for the student no matter what level of writing they are entering. Definitely a GEM!!!
This was now of my favorite experiences during my freshman fall. Jen is such an amazing professor. She is very conducive to in class discussions. Between classroom discussions and office hours, I felt that I was fully engaging with the material. The course content is extremely interesting and the readings are rarely daunting. Our first two essays are very straightforward, because Jen does a great job of guiding you through them. If you want an expos class that is both fulfilling academically and not too daunting, this class is for you. Most people, including myself, go into the class without any prior experience with philosophy, so you should not be worried if that is you.

But if you work at it you can figure it out. And you really do get a lot out of the class. Jen is a great instructor. She knows exactly what she's talking about. And the readings are very eye–opening.
I think this is an interesting class to take and would recommend it.
For background purposes, I have never taken a formal philosophy course and am planning on being a STEM major.

I chose this class because I believed that the topic sounded interesting. I did not read reviews beforehand or during, so everything I say is with an unbiased view of the class.

The readings we do in this class are difficult and time–consuming, I won't lie. For instance, sometimes I would spend 4–5 hours reading something because I would have to slow down on each page to fully understand an argument being made. Readings like this were not often, nor did they constitute a bulk of the course. I'd say maybe 3–4 weeks. The quizzes that we had for those readings were simple and doable, so long as you did your readings. Even then, they were not mean to trick you.

The class is very structured, in a way that everything we do has a very specific reason for doing it. Paying attention to what we do in class is vital to doing well on your papers.

The feedback we receive for our papers is very specific and targeted. They are there for a reason. Follow them and you'll do well. Again, using class resources and discussions will greatly help in this regard.

The content is super interesting, not something I would've considered to enjoy this much! The choice of philosophers made sense and offered 2 very different examples about how philosophy is presented.

Workload: Most weeks, not too much. Weeks with dense readings? Couple hours. Papers? More than a couple of hours.
Classes: Digestible and specific. Everything we do has a very specific purpose.
Quizzes: Reading is enough. These aren't difficult.
Feedback: Very targeted, helpful. A lot is given for you to work with.
Getting Scores / Feedback?: Takes less than a week.
Difficulty: I won't lie, difficult. Even more difficult if you don't do the readings or listen to discussions.

Again, STEM kid, found it difficult at first, but you can manage, and if you do, you will be so fine!
Take advantage of office hours, simplify your argument to the most basic form, proving each claim, in service of the larger argument. Take your time, and discuss structure in office hours.
This class is quite philosophy heavy, but you do not need to have any prior experience with philosophy. The class also always has a fun, lively environment, and I looked forward to it every week.
I enjoyed this expo a lot. The readings can be a bit more difficult to understand than the average text, however the discussions really help clarify things and can give you something to write about even if you don't really understand the readings. I think the workload is certainly manageable.
Take this course. This course was excellent, as it both vastly improved my writing and gave me a deep understanding of the philosophy of friendship. Through her lectures and feedback, Jen taught me how to express my ideas in a way that was concise and easily understandable for my readers. She continually emphasized the importance of "writing for the reader," which is a concept I plan to apply in all of my writing going forward, as this style of writing allows me not just to share my ideas, but to convince my audience of my ideas. Jen was also very accessible, and she took a great deal of time and care when giving me her feedback on my essays–––her comments really set me up to succeed.

Another major strength of this course was that Jen made it very easy to understand difficult concepts. Some of the reading in this course is difficult, but after each reading, Jen fostered illuminating discussions that enhanced my understanding of the text. She welcomed student input, and many times when I didn't understand something, I actually ended up learning it from one of my classmates. Jen also always made herself available after class to talk through any outstanding questions I or my classmates had.

Many people see Expos 20 as just a requirement, but this was actually my favorite course this semester. The only problem with it is that it doesn't last for more than a semester! The content we cover is engaging and relatable to everyone, even if you don't have a background in philosophy. At the end of this course, I find myself wanting to study this topic more. I am currently not a philosophy concentrator (nor am I concentrating in anything in the humanities, for that matter), but I feel that this course is very much a philosophy gateway drug.
I would definitely recommend taking this class. Jen is an amazing professor, and you learn a lot about her not just in writing but about yourself too. The texts were (although not always easy) but interesting to read and there is a lot of help and support for essays and assignments. Class discussions are usually the best part. Just don't procrastinate on essays!
One of the hardest aspects of this class is managing your time. Although it's easier said than done, it will make your life so much easier if you break down the writing process into steps and allocate your time over days or weeks. Coming from personal experience, submitting my revision after working on it for 7 hours straight on the day it was due was not fun. :D Don't be like me! Essentially, the hardest part is starting your work, so once you get that over with, the rest should be a little bit easier. Good luck!
