What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Really good introduction to ethnography.
There is no one right way to conduct the semester–long ethnographic research project. As long as you enjoy the subject of your project, you will be able to succeed and feel even more compelled to engage with your field site.
I enjoyed this class primarily because of the teaching staff and the community within the class. Three hours often felt a bit too long and there were too many readings for everyone to do in full so class discussion was often a bit lackluster. But if you choose a fun, chill topic, the research component can be enjoyable and I really appreciated how deeply and critically we evaluated the ethnographic method’s ethics. I took it for my social studies methods requirement and found it helpful for giving me a feel for the method and how to properly (and ethically) conduct ethnographies.
Take this class!!! Especially Social Studies students who are interested in completing their methodology requirement. If you are planning on using any ethnographic method for your thesis, this will be a class to take. Not only is it enjoyable in terms of course content, but also Annikki and Chiara are the best!! The project felt personally significant, and despite the many hours spent in class, did not feel all too repetitive.
this class is so, so, so good and SO fun (especially in devising and carrying out your own ethnography). annikki and chiara are both incredibly supportive –– they are both blessings in the classroom and do so much to cultivate an enriching class community i was always excited to see on thursday afternoon. you get so much out of this class just by engaging thoughtfully with course materials and the project, and it will make you a much better researcher by the end of it!
This class was the single biggest contributor to my anthropology skillset –– you learn to structure and implement research methods in anthropology (interviews, participant observation, ethnographies, artifact analysis, and so many important qualitative methods that everyone should learn) that will directly carry over to any future research or thesis! I have had so many takeaways from this class, especially since every single assignment was well thought out, applied course concepts, and resulted in so much amazing feedback/further directions from Annikki and Chiara. I will certainly be referring back to notes and feedback from this class for designing future ethnographies, participant observation, consent forms, etc. that really form the basis of my anthropological skillset!!
