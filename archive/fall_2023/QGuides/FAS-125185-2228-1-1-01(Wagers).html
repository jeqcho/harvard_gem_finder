What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

If you want to learn about how to read a scientific paper and also about leading research in aging biology, take this course! There is really not too much work – the psets are pretty short and the exams are (relatively) low stress. However just because there isn’t that much work doesn’t mean that its a gem – the PSETs are graded pretty hard. The course casts a pretty wide net and you will go through information very fast, but there is always time to focus in on something specific that you are interested in for the final project or on certain PSET questions.

The content can also be pretty hard to understand sometimes, but I feel like I’ve gotten a really good overview of the entire aging biology field.
This class is one of the best–structured science classes at Harvard. If you are passionate about aging biology, a degenerative disease, or just biomedicine in general, I would highly encourage taking this course. The course staff truly cares about helping you learn, and the material manages to cover a sheer breadth of material without seeming overwhelming. Not a super easy course, but at the same time not unmanageable, frustrating, or difficult.
This class is amazing. One of the best bio classes I've taken at Harvard. Amy is fantastic. Take this course!!
This is a fantastic course to take! It has an extremely manageable workload, interesting papers, great lectures, and will change the way you think about aging on both a molecular and social level!
This is definitely a REALLY great course. Amie gives very interesting lectures and really knows her stuff. The course has heavy focuses on not just the biology aspect of aging, but also the social/political consequences of it (without the course getting political) as well as drug development. I would recommend having taken a biology class or two (SCRB 10 was super helpful) just so you can follow easily in lecture. I'd say whereas SCRB 10 had a focus on being creative and thinking big, this course encourages you to think very practically. This aspect is reflected in the psets and final project where the teaching team isn't afraid to push back if they think experiments you propose aren't realistic or feasible, so keep that in mind when doing assignments. However, it is very evident that Amie genuinely cares about what you learn and wants you to take certain things away from the course. The course overall is extremely interesting, but appropriately challenging. The psets and midterms are challenging but doable if you put in a little bit of time to study and get them done. The final project can also be a lot of writing, but they gave us feedback based on our outlines so we knew how to refine our proposals, so I'm hoping they turn out well based on that. I agree with previous years' Q guides that it's not an easy A, but definitely possible. Still waiting for grades to come out, so we'll see how those turn out.
This class was super great. It was really interesting, and also introduces you to a field in biology that often isn't covered much in other science courses. This is a super fun upper–level SCRB class; the entire teaching team is supportive and always make themselves available to help students. Amy is such a great professor, and she is incredibly supportive of the curiosity and interests of her students, and works to foster a great classroom environment. Heidi and Nafta are also great, and are super helpful.
Amy is an amazing professor and the course staff are super nice! The content is really interesting you will definitely learn a lot, especially if you invest effort. Pay attention to lecture and read all the papers, it will help a lot for the exams (which are also not too bad if you study!)
This is a very solid course. The teaching team makes it a great course! Amy, Heidi, and Nafta were awesome to work with all semester. They care so much about the students and about the content matter. This makes everyone in the class much more engaged and passionate about the course as well. This class is NOT a gem, but if you are interested in learning about aging – go for it!
This is a challenging but very rewarding class! Amy is the best and is so dedicated to helping her students get the most out of her class. The midterms are very fair and the best way to prepare is by engaging with the material by putting thought into the psets and going to office hours.
