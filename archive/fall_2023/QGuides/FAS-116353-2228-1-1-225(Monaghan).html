What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great class if you are looking for a rigorous means of sharpening your writing and research skills as you begin college and have at least some experience writing on an academic level. The class is very work intensive and centered around a rather heavy topic, and while there isn't a large amount of reading, you will likely be spending some late nights in libraries drafting, proofreading, and researching your essays. Good time management and communication skills are essential to doing well.

Professor Monaghan is always great at giving timely feedback on work and making herself available to help students. She runs a tight ship but is very fair in her grading and is supportive and understanding of the needs of students. I had more accidents in this class (having to miss class, getting lost, technical difficulties, etc.) than my other three combined, but we were always able to find a solution together.
Take 20 because you get it over with and 10 wouldn't be worth it
I have to be honest, the readings will be hard unless you absolutely love the topic. If you are a war history fan, I would take the class, but otherwise, I would not put it on your list for fear that you will get the class. It is easier to write about something you're passionate about, curious about, or can forsee yourself being passionate or curious about. If not, it's going to be an uphill battle, as I learned this semester.
Don't take your essay's feedback personally, and don't skimp on peer reviewing others' papers. It will be very helpful to them, and you don't want to be the person that only comments "looks good" on an essay.
Prepare for a lot of research and writing styles you may not be used to.
Expos is always just expos, either choose the course based off of your interest to the topic or based off of how easy the professor grades.
Don't shy away from this course just because the topic might be a little heavy. Professor Monaghan is great and provides a lot of feedback for your draft papers. She is easily accessible to get more help if need be.
