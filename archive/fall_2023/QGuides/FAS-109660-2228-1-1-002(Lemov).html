Pretty chill on assignments but plenary often felt like a waste of time although the course staff is very kind. They are definitely there to support you and make sure you do well.
Take each assignment seriously and write it as if it's going into your paper––think of it as a way to save you time and energy down the line when you have to put together your paper draft! It'll make your work so much easier at the end of the semester. And go to office hours with Professor Lemov or your TF, they're always there to help!
I personally did not really enjoy tutorial. The class is mandatory but I did not feel like I took away much that really improved my writing skills. It is a helpful course into introducing you on how to use research tools and archives.
this class does a wonderful job of clearly identifying what historical methods exactly are and how to engage with them through research. however, i did feel as though it can be difficult to find support when your methods or interests don't align with traditional ways of doing things in history, like if your topic lacks archival work. regardless, professor lemov is so incredibly sweet and knowledgeable, and she can really offer a lot of help to individual projects if you approach her.
