What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The main work of this class is exploring "freedom dreams" ––> an idea you have about how the world could be more just that is not tethered to narrow limits of what is probable/feasible. This work is done by bouncing ideas off of the teaching team and others in the class. There are weekly readings that are set at the beginning of the class that included the writings of Robin D.G. Kelley, Bell Hooks, and others. Like most classes (but especially some over others) the amount of value you get out of this class is dependent on how much you put in. This class is less about taking in lots of information and is instead about having time to process and think about the world we want.
If you can take this course or any other with Dr. Bell, I mean this with the utmost sincerity, take it. I’ve never been in a course made so overtly for the benefit of the students, and I truly think that change in academic perspective can be life–changing, especially at this school.
This class will offer you a very new way of approaching academia, as well as the safe space to explore "outlandish" ideas about activism and reformation. I also really liked that it wasn't just an undergraduate class, and appreciated the perspective of the grad students! Took me a while to figure out how this fit into the TDM department, but I would say that anyone interested in any form of artistic expression should consider taking this class and just experiencing the environment themselves.
This is not a TDM class. Do not expect to learn about theater, dance, and/or media. This is a class about community–based activism. That's an awesome topic, just not what I was looking for in my studies of theater. I might have liked the class anyway, except it was so disorganized that it was difficult to follow. There was no real syllabus with dates and assignments. I didn't get the flow of the readings because they all seemed disconnected from each other. We did different activities that seemed to barely relate to each other. The professor did not come to every class meeting. The final projects were tangentially related to the class. I'm not sure I really learned anything.
Prof. Bell encourages you to be you in every way that could possibly mean and to really think about how you want to make an impact on the world and your community. She helps cultivate the passion and future–dreaming necessary for transformation.
