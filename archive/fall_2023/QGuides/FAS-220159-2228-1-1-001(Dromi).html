The assignments and midterm are super manageable and the final project is a lot of fun––everything is very low stress! Take this class if you want to analyze some of your favorite TV shows and movies to learn about the deeper message they hold and open your eyes to the power of pop culture.

This has definitely been one of my FAVORITE classes I've taken so far. Professor Dromi is absolutely amazing. Lectures were always so engaging, usually focused half on the readings/theory behind the theme of the week and then tied to some form of popular culture. We almost always watched a TV show, movie, or Youtube video and analyzed it through the lens of a theorist. Work–wise, the class is super light. Weekly quizzes are very low stakes, you never have to read in depth to get a 100 (however I recommend doing so, sometimes they're long and dense but really interesting for the most part). 5 photo posts and 5 response posts, which is way more fun than your typical discussion post. The photo posts are so much fun because it lets you into the lives of other classmates, and pushes you to see the world through a sociological lens. For both quizzes and posts your lowest grades get dropped (this class is about actually learning, grades were never a concern). Lastly, a midterm paper and final assignment that were relatively "easy". The prompts are given way in advance so there is tons of time. There is so much support from Dromi, the TFs and the sociology department overall. Course content itself was so interesting especially towards the middle when we got to Economic Sociology, Black Feminist Theory, and Critical Race Theory. Section was also very chill, usually a review of one of the readings we didn't cover in class and some sort of application to more pop culture. I truly learned so much. If you're interested in popular culture (TV shows, movies, books, social media) and how sociology theory applies, I cannot recommend this class enough. It seriously is a GEM. I never felt overwhelmed, bored, or lost. This class is a great intro to the sociology department, 1000000/10.
