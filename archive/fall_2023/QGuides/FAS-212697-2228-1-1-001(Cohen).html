This is a tranquil class that usually has a 2 page paper due every week, however sometimes there will be assignments where we watch a movie, or visit a location, or both.
I would strongly recommend this class to anyone that is worried about a busy fall schedule. A benefit to this class is the bonds you will form with other first–years as well.
It is a lot of fun and is more interesting than it seems.
I would recommend this class to anyone who is looking for a course with an easy work–load that is manageable and interesting.
I recommend this seminar to all upcoming/current freshmen. The readings aren't very interesting, but the class discussions are fun especially when you have a diverse group of students.
I would tell students that this class will expose them to multiple cultures in different time periods and will cause you to think about what it means to be human since death is a large topic of the course.
This class provides a super intriguing look at the afterlife through readings, guest speakers, and readings. There is a weekly 3–5 page paper plus reading / movie, but both of these are, for the most important, fairly enjoyable to complete and low pressure. My favorite assignment was watching a ghost movie of our choice, but some of these more entertaining assignments are interspersed with longer, denser readings. Overall, however, the classroom discussions are super engaging and enjoyable, and all of the speakers were fantastic. If you have any inkling of an interest in religious studies, the concept of the afterlife, or just getting to watch movies for a class assignment, this is definitely a solid course for you.
I would tell anyone to take this class as it is stress free, fun, and super interesting. My only advice is that you should be prepared to have mature conversation about other cultures as well as your own beliefs if applicable.
