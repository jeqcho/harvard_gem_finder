What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is especially beneficial for specialized, advanced students interested in the fields of the immune microenvironment and immuno–oncology. The constituent lectures and proposal assignment were very beneficial for advancing knowledge of the material.
Dr. Jain is great and lots of interesting guest lecturers. I learned a lot in this class! Class sessions require no prep but the final assignment is a bit of a beast. They are helpful with any questions about the assignment and set some early deadlines to hopefully push you to complete it earlier in the semester. Still ended up being a lot of work at end of semester for me but worth it.
This is a great class for anyone who hasn't taken a cancer biology class yet. Professor Jain does a great job with bringing leading researchers from Harvard and MIT to talk about their research and how it applies to solid tumors. The course is very flexible, where the assignments are not actually graded but are simply there to help students manage their time to write and complete the project. The final project itself is challenging and takes a lot of time (roughly 2–4 hours/week on average, with the last couple of weeks or so being more like 6–8 hours/week) and requires you to take meaningful time to think through the scientific problems you want to solve. However, the project is fulfilling and can be taken in any direction that the student wishes. I felt like I learned a lot in this class and am glad I took it despite the heavier than average workload.
If you are looking for a more physiological and cellular perspective on cancer, this is a great and broad introduction to just that. It is accessible to many students and gives you the tools to speak and write about cancer.
