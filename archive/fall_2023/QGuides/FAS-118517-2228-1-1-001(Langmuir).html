This class is exciting, and is more thought–provoking than I imagined a STEM class could be. Tons of fun, and often philosophical.
This class is not taught to be a gened course, it is taught like an introductory planetary science course. Strong background knowledge is needed to understand the material and the content–– I would advise you to think strongly about if you understand fundamental chemistry, biology, and geology before taking this class. It was not easy, but is interesting if you have ambitions in the planetary sciences and want to know about the world. The lectures are not recored and slides and the textbook do not contain all material needed for the exam–– you have to listen to the oral presentations done by the professor to succeed in the course. This can be frustrating if you must miss a class because you are sick, or you you did not hear what he said. There are very fun demonstrations and labs done, you feel like you are back in your high school science class–– this is definitely the highlight of the class.

The professor has some very adamant moral beliefs–– he is a strong vegan and will somewhat preach this to you in the course. He is also very stern and not easily approachable, typically answering your question with a question instead of helping you understand materials better. Additionally, if he does not like you or disagrees with you path of study or career choices (should you tell him) he will make that very apparent. Instead of encouraging more people to enter the sciences, he instead shames those who are not. However, the teaching staff (the two TFs) are incredible, both of them are very easy to talk to, very intelligent, kind, and will help you understand the materials in a very constructive way.
Take the class, it's great!
Take this class, you get a lot out of it.
Great class. Not a lot of work, but the concepts can be challenging. Pay attention in class, study before midterm and final and you'll do great.
I would highly recommend this class to anyone with an interest in better understanding the Earth, the role of humans, and earth and planetary sciences. Professor Langmuir is an amazing instructor with so much unique knowledge and insight. He, and the rest of the teaching staff, are incredibly supportive and kind. This course has helped me better understand science and reframed my view of planetary evolution and the role of humans in it.
I want to try and give a wholistic report of the class. I personally loved it and would highly recommend. The course title is deceiving: the class is not just about the future of our planet's habitability but also incorporates the story from the birth of the universe and what has happened to make it habitable. You learn A LOT. best way to get through the course is by annotating the lecture notes, or you could instead read the book. Either will do. The professor is super passionate about the subject and wishes to see you succeed. Take this course if you're interested in learning anything new about all fields of science (chemistry/physics/biology). You'll get to visit rock museums and power plants, which are really cool!
I sort of stumbled into this course but am so glad that I did. It was one of my favorite classes that I have taken because I got to learn about a totally knew field of study in a relatively stress–free way.
Enjoy the lectures and the demonstrations!
The workload for the course is relatively light but the content can be difficult for those not studying planetary sciences simply because the scope of the course is very wide. You learn many things from how atoms interact to the creation of stellar systems. Just make sure to keep up with lectures and explore any questions you have because the content does all tie together in the end, so it is useful to make sure you have a solid understanding in each topic.
Take this course! The professor is an absolute character. The subject matter is interesting, and presented as the story of humanity and all life on Earth from the Big Bang to the present – the greatest story ever told. The labs aren't much work outside of class and are generally fun. The final project gave me a better understanding of humanity's strongly negative impact on our environment and the possible resolutions. You need a science Gen Ed, you might as well take this one!
This class is a gem if you need a gen–ed requirement. It's pretty boring but a good way to get credits
This class doesn't have any tests (apart from the midterm and final), and the only homework is completing unfinished lab and the final project, but don't let that trick you into thinking the class is easy. This is not a class where you can not attend lecture/zone out/skip the material and still hope to score well. Still, this class is well worth it. Professor Langmuir's in–class demonstrations are the highlight of the course. (We had a count going of how many times he brought a blowtorch to class. We lost track.) Overall, this class is a great way to learn "a brief overview of the history of the universe," and to learn about our own roles in shaping the world around us.
