What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class has an excellent promise of teaching about solving inverse problems with statistical tools. That is definitely a very deep and useful topic. But the materials could use some more polish. The initial 1/3 is excellent, the middle 1/3 starts to feel weak, and the final 1/3 covering unsupervised learning and reinforcement learning is not well–organized. As we paced through a range of interesting topics, we barely had the time to even properly scratch the surface. As a result, please think twice the reason that you want to take this course: if you want to learn some tools and interconnected concepts, great, this course could do it, but if you want to develop a rigorous and cohesive understanding of machine learning and its connection to information theory of physics, I'd probably suggest taking a difference course from CS, Stats, or even applied maths department.
The class provides a bigger picture that I think is important.
Sharad is a great guy who delivers exciting lectures and the TFs are very helpful. the material is also quite interesting –– even coming in with a healthy ML background, this class unified a lot of seemingly disparate concepts for me under the framework of information theory. that said, the PSETs are oftentimes unbearably tedious, which is the worst part of the course. the project is also nice and open–ended –– Sharad is always willing to chat and develop ideas with you.
Truly excellent lectures and course materials (lecture notes)! HW can be tedious at times but there were only 5 assignments, so ultimately not too bad. Would highly recommend this course! Very useful course
The lectures are fantastic, and it is a great class to get a new perspective on things like information theory and machine learning, even if you have seen many of the materials before. Sharad is fabulous lecturer, and I will not hesitate to recommend his class for the lectures. The homework’s can be long and tedious, but it’s normal since there aren’t any exams in the class
Content–wise, one of the most interesting classes I've ever taken! The prof is also fantastic – really clear lectures and tries to get to know the students. Workload is quite heavy, especially if you do not have as much background in stats or ML.
