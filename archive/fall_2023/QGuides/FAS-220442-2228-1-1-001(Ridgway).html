One of the best classes & favorite professors I've ever had at Harvard. Every single class I walked out of feeling like I had learned immensely and also had one of the best discussions of my life. Truly, truly a phenomenal class, both to the professor's credit and all the other students in that class. cannot overstate how wonderful this class was.
TAKE THIS! Really loved this course. Dr. Ridgway is awesome. They are sooooooo cool, and their outfits are amazing (I came into class excited to see their fit of the day). Beyond the cool and amazing teaching staff, this course offers valuable lessons on misconceptions of Native Americans. I left this course with an entirely new outlook on Native people in this country. It opens your eyes up to the injustices of colonialism, while also allowing you to imagine a different future. The assignments can be tough, but they allow you to improve your writing and critical thinking.
I really enjoyed this class because of the course content and classroom community! It was definitely a challenge, but probably one of the best classes I have ever taken (but I am just a first–year). If you are worried about your college–level writing skills or that you are not a match, speak with Dr. Ridgway for guidance. They are such an amazing person and I am so honored to have been their student this past semester. This class is so rewarding and I cannot express in words how amazing it has been and how much I loved it.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Trust me
Amazing class if interested in Indigenous history and really delving into all of the complexities, from governmental contradicting policies to salvage tourism, etc.. I would say it might be a bit intimidating if you have little to no prior knowledge or have never taken a seminar class. But overall, it felt like a safe place for meaningful discussions and getting to understand the material.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! I am a senior and this has been my favorite class at Harvard. I think a lot of Native focused courses tend to focus on the distant past and not about Native people today, but this class made an active choice to concentrate on the recent past and present. Putting the majority of the focus on the contemporary was refreshing and helped put emphasis on the importance of paying attention to Indigenous issues. The small group we had all contributed to conversation, and Professor Ridgway was very intentional and helped facilitate really meaningful discussion. It was so nice to learn from a Native professor as a Native student and just feel understood. I loved everything about this course.
