What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Like every course, there are good and bad days. However, the bad days can draggggggggg out and you may feel like some classes were a waste of time showing up to. However, for a mandatory course all these feelings are expected. It is a unique topic, but some of the class may feel more like a film analyzation class than anything else.
When I was reading the Q reports for Expos courses this fall, there seemed to be a popular opinion among Harvard students that Expos is a pain and nothing more than a required course you have to get through to graduate. But in my experience, the Expos experience is invaluable to being prepared to write at a college level. This class was honestly one of my favorites this semester. I've never had the experience of taking a course centered around the topic queerness surrounded by other queer people–– though, of course, don't expect this to be a queer studies class. At the end of the day, it is a class that teaches you to write, and what you will end up writing about will be interpretations of different types of queer media. Dr. Pietras is very helpful during the revision process of your essays, but you need to make sure that you go to draft conferences with a draft that you have put some thought into. Even though the drafts aren't graded, it is difficult to get feedback that will help you turn in a polished final essay if the draft was just a few pages of writing without thought that you don't plan to influence the final essay. Don't be afraid to approach him with questions throughout your writing process, and when he has office hours, grab an appointment, even if you're not totally sure what you will talk about. Expos is one of the few writing–based classes where you have this level of involvement from your instructor throughout the writing process and ABOUT the writing process–– i.e. giving you strategies to improve your writing and streamline the way you write/edit. If you work hard in this course and show your instructor that you care, your writing, including structure, argumentation, and ability to persuade, will improve. Take this Expos!!!
Please do not take it. The lectures are unhelpful, the assignments are meaningless, and the feedback received on essays is not valuable enough to outweigh the negatives of this course.
This is a nice expos course overall. It will cover expos material, and the readings are interesting, specially if you are queer. The professor is not great however. His feedback is not backed up by tangible evidence, but by personal style and topic likings. Overall, it is less classwork than other expos classes since film is relatively easier to analyze, but the professor is not someone you will likely enjoy spending time with, and his one on one meetings are a waste of time.
The topic itself is interesting, however, I wouldn't recommend taking this class if you are a developing writer. You may be better off with an instructor who provides better feedback.
