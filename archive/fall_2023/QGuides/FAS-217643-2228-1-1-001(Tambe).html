This is a great class. Light workload week to week, but make sure you start the project early.
This is a great class, and I highly recommend it! You get to read a lot of cool papers and listen to a lot of interesting talks, including from people in a variety of fields. Getting to carry out your own project is also very rewarding. As long as you've taken some form of intro ML class, you'll be well prepared.
Decent class, some of the guest lectures were hit or miss, but generally good discussion of how to use AI for social impact (and when not to). I liked that the course staff encouraged us to produce a workshop paper as a final deliverable. I think the class could have been better organized, as some assignments were sort of unclear.
A must–take class if you care about AI, and, more importantly, social impact.
I highly recommend this class to any with experience in implementing ML. It teaches you how to actually make ML systems have impact when they are implemented, which is critical in the social domain studied in the course but also key generally for ML practitioners. Note it does not focus on algorithms, but on people and how their lives can be improved by the use of AI. It is predominantly reading–based with a class project that is quite easy to turn into a paper as the project partners that the teaching team gather are great.
Overall, a fairly easy class. Expect lots of paper readings with discussion, with one final coding project.
Amazing class where you have a free pass to learn about technology and the positive effects it can have. Super helpful if you're at all curious about social impact or just the general societal impacts you can have. Definitely a must take in the CS curriculum.
I would definitely not recommend this course. Professor Tambe does very little for the course and its mostly run by his TAs. About half of the semester is wasted by reading papers and presenting them in class. The highlights were guest lectures by professors from Harvard and across the US. Some of these guest lectures were very good but some of them had basically nothing to do with AI 4 Social Impact and seemed pretty random. The main assignment is a final project that is pretty wide open for interpretation and not too stressful. Overall the course is pretty easy and boring, and I don't think I learned anything.
great class with excellent instructors. would recommend if you're looking for a course that is not demanding but where you can learn about social impact in CS.
The class is more of a "you get out of it what you put into it". If you are truly interested in the field, you can plan your project such that you can partner with an existing organisation and even submit to a workshop.
Take this course! Will give you a feel for how you can use technology and relationships to drive impact!
