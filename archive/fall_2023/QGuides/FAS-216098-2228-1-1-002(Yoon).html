don't put the reading off (having to read hundreds of pages in one night will make you feel sick). and don't put the writing off
This course, led by Professor Yoon, serves as a very fun and inclusive training ground for aspiring fiction writers who have perhaps always wanted to work on a long–form fiction project but never knew how to get started. The class offers a great balance between reading the works of rising fiction authors and workshopping your own work with classmates.
If you have any interest in writing a novel, this is an important class to take. From what I can tell, most of the other classes in the creative writing department focus on shorter forms of fiction. In this class, you'll read a lot of very modern literature (ie authors who are very recent/are still publishing) and will also study what the novels being published today look like. Paul is also a very accomplished writer who has a lot of great tips to give to you. Moreover, he provides insanely good feedback on your work and assignments. All in all, it's a great class, and you get out what you put into it.
This is my favorite class of all time. If you have ever wanted to write a book, definitely take this course. You will learn so much, be inspired, find creative things to write about, and will enjoy every moment of it.
take it. it's awesome
TAKE THIS CLASS! The readings are excellent and fun and the discussions are great. Paul is the best teacher you could ask for. You will learn about the craft of novels and get to a point where is feels very possible that you could write a good one, and then you will! for like thirty pages at least. Absurdly absurdly valuable course for anyone interested in the practical craft of novels or a career as a writer.
