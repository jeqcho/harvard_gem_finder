Overall, I had an excellent experience taking HS 100. Professor Csiszar was highly knowledgeable and brought a unique perspective to the course, and he put a lot of effort to make this class fun and interesting, such as by doing demos and experiments in–class. Don't expect this to be like a high school history class, where you just get a brief overview of each event. Instead, the goal of this class is to critically analyze the social contexts of certain scientific discoveries. For instance, rather than just learning the events of the human genome project, you discuss the ethics of patenting DNA and try to understand why companies such as Celera genomics wanted to privatize science. You'll also discuss important issues like whether the government should determine what scientists study. The lectures felt slow at first since we were discussing physicians from thousands of years ago that you probably have never heard of (Ibn Sina), but I found the class to be both engaging and informative, especially towards the end. I highly recommend this course to anyone interested in learning more about the history of science, and I feel that this should be a mandatory class for anyone intending to go into scientific research.
