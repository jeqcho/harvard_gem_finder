take this class if you're interested in infrastructure! not as boring as the title sounds lol and manageable workload
This class is very chill, and it's nice that it's a mixture of undergrad and grad students!
I took ANTHRO 1450 as my first anthropology class, and I would highly recommend it. You learn more than you could have imagined about water and water infrastructure in Boston's waterworld, and going on the 3 field trips (Hobbs Brook, Chestnut Hill Waterworks, and Fresh Pond) is refreshing, exciting, and valuable in terms of furthering your learning). The interviews with the authors of the reading are also a really cool experience.

The course staff is also excellent: Professor Caton is an effective lecturer and has a wealth of knowledge about the topic area. Majid is awesome TF, super accessible and provides helpful feedback . The class is also great for its inter disciplinary nature and the diversity of academic backgrounds of the participants in it.
