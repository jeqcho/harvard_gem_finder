Additionally, there are only 2 large papers, one 2 paragraph assignment and a capstone project which is so much better than 3 big papers and you get to to go the learning lab which is so much fun. Compared to other classes my friends were in, this was a lot less stressful and a lot more engaging. Couldn't recommend Dr Gold as a preceptor more she made Expos (which I was dreading) one of my favourite courses I'm really going to miss our class.
TAKE EXPOS 20 WITH DR. GOLD!!! She is the absolute sweetest professor ever and is SO understanding and kind. She is deeply knowledgable about feminism/poetry/art and is an incredible teacher. You will learn about the various aspects of feminism, which was so cool for me to learn as someone who came in not knowing a lot about feminism. You will also definitely become a better writer by the end of the semester if you follow all the steps that she provides for you. The Gold Guide is a tremendously helpful resource. The readings aren't excessive at all and they're super interesting, which made for amazing class discussions. My best advice for essays is to START EARLY! The more you write, the better feedback she can provide. She also does audio–visual feedback via Loom videos, which is really cool and something that I personally have found to be incredibly helpful. She is super accessible even outside of office hours via Slack. It was also a lot of fun to talk to her about other things outside of Expos/writing during office hours! This class was my favorite of the semester, and I cannot recommend it highly enough.

Hmmm. Overall it was a decent experience. The grading was about what is to be expected of an Expos 20 class I feel like, nothing out of the ordinary. I do feel like this class could be trickier to take if you are a boy, it was strange it almost seemed like the girls were elevated over the girls voices even though it was a predominately male class. Press conferences are important. This may have just been me but I felt like the class dynamic could be kind of cliquey and superficial at times and the extroverted personalities really shined as compared to more introverted people.
Dr. Gold is a very understanding professor. You get two extensions and the second essay is shorter than most expos. There is a capstone final project at the end, but that's very fun! The is a little hard on grading though but provides you with detailed explanations of what you need to improve on. The class discussions are rich! It is a more humanities–based class though so the research project isn't about conducting an experiment but rather on finding a topic you enjoy related to feminism and writing a paper on that, using past research as evidence to back up your claim.
Take this class! Dr. Gold is an exceptional teacher and the topics you discuss are so interesting. Completely changed my view of writing and gave me the tools I needed to write better than I ever have before. Easily my favorite class this semester!
