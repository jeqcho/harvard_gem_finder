This is an excellent introduction to US foreign policy and and American–focused world history of the past 150 years, which is nice because it's world history that's relevant to you but also broadens your mind past what you'd have learned in high school. Highly recommend for anyone who's looking for an intro class in History. The workload is extremely manageable and the course staff are very helpful, which makes it much easier to do well even if you have no experience with a history class. Took as an elective and would highly recommend for anyone interested in American history and understanding the current state of world affairs.
Take this class. You will leave a better global citizen, and the work really isn't that bad. I think the exam structure is a little reductive in that it prioritizes memorization over analysis (at least to get like an A–), but I think that is true of most undergrad gov/hist courses that have finals exams instead of final essays. That being said, even if you kinda skimp on the readings until the week before each exam, you should be fine for an A in the course. I would note that the final being worth 40% is brutal though — really not much room for error there.
Engage in section and you will learn more.
Take this class! All students should take this class for a great understanding of how we got to the present moment!
Out of all classes at Harvard, I learned the most from this course. This course does a great job at explaining why the world order is the way it is and how it's changed over the past 120 years. It is such a good introduction to 20th century affairs, especially from the US perspective. I think it's perfect to take this course and then branch out to specific US foreign policy courses or international history courses. The work load is definitely not too much, and there are only two assignments (other than midterm and final). Professor Manela's lectures are like watching a movie, and the section is so engaging.
A great overview of US history in relation to the concurrent world history. If you learned US history from the point of view that the US was always the virtuous main character, take this course. Some parts of the course may surprise you. The workload is completely manageable. You write two short papers on weekly readings of your choosing.
This was a great class with very comprehensive lectures, great readings, and active section discussion. I have not taken any history class before, but I could learn a lot out of this course. Highly recommended to anyone interested in understanding the historical context of modern international affairs!
This course is perfect if you have an interest in history, but do not want to take a super intense course. While this course is a good amount of work, you are not typically overwhelmed by it. The teaching staff is very well coordinated with one another and they provide helpful insight and resources to allow you to succeed. If you are dedicated to showing up to lecture, you should do well and enjoy this class.
Be sure to do the readings and attend lecture. Don't let yourself get behind on either of these things and you will do fine in the course.
Be prepared to be entertained, and as Professor Manela says "expect the unexpected".
