Take this class if you want an easy class to meet the requirements. There are only 4 papers to write, most being under 5 pages, and the classes were laid back.
This class was pretty nice. The teacher was super friendly and nice. Even though I felt like we lacked guidance here and there, the teacher was very open to questions from us.
Coming into Harvard, many students complain about having to take Expos. Please do NOT listen to these students, they are missing the point of this required course. Simply put, this course has provided me with a foundation in my writing that is essential for future years here. More importantly, what you put in, is what you get out. That being said, by keeping up with assignments with your best foot forward along with engaging in class discussions and one–on–one conferences, you will guarantee your success in the course.
This is an amazing class! Take advantage of this opportunity to explore topics that interest you, all while improving your writing.
Take this Expos! Julia is really sweet and is always available if you have questions about your writing or the process. She provides really thoughtful and considerate feedback and you can tell she is really passionate about teaching and writing. This subject matter is interesting (although I was a bit surprised when we started talking about NFTs) and there is so much freedom as to what you can write about in your last paper! The class discussion is also really thought–provoking and I got so many interesting and helpful tips regarding how to approach academic writing and thinking about arguments.
This is a fantastic expos section! Julia provides extensive useful feedback on drafts, helps answer any questions you have during conferences, and is a fair grader. She's extremely understanding and kind. I learned so much from this class and highly recommend it. You will have to put in time and effort, which is the case with any expos, but this section is very rewarding. I learned a ton from this class and saw substantial improvements in my writing. Readings are also engaging and not terribly time consuming.
This is a good Expos class with an interesting topic. The readings aren't too bad and there's plenty of time in class to understand them. Julia is a great teacher and gives excellent and detailed feedback. But, like any Expos class, be prepared to start early on the essays to avoid getting slammed.
