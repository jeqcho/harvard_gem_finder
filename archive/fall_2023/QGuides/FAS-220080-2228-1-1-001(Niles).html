Do not take a course with Professor Niles. Also, I have heard that he hides the Q reports (could just be rumors), so I am not sure if anyone will even be able to see the honest reviews. Anyway, here goes:
Reasons why this course was unsatisfactory:
1) Professor Niles mumbles. Truly, I and any other students I talked to had tremendous difficulty understanding well over 50% of what he said.
2) Professor Niles would give summaries of every play. Often I left class thinking I had not learned anything that I could have read on Sparknotes.
3) Professor Niles made discussions uncomfortable. He would constantly talk about topics including sexism, sex and sexuality, racism, and homophobia (which are crucial in discussing any text), but he himself would act deeply uncomfortable discussing these topics (ending every sentence in a question, again mumbling, and dodging topics in strange ways while simultaneously bringing them up)
4) Professor Niles was just not a very personable professor. I don't know how else to put it, but I've never had a professor seem like less of a "people–person." I never went to his office hours, instead choosing to go to the TF's office hours, since Adam was tremendously helpful, engaging, and just a pleasant person to talk to.

I am sorry that this review is so negative, but I have been recommending all of my English/humanities friends to avoid Prof Niles' courses at all costs. This was the most disappointing class I have ever taken, and I am a junior at Harvard.
The section component of this class was wonderful. Adam is the best TF I've ever had and made sections a joy to go to. Lectures were also fun because of the mix of group work with the professor's presentation. Learning about Shakespeare's plays as somewhat scandalous/radical works was interesting.
Many of my peers did not engage with the material because they were not interested in Shakespeare. If you don't find Shakespeare engaging and don't make an effort to participate in the class, you will likely be bored. Personally, I found it very fun and looked forward to going to class every day. I think it was an overall positive learning environment, and that everyone definitely took something away.
I did not enjoy this class. I would strongly encourage you not to take a class with this professor. He had nothing original or interesting to say about the texts and I did not enjoy his lectures. You have to really love the subject matter to be able to get through this class.
Participate in lecture!!! Share your thoughts!!! If you think Hamlet is a whiny incel manbaby, you can use that exact wording and you will have more fun doing so.
Even if you aren't sure you are a Shakespeare fan, this class is totally worth taking.
I don't recommend this course if you're interested in the literary study of Shakespeare. There's not very much focus on the form, the wordplay, etc. of the plays, which was disappointing to me. It felt much more like a gender study or cultural study of Shakespeare; I also thought there was excessive focus on adaptations and not enough on the originals.
Take this class if you really like Shakespeare.
