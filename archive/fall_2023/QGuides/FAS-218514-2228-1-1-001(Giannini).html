What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would highly recommend this seminar to every person reading this. The discussions are in–depth but fully manageable for any level of prior knowledge of human rights and legal issues. You can come into this class with no experience in the area and learn so much. Every discussion is a positive environment where even the smallest contribution is valued. The class community among the students is incredibly well fostered and the course materials and assignments are not all readings and discussions (we did simulations, watched videos, had mock debates, and more). I would take a course with Tyler every semester for the rest of my Harvard experience if I could! I learned about how negotiations between companies, governments, communities, and NGOs work and how difficult they are (having participated in a 3 week negotiation simulation in this seminar which was amazing!) and how to center community in worldwide issues with human rights. This class emphasizes what it means to be a part of a community and how all communities can be lifted up around the world through legal and other means. Please take this seminar–– it is not too much work, has a great environment, is an interesting topic, and is well done overall!!
Do the readings ahead of time (ie, not the night before)
This course is going to be an amazing experience and will give you a great community as long as you are genuinely and passionately interested in the course subject which largely centers around business and human rights law! I loved it so much, but make sure this course it for you!
If you have an interest in human rights law, I cannot recommend this course highly enough. Professor Giannini (who goes by Tyler) is an incredible individual who has spent his career doing groundbreaking work in the field. It was such a gift to be able to learn from his first hand experience and the class is very well designed with a mix of case studies, discussions, and a simulation. This is an amazing freshman seminar with a great community of students taking it.
Keep up with the readings as they are essential to class discussions. Moreover, some of the topics discussed in this course can get heavy and emotionally draining. That being said, I would encourage students to not be afraid to lean on other students for general support throughout the course. This is what built the friendships and community that I was able to walk away with after this seminar.
