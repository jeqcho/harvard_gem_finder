First, this is a complex course, with many moving parts and things you have to learn in order to complete the final project. At the end of the class, I feel that I’ve gathered so much knowledge in multiple art mediums. No other course at harvard compares to this one, it truly is unique.
Second, prepare to spend every day at the end of the semester on this course. I spent over 25h / week for the last three weeks before the final project was due.
Third, given that Young is leaving, I don’t know if another professor will continue to teach it. But it’s hard for any professor to be as great as Young, really. I feel so lucky to have been able to take this class.
This course is one of the most practical art classes at Harvard. You get to learn so much about different software and you're really given the freedom to create whatever you want to. Some background in software is definitely helpful (Adobe Suite, Blender, Unity or just general computer savvy–ness) but if you put the work in you can definitely learn them all from scratch!
This course is great! Whether or not you're very familiar with VR/AR going in, the class will show you different perspectives on how to look at extended reality – it doesn't take a sensationalist perspective, and instead teaches you to view it as one of many art mediums. The immersive art you learn about is also not at all limited to VR and AR works, which is awesome. The technical side of this class is good, but had a few hiccups: in theory, you learn unity and blender, but if you actually want to do stuff with either, there will be a lot of self–teaching involved.

For most of the class, there isn't too much work, but the final project is a doozy and will eat up a ton of your time. That said, they provide a ton of support and flexibility. Putting together the final project for this class was easily one of the most fulfilling experiences I've had at Harvard.
My favorite class I've ever taken at Harvard!! Having a CS background was incredibly helpful with the technological part of this class cuz some coding was required to learn Unity. Felt like we had an incredible amount of freedom with the art we could make and loved how much class feedback we got. TF and Professor both incredible & super passionate about XR. 10/10 class wish I could take it every semester
