This course is a good introduction to unsupervised learning. Alex is a wonderful lecturer who makes concepts very clear with interactive discussions and a reasonable pace. This is a solid class, but the title bites off more than is actually chewed; to be honest, a better name for this course would have been something like "Linear Algebra Applications in Machine Learning" "Algorithms for Dimension Reduction" (which, fun fact, is pretty much what the class used to be called long ago). The course's focus was algorithms like PCA, SVD, NMF, ISOMAP, and then some stuff on clustering, with LOTS of linear algebra and a bit of theoretical background (but no proofs), and in some places felt like it was running pretty slow; we spent like the first 3 weeks talking about PCA, which kind of dragged on.

If you have aligned your expectations with that reality, the course is good. Alex lectures well (but writes everything in all caps for some reason, which is a little hard to read; anyway...) and is a pretty accessible guy. Ultimately I enjoyed the class, but I will say that if you choose to take it, get ready to get good at linear algebra.

Stat 185 was a fantastic class. The course focused on unsupervised learning and dimensionality reduction, which are some of the most exciting and cutting–edge topics in statistics. Professor Young is an expert in the field and he does a great job of explaining complex concepts in a way that's easy to understand.

The class was challenging but also a lot of fun. We covered a wide range of topics, from clustering and principal component analysis to manifold learning. The homework assignments were long and time–consuming, but they were also very useful for reinforcing the concepts we learned in class.

The midterm was tough, as most midterms are. But Professor Young was always available to answer questions and provide guidance. And the final term paper was a huge undertaking, but it was also incredibly rewarding. I learned a lot about unsupervised learning and dimensionality reduction, and I'm proud of the paper I wrote.

All in all, I highly recommend taking Stat 185. It's a challenging class, but it's also a lot of fun and you'll learn a ton. Just be prepared to put in some long hours on the homework and the final project. But trust me, it's worth it.
