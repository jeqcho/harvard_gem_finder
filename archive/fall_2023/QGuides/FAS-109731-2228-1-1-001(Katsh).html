What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Gabe is a great instructor and does a great job facilitating discussion.
I highly recommend this class – it's one class that really makes you think!! Great combo of healthcare, politics and philosophy. If you have to take a philosophy course but do not like philosophy I recommend this one because Gabe makes it painless!
This course does a wonderful job surveying the larger theories applied to issues within the purview of medical ethics. The versatility of this course’s themes tended to draw a diverse group of students whose unique interests and perspectives made for class discussions that were equal parts interesting and provocative. I would recommend this course to just about any type of student — whether you’re planning to pursue a career in government, medicine, philosophy, economics, or literally anything else, there is a lot you can learn in this course about how to think critically and purposefully in whatever work you choose to do. Additionally, Gabe has a wide range of knowledge on many subjects (ethics, medical history, con law, you name it), so learning from him is a wonderful experience in itself.
More ethics than politics
This class is fascinating and gives you a lot of freedom to explore topics that you might find interesting in the final paper, regardless of whether your interests are more normative or empirical! Gabe is a fantastic discussion leader and creates a very welcoming environment for discussion and debate over the ethical problems in healthcare.
I really enjoyed this class. As someone on the premedical track, I found this course to be a fulfilling way to gain more perspective on the healthcare field and the political, social, and economic side of medicine.
Take this course. The best gov class I've taken at Harvard hands down. Gabe has had time to refine the course and it really shows. The readings complement discussion well, and are very thoughtfully picked out to reflect current trends as well. You also have enough freedom to pursue the questions you're interested in, but still get to know the basics about ethics / healthcare policy that will supplement your exploration of those questions. Great, great course.
