Good, manageable class. Interesting look at architecture's evolution from classic antiquity through the Gothic era.
If you are interested in architecture, religion, or medieval history you should take this course. The professor has a great pedagogy that involves some level of memorization, which is easy if you do a little bit each week, and not cram the day before the exam. But once you have the memorization down you will really begin to understand how to look at a building. Aside from that you gain a great knowledge of what influences architecture and what its meaning is, the interplay with history, theology, and other factors. Truly an excellent course, and it counts for history concentration credit.
