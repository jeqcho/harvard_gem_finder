Be present in lecture. Indulge in the exceptional, urgent literature that's been selected for you. And always remember that being charitable toward a text — exercising empathy, considering where the author is coming from, suspending your own aesthetic judgments to really understand the text's distinct logic and impulse — does not preclude you from being critical of it. To be both charitable and critical at once is a powerful tool. It makes the literary experience so much more generative and joyful. What started as an evaluation becomes a textual conversation. An evaluation ends the moment you are done writing; a conversation with a text lasts for a lifetime. This is a phenomenal course. The readings presented and the questions posed are rich, exhilarating, sometimes even frustrating. And for good reason, because that is an apt reflection of the humanistic project, of the project of letters. Prof. Smith and Prof. McCarthy have formidable intellects matched only by their humility and compassion ... it's a great course. Enjoy it to its fullest while you can.
