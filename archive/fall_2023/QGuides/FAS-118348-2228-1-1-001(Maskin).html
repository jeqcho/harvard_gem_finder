What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Go to office hours! Such a privilege to be able to engage with Nobel Prize winners who are interested in your thoughts
I loved this course, in general. It was the type of course that sends you home after each session, mind a jumble of complex ideas and thoughts, with plenty to chew on, unpack, and decipher for days to come. The incredible cast of professors was a special experience and one I will remember for quite a while.
The course takes the approach of teaching math and economics at the level of philosophers and philosophy at the level of economics students. This severely limits it. I think it would be much more effective to teach math at the level of mathematicians *and* philosophy at the level of philosophers etc. That may not have been possible with the composition of the class this year, but there certainly enough students at Harvard with a serious base in math, philosophy and economics to fill a seminar, and I wish the class had made more of an effort to find them.
Come prepared to read a great deal and examine your thought process critically!
Enjoyed the course greatly. Some incredibly interesting material with very impressive profs. Quality of discussion in class can vary (presumably depends on who you happen to get on the course). Prof. Sen is certainly no longer at the peak of his powers but still has something to contribute. Nice to be able to pick whatever you want for the term paper, with support from the profs if you ask for it.
It was a delightful experience to take a course with Professors Eric Maskin, Amartya Sen, and Barry Mazur. Each of them had unique teaching styles and areas of expertise that made each class its own fun adventure. This class also attracted students from the college, GSAS, HKS, HLS, and GSE to name but a few. Having this diversity in the types of students made all of the discussions quite fascinating. The one thing I would say is that it is kind of scary having a whole semester be graded by a term paper. It would be nice if the class had some more intermediate assignments just so you could gauge how you are doing. Nevertheless, take this class!!!!
PHIL 248R is a class that encourages you to reflect on the way we think. It's not abstract because you will consider this in the context of certain disciplines and topics, and Professors Sen, Mazur, and Maskin have so many insights to share and discuss. Overall, a class that is very much worth your time.
To get the most out of the class, do the readings and be engaged! Also ask your professors for help, since there is not much guidance for the final paper.
Incredible course taught by three of the brightest minds in the world. Take it if you have the chance.
Be open and ready to explore beyond the words.
