Overall a good class if you're interested in understanding complex analysis and its applications. The lectures weren't super enlightening, but the readings and section notes were great and helped me a lot with the homework. I really enjoyed the material, which was presented with a focus on applications. The class culture was frustrating; it felt like most people didn't really want to be there, and that was definitely reflected in section and OH. But it's a fairly chill class overall and a great way to learn about complex analysis, which is very cool.
I liked this class overall. The material is really interesting and the class isn’t too challenging. The material gets hard towards the end of the semester but it never gets unreasonable. Professor Mahadevan is a good lecturer but he sometimes moves through the material so fast it’s impossible to follow.
Take AM104 if you have to. I'm not sure if it's the easiest way to satisfy the requirement for AM concentrators, but it definitely isn't an easy class. This is especially true if weird "math tricks" don't come easy to you. The take–home final was difficult but largely fair, so if you have to take it I would recommend doing it in a year where Maha is teaching it. He's a great professor, but might be hard to understand if you're (like me) slow at understanding math.
Attend class, do the readings of lecture notes, and do assignments, and you will do great in the class.

Its a fun AM course and you should take it, I think a lot of AM majors do take it so it works out.

This course is great for intuition on courses like ES156 and ES155. Highly would recommend for electrical engineers.
If you need an AM requirement, this is the one to take. The psets are very reasonable and adequate in length, and I was able to start them on Monday and finish them by Wednesday when they were due. Take advantage of OH to work with other students, ask teaching staff questions, and get the pset done. In addition, the midterm and final are very similar to the psets, and the final is a 48–hr take home exam. However, lectures might get a little confusing, and it feels a little intimidating to ask the professor questions during lecture. But the teaching staff are very willing and helpful to explain concepts outside of class and at OH on a more one–on–one basis, so don't hesitate to ask them.
This course is difficult, but if you engage with office hours and the teaching staff it is manageable. There is less of a focus on the applications to art, science, and engineering, but it does become apparent occaissionaly. This is mostly a math course, and if Professor Mahadevan teaches, a fun math course. The homeworks and exams are difficult, but with the necessary time, become do–able and understandable!
Best math class I have taken to date at Harvard. Professor Mahadevan is awesome, and his excitement for the subject matter was contagious. The course was rather large (relative to other APMTH classes), but it should be evident as to why: At best, this class changes the way you see the world, and at worst, you still learn some pretty cool math along the way.

The workload starts off rather light (<3–5 hrs), but the last PSETs require a more significant time commitment (<8–12 hrs). There were no surprises on the midterm; while it was still challenging, we were allowed a study sheet; all of the problems were variations of HW stuff we had seen before.

I don't write reviews like this often, but this course was incredible, and I hope to take another class with Professor Mahadevan if possible.
