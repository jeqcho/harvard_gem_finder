This class was what I wanted it to be – moderately informative, and quite easy. There were a few scattered writing assignments through the course of the class, and two longer essays which took me around 3 and 6 hours. The midterms were pretty easy even if you slept through a lot of class. Would recommend this to anyone who's looking for a fairly straightforward and light Gen Ed course.
Anyone who is passionate about climate change should take this course since it provides you with a valuable and knowledge perspective on how to confront climate denialism, the timescales of climate change, the steps and challenges that come with reaching a net–zero target, and a greater understanding of energy systems that is often absent from many who write climate policy.
I would highly recommend this course if you are passionate about climate change and want to get a better view of what climate change and combating it really entails. The courseload is more than manageable and it really depends on your enthusiasm and willingness to learn a few key concepts to do "well." Professor Schrag's enthusiasm and passion on climate change is also an aspect I would highly recommend – he is very insightful and you really learn a lot from him.
I cannot recommend this class enough for people who do not know a lot but want to understand climate change. This course gives a great overview of the elementary science of how we know climate change is a problem (first 3rd of class) and what the impacts will be (second 3rd of the class). Then, you learn about what the solutions will be and what policies are needed (final 3rd of the class). The class is really low in workload (and stress) but gives you so much information about climate change. Prof. Schrag is extremely dedicated (and a great lecturer) and very nice (ex. offers a huge extra credit opportunity!). If you already know a lot about climate change, this may be basic. But, I found it to be a great start to now be able to explore specific issues more in depth.
