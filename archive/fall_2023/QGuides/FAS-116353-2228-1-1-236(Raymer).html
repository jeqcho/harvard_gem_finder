Great class definitely rank it as highest for your expos selection! Raymer is literally the nicest and more caring professor at Harvard this class is a gem!
This is a great choice for expos. Professor Raymer is super nice, and the material is incredibly interesting. At the end of the day, try to pick an Expos section that sounds interesting to you. If you don't enjoy the material, expos won't be fun, but if you're engaged with it, it can be a very good class! With that said, the material in this course is very interesting and very engaging.

We began by investigating the ethics of gene editing, looking at the issue from a wide variety of perspectives. There are some super interesting readings, for instance a couple that wanted their baby to be blind – there's just so much to unpack in this topic. We then moved into the topic of death, and how to approach the end of life. Did this get a little dark at times? Yes. But it really raised some incredible questions, and I'm glad we dove into this issue. For instance, I'm completely making up the numbers here, but let's say there's a treatment that gives you a 10% chance to survive beyond 5 years, but 90% of people on this treatment die within 6 months quite uncomfortably. Now, if you decide to stop fighting the disease, but instead focus on comfort in the end, you have a 70% chance of living to 1.5 years in hospice care. These aren't real numbers, but the idea behind this can often be quite true. So how should we approach the ends of our lives? Focus on extending them as much as possible, or living a fully as we can? And what is the responsibility of medical practitioners in cases like these? How do you balance giving hope versus being a realist? How should we think about our own deaths, and the dying of our family members. A lot of very interesting things. Finally, we moved on to our research projects. People studied a whole range of topics, from euthanasia to DIY biology to human–animal chimeras to healthcare, and it was a great chance to dive into a very interesting area.

Professor Raymer would have our final papers due one week after we received our feedback from her, which was incredibly helpful – it gave a chance to really take advantage of her suggestions and work them into our papers well. She was very willing to help with questions, and her feedback itself was very useful.

This class was really great – highly recommend!!
This is a great class! It's probably in the middle of the scale of Expos classes in terms of work. It sets you up well for scientific writing if you're interested in that, but it's also fun to engage with the material from a more philosophical standpoint, as that's what this class is.
