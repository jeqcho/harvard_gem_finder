This class is amazing! Professor Lepore takes great interest and care in her students and takes your projects seriously –– you will grow a lot in a environment that pushes you but also ~holds~ you.
If Professor Lepore offers a class like this again, apply to it. She thoughtfully selects course materials, provides useful feedback, and really cares about how you grow as a storyteller. If you're prepared to spend tens of hours cutting and transcribing tape you recorded, writing and rewriting your script, and listening to your own voice, then this class is for you! This class is truly singular. You get out what you put in, so I would advise to take this class a semester where you have a good amount of time and energy to listen, write, research, record, etc. Professor Lepore is amazing and your class will get to know each other well. A bonus is that at the end you have two podcasts to add to your portfolio!
This is an excellent class – cannot recommend enough!! Jill Lepore is a fantastic fantastic professor. She is so warm and generous with her time and advice. She creates a space for workshopping ideas and writing that is fun and inclusive.

The only caveat is that it is a very self–driven course. Most of the class (slightly above 50%) is just workshopping your/peer projects. This means that you need to devote a LOT of time into the class!! You cannot turn up to class without having workshopped your piece. And there is nowhere to hide when a dozen others+prof is picking apart your work. It is obvious if you have not done the work you should have done. Everyone always hugely underestimates how much time creative projects take. Especially in audio form, you often spend many many minutes editing precious seconds, maybe even milliseconds. It takes forever!! If you want to develop your writing and your audio editing skills, you MUST carve out a significant portion of your time for this class. This will be one of your heavy classes!

But you will get a lot out of the class if you put the effort in. You will develop a more discerning ear – you will just be able to hear more of the layers in the sounds of the world. Even beyond gaining a critical appreciation of audio media (e.g. podcasts, radio), I have found that I can hear more layers in the pop songs I listen to and pick up subtler sound details when watching movies or even just pick out bird songs a bit better when walking through the park. It's quite a nice feeling. You also learn to use Adobe Audition, which is a nice practical skill.

tldr fantastic fun class but a LOT a LOT of time commitment
If you like podcasts and radio, TAKE THIS CLASS. It's really quality content you learn. The projects are quite self guided, so go into it expecting to explore any subject you want. But the feedback and collaboration are incredibly helpful. If I had any advise, stay on top of your final project. If you do that, there isn't any point where the work in this class is overwhelming.
Please do take it. It'll transform your writing alongside such an interesting cohort of students and experts. You'll never think about any podcast or newscast the same way again.
