What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course was amazing—Professor Viterna is an extremely brilliant Professor, and the students who enrolled were so passionate and so inspiring. The material we read was pertinent, interesting, oftentimes jarring, and extremely important to know. Our projects and frequent presentations were extremely informative to both craft and learn from, and the class speakers we had were amazing (Michele Goodwin, Kate Kelly). Learning about Professor Viterna's research in El Salvador was amazing, and the chance to meet with women she worked—who were unjustly imprisoned in El Salvador after miscarrying—with was absolutely invaluable. The class became so close–knit and we are hoping to still work together on the issues we discussed—it was truly a one of a kind, life–changing class.
This is the most rewarding class I have taken about Harvard. Not only did I look forward to the incredible discussions we would have in class every week, but the course materials were riveting and the creativity allowed for the final project was greatly appreciated. Above all, the relationships I am leaving this class with – with Professor Viterna and my classmates – will be beyond special to me for years to come. I CANNOT recommend this class enough!
I could not recommend this class more enthusiastically. It was by far my favorite course in college (as a senior). I learned so much about the history of and current state of reproductive justice in the U.S. and internationally. This class community was so special, in large part because of Jocelyn and because of the passionate individuals this class drew. If you have the chance to take it, this course is absolutely phenomenal. It became a unique, safe space to discuss, act, inquire, and learn.
