What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The class is focused on historical methodology and pedagogy. Interesting if you are into that, but not the focus on historical thinking that I thought it would be.
This is an incredible course taught by someone who deeply cares about their students' knowledge and well–being. Professor Chalhoub has a lot of experience working as a historian and helps break down difficult concepts in understandable ways, while also making class very interactive and enjoyable. The texts chosen for the class are difficult and sometimes dense, but I encourage you to stick with them as they will leave a lasting impression on you––especially if you are a history concentrator. If there is any particular historian you have always wanted to study more in–depth, this is also a great way to do so. Professor Chalhoub is an incredible support and someone very approachable who will make your time in this class very memorable.
Take this course! Make it a priority in your schedule. The readings are thought–provoking classic texts in history, and you'll leave the course with a more sophisticated understanding and deeply appreciation for history as a craft. This course will also teach you important skills for a historian, such as how to read and analyze a historical monograph and how to analyze how historians' construct their narratives. This course is one of the best history courses I've taken at Harvard, and I strongly recommend it.
