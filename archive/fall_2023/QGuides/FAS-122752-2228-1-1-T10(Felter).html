This class is amazing! Professor Felter is great. He truly cares about this class and puts a lot of effort into it. It does require a fair amount of time for readings and essays but I would say its definitely worth it. You take away a lot from this class and definitely end up better prepared for harder courses at Harvard. If you are interested in the topic, take it!
Take this course if you're interested in law or thinking about law school! Really cool to apply economic principles and theory to law. It's a decent amount of work, but you really do "get out what you put in" with this one.
This class is fantastic but be prepared to do a lot of work. Professor Felter is amazing and really cares about his students. He tells great stories and has cool insights from his career as a lawyer. There are a LOT of readings though and papers to write almost every week alongside the course wide psets. It definitely is not a light class, especially if you want to get the most out of it and the discussions.
This is a great class! I would say it has been my most challenging yet most rewarding course at Harvard. It requires a lot of reading and analysis for papers, but the discussion is always fruitful and you learn about law in the process. If you have any interest in law school (I do not), I would highly recommend this course. Even though I have no desire to go into law, I really enjoyed this class. The final paper is a bit of a grind though
This is an outstanding class. You will get so much out of it and feel very fulfilled. There's a decent amount of reading and assignments, but this is a tutorial so that's to be expected. Pick this tutorial so you enjoy it at least.
This class takes up a lot of time and is definitely a little overwhelming at first. However, I became more comfortable after learning how to skim the readings faster and having a few points ready for discussion in class. Papers and problem sets are very time consuming but also seem to get a little but better throughout the semester
