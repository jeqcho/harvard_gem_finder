This is the perfect course for economics students who want to apply what they're learning to government policy. Professor Stantcheva is always willing to take questions, explain concepts in more detail, and move slowly when necessary. The assignments and exams can be a bit long but are fair.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!! It is not quite an economics gem, but it's 4 psets, a midterm, and a final. These are the most fair exams I have ever experienced, where you are asked to use concepts from class and problem sets. Stefanie Stantcheva is a wonderful professor who is great to learn from, and the TF, Antonio, is great at explaining math! This course is my favorite undergrad course I've taken so far.

Make sure you have a decent understanding of micro and/or derivatives before you take the course, though, but nothing should be more difficult than 1010a concepts+stuff, just applied in different context. 1011a is helpful, but definitely not required!
This course does a great job building on many important concepts from earlier economics courses.
The psets are no joke. The grading is pretty confusing in this class. I would receive a 70% on a pset and get a comment of "great job." The written feedback on the pset is difficult to read. Overall, the psets and midterm are difficult, but the curve (1/3 As, 1/3 A–s, 1/3 B+s) makes up for that. I found the material an interesting way of furthering the econ principles we learned in 1010a/1010b. I'd recommend if you're fairly confident in calculus and fine with doing "poorly" but getting an ok grade. Not a big time commitment.
Professor Stantcheva is a great lecturer and I learned so much that I'll actually use throughout my life
