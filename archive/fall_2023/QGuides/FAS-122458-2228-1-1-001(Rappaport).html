This class is amazing: being a part of this classroom is a truly irreplaceable experience. The learning you'll gain extends beyond academic knowledge – you'll learn more about your classmates and hopefully realise that you deserve a place in this classroom and college in the process. Nancy is really good at asking questions and provoking discussion, and makes the environment incredibly welcoming and warm.
This was my favorite class. I absolutely recommend it. Professor Rappaport is open, kind, and no matter who you are, she will help you feel welcomed in class. She is very passionate about her work and genuinely cares for her students. Although the class is wonderful intellectually, I highly recommend that you take it just to hear the wisdom from both her and your fellow classmates. I know that the first year is extremely intimidating and choosing classes is stressful but, trust me, you will be OK. You can do this! This class is great I give a 10/10 recommendation. Good luck!
You should definitely take this class! It will give you the opportunity to meet a really interesting group of people and have a broader perspective of what others have to go through every day.
This could be hands–down the best course you take this semester, maybe even the entire year. You'll learn tons of interesting things, meet people with exciting stories, share personal accounts, and build family–knit bonds. This is not a class you take for the credits or an "easy sat grade"; instead, it's a life experience with memories that will last a lifetime.
Take it! Very interesting and professor Rappaport is very good at her job. You will learn a lot without having to worry about grade (she is also very understanding with assignments and deadlines)
Don't hesitate to reach out to Nancy for advice, clarification, or inspiration! Do the reading—they are valuable! And write honestly about your life—she'll enjoy it and it'll benefit you!
Take the seminar! It's a great experience.
MY FAVORITE CLASS OF THE SEMESTER!!! I loved loved loved this seminar and Dr. Rappaport! Dr. Rappaport is so sweet, intelligent, and has a great sense of humor. She facilitates wonderful discussions, and there is SOOO much you can learn from her expertise and knowledge of psychiatry. I loved hearing her stories. She also really cares about what you have to say. She will read your reflections with very careful attention and give back very thoughtful comments. Once I told her about my love for Indian food in a reflection and she bought and gave me chai the next class. I'll never forget that. She even recorded my final presentation and sent it to one of her patients. She'll make each student feel super special and like we belong here.

Also, I loved getting to know all of my classmates–––I prefer having small classes like this because you can make some really good friendships and get to know students at the deeper level. Dr. Rappaport created such a welcoming and friendly environment, and everyone was allowed to be vulnerable with each other. It was so powerful to hear each other's stories. This was also an incredibly chill class. Like no stress at all! I cannot even stress to you how amazing this class was. PLEASE take it the next time it is offered.
Take this class if you are ready to meet other very interesting classmates and have one of the sweetest Professor as someone you can count on. Nancy puts a lot of effort into making this class an open space for us to share our own experiences, and she genuinely cares about us in the Seminar. She is always taking notes and paying attention to what you say.
The workload is not intense, lots of readings but they are all interesting and you get to present something you care about as a final project (the way Nancy has suggestions ready for each project really shows how deeply she gets to meet each of us), and we had lots of people who had the conditions we discussed come to class and talk to us (very interesting perspectives!). The other good thing about this seminar is that you should be done way before reading period! Other seminars have quizzes or papers that can make your finals season even extra stressful. I enjoyed this class, and it serves the purpose of a freshman seminar (not so intense, meeting a professor, small class), but if you want to learn a lot of science/psychology, this is not the best place; you learn some of it, but it is not the focus. You learn more about having the right heart, eye and ears to deal with what makes other people different. And that is valuable.
