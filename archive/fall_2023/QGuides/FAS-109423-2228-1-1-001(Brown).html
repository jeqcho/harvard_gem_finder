What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Pretty relaxed course. Great professor, good discussions.
"African Diaspora in the Americas" is one of the best offered courses at Harvard. The class taught topics that everyone, especially Black students should know. Use the materials, office hours, and readings to learn more and expand your knowledge.
I would recommend taking this class, but only if you're willing to put the work into it. This is a class that has a lot of really interesting, high level concepts, but is not particularly forgiving about not doing the reading. If you don't do the readings, you'll struggle to participate. Especially given the lectures doubled as discussions, you had to participate with Prof. Brown present (who is amazing, but a little intimidating). If you think you can do the work, I recommend, you'd be rewarded for it.
You'll love this class!!
Be prepared to read and discuss difficult material, but do so with the understanding that this is the history of an entire people and their descendants.
