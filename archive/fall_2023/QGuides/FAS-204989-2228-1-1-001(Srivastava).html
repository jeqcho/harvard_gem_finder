Absolute GEM of OEB. Mansi and Stephanie are wonderful lecturers that really care about making a connection with students, and the TFs made section something that I actually looked forward to going to. Having a professor with you while in the lab adds a lot of depth to learning that you don't get much of in other classes. The only downsides are the nature of the course–– with an OEB 10 and NEURO 80 background I found it to be much, much more manageable, but with learning about a new organ system each lecture (both physiology and evolutionary history), you get hit with a lot of new information all at once. However, you have a week to absorb each lecture and the course staff make it very manageable. Much of the learning and absorbing you do is hands–on in lab and section! And a big portion of the weekly sections is just reviewing exam–type questions, so they really make an effort to prepare you for the midterm and final.

This class was a unique experience that you can't really find elsewhere. If you have a good bio background, please take advantage of this cool opportunity!!!!
Take this course! Fantastic OEB mid level. I would recommend making quizlets for the exam in order to study, and studying a week in advance. Also, go to office hours! The professors are amazing people, so it's a good opportunity to ask them questions about anything.
Easily the best premed class I've taken here. Not because it's easy per se – the midterm and final, while totally fair, require a lot of memorization – but because of the teaching team and the structure. Labs are relaxing and only 75 minutes (not the 3 hour nightmare of other premed classes...), only one lecture a week (that's split in 2 so it's really easy to digest), and no psets.
This is an incredible class that I would highly recommend to anyone and everyone! The course staff is great and the content is so interesting! We cover a lot, but somehow the way it's presented isn't overwhelming.
I would say take it but good luck with the exams. You will love the class till you take the exams. Then you will loathe it entirely.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Very interesting and very little work . No psets, only post–labs and some short reading responses. Structure is one lecture and one lab per week, both 75 min. Cannot recommend enough. Great way to avoid other scary pre–med bio courses.
This is a good course, but make sure to take good notes and study since the exams are unreasonably difficult.
This class is fascinating, and the labs are great. Be prepared to study a lot for the exams though, they are tedious and memorization based for the most part. Don't let that deter you though, this is a class you won't regret taking.
This class is great! The small class format lets you get lots of specific feedback, and the labs are so interesting (not labs you'd get to do anywhere else!). You can take this course with minimal biology experience, but I'd STRONGLY recommend not doing that. There are so many biology specifics which you learn quickly in this class, and it would majorly benefit you to have a strong basis in biology before taking this course.
This is an amazing class and you should take it! Dr Pierce offers a biodiversity and vertebrate morphology perspective, while Dr Srivastava teaches how the organs develop during embryonic development. They are both excellent lecturers. The lab activities on Thursdays are really fun and hands–on, involving dissections and interesting experiments. One of my favorite things we got to do was spawn sea urchins. The Professors and TFs are enthusiastic about biology and share this interest with students and through their teaching styles.
It is a very interactive and fun class! Mansi and Stephanie are a joy to learn from. START STUDYING FOR THE MIDTERM EARLY! The more specific you can get with your recall, the better.
