Take this class! It's a wonderful physics class, the material is really interesting, and varies from week to week so you never get bored. Prof. Mundy is a great lecturer, and there's lots of help in office hours and section so you can get through the psets and really understand what's going on.
This is a good class if you are curious about solid state physics. It will teach you all you need to understand most of modern research papers, meaning you can now explore more advanced topics on your own. It is also a good class to make it obvious that all of these physics theories are just models or guesses about how the world works, and there is no real reason the ones' already presented are the best ones someone can make. Hopefully, that inspires you to dare to create a model on your own using what you have learned, and in doing so, spark a sense of joy in discovery.
Take this class with Mundy if you want to learn from the best lecturer in the department! Mundy is by far the most eloquent prof. I've seen in the sciences. The class structure with OHs immediately after lecture make psetting easier. I NEVER had to work on the psets outside of office hours. Super chill, and you learn a lot. what else could you want! The final project also didn't matter at all, making it very chill and a good opportunity to learn whatever you want. easy to balance with other classes.
If you are a senior and want to take a fun elective where you still get to learn and practice your physics muscles, definitely consider this course. The assignments, grading, and pacing of topics are all very fair and you get to see a synthesis of ideas from your past physics courses.

But overall, pretty good class, and a solid recommendation for sure within the department.
Professor Mundy is great and does a great job in this course. The material is very interesting and relevant for anyone aspiring to work in materials science or just pick up a bit more information on how macroscopic material properties are derived from quantum properties. Also, if you take the class pass/fail, your only requirements are to do the problem sets, so you get to skip the midterms and final. Great senior year course for a physics student interested in the topic but not planning to study it further.
This is a great class. Professor Mundy's teaching makes the course engaging and the class environment a lot of fun. The assignments will get you thinking about the material a lot more closely, and you get to show why real–world phenomena are true in these assignments.
Prof. Mundy is a great professor who really takes the time to explain content and make sure her students understand the material and have the opportunity to clarify any areas of uncertainty.
didn't know professors like mundy existed, truly life changing
The lectures are pretty good in general. Prof. Mundy is an experimentalist so she might not be super familiar with some detail but she gave pretty good intuition. Overall, some of the students found the course pretty slow. Some interesting topics like phonons and superconductivity are not introduced in detail. But for most student, I believe the pace is alright. I would recommend it as an intro to the field of condensed matter physics.
TAKE IT IF PROF MUNDY IS TEACHING!!
This course is really well taught and well run, and the content is really interesting! Solid state is a great topic because it combines a lot of the physics you've been learning about (quantum, E&M, etc) and applies it to understand the macroscopic properties of the world. Also, the mathematical/physics tools that you are using change every week, so the work is varied and never gets boring. If you're feeling a little disillusioned with physics after quantum or stat mech and want a course to get you excited about it again, this is probably the best one you could take.
This should be a no–brainer for any physics student, especially if interested on the more applied side. Cool material and great professor. Workload is not too bad and you don't waste time because of the generous office hours. You can understand cool things around you and start speaking the language of solid state physics. Why wouldn't you take it?
Very interesting and fun class on solid state physics! I highly recommend.

However, for people who want to get a more comprehensive and in–depth knowledge about this field this course can feel a bit disorganized and too on the surface at times. Overall good course.
This is a fantastic course, regardless of interest in solid state physics.
