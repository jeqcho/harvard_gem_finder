This course is a bit of a mixed bag. As interesting as the course material is, you may often find yourself coming away from lectures unsure of what you've learned. This is due to the loose way in which lectures are taught, which involve a lot of self–teaching with students at the boards. If you don't have a background in ocean chemistry, it can be difficult to determine on the spot what processes are dominant or how the math of it all works. I wouldn't recommend this course to any non–STEM majors, and I would only recommend it to those who have a prevailing interest in the subject matter such that you can overcome the shortcomings of its lectures.
