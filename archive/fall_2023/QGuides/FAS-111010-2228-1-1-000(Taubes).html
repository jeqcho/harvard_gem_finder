What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class if you have interest in going into higher mathematics courses. It provides a strong foundation and will help with almost any other math class you take as the content is very relevant. The 3 psets per week can be a lot of work, but it is definitely manageable if your schedule is not too crazy. Overall, I highly recommend this course.
If you are taking this course, it is mostly for a requirement so rather than telling you whether you should take the course, I'm going to tell you how to do well. First start p–sets early, like right after class, so you remember the info. Second go to MQC. Most of the CAs took the course and understand its difficulties, and are amazing in helping you with p–sets. Third, do any practice exam they give as they essentially the exact questions they'll give on a test. Fourth, take you p–sets very seriously, unlike a lot of courses, the psets grade is weighted similar to the mid–terms, so p–sets can make up for a bad midterm grade.
Be patient. This is a good class, but you might have to seek help often and there are plenty of resources for that. Go to all of the office hours, MQCs be friends with your CAs and you will do great in the class.
Take this if you need it for higher–level math. It's a very easy course, and the course heads make it easier than it could (should?) normally be. For reference, 80% of the class got an A or A– last year (per the course website). Overall, it felt a lot like high–school math. I wish it was more rigorous.
This class will not be a cakewalk no matter how good at math you are. It's not because the math is hard, but instead because it's repetitive. The psets can be a lot and the occasional one would take over 5 hours to complete. That said, you will learn the math and learn it well. The key is making use of the extra resources page and the videos they provide, which are super helpful for the entire course. Also, make sure your section leader is good. All due respect to my boy Cliff, but he was not the most effective instructor for me. If that's the case, don't be afraid to see if you can switch sections. Amadeus is incredible and does a great job teaching, you'll be set if he's your professor. If you're diligent and don't fall behind on the material, you'll be well prepared for the midterms and the final. Also, don't sleep on the Psets, for us our pset average was worth more than the two midterms combined.
Not as bad as people make it out to be! Pay attention during lectures, PSets shouldn't take you more than 2 hours if you do them at office hours/MQC.
Go to MQC. It is a great place to meet new people and get help on math.
I would be cautious to taking this course and be wary of the amount of time it will take out of your week due to 3 long p–sets per week. I also did not find the material too interesting, but I do think it can be good to take if you want to refresh some calculus.
Be prepared to work! The class will push you, but be sure to talk openly with your TF and CAs. You may really benefit from their help, but you need to take the initiative to get it
