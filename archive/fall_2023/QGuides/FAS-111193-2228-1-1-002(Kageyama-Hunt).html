Try to at least spend 15–20 minutes after the class reviewing the materials after class.
I definitely recommend taking this class if you're interested in Japan / Japanese. This class is a bit more intensive (meets 5x a week), but 1) it is a new language and 2) the drill sections (2x a week) are a lot of fun because you're just practicing speaking with your classmates and teacher.
This was hands down my favorite class of the semester. Kageyama–sensei, Sakomura–sensei, and Nakatani–sensei are all fantastic. You will learn so much (and you can "practice" by watching anime!) This is the BEST way to fulfill the language requirement (but it is so much fun so you might end up like me and decide to take it every year from now on and try to study abroad in Japan (on Harvard's money!))

Some tips:
–if you're bored over the summer before the semester starts, try to learn the Japanese alphabet––hiragana (and katakana) if you have time, you don't need to––they teach you, but knowing it really gives you a leg up, here's a super good website for hiragana: https://www.tofugu.com/japanese/learn–hiragana/ (they also have a katakana one)
–don't get behind in the vocab, in addition to the workbook always take time to memorize the vocab––it is so much harder to learn the grammar and understand lecture if you get behind on the vocab, quizlet if your friend

Absolutely take this course, it was so much fun :)
Be prepared to work hard from the beginning to stay on top of your work.
This class was my favorite class during my freshman fall semester. The work never felt challenging, yet at the same time, it required brainpower. I learned a lot and I plan on continuing to learn Japanese!
