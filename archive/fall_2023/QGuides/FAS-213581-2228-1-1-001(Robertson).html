I think this is a very worthwhile course if you at all interested in the subjects of ethics or ancient China at all. Give yourself ample time for the readings, they can be confusing at first, so one shouldn't rush through.
I would say week/week it is not particularly hard. You can scrape through the reading responses. However, if you are not really interesting in this subject, I would avoid. Yes the class is easy, but the essays are pretty tough because philosophy writing is challenging. I think people take this class because its easy (less than 3 hours of work a week besides the essays) but I don't think I learned 1 piece of information. Seth is so so so nice, but his lectures can be difficult to pay attention to.
If you want to learn some Chinese philosophy, this is a great class! Seth is a super enthusiastic instructor who makes so many great connections between different thinkers, both just Asian as well as between Eastern and Western thinkers. You only have two papers to write: one application paper and one analysis paper. If you put in the work, you'll get an A, and you'll learn a lot.
Really enjoyable class, not too difficult and great way to get introduced to philosophy. Seth is awesome.
This class is super interesting and I think is a bit of a unknown GEM. No prior experience required, the professor is amazing and workload is totally manageable. It may even change the way you live your life.
Take this class! This was my first philosophy class and it was great. While there were times when I was a bit lost as I didn't have any philosophical knowledge/background, Seth does a great job of making difficult concepts easy to understand.
Seth is such a wonderful teacher! He authentically cares about his students and is just all around a good human being. I would reccommend going to OH before your papers just to run through your idea with Seth and/or the TF. Other than that, this course was not super stressful, but was very accessible and interesting! I'd highly recommend.
The class is well taught and well paced but doesn't feel like it pushes a deep level of engagement with the material because it's mostly just a lecture on how to understand the readings. The workload was very manageable and you receive good feedback on your writing.
The reading list and topics are certainly interesting and perhaps justify taking the course. However, the lectures do very little to allow the students to actually engage with material. Any and all learning occurred at home when preparing for assignments.
