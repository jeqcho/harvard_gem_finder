What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Please take this class! You'll learn about the lives and inspirations of wonderful artists, access items that are not on display, and discover connections in Chinese art history that span centuries (you learn way beyond 'modern ink landscape').
If you are not from China or Hong Kong, you will be in the minority. It will be hard to follow at times because it seems that the student make up of the class would consistently be graduate students studying under Professor Wang or students who work at CAM Lab. Perhaps it is because the offerings at Harvard are more limited for those specializing in East Asian Art, but it felt unwelcoming if you do not specialize in the subject matter or have personal connections/prior interest. That being said, I have no regrets taking this class. There was a lot of highly engaging object–based discussion, and Professor Wang has contagious enthusiasm for the class's subject matter and is a joy to study with.
Take this class if you have somewhat of a background in Asian art and if you're interested in learning more about ink paintings through a Chinese lens. There's not much work throughout the semester and what you learn is basically for you to gain more understanding and you won't be tested. You only have class participation, and a final paper + presentation, which can be about something not covered in class.
This is an amazing class. If you have the chance to take it take it!!!
If you're interested in the material, take this class because Professor Wang is extremely knowledgeable and enthusiastic about these artworks, and the Harvard Art Museums' collection of modern ink landscapes is actually fantastic. The class can be a bit disorganized sometimes because there isn't a hard structure to the class, and I wish there were more background material taught at the beginning, but the discussions and exercises we had in the class were really helpful in learning how to analyze art and artist's career.
Take this class! It is a really special class that covers both artists from vastly different points in history, and Professor Wang is extremely knowledgeable. There aren't that many assignments, but it is really helpful to do the readings to engage more during class. This class was also amazing because we got to visit the Art Study Center in HAM and actually view all of the artworks in person every week. You really get to just look at, experience, and just enjoy art. I now feel like I can interpret these kinds of paintings much better in their mental and emotional narrative, which will change the way I think about art (especially Chinese art) when I go to museums. I have a newfound appreciation for landscape painting as a whole.
This is a perfect class and the best choice if you are interested in Chinese history (from the Ming dynasty to the modern days) or east Asian art. The class is so dense in the knowledge that even walking into the viewing room felt already enriching. It made me look forward to every week's Wednesday afternoons. The course compares not only contemporary east Asian art with its ancient models but also western artistic styles. Professor Wang makes sure that everyone is, no matter Ph.D. students or college first–years, on the same page during discussions, and makes very sophisticated knowledge easily comprehensible.
