Prof. Mansfield uses the lectures to convey more about his arrogance, sexism, and elitism than about relevant material. Anyone who is not a rich white man will find themselves, their family, or their friends insulted and maligned. As a woman, it was difficult to endure the snide and sexist examples constantly offered to illustrate points that could easily have been made without insulting anyone. For example, he declared that we all agree that the most recently appointed Supreme Court justice got the job because she is a Black woman while ignoring the fact that being a white male was a requirement of the job for the previous 300 years. He stated as fact that we have reached a "gender neutral" society where gender does not impact the way we live; the fact that white male professors can still disparage women to their faces as part of their professional work, without consequences, suggests that the sexist world is alive and well.

Apart from women, Prof. Mansfield singled out for condescension anyone who not an Ivy–elite. For example, he said that when he had to call someone in to fix his dishwasher, he was struck with the realization that the tradesperson could do that so well but would be flummoxed if asked to converse on religion or politics. This was just an assumption, as Prof. Mansfield is too elite to converse with a tradesperson, but it is obvious is that 50 years in the Harvard cocoon have produced a caricature of a human being who, when he realizes that a less–educated person can do what he cannot, bolsters his self–esteem by reminding himself of his superiority, and recounts the story with zero self–awareness.

Prof. Mansfields outdated notion of academic aristocracy allowed him to use lectures to blithely disparage Harvard workers; he described the dining staff as "less competent" than Harvard students and faculty, and overpaid, while stating that we socialize with them only because we need them. (Ironically, this is exactly how I ended up feeling about Prof. Mansfield).

Future students reading this should know that they have missed out on the precious opportunity of being able to take a class with Prof. Mansfield.
