Take it! Required reading materials were intriguing from start to finish and discussions were lively, useful, and thought–provoking. Plus, Adam was an amazing professor and gave excellent feedback.
I highly recommend this course. The topic of hell is very flexible, and I think anyone can find something that appeals to them that they can analyze within the scope of the class. In–class discussions were very fun and respectful. Adam is an excellent preceptor, he's very understanding and encouraging, and he was capable of discussing a grim topic like hell in a very enjoyable and light–hearted manner. His feedback and conferences were always very helpful and offered constructive feedback. A fair warning however, some readings might involve sensitive topics, and you might have to watch horror movies that are hard to sit through. If that's not an issue, then I think this is an excellent expos20 section to take.
TAKE THIS CLASS. 10/10 would take again
I highly recommend this course. I am honestly a little puzzled reading previous Q guide comments about this course. I found myself thinking about the concepts we discussed in class a lot, such as the nature of suffering, punishment and criminal justice, and the relation of contemporary political issues to hells and underworlds. I was actually randomly assigned this course after missing the expos sectioning preferences deadline, and at first was really worried about the course workload, grading etc. based on rumor and the syllabus. However, in hindsight, I'm very happy I took this course. Professor Scheffler is actually a very reasonable grader – my peers were averaging A– in their papers. He also gives INCREDIBLY detailed and useful feedback on response papers, such that there is absolutely adequate support to do well in your papers, despite the slight scarcity of office hours (sometimes his comments are longer than your entire response paper). He is also excellent in facilitating discussions in class. He has an amazing ability to sum up and evaluate people's contributions super clearly and offer counterarguments when appropriate, which I found helped my understanding of the texts a lot. The workload is totally reasonable as long as you spread out your work throughout the weeks (and not do what I did and spend 30 hours straight in Cabot cramming in the final paper – that was one hell of a katabatic journey XD). I found the papers pretty enjoyable to write about (I wrote about Dante's Inferno, and the films Pan's Labyrinth and Parasite) and was happy we got to study some films, not just texts. Personally, I enjoyed the modern stuff about hell the most (at the beginning we cover some older/classical texts like Homer, Gilgamesh and Milton).
Learn how to read efficiently, and how to take effective notes, to present your ideas in the seminar component of the class.
