What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an absolutely excellent course on early modern philosophy! Prof. Simmons is a fantastic professor who has a phenomenal ability of presenting the material in a very clear and understandable way, and makes you really enjoy working through it (even during those times when you are just completely confused about what is going on in the readings). The course environment was also excellent and, despite not being always easy, it is certainly a course that is worth taking and investing time into.
Take it!! Professor Simmons is one of the best professors in the university, and if you have any interest in the material, you will become a stronger writer, more rigorous thinker, and overall better scholar.
This is a fantastic philosophy class, and you'll read a lot of philosophers you'd probably never read otherwise and rethink how you think about your mind and your body in your everyday life.
This course is really not an easy course. You have weekly writing assignments on top of the readings, and a very open–ended project which can be intimidating. It was for me, as a student who has generally been tasked with responding to a very narrow and specific task in most philosophy papers. That being said, you will definitely learn a lot because of the work, and Dr. Alison Simmons is simply the best teacher there is for this subject. Her knowledge of Early Modern philosophy and Descartes in particular is extensive, and she's incredibly charismatic and excited about the subject material.
Professor Simmons, as always, is a fantastic teacher and knows more about the material she teaches than better than any other professor that I've ever encountered in their respective fields. She was always open to changing the course of discussion in class to whatever her students seemed to be interested in. Weekly assignments were certainly difficult and the semester research paper was a new challenge for me, but I was forced to stretch my writing skills and found them improving throughout the semester. Any course taught by Simmons is a worthwhile one
