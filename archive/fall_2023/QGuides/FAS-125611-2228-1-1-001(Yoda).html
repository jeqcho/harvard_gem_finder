TAKE THIS CLASS!! This is an extremely chill class to take if you are looking to fulfill the aesthetics and culture gened requirement. As someone who is not necessarily an avid watcher of anime, but has watched a few animes in the past, this class is a perfect combination of an interesting topic and relatively painless work. The sections are some of the highlights. The only qualm I had with the course was the mandatory weekly screenings that were 2–hours long, sometimes longer. You don't really need to use your brain for those screenings, but it is a time commitment. You can definitely get by in the course without doing the readings and just reading up on a few for the papers. As someone who would rather do 3 psets than write an essay, the writing component of the class is totally find (just two 4–5 page essays + a podcast). The podcast assignment is actually very fun! Overall, I would highly recommend this course.

This course is an amazing option for anyone with any interest in anime, from veteran viewers to people who have never seen a full episode. The class does include two lectures, a required section, and a weekly two–hour screening, but outside of this class time there's rarely more than a few relatively short readings and an ungraded paragraph or two. The graded assignments (two papers and a podcast) are open–ended and can be as engaging as you choose. While you can easily float by in this class and get a good grade, I would definitely recommend putting effort into finding topics you're curious about and participating in section simply for the experience; the TFs and professor are fantastic! Professor Yoda clearly has a significant amount of enthusiasm for the topic (especially the last unit on Mawaru Penguindrum), and my TF, Shaowen, was absolutely amazing in every way! Regardless of whether you already consider yourself a huge fan of anime or you're considering watching your first episode (or even if your interest lies more in cinematography in general), I would definitely recommend this course!

Great class that really breaks down the culture behind anime! Covers everything from film theory to production processes to fandom. I didn't realize this until near the end of the course, but the selection of viewings is very well thought–out to tackle the question, "How did anime become a global culture?" It's really quite interesting, especially as someone who has just passively consumed anime. The first half is focused on formal analysis (watching things like Japanese WWII propaganda or Astro Boy or Miyazaki to understand how the "look" anime broke away from other cartoons), while the second half is focused on more macro–theory (consumption, fan culture, etc.).

Learned a lot––as a STEM major, this class did push my theory–reading ability. Personally have learned that I'm not a fan of film theory though. The sections were the best parts of the class––everybody is so nice and the TFs help foster a fun community. My biggest gripe with the class is the mandatory attendance, but if you're somebody who goes to class anyway, you're in luck, because participation is like 30% of your grade.
