TAKE THIS CLASS
Take this class – Yiling is amazing!
This class has honestly been one of the highlights of the semester –– if you come into it willing to read papers pretty thoroughly and discuss them, you'll get a lot out of the course. A lot of things definitely flew over my head quite a bit, but you can definitely ask clarification questions and spark discussion in class and shouldn't be shy to. Yiling is really great and helpful and loves discussion, and all the people taking this class will be coming from a variety of backgrounds across CS/econ/math/applied math and a variety of educational backgrounds as well, which is really fascinating. Overall, I'd recommend this class a ton as long as you feel ready for it!
CS236 has been one of my favorite courses I have taken at Harvard! This course is a fantastic way to gain exposure to a field of research in EconCS and get comfortable reading/presenting academic papers in a chill setting. The course isn't too much work, but you get a lot out of it through the paper reading and class discussions. Also, Prof. Yiling Chen is the GOAT! She makes the class very fun and enjoyable and is extremely knowledgeable about the field. Def take CS236 if you have the chance!
This course covered a broad and highly emergent area of research. It was a seminar style course and if you have a general interest in this area and are looking for research topics, there will likely be one or two papers that pique your interest that you'll want to delve deeper into. You won't find everything interesting, and that is okay. But you will find something interesting, which is largely the point of seminar courses. In terms of workload, it's pretty relaxed. 2 assignments that are of normal difficulty and a project where you are really free to explore and create something new.
Great class, super chill, just present one paper during the semester and straightforward problem sets. Yiling is great and really kind. A great reading course to learn about the topic for the semester and dive into some really cool papers. If the topic isn't that interesting to you then you probably shouldn't take this course because it is a lot of paper reading (2 per week) and many times you'll only understand the overall idea rather than the technical details for every paper
For this type of paper–reading class, you get out how much you put into the course. Would recommend taking it if you're genuinely interested in putting in extra time to reflect on the papers. Otherwise, it can be chill but you also don't get much out of it. Background in probability is a must. Familiarity with some concepts in micro is helpful.
I had a great time!
This is a great class: Yiling is a passionate professor who really cares about her students, and is also very organized and accessible. The papers do get pretty technical (this will probably depend on the year's topic) which might make it difficult to stay on track/fully understand what's going on. However, complete understanding of all material is very much not needed for the assignments (including the final project), so this is fine. Paper selection was very interesting and I definitely took a lot away!
Very chill class – Yiling is great!
This class, after the first few weeks, is completely student driven (students rotate presenting papers to the class), so you'll get out what you put into it. The assignments are very fair and not too difficult.
CS 236r can be an amazing course if the topic that year is something that you are interested in. Yiling is a super cool professor, very interesting researcher, and has some incredible insights on important papers. To simply do well in the course the workload is actually decently light but to fully take advantage of the course and really understand all of the papers takes a lot of work (and familiarity / maturity with reading academic papers and doing proofs). If it's a topic you like I would highly recommend this course (they rotate so you can always plan for a year that is a good fit) and either way Yiling is a professor well worth learning from and getting to know.
