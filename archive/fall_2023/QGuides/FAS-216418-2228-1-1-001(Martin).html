This class has pretty interesting lectures and a manageable workload. However, be prepared to really read the textbook and do a lot of googling to figure out the homework assignments. Also be prepared to give a presentation or mini presentation every other week. However, they are usually pretty quick to make and low stress.
The profesor will be very engaging and energy this stud and be prepared to participate by asking questions and doing strong presentations. The textbook is dense and annoying but you can get away with just skimming it.
This class is very self–guided through weekly textbook chapter readings and student presentations. If that works for you, then great! If not, maybe this isn't the class for you. Each class is 50 minutes of lecture by Prof. Martin, followed by 25 minutes of student presentations. The lectures are pretty interesting because Prof. Martin does a great job with teaching and keeps it engaging, but at least half of the class was usually zoned out during the class presentations and they don't really amount to anything unless you're the one presenting. Section was good and crucial for doing the psets, as we basically just did parallels of the pset questions but with different values. It is, therefore, very easy to do well in this class. The other homework was in the form of reading a chapter of the textbook and submitting a discussion post about it before the class in which we were going to learn about that chapter's content. The textbook was dense and hard to digest, and I don't think it actually taught me anything at all. The lectures are based on the textbook chapters, however, and I definitely did learn during lecture. Also, Scot is enthusiastic and accessible, and he's able to answer any question you have for him. Finally, I was really not a fan of the final project. Basically all that we could do was just a literature review of an environmental topic, but we were then expected to have some sort of quantitative analysis and propose a solution to "save the world."
Its an alright class, not too challenging but not the most interesting. Not very much work or class time which is nice

Great class to take if you like chemistry!! Very applicable to real–world problems. You will struggle if you don't like chemistry or have a good background in it, but even so, it is still a doable class if you need a concentration requirement.
