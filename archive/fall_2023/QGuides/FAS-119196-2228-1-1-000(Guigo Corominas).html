Math 21A is a requirement for a lot of concentrations and I think overall it was a great course for teaching multi. For context, I took multivariable calculus in high school, but Math 21A is much more conceptual and might even be more conceptual than it is computational. The course is great in that I think it has a lot of built–in features to help you (MQC, Office Hours, Pset drops, fair grade distributions, exam retakes for Mini Exam). Even if you've done multi before, 21A won't be hard but I won't say that you'll coast by just because of the amount of psets there are and the conceptual emphasis. TLDR: Go to MQC

If you are debating between Math 18A or 19A take 21A. 19a is hard for no reason and there's a lot more support in 21A than these two classes. Additionally, 18A and 19A don't have MQC which makes doing psets so much worse. People in 22A seemed to be more miserable (endless midterms and significantly harder material with no MQC) so if you are taking 22A for the clout or because you think 21A is just computational, I'd say rethink.

Problem Sets – There are 3 Psets a week which is a lot (each pset takes 1.5–2 hours). My only advice is to go to MQC because it will cut the time you spend on Psets in half while helping you absorb the material 2x as much (and you can probably get a 100 on every pset). There is no reason to sit by yourself and struggle with your Pset. Just start and finish your upset in MQC. Also look at the problem set solutions from time to time because you can learn what wording they want you to use. Also, look through your comments when you get a grade back because sometimes they will misgrade your pset.

Section – I haven't heard of anyone with a particularly bad experience with their section leader. My section leader was amazing (Roderic) and I've also heard Janet's section is great too so try switching into one of those if you are not happy. It is flipped classroom and they release worksheet solutions at the end of the day which can be tough. Try and keep a document somewhere with just the essentials (formulas, properties, etc) throughout the semester because when it comes to prepping for exams it can be a pain to have to scroll through all of your worksheets and psets to find the core information. Also, there are no attendance drops for section which personally was weird since you meet 3x a week (just send them an email before if you need to miss for any reason and if it's medical then they'll understand)

Workshop – Workshop is useless. I don't know why they introduced it this year but just do work for another class and listen when they give you the answers to the workshop problem you need to rewrite for your upset.

Exams – The practice exams are significantly harder than the actual exam, which in my opinion was very helpful. The actual exams are very straightforward and very fair representations of the material you need to know. If you study and do all the practice exams you will be fine. Also, you can retake the Mini Exam for full credit and the grade thankfully works in your favor with how they distribute the weight of the exams.
If you work hard and study, Math 21a should be a manageable course. However, I would recommend taking Math 22 if you learn better from lectures than from small groups. Also, make sure to take all of the Practice Exams and review the questions you got wrong in office hours. It is very helpful when studying for the exams.
This is a great course, especially if you enjoy small class sizes over larger lectures. The class moves very quickly and you will learn a lot. Keep in mind that there are 3 psets a week, so there is a lot of work that comes with this class. I really enjoyed this class and would recommend it if you need it as a requirement or just want to learned about multi and its connections to real problems (in some instances).
