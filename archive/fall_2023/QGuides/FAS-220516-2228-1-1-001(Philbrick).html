What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course was unlike any other Latin course I've taken because of its approach to a single figure rather than an author or period and I really appreciated the alternative approach. The course also accommodated different skill levels with Latin. The readings are pretty doable staying between 40 and 130 lines depending on the contents of the lines and the author, but sometimes assignments pile up with readings as well as assignments like exam corrections or papers being due at the same time.
This course is a significant amount of work but it is rewarding if you are interested in the Medea myth. I would note that this course veers more in the direction of a Latin "grammar" course than a Latin "literature" course, as we didn't devote much time to the literary aspects of our texts as I probably would have liked. Instead, it feels like a lot of grammar nitpicking, which is fine, but feels a little arduous and sometimes unnecessary. You will get better at Latin because of this course, but you might also frequently be frustrated by the level of perfection and work that it demands.
Medea provides an interesting subject, although keep in mind that this class is much more of a literature class than a mythology class. The class itself is very reasonable with fair expectations. Classes are mostly discussion/translation–oriented. Besides a couple short papers, the bulk of out–of–class assignments are passage translations, typically 40–100 lines between classes. Dr. Philbrick is very approachable and has a deep understanding of Latin literature and language. If you enjoy classics, are looking for a somewhat non–traditional reading list, and find textual analysis/comparison interesting, this is an applicable class.
This class is not a walk in the park, but it is definitely one of the easier 100–level course I have ever taken. If your grammar is shaky or you are looking for a course to take straight out of the intro sequence, this course is for you. If not, you might find the course a little too easy other than learning a new meter for tragedy.
This class is really great and if you have any interest in storytelling or different versions of mythological figures, this is definitely the course for you. Also, it is so rare to see an unapologetically evil woman, like Medea, so this course really gives a great perspective on that and Dr. Philbrick is an excellent teacher – she is very nice and more than willing to give extensions etc with proper warning, but also very honest academically.
