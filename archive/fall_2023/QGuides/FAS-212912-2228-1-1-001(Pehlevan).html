Be prepared to work a lot. Homework is hard and you should go to office hours.
Worth taking! From past reviews I was worried that the problem sets were going to be too much work, but I actually enjoyed them a lot. Spending time on them definitely helped solidify understanding of the course material. There were some parts of the class related to dynamical systems / optimization that I didn't have a lot of prior experience with, but I was able to catch up on my own (the optional readings were very helpful). Overall this course is very up–to–date with recent research in neural computation, much of it done by Prof. Pehlevan's group, and provides a very good overview of relevant topics such as neural network generalization, computation via dynamics, and some other unsupervised learning mechanisms such as Hebbian learning in various forms and also Hopfield networks.
I recommend this class if you have an interest in thinking about or pursuing research in computational neuroscience and neural models of representation and learning in the context of machine learning. The instructor is accessible and a good lecturer. It's not very difficult, but the problem sets are long and will probably take 3 or so days (~10–15 hours) to complete.

Hi –– I'm an senior undergraduate student in the AB/SM program. I took this class for my Master's requirement for Applied Math, and it was not very enjoyable. The first two weeks were good –– and after that not so much. Lectures are boring and unless you're seriously interested in neuroscience, the content and professor are not super engaging. The homeworks are biweekly, and pretty hard –– I consistently spent 20+ hours on them and needed to go to OH to solve the problems (some of them required math tricks using Fourier transforms which I would not have gotten otherwise), even with a pset group. If you're looking for 200–level requirements, would recommend looking elsewhere first and take this class really only if you have to without other alternatives.
