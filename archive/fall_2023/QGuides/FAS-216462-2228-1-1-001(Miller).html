What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class!! I don't know if the structure will stay the same as an engineering class, but it was SO fun and honestly a great time every week!!
A great, low–stress class. For the engineering major this will be a walk in the park, maybe a teeny bit less for everyone else. If you don't know Python make friends with someone who does!
This iteration of the course was very much a work–in–progress, so bear that in mind when reading these responses as the next iteration will likely look fairly different.
I came into the course thinking I'd learn the physics of sports as limited to physical equipment, say how a ball moves through the air. The course actually didn't cover that much physics in class, relying on take–home readings/assignments or self–directed research to cover content (make sure to do this work if you want to learn the content!). Rather, much of the first half of the semester was dedicated to figuring out the idiosyncrasies of the motion sensor provided and how to analyze the data in Python. Also, there was a focus on biomechanics that I wasn't quite prepared for.
The project for the second half of the semester was interesting, but be sure to pick good teammates and a topic you're actually interested in so you still have motivation to clean messy data and analyze it to get useful results (on par for any project–based course though). You can really take the project any direction you'd like (any sport/physical activity, any focus including physics of equipment and biomechanics, etc) as long as you're prepared to put in the time and effort to see it through to completion.
Take this course if you are interested in data analysis, research, etc. It is a great way to conduct your own project under supervision.
