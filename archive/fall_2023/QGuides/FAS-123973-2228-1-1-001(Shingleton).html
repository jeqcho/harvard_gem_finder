Psychopathology is a really interesting topic, so it's a shame that this class fell flat. Professor Shingleton was very kind and always answered questions during class, but I found the class to be uneventful. First, the lectures were essentially a high–level summary of the textbook. I rarely felt that I learned new information during lecture that I hadn't already read in the textbook. In Psych 1, I felt that the textbook served as interesting background information for the more in–depth lecture material (such as looking at studies that were done), but every lecture in Psych 18 was the exact same format. The course felt like being forced to memorize the DSM criteria for every mental illness, without very much more in–depth. Second, I felt the assignments didn't push us to engage further with the material. Before every exam, there was a "core concepts" sheet that was essentially a list of every topic we learned in the class. To study for exams, I had to memorize every slide of the lectures. The final paper was also a regurgitation of lecture material basic questions with no additional outside sources needed. My favorite part of this course was reading the memoirs written by those with mental illnesses, but even then we didn't discuss the books in section and just had to write a basic summary of the book (almost like a book report you'd write in middle school). Third, the sheer quantity of assignments in this class was beyond any that I've seen in other courses. We had two midterm papers, two midterm exams, a final exam, and a final paper. By the end of the class, even my TF was apologizing for how much was given to us. Fourth, section didn't really add much to my already surface level understanding of psychopathology. Three of our sections were spent as a "study hall" for the exams. Fifth, the course policies lacked any understanding for students who had to miss a section or miss a lecture (all lectures were recorded, but you needed special permission to access them until they were released a week before the exams!) To have to send in documentation to get a lecture recording is beyond me. Psych 18 was an exercise in brute memorization and regurgitating exactly what was said during every lecture. I would NOT recommend this course.

Lectures: Go to lectures!! They are one of the key factors that helped me pass my exams and do well in the class. Take notes while Dr. Shingleton speaks. She posts the lecture slides before class, which is great if you want to annotate over them! If you need to miss for any reason, she also makes lecture recordings available prior to exams. Don't rely on these only please, as there will be too much content to review within the last couple of days if you missed several lectures.
Memoirs: The memoirs aren't too dense, and the papers are possible to write even on a time crunch. Don't be afraid to request an extension from your TF if necessary! Before you begin reading, make sure to see what questions you need to answer about the memoir (this way, you can highlight or annotate the key quotes that will best serve as evidence or support for your paper).
Term paper: It was very helpful writing the term paper in preparation for the final exam. It allowed me to review key concepts before testing, which in a way helped me study. It's also not long (2–4 pages), so it shouldn't take longer than the memoir paper!
Section: A great place for relieving stress and consolidating the material that we went over throughout the week. There are some required readings on a couple of the weeks, but the papers are really interesting to read and reinforce discussion about the real–life scenarios.
Exams: The midterms are not too overwhelming. The first one is on the fourth week of the semester, which may feel very soon, but I am grateful because it can before the huge flow of my other midterms the following week. It also prevents there from being too much material on a single midterm. There are both multiple choice and short answers. For preparation, I recommend going through the glossary for each of the key concepts for the midterm and going over lecture slides and notes. The same applies for the exam, and if you prepared well for Midterms I and II, the exam content should not be too bad!
