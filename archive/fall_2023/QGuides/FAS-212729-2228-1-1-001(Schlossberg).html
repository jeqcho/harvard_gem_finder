If you are queer (or an ally I guess), and interesting in reading and analyzing stories of queer people that are well written and interesting, then this class is a great choice for you.
This course will be extremely worth it if you are willing to put in the work and if you are genuinely very passionate about the subject matter. The class is a decent amount of work, especially for a pass/fail classâ€”most of the semester is a book per week. The books are very interesting and emotionally impactful, but you will need to spend time reading all of the books (but they are relatively easy to get through). Aside from the reading, there isn't much work for the class (it's mostly reading with very few essays).

The discussion is relatively good, but it tends to veer towards very general, big–picture discussion, rather than specific aspects of the books.
Take this course if you have an interest in studying queer and trans literature that is so often overlooked in conventional courses!!
Linda is an amazing teacher who is extremely passionate about her course. In my opinion, this course most definitely is a hidden gem. I would strongly recommend taking this class if you like book seminars and are interested in queer topics.
This course is really great if you want a chill freshman seminar and are interested in the topic. Not too busy of a workload and you meet a lot of cool people.
You should take this class if you are interested in the WGS department, and want an introduction to it. Discussion was usually fascinating. However, the conversation did sometimes lull. Readings are fascinating, but be prepared to dedicate some time to them.
If you come from an area where there is largely no queer representation, you should absolutely take this class.
