Comments
Really fantastic class. A must–take if you have an interest in diving into a fascinating niche of population genetics. John is both incredibly knowledgeable and super kind, always encouraging questions and letting people in the class learn from each other. That being said, you'll get out more of this class if you a.) have some prior statistics (i.e. STAT 110) background and b.) prior experience in population genetics plus a desire to put time into this class. But a real favorite of mine this semester.
This is a very useful course for students interested in population genetics or coalescent theory. The course has a relatively light workload, but it is effectively designed and you will still likely learn a lot.
Take it if it is relevant to your research, and/or if you are more interested in learning than in getting a good grade. And absolutely use your peers as resources. You must do work outside of this class to understand it.
I would recommend future students to take pictures of the equations John puts up on the board, as he will ask questions on the problem sets which are directly related to these concepts
Professor Wakeley is fantastic and knows his stuff really well. This course won't overload you with work and will provide a strong foundation for reading and working in modern coalescent theory. Take it if you know you're going to work in population genetics, or if you just want to test your interest in it.
A very fun class overall, definitely do it after stat 110 if you want to be able to focus on the bio components. John cares a lot about how individual students are keeping up with the content, and there's always support even as a one man operation
