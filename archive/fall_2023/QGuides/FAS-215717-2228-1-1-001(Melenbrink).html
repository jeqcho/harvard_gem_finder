It is a great, fun, practical class that you will learn a lot from! If you want to learn engineering or just thought of getting into it this course is perfect for you!
This is an amazing course and I cannot recommend it highly enough. However, I do recommend you take it during a semester when you aren't as busy. I took this as my fifth class during a very busy semester and it was not my best idea. Especially toward the end of the course (last 4–6 weeks), the assignments get bigger/harder (at least for me, not necessarily for everyone), and I was so stressed about being able to get things done and semi–functioning by their due dates. However, the course staff is super flexible, understanding, kind, and supportive. When you inevitably run into issues trying to get something to work or figure out how to do something, you should definitely turn to Google, YouTube, and other peoples' projects and websites first. Then, definitely talk to/message the TFs or Nathan, who are so helpful. It's also very useful to chat with them about your plans for projects so they can give you their recommendations and expertise. Finally, be sure to start your documentation early: ideally, you'd do it as you go. Creating thorough documentation is vital for succeeding in the course (and also just generally useful).
