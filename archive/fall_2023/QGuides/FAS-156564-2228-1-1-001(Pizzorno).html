What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is great for humanities concentrators looking for an introduction to computational methods to do their research. You learn a lot and get a lot of freedom to do a project that you care about/are interested in.
This is a great and low–pressure environment to learn really nice fundamental analysis skills applicable to a wide range of different types of data. It requires a lot of self–motivation to be successful with your project (there's no hand–holding / no one will make you do the work), but Professor Pizzorno is extremely accessible and helpful.
Would encourage brushing up on basic Python before this course—definitely helpful if you're looking to delve into something analysis or programming–intensive! Great course—really allows you to dig deep into an area of interest. There's such a wide range of projects from student to student, which I found to be very exciting.
