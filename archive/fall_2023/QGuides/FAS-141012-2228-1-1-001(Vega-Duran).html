This is by far one of the best classes offered at Harvard! Raquel is an amazing professor who is very entertaining and makes the material easy to learn. The movies are very interesting and the workload is balanced, without any tight deadlines. I highly recommend you take this class if you want to learn a lot, but also have an easy class that doesn't take too much time out of your week.
Profesora Vega–Duran is absolutely incredible. Her depth of knowledge about both the history of film and the history of Spain makes her the perfect person to teach this course. Her enthusiasm is contagious and she really makes you love the films the course teaches. If you get the opportunity, take this class!
This is an amazing class! Professor Vega–Duran is incredible. She clearly cares so much about the material and has selected so many great movies for us to watch. She helps explain film concepts and Spanish history very well and is so welcoming and friendly. I would recommend to anyone with any interest in film, Spanish history, or Spanish culture! You don't need to know anything about film to take this class, and you will learn so much.
This class will have you learning so much. You will cry, you will laugh, you will be astonished. You will also meet amazing people.
I highly recommend this class. It's rare that I feel really good about a class after I've finished it, but I can say that after having turned in my final paper before I wrote this, I'm super grateful that I got a chance to take this class. It's a tough call between this class and Stories from the End of the World, but I think that this class might be might favorite that I've taken at Harvard (at the very least, this class and Stories are far ahead of my other classes in terms of favorites). I learned a ton in this class and felt like I was having fun doing it, Raquel does a great job in making the work not feel onerous and instead makes it feel like you're just talking about what you liked/thought was cool about a movie you watched for class. The movies she chose are also super good, so even if you don't take it, I'd recommend looking at the syllabus and watching some of them (my top recs are Mujeres al borde de un ataque de nervios, El orfanato, The Others, El espíritu de la colmena and El laberinto del fauno, but honestly all of them were good).
If you have any interest in film, or even if you don't, take this class! You will learn so much about movies while also diving into Spanish history. Profesor Vega–Duran is super welcoming and willing to help in any way!
I loved this class!! TAKE IT IF YOU CAN! We watched so many movies and got to talk about them in class. The professor is so passionate about everything she teaches and is one of the most accommodating and supportive teachers I have ever had. I took two classes with her and this was my second one and she really showed how much she can master the topic of Spanish film. I enjoyed this class so much.
