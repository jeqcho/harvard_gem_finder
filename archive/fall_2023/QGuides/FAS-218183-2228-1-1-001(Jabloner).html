The readings in this course are quite challenging, but the conversations were so fruitful, and it was a very unique and awesome experience to be in class with undergrads and grad students from such a wide variety of backgrounds. Highly recommend!
This is a good introductory course to feminist science studies for students coming from diverse backgrounds and contexts.
Take this class!! I was a little daunted by the fact that I had very little background in theory, but Professor Jabloner is wonderful at breaking down the readings and giving students space to be uncertain and ask questions. I always looked forward to discussions, and I learned so much about feminist science theory that has encouraged me to look at my history of science work in new ways (as challenging as some of the readings were). The environment she fosters is so inclusive and low–pressure, and if you're willing to put in the work to understand the readings––or if you're interested in the many politically and socially relevant intersections of feminism, science, technology, and medicine––you will get so much out of this course.
SUCH an amazing course, completely changed the way I approach the sciences and disciplines in academia in general. Professor Jabloner is wonderful, this class is a must–take!

I learned so much from this course in the well–chosen readings, and appreciated being in class with a mix of undergraduate and graduate student. Anna is a great, kind, thoughtful, knowledgeable instructor who made the experience great and I feel like I learned so much from this course. For future students, I would remain open to the experience and allow for your thinking to be challenged.
