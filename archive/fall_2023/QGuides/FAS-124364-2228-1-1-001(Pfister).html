Course does not teach React so make sure you take time to do that on your own if you want to learn more up to date techniques. Grading is sometimes arbitrary between different staff members, and office hours are built into very inconvenient times during the week with some staffers clearly more equipped to handle student questions than others, so make sure you go to all of them early in the semester and figure out which ones are useful. Sometimes the instructions for homework and assignments are outdated and make it impossible to solve the task at hand, so get to know people who have a CS background and work with them on labs to succeed in the course. Start early on the final assignment and make sure you are aware of the course's deadlines. This is the only class I had that made homework due on university holidays and was generally fairly reluctant to accommodate student needs unless you have AEO exceptions AND extensive physician documentation on top of that (one wasn't enough) so just be aware.
