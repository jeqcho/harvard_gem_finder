I recommend picking this Expos class if you are at least a bit interested in the topic of animal welfare and the food industry. Also, if you are like me and reading and writing is not your thing, I also recommend choosing this class, as the readings are not as extensive as other classes, and there are only two papers. This class is also good for people concentrating in the political sciences, as it teaches how to write political science papers.
This is an awesome class! I think this expos genuinely changed my view of animal agriculture and ethics and encouraged me to tell others about it as well. The capstone project was a really cool assignment, but it was a ton of work – if you're more of a person who wants a traditional expos with papers/writing assignments, this may not be the class for you. Also, it's very open ended and some of the assignment expectations are not as structured, so if you feel like that's something you really need, you may want to try a different expos. The professor is great and super passionate about the topic!
Literally the best expos you can take. I hear horror stories all the time about other expos courses, but this one is worth placing as your first. Dr. Saha is extremely nice and passionate about the subject. She gives out extensions generously and grades nicely. Most weeks, I find myself having little to do for the course. The two writing assignments and one video assignment take time, but are not a burden.

TAKE THIS EXPOS AND PUT IT AS YOUR NUMBER ONE!!! This is a GEM and I can rave about it on and on. 1) Dr. Saha is an amazing professor. She guides you through EVERY step of the writing process, and her constructive criticism is extremely helpful. I can confidently say that she has made me a better writer. Also, section discussions are interesting and she knows how to keep the conversation from lulling. 2) The material is so interesting!! I'm pre–med and mainly had a general interest/curiosity in the topic because I didn't really know much about it at all, but I left the class with a newfound passion for the subject and I turned out to be genuinely interested in the material. 2) The workload is relatively light, especially when compared to other Expos classes. Throughout the entire course, we have read mainly a handful of excerpts, with all of them being less than twenty pages each and also were very thought–provoking. The most reading you'll do is probably when researching sources for your essays.
