Your experience with the class is very dependent on how well your learning style meshes with the teaching style of whoever teaches the class. Since the professor who teaches the class regularly changes from year to year, this is something to keep in mind.
This is a great course! I took this course as an elective (I am a STEM concentrator) because I wanted to learn more about history. It is a well–taught and fun introduction to the classical world. It's not too hard, and fit well into my schedule full of pset and lab classes. I would suggest taking notes in class and keeping up with the textbook so you don't have to cram the 3 days before the final.

However, the class was taught with such depth and insightfulness that I recognized that I am not passionate enough about the subject for it to be my concentration/secondary. I loved the sections, readings, and discussions. The lectures were a bit mundane, but that might have just been my lack of interest at times. However, this was a very good class! I would recommend.
This class is comprehensive and requires a good amount of work and attention, but it's worth taking if you're interested in Greek history and want an introduction to classics that doesn't involve knowledge of Latin or Greek. Still, it was a fair amount of work with a midterm exam and midterm paper as well as a long final research paper and final exam, so know what you're getting into before you decide to take the course. For classics concentrators who are required to take this course, enjoy it! Lectures can drag at times but there are some really great moments you're learning about.
