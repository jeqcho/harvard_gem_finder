I really enjoyed this class. Professor Elgin is extremely knowledgeable and super nice. It's a great way to kick off your Harvard experience.
I would recommend this course if you are interested in philosophy and want to try it out in a non–graded setting. However, the classes do feel incredibly long, and there is a moderate amount of work.
Prepare to have your mind blown. You will likely question the very essence of your existence on day one, but save the deep thinking for class so that you'll actually be able to operate on a day–to–day basis. This is pass–fail, so just submit your stuff and do the readings (or at least know the main ideas so you'll be able to participate in class discussion–––trust me, it's more enjoyable that way).
I honestly didn't enjoy this class very much. Coming into Harvard, people told me to take a freshman seminar outside of my area of interest and that it would change my life, but I was very disappointed. Discussions didn't feel very lively – more like a lecture interspersed with student questions – and I found myself pretty disengaged. I wouldn't recommend this class.
HIGHLY HIGHLY RECOMMENDED FOR THE PHILOSOPHICALLY CURIOUS/CONFUSED.
You don't need any background. Just be interested in philosophy or the skeptical argument or general doubt or some existentialism. If you're prone to debate or you like discussion, all the better, it will serve you well. And if you don't have background or have not previously thought about this all that much, be prepared to be confused about life forever.
It will get to be a lot of decently heavy philosophy text readings, but as long as you try and understand the general idea, you will get a LOT from discussions in class. Learn from your peers, and open your mind to considering some crazy situations. There will be a lot of hypotheticals. Assignments are not too bad at all– you can put in as much or as little as you want, into both assignments and the class as a whole.
Professor Elgin knows exactly what she is doing and how to guide you to and through some pretty heavy non–conclusions/doubts. She provides great feedback and leads discussion well. She also somehow knows something about everything and will connect skepticism and philosophy to, like, every other subject all at the same time. If you are interested, you might emerge forever confused with life, but in a really good way. Loved this course a lot.
I encourage students who have no plans in concentrating philosophy, or plan on solely focusing on STEM courses, to take this class.
