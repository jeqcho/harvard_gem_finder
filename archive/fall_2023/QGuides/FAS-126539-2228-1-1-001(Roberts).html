What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great course! The lectures and readings were super interesting, the environment was quite chill, and the class discussions were always great. Loved all my classmates and Isabel, definitely take this class!
This course is great. It's not super difficult and gives students an intro to the methods and types of art and architecture. The seminar format is also awesome because the class becomes like a tight–knit community over the course of the semester.
Ultimately, this is a required class if you're an HAA concentrator, so if you're not a huge history of art person you will probably not like this class. This course varies on a week to week basis because of the guest lecturers, and it really depends if you're interested in the guest lecturer's work that affects if you like this course. Personally, I found the majority of guest lecturers very boring –– though some really piqued my interest –– so again it's more of a personal thing. Assignments are not hard at all –– the class is just a bit dull and a requirement you have to take.
I looked forward to coming to this class every week. Isabel McWilliams was a wonderful, reliable TF. It's like HUM 20 but even more intimate and personal!!

This course had such wonderful breadth (as promised)!! The response paper prompts challenged us to engage with the weekly readings/material but allowed us to explore our personal interests. Our time at the art museum was very special and kept us on our toes/kept things from losing any energy on a Wednesday afternoon. We got to meet amazing professors and get a comprehensive taste of the HAA department. The TF was definitely a strength of the course –– accessible, energetic, encouraging, and willing to share her own interests and expertise!
