What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would strongly recommend taking this class for an overview of African history and politics. This course provides a depth of knowledge on many different facets of national identity. The central motif of African solutions to African politics was evident in the structure of course materials and discussions. Though a heavy reading load, the course assignments are quite balanced and Dr. Rhodes is very engaged in class contributions and through out–of–class support. This is a great course to orient yourself to the broader context of politics and relationships on the African continent, and the structure is quite conducive to practicing self–study and individualized research and exploration.
The professor is amazing at what he does, and even if you are not generally interested in African politics, the class discussions are interesting and engaging enough to captivate you. (If you do the readings and choose to engage)
Take this course! It is a great course with a great instructor. I often looked forward to the course every week. As long as you are willing to participate in the discussions this is a good course to take. There are few Africa–related courses in the Government department and this course provides a good baseline for future courses one can take related to Africa or Government/policy in general.
Take this class. For anyone passionate about Africa, you learn so much about the complex relationship between history and political theory to answer all the "why" questions about how Africa is the way it is. With this insight, you gain so much understanding and have a clearer more objective view of Africa, afterwhich you can form and propose various solutions to Africa's most pressing issues.
This course is an excellent discussion course on African political theory and history. Students should have an interest in learning more about African politics and society and a willingness to do some reading outside the class. The class is also fairly writing–heavy.
A great class for an introduction to the history of Africa, from pre–colonial right up to independence and into the present day. No pre–knowledge is necessary, and the readings will help you broaden your understanding, whilst class discussions will force you to think critically about the complex topics covered.
