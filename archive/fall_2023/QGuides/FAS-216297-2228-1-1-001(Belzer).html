This class is not easy at all. It’s hard. The professor is a great person, but she is very tough in a way that made me feel like I was in high school again. She mainly would only give negative or vague feedback. Several people in this class, myself included, felt that this professor did not believe in them because she frequently made comments insisting some students were not putting in the effort that they were, which was oftentimes confusing and discouraging. I personally did not feel like she believed in me as a student. I also did not understand her expectations for student’s time commitments to the class. There is no doubt that this class requires close to 20 hours of your week, and that time commitment certainly helped me to grow as an artist, but this professor would frequently make remarks suggesting she did not see me working in the studio… outside of class time… I was spending around 25 hours in the studio most weeks. Because her actual feedback on assignments was usually so vague and expectations were so unreasonably high, accompanied by her often rude tone, it is really hard for me to feel good about this class.
Like a lot of classes, you will get out of this class what you bring to it. Come open to changing everything about your painting practice, at least as an exercise.
This is such a rewarding class! Judith is an incredible instructor who does a great job individualizing her feedback to each student and tending to each individual need. She is so knowledgeable and I felt that after each conversation with her I learned something new or a new way of thinking. The long class hours are daunting at first and can be tough, especially when you're stuck creatively, but it is a really valuable class time which leads to really dedicated time to painting and also a warm and enjoyable classroom community. Give the this class the time and focus it deserves and you will get so much out of it!
please be very careful taking this class!!! the expectation in this course is that you will dedicate your life to it no matter what, and there is very little tolerance or sympathy for those who encounter any struggles that may take them out of the studio for some time. there is also very little understanding of the fact that people work at different paces. it felt like taking breaks from the workload throughout the semester for genuine reasons (getting sick, religious commitments, loss, stressful extracurriculars, mental health, etc) was often perceived as an attitude issue rather than an extenuating circumstance, which was very frustrating and disheartening, and the course head was very passive aggressive to students who she didnt think came in enough or who missed classtime, even if they had legitimate reasons. even after making up the assignments i missed, i still felt that i was expected to "make it up" to her somehow, and i often left class feeling like i couldn't do anything right. ultimately, i spent 10+ hours outside of class time in the studio every week for the second half of the semester, and over 30 hours on the final project, only to be told by the course head that she didn't think i prioritized the class enough, didn't feel like i cared, and didn't perceive me to even like the paintings i was working on – which was especially frustrating as i was pretty happy with the work i eventually put together. though of course she was very "nice," i did not feel supported by professor belzer. most of the critique i got from course staff was centered around their perception of my dedication to the course or time spent on a given piece rather than the actual artwork i was putting forward, and the relevant critique i did receive was very vague and not up to the standard of feedback i would normally expect from a course at this school. that said, i got great feedback and advice from my peers in the class, and am extremely grateful for the opportunity to access the spatial and material resources this class offers, as they are pretty awesome. to me, it was worth withstanding the awkwardness of feeling constantly judged to access these resources and make some work im proud of. ultimately, im glad i took the course because it was still a meaningful experience to me, but that doesnt mean it will be for everyone. if you do take this class, please be prepared to live in the studio and churn out as many paintings as you can –– slow painters beware!!! also, if you think you might ever need a week off of studio time, or if you cant keep a straight face while someone is being passive aggressive to you, this class is not for you
I feel that if you're at the point of looking at an intermediate painting class, you already know what you're signing up for. Judith and Scott are both incredible and helped me develop as an artist in this class in ways I hadn't before.
This course represents a wonderful opportunity to learn and grow, but you simply must be prepared to work nearly every day of the week on your art in order to achieve good results.
