What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
You can choose to learn a lot from this class if you wish, but there aren't many enforcement mechanisms for learning that material, other than eight reading responses and a final take–home essay/exam that, for some reason, accounts for 70% of your grade. But the material is great, the lectures and readings are fascinating, and the teaching staff are great at what they do. Overall, this course was a net positive for me, but I can see why others might not have had the best experience with it.
This class requires a lot of work and background. You will feel like you were sprinting all semester. However, the topics and themes covered are very interesting and you will learn so much. Not only will you learn the history of the first milennium in the near east, but you will learn how this history contributed to the writing of any text, be it the bible or stelae written by kings or inscriptions on temple walls.
