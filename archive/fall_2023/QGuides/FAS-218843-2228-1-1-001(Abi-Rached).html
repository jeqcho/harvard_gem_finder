I liked this course. It was incredibly interesting. In class discussions helped to give me a different perspective. I would go into class having one opinion about the readings and leave class with a different, more nuanced view of the topics we discussed. I think this class is doable for freshman, but I would honestly recommend waiting until at least your sophomore year. I think it is a class with a two big writing assignments and doesn't have as many smaller assignments to help one get used to college level writing. I think taking expos before taking this class would be very helpful, but isn't required. I also strongly recommend this class for people planning to go into medicine!
This class is amazing! Completely changed how I think about and interact with so many parts of the world. You will get the most out of this class if you engage in the readings and in class discussion; you can learn a lot from both the readings and the people in class.
If you are interested in global health take this class. The content is incredibly interesting and important, and Dr. Abi–Rached provides amazing insight and synthesis. The readings take time but are interesting, and the essays are entirely self–guided based on your interests.
This is a great course! Professor Abi–Rached is such a great lecturer. She is also an incredible teacher; going to her office hours helped me so much with my ideas and she cares about her students.
Very good class for anyone interested in colonial theory or global health; there is a lot of freedom in topic for the midterm and final, which is helpful; the lecture section of class can be tedious and even border on boring
This class is reading heavy but is very interesting – I definitely recommend it! I think it is so important especially for anyone planning to go into medicine or a health care related profession.
This course is what inspired me to minor in GHHP – It completely changed the way I think about global health/development and am much more aware of the legacy of colonial medicine perniciously still effects global health today. It also changed how I am considering/evaluating internship oppertunities for this summer and ultimately how i will engage with this topic once I graduate.
"From Colonial to Global Health" is a FANTASTIC course. I strongly recommend it! The readings every week are very carefully chosen and I found them really interesting. The topics we covered were so wide–ranging, from how colonists weaponized outbreaks to clinical trial ethics. As someone interested in medicine, I feel this course gave me a solid foundation for what global health has and could be. Joelle Abi–Rached is an incredible lecturer — I felt I learned so much from her and she was very accessible outside of class for office hours and super quick at responding to emails. Her slideshows and videos were always very carefully put together and it felt like every minute of her lectures was worth listening to. The course assignments also felt meaningful. I feel really proud of the final paper I wrote for this class and all the research that went into it. This said, my one reservation about the class was that it was designed to be a seminar, but transformed into a lecture–seminar hybrid because it was oversubscribed. During class we'd have about 45 minutes to an hour of lecture, then a summary of the readings given by students, and finally an hour of discussion. Then, during section, we'd essentially repeat the same discussion we already had during class, which felt unnecessary.
This is a must take class if you are interested in global health or any sort of health! You will learn how the current biomedical systems came about today, and how they are shaped by colonialism. I think it is impossible to practice ethical medicine without knowing about these kinds of histories. This is truly a once in a life–time class. It has really shifted my perspective of global health and how I look at history. It also helped me work on a lot of essential skills like writing and analyzing historical sources, as well as forming an argument.
This is a fantastic class!! Prof Abi–Rached is amazing, and Adri was also a wonderful TF. You learn so so much about colonial global health in this class, and the readings are really very interesting. The essays can seem intimidating, but Prof Abi–Rached gives great feedback! You should definitely take this class, even if you aren't premed or Histsci. It's definitely worth your time.
Take this class! The topics for each week are so interesting and SO important. Your classmates will enrich your experience and you will get away from class with so much knowledge!
I would definitely recommend this course if you are passionate about medicine, global health, health policy, and Indigenous communities. Professor Abi–Rached does an incredible job of tracing the colonial past of global health, starting the class with tropical medicine and ending with conversations on the COVID–19 pandemic and the post–pandemic future. I think everyone considering a health career should take this course.
This course is definitely a highlight of my semester. The class takes you on a journey with historical lenses to contextualize colonial and contemporary practice, narrative, and debate on the coping of various diseases. Before this class I have never taken any history courses, but course materials are structured in a very engaging and straightforward manner that helps us to understand how global health ends up as it is today. The professor abi–rached and TF (adri) are tremendously supportive as they provide constructive feedback to assignments and are always available to you, academically and emotionally. They create a space in the classroom and sections that make you feel connected to your peers and want to delve deeper into the materials. Take this class it's a real gem!
I highly recommend this class, the structure and content was amazing, assignments enjoyable and it really got me to reflect about the complexities of the historical basis of current global health
Professor Abi–Rached is AMAZING! Whether you're stem or humanities, it seems like HistSci is able to bridge the gap between the two disciplines and Abi–Rached is someone who embodies that wholly. It was a breath of fresh air being able to see sources that I was both familiar and unfamiliar with and learning how to use and interpret them. Everyone in the class is very friendly and smart and wants to do well!
Everyone studying or interested in global health should take this class! It really highlights how global health was born out of colonial medicine and was used as a tool of control. The readings were manageable and thought–provoking, and Professor Abi–Rached gives very engaging mini–lectures at the beginning of the seminar.
I learned more during this course about postcolonial theory, the development of global health, and theories for reaching equity, than I have in several of my other courses combined. I cannot recommend this course and the teaching staff highly enough. This course should be a requirement for anyone looking to pursue a career in healthcare so that way they understand the complex histories associated with global access to healthcare.
It is a very enriching class with engaging materials. However, they can be very triggering and traumatic. Understanding and analyzing these works will require a lot of work, but you are forced to become a critical thinker who interrogates sources.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Professor Abi–Rached was the highlight of this course. In addition to giving fascinating lectures, Professor Abi–Rached was extremely skillful at facilitating discussions and keeping students engaged throughout the weekly two–hour sessions. Also, I was very impressed by how large of an effort she made to get to know her students; she encouraged all of us to sign up for her office hour sessions, and if a student hadn't made it to her office hours yet, I noticed that she would go up to that student before class began to re–encourage them to sign up. She really made me feel like she was invested in our personal interests and academic goals! Beyond the professor, this course's materials were extremely interesting and guidelines for assignments and other class expectations were very clear. This class's community was also exceptional; I felt l knew most of my classmates pretty well by the end of the semester, and Professor Abi–Rached and Adri did a fantastic job maintaining a positive and supportive class environment.
This course is extremely helpful for understanding history of medicine and the implications of contemporary global health care. Dr. Abi–Rached and Adri are fantastic educators and they are great mentors if you seek out 1:1 conversations at office hours. Definitely do the readings because they are all so different and you will learn so much. Do not procrastinate your papers!
