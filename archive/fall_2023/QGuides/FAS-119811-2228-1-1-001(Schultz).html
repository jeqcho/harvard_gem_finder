What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
In its current form, this class doesn't do anything for your musical development nor creativity.
If Andrew Schultz is the Professor, don't take it. Otherwise, might be fine.
The class in its seminar format can become extremely fruitful in terms of critical discussion and individual mentorship. However, the design of this particular iteration of the course wasn't as successful given some questionable choices in the curation of materials. That being said, I personally enjoyed the course for the chance to compose and to learn about a lot of music, especially non–Western. I would take this course depending on your level of interest rather than standard of current craftsmanship.
This class is very fun– definitely take it if you want to experiment with your abilities as a composer.
The lecture component isn't demanding, and most time should be spent on composition (and using opportunities for feedback as much as possible).
