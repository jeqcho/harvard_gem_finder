Obviously, take this class. Darcy is an amazing professor and his feedback is thorough and thoughtful. He facilitated the best part of class, which was discussions about pieces with my classmates. This was my favorite class I've taken so far. I looked forward to it every week. There's nothing else at Harvard like a writing seminar!!!
Absolutely take this class. It is such an amazing opportunity to explore creative nonfiction (in whatever form you want), with an amazing professor who knows an infinite amount about the craft and is such a kind, genuine down–to–earth person! There are so many classes to take at Harvard to further a certain end, or that don’t give a chance for creativity — this one you can take just for you.
This is a great class to take if you want an excuse to write creatively. Darcy provides great feedback on how to constructively improve as a writer and is extremely approachable and not pretentious. He will make you a better writer.
This class is fantastic and I wish that I could take a creative writing department class every semester. I loved becoming close with my classmates and having a real community, particularly based on the type of memoir/personal writing being done in the class. I looked forward to hearing from Darcy every week, reading the assigned short stories/essays, and reading and revising my peers' work. I loved getting to write my own piece and carve out time for creative writing.
Darcy is an absolute beast — a thoughtful instructor, an astute editor, and a wonderful mentor. The reading list was fascinating, and the writing assignments were useful (no busywork). Highly recommend for anyone looking to strengthen their creative abilities in any form of nonfiction writing.
Take this class. It will change your life!
Creative Nonfiction is not only the best class I've taken in the English department but the best class I've taken at Harvard. If I had to point to one class out of my four years here that unequivocally improved me as a writer, artist, and human being, it would 100% be this course. I could see myself becoming a better writer over the course of the semester, as well as being better at capturing retrospective reflections in both conversation and writing. In a lot of ways, this class can double as a minor form of therapy given the highly personal topics students choose to write about.

This class is more rigorous and intense than other creative writing workshops here. Yes that means more work, but it's extremely worth it; no other workshop has such a constant stream of feedback throughout the course of the semester. Darcy is incredible and fosters a very non–judgmental, open culture of sharing. Take advantage of his office hours –– every additional minute you can get talking to Darcy will yield amazing value. Definitely take this class if you want to take a stab at writing creative nonfiction – personal memoir/essays, journalism, or anything in between.
This is a delightful class, and Darcy is an expert that you simply have to learn from. You should also know this class expects a decent amount from you. Toward the end of the class, you will be reading two 10–page papers your peers have written, annotating them and writing a one–page reflection, as well as reading a published essay and writing a one–paragraph response. But this is a good thing because it means everyone in the class is very heavily engaged with the course material, and your workshop comments are generally excellent (if not sometimes conflicting – but hey that's life).
Take this class if you can!!! Darcy is a great teacher, and I genuinely feel like I learned so much about the craft of creative non–fiction from his insights and from the example essays we read each week (an incredible selection that I will definitely return to). The structure of this class is a little more rigorous than those of previous creative writing courses I've taken. Each week you'll read a not–insignificant amount and have to write a response (whether that's analyzing published work or synthesizing your suggestions/edits for your peers' writing), so you can't just show up without preparing anything. That being said, it was totally worth it — it was really fun diving deep with good writing, and it made class discussions SO rich and enjoyable. Most of the time it felt like I was part of a fun intense book club and I genuinely wanted to bring my A game each week, since people were such helpful editors and gave such kind and useful feedback for my work.
