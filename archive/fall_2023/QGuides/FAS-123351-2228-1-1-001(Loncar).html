What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Great course if you want to learn about the field of optics and photonics. Very useful for future research in the field. Marko is patient and willing to take questions so the course can also be adapted to your interests up to a point. Would highly recommend the course for an application/research focused introduction to optics. If you are looking for a Jackson style course with lots of analytic calculations and solving Maxwell's equations in different regions explicitly, I might recommend a different course.
If you want free credit, go take it. But if you want to learn E&M, take other classes. We had two problem sets, one midterm exam and one final project. Problem sets were 50% analytical, 50% simulation. Simulation it self could be very helpful for your research or good for understanding the subject, but they do not tell you how to start or setup the simulation. Therefore, you need to read the manual or learn from somewhere else how to even start the simulation. I waste so many times just to learn about very details of the simulation package which did not teach me anything about optics or physics behind it. This class is really for people who wants free credit.
Take this class as an introduction to the basic concepts of photonics. It will definitely be useful as a resource of compact knowledge about the field, to start exploring in depth some of the concepts.
I think this class isn't fit for undergraduate students unless they have a deep understanding in physics that is much deeper than the required PS 12/15 series.
