What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class helped me a lot with my writing. The class taught me how to find research sources on HOLLIS, navigate the Harvard libraries, cite sources, and overall just become a better researcher and college writer. In regards to the topic (vampires), the subject was definitely the most fun way to learn the basics of writing.
In terms of satisfying a core requirement from the university, I think this is probably one of your better options if you are looking for a fun subject rather than a more serious one.
Ricky is really nice and is usually available to help, but you need to work a little to get an A in his class.
Take this class! Dr Martin's feedback really helps each of his students improve. You will learn so much about the writing process, how to craft arguments, and best of all, a LOT about vampires! This course was one of the highlights of my semester!
If you're looking for a more creative approach to accomplishing your Expos requirement then I 100% recommend this class. The books, articles, and essays you read throughout this class are genuinely so interesting. Whether its literature, history, psychology, sociology, or anthropology, you quickly realize how much you can actually learn from vampires, which leaves a lot of room for you to decide what angle you want to take with the essays you write. I've always been more on the STEM side, so I wanted to take an Expos class that kind of strayed away from that world in order to use it as a break from science and math and I think that was one of the best decisions I made. Dr. Martin is also an amazing professor and provides incredible feedback that I truly think is the reason I have such a positive perception of this class. I can genuinely see my improvement from beginning to end and I owe a lot of that to his teaching style and dedication to providing useful feedback.
You will not regret choosing this class! I do believe that everyone should take this class. Monsters and vampires are projections of Humanities fears and desires. Therefore, to understand or master the study of vampirology is to understand society. you will understand what drive people, what scares them You will understand the intersection of humanity and monstrosity.
Do not take this class if you are not really into vampires. All of the assignments have to be on vampire related media, so not having background in it (like an obsession with Twighlight or Vampire Diaries or something) will mean that you will have a hard time finding something to write about. This is not a class that teaches about vampires. Its a class where you further analyze stuff about the ones you already know. So, if you are really into vampires, this class is perfect for you.

Also, expos in general is like actual pain (especially that research paper) so if you are not a fan of spending long nights searching for scholarly articles and analyzing expert opinions or whatever, good luck
