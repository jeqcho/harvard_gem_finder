What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is almost all about taxation –– optimal taxation and the like. See the lecture slides Prof. Stantcheva posts online. So make sure that you're actually interested in taxation.

Also, there are very few assignments in this class, so make sure to discipline yourself to learn the material on your own time. Since some of the material is cumulative, it's important to keep up.
This is a fantastic course for students interested in public economics.
an overall great class that could use some tweaks – the lack of psets were a bit scary since it was difficult to keep track of how well I understood what was going on (a lot was going on), and the final was a bit over–harsh since they tested problem–solving a new extension, which we did not practice for.

but the greats were great. how do we decide as a society what the tax rate should be? what should the tax rate be with respect to income? can we set the tax rate upon different characteristics other than income? how do we socially weight the utility of different members of societies? how do we make sure that people are not avoiding taxes? how do we make sure that tax codes don't incentivize laziness? within each of these questions are packed a great deal of political combustiveness, and public debate tends to drown out what we set out to do – to figure out what best to do as a society. this class will help you put the policy beliefs you hold in your mind into math, and maybe your opinion will change or perhaps it will stay the same, but either way, you will have a much greater precision of thought in thinking about issues of justice, equity and redistribution
