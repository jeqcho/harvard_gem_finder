Take this course!! Dr. Quigley is an exceptional instructor who is extremely passionate about this topic. If you are interested in psychology or mental health, this course is perfect. All of the readings offered perspectives I had not even thought about before, and I learned so much. While the readings are not that long, the content can be dense, but they are all so interesting. I was also surprised to actually enjoy writing while taking this course.
No words can explain how much I think everyone should take this course. Do not be nervous about the subject matter – I’m a potential English major and went in knowing absolutely nothing about psychology as a subject. All the thinking in this course stems from natural instinct and intuition, so do not feel intimidated if you lack experience in the field of gender and mental health. The professor Dr. Q is probably one of the best I’ll ever have – she’s super kind, down to earth, hilarious, and makes all class discussions feel easy and free of pressure. My writing has seen a noticeable improvement just from taking her feedback on three papers into account. I cannot stress how much you should take this expos.
Definitely take this class if you are interested in learning more about gender and mental health, which I know seems pretty obvious from the title of the course. But I really do think this course gives great insight into how psychologists approach research and clinical psychology. It teaches you how to assess other writers' work and identify flaws in logic or places where multiple perspectives were not considered. It therefore also teaches you to write in a way that avoids making these same errors and also, how to build a strong, progressive argument in which points build on one another.
I'd definitely recommend this class! Dr. Quigley is really fun to talk to and knowledgeable, and the in–class discussions are great. My only warning would be to prepare to do a lot of reading. However, the readings are really interesting. This class is a bit scientific and psych–leaning so if you're a STEM concentrator who doesn't love the humanities/writing, this would be a great EXPOS to take. It was really interesting to look at how my daily experiences are informed by gender and psychology.
CHOOSE THIS CLASS!!! THIS CLASS TEACHES YOU WAY BEYOND A WRITING SEMINAR
I LOVED Dr. Quigley's Expos class. Some of the most interesting content of the semester! I love psych (but wasn't able to take Pscyh 1), and this class more than enough made up for it! Dr. Quigley herself is such an intelligent, well–spoken, kind, and helpful professor. She is also extremely knowledgeable about the writing process and gives her students the BEST and very fair advice on how to improve papers. I'm not so sure why so many freshmen dread Expos–––this class was one of my favorites. You get to make tight–knit connections with a lot of your peers (which may be more difficult in large lecture classes). And Dr. Quigley creates such a welcoming and friendly environment. This class is a must–take! I highly highly recommend!
This class is worth taking, especially if you are interested in psychology or gender studies! Dr. Quigley is a fantastic professor and a compassionate and kind person in general. The workload is very manageable and as long as you engage in the class, you will definitely learn a lot about psychology and improve your writing/analytical skills easily.
Dr. Q is amazing and genuinely cares about the growth of her students. I was worried that my writing background wasn't sufficient, but I felt very welcomed in the class and learned so much about the writing process.
