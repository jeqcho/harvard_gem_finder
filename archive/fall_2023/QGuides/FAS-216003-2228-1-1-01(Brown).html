What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is truly amazing but will require you to put in the work. Beyond focusing on the specific subject matter, which is itself a difficult one that requires careful readings of the assigned readings and nuanced discussions, the course is also very driven to hone your writing skills and historical thinking as a whole. The professor and TF are absolutely wonderful and willing to help if you go to them, but you will need to be able to hold yourself accountable and stay on top of your work.
This course made me think "wow, I kind of wish I concentrated in history" because I just learned a lot of things that I was not exposed to or was exposed to incorrectly. The course made me want to shift my thesis topic and I am happy I did. Dr. Brown is a great professor and cares about each students' project which I was really appreciative of seeing each time we had a workshop session in class.
This could a great class depending on your commitment to it and your experience with reading and writing history.

Professor Brown is a fantastic teacher –– every sentence he speaks manages both to seem intuitive and pack a punch of insight. I learned so much about how to think about history in general (as well as the history of the Atlantic slave trade) just from hearing him talk in seminar. He also does very well to make the seminar a comfortable environment, both for our discussions and for our stress levels. I wish we had been a better class, because we definitely did not give back what he (along with our TF Emmanuel) was putting in.

The class is also so fascinating and important — you will learn cutting–edge history that upends a lot of high–school–history assumptions about slavery, race, war, and power in the Atlantic.

That said, there was too much (very dense) reading, and not enough people did it. You got out what you put in, but also most people weren’t putting in enough. The deadlines were very loose, which counterintuitively added stress for me (and my friends in the class) because I didn’t prioritize my research enough and then was always worried because I turned everything in late. These are problems may be fixed in the future, but if you’re someone who has a lot going on or has less experience doing big research papers, think carefully about what’s on the syllabus.
Read what is assigned. Engage with the material. Be prepared to discuss your thoughts in class.
This is a fantastic course taught by one of the best professors in this university. Highly recommend taking a course with Prof. Brown at some point in college, but this is only a course for people who are truly interested in doing a lot of research and want to improve the writing skills. This course is a lot of work, but it is worth it!
