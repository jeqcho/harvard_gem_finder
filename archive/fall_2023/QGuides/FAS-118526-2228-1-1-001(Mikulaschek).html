I highly recommend this class. The readings are challenging but interesting, and you get a lot out if it.
If you are even remotely interested in international law, I highly recommend you take this class. This course goes in–depth into every important topic related to international organizations and international law, including the institutional aspects and key issue areas like human rights. Professor Mikulaschek never failed to incorporate current events and historical examples into lectures and engage students in fun forms of participation, such as simulations and debates, that helped to deepen our understanding of class concepts. He also brought in several speakers who worked in international law to provide us with different perspectives on class topics, like the EU and sanctions. I feel incredibly lucky to have found this class and to have learned so much from it!
I would definitely only take this class if you have some background in international law/relations (those who have taken gov40), as this is not and introductory course and assumes prior knowledge about history, international organizations, and some theories. I would also only recommend this class for people who want to focus in IR. There is a lot of reading and information to know (which you definitely need to keep up with if you want an A), and the material can quickly become very dry unless you're very interested in the subject matter. The professor is very engaging and facilitates discussion, and is clearly enthusiastic about the subject matter. Section was helpful in clarifying confusing topics and summarizing and analyzing the readings. However, there was very little review for the midterm and final, and grading policies were very unclear. It would have been very helpful to have practiced writing in the class to prepare for the exams. Overall, this is a well–taught course, but is probably best suited for people with a background and/or strong interest in IR.
This class made me realize I don't like government, but I also enjoyed it. I would really recommend it if you are interested in international institutions beyond the United Nations

Professor Mikulaschek clearly has an intimate knowledge of the topic, but again, he does move very quickly, so if you don't have an IR background, it might be hard to keep up. We don't really do a deep dive into any particular topic during the course, but it's a good way to learn about a wide variety of topics.
