What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great class! It's low–workload, so if you're interested in education and/or need to fill some requirement, this is a good option. Your grade depends entirely on two equally–weighted essays which are 1500 and 2500 words. Highly recommended.
A great class. You get out what you put in, so I would try to be an active participant. It makes the discussions we have more interesting.
This course feels a lot like a seminar in the sense that you're encouraged to raise issues to Professor Elgin during the lecture and be engaged in a dialogue –– I found this key to enjoying the lectures and learning as much as I could from them. Professor Elgin is also incredibly committed to your success and understanding of the material –– she takes every question seriously, and oftentimes notes that you might be asking more underlying questions within your question before you even realize it!

Assignments weren't too demanding, just two papers over the course of the semester and brief commentary on readings. And you have ample time to complete all these things and talk to teaching staff about your papers, so you never feel rushed.

You don't need to have a background in writing philosophy to succeed in this class; rather, you just need to come in with a willingness to engage with the material, and it's even better if you talk to the teaching staff about it. Everybody involved in the execution of this course is working on something really cool in the education space, and they make themselves available to chat about the field, graduate school, their work, and assignments. You can tell the teaching staff really care about this course.
Do the readings as they are very important to your experience and will make you enjoy this class,
This course is a wonderful foray into education philosophy that any student can take to further understand a number of wonderful thinkers from the previous generations. I strongly recommend the class for any student interested in philosophy or education!
Although this class doesnt require a lot of work, you definitely get out what you put in. I would recommend it for anybody interested in any type of educational or philosophical career path.
Take this class if you enjoy philosophy.
