This seminar is awesome. You keep a dream journal, learn about how to interpret dreams, and control your own. The field trip we took to MIT was also super cool and everyone in the class was really invested in the content. Beware, getting the most out of this course is mainly possible for people with interesting questions about dreaming or interesting experiences in their dreams (i.e. lucid dreamers, hearing music in dreams, productive dreamers, or people who've thought critically about their dreams). Fortunately, as a freshman seminar, this class is application based so people with interesting considerations or burning curiosity about dreams are selected. No prerequisite knowledge is required but understand that sitting in a room for two hours talking about dreaming may not be for everyone. The class can feel a little long at certain moments but overall it's a great experience. This course strikes a nice balance of being extremely interesting and not a ton of work.
Absolutely take this course. It is such a wonderful experience and my highlight of first semester. You get to meet such amazing people and Deirdre is an incredible professor.
This class is basically no work, but can be quite boring at times.
This is an amazing seminar, and our group was able to get so close and form friendships outside of class!! Absolutely take this course if you have an active dream life, have been curious about your dreams, or simply want to discuss dreams with others. This was one of my favorite classes this semester, and it's a great way to spend 2 hours a week discussing something that really engages you.
Some aspects of this course aren't as interesting as they would first seem to be, but other aspects ended up being a lot of fun, such as the MIT trip, animation activity, and hypnosis. The course load is very manageable, too.
This class is very interesting and niche so definitely make sure you're really interested in the subject of dreaming to begin with. I would say it was a little slow at times but we were also afforded the chance to visit a really cool sleep lab and combine our creative skills in class with the course themes and assignments.
This course was made particularly great by the final projects at the end of the semester, and we spent the last two classes hearing presentations from each other. Around 90% of students choose a creative approach, and the modalities ranged from realistic pencil drawing to poetry, creative writing, composition and screenwriting.

The seminar definitely requires an interest in dreams, and it's one of those classes that you'll get out what you put in. As with most Freshman Seminars, you could probably survive without engaging/doing any of the readings (it isn't overly challenging), but you'll thank yourself later if you do – it's incredibly interesting and a topic that you're unlikely to explore throughout the rest of your studies at Harvard.
The course has a lot of very interesting material and it is very relaxed which is super nice.
I'm really happy I took this class. It is a very low time commitment (weekly readings + a final project), and the content is really interesting. You will learn a lot from it, about dreaming as a whole but also about the way you specifically dream. It is just a really fun class and you should take it!
