You will learn to love the word control.
Really enjoyed the material, especially with its applications to a wide range of fields. The content was also presented in a way that was really manageable to learn.
My opinion is mixed. The good things are that Lina and the course staff (especially Krithika!!!) are wonderful and are especially helpful and reachable. Lina was always so approachable and great at offering suggestions to help. The recommendations Lina made for final project after only a few minutes of consideration were spot on and helped us refine it; she truly has controls figured out. Krithika was such an amazing TF and was always quick at responding to my questions. She's so nice and I could tell her vast knowledge of the topics.

I think you learn quite a bit in the course about control theory although I think it can go deeper, especially into the math. We never really saw proofs of any of the theorems we used in the course and I take issue with this. We should know more of what's going on and I wish Lina assumed that we had more mathematical maturity. For this reason I think the lectures could be better and more intuitive– lectures are "required" but I feel like I could read the lecture notes and the textbook without going to any of the lectures and have the same understanding of control theory. The textbook also had proofs of the theorems we used so I went there usually when the proofs were omitted from class which was most of the time.

The final project was a strength of the course; it is not like other courses' final projects. You start this one the first few weeks of the semester. Every few weeks, instead of a problem set due, there is a final project milestone due where you work with your team on the final project and apply what you just learned the past couple weeks to the final project of your choosing. This is a wonderful idea and I'm glad this existed. When it comes time to hand in the final report all you have to do is fix up some of the project and maybe add some more things if you want and that's all–– because you've already done the core of the work for the milestones earlier in the semester. I think more courses should do this.

There are a few administrative things which stressed me out a lot. There was a problem set (notorious, if you know someone who took this class, even last year or the year before, they will immediately know exactly which problem set you're talking about) which would have taken a few hours if it weren't for the stupidly high amount of tedious algebra the course staff insisted we do. No other problem set was like that.

There was also the second midterm to be done entirely during the confines of reading period. This was a de facto final so I'm unsure why the staff decided to stress us out even more and have us do it during reading period. The final project was not so urgent as to necessitate pushing the "midterm" to be during reading period.
Study, that is the only way to survive
