What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is a great neuro elective if you're interested in exactly how plasticity and development works on a genetic, molecular level. The readings for each class are low pressure and you usually don't need to know every detail but just the general goal of the paper. Go to office hours if you need help and Prof Kunes is happy to answer questions and he was a super nice teacher and even brought food in every morning for the class. Papers get put together easily if you have a good idea, so think of one early and clear it with Prof Kunes. Problem sets can be detailed, but answers are easy to find if you take notes on the readings and look through slides. Impossible to get a bad grade in this course if you pay even 50% attention in class. I enjoyed it – highly recommend.
This class is good. The professor brought us snacks every class, and was always understanding when it came to extensions. The problem sets can be complicated, but if you go to office hours you're set.
Super interesting class! The professor is so sweet and is always there to help you understand the (very) difficult concepts. Not too much work. Very understanding teaching staff, gives extensions and brings banana bread to EVERY class!!
Take this class if you need to fulfill your advanced neuro class and want to get better at reading journal articles. However, this class would be most useful if you are doing relevant research and want a more foundational background.
