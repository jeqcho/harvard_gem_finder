Comments
The class is a great introduction to philosophy. Readings were heavy at the few days in start of each unit and there is a reading quiz during these days. So it may take quite a lot of time, but the quizzes are usually just to see if you at least skimmed the material. The structure of college writing is drastically different from high school, but Jen gives clear expectations and guidelines. Jen is also very helpful during office hours where she helps to refine your essay roadmap and guide you in the right direction.
It's a good expos class but heavy in philosophy. The workload isn't too much in terms of the reading and they are interesting but a lot of it is harder to understand. It wasn't the easiest but it wasn't too hard either.
If you are interested at all in philosophy (because this really is a philosophy course), I think this is a good way to both fulfill the expos requirement and explore a discipline you may not have tried before. I definitely appreciated being able to learn all that I did about the philosophers we covered in this course (Aristotle, Nietzsche, and those I read for my final paper) while also becoming a better writer. Sections for this class did feel dry at times (maybe because mine was at 9am), and people rarely wanted to actually engage in discussions, which could have made the class more interesting because there is lots of room to debate the materials we read. Overall, however, I think this expos is a good choice for anybody who wants to take something outside of what they have studied before or wants to learn about interesting philosophy.
Listen closely to feedback and make the adjustments you're told to make to your writing even if you don't necessarily agree at first. I second–guessed the writing style a lot at first but by the end understood why we were encouraged to write like that. It was very different than anything I had ever done and felt wrong at first.
I would tell future students that this class is engaging and thought provoking. Every assignment is manageable and the teacher is very supportive throughout the whole journey
This course definitely improves your writing by teaching you to write through a reader–centered approach. The homework and reading assignments vary with some nights being heavier and some nights being light. There are reading check–ins (small quizzes) most classes, but be sure to just read the material, and you'll do well.
Take Friendship if you want exposure to philosophy! Jen is a wonderful preceptor and really helped illuminate the complex topics that we discussed in class. I will say this class will make you think in ways that you have not before. Be prepared for your drafts to be ripped to shreds, but it's okay because the class is a really comfortable environment to bounce ideas off of each other.
This section of Expos 20 is run very well, with well–focused and applicable lessons.
The philosophy behind friendship is a topic that shapes our lives every day, especially as we are put in this new environment and make new friends while exploring our interests in a new realm.
If you're passionate about philosophy and a topic such as friendship and human relationships, this is definitely a good class to take. This course taught me many things that were applicable to my own life, it allowed me to be more aware about the relationships I have with people.
I think this class is challenging but in a doable and interesting way. I was apprehensive at first at the idea of this being a philosophy class but the expos class style means you are supported throughout so you do not feel out of your depth. There is quite a lot of reading at time but then there can be weeks without any, so it does balance itself out. Jen is super friendly and wants to see everyone succeed but you do have to put some effort in, and there are probably easier expos classes for sure.
Make sure to read thoroughly and manage your time well
