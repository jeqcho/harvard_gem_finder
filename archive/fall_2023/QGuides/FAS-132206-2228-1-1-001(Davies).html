The course is really math–heavy and not so much behavioral or conservation ecology. The course is interesting but you should be prepared for math.

Good class, consult with teaching staff for any questions when needed

This is a really interesting class. Davies and Taylor are great, and kept the classroom spirit very convivial. They restructured the course for Fall 22 and it was really nice. Workload was evenly distributed throughout the semester. Hardest part about this class is just the sheer amount of content you cover, as well as some of the more mathematical modeling, but it was really cool to learn! Great class. The fields trips are super fun too! Get to know your classmates.
Great class if you are interested in ecology. You learn a lot. Becomes very quantitative very fast, which isn't for everyone.
I wish someone had told me how much math and statistics there is in ecology!! It's NOT just learning about examples of mutualisms or nutrient transfer –– to do well in this course you will need to learn about 15–20 different equations (luckily an equation sheet is provided on exams) as well as the graphs for multiple different concepts. That's not to say that I didn't enjoy the class; it just wasn't what I expected. They don't do a great job of applying the equations to practice questions during lecture, so you will likely need to attend office hours to make sense of what's going on. The best part of this class is by far the field trips –– it's really cool to collect your own data, come up with your own ecological questions/hypotheses, and write it up into a mini paper. However, you will also need at least a basic knowledge of statistics to be successful with these reports because they do expect you to use STATA/R/Excel to make graphs and do different types of statistical analyses with the data. I would recommend this course to people who want a survey course in different types of ecology, but do keep in mind the mathematical expectations for the class.
Take this class for your ESPP bio requirement! Professor Taylor and Davies are incredible, the field trips are fun (Really) and the topics are super interesting. Really loved this classs and learned alot!
This is a really fun course. The field trips really show you the application of the material you learn in lectures. The PSETs are super manageable, but the exams do require some extra study time. The field trip reports take longer than you think but are graded more loosely. If you are ever worried about an assignment, go to Davies's or Taylor's OH. Both of them will spend the time teaching you the material so you can complete the assignments with confidence.
Take this class! The field trips are great, Davies and Taylor are awesome, and the quantitative stuff isn't too spooky. It's a fair amount of work but evenly distributed, and the grading can be a little unpredictable but it's mostly fair, as are the exams. You get to learn basically about all of earth and all of life, which is crazy. And you get to hear about the "EEEquator" a lot.
This is a super interesting class that is focused very specifically on ecology. It does include some equation/derivation work but otherwise is not math heavy. A background in statistics would help with writing and reading reports but not required.
Make sure you actually keep up with section material because specific examples from those readings are often drawn from on the exams. Also be sure to practice defining terms because I often found it easier to apply concepts than exactly define them in my own words. I didn't find reading the textbook to be all that helpful or necessary to keep up in class, but if you are less familiar with biology (I am a senior IB concentrator) it's worth it to have a copy. Overall just don't underestimate how much you cover in this class.
This is an enjoyable, not too taxing way to fill a 50–level OEB requirement. You'll learn a lot about the basics of ecology, which will probably come in handy in future OEB classes, should you be in the concentration. The field trips are fun, although you'll want to start early on the write–ups. The biggest recommendation, though, is Professors Taylor and Davies, whose banter is impeccable and who are both very accessible and helpful on their own.
An amazing course with incredible professors. I loved the lectures and the field trips. The professors' enthusiasm is so infectious and it feels like they genuinely care about student's questions
Professors are awesome and there's a great class community , especially from field trips!
I cannot stress enough how good this class is. This is coming from someone who wants to study and do research in ecology, but if you are at all interested in ecology this class is the best way to get experience actually doing ecology, along with learning all about it. At times it can get kind of math heavy, but you don't really need any special prior math knowledge (although 1b or 21a would definitely help) as most of the class is conceptual, and the whole teaching staff is always prepared to help you out with the parts that do get more math–y. Andrew and Ben are some of my favorite professors that I have had during my whole time at Harvard, so if you get the chance to take a class with either of them, definitely do it!!!

My best advice is to go to a bunch of office hours with each of the professors and the TFs because it will make going through the psets and field trip reports a lot more manageable. Also – go on all of the field trips if you have the chance!!! They are DEFINITELY WORTH IT!!! Going on the field trips and doing the reports I think was the most rewarding part of this class because you actually get to do real ecology and practice putting together a scientific paper.
The class is very fast–paced and you have to be vigilant and pay attention during lectures. The equations that are introduced in class are not provided with examples so it is helpful to make sure you fully understand how they work before tests and problem sets!
For someone who had never taken an ecology class before, this was a great introduction to the field. Attending OH is really helpful, especially since some of the pset questions are a bit ambiguous. The teaching staff is really friendlyoverall solid class!
