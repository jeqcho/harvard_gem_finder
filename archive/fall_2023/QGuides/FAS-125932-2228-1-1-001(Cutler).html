ONE OF THE BEST CLASSES IF YOU ARE INTERESTED IN HEALTHCARE AND ECONOMICS. This class can be a great introduction to the complexities and details of the healthcare system and the economics behind it (you will learn about epidemiology, pharmaceuticals, health insurance, and some basic statistics). Some knowledge in economics and statistics might be helpful, but certainly not needed (they will teach and introduce all the concepts). The class meets twice a week, has section once a week, and is structured in three different units. There are pre–lecture quizzes before every class, 3 PSETS (you can also get the economics writing credit if you complete extra work in addition to the PSET), 3 unit group presentations, a final project. This GenEd is not a Gem (it is not an easy A), it does require a lot of time commitment (PSETS are long and Unit Presentations have to be well–done) and the TFs can be harsh graders (they are really trying to help you understand the concepts and terminology)— you will get what you put in. However, despite the fact that it is on the more challenging side, it was my favorite class this semester. Professor Cutler is amazing and all the TFs are always willing to help you! At least once a week, Professor Cutler brings in a guest speaker; the speakers are the best way to gain insights and different perspectives in the world of healthcare. Everyone has different opinions with this class, often students complain about the workload and how the TFs grade the assignments. However, If you are debating on taking this class because of the grade you might get, but you are really interested in healthcare, TAKE IT! You will not regret it!
