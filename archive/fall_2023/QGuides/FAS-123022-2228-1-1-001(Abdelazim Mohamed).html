The class can be a lot to keep track of but it is a great course with great staff!
Amazing class. A fair bit of time commitment but those hours are incredibly rewarding . The advocacy may seem tedious but if you get the chance to go to DC absolutely take it, it was a fun trip!
This course is a great way to gain exposure to research in chemistry while also exploring how chemistry intersects with other disciplines, including biology, physics, engineering, and policy/political science, especially considering the aspects of the course that center around communicating science to the public and advocating for legislation related to science. The instructors are clearly passionate and enthusiastic about the material and the research that they lead, and that effect is very much palpable. I very much appreciated becoming a stronger and more fluent science communicator and advocate, and developing robust research skills along the way is, in my view, a great plus. I would note that it would be very wise to choose a good partner/group for your project. This will be the partner/team you work with for the entire semester, and not splitting work evenly could become really frustrating. Overall, this class really helps you see the power of chemistry and research, and how transdisciplinary it can be, so appreciate all the cool possibilities––like the trip to DC, the final symposium, etc.––that come with it!
This is such a fun class! Though lab days can be long, it's really worth it and has the kindest teaching staff ever. Monday and Wednesday labs from 1:30–6:30 do take up an incredibly significant chunk of the best time for studying, classes, and other activities, so do plan accordingly.
Take it
This course is very helpful in helping students get acquainted with research. The staff provide a lot of guidance in the project, and the assignments are well–tailored for helping students get the knowledge they need to do their experiments. However, your experience will largely depend on the research project you choose and your lab partners. Make sure you have good communication with your lab partners, especially as you are nearing the final paper and presentation.

The advocacy portion of this course is less well–structured, but it can be very rewarding. When choosing what bills to advocate, please try to tailor them to the interests/working committees of your representatives.
I have to be honest — I wasn't quite looking forward to a 10 hr / week lab course, but it was necessary to fulfill my chemistry concentration requirement. I took it dreading the amount of work it would be. I have come out on the other side extremely proud and appreciative of all of the work I've put into this course, and I am still continuing the research I started even after the class has completed. By the end of the semester, I was so excited to go into class each week and see everyone / work on my project. Also, the DC advocacy trip was a highlight of my college experience. Professor Vollmer–Snarr and the rest of the teaching staff were all so knowledgeable and kind, which made the whole experience so much better. It definitely is a lot of work, both in and out of class, but if you pick a project that you are interested and invested in, you will genuinely have a good (and very memorable!) experience.
