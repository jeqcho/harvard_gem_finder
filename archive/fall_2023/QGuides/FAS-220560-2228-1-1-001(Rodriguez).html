What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is challenging so be prepared! The way that I have consistently described it to my friends is "Expos but in French". It helps to be fairly comfortable with speaking in French because you will be expected to contribute to in–class discussion on the tests that you have read in French (and so it also helps to have read extensively in French). However, if your experience in those areas is a little rusty, you can still take the class, you might just have to put in extra work (and def go to office hrs!).
This course isn't the easiest, and you do have to spend a lot of time on the readings. What you put in is what you get out, but if you come prepared and participate, you can have a lot of fun.
This class is truly amazing! The course readings, assignments and the final project all breeze by in such a pleasant manner because Matthew never makes it feel daunting or pressuring! The class discussions are awesome because every student catches different elements that stand out to them, and then, as a class, we dissect and explore those literary devices/methods together. This was a great class for learning more academic French through readings of classic and novel autobiographies (and different styles of writing autobiographies), and for exploring our own motivations if we choose to write one of our own! Definitely recommend:)
Very rewarding course with really cool texts. Discussions were always so interesting. Take this course, but the readings each week are substantial
