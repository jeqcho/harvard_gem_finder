This class is exciting and engaging and provides a strong introduction to conservation biology.
I would recommend this class to students interested in an introduction to ecology and conservation. It covers a wide range of conservation–related topics and takes a fairly interdisciplinary approach. The teaching staff is awesome and very accessible. The workload is quite manageable, just a little dense towards the end of the semester (final paper & final pset due the same day, followed by a final exam).
This is a good class! I love the teaching staff and they really try to help us. Lectures are entertaining, and the guest lecturers are very interesting.
This class is a very accessible OEB course and does not require a science background. This course is worth taking if you have space in your schedule.
This class is really interesting, but it is also quite difficult.
The course teaches you a lot and the problem sets are pretty chill. I think the most difficult thing is that sometimes the teaching team's grading can be nitpick–y and their expectations for assignments might be difficult to figure out unless you go to office hours. I felt that the midterm and final were pretty fair; if you just review all the slides and pay attention during lecture you will do well.
A great class as an introduction to conservation, especially if you don't know much about it. The course is not difficult, as long as you attend lectures and do the readings.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! Very interesting, low time commitment and pretty tangible to grasp. May not get an A, but thats because exams are a tad tricky, but if thats fine with you then the course was great.
This a very good class for the most part. I feel like I have a much better understanding of the complexities associated with conserving biodiversity, but I am not sure I have a toolkit for action to address this problem. If you are interested in expanding your knowledge about the existential threats facing the planet, this a great course. Davies and his teaching staff are very well–informed and passionate and you touch on a lot of really interesting topics.
You will learn a lot about what modern conservation biology looks like through this class! The professor and TFs are very knowledgeable from their work in the field, and are very helpful and excited about getting students involved in conservation biology. They will help you get involved in research if you're interested in that, and are otherwise available to just talk about different careers and pathways in conservation. Would recommend this course!
Be prepared to do some work, but it will give you a lot of payoff!
The course material in this class is really interesting and highly relevant to anyone interested in conservation, restoration, or preservation work in the natural environment. The lectures can be a little dry and dense at time, so you need to stay on top of your note–taking throughout the semester. The teaching staff is helpful and section is a great resource for clarifying lecture concepts in addition to discussing the assigned papers. For the final paper, you need to start it early, as the deadline approaches quickly at the end.
You should take this course if you're interested specifically in the practice of conservation biology – that is, in learning how to go about conserving. Significant chunks of the course are spent on political and social issues related to conservation, and on reserve management strategies, etc. This is great if that's what you're looking to learn! But if you're hoping just to learn about ecologies and the threats facing them, this goes beyond that (and spends less time on the details of those subjects than it otherwise might – we learn general principles more than specific case studies of specific ecosystems, although there are a few of those too).
If you are interested in figuring out how to save the world under climate change, take this course! Professor Davies is a leading figure in an emerging field, and this course provides a great introduction to it. OEB background not necessary to understand, learn, and enjoy this course.
