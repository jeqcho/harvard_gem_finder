TAKE THIS COURSE!
Take Queer Theory!
I think the professor is clearly passionate about the material, and if you take this class, you will learn a lot about queer theory. You will also learn a lot about the historical implications surrounding the texts, as the professor has a background in hist and lit. However, I would suggest not taking this class if you don't have a pre–established grounding in theoretical material. Several times when students would not participate, the professor would become cold and passive–aggressive, which created an uncomfortable learning environment. The professor often assumed that we did not read the texts and was shady, verbalizing that we did not read the material and needed to do better. I enjoyed the course, but how the professor acted sometimes put me off. If students are not understanding the material, you should try to help them understand it instead of making many people in the class uncomfortable. If you can handle a professor like this, I recommend the course. He is dedicated to this field, and you will learn a lot from him. However, I think it is a bit unnecessary to act the way the professor did at times.
If this class is with Dr. Conners, you absolutely need to take it. He's a once–in–a–lifetime kind of instructor in terms of the care he takes with the material and his ability to maneuver through discussions. The course material itself is truly diverse in its list of theorists, and it offered some truly radical arguments about what queer theory looks like and what it can do. I learned how to really think through gender in a high–level, theoretical way without ever losing sight of the importance of lived experience and the need for change. The assignments very subtly builds to a more involved final project (I chose to do a 10–15 page paper, but you have total creative freedom on what the project could look like) – I never felt like I was being hand–held, but I also never though an assignment was out of my reach, even though I started and ended on two different planes of capacity. Tommy offers incredible feedback and truly cares about our successes. I can definitely see how this class could suck if it was in different hands, so if you're lucky enough to have Tommy, take advantage of it.
