I knew little to nothing about this class or Prof. Sweeney before enrolling–– taking this class was one of the best decisions I've made at Harvard. Prof. Sweeney is legitimately the pioneer of the field of tech–science research, and for that reason this course has a really special combination of content and her own experiences. There's a lot of freedom in this course to explore what you are personally interested in, and the final paper is a really well–organized assignment that acts as a great culmination of the semester's learning. The workload is relatively light and you will learn SO much. As a life–sciences concentrator where social sciences isn't necessarily my strong suit, I thoroughly enjoyed this course, and feel like I've both learned and produced so much interesting material to be revisited in the future.

This class is a disaster. The lectures were pretty interesting, and the professor is engaging and knowledgeable. However, I thought the class was very overall disorganized and unstructured. I highly recommend this class! Professor Sweeney will help you view technology from a new perspective, and you’ll be able to identify technology–society clashes in the products we use every day. As innovation grows increasingly prominent around us, this is an outlook that everyone can really benefit from.

I think that students will particularly enjoy the course if they have an interest in technology or entrepreneurship (but note that this isn’t a class on entrepreneurship/startups), and especially if they are interested in how technology often clashes with society's best interests. That being said, the subject is interesting enough that I think anyone and everyone can really approach and enjoy Gov 1433. The course is also not demanding work–wise, and the final paper can be fun if you pick a fascinating topic!

Lastly, Professor Sweeney is a huge asset to the course. The insight and stories she brings from her own experience in this field make lectures engaging, and she is wonderful to talk to in office hours or after class!
