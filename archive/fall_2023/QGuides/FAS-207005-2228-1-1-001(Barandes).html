I have heard amazing reviews of this class from previous students saying that this class has low work load and gives one a broad overview of theoretical physics. This is mostly true. Jacob is one of the most passionate, knowledgeable, and down–to–earth professor I had this semester, he truly reminds me of Richard Feynman the great explainer. However, the course is not without its downsides. For one thing the psets are not very enlightening, they are mostly computational and do not give much insights into the material covered in lecture, just 7 hours of crunch work. About one out of three lectures are purely mathematics, stuff like deriving the Lagrangians, vector calculus basics, were covered in class time. Moreover we had a pset 13 which almost no other class has and it was due during reading period, making it quite inconvenient studying for other exams. I am not exactly sure how much I am going to retain from this course, if I could do it again I might chose to audit this course instead of enrolling in it to gain the beautiful lectures but not sit through the psets.

I took this course as an elective because I felt like my high school physics experience wasn't fulfilling. I struggled back in high school and didn't find the material very interesting. I thought that Physics 19 would really show me the beauty of physics and encourage me to learn more.

Unfortunately, I still do not feel that way about physics now. Don't get me wrong, Physics 19 is very solid. However, going into the semester I didn't really understand what was different about a survey course from a more traditional STEM course. Hence, my expectations of learning profound ideas and becoming captivated by beautiful problems were not completely fulfilled. Jacob is an incredible lecturer, definitely a contender for one of the best at Harvard. He doesn't use any notes and is able to deliver a lecture jam–packed with content every class without fail. However, some lectures just end up becoming him deriving a bunch of equations on the board. His typed course notes are very good, although they are also very long. Sometimes it became too overwhelming to read them in their entirety. The assignments were great at helping me review the content, but to be honest, I would usually start them a day or two before they were due because they were not very hard. This was doable because they often just boiled down to doing a handful of algebraic manipulations. Lastly, the final project was pretty much just the equivalent of one pset problem. This was good in terms of being a low–stress assignment, but part of me wishes we had a more complete, fulfilling final project.

Ultimately, I think that Physics 19 is a very solid course, and definitely great if you are just looking for an interesting, well–run exposure to the key concepts of physics. However, it is ultimately a survey course that lacks some depth unless you really try to explore the topics further either through office hours or on your own.
