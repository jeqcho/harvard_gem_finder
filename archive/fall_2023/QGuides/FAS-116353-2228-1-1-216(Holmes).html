What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Overall a great class! I'd say take it if you're interested in the environment or want to learn more about the social impacts of climate change. The readings are very interesting, discussion is engaging, and our in–class activities are fun. The class is also a good way to meet people who are also interested in the environment. Professor Holmes is very nice and gives great feedback. Sometimes the turnaround time for papers is slow, so beware of that. It's a reasonable amount of work, about the same as most expos classes, but it's not overwhelming.
Don't be fooled: Expos is not the cakewalk that it appears. Although it's a mandatory requirement for all freshmen, the rigor of the class varies highly depending on the preceptor (teacher). Eliza is an absolutely phenomenal teacher and a lovely individual, but that does not mean that you will need to put out some of the best writing you can create. Personally, I was a very strong student in my high school English department; I have always loved writing and received a few prizes for it. I came into Expos with the hubris that I would treat the writing like it was high school and slip through a boring college requirement. Getting the grade that I wanted made this the most demanding class I took this semester, and I hardly feel like my writing grew. This class is stressful if you walk into it with closed eyes, so be aware that Expos will be demanding and relatively unrewarding in terms of writing.

With all of that said, this section is elite. If you're an environmentalist, these readings challenge your assumptions and force you to grow your thinking! Also, you'll be in class with other people who are generally environmentalists and bring great perspectives to the work. Eliza's great; the content of this section is great.
Make sure to spread out time spent on major papers. Good class overall, especially to fulfill the expos requirement.
Climate Fictions is a good choice for an Expos. Although Expos in general can be a heavy course, this particular section isn't too bad. The instructor is clearly passionate about the topic and literature as a whole.
Please take this Expos! It was super enjoyable, and Eliza makes sure you receive the best possible feedback/support so you can effectively grow was a writer. Overall, this course made the Expos experience VERY bearable.
Take this Expos!! I think what makes it is a combination of Eliza's awesome teaching and the fascinating course content. None of it feels like busywork, and people come into the class with real enthusiasm and passion.
