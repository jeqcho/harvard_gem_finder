It's amazing. If you want politics intermingled with English and literature, this is the class to take!
I would strongly recommend any future student to take this class. It's relatively low–effort, and although there was a lot of reading to do in the first few weeks, after we finished the book the workload went down a lot. This made the class super manageable with other courses, and I still feel like I gained just as much from the experience as my peers who did expos classes with lots of separate readings. Every assignment felt very purposeful, not like busywork, and there really weren't that many to begin with. Professor Cole is absolutely amazing and such a fun teacher, although class instruction can be confusing at times he is always open to questions and I always felt much more confident in my writing after any conversation with him. Definitely go to office hours if you're ever confused! While the course did get a bit boring towards the end when we basically just had lectures on our final research paper, I still felt like I was learning something. Overall, if you're looking for a "gem" in expos – this is it!!
Take 1984!! Especially in comparison to other EXPOS classes, It's a perfect workload where it's not like your not doing anything or doing too much. Its a great balance and I feel like I've left the class with sharper writing skills.
Listen to any feedback given to you. Your paper can always be improved.
It is a fantastic class with a great teacher. He knows everything about Orwell and the subject and will grade your papers very fairly. This class really broadened my mind and I enjoyed it a lot.
I absolutely loved this course. Professor Cole is an extremely interesting speaker, created a collaborative environment that really brought the students together, and substantially improved my skills as a writer. The readings are fascinating and class discussions are highly engaging. I believe the assignments are manageable and encourage us to express how we understand the texts, though towards the second half of the course we don't have many assignments to turn in (for better or worse). The smaller assignments also build very well into our final essay. Professor Cole is a fair grader and is very accessible (many opportunities to discuss drafts and ask questions). This was by far my favorite class of the semester (which I heard isn't very typical of Expos), so I would HIGHLY recommend ranking this Expos highly!!!
