A very tough class (probably the most difficult psets I've ever had) but incredibly rewarding (and Dori and Alice don't make it harder than it has to be). Compelling and enlightening lectures, terrific feedback, outstanding instructor support. If you have the background and the time, I'd recommend in the strongest possible terms.
If you want to do more math in the future, definitely take! To be fair, the course quality varies widely based on professor. If the primary textbook used is Vakil's, you're lucky. If Hartshorne, play time is over. If something else... questionable? But Dori was an amazing lecturer and there was just _so_ much content. I think pedagogically, it's good to get exposed to these things earlier, because as Dori says, you never really start understanding AG until the third time around, so if your first time is earlier, all the better for future studies.
This course will change how you think about math. Algebraic geometry is really hard, but the payoff is immense. The time investment is definitely worth it. Seeing the intricacy of the definitions and how the topology, geometry and algebra are all baked together was quite beautiful. The course staff was also just wonderful. Both Dori and Alice are so accommodating and nice, and they really care about your learning.

However, I should add a disclaimer about the amount of work it will take to actually understand things. Unlike other math classes, half of the battle on the homework is just figuring out what the problem is saying. You will probably have to spend a large part of your week thinking about AG, which is, in most circumstances, a good thing, but it also means that if you are taking other hard classes (e.g. other 200–level math courses) then you will be spending a lot of time doing homework. There were a couple homework problems that a group of us spent an hour or two on and still could not solve without help. Also, if you are considering taking 232, be mindful of who is teaching the class. Part of the reason my experience was so good is that Dori is an exceptional lecturer, and he provides excellent motivation for every concept and explains things really nicely. I think the experience would have been much more difficult with a worse lecturer.
You might as well just read the textbook (Vakil) on your own time, unless a professor is teaching.
TLDR take this class, but it is very much fruits and vegetables.
Look, this is a cool class. We followed Vakil's book essentially verbatim, and he goes through things at a very reasonable pace. You should 100% supplement the reading with other sources that explain things more concisely, however. I learned a lot in this class but if you want to do geometry, keep holding your breath. Most of this was commutative algebra and writing out the details for things you wouldn't need to think twice about and always just looking at the affine case blah. It's good material and Dori makes me want to learn and pay attention, but the cool stuff in Algebraic Geometry will be covered in the second half of the course and 232b. If you want to go to grad school, def take this course. I took Alg Geom topics courses in the past and it would have helped to have this background just so the language was a bit easier to pick up.
This is a great course if you are interested in learning about schemes, even if you have no prior exposure to classical algebraic geometry.
This is a great class. Dori is a great teacher. The book (Rising Sea by Vakil) is a great introduction to scheme theoretic algebraic geometry. My only comment is that at the end of the class the class sped up like 3x speed, and covered the hardest stuff of the course way too fast. Should have gone faster on the easier stuff in the beginning. This is just a minor complaint. Like I said, Dori + this class are great.
This is essential for any students pursuing a career in academic mathematics.
Awesome class – take it! Dori went above and beyond and made the class especially good, but if he isn't teaching next time, Vakil's book is really, really good imo, so if the instructor's using it, the class'll probably be good.
This course is worth taking,.
The class is broadly very good, and the content is valuable. With that said, it can be easy to be lost in the web of definitions which depend on other definitions, and there will be plenty of terms which describe schemes and morphisms between them which are easy enough to mix up (or forget nuances/consequences of) that often the best way to approach a problem is to Ctrl + F all the associated definitions. I would recommend the class both if you are interested in algebraic geometry broadly, or if you're looking for a reasonably difficult class which will stretch reasonably far into abstraction.
