This is hands down the best expos class you can take. Keating is an incredible teacher and highly knowledgeable. He's super organized, classes are fun and interesting, provides great feedback, essays are manageable but not easy, and the topic is super relevant. You will have a great experience if you are lucky enough to get in this class!
I keep descriptions simple, so I'd just say this: If you're passionate about sports and/or race,gender, and identity, take this class. You WILL NOT be left disappointed.
This was an amazing and very interesting class. You don't need to be obsessed with sports to take it. The instructor is very chill and gives amazing feedback on essay. Bottom line is, you get what you put in to this class. If you actually care about the class and not just getting the expose requirement done with, you'll take a lot of great writing skills away from this class.
If you love sports, take the course. Professor McKeon keeps the class extremely engaging and I learned so much about how to write an academic essay. He also provides great feedback with clear steps on how to improve.
More Than a Game is definitely one of the better Expos courses. It introduces you to writing at a college level. If you wrote at a similar level in high school, you may not gain that much from this course. It does teach you to leave behind some conventions you may have picked up at lower levels of writing. Getting to write about sports is certainly interesting. The sources provided to you for the first two essays pretty much guide everyone toward similar theses. Unit 3 gives you more freedom to explore your own analytical question and is a lot more interesting in my opinion.
This class is very fun, and you learn a lot about the intersection between sports and politics.
