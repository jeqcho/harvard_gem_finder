What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This an important course not only for those interested in the Islamic world or architecture but everyone who is interested in trans–cultural dissemination of information, power iconography, aesthetic values throughout the centuries.
This course is interesting if you would like to learn more about islamic architecture. The readings can be a little bit dense, though you get most of the important information through lecture and section so if you are confused about some of the readings thats okay. The lectures can sometimes be a little bit dry but the professors and TF are so knowledgeable and really care about the students and their experience, and I found the content to be interesting.
You should definitely take this course! It gives knowledge on a topic most people don't know about and it is so interesting. The assignments are doable as long as you put in the work and check in with your TF at the office hours beforehand. The professors are lovely and are very passionate about their topics. They are also experts in their field as they manage the Aga Khan program, so they're really dedicated to make Islamic architecture more accessible to people.
This course is enjoyable and is well balanced. It teaches architectural basics, though does have a very strong focus on islam as the course suggests, and covers history of the regions as well. Even if you are not particularly focused in the history of Islamic regions, this course was quiten enjoyable made by the professors and TF and course organization
Take it if you're at all interested in learning about the past through a cool lens.
