What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in environmental law and policy, take this class! It's a great crash course that exposes you to a lot of different legal ideas and debates in the environmental law space. Professor Joroff does an incredible job of breaking down complex concepts and legal cases. I really loved this course appreciated the way it was taught (more of a casual lecture style, with interspersed discussion, where questions were always welcomed). It is a reading and writing heavy seminar, so if you enroll be prepared for significant weekly readings, consistent (single spaced!) writing assignments throughout the semester, and a long written final – but in my opinion very worthwhile for what you get out of it!
If you are interested in environmental law but are unsure what that means in practice, take this course
I think everyone who is at all interested in environmental policy should take this course. It's quite an extensive course and, if you're interested in law school, is probably a great preemptive course. It has opened my eyes to the various subsections of policymaking that all need to be considered when pursuing environmental policy changes.
The assignments are not easy and there's not much direction for them but the material you're dealing with is super cool.
This course is an amazing introduction to environmental law! Aladdine is a wonderful professor, and she makes sure that everyone understands the course topics and materials.
This course is great! You get a good understanding of environmental law, and it will make you feel more informed. I loved most of it, but my only complaints are about how long it takes to get feedback and grades on assignments. Also, having the course at an earlier time would definitely have made it better. Having free coffee and tea in the HUCE lounge definitely made it tolerable, though!
Take this class if you want to learn about the more pragmatic parts of approaching climate change. It may not be the most exciting class where you learn exactly how to solve climate change, but it offers insight into the structures that make mitigation and adaptation possible and/or challenging.
This is a great course that provided a new perspective on climate change for me. Learning about the basics of torts, takings, and fiduciary duties sounds boring but it's really fascinating when applied to climate policy and lawsuits.
Just a fantastic course. This is one of the few classes at Harvard that has taught me practical skills and illuminated the actual inner workings of how policy gets made instead of just gesturing to it. Learning how to write in a bunch of different styles that highlighted a number of different ways to get involved in the decision making processes of our government was really interesting. Aladdine is also one of the most understanding and well–informed professors I have had and she seemed to be genuinely excited about sharing the law with undergraduates. The assignments can be a bit stressful (I am begging for them to stop being due on Saturday nights) but mostly because each of them was a completely new style of writing I had never explored before. I would recommend this to anyone who wants to be involved in policy to any degree (legislating, analyzing, consulting, etc.)
Professor Joroff's Seminar is extremely interesting and worthwhile if you have any interest in Climate Change, Public Policy, and/or the Law. The discussions were extremely interesting and the materials were well designed. Likewise, the assignments were very interesting to work on. The only negative I have about the course is the timeline for grading — while the feedback/grades that we did receive were extremely insightful and fair — we did not receive grades/feedback for many of the assignment that we submitted.

Receiving grades/feedback for these remaining assignments would do a lot to alleviate stress regarding final grades. Nevertheless, this course was still a highlight of my semester, and I would still recommend it!
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Whether you are pre–law, environmentally oriented, or just have any sort of interest, it is an incredible course taught by an amazing professor. Definitely one of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. Professor Joroff is so kind and willing to help students to learn. Don't be intimidated by the long class, it goes by really quickly and for the rest of the week you're done! assignments aren't heavy nor is the readings so it is a hugely high return class.
