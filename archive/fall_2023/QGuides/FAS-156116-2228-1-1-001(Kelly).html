It's a fine class. I definitely learned about Heidegger, and I like Sean Kelly's lecture style and getting to know him. I would say that though Heidegger was a Nazi, fascist, and generally bad person, and many of his contemporaries (eg French existentialists) had better ideas than he did, this class is not about that. The entire class is about understanding what Heidegger was trying to say in his intentionally confusing writings, not really about analysis or criticism. The ideas are relatively interesting and unique compared to other Philosophy classes, but don't take it unless you really are interested in what Heidegger has to say.
If you are in any way interested in philosophy, take this class –– it's the best piece of continental philosophy that Harvard can offer, and Professor Kelly is incredible.
This is a great class! Prof. Kelly is an excellent and engaging lecturer, and learning about Heidegger transformed some aspects of how I see the world and our purpose in it. I highly recommend this to anybody studying philosophy, or not studying philosophy but interested in meaning, values, or cognitive science.
Only take this class if you are going to be serious about it. Clarity of concepts is a requirement if you are going to succeed in the class. Make sure you understand the basic concepts and arguments of every text.
ABSOLUTELY TAKE THIS CLASS. Kelly is amazing. One of the best lecturers ever. This class is, of course, very niche, but it somehow feels feels radically important for our every day existence. This combination is very special. Made me fall in love with philosophy. Even if you aren't a concentrator, it teaches you how to think critically about some of the most important questions of our day, namely "what is it to be?". Plus, you touch on all sorts of other relevant and engaging topics, including art and technology.

This material in this course is fairly complex, but also very interesting. If you are willing to meaningfully engage in the material, you will get so much out of it.
Take this class to think differently and engage with an entirely different approach to understanding philosophy/the world. It's hard but rewarding.
Prof. Kelly is an incredible resource in elucidating the almost impenetrable writing of Heidegger. If you have any interest in Heidegger (or any Continental philosophy, really) this course is a must.
Read stuff multiple times
I wholeheartedly recommend anything Prof. Kelly teaches; he's a fantastic lecturer, and if he can't get you interested in the material, no one can. I would advise that one take his Being and Time course, or at least have some general familiarity with Heidegger, before taking this course. It's true that this course doesn't often draw on early Heidegger explicitly, but a sense of what he's up to there will make Later Heidegger seem a little less bewildering. You'll have a much more enjoyable experience that way, I think.
Phil 139 is an excellent course for those with some background in continental philosophy or existentialism. If you have taken Phil 34, you will be equipped to take this course. This is a great opportunity to explore some foundational (and obscure!) philosophical ideas and stretch your capacity for philosophy in new ways. Be prepared to spend many weeks talking about the nature of being and other equally mystifying ideas!
Take this class even if you have not taken Being and Time! Sure, the readings are hard, but after three weeks you really get into Heidegger's headspace and he is no longer impenetrable. Professor Kelly does not shy away from Heidegger's checkered past but shows you exactly what makes him the most influential philosopher of the 20th century. I feel like after this class I saw Foucault, Arendt, and many others in a new light.
