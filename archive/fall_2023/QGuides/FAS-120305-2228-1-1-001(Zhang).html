What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Highly highly recommend this class for heritage speakers, whether you have a language req to complete or not.
Here's some strengths of the class:
Incredible classroom interaction: Every lesson students were given opportunities to converse with other students and their teachers in real–world settings.
Awesome teachers: Always open to talking to students 1–on–1, understanding of illnesses/personal issues, prompt grading of homework/tests (I honestly don't know how they returned our final the day after we took it), very approachable and makes each lesson super fun
Great community: My other 3 classes for the fall were STEM classes, and taking Chinese Bx let me meet a different community of people that I am super grateful for. Our drill section got super close (shoutout 10:30–11:30!) and we planned trips to Chinatown, homework grind seshes, and Annenberg dinners. Awesome way to meet other students of similar cultural backgrounds.

Here's some advice for the class:
Work out a daily/weekly routine for studying. It's very tough to "cram" when learning a language, since daily practice is key to learning the grammar/vocab. So set up a Quizlet/Anki deck, and put aside 15–30 minutes a day to practice. You'll find that the quizzes and homework become much easier once you are familiar with the vocab.
ABSOLUTELY TAKE IT! The Chinese language department may be the best language department at Harvard (biased but true), and the instructors are all kind and lovely people. While the homework can take several hours to complete, and you are expected to learn a decent amount of new Chinese characters per week, your Chinese proficiency in all regards will be improved, and you will come away glad that you took this course. Take it for sure!
If you already speak Chinese at home (regardless of origin) but genuinely want to learn how to read and write more in the language, TAKE THIS CLASS. You will learn at an accelerated but manageable pace that will definitely help your Chinese reading/writing skills improve in just one semester. The course is definitely designed for those who already have a basic grasp of Chinese (ie not total beginners, can differentiate pinyin sounds). I could barely read anything more than Chinese numbers when I first started, and now I can comfortably read most basic passages in Chinese (you'll understand when you see the textbook for this course). The teachers are wonderfully warm and friendly, and make the class environment really enjoyable. The workload isn't too heavy/difficult, but definitely requires several hours of commitment per week (especially the homework sets). However, I would say the payoff is amazingly worth it. The fact that you're reading this probably means you're already considering Bx, so go for it!
If you're a heritage speaker that's always wanted to learn to read and write (maybe so that you can tour China without needing your parents' assistance) and are looking for a relatively light 4th or 5th class, this is an amazing option. You'll learn a ton of characters and become a much better and faster reader, while also getting to know much more about Chinese culture and customs. Before I came into this class, my father and I tried going over all the words I knew how to read/write, and there were like a grand total of 50, so don't feel like you need too much preparation coming in.
If you are a heritage speaker, this is a great option, because it tailors to your weaknesses and skill levels quite well.
