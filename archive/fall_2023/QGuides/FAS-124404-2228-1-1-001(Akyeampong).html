What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I entered Hist 1700 with minimal knowledge of African History. Professor Akyeampong's course introduced me to the field through layers of grounding both to African History and the range of methodologies employed for its study. Further, professor Akyeampong acts as the perfect guide. He adjusted his course to fit the rhythms of the semester, along with student interest, while maintaining the rigor and breadth of the material covered. Professor Akyeampong's masterful storytelling and deep knowledge of African History made Hist 1700 an ever–exciting environment to learn and expand my knowledge. Not only do I now have an understanding of the vast array of rich history available to those who look into African history, my knowledge of how history is made––both in universities and in the world––expanded tremendously. I thoroughly encourage anyone looking at Hist 1700 to take the course and anyone interested in history, or understanding global affairs, to look a the course.
Take the course!!!
This is a truly wonderful class. I came into it knowing relatively little about –– especially premodern –– African history, and am leaving it feeling like I both have learned a lot, and that I can appreciate just how much more there is beyond the course materials to learn. In that sense, it's an incredibly successful survey course; wish there were more African hist offerings at Harvard. Prof. Akyeampong is a passionate and truly wonderful lecturer, whose anecdotes and clear interest in the subject will make you care deeply about the class and look forward to lectures. And Grace's endless kindness, generosity with time, and willingness to engage students on tangential threads both is really helpful when preparing for the essay, and makes section a wonderful time. Going to miss them both next semester.

As for workload and all: the readings are considerable, but outside the core lecture and section texts the point is more to understand arguments than to remember dates/names/etc. The exams require some prep, but esp. if you take reading notes it's very manageable, and they'll give you advance notice of the question categories. Just be sure to do some wrist stretches beforehand.
This is a great class to learn about Sub–Saharan African history, but only take it if you really want to learn! You will have to put in a fair amount of work to studying for the exams, but the teachers are really lovely if you do put in the energy.
