This is a very gemmy class. That being said, a huge part of your grade is tethered to active attendance in class (40%) which is completely fine, but difficult to manage when emergencies happen (ie sick, death, etc). You don't even have to talk that much because the Prof gives you an opportunity to engage w the texts through very doable journaling assignments. Honestly, for a class that was taught for the first time this year, it was well done.
EMR 155 was an incredibly valuable experience! I learned so much about sifting through census data and historical sources. The material we were introduced to was gripping and eye–opening. I 100% recommend taking this class.
This is a great course and draws in a wonderful community of students and thinkers! The readings are fairly manageable, but the connections you draw between the material and your own life are what make this course so rewarding. It's definitely a "you get out of it what you put into it" kind of class.
This class could definitely use a little bit of tweaking, but overall it was pretty good. It was my first class in EMR, and compared to the amazing things I had heard from friends about other EMR classes, this one was a little bit disappointing. Having said that, I still definitely learned a lot and enjoyed the class. It really doesn't demand too much time, and class discussions were usually pretty interesting.
This class is good if you want to know more about immigration legislation across time and the non–legislative ways Whiteness was used to mark boundaries.
This is such an amazing class! Prof WS is very engaging and really makes students feel welcome in class. The materials were super interesting and led to fun conversations. The assignments were pretty chill, and the final project was really cool and I had a lot of fun doing it. Would totally recommend :)
This class has great readings and interesting assignments that feel both personally relevant and academically enriching. The discussions in class feel like we were rehashing summaries of the readings instead of actually deeply engaging with the texts. However, I really liked the class and the professor. If you want a manageable class with a great selection of readings, this is a great choice.
Take this course! It is well–structured and teaches you to think critically about place and discrimination. It also teaches you how to dig deeper into archival resources like maps and census data.
TAKE IT!
This course is interesting and I would definitely recommend it, but it is necessary to note that the class deals with a few triggering topics and I wish they had been addressed in a more caring way. The class occasionally felt a little bit more like a history class than I had originally expected, but I learned a lot. Generally, I would say to take this class if you'd like to get a more in–depth understanding of Sundown Towns and spatial discrimination, but know that it can only be so comprehensive. In terms of assignments, the Sundown Town report was an engaging assignment and I think it was very valuable. The readings are not difficult and you get so much more out of the class if you do them, but you can afford not to once in a while.
A good class to take if you are completely unfamiliar with immigration, racial discrimination, etc. in the United States, otherwise, you might get a bit bored
