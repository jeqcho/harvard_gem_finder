This class requires a lot of critical thinking skills along with patience. There will be students with differing opinions from you and this class allows you to vocalize your opinions and discuss why others might think differently than you.
Great class if you put in the work. Doing the readings was important if you wanted to learn anything. Not sure why you wouldn't read, but read!!
Great class if you love politics!
This is one of the only freshmen seminars that count as an elective for government concentrators. If you want to have an easy class that will be actually interesting, with passionate debates, and an excellent professor, this is the way to go. I recommend it if you are taking another time consuming class, such as GOV20 or LS1A. However, if you already have a lot of easy classes, I think this could potentially be a waste of time IF this does not count for your concentration. Overall, I would recommend this to everyone. I left hyper conscious about manipulation in the media, polls, etc, while simultaneously meeting an excellent group of friends.
Make sure to research a topic you are actually interested in! You will spend the entire semester delving into this field so liking what you're writing about helps you to avoid burnout!
