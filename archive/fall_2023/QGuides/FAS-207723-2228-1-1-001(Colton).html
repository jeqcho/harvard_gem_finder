What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in IR, regional cooperation, and comparing the regional bodies of different world regions, then this class is great for you. The readings are a bit comprehensive/long and you should make sure to stay up to date on them, but they are very illuminating. Professor Colton is also very knowledgeable about a huge variety of international regions, countries, and histories, and it was great to learn from him.
If you're interested in IR and political theory, you will love this course. Some experience with political theory can be helpful, but is not required. I went into the course having never taken a government course at Harvard before, although I did enroll in another government course concurrently this semester (which I found helpful). While I had to spend a bit of additional time researching unfamiliar terms and concepts, I found the material accessible and extremely interesting, and I learned a lot from my classmates. Overall wonderful and informative course. Also a great opportunity to do independent research on a region that interests you.
You must be interested in IR theory to take this class, as Professor Colton places a fairly significant emphasis on it.
Take this as a medium–light and engaging gov elective. The first few weeks are more about theory, which can be a bit slow. Starting on week 4, we start to address specific world regions (Middle East, SE Asia, Latin America, etc) one per week. The first hour of class is lecture–style presentations where Professor Colton reviews the history and major dynamics of the region. This is always interesting, and Colton is v knowledgeable. The second hour of class is a group discussion of the readings, but I found this part less helpful cuz the discussions were sometimes a bit slow and the class was a bit too large. Overall, a good class though.
I would encourage them to take this class. Discussions can be very interesting, especially when the class has a diverse set of perspectives and experiences. Its a very solid class and a good Gov 94.
It's a lot of reading, but it'll be worth it if you do it. Start reading for the next week right after class or else you might not finish/have time for the response papers. Professor Colton is amazing, though!
This course was very interesting, and Professor Colton is highly knowledgeable on the subject matter and just great to talk to in general. However, the readings were overwhelming at times, both in terms of difficulty and quantity each week. Assignments were a bit unclear sometimes, so it was hard to gauge what was expected––feedback was helpful, although again, having more guidance would have helped in this aspect as well. Overall, a great course, just requires a lot of time, energy, and critical thinking.
Take it and be prepared to read!
This course is great if you're a regional specialist looking to broaden your cursory understanding of different world regions, or if you're more of a generalist looking to find a region that might interest you in the future. Be aware that there is a good bit of reading and that Professor Colton is much more of a Russia specialist, but the class is definitely worth it if you're looking for a cursory regional comparative overview!
