1. If you are interested in any development related topics, including transportation, schooling, immigration, climate adaptation infrastructure, etc., this course covers frontier research in these topics in a spatial setting.
2. You can learn how to build structural models, how to estimate them, how to conduct empirical analyses, and how to use GIS models, some useful math/modelling tricks. Very worthwhile!
3. The instructor (Gabriel) and the TF (Tilman) teach well–organized materials very clearly.
4. I feel the course is very inspiring.
Great class! Very modeling focused, not necessarily dev focused. Was the hardest class during my semester simply due to the difficulty of papers and subject matter.
Coding skills are needed, otherwise problem sets are not that doable.
Absolutely take this if you're at all interested in transportation, spatial questions, or applied micro as it relates to development. Accessible teaching staff and great readings.
Really interesting class, highly recommend! The problem sets varied a lot in terms of how well set up and labor intensive they were, but you could certainly learn a lot from them if you were willing to put in the time. Lectures were very engaging.
The problem sets are difficult, but useful, and it's okay if you can't get it done all the way. Don't stress too much about the end–of–semester research proposal. This course has a heavy statistical–software coding emphasis.
