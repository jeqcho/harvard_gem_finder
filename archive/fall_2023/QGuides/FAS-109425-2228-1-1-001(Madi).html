I would say that the professor is incredibly nice and focuses more on dialect than other professors.
This course is an excellent course for learning Arabic. The strides that you make in your ability to speak, read, and write Arabic is tremendous. However, the course requires a lot of work. This class will require you to be dedicated to learning Arabic. Expect to put in 10+ hours each week.
This is definitely a high–maintenance class, so you probably shouldn't take it if you're not serious about learning the language. It is a LOT of work. But in my opinion it is so worth it :)
This class is difficult and requires a lot of time from you – you must do the homeworks in order to get the most out of class time. Go to office hours and take advantage of the essay assignments because you get what you put into them! Essays are an opportunity to learn a lot more. Ustaadh Madi is the greatest teacher and the class sessions were energetic and didn’t waste a single minute. I highly recommend this course – I feel prepared to continue with the Arabic language in many directions now.
This is a solid intermediate Arabic course. It does focus mainly on the formal dialect but depending on the teacher, you may also practice spoken dialect. Homework every night certainly adds up time–wise but they're productive. I did think that the course could push us a little bit in terms of improving accuracy and activating vocabulary. The class sometimes slipped into English even when we knew the vocabulary to understand in Arabic.
Take this class!!! This was my first Arabic class at Harvard and it was a fantastic experience. Amr is amazing and makes the class very welcoming and a comfortable learning space. This was my favorite class of my first semester and I highly recommend it to anyone interested in learning Arabic! Hada as–saf mumtaaz!!!
