I enjoyed this class a lot and would highly recommend it.

I came into this without much experience in abstract math and found this course to be a great introduction. We covered linear algebra with a lot of rigor and I really liked the linear map–based approach in contrast to a matrix–centric approach. I definitely learned a lot! Math 121 covers roughly the same material as Math 25, but meets twice a week instead of three times; it works at the same level of rigor, but goes through material a little faster to fit everything in. I found the pace to be quite manageable. Ana is a really wonderful teacher – I've heard nothing but good things about her teaching, and I think that is true. She is incredibly organized and clear in her lectures. When introducing a new topic, she will often gauge the class's familiarity with some basic concepts, and she checks in with the class after the more convoluted proofs, making sure everyone is on the same page. The material is well motivated, rarely feeling arbitrary, which I really appreciated.

The workload of this class was doable, but problem sets did take a fair bit of time to complete. There were a few particularly tricky questions scattered throughout the course, but this is Ana's first time teaching it, so I imagine such things are to be expected. While they were a lot of work, I really did learn a lot from them. One's first foray into abstract math can be difficult, and while the lectures were always understandable, I definitely did not feel solid on the psets at the beginning. I think this is to be expected, it is quite a transition. But learning how to make good use of office hours, and how to collaborate effectively with peers, is an incredibly important skill to learn, and office hours in particular helped me find my footing. So go to office hours, they were incredibly helpful! There are no answer keys for the problem sets, but CAs were always happy to answer any questions I had.

The exams were generally reasonable, although I felt a little short for time when I took the midterms. Her grading was quite fair though. I do have to say though, I was not a huge fan of the grade breakdown for this course – the midterms and the final make up a combined 65% of your grade. Somewhat unfortunate. But oh well.

I will say, if you are thinking about taking this course, check who is teaching it. It can change drastically depending on the Professor – I understand it was quite different last year. But if Ana is teaching it, I'd definitely recommend it! I learned a ton and found it to be a very good course.
This course was a lot of work and there was very little support. No worked solutions and CAs were not very helpful or knowledgeable (partly because of there being no worked solutions). There are also no example questions for exams. If there is another course that can fill your concentration requirement I would probably recommend taking it. Having said that Ana is a lovely professor.
The key to succeeding in this course is staying consistent with studying and reviewing notes/progressing on assignments on a daily basis. It is so easy to fall behind and since the content builds on itself and the course moves at a fast pace, so once you're behind it's very difficult to catch up. Don't cram psets/exam studying, go to office hours, collaborate with peers, PREVIEW material (in Axler textbook so you know what's going on in class) and review afterwards too. It's a hard course but if you put in the time and effort you should be okay.
