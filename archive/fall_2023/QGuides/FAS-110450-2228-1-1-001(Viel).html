This course is confusing. I would really only recommend it to students who for some reason need a biochemistry class other than CHEM 27, like I did since I did organic chemistry during the summer. The assignment structure of this course is excellent, and is the only reason it's feasible to do well in this class. You have a problem set every other week (which in total are worth 50% of your grade!) and since they're fairly easy to score well on if you do good work on them they're phenomenal grade boosters. On non–pset weeks you have SGAs (short graded assessments), which are short, NON–CUMULATIVE quizzes. They're hard, and you have to study hard for them, but since they're non cumulative and you can drop one of the six SGAs just keep your head down and before you know it you can actually stack up some decent grades on them. There's no final, just a group presentation that's completely reasonable.

Now for the hard stuff: lectures are really complicated. They go very in depth and very in detail, and it's impossible to know how much of the material you're responsible for knowing. The course felt like a continuous game of cat and mouse, where every week I was drowning in biochemistry and just had to overstudy to try and do well on psets and SGAs, only to realize that the assignments weren't as difficult as I expected because they didn't go in depth as lectures. The problem is, you never know what's going to be assessed in detail and what's going to be assessed at a more superficial level. Your only hope is that you get a decent TF and some good study buddies and that it works out.

Overall I'd say it is a moderate workload/difficulty class that is a decent option, particularly if it fulfils a requirement.
