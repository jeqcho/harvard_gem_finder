What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Engage in the class and make the most out of the resources available
An excellent course to take in your freshman fall, and especially if it is taught by Jonah Johnson. All your class meetings and conferences will be very interesting and useful! He explains academic writing in a way that makes the process very intuitive and understandable. Additionally, he is very knowledgeable on a wide range of topics (including sports, history, the process of learning, and how languages work), so he can really find mutual interest with each student and build a personal connection. Jonah was extremely understanding when it comes to deadlines and scheduling conferences. He always made sure that Expos work is flexible enough so that it doesn't interfere with important deadlines and exams for the rest of our courses. This course is perfect for freshman fall as it is enjoyable and it has a relatively low amount of work.
This class can be fun if you make it fun , especially if you want to improve your writing skills.
If you're reading this, you're probably trying to decide between Expos 10 and Expos 20. As a student in a similar position, I did find that Expos 10 for me personally was the right choice. While this technically means I have to take an extra class (both expos 10 and 20), I think expos 10 improved my writing ability enough that it actually led me to save time. Expos 20 is a hard class –– so while it may be possible to skip expos 10, skipping may lead to a lot of pain if you've been recommended for expos 10. The workload of expos 10 was a lot more manageable, my instructor was good so the class was enjoyable, and the skills I obtained in the class were genuinely useful.
