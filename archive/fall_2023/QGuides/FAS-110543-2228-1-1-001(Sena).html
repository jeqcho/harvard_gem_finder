What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is amazing if you are looking to learn about literary Chinese, but it will require a serious amount of effort and time. Professor Sena works so hard to give whatever assistance anyone needs and truly upholds the value of "there are no stupid questions." I wish he could teach more than just literary Chinese at Harvard.
Professor Sena is a knowledgeable, personable, and caring instructor, and teaches this unique course with dedication and organization. The pace of the course is fast: it starts easy so that we focus on key grammatical concepts, but picks up pretty quickly, so you know what you're getting into before Add–Drop. This class is a significant amount of work, and it's well worth it. You'll not only learn about how to read classical Chinese, but also learn about the cultural norms of the time and think critically about the art of translation. Don't miss the opportunity to take this course with Professor Sena.
This course has a fairly niche appeal, but if you're thinking about it but nervous about your ability or the instruction, then please give it a try. David Sena is an instructor whose pedagogical abilities I can't praise highly enough: he's extremely knowledgeable, very attentive and responsive, always willing to answer any range of questions, engages very well with students... I can't imagine what a better teacher for the topic would look like, honestly.
I really enjoyed this course to the point where I was looking forward to waking up in the morning to attend! Professor Sena creates an environment in which students felt very comfortable to ask all sorts of questions whether they are regarding the language, cultural context, or other difficult parts that they don't understand, and I felt like I was learning so much from this very friendly and discussion based environment. I usually struggle with language classes but I have not felt this way in this course despite the difficulty of the material, because the vibes are so good and I feel like I can really immerse myself in the content.
This is a pretty hard class, but it's a ton of fun and very much worth it as a way to see the Chinese language in a new light (and the stories are wonderful)!
This class moves fast and is a lot of work but it is amazing and you should take it! It is so cool to understand Chinese more deeply and be able to read the historical texts.
