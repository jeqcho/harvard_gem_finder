The class is great for anyone wanting to learn more about Boston. There were a good amount of readings but they could be skimmed for understanding. The best part of the class is the guest speakers that come in weakly to speak on moments of social change that they were involved with. I walked away with a greater understanding of Boston's context and some general points on leadership. Definitely a good course for someone looking for an engaging and interesting class that will not exhaust you.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Chris, Ira, and Madison are such incredible scholars, and their love of the subject really comes through in the course. This is a great course to do a deep dive in the history of Boston, as well as get some practical experience in through the various field trips and projects. I've always looked forward to this class, and the coursework is very manageable. I really enjoyed doing the essays, especially since I had a lot of freedom to guide my own research and learn more about something that I wanted to learn. This is easily one of my favorite classes that I've taken so far, and I'm so glad that I found it.

Chris is one of the best sociologists in the field, and I feel so sad that he's retiring this year because that means that future students won't get to learn from him. He's very kind and patient, and takes the time to help us truly internalize the course material.

Ira is an amazing professor. He's from Boston and has a lot of practical experience when it comes to the topics we cover, so he offers a lot of knowledge that you wouldn't get otherwise. Moreover, he's the nicest person ever. I was going through a tough time this semester, and he would make sure to check in whenever he saw me, and would even email me sometimes to make sure I was doing okay. He clearly cares very deeply about his students and makes every effort to get to know us as human beings.

Madison is an amazing TF. She would always be available and was very patience and accommodating. She would make sure to send us reminders to keep us on track with the coursework, and took the time to check in if she noticed anyone was missing an assignment.
This class was absolutely amazing!! We had a slew of just unbelievable guest speakers who shared all sorts of wonderful insight with us about how to actually craft meaningful policy change. Ira and Chris are wonderful and kind and offer tons of support.

I loved the ways that the class encouraged me to actually get out into the community, too! The field trips were super fun, and shadowing a city leader was so meaningful and very cool.

I wish Harvard had more courses like this!!
I think that every Harvard student should take this course! It teaches you so much about Boston's history and the shadowing assignment is great!
I know you will read this a lot but I cannot recommend this class enough. In a liberal arts school curriculum that is often very theoretical, this course meshes interesting theory directly to case studies on Boston and important social issues. Every week, you read about an important case (ex. same–sex marriage in Boston, Boston Catholic Church sex abuse scandal, etc.) and then meet a leader who directly lead the effort. You meet politicians, journalists, activists, and all in between. The midterm assignment is to shadow a leader, and the professors help you find really top–level people (it was my favorite assignment in college!). Professors Winship and Jackson are so nice, dedicated, and kind. I felt so relaxed during this class and learned so much (a perfect combo). This is not a hard–skills course – so you won't come away with hard rules – but you will definitely learn about the art of leadership and its impacts on society. 10/10 easily.
YOU SHOULD DEFINITELY TAKE THIS CLASS – it was one of my favorite at Harvard. You will (1) learn a lot about Boston, (2) get to listen to incredible speakers each week and ask them questions and (3) learn from two brilliant teachers at the top of their game, Professors Winship and Jackson. The course work is reasonable (a weekly discussion post, midterm paper, and final paper) and you will learn so much. Just take it!
If you have the chance to take this class absolutely do, it was one of the most engaging, interesting, and thought–provoking classes I have taken at Harvard. Every speaker was absolutely great and both Professor Winship and Ira Jackson were wonderful. It is not that much work and really just amazing. I can not more highly recommend it.
Get ready to be blown away!!! Treasure every lecture, you will get to learn firsthand from some of the preeminent leaders in the Boston community, as well as two incredible professors who complement one another perfectly.
Professor Winship mentioned in our last class that this course exemplifies what all classes at Harvard should aim to achieve to realize the colleges mission: applied liberal arts and I could not agree more. Both Ira and Chris care very deeply about this class and that shines through the leaders that are invited to the class and the way we engage with the course content. One of the few classes at Harvard where I get to think deeply about my passion and how to use them to effect change. This has, no doubt, deepened my connections with Boston, geographically and community wise––which I think is so important for all of us as students dedicating such a huge part of our lives in should too. Please do yourselves a favor and take this class––hands down the most meaningful class I have taken from my time here. Can not recommend this highly enough!
This class is amazing. If they teach it again, definitely take it. Ira and Chris are so devoted to the class and the speakers that they get are unbearable. Could not recommend more.
Take this class! The assignments are not too difficult and most of the readings are articles or case studies, and are pretty easy. The course staff is really strong.
This course was a transformational experience. Getting to meet and pick the brains of influential Bostonian leaders led to a much fuller understanding of the power dynamics at play in Boston. And this experience came with layers, the first being the guest lecturers every week representing a new aspect of social change. The second were the hands on experiences with shadowing leaders and going on field trips to see some of the work we learned about in action. Third, the teachers are both influential leaders in their own right. Ira Jackson and Chris Winship are both titans of their fields who are eager and excited to provide an experience that outshines the rest of Harvard's course catalogue handily.
This is the best course I've taken at Harvard. You learn so much about how the place you're spending your college years has been shaped and you get the chance to hear from some of the people that helped shape it.
