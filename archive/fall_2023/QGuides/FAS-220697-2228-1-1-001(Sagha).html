What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this course if you're interested in Iran/Middle East! Assignments are reasonable and fair (I'm taking this as a stem concentrator), and I could not emphasise more that Prof Sagha is a really knowledgeable lecturer who is also a good speaker and teacher, which makes the lecture experience really fruitful.
This class is a great opportunity to understand history in a practical, locally focused sense. If you are focused in the type of history that focuses on tracing different ideas over time through a single place (as opposed to tracing one idea across time and place), this is a great course!
Lecture might seem like it’s a lot, but it contains very important elements you don’t want to miss out on!
This class is an excellent introduction to the modern history of Iran. If you are interested in this section of history an wish to understand the history of Iran not only through the facts but through the religious, mystical, and philosophical currents that push history forward within the country then this class is appropriate. Professor Sagha is not only extremely knowledgeable on these topics but is warm, welcoming, and is always available to talk about your questions or concerns.
If you want a deep dive on modern Iran and a firm grasp on relations in the Middle East, you should definitely take this course. It will also teach you A LOT about Islam.
Overall, I would highly recommend this course to anyone who is interested in learning about the history of Iran. I feel like I learned a lot, and I would definitely take the course again in the future. Take a course with Professor Sagha if you can!!! This was the first essay–based class I took since Freshman year EXPOS and it was entirely manageable, the Professor will work closely with you to make sure you are succeeding in the class.
If you are interested in Middle East culture or geopolitics, this is an awesome class. I learned SO much (I knew very little about Iran before the course). The workload is relatively low with only three assignments (response paper, midterm paper, and final paper). Professor Sagha is passionate about his realm of study and truly cares about his students. He allowed us to suggest prompts for our final paper other than the ones provided, was very encouraging and supportive when I wanted to explore something we had only briefly mentioned in class. I highly recommend, but only take this if you are interested in Iran from a theological perspective, religion, and Islamic ideology.
