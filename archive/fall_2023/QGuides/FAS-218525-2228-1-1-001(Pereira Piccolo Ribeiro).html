If you have any interest in mental illness, take this class! Even though you explore mental illnesses mostly from a reward processing perspective, you learn a LOT about experimental methods in psychological research, and it turns out reward processing is actually a lot more broad than you may initially think. I was initially hesitant that the class would be too "bias" in that reward processing perspective, but Mayron does a great job in facilitating discussion beyond that scope. You explore a wide variety of disorders, and I often found myself thinking about the concepts in this class even outside of my work for the class. Highly recommend! Not super time intensive and really interesting!
I highly recommend this course!! If you are interested in cognitive psychology but don't have the strongest neuroscience background, PSY 1816 is absolutely fantastic.
This class is a gem in the way that it is very enjoyable and you will easily get a good grade since the professor wants you to succeed. The course is truly structured to make it so that you do not have too much on your plate at once so that you are actually learning the course content. I'd definitely recommend this class to anyone particularly interested in research on reward processing, psychopathology, neuroimaging (especially fMRI/dMRI), and just generally understanding different types of psychology research.
If you are interested in learning the relationship between reward–processing and mental illness, take this class.
