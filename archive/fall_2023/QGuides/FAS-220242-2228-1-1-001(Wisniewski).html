It's a great course, and it's a lot of fun to be exposed to a lot of comedy. Just know that it's definitely geared more towards people who are really interested in comedy and analyzing comedy, as opposed to merely enjoying it passively.
If you are interesting in 21st century comedy, like I was, know that this class does not focus on that at all. But I still loved this class. We read a lot of interesting works, but do know that you have to very interested in comedy to take this class as the readings are AT LOT. Other than that, this class is great. The professor is very nice and this class attracts a great group of people. The response posts that you have to do every week may be slightly annoying but you can get them done in 15–30 minutes. Also you can have a lot of fun with the final project
Take this course if you’d like an academic approach to comedy. It’s definitely a fun class for the right type of person. If you’re expecting to sit around and watch standup all class, this won’t be it. Rather than wit irony comedy, I’d advertise this as a comedic literature class. I did really enjoy the final project and final paper. Thomas is a great prof and a nice grader. You’ll enjoy this if you put in the work.
This is a good course. If you don't like reading (and writing to a certain extent), though, you may not enjoy it. The majority of the readings for homework are dense and dry analysis of comedy and can prove quite tedious to read. If you're surrounded by cool people, though, this course can be really fun! I found some really fun people and can't wait to work with them later!
Loved this class. While there were times in the course where the workload was a lot, it was worth it when we got to discussions.
TAKE. THIS. COURSE. I have never been so excited for a three–hour class in my life. The other students in the class are brilliant and hilarious, Thomas is a wise and kind professor, and the homework is fascinating and entertaining. Truly my favorite class of all time.
