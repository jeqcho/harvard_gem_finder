What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is probably the best Chem class I have ever taken. Part of that could be because I always liked inorganic better than organic, but I truly had a wonderful time learning about metal complexes and small molecule activation. The psets are concise and make you understand what you should know without being excessive. Some psets require the reading or skimming of 15 papers, but at the end of the semester, my ability to read and understand Chem papers drastically increased from not having read a single Chem paper before this class to reading around 30 for my final project, and it not taking too long. Also, you will get the chance to see that molecules can be engineered beyond just their sterics, and the actual electronic structure can be tuned to give the desired reactivity. The exams are stressful, but understanding mistakes on psets will greatly help with preparation. Ted gives very good lectures, and gave me a new perspective on Chem and research as a whole.
This is a very enjoyable course with a supportive profesor and TF.
Fantastic survey of small molecule activation literature. I didn't have enough time during the class to go over all the interesting papers mentioned in class, but the notes serve as a great reference to read those in future. Made me consider getting into the field as a scientist.
Enjoy it!
Go ahead and take it.
