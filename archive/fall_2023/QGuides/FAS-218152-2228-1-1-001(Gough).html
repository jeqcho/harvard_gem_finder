I recommend this course to everyone! Professor Gough and TF Rachel are incredible lecturers and section leaders. They lead discussions with so much knowledge and made Art History very approachable to those who hadn't done it before. Learning Modern Art by considering the colonial matrix changes the narrative completely and it's especially important as the art world reconciles its past. You'll find the readings of Aime Cesaire, Hanna Azoulay, and others, thought–provoking in terms of how the discipline have evolved. I cannot recommend a course more. I always looked forward to coming to class!
Take this course! At its core, it's a survey course on modern art, but it frames the history of modern art through the lens of how artists engaged with colonization and modernization in the 19th and 20th centuries. For instance, you will learn about Picasso, but also his appropriation of African masks and objects to make works that sell for millions of dollars. Professor Gough is kind, knowledgeable, and cares very deeply about her students.
This is a phenomenal course. The lectures are so interesting & workload is not bad at all.
Great introduction to art history for a student with little humanities background. Professor Gough's lecture style is engaging and enjoyable and Rachel provided excellent support throughout the semester.
TAKE THIS CLASS! This was an amazing introduction to modern art, and Prof. Gough is one of the best lecturers at this school. This class completely changed my perspective on some of the most famous artists in history by putting them in the context of colonialism. There are also several museum field trips where you basically get a private tour of the MFA by a leading world expert on modern art (prof. Gough). The readings and workload are all very manageable and fair, and there is significant creative freedom to study the aspects of modern art most interesting to you.
It's a good class and the content is interesting but it takes itself way too seriously.
Professor Gough is a wonderful lecturer! This goes over many of the greatest hits of modern art while also placing the artworks in context of broader trends of cultural appropriation/historical context etc. I appreciated that arguments were set forth by the readings, but Professor Gough made it clear we did not necessarily need to agree with the argument. We were encouraged to think critically about its merits and drawbacks. On the whole, I enjoyed the class!
Please take this class even if you don't have any art history background, or don't plan on taking any other classes on the subject! It does a fantastic job in introducing students to the highlights of modern art while evaluating and enabling students to take on a critical perspective towards the motives and intentions behind each work as they are implicated in colonialist history. It's a fairly low–pressure class, with a couple of readings each week and a few written assignments towards the end (for which you will receive lots of guidance). The variety of artists, mediums and locations viewed in the class make it rich and always interesting, and the professor and TF care a lot about their students. This class will change the way you appreciate art forever!
Highly recommend this class to anyone with an interest in modern art. It offers such a broad yet detailed survey of the key protagonists in European modernism using an important critical lens that will change how you look at all art from the period and beyond.
Few assignments, but they were time intensive
If you were going to take one art history class as a non–specialist, this should be it.
MUST TAKE CLASS! Really changes your perspective of how artists draw inspiration and how you interact with objects in a museum space.
Enjoy every moment! I miss this class already
This was my first HAA class, and it was an amazing intro to modern art. It took me a little while to get the hang of formal analysis, which was needed in the exhibition proposal assignments, but we did some practice in sections. I really enjoyed the museum visits because the works (especially paintings) were so different in person, but they were a little strict about attendance. Overall would definitely recommend for anyone interested in modern art or colonialism.
This is an incredible class! Teaching staff is so genuinely invested in your learning. Getting to look at art and learn about how each of the artists we learn about navigated/viewed their individual involvement within colonialism is really eye–opening. Also so cool to be able to look at many of the works we learn about in person through museum visits, which I think are so rare and underutilized in so many classes. An introductory course that will leave you with an incredible foundation in art history.
This class can tackle complicated, problematic histories and students should be prepared for that.
