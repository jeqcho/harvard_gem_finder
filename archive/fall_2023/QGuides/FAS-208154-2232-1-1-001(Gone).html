As long as you do the readings and engage in discussions, you will do well in this class. There will be many novel ideas presented, so come with an open mind and you may gain some valuable insights.
This class is boring. It's 2.75 hours at a time and it's dry as heck. Prof. Gone dropped the R–word multiple times the first two lectures (referencing it in the DSM – FYI it isn't used in the DSM 5, and massive social campaigns have been done as this is a wildly inappropriate word). All the readings feel the same: unless you're so interested in the topic you want to pick apart psychological minutia, it's excruciating.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It's one of the most well–structured, educational, inspiring, comprehensive, rewarding courses you will ever enroll in at Harvard. Professor Gone is truly an unsung hero in the field of clinical psychology, and the incredible speakers he brings into the course are only a small testament to the rich plethora of networks and accolades credited to his name. This course was exemplary of the kind of stellar educational experience I was most excited for when coming to this school. You will get so much out of this class and emerge all the better for it.
You need definitely some sort of fluency or at least great interest in mental health diagnosis/term and a willingness to engage really deeply with a pretty narrow range of subject matter. The assignments aren't too difficult, but the course can be really emotionally difficult at times, especially if you have mental health issues of your own; having diagnostic categories questioned with such persistence can be a little, or a lot, destabilizing. That said, this course gave me so many vital re–thinks about how mental health issues are conceptualized in modern times and how treatment should be navigated, and (as many anthro classes tend to) exploded my preconceived notion that certain things just "are" the way they are, and weren't constructed to be so. Long story short, it's a heavy experience but the hours you put in won't be too many and you'll learn a ton about really important stuff.
Highly recommended if you are interested in Indigenous studies and/or mental health. Professor Gone is wonderful and he will invest a lot in your learning if you put in the work. This course will make me a better mental health professional. 10/10
