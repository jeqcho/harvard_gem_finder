What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I believe this class was a one–off class taught by a guest lecturer, so these comments may not be that useful for the future. This iteration of 268 was quite challenging and involved very advanced material. It really is information theory for physicists, so expect everything from a physical perspective. The homework assignments were reasonable, and our TF Saranesh was also reasonable about them. The lectures were pretty tough to understand in person, and their content pretty much follows from the lecture notes/textbook, but those aren't written very well, so it was hard to understand what was going on at times.
To understand the physics behind this course, you need to be completely fluent in statistical mechanics. However, to understand the information theory, you need almost no background.
Prof. Falkovich's version of Physics 268aR has a great lecture note and wonderful material. However, I found the lectures quite confusing oftentimes, although I had a good preparation in terms of physics prerequisite before taking this class. Since the TF in our year was amazing, I recommend this class as you will learn some interesting contents in information theory and how it is related to physics. However, you might not learn a lot directly from the lecture but more from the TF or the lecture note.
