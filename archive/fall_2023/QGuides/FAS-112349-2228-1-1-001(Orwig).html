However, if you think that missing three weekends of the first semester of college may detriment your college experience, maybe take a closer examination into whether this course would be worthwhile for you to take.
Take this seminar!!! You'll likely never again have the opportunity to take a similar class. It's amazing to get off campus for a weekend each month, and you'll probably make lasting friendships with the people you share the experience with. Plus, the content is interesting and you'll meet a lot of different professionals who are involved at the Forest. Definitely the best, most unique freshman seminar, and an unforgettable experience!
If your up for a hike and up to learn about our Forest and how it is impacting the NE and the world this is the class for you. PS its on the weekends only.
This is the best class you could take in your first year if you are interested in climate change/ecology or just enjoy nature. You learn a lot while sharing with other students and having fun during the field trips. It is truly a unique experience. It provides moments to recharge outside the regular campus environment. It does entail being organized with your work as you will be out during the whole weekend once a month, but it's definitely managable and 1000% worth it. Definitely the best freshman seminar you could take in order to help your first semester be more relaxed while learning a lot.
TAKE THIS CLASS. It is one of the most memorable experiences of my first semester at Harvard and there are only good things to be said about this course and professor.
Take it! It was truly one of the best parts of my Harvard experience thus far.
I cannot stress this enough, TAKE THIS CLASS!!! A lot of freshman seminars aren't as life–changing as they seem––except this one. You learn so much without the readings or work required by other freshman seminars. The only time you meet as a class is once a month for a weekend in your new happy place, the Harvard Forest, where you learn about the actual natural world around you almost entirely hands–on. It also is an amazing way to adjust to your first semester in college as you basically get a vacation as a class. You also bond greatly with your classmates, and I've had some of my best experiences of my freshman year so far with this class. Dave is of course amazing as well and makes the class even more enjoyable. This class is the "Transformative Experience" they always talked about, and it has single–handedly convinced me to double major in IB. If you enjoy escaping the business and stress of campus for a lovely weekend where you get to go on walks and learn about how amazing trees are, this class is for you!!
BEST CLASS AT HARVARD! You don't meet during the week and go to the forest once a month from Friday–Sunday, going on hikes to learn about trees and stuff. Homework is a 1–2 page writeup for each weekend at the forest usually due the following Sunday, and doesn't take too much time at all. The final project is not too bad, and Dave Orwig is an excellent teacher! You form a great community with other people in the class too.
If you are at all interested in forests, ecology, or the outdoors—take this class! This is the best class I've ever taken. Imagine an engaging lecture/discussion with a small group, but you get to be outdoors the entire time. You learn concepts by actively seeing them in action and engaging with the researchers. Dave is so knowledgeable, approachable, and genuinely kind. He is passionate about the subject and it carries over to all his students.
