It is a great class that really allows you to explore statistics in this setting. Although a lot of work, it is a pretty fun class!
I would tell them to take this class knowing a replication paper ahead of time or to be strategic and take this course with friends you want to coauthor something on. I'd also recommend taking gov2002 before taking this course or be really comfortable or have a tutor who is going to engage with the material behind the lectures.
Expect to put in a lot of work for this course; I spent more on this course than all my other courses combined. You will sink/swim based on a good problem set group and the dedication/ability of the TFs.
Great class, the TFs were wonderful in helping students with no background in R. Do make sure to make a pset group very early on. Prof. King’s lectures are very interesting but the psets can be challenging.
If someone wants a serious statistical methods course, I would strongly discourage them from taking GOV 2001. This class attempts to be both an overview of the research process and a survey of statistical methods. Yet it accomplishes neither well. It is bogged down by busy work and understaffing. And when it does engage seriously with statistical methods, it often presents models and approaches that are outside of the mainstream of applied political science and economics.
This class is only bearable if you create a helpful study group that will be dedicated to working together on the course material every week. It's important to choose your study group wisely!
Do not take it and wait for GOV2002 instead. If you want to take a methods class in your first semester, look at MIT's offerings.
Go to OH every week!
This is a great class that develops a really practical and useful skillset for future studies. It has a lot of moving components, so you do have to be on–top of your work, but if you put in the time, it is very rewarding. You definitely get more out the course with the more work you put in.
This course teaches you to do meaningful statistical data analysis using the R programming language through entertaining lectures, but course requirements can be uncomfortably exhausting and rigid if you have any other commitments. If you don't attend Harvard and are just looking for a graduate elective to broaden your knowledge, it might be better to first search for a similar course within your own school.
The class is undoubtedly very difficult and its time consuming nature, but I found this a very fulfilling experience. I felt very supported by the instructor and the TAs, who went out of their way to help us learn. It provides essential background to the methods used in political science and improves your quantitative intuition. While you should definitely take that you have enough bandwidth to take this class in terms of time, every aspiring political scientist should take this course at least once while they have the opportunity.
The concepts covered in this class are important; however, they are covered at too fast of a pace to allow for meaningful learning of the course material. This class has a "more is more" attitude — rather than ensuring students understand a few concepts really well, students face an onslaught of material and just have to hope and pray that some of it sticks.
I overall liked the class, but the material wasn't new for me. I picked up R skills and wrote a co–authored paper. There's too many assignments, you'll spend most of your semester doing assignments for this class.
This isn't really an introductory course and you will need to work hard with other people and go to office hours if you are going to be successful in this course. It requires a lot logistically and you need to make sure that you're organized from the jump to figure out all of the different deadlines and assignments throughout the week. It's not that easy and it will probably start to make sense near the end, but it really requires you to be very comfortable with coding and moderately comfortable with statistics and math if you want to understand most of it. There's a fair amount of your time that will feel wasted by this class. The teaching staff is really kind and wants you to succeed, so it's worth it to take if only because you'll get to know wonderful people who will be good advisors and mentors later on.
You have to take this class, so you're going to have to take it! The weekly snacks are fun and effective as a memory tool. Be really careful about who you choose to be in your replication paper group – this can actually have some impact on whether you have a fun or miserable semester. Choosing the three best coders in your class is NOT the right strategy because what makes a good group project is having a group of people who work well together, don't shirk responsibility, and act maturely NOT knowing how to do data mining or build an R package.
As I said above, I would recommend with reservations. It has some wonderful qualities, which I already mentioned, and some serios shortcomings.
