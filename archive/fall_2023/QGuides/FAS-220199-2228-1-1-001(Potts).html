This is a great Freshman Seminar! It won't demand too much of your time and encourages you to think deeply about grief, loss, climate change, and morality. The selection of texts and films you'll explore are broad and diverse, and they all provide new insight on the course's topics. Professor Potts is truly amazing as well. He's really warm and welcoming and will never cease to reshape your perceptions and valuations of texts and the world around you.
If you have a chance to take this seminar, please do! The course material touches upon pertinent questions of hope, loss, faith, and responsibility, many of which are relevant to key social issues in today's world. Professor Potts is incredibly warm and encouraging, and facilitates wonderfully thought–provoking discussion every class. You can engage with the material however much/little you want to, but if you put in the time and engage in class discussion with your peers, the class is extremely worthwhile and really pushes you to dig deeper into the purest questions of love and grief.
I couldn't recommend this course more. I'm only a freshman so I can't speak on most Professors at the college, but I wouldn't be surprised if I come away from these four years thinking Professor Potts was one of the best there are. He's incredibly talented at guiding discussion and getting us to the most thought–provoking question at the heart of the material for the week. He guides conversation without dominating— this class is very much a seminar and students do the bulk of the talking. Assigned readings or movies were always so interesting and fun to read/watch. I learned so much and changed my way of thinking and looking at the world through this course. I genuinely looked forward to the two hour classes every week. Absolutely take this course.
This class is a great freshman seminar. Professor Potts is very kind and very knowledgeable. I felt I learned a lot.
Taking this class was absolutely the best choice I've made so far at Harvard. Professor Potts is an incredible and dedicated professor, and his passion for the subject matter is abundantly clear. Though it deals with heavy handed topics, as implied by its grim name, these topics are so important in understanding our future and how we can shape it.
If you're the sort who loves late–night conversations about subjects that are fundamental to human existence – love, loss, hope, grief, for example – and loves dissecting media for its themes a bit too much, then you will absolutely love this course. Every week, we read a new text (mostly fiction, which includes films) and have a table–discussion about it for 2 hours, book club style; and while discussions are fun, the best part is always listening to Matt's own interpretations which range from thought–provoking to mind–blowing.

That said, because of the emphasis on discussion, if you want to take this course, it'd be a benefit to everyone if you actually read the weekly readings and engage in thoughtful discussion. For that reason, I think there's some level of work expected to be put into the course – some of the novels can be pretty long (I learned to be a faster reader for this class alone)! But the readings are mostly pretty fun to read (and if they aren't just know that Matt's take on said reading will probably completely change your perspective on it).

The content of the course tends to be rather heavy, but I think an existential crisis or two is worth the deep inspection of truths about humanity and society that is often so sobering and clarifying. I personally would say that some of the revelations can be life–changing.

All in all, I strongly, STRONGLY recommend this course – but with the caveat that you must be ready to read the weekly readings. It's what makes it fun, anyway!
This was an incredible class that I would recommend you take. Professor Potts is one of the kindest and most understanding teachers I've ever met. Class discussions with him and other students are always a super fun and enlightening experience (albeit sometimes depressing because our discussions are about apocalypse and grief...). If you're looking for a seminar to try out your first year and in a small classroom discussion setting, 10/10 would recommend it!
This class is amazing! Matt is incredible and so so passionate about the topic. The name of the class sounds a little sad at first but we actually talked a lot about apocalypse in terms of climate change. It also may sound like there's a lot of religious content, but there's not that much at all and I'm not religious at all and still enjoyed the class immensely. The assignments are super chill, the only work you really have to do is read the book/watch the movie we're going to discuss before class! Reading one book in a week might seem like a lot but I found myself actually using it as a break for my other work. In class, you just sit and talk about the book and our reactions and that's it! It's super cozy and if you like reading and analyzing books and movies this class will be really fun for you!
