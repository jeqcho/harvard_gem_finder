I LOVED this class! I would 100% recommend! No particular background needed, but you should be very passionate about the material!
This course is definitely a gem of Harvard. The students who take it are so engaged and knowledgeable about fashion across time, and so the class discussions are always lively and participatory (no silent, awkward classroom vibes). Professor McCormick and Carolyn, our TF, also poured so much of their hearts into making the class experience flow super smoothly, and really encouraged us to think across weeks in the course, so it definitely is one to take if you would like to engage in synthesis–level thinking. The course staff were also super reachable outside of class and receptive to student ideas. This is also a really accessible introduction to HAA –– I had never taken an HAA course before taking this course. Take this course if you have any interest in fashion, embodiment, and body.
If you're interested in fashion and/or clothing, take this class.
This course gives an excellent overview of Japanese fashion history. Both Professor McCormick and our TF are extremely knowledgable and helpful.
It's an interesting class but with a strong focus on theoretical over material and I often found myself getting lost (despite having background in fashion). With this specific group of students, discussions got unnecessarily intense and could be draining. I wouldn't say skip the course just because of these things, but honestly it wasn't really what I expected or wanted. It just depends on your goals in taking the course. It's also relatively easy. It's not a bad course. Just not really for me.
Loved the course – was a bit difficult grasping the theories and keeping up with other members of the class who seemed to know so much about fashion, but teachers were responsive and engaging, and the course gives you a chance to be creative and explore something different and really interesting.
This class is AMAZING! If you have the opportunity to take it, do it. You learn so much in such as small amount of time. This was in my top three favorite classes taken at Harvard.
I highly recommend this course if you are interested in fashion or history. While I didnt have a strong background in either, the course was an excellent gateway. Every lecture was interesting and I felt like the Professor was always eager to answer our questions or promote discussion. The section is honestly the best part of the course. Getting to view garments in museums or doing hands on fabric dyeing added so much to the experience.
