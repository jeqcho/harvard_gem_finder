It is a great class, but not a gem. It definitely involves a considerable amount of work, particularly if you are not used to reading literature and writing about it at an undergraduate level. I would definitely advise you to take it only if you are willing and capable of putting in the time. After all, the reading is the entire class, so if you don't do it then there's no point in taking it. The workload is manageable though, an average of around 100 pages a week. Depending on how fast and carefully you read, that can take different amounts of time.

I am very grateful I had the opportunity to read War and Peace with others, and think Professor Buckler and the course staff were amazing. I highly recommend it if you want to fulfill your distributional requirement for Arts and Humanities. If you are not super comfortable with literary writing, I would advise you to take it pass fail if you just want a great experience but don't want to risk getting a lower grade.

I came into the class thinking reading it might be a bit of a slog, but I actually found it very readable and entertaining. Professor Buckler is fantastic and extremely knowledgeable about the novel and Russian literature more generally. I can't recommend this class enough.
I would highly recommend this course to anyone at Harvard. You learn lots about yourself and the world reading War and Peace, and Professor Buckler really brings the book to life in class.

This class is truly fantastic. If you have ever had any interest in reading War and Peace or even just want to read Tolstoy, take this class. The workload is really reasonable and I enjoyed doing the readings for it each week. The professor is really amazing and the course staff gives thoughtful feedback on discussion posts, essays, etc. This is by far the best course I've taken at Harvard and prior to seeing it in the course catalog I never had any interest in reading the book. Take it and enjoy Tolstoy –– he might change your life.

The book is wonderful, and being able to read it at a guided pace with someone pointing out key themes/questions/characters throughout really makes it a lot easier to digest and finish the novel. Definitely take it, very good way of reading a book you should definitely read at least once
Take this class! War and Peace is a BEAST of a book and having the structure of a class is super helpful in being held accountable and actually reading it. This is the kind of class though that it is very difficult to catch up in if you do fall behind, as there is about 100 pages of reading each week, which is manageable if you plan your week right and set aside a few hours on sunday or break it up throughout the week. The discussions in class are very interesting and Professor Buckler provides historical background that adds an additional layer of meaning to the reading. The TFs are also wonderful and sections fly by so quickly. This was an incredibly enjoyable experience for me and I highly recommend the class to anyone remotely interested in reading, Russian literature, or just fulfilling the arts & humanities distribution requirement. As a stem concentrator, I can confidently say that I really enjoyed the class, even while taking other, more time consuming courses at the same time (ie organic chemistry), and I would take this course over again if I could. War and Peace is a classic for a reason and you get so much out of the book not only from just reading it but discussing the plot, Tolstoy's writing, historical events, and even seeing how universal the human experience can be in things like love, death, and family relations.
If you're like me, and have been wanting to read War and Peace but just haven't gotten around to it, I couldn't recommend this class enough! Not only was it wonderful to finally get through this novel, but having a class setting in which we could engage with it in a more critical manner was also wonderful. I also thought the class moved at a really good pace, so there was never more reading assigned than I was reasonably able to get through. Prof. Buckler is also just a wonderful professor – she really encourages class discussion and makes sure everyone feels welcome and encouraged to participate, which made the class a very collaborative and welcoming space overall.
I strongly recommend this class to students. This course is extremely interesting and rewarding. What better place to get the most out of "War and Peace" than with a passionate, knowledgeable professor at Harvard.

TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Buckler is amazing and the TFs this year (Geordie and Nathan) were also fantastic. When else will you get the chance to read a text like War and Peace in a community setting and at such a close level? One of the most rewarding courses of my college career.
