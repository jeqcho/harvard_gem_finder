Katarina is absolutely AMAZING and if you're even on the fence about taking an AFVS class, please start with this one. It's a lighter courseload than some other AFVS classes in the department and is a great introduction. We had lots of creative freedom and Katarina provides great advice and feedback. The course also provides art supplies, invited live models for gestures, and allowed us to play with so many different mediums. We also got the chance to visit the art museum during class and visit exhibitions! 100% would recommend, but it is a class where you get what you put into the course. Sure, you can slack off, but it's not worth it.

Take this class! You'll have a great time and get to talk about and create artwork!
A great course for anyone who has an affinity for drawing they want to expand upon. The class teaches you how to look at art and widens your artistic pallet, introduces you to new artists and ideas, and helps refine your technique and skill. The course begins with drawing exercises on perspective, shadow etc. including several figuring drawing sessions with models. The later part of the course focuses on creating pieces and ends with the final project, an opportunity to create and curate a body of work. I learned a lot from my classmates, who all had their unique styles and directions that they developed over the class. Katarina does a great job of helping you break out of your habits and expand your visual vocabulary with very personalized feedback. This class also has a great atmosphere and benefits from its small size. It can be a lot of work, but if you are into drawing and can fit this in your schedule it’s good homework to be doing since all the assignments involve actually drawing (rather than writing or whatnot). Also you get free materials. Take it!
