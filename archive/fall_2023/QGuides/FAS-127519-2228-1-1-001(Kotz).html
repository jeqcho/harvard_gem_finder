Awesome course taught by an awesome professor. Would absolutely recommend if you are choosing an economics writing course.
A great class with lots of width to write about the topic that you find interesting. Prof Kotz is a lovely teacher who is more than happy to meet for office hours and will send a TON of material to you for your essays. Not much work to do in the class other than the two writing assignments. But beware, these assignments are pretty hefty and should not be left to the last minute. Final project is a 20–page research paper, so give yourself a lot of time for that (I did not).
This class actually applies economics to the real world, which is really cool! I learned a lot about the EU and the teaching staff (i.e. Professor Kotz and TF Eni) were always willing to help at OH.
It's a very cool class that gives you a lot of freedom to explore your own interests: there are almost no restrictions for the choice of topic for either one of the two papers. You also get to learn a lot about how the EU's unique institutional structure and characteristics influence its way of dealing with various crises. Definitely get started on the term paper as soon as possible, in order to get feedback from the course staff! For the papers: be as specific as possible and narrow as possible when choosing your topic.
This class is incredible! Absolutely take it. It was so interesting and engaging, and I really loved the material. The prof is very excited as well and a great person to learn from. I think it was so worth it and a really interesting way to look at econ from a new perspective.
start writing the 20 page essay early
Take this course!!! Professor Kotz is a great professor and you can tell that he's very passionate about what he's teaching and genuinely cares about his students. The response paper and final paper may seem daunting at first, but there is a lot of support from the teaching team through office hours, so long as you reach out to them! The academic conference is also not something you should be afraid of, but instead use as an opportunity to get useful feedback from the teaching team and your peers! Overall, I feel like I learned a lot in this course about the economics behind the EU, as well as really interesting details about current events in Europe. I definitely recommend this course to anyone who is either a. interested in economics' extension to Europe, or b. trying to find an interesting and fulfilling way to complete the econ writing requirement.
Professor Kotz is one of the most available professors at Harvard. He is always more than happy and willing to meet for OH. The feedback he provides on assignments is extensive – it shows a lot of care for his students and interest for the subject matter that he teaches. The final paper is a lot (16+ pages), but given that this class doesn't have weekly problem sets, nor exams, I think that it's a fair ask. Besides, there's a wealth of resources at your disposal, and paper "milestones" along the way which make it manageable.
Take this class! Professor Kotz is a great educator that genuinely cares about his students. He consistently makes efforts to meet with and engage with everyone, and it's honestly really refreshing to see a professor care so much. There's so much support for the two big assignments, and you're given a lot of academic liberty in deciding what you what your final term paper to be about. Take this class if you're interested at all in European economics and want to learn from the best of the best. :)
