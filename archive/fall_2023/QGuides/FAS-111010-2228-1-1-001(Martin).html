This course provides good instruction for integration, series, and differential equations, and fills in gaps in understanding even for those with prior calculus experience. The section–based structure of the course is quite helpful, as are office hours and the Math Question Center (the MQC can be very busy, but it is useful for identifying misunderstandings).
This course is a lot of work, so be prepared! In fall 2022, there were 3 PSETS a week (33 total). But, if you start the PSETS early (i.e., right after class) and go to office hours, you can learn so much math and do well. The teaching in this course is FANTASTIC! I had Amadeus, and he taught us everything in class, without anyone ever having to use the textbook. Additionally, unlike other classes that are graded on a curve, where the top X percent gets a certain grade, this course is structured so that it rewards understanding. If you work hard and learn the material, you will do very well, regardless of how others are doing. If you are on the fence about which math class to take Freshman fall, math 1B is definitely up there for the most work, but the grading is much better than some of the classes that require less work (i.e., Math 21A, 22, or 25).

If you come in with a weaker math background, the course may feel undoable at the start, but KEEP WITH IT! You will eventually get to the other side and be so glad you stuck with it.

Math 1b is certainly not easy with 3 PSets a week and very challenging concepts to understand, but if you zoom out and take a big picture of everything before diving back into the details, it really helps out. It will require a lot of independent review and effort to do well in this class and understand all of the concepts if you are not quick to understand math concepts, and the questions will be application–based, sometimes what you have never seen before, but if you keep the basic foundation of concepts in mind and make sure to get lots of help from the professor and TFs, you will do well. Never hesitate to reach out over email as they are always more than happy to help.
