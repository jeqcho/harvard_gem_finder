What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you would like to learn how to work with other people to create scenes for films (or even plays), this course is great. Through class, you'll be able to develop your own project / scene and in the process learn a lot about coordinating with others and learn valuable lessons about writing, casting, rehearsing.

If this course is taught again by Professor Sotomayor Castillo, even more of a reason to take this class. She is so insightful, kind, and made me consider the importance of simplicity.
If you are interested in fiction filmmaking, this is the most important class you can take as an undergraduate. Learn new paradigms of directing, practice them, then subvert them.
Take a very close look at the syllabus – the class was extremely unstructured and while working with Dominga is always great, I think the class as a whole needs to be developed more clearly in order for it to be worth taking.
Come in with a specific project in mind with time and commitment to actually create something by the end! I think it's hard to find the time to work on your own films or projects – so set aside time for that!
