What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would highly recommend taking this course. Professor Monaghan gives very useful feedback and is very knowledgable about the subject. You do not need any prior experience except for genuine interest in the history of warfare. I wrote a total of two essays in the last half of high school and this course improved my writing immensely.
Do your readings and keep your notes organized.
This course is quite helpful in improving skills and growing as a writer. Dr. Monaghan is a wonderful resource; she is always willing to provide feedback and encourage continued reflection throughout the entire writing process, and her comments are very helpful (discussing them with her is also a great opportunity to learn more about ways to improve). It is helpful to have an interest in history and historical perspectives on war, but ultimately, the course is primarily skills based rather than content based.
Pros
– Dr M is a very fair (reasonable) grader
– Dr M gives very useful feedback and is very fair on work time allocation.
– Dr M is very welcome to the class discussions and accepts everyone's participation/ideas.
– You learn about the brutalities of war and you end up a pacifist.
– You get to visit the museum and see cool (though pathos–evoking) art

Cons
– Dr M is a very strict (reasonable) grader
– It demands considerable time and mental effort (see the following) compared to other expos class.
– The class is very depressing. Everyone comes in and out of the class with a gloomy face. It's war, with all its suffering and pain and trauma. I had to drop a hard class to cope with this.
– You might end up not knowing what exactly to write and end up with a bad grade. (If you do know, though, you can get a good grade)

Summary
This class can be a growth of character for you, but more as a person than as a writer. If you are up for the challenge, go for it, else maybe it is a better idea to put something else as higher priority.
There is awesome support from the professor available. Make sure to plan ahead and stay on top of due dates so you can fully use this help!
This class is challenging, but it is very helpful and the content is interesting.
Work hard early and get a head start on your assignments.
This course is a decent workload. Only take it if you have a genuine interest in reading about war. If not, the long readings will be painful. Dr. Monaghan's feedback on drafts can be helpful in re–orienting your paper so I suggest taking your drafts seriously. The final paper is interesting because you can basically pick any topic. Good luck!
