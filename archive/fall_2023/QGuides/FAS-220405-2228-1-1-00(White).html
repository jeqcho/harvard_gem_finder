Comments
– You will start thinking about the world more philosophically even after the first class session.
– If you are an artist or at all interested in philosophy, art, art history, or the philosophy of art, take this course.
– This course is more philosophy–focused than art history–focused.
– The course load is perfectly manageable for any student, especially with only one class per week. Be prepared to read lots of philosophy and not understand it all on your own. This being said, Quinn will help you understand it during class.
– Quinn is an outstanding teacher and human. He creates a stress–free class environment.
Take this class. The workload is so manageable and for anyone who wants to take a freshman seminar but doesn't know what they're interested in, Ethics of Art provides a glimpse into the philosophical world and encourages us to rethink how we view life. Plus, we've had movie and game nights where the professor catered food from a class–voted restaurant (for us, we got Dumpling House!).
If you love art, and especially how art interacts with morals and politics, this is a wonderful course! The topics are fascinating, and Quinn is an incredible professor.

Also, hot tip for people thinking that taking this course will definitively resolve their anxiety surrounding issues of immorality with art: it will not! You will have to do that yourself with the information you learn. Unfortunately, this requires thinking, but fortunately, it is very interesting thinking.
Take this class. I'm not kidding. I don't know if or when it'll be offered again, but if you come across it, you must take it. It is such a fantastic class and the professor is incredible and kind and so invested both in the focus of the class and in getting you to become invested in it as well. The readings can be lengthy but they are entirely manageable and very interesting, and the essays are relatively short and a great introduction to philosophical writing. Even if you have no interest in pursuing Philosophy as a concentration, take this course. You'll learn so much in a very low–pressure environment, and the professor is now one of my favorites :)
If you have any interest in art or philosophy take this class, and even if you don't take it too! It'll either be a great introduction to, or an enhancement of your knowledge of aesthetics, and will help you think about art, ethics, and art in a new way.
Take this class if you can! Quinn is amazing! I'm not planning on concentrating in anything philosophy related but this class was still such a great experience
