What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This was a very relaxing and fun way to get an introduction some pretty difficult math and physics concepts – but make sure that you are genuinely interested in the covered topics! Dr. Vafa is an extremely knowledgeable professor who is very clearly passionate about the topics he teaches in this course. He had no interest in burdening us with unnecessary, tedious work, but rather wanted us to listen and absorb the concepts that he brings up throughout the class and have fun applying them to the puzzles that he presents each week. I would, however, recommend that you have a very solid foundation in physics and math if you are interested in this course. I am by no means a physics or math major – I was taking this class out of pure interest – and I think that was something that may have held me back compared to my classmates because I definitely felt like we were moving at a very fast pace and I seemed to struggle with the concepts more than others. However, that shouldn't stop you from taking the class, especially if you do have a strong background in either subject!! The class is fun and Dr. Vafa is great :)
This seminar is a great low–stress way to learn some math and physics from an expert and do some fun puzzles! Professor Vafa is extremely knowledgeable about the topics presented, and while the seminar does not go that far in depth or rigor, it is because the topics are often near frontier and open questions in math and physics, and the professor is approachable with questions and general curiosity about the subject matter. The workload is very little, and you get to bring your own puzzles to share at the beginning of class, and our class had a tight community, so overall the class excels at being light, fun, and informative.
The class requires a lot of prior knowledge about physics and can get quite complex. Everyone in the class was super passionate about and experienced in math and physics. As a freshman seminar, the course in general requires very little work and you could definitely get away with not knowing a thing about physics.
I would tell future students to absolutely take this as their Freshmen seminar if they are interested in math and/or physics. The puzzles we get both on the homework assignments and in class are genuinely fun to think about and try to solve. This may be the only class in which you genuinely enjoy doing homework and you never have to worry about getting it completely correct because for Professor Vafa it's important that you simply try. Also, he brings in fruits/sweets for every class and gives us a small break during which the students in my class would often look for ways to physically replicate the puzzles and then present our discoveries to Vafa.
Absolutely take this seminar if you have the opportunity! Professor Vafa is a gifted teacher and makes very complex topics in physics easily understandable. He's also very personable and makes the course fun. You get a taste of a lot of theoretical physics. The puzzles are fun and engaging. It really was a joy to come to seminar each week. Especially considering that the time commitment is negligible, you get an incredible amount out of the course. It truly was a worthwhile experience.
Super fun class– if you enjoy math and physics, I would highly recommend!
Apply for this class!! The evening time is not that bad (and we always get a lot of snacks), and Prof. Vafa is probably going to be one of the sweetest professors you will meet at Harvard. He is extremely passionate about his work and puzzles in general, and while this course is very "light and easy" (especially for physics/math concentrators), it is still definitely worth taking, especially if your plate is very full.
For the target demographic of the class, this class is amazing. It definitely requires a decent knowledge of physics and math in order to get the most out of the class and it is very much worth taking. The workload is super light (weekly "pset" of a couple puzzles and a short reading to ponder and only checked for completion) and the little time that is required to spend outside of class is time that I gladly spent mulling over some puzzles, to the point that I would call the class a gem if it didn't necessitate a relatively strong background in physics and math. The problems Vafa gives are very interesting and his teaching is humorous, light–hearted, and engaging at the same time. If you like physics, math, and/or puzzles, I would highly recommend you take this class.
This class is amazing if you like any combination of the three subjects in the title (Physics, Math, and Puzzles). The class attracts a certain type of student that is super enthusiastic about the material, and it makes the class very fun. The assignments are also both interesting and relaxed, so it is a very low–stress class, which is amazing for freshman year. Highly recommend.
If you like math and mathy puzzles/riddles, this class is a great fit for you. The puzzles range in difficulty from pretty easy to very difficult, but you never need to worry if there's one or two you can't figure out. The expectation is simply that you spend a little time each week thinking about the puzzles, not necessarily that you need to figure them all out. In my opinion, that makes this class a hidden gem, and I loved having this experience in my first semester at Harvard.
