This is a great course that shows the interesting ways theory pops up in the Computer Science literature..
A good intro to CS research if you haven't done it before. Paper topics are interesting and certainly important to know (almost every paper we covered had a huge impact on computing, if not the world in general). Mitz is helpful with regards to the final project and is more than willing to discuss ideas and provide feedback.
I like the papers, but I'm not so sure about the format of the classes. Typically, you would read the papers and then go to class to discuss them. I feel like the discussions in class don't have a clear direction. Oftentimes we would end up discussing if the paper is good or bad. The class would be better if the professor had a more structured way of leading the discussions.
Take the class.
The whole course is just reading papers. By the third week or so you learn to spend just 1 hr per paper. There are 2 papers per class and 2 classes per week so 4 hr of paper reading a week. Pretty reasonable workload. Very thoughtful and educational selection of papers. I learned a lot of practical ideas. Ends with a research paper project. Could use more guidance on the research project from course staff, but if you have experience in writing CS papers or doing research in general you will be more prepared than I was. Problem sets were fun, engaging, and relevant, which is rare for me to say of a CS course here. Office hours were not very helpful. Basically felt completely unguided for the final project and ran into multiple brick walls and had to restart as a result of a bad formulation of my research question.
Prof. Mitzenmacher is a great lecturer. I think he gets a lot of hate for no reason, but he comes off as a really genuine and kind person and really loves his work.

I question the relevance of spending three weeks on web–based graph algorithms, like PageRank.

The psets were fun and reinforced the material.
I think this class balances breadth and learning specific details quite well. It's fairly low pressure as well.
If you're interested in how industry–relevant algorithms (set membership querying, cardinality estimation, link prediction, search engines, range filtering, hashing, etc.) scale on big data inputs, this is the course for you! Prof. Mitzenmacher has an exciting lineup of state–of–the–art research for you to read about and discuss with peers. While you do have to dig deep into these research papers and write summaries about them every week, the papers themselves are generally engaging to the point where writing about them doesn't feel too tedious! The assignments (psets) take time, but are not burdensome (you can feel yourself better understanding ideas from the papers as you apply them through problem–solving). The final project is a hefty undertaking, but a great opportunity to challenge yourself if you're interested in research. I took this course as an undergraduate, and it has strengthened my resolve to continue with research and potentially go to grad school in CS. Prof. Mitzenmacher and the rest of the course staff are accessible, supportive, and highly knowledgeable.
If you are interested in the topics reading research in the different fields, take this class. It's a great way to explore the topics, and there is a large focus on understanding how research, paper submissions, and program committees work that was also super helpful for understanding what further work in academia or grad school might look like. The final project is also very flexible (as long as its related to the material in the class) which was nice. If you don't enjoy the material, definitely don't take the class though.
Not a typical theoretical CS class. Expect to read a lot of research papers and understand their contributions. Final project was quite open ended and a rewarding experience, though it can be hard to get started. Good class to take if you are curious about research and grad school.
I thought that overall it was a very interesting course. If you're an undergrad taking it like I was, the course has the style of some other high–level electives. The work is pretty laid back but the material is all quite rigorous. I learned a lot.
This is a great course!! Really interesting topics, great papers, good workload. The mock PC exercise is fun and educational. The problem sets are short and helpful for learning, not burdensome at all and good for illustration. The instructors are accessible, flexible, and supportive. Overall I learned a lot from this class and really enjoyed it.
