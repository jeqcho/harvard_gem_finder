What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is really helpful to future STEM concentrators, especially those wanting to do research because it really teaches you how to read a scientific paper. It can be a little hard to understand the content at times, but since it is a freshman seminar, there is really no consequence to being lost or confused. Good course!
This seminar is a great opportunity to engage and familiarize yourself with scientific papers and research. Some background in life science is VERY HELPFUL. The class meets once a week and as the semester progresses, papers get more challenging. Professor Hunter is very sweet and always available to meet with you; his passion and love for science is evident in every class. Although it wasn't a very discussion based class (seemed more as a lecture), it was a great way to get exposed to research.
Be sure to thoroughly read through the scientific papers before class– though the class only meets once a way the material develops quickly and you will quickly become lost if you don't follow along the whole way.
If you already have some experience with research and the topics discussed in the course I would highly recommend taking it; however, if you do not have a strong biology background you will most likely struggle a bit with the concepts.
This is a great class to take if you are interested in biology. However, if you are not someone with a strong background in biology or chemistry, you may find yourself struggling to keep up with the pace of class content.
This course is a really fascinating look through papers about RNAi over ~5 years or so. Definitely a nice, relatively easy class that's worth taking if you have a strong interest in research and/or molecular biology. However, it does advance into more complex science fairly quickly so having some background in the subject matter is quite helpful. Also, Professor Hunter is super nice! Take note that the readings and seminar discussions can become a little dull unless you really engage with the subject matter.
This class is challenging, but very informative and an interesting topic to study!
I enjoyed this course a lot! I think that it was a good course to take in my First–Year fall semester since it was relatively easy and did not require overly much work, while simultaneously it explored fascinating content. Regarding the RNAi content, it is extremely interesting, both from a mechanistic perspective and from an evolutionary perspective. I would say that it is a fair niche subject, but I enjoyed some of the broader themes of the course, such as how sequential science drives innovation, and how proteins and systems are conserved and changed between species. If you have limited exposure to biology/don't enjoy learning biology, this may not be the right course for you, but it would still be extremely beneficial in developing a critical perspective on science and the ability to quickly read scientific literature. If you have any interest in cell biology or biological systems in general, I endorse this class strongly!
