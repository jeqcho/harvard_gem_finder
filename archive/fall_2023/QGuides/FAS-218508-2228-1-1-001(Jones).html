Jess put a ton of effort into this class and trying to create a good learning environment for everyone which I really appreciated. However, I felt that assignments were unnecessarily tedious and more demanding than they needed to be. Take this class if you prefer to have A LOT of smaller assignments that slowly accumulate to your final grade. I personally didn't like this course style and thought the grading rubrics were excessive and stressful, especially for having big assignments every ~2 weeks on top of the weekly reading analyses. The readings were fairly short and digestable, and the grading and feedback were helpful.

TAKE THIS COURSE! It's super chill and the teaching staff is amazing :)
This is a great class if you want to learn about emotion regulation. Jess is an excellent professor, and nearly all of my critiques of the class are critiques of the research literature itself.

Out of all my classes, Jess is the professor who cares about my learning the most. It's not even close. She's given so much detailed feedback and structured the assignments in a way where I actually put in the work to get things out of the class. If you don't want to do the readings for a class, don't take it. The weekly readings are fairly reasonable and can get done in 30–60 minutes, so it's really not a big deal.

The class itself is 2 hours and usually split equally between lecture about the weekly emotion regulation topics and discussion led by other students in the class. There are no phones/computers allowed and the discussions can be pretty fun, but sometimes because the class is so long it can drag. It's very nice that it only meets once a week, though.

Emotion regulation research is very early. Some of the studies we reviewed are not nearly as rigorous or interesting as I'd hoped, and it felt that a majority of the field's findings converged on a few emotion regulation strategies (e.g.,, reappraisal, suppression, etc.). Moreover, the curriculum could've focused more on how to apply these findings – how can you *actually* improve emotion regulation in yourself or others? How can you spot good/bad emotion regulation behaviors?

TLDR: definitely take the class if you like the topic, Jess is great
