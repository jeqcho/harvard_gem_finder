I loved the topics of the course because of their urgency and relevancy. It is generally considered good practice to make decisions based on research evidence, and the course teaches you what is considered good research, how to be critical of studies and experiments, and how to conduct research properly. I would even say the course has made me somewhat depressed just thinking that we are never able to eliminate all biases and carry out an objective study, but it also motivated me to at least try to approach the truth. The course is not difficult at all, most of the assignments are completion based, and the teaching staff is very approachable and supportive. Highly recommend!
This is a class that requires coding experience. Take it when you have that experience.

If you’re up for that, then go ahead (though you really need a stats background for this course).
While the structure of assignments is fair, you need to make sure you stay caught up on video lectures (or get ahead). It is easy to fall behind if you don’t stay caught up on lectures, but the course is a great introduction to how psychologists conduct research and how to discern good research from bad research.

Familiarity with R will make the final project easier.
This class is basically unavoidable for the Class of 2024 and on, so best of luck to everyone concentrating in psychology. I would highly recommend taking PSY1900 either before or concurrently with this class. For some reason, this class expects you to know a lot about stats and how to use them but honestly does not do a good job at all teaching that material. You will feel a false sense of calm for the first half of the semester, but rest assured that the second half of the semester is a serious storm. You have about two weeks to design an experiment and collect data –– which they expect that you are able to collect data for at least 30 people (would highly recommend doing something involving asynch online surveys to make this easier). After that you have basically a week to analyze your data and write up the first draft of your final paper. The teaching staff took about a week to give everyone feedback on their drafts and then you have basically a week left to write out the rest of your paper and submit it. They expect some high level data analytics that they do not teach you how to do so be ready to become besties with google.

This is a hard class. The weekly lectures and homework are not hard, and it's extremely helpful that the homework assignments are completion based. The hard part about this class is the final research project that is a "group project" but doesn't actually allow you to work with your group when it comes to analyzing data, literature review, discussions, or any part of the paper that is not just conducting the data collection. There is also minimal support in statistical analysis. None of it is really taught even though students are expected to be very comfortable with it in order to complete the project. I would not recommend this course if you do not need it for the concentration.
