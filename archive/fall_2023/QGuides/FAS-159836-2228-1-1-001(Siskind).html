Great course! Mariano is super friendly and enthusiastic about course material. Course is also very much discussion based! Was very interesting hearing from peers on views about readings. Also, final projects were super fun to put together and to see from peers.
Great class! Highly recommend to learn about more than just English texts.
Take this class! It is fun and low pressure and you read great literature and Mariano is awesome. It is a good amount of reading so think about your total reading load before. It is very possible to take this and another high reading load class like Hum10, but you should think about total amount of reading.
It's a wonderful course and Mariano is a great professor. The selection of works was diverse and interesting. I gained a lot more insight into Latin American literature through this course and it made me really excited about taking seminars in the future.
The discussions were mostly based on providing background knowledge from the reading, which meant I thought that not much of the discussions were spent actually analyzing the texts (by the students). The readings are amazing, but I felt I would have gotten more out of our class discussions if more was spent tackling themes and form in the books/stories instead of the history behind them. It felt like the course had too much material and not enough time to devote to each work.
This is the only class that I can truly recommend to everyone without reservations. If you are interested in interesting things, this class will interest you. Take this class.

Some things about this class. In this seminar, every week you read a cool book/short story from Latin American literature, and then you meet to discuss it. I really did find many of these discussions to be fascinating –– Professor Siskind has so much to say about all of these books, but he is also committed to maintaining the discussion format of the seminar. There were some points made, both by Professor Siskind and by other students, that genuinely blew my mind, and made me see the whole book in a new light. Where else will you have the chance to read these books, and then discuss their meaning with a world expert on Romance Languages and Literatures?
Interesting class! I enjoyed the books we've read, and the workload is slim to none –– wonderful class if you just want to read and talk about literature.
