This seminar should be your number one choice if you are interested in U.S. law, or repelled by it. Professors Nesson are incredible teachers and students themselves, and are wonderful facilitators of nuanced conversation that can change your perspective on complicated topics by shining appropriate light on overlooked details. They are delightful hosts, engaging conversationalists, and will encourage you out of your comfort zone to expand your scholastic horizon. However, you must have an open mind, coming into this seminar. In cases of extreme complexity, there is no tolerance for picking a side based on emotion alone – you are prompted to back it with fact. And they bring their adorable dogs so thats definitely a win.
Absolutely take this course if you are interested in law and a laidback class with great professors. The class is full of deliberation and light reading about interesting and controversial cases revolving around the jury system. You will be with none other than the famous law professor Charles Nesson and Dean of Academic Programs Rebecca Nesson who are by far one of the best professors I have ever met. Want to deliberate with others on complex issues? This is the class for you!

This is a very doable course that you will definitely get a lot out of and you will be very glad to have taken it.
