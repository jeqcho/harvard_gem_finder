What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Super supportive environment for a total beginner ~ 3 papers and a little recital, so fun!
I would definitely recommend this course, if you are at all interested in consort playing and in the viola da gamba. It is a great course to take to balance out a schedule as it requires a different type of work (a lot of exploration, trial and error) than most classes. It's also just such a cool opportunity that is so rare at Harvard and at colleges in general to study both performance and history/culture together in this way.
This is one of the most amazing courses that Harvard has to offer. The fantastic blend of musicology and practice of playing makes it a very well–rounded course from which you can gain a lot, not to mention the mind–blowing fact that you get to learn a period instrument from an amazing professional player for free! I cannot recommend this course highly enough, and it is easily one of the best courses I have ever taken at Harvard. If are ever unsure about whether you should take this course: you should.
This class is a delight and a treasure. One gets few chances in one's life to learn to play a rare renaissance instrument and enter its world. Strongly recommend.
This class is such a blast! I grew so much as a musician and a learner. Learning something completely new is so freeing. You're allowed to be really bad! It's all part of the process. The course grades you for improvement and the instructors will meet you where you're at and applaud you for your enthusiasm and willingness to learn.
If you'd like to see how people used to make music back in Renaissance England, jump into this train to the past!
