What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in learning more about what feminism is and the issues it covers, take this class! However, if you have read lots of feminist theory, there might be parts of this class that feel like review of ideas you've been acquainted with for a while, and that's ok! Just something for you to know going into the class.
If you are interested in feminism but haven't really read feminist theory, novels, or memoirs, take this class! A great, low–key way to learn about feminist ideas.
If you want to learn more about gender and justice literature, I highly recommend this class. The professor is amazing, always available and willing to help. The class environment is also super welcoming, and I always felt comfortable sharing my thoughts. The readings can be demanding but manageable, but they are really interesting overall.
Take this class! Professor Thornber does an amazing job at selecting the readings and leading the class discussion. I've learned so much about intersectional feminism and how literature can serve as a tool for advancing the voices of those unheard and telling the stories of those who are forgotten. The readings are enjoyable (although they bring up difficult problems) and it is a perfect class to balance a heavy academic schedule. The class environment is safe and friendly, and you will have the opportunity to expose your thoughts and experiences.
I would definitely recommend this course. It is pretty light coursework, and you get a lot out of it with different readings and discussions. You should be prepared to do the readings thoroughly so you can get the most out of the course.
My favorite class this semester! I learned so much and enjoyed doing the readings. Not too much work.
