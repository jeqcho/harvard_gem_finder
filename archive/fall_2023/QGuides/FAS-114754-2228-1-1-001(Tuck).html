What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I strongly recommend this class to anyone (not just gov concentrators). You gain a greater understanding of key ideas in ancient and medieval political philosophy. Keep in mind that this is a survey course – you won’t go too in depth on any one particular writer. However, reading many works over the semester made it easier to see the connections and commentary between the works over time (for example, when later writers would cite works that we had read earlier in the semester).
TAKE THIS!!!

This course is amazing. Working on and with classics like Plato, with genuinely one of the foremost experts on the topic, is such a gift. This class has really changed how I think about the world and especially about government and the state. Tuck does something so difficult; talks honestly and incisively about government, with the effect being OPTIMISM!!!
Professor Tuck is retiring at the end of this year, so you'll have a different instructor if you take the class in the future. Much of my experience in the class was shaped by his unique character and expertise, so I don't know what your iteration of the course will look like! That said, I really enjoyed the subject matter and the emphasis Professor Tuck placed on reading complete texts; I think this does allow students to feel more confident in their knowledge of each thinker's works. If you're interested in classics or political theory, Gov 1060 provides a great foundation.
Listen to Prof. Tuck's lecture first, then do the readings. You will be able to pick up a lot more then.
Gov 1060 is a great course! It's a shame future students will not be able to experience it with Professor Tuck, whose knowledge is infinite. All things said, learning in this course requires tremendous discipline and a genuine passion for the material as it is very self–guided beyond lectures and sections. If you put in the work, you will learn a lot and be appreciative of having done so.
I wish you could take it with Prof. Tuck, as he probably won't teach this one again. Was an honor to have it.
