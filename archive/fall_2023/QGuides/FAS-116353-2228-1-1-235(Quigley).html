LOVE THIS CLASS!! I don't even like psych and I enjoyed the class a lot. Dr. Quigley is the most wise, perceptive, hilarious, and refreshingly frank teacher I've encountered. It means so much when you're having a bad day and you can be real with her. In class, her lectures are incredibly engaging and relevant to everyday life. She talks a lot about her experiences as a psychologist, so if you're interested in that, you'll get a lot of really good insight. She does a fantastic job of facilitating a laid back, wholesome, supportive dynamic. Also, I was surprised by how much targeted feedback I got. Hot girls take Gender and Mental Health (seriously, everyone in this class is really pretty).
Take advantage of the lessons taught in class that might not seem directly applicable to your papers. They will be very valuable overall.
This class is interesting and provides a different view on psychology. The materials are interesting, but often you will not need to read them because the class doesn't really stay on pace with the schedule for that day (read the readings in the beginning of the year so that you can participate, but after you get to know the nature of the class you'll see when you don't need to read certain things). It should be noted that the only things the make up your grade are the three response papers which have a lot of weight on your final grades (20, 30, and 40%), so make sure you communicate with Dr. Quigley often because she'll help you. Dr. Quigley is also really helpful for completing work, but make sure you reach out to her before 1pm or you won't get a response soon enough. She's also very strict with the expos policy on only looking at a draft once.
Please, reach out and ask for clarifications.
The class is a good taste of writing and psychology. Although the course assignments (the final draft of each unit paper) is a little difficult, every Expos class is going to have the same challenge. I think this is a great Expos class to take if you are interested in learning more about the intersection of gender identity and mental health. I will admit that the class focuses more on the course material (lessons about psychology) than it focuses on writing basic. Therefore, if you feel like Expos is more about learning writing mechanics than it is about the subject, I would not recommend this Expos.
The class was very bonded. Dr. Quigley cared about each of us on a deeper level and made it a privilege to show up for class.
Very interesting class if you are into psychology and the psychology of gender/mental health specifically. Prior psych knowledge is not needed but does help understand readings. If you are thinking about taking this class, make sure you have a good grip of writing skills. At times there is a lack of help with these skills so it is best if you come into the class being comfortable with writing.
I may be biased, but I believe that this is the best Expos class there is. Dr. Q is an incredible instructor for both writing and an introduction into the field of psychology. You will learn so much from this class and actually enjoy doing it. Class discussions are relaxed but engaging, and Dr. Q facilitates a wonderful environment for you to have one of your first classes at Harvard in. It is an especially great course for any STEM concentrators (regardless of your interest in psychology) because many of the readings come from scientific journals and you learn how to write for STEM. Also, in regards to reading, this class is less reading–intensive than most of the other Expos classes I have heard about, which is great because most Expos classes are about the same amount of work, the only variable part is the reading. Feedback on essays is thorough and specific, but once you get over the initial shock of actually having your work critiqued (if you're like me and did not get a lot of that in high school), you never feel embarrassed or bad about your work because everything is framed in such a way as to help you get better. All–in–all, I would highly recommend this course to anyone remotely interested.
This is one of the best EXPOS options and I would recommend to incoming students, the workload is manageable and doesn't feel like busy work at all. You also learn some things about behavior that is applicable to everyday life.
Great class with a great professor. If you are interested in this subject, definitely take this expos class.
