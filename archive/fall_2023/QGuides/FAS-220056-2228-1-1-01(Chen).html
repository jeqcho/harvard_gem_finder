While this course required a lot of time in class, it wasn't very much work outside of class (typically 2 or less hours per week and a bit more for the final project/when there's a lab report due soon). I'd definitely recommend it if you're looking to gain lab skills in a class setting and thrive in really structured environments where you come in, do the experiment, and then you don't have to plan your schedule around experiments. The only exception to this is the final project, which is much less structured but still really interesting.
Excellent class, cannot recommend enough. Very chill but you (can) learn tons about imaging. Final project is pretty fun too.

The teaching staff is really nice and helpful, and the final project is a great opportunity to experience a research project from start to finish. Taking SCRB 164 helped me realize that I really wanted to do research and inspired me to pursue it as a career.

This is an amazing class. You learn a lot about new wet lab techniques and get to do them yourself. I think that the coolest lab was IVF on mice. The final project for us was an introductory wet lab research project on patient–derived progeria cells. It is a completely lab–based course, so there are no extra psets or exams (but there is a lot of class time). I highly recommend!
