I don't know about other seminar courses, but I enjoyed this one.

Readings: A bit much, but useful. Later on, when people do presentations, you can do these more optionally, but I'd do them or at least skim them for the experimental design and abstract, as it'll teach you how you might add to the work in your final project. Also, we actually CRITICIZE the readings, which is super huge. Not many courses actually have us read a scientific paper and go: 'wait the experiment is super silly'.

Assignments: Super manageable. Presentation and paper along with it do not need to be super long, and the research question and presentation are good milestones so you don't end up waiting till the last second.

Project: Professor Hiscox is awesome, and the way the course is designed, you'll find that the part you do a presentation for ends up teaching you about the literature in that field, which then lets you really get into it to figure out your research design.

Professor: Awesome, super approachable, has depth of knowledge in field, including practical, is willing to help and accessible and checks in!

Class: Very cool, basically a 2 hour discussion. You don't need to be online all the time, as there's enough people to let it kinda flow.
The course was a really great way to learn about the subject and Hiscox is an incredible professor. I would recommend anyone considering this class to try and get in if they can.
I would highly recommend this class to anyone. Every week of this seminar provided a new interesting topic for students to embrace. Though the readings for the course were interesting, the class discussion made every meeting exciting and fun. The greatest asset of the course, however, was Professor Hiscox. He is an outstanding human being who elicits strong enthusiasm and participation from his students, and he did a wonderful job guiding class conversations in a way that made every discussion productive and fruitful.
This is an amazing class! You learn so much about a topic that other courses really don't cover and the workload is very manageable. Hiscox is awesome!
Professor Hiscox is the reason to take this class. You'll learn a ton while not feeling stressed out about your grade (he's generally pretty lenient as long as you put in work). The content itself is super interesting and relevant to many of the interests of Harvard students who want to make change on social justice issues but feel frustrated by government inaction. This class will discuss different options by which to put pressure on companies to change these issues when the government fails to do so. Not a ton of reading, interesting discussions, great professor – definitely take the class.
an incredible class to take esp. since there's so much pressure to go into consulting, finance, etc. this class discusses the potential + detriment private companies can have.
If you can or care about global social issues, take this class. Or at least take a class with this professor. The course content is highly relevant to global and social problems worldwide, but it also gave me a new perspective on which to look at social issues and influence big alternative players (corporations) to help drive change. The course can have some heavy readings at times, but I enjoyed it so much that I never felt like the course or the final paper was 'hard' work. The final project can be challenging if you study a broad topic, but it is entirely manageable and the professor is great at guiding you there! It's even given me topic inspiration for my senior thesis. Take this class!
