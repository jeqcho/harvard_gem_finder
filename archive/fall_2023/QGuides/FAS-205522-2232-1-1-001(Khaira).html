What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This tutorial is a great way to connect with a grad student & mentor individually to help setup your thesis research! I honestly think this could be required for all students who are thesising since it was great to have structured time to work on my thesis and be prepared for summer research. I got a lot of great feedback and discussion with my TF, Javid, who was really instrumental to how I think about these questions.
This class is essential in setting up your thesis. Not quite sure how people do a thesis without this class.
If you are thinking of writing a thesis, definitely take this course! It will give you structured time to write proposals, get grants, and will help your thesis to be in great shape to do research in the summer!
Definitely take this class! Even if you are a joint concentrator with Anthro as your Allied Field, this class is so valuable because it effectively gives you credit for preparation for your thesis would you have to do, regardless. Working with a graduate student mentor has been one of the most useful experiences of my academic career thus far, and it is a kind of support that is like no other –– your mentor is solely invested in to you and thinking through your project. If you want to learn how to approach your thesis in a dually methodological and theoretical way, definitely take this class!
The opportunity to work with a graduate student one–on–one is truly unique and especially rewarding within anthropology. We are taught the ethics of ethnography, "deep hanging out", and how knowledge and affect is produced in ordinary moments of connection and care. Ironically, academic spaces are somewhat deserts when it comes to this, and we are all too familiar with it. This class (and of course I was lucky+ to be paired with a dope grad student) really gave space to be a "person" within scholarship.
