This is the best class I have taken at this point in my life. If you pay attention in class and complete your coursework, you will leave this class as an expert of multivariable calculus. It is adequately paced, well taught, provides adequate support, and is an appropriate level of difficulty.
Math 21a is a good class for the right student but not for everyone. If you need to learn how to compute integrals in multivariable and vector calculus, this class will teach you how to do that. If you want a class taught in sections that allow you to practice and ask questions, Math 21a provides this. However, if you want to take math because you find it beautiful, you may want to consider Math 22 (which to be fair, I have not taken, but it is proof–based). I do not think that Math 21a presents mathematics eloquently at all. Further, section (which takes the place of a lecture) is effective but inefficient. If you pick up the material quickly, you will probably find yourself bored in class.
Don't be afraid to go straight into 21a without having done 1a and/or 1b.
Avoid at all cost
This class is awesome! It was so fun to take and I definitely recommend it to everyone. As someone who was never passionate or fascinated with math, this class changed my perception of the whole field. Although it has a daunting 3 psets a week, they are short and manageable and help you learn a lot and the teaching staff is fantastic. It is one of the more chill math classes and was my favorite class this semester as a premed student who isn't a math person.

If you take this class, I would definitely recommend finding a good group of people to work on psets with and study for exams (3 hour practice tests for the final are brutal without a pal). MQC is a really underutilized resource because the CAs are super helpful and willing to sit down with you for however long you need. Also, start studying for exams early!!!
