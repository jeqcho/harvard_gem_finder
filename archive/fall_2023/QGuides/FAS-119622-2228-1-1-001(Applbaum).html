Comments
This is a wonderful seminar if you are even slightly interested in political philosophy/history. Discussions in class are engaging and entertaining even when you're reading tough authors because Professor Applbaum does a great job at making classes dynamic. Weekly work didn't take too long, and midterm and final assignments were not much extra work either, so it won't make you suffer.
This class is an excellent freshman seminar for anyone even a little interested in political philosophy, the professor is excellent and the course material is very very interesting.
Take it! If you are genuinely interested in philosophy and history, this class is absolutely perfect. It's definitely intellectually demanding, but we always had room to get things wrong and discuss and improve. It is one of the classes that will stick with me throughout college. The professor is absolutely fantastic. He made the class fun, exciting, and honestly pretty casual for the kind of material we covered. Lots of inside jokes and obscure references. The class often felt like a performance or a debate, but in a really genuine and constructive way – not like you had to memorize lines. Really fantastic.
This is a great class for students interested in political philosophy and history. However, students taking the course should be aware that this seminar might have relatively more work than an average freshman seminar does.
This was a nice freshman class. Without taking up too much time, it gives students an introduction to the political philosophies of Hobbes, Locke, and Kant, following an exploration of Henri of Navarre, one of his advisors, and Michel de Montaigne in the French Wars of Religion. Prof. Applbaum makes the class enjoyable and helps students understand the texts while respecting differing interpretations. One word of caution: if you are looking for a thorough study of any of these texts, that is not what this class is designed to be.
If you're even remotely interested in this class, please take it! An incredible professor, fascinating readings, and assignments stimulating enough to get you thinking while also matching the lower workload of a Freshman Seminar. Prepare to walk out knowing much more about the French Wars of Religion and proto–social–contractrian theory than your average peer. Prof. Applbaum does an excellent job at bringing the sometimes complex content of your readings into clear light, as well as using a very engaging pedagogy to always hold your interest, something easier said then done for a nearly three–hour class. Make the most of his office hours – you'll always walk in welcomed and walk out with your mind expanded. My favorite class of the semester!
The course is great. Professor Applbaum is a fantastic teacher and it makes the class super interesting. At the beginning of the semester, I complained a lot because we had an essay due per week and several readings, but as the course move forward I realized it was really chill and it didn't take me that much time. When taking this course bear in mind that it's not just a history seminar, but also politics and philosophy, and they make up a great portion of the course. If you like those topics, you should definitely take this freshman seminar.
