Start the problem sets early.
Prof. Jacobsen is a great lecturer, and the material in this course is interesting. However, it is extremely difficult and requires a lot of work for all of the psets and exams. Go to office hours and review as you're going.
If you thought chem 20/30 were fun, you will love this class. It has a much lower emphasis on memorizing synthesis pathways, and is based more in investigating the theory behind all sorts of things. This class will go into the weeds of gray areas that were glossed over in lower organic courses. It surveys a lot of topics and contains tons of information, but I think the core information that you need to understand for psets/tests is a reasonable amount. I recommend trying to keep up with lectures and teach yourself course content as you go, because it's a lot to catch up if you fall behind. The course materials were high quality and the exams were fair, and the course was never unfairly burdensome. Overall a great class with a kind course staff that I'll look back on fondly!

I got to see lots of examples from current research which was very inspiring. I honestly could not recommend this class enough for anyone interested in orgo. A really, really amazing experience!

I learned a lot about "why" chemistry happens the way it does and how to think about reactions/mechanisms. That being said, there was a misalignment between what is taught in lecture and what is emphasized on exams. Exams focused heavily on mechanistic analysis, but because of the lack of office hours, practice problems, and sections dedicated to lecture material, we didn't get nearly enough practice with the kind of analysis required on exams. This ultimately left me dissatisfied and frustrated with the course.
This course is a great continuation of the chemical knowledge developed in introductory organic and inorganic chemistry. Eric is a really engaging lecturer and has immense knowledge in the field, and his anecdotes and stories about different discoveries and chemists were always entertaining. The class is challenging overall, but the problem sets are manageable with office hours and help to keep the course content fresh and digestible.
Very interesting class! I now have a much better understanding of mechanistic analysis –– something that wasn't really covered in previous organic chemistry classes like CHEM 30. The class moves at a fast pace, and a lot of content is covered, but lecture slides/notes are detailed and easy–to–follow, and assignments and psets were manageable. There's also a great teaching staff –– Eric was a great lecturer and Peyton was a very knowledgeable TF.
Start psets early and go to office hours. Make sure you have a solid understanding of each topic before exams because each lecture is very dense.
Take this class if you want to be mind blown. The content is fascinating. Be ready to put in the hours and I would strongly recommend taking Chem 20/30 track (if you are an undergrad considering this class).
