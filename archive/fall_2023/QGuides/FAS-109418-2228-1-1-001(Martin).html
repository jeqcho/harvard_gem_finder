Comments
If you're interested in learning about why communism did not work and in Soviet history, this is the class to take! Readings could be a lot / very dry, and lectures could also be somewhat dry, but overall I feel like I learned a lot. The chronology and ordering of the class was a bit disorganized, though, so keep that in mind as you're organizing your notes and going into lectures.
This class is super interesting and I recommend it to anyone who wants to understand current events (i.e. the Russia–Ukraine War), how communism can function in practice, or the history of the Soviet Union in general. The readings are totally manageable and Professor Martin gives good lectures, although his slides are also his lecture notes so be careful trying to decipher them.
Very interesting course, especially with the war currently going on in Ukraine. Lectures can be dry sometimes but the readings are generally interesting.
This course was one of the easiest history courses I've taken, as well as the one that has taught me the most at an intro level. In the beginning, it feels a little bit like you're drowning in all the information, but the professor and TF are both aware of this and don't expect you to really understand the nuances until later. The assignments are graded fairly and the reading isn't too overwhelming, especially considered that each student is given one piece to really focus on. The no notes or computer policy seems a bit strict at first, but the published notes each week are really adequate enough, especially considering the assignments. Professor Martin really is an expert, which means that sometimes it seems like he's teaching a little bit above an intro level, but as the class goes on you figure out how to keep up. This course almost reminds me of a language class, in that you're really thrown right in the middle of the Soviet period and have to figure out what's going on by 'immersion' in the topic. I would really recommend this class to people who have experience with history classes and want a class that's a lighter load, but still really learning intensive.
Make sure to actually do the readings before lecture. At least look at them. If you don't, there's a very good chance you'll be lost and confused during lecture.
Definitely take this course. The material is fascinating and the lectures are incredibly well put together and very engaging. The course gave a far more nuanced understanding of the collapse of the USSR and the evolution of modern eastern Europe. Not only was it incredibly enjoyable, I feel that I have become much more informed when reading and thinking about many important current events. I definitely recommend this course.
I have never experienced a class that made me reconsider what I wanted to do with my life as much as this one (in a positive way). Perhaps owing to the relevance of the material, or to the professor's and TF's genuine enthusiasm for teaching the subject, this class is extremely formative. If you are interested in communism, Soviet history, and their legacies vis a vis the ongoing Ukraine war, I strongly recommend this class.
Really interesting and timely topic because of current conflicts. This class gave a very nice and broad overview of the societ union, its collapse, and its long lasting effects. Highly recommend if you are at all interested in the topic
