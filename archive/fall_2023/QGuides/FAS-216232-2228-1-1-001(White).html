Take it! Duncan is a great professor and the course materials are by far interesting enough to justify the amount of time spent reading/watching. If you've ever enjoyed a spy story, this class is for you.
This class is hands down the best class I've taken at Harvard! I would strongly recommend that anyone even remotely interested take this course. Duncan makes the materials so approachable and gives fantastic context to ground discussions in class. Even though there are three papers, they were three of the most enjoyable papers I've written, mostly because the materials were so fun!
You might feel a bit guilty taking this course because most of the things you read and watch are way too fun to be on a syllabus. But what makes this course special and not just enjoyable is the Duncan is always pushing us to think about bigger and bigger contexts. One second we'll be talking about Jason Bourne and the next we'll be having a fascinating discussion about how to define a literary canon or about the American Empire. Sure, it's a course about espionage literature, but it's also just a really nice intro to hist–list as a discipline. Plus, the workload is super manageable so would 100% recommend.
This class is fascinating, but it is a lot of work. Be prepared for much reading and writing, and a lot is expected from you for the writing assignments.
This was my first semester at Harvard, and this class was definitely my favorite. The course materials were all super interesting, so although it was somewhat time consuming (usually one book or movie and one shorter secondary source a week), I looked forward to completing my work outside of class. Duncan was really great at facilitating discussion, and I often noted how quickly the two hour time slot flew by.
There are two essays due during the term and one final essay. The weeks that essays are due, Duncan usually tries to do a lower workload for homework, which is super helpful. I was amazed at how detailed the feedback I got on each essay was. It really allowed me to improve my writing with each essay.
Overall, this class was so engaging. I learned so much and had a lot of fun. I have already recommended it to several friends and will continue to recommend to many more!
If you get the chance to take this course, do it!!!
Take this class. Prof Duncan White is incredible and the materials you engage with are fascinating, and fun. This was my favourite class of the semester.

In terms of what I learned: I learned SO MUCH in this class. I learned how to close read novels, nonfiction excerpts, reports, movies!!, and other forms of media. I learned how to put these works into historical context. I learned about the principles of good discussion and how to contribute in seminar. I practiced writing. I challenged myself with reading quickly and thoroughly (usually 2 books a week with this and Hum 10). And in terms of content, I feel much more situated in the literary and cultural history of espionage fiction, British, American, and internationally from WWI to present.
Take this class!!! The material itself covered in the course is fantastic and homework readings may not even feel like work because they're so interesting. There's also a great balance of films covered in the syllabus too which is a nice way to enjoy something different after reading a book. We didn't have weekly responses in this class even though they were listed on the syllabus, which was helpful in taking away some stress and focus more on discussion. Don't be scared off by the essays, which really aren't bad, especially if you pick a topic that interests you, and they build up gradually to the culminating essay so you can focus on specific skills in each one. As a first–year, I was worried that the essays would be difficult since I haven't taken Expos yet, but they were the ideal amount of challenge for me to grow as a writer, and I feel MUCH more confident in tackling essays after this class. Professor White is so helpful and supportive and provides really encouraging, detailed constructive feedback that gives you specific things to work on, which makes it less overwhelming. He is also available for office hours even outside the scheduled office hours times, and I'd recommend attending them because they're really beneficial for figuring out how to get on the right track for a paper. This class was definitely my favorite of the semester and has me considering a Hist and Lit concentration, so I can't rave about it enough. For me, it was the perfect mix of informative, challenging, and fun, and I'm really going to miss it.
A lot of reading. Definitely a good course for learning what the Hist–Lit dept is like and good feedback for learning how to write for it.
TAKE IT. Nothing else to say but take it if you want to do Hist & Lit!
I would definitely take this class if you are at all interested in espionage. The course materials are very enjoyable and different than what you would see in other history courses. While there is a good deal of Homework, it is all fascinating and fun to discuss in class.
