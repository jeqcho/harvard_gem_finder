Great professor! Kind, supportive, accomadating. The readings are incredibly insightful. Definitely a gem EXPOS.
This class is AMAZING. You will learn so much from the course material and discussion, and the course structure is very flexible and open–ended. Erin is the sweetest person ever, and she's extremely flexible with grades / deadlines / extenuating circumstances. You will definitely leave the class with a much deeper understanding of the immigration system and criminal justice system in the US — the course sheds so much light on issues that you otherwise would not have learned about. Please take this class! The workload is very very manageable and enjoyable.
This is arguably one of the most chill but still interesting Expos courses out there. If you have a genuine interest in the subject, the (minimal) readings and papers will come easily. Even if they don't, Erin is someone who genuinely cares about your circumstances and is interested in helping you succeed.
The amount of support from the instructor is excellent. If you have writing background, the class will be enjoyable. If not, the instructor can provide as much support as you need for the essays. The best way to get the most out of the course material is to actually go outside it and explore further than just what is provided as reading assignments.
This course is amazing, it is very engaging. Workload is not a lot aside from the weeks we have our essays. The professor is amazing and very understanding.
This is hands–down the best Expos course out there. The information you get to learn is absolutely crucial as humans of the world, but also very intriguing and eye–opening. Repeatedly, our class was shocked that we had not learned this information previously regarding the history of immigration in this country alongside the crises involved in that history. Each week, there is simply two journal responses from very manageable and interesting course readings, and then three essays total in the course which can be of your topic of choice under the given prompt so there is a lot of room to explore your interests. Finally, I have never met such a kind and positive professor than Dr. Routon; she makes the course what it is and the collaborative space is so friendly and rich in learning thanks to her. She is incredibly flexible in deadlines and will help you out with anything you need, whether it's course–related or even life advice in general. Dr. Routon (who is so kind to let us refer to her as Erin) never fails to make everyone smile and laugh, and she always lights up the room with her presence. Highly, highly recommend this Expos course without a doubt! Best class of my first semester at Harvard.
Probably one of the best expos classes, you won't really learn how to write but the material is interesting and Professor Routon is super sweet.
It's a great class for people passionate about issues surrounding immigration and incarceration. The readings are interesting and mind–opening. The workload is manageable if you pace yourself for the essays and make sure to take advantage of the resources provided.
I highly recommend this course!! Dr. Routon is amazing and teaches so well
Rank this Expos first, or if not first, second! This class makes Expos more than just bearable; it is filled with thought–provoking readings and a podcast that everyone should listen to. The course helps you view immigration through a political, historical, social, and cultural lens, which is so important in today's age. The work is manageable and the papers seem to be more exciting to write than other Expos. In addition, the preceptor is completely understanding about extensions, and you will never feel like you need to pull an all–nighter to complete work for this class. You won't regret taking this class!
