Overall wonderful expos course! Collier couldn't be a better instructor, and his feedback on essays were extremely helpful. Highly recommend!
This class is probably one of the best Expos class' available. You get experience writing in a lot of different formats, starting from literary analysis, then moving more into the nonfiction realm through a lens essay, finally finishing with an entirely research based paper. Professor Brown also gives super helpful advice after each essay and helps you improve your writing.
Choose a topic you're actually interested in.
Pretty chill course and instructor is very kind
I highly recommend you take this EXPOS class! Prof. Brown provides excellent feedback on all writing assignments, the conferences are very instructive, the workload is low, and the grading is fair. I found the discussion of wastelands to be fascinating and I learned a lot of new writing skills.
The class is overall very solid and will teach you a lot about writing in the end. You may not realize it while you're in it, but by the time you write the research paper, you will have felt that you have improved as long as you keep in mind the feedback that Collier gives. I came in with a good idea of how to write literary analysis essays, but I felt I needed to transition to the analytical style taught in expos. There were times when I felt consistently confused on what was going on and eventually would figure it out 2 days before the essay was due. Essay weeks were rough. I would spend like 8 hrs/day for 2–3 days writing and editing, but again, this was mostly due to my first time writing in analytical style. During non–essay weeks, class was very chill, readings were brief but interesting, and discussions were great. Out of all the expos, this is the one to take.
This course is a great one! Very interesting subject matter, and Professor Brown is very caring and helpful throughout the paper–writing process.
This class has a lighter workload than other Expos classes, from what I can tell: over the course of two weeks, there's probably an hour of reading or 90 minutes of movie watching. The teacher, Collier, is lovely and cares about his students. He provides great feedback and takes the time to meet with each student individually to discuss drafts of each paper. That said, the class itself can be a bit boring, especially at 9 am. In the end, this is a required class, and many of your peers care little about the actual subject matter (Wastelands). However, given the other possibilities you have for Expos, I'd generally recommend this one. Just temper your expectations: this is a required class.
Collier is such an amazing person! He is extremely easy to access outside of class for additional tips/help, and he really wants you to succeed!
Do the readings and start writing and planning your essays early. And definitely take the feedback from Dr. Brown seriously as it is really really helpful.
Wastelands is probably the best expos course because of how passionate Collier is about the topic. The earlier and more you buy into the topics, the more interesting the content and papers will be. Compared to other expos classes, there is significantly less work and the work we do is more enjoyable in my opinion (3 essays, no other projects or papers).
Take this class! Professor Brown is so helpful and gives great feedback. The wastelands topic can be connected to so many different subjects, especially across all three units with fictional lenses and non–fiction lenses, you'll definitely find something in the topic that you feel passionate about.
