What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Prof. Elmer is a great teacher. Try to get to his office hours: this material is hard and may take a good deal longer to translate than the sheer amount of Greek indicates. If you can do it, though, lyric poetry is some of the coolest material out there, and it's not so commonly taught.
Take this class! Or any other class with Professor Elmer, for that matter. Readings are pretty simple once you get the hang of things (dialects might be tricky at first if you don't have that much experience with non–Attic), and it never gets dull since, as a genre class, you get exposure to a lot of different authors. Most of class is usually spent on translation, if that's your thing, but you'll also talk a lot about the poetic traditions, etc. Definitely my favorite class this semester.
This course is about the most beautiful pieces of literature ever written. For example, Sappho's poems became so famous that she was probably the only poet in world literature where you could become a famous poet yourself by just translating here.
Dedicate as much time as possible every day to work through the readings. If it has been a while since you took any Greek classes, keep a copy of Smyth close by. Also, do not be shy about sharing your translations, especially where you are not sure or think you have made a mistake, because you will learn so much (or remember so much you had forgotten) through the professor's feedback.
