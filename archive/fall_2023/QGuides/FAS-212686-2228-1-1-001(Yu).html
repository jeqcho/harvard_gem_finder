What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great class to get started with writing a paper, beginning a research project and learning about networking research. If you are interested in any of those things, I would recommend this class.
Take this course with Minlan Yu. She's an amazing teacher and really cares about her students (incredibly accessible, always willing to meet and give feedback/counsel to where you are on your project). She takes the pedagogy of the course incredibly seriously. The courseload itself isn't super heavy aside from the final project, which by nature of being systems/ML tends to eat up a huge chunk of time toward the end of the semester. Ended up a lot of it over Thanksgiving and reading period. But the final project is also really open–ended and again, you could get so much feedback from the professor on your topic idea. Otherwise, you basically have two paper reviews due per week (so it's up to you how much time you want to spend on them) and a paper presentation.
This course is a must–take for any advanced computer scientists! Professor Yu is a wonder in the classroom and encourages students with prodding questions throughout the presentations and lectures. The flipped classroom format where students present these papers really helps cement these seemingly complex topics into introductory and digestible language.
If you find the list of papers/topics interesting, you should consider taking this class! Prof. Yu is extremely thoughtful and provides great feedback on the final project.
The class is a good opportunity to learn more about the state–of–the–art in a given field. Note that the exact topic the class focuses on changes from year to year. The topic plays a big role in determining how the class looks like, so you should take that into consideration when deciding whether to take CS 243.

The weekly workload is fairly light –– you have to read one paper per class (two per week), and then leave a review. You can probably get away with just skimming the paper (can help in tough weeks), but please, please do your best to read the papers . The whole point of the class is discussion, so if you're not going to read the papers, you might as well take something else.

The final project is as much work as you want, but either way it is a significant amount of work. Plan to spend a lot of time in the last 4–5 weeks on the project. You will have to propose a topic, do research on related works, design a system for your idea, implement it, evaluate it, interpret the results, draw conclusions, present your project, and then finally write a comprehensive report about it. This is a lot of work; don't procrastinate. There is little guidance with the project, so you should have some experience with doing independent projects.
Make sure you are interested in the applied area or this course won't be enjoyable. Otherwise, if you read the papers and participate in class this course can be extremely valuable!
