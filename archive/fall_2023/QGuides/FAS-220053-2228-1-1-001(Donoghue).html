I have complicated feelings about this class – on the one hand, the texts are interesting, the teaching staff is extremely kind, and the class is a nice environment. On the other hand, I consistently felt confused with the language week after week and found that since each class time is spent similarly (going over the translations you completed for homework) and the sections are often a little repetitive, if you are not teaching yourself out of class, your knowledge of old english will not really increase week after week. I always read the textbook (our main source of learning the grammatical parts of OE) but it was difficult to truly learn from it, and I wished we had sessions dedicated to learning different parts of the language as one might have in a typical language class. Additionally, the final tested skills in ways we had not been practicing them throughout the semester. I think the class has great potential, there are just a few organizational quirks, at least this year. Old English is really interested linguistically and historically, so we would often get into discussions about that in class and section, which were extremely interesting, but I felt that sometimes our actual learning of the language was put on the backburner.
Fun! Prof D and Seif are wonderful, and really passionate. Charm project is a great time
The course is a bit difficult but is still doable. I think if memorization is difficult for you then this may not be the best course to take, but I feel as though that is likely the case with most languages courses. Professor Donoghue is really passionate about Old English which makes it really fun for students. Obtaining exposure to really old texts is also a super interesting aspect of the course! Learning about a different time period and being able to see for yourself how much the English language has changed is really cool. As for the workload, the translations each week take longer than you would think. For example, it would take me several hours to translate 13 sentences because of having to go through the textbook glossary to see what the words mean. Also, it is absolutely necessary to consistently study the grammar rules throughout the semester in order to prepare for the final exam. All in all, though, if you are looking for an interesting course to fulfill the language requirement or simply to take on your own time, I would say this one is a good option.
Pros: Prof. Donoghue is honestly such a great guy! He is super responsive and does not want the students to be stressed out. He listens to concerns when they are voiced and then adjusts. The workload is honestly very manageable throughout the semester –– a lot of the stuff is graded on completion. It can feel like a gened in a fun way –– you pick up some random fun facts along the semester. Anecdotally, this class is definitely less work + stress than other languages for most of the semester (EXCEPT THE FINAL!). Both Prof Donoghue and Seif are awesome people who have your best interests at heart, are always willing to meet, etc.

Cons: 1) The setup for the final was extremely stressful and unnecessarily disorganized. We did not know the format until basically a week beforehand, and after being instructed to use many resources for translation throughout the year (online dictionaries, glossaries, etc.) the final was closed not. This honestly does not make sense to me at all! Why are we tested on a skill/in a way we did not get any instruction or practice in all semester? I STRONGLY believe the exams in this class should be open note.

2) The organization of section, etc. is not ideal. We never get handouts or organized outlines about what we need to know, even verbally. Seif goes over the information in an interesting and helpful way, but its never a logical/consistent path –– we hyperfocus on some stuff and skim over other. I think this class could be extremely improved by the addition of handouts regarding the grammar skills we need to know. Most people in the class are very confused on the content for pretty much all of the semester.
English aged like wine – I'm grateful we don't have this much grammar in modern English, but appreciate seeing how it was before.
Look out for the semester's theme! It rotates. Also, you should be a self–motivated language learner.
You are going to be confused, and you are going to feel lost. This is okay! Your peers will be an invaluable resource in this course, and talking through issues will help more than you might think it would.
This class was really fun. Just expect a ton of memorization. And the class will be really easy at first and then suddenly get really hard, but only in terms of memorization and translating. You won't have to know how to speak Old English.
This course is super interesting but suffered due to the lack of organization. You will not know what your grade in this course is shaping up to be until after the final. The quizzes, while they might initially seem easy, will become significantly more challenging and a large stressor. This is unfortunate as these stressors take away from how cool this material is and how fun being in class is. I would recommend taking this pass–fail so you can enjoy it to the fullest without worrying about the grading.
