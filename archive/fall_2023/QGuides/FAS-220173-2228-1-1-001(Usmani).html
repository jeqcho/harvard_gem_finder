Take this course! This is by far the best class I've taken at Harvard. At a school where we are often rewarded for expressing opinions that align with everyone else's, this course will challenge you to think deeper about what you believe and teach you how to form strong arguments against what other's believe — in a way that will convince them, rather than just in a way that would convince other liberal minds. Amazing course if you're interested in criminal justice.
TAKE THIS CLASS! the most worthwhile course I've taken here so far—it's truly a gem in the sense that you'll leave class everyday feeling like you engaged in the most intellectual discussion ever, because of the way everyone is so invested in making class time an eye–opening experience. I took this as an elective, and I'm so glad I didn't drop out of it, because it taught me how to defend any given argument and correspondingly how to critique an argument (even if it seems airtight). Chris and Adaner are the GOATS... really hope they can teach together again. truly a unique experience witnessing the blend of sociology and philosophy. I for sure gained a new appreciation for how theory and empirics can and ought to work together when tackling difficult and controversial issues. this was always the class I looked forward to the most this semester—it was an honor being in a room full of such inspired and thoughtful people. I'm not normally this awestruck by a class, but this is not your average class at all. I often found myself talking about the topics we discussed in class outside of class, which is pretty rare for me. all in all, I loved this course.
This is the best class at Harvard. Adaner and Chris might just be the most caring and insightful profs on campus. It blends philosophy and sociology, normative and empirical. Your understanding of mass incarceration, racial inequality, deontology, normative ethics, the war on drugs, racial capitalism, police, criminal justice reform, and abolition will be radically changed and strengthened! Take it, you will not regret it. And, it's not too intensive, very few assignments, the course just requires your attention (and it captures it very well). Read the readings! You'll be amazed.
Do the readings. Engage. Don't be afraid to put yourself out there and argue.
I would tell them to not take everything the professors say as fact because the point of the course is to question them
You need to be open minded. This course will teach you how to be a better argumentative writer while exposing you to the professors' years of research in the field. Both Chris and Adaner are very accessible, they are very approachable and office hours are very casual and useful! Take the class!
This course is really unlike any other course. The structure (and no tech in the classroom) definitely lends itself to particularly engaging discussions in class, the professors definitely bring a new and fresh perspective on issues of crime/punishment/policing and actively facilitate and encourage productive debate. At the same time, the course feels at times like it's completely divorced from the reality of the criminal justice system and exists very much in the world of academia.
The class has lots of theory (especially at the beginning) so be weary of that. Also, it may feel like you are learning about the same thing in a different form. But overall, I enjoyed the class and the material we were taught is very useful to the real world of policing.
This class is so amazing I can't recommend it enough. Truth be told, I was intimated when I saw this class is taught by an Undergraduate and Law School Professor. However, I think that dynamic made the class that much more enjoyable. Engaging and talking with law school students about such an important subject matter improved my understanding of the subject. However, this class isn't a gem. Nonetheless, the class is so amazing that it genuinely feels like one.
This is the best class I have taken at Harvard and I would recommend to everyone! Professor Lewis and Professor Usmani present very interesting ideas throughout this course and facilitate debate in a way that truly makes you contemplate society and deep philosophical issues. I loved this class and wish it was longer as I am sad that it is over.
Definitely a wonderful course! Super interesting and engaging, only major efforts outside of class are short assignments, readings, and the final but all were interesting enough to stay enthusiastic.
This class will challenge many of the beliefs that you hold about why people commit crimes and what is the fairest way to simultaneously reduce crime and incarceration. While I personally disagreed with some ideas brought up in class, Chris and Adaner created a great learning environment, where we had the opportunity to argue against their perspective. I would even say that I benefitted even more from this course precisely because the professors presented views contrary to my beliefs. This way I had the chance to reevaluate my stances and strengthen my reasoning. Also, the readings were very balanced, so each class we were exposed to different sides of the most pressing debates about the criminal justice system. Lastly, the section was super informative and helpful due to Ash's extensive knowledge of the criminal system in the US. All things considered, this was a great class that I strongly recommend to anyone.
