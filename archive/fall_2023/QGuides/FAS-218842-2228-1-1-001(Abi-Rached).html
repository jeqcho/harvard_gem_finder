This course is awesome! It teaches you a lot about the militarization of the medical field. One reservation is that the class this year was too big and it wasn’t a seminar style, but more like a two hour lecture with sections.
Not your normal class on war! So engaging, not boring, very thoughtful and fun to have discussions and debates!
Overall, I strongly recommend this course to anyone with interest in the subject. The themes discussed each week by both Professor Abi–Rached and the student presentations are incredibly thought–provoking, and raise a lot of good questions on how the interplay between war and medicine, from attacks on medical humanitarian workers to the trauma experienced by soldiers, has operated. The sources (we watched a few documentaries in addition to doing readings) also gave me a lot of insight into the various themes of the course. While the content can sometimes be a bit graphic or emotionally draining, Professor Abi–Rached is very kind and supportive, and always gave me good direction when I was thinking about the sources or assignments for the course. Speaking of, assignments were difficult at times but overall very manageable. Therefore, I recommend this course to anyone with interest in the history of medicine, war, and its connection.
This is a great class. It can be heavy, but is very engaging and interesting, and Joelle is fantastic. I loved it.
Dr. Abi–Rached is an incredible professor. Schedule office hours with her. Get to know her. She will likely take notes about you while she's talking to you and it will make you feel special. She is such an incredible person and SO enthusiastic about the content.
This course provided me with an incredibly transformative experience. Professor Abi–Rached's passion for the material was truly infectious and inspiring. Each week's readings answered previous questions I had regarding the relationships of war and medicine but then prompted me to ask even more. The course materials and presentations (both by Professor Abi–Rached and by students) were always very engaging, and I never failed to leave class without having at least one moment of sitting in awe. The midterm and final papers (10 pages and 20 pages respectively!) both seemed daunting at first, but I found them to be so rewarding; I would even say that my final paper for this class has been my favorite so far in college. You will learn so much not only about the complex intersections of war and medicine, but also the dependency of medicine and society. I recommend all interested individuals to take this class, even if (nd perhaps, especially if) you are not a History and Science major!
I LOVED this course! You should take it if you can! Joelle is an EXCELLENT instructor and facilitates a great environment for conversation. You will learn so much from classroom discussions and the texts that you read.
This class is very very chill from week to week. However, when the essays are due they are a lot of work, and you would do yourself a big favor by starting on them early. Class is moderately engaging. The student presentations are a bit boring if you've done the readings. If you haven't, if you pay attention you'll learn about them though. The theme changes each week so you investment in the material can vary a lot from week to week based on the readings. You also can very well get by in the class by just skimming the readings, so this is a class that you will get out what you put in to some degree. Overall, though, I think the class is worthwhile if you're interested in the topic. The weekly readings and movies are often really interesting and engaging, Professor Abi–Rached is extremely knowledgeable and kind, and it isn't too difficult.
This class is wonderful, especially due to the depth of knowledge Professor Abi–Rached brings to the class. We watched movies, read books, had enlightening discussions, and got to hear from a variety of guest speakers who hold positions in humanitarian aid and academia all around the world.
The readings can be emotionally heavy and often disturbing, but they are also very valuable works that show important perspectives and stories that should be told.
If you are interested in history, medicine, humanitarianism, and the intersection of health and society, take this course. Professor Abi–Rached is great. She is very passionate about the issues we talk about, and she is always able to generate a very fruitful discussion. In many ways, this course is eye–opening. Some of the material we go through is kind of intense because we are looking at many injustices and tragedies that come from war. However, I believe all of the reading was incredible interesting and worth reading. Lastly, the weekly comittment is not too stressful. Just think critically about the readings and be willing to participate in the class discussions.
If this course is capped, then definitely take it. I loved the course. Otherwise, take this course if you want to work on a semester long research project at the intersection of health, migration, war, and more.
