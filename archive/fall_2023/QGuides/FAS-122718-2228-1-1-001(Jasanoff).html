What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class has a demanding workload but is ultimately very satisfying. Prof Jasanoff is a leader in this field, and it is a great opportunity to get her account of how sts developed, what it draws from and how different strands complement each other
This course will undoubtedly expand your perspectives. It is a valuable experience to have under your belt no matter what your post–grad or career plans are!
I think its an essential class in the contemporary moment.
This will be the best class you ever take at Harvard. There is no other course that allows for as much critical engagement and reflection with what it means to live in a world so prominently shaping by and shaping of science and technology — without descending into techno–fetishism nor abandoning S&T all together. If you've ever felt at the intersection of questions or interests that include S&T, how we govern emerging technologies, the future of democracy, the state of truth, the state of knowledge, quantification, constitutionalism/citizenship and/or individuality and collectivity, this class will be what helps you make sense of these as inextricably linked dimensions of questions of past, present, and future. Science, Power and Politics is so tremendous because it's not just about "studying" science/technology, power, and politics — it's about boldly interrogating and aspirationally envisioning the types of worlds being articulated as ones worth living in, what might be at stake in that, and what we should do about it. There are very few classes at Harvard the strike at the heart of the human condition, and this is far and away the best academic and personal experience one could ask for in this regard (among many others). Sheila Jasanoff is a professor unlike any other at Harvard, and her brilliance, generosity, wit, and kindness can not be overstated in how legendary and incredible this stalwart of a class has been and continues to be over the years she has taught it. Take it! It may be intense, unlike anything you've done before at Harvard, or a lot of work — but these are take it "because" reasons, not "in spite of" ones. Your time at Harvard should not be an intellectual nor personal wasteland, and it's SPP and classes like it that turn a wasteland into a flourishing garden, trees of knowledge and all.
Take this course if you either subscribe to "STS speak"––willing constantly reference the themes and analytic frameworks in your discourse––or if you are confident enough to engage in the intellectually stimulating material and explore the intersections of the field with your own voice.
Invest the time to do the readings and this will be an incredibly enriching and fulfilling experience
Be prepared for a lot of reading and you are doing yourself and your classmates a disservice if you do not participate in the discussions
Do all of the readings every week and meet with your peers before class (the morning of, or the day before) to discuss them. Read responses and discussion posts in advance.
