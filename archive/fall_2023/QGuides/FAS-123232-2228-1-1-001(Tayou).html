Definitely professor–dependent, but if Salim is teaching it, definitely take! He makes the class quite accessible, and he's always so friendly and open to answer questions. Workload is extremely manageable, probably the easiest math grad class offered, but you still gain a lot out of it.
I liked this class, though maybe I would've liked it more if I had attended lectures more. Salim is super nice and a pretty good lecturer, though lectures sometimes feel more like reading a textbook. This might just be the subject matter though. The problem sets were fun and reasonably quick!
Math 221 is a very accessible graduate–level course for advanced undergraduates to take. It will be essential to have a strong background in algebra before the course, and I personally recommend (while not totally necessary) taking Math 123 to ensure a strong background in Galois theory. This is a great introduction to a foundational subject as well as some advanced topics, and Salim makes the subject very easy to digest with his skilled lectures. If you are interested in taking a graduate–level course in mathematics, I strongly recommend Math 221!
It is worth taking.
I would not recommend commutative algebra to someone who is not planning on taking more math courses, not because of the course but because commutative algebra by itself doesn't seem as interesting as other topics. But, for someone who is planning on taking many more math courses, it seems like essential material that is taught very clearly and well and I would highly recommend the course.
Math 221 is one of the best classes I've taken – really interesting content, fantastic lectures, and reasonable grading.
Salim is absolutely amazing. He puts a lot of time into the class and it really shows. Salim has amazing spirit, both as a human and as a mathematician. He has been one of the best teachers during my time here. Go to his office hours just to even chat with him! He is so warm and inviting. Ask him all your burning questions. Even if you think they're dumb, Salim is always receptive. This was one of my favorite math classes by far, and it is really because of Salim.

A note on the material outside of Salim: people debate about whether you should take algebraic geometry (232) first or commutative algebra first. A lot of algebraic geometry uses commutative algebra, and a lot of commutative algebra will feel unmotivated if you haven't seen algebraic geometry. My honest opinion is that, if you can handle it, you should try to take this class concurrently with Math 232 (algebraic geometry). This might not be for everyone since taking both at the same time seriously is a lot of work. But if you're looking to go into math, it's worth it in my opinion. This is because if you take 232 without 221, you'll be drowning in commutative algebra that you don't know of. If you take 221 first, it might feel dry and uninteresting, and you'll have to wait a year before you see it in action in the context of algebraic geometry.
This class is honestly the best class I have ever taken here at Harvard. Salim is the best lecturer you'll ever get to see in a math class, and the material was very useful and foundational to all things algebra. I went into the class with only a basic understanding of group theory from freshman year (and I did not take math 123), but still the class was incredibly accommodating and I was able to keep up with the course material pretty well. 10/10 would recommend, especially if Salim is still teaching it next year.
