Take this course! Super helpful in terms of real–life project ideation and creation. Better than CS 120,121, and 124. PLEASE TAKE!
Take this course! It was a great course for me as I was exploring the CS world. The course is basically project–based. The course is doable and generally only gets busy through tedious work during weeks of important milestones. There are some benefits of taking this course early in your college years (soph yr) and some disadvantages. Most individuals taking the course were jr/sr and I felt generally those who had more background in applications like Figma. So I felt that I got more out of the course because I did not have prior background in applications like Figma but it was a learning curve at the beginning of the course because of the heavy reliance on Figma for project prototypes.
its very encouraging and best to train design thinking
Lecture content is interesting, and Krzysztof is a great professor who makes the material very engaging. The class is composed of 3 group projects that aren't quite paced well, especially the first two projects. You are also randomly assigned teammates for the first two projects, which can make or break your experience in the class, especially if you run into personality clashes and/or students who straight up do not care about doing well in the class.
This is a great introduction to designing for user demographics that are different from your own. I learned a lot about being able to empathize with user perspectives and design both useful and usable solutions to their problems. I would have liked more in class design exercises so I could get immediate feedback on how I'm applying very abstract concepts we've learned in class (as opposed to group projects alone, where we have to wait longer for feedback and might not be able to implement changes in time).
Very interesting class that prepares you well for a career in design and/or product management. You’ll likely put in more time than advertised, primarily because the projects are interesting and you’ll want to put effort into them.
Super interesting course! Group work is stressful, but your TF is a grade resource to navigate this
It's a great class, but you really get out what you put into it. It's easy to put forward the bare minimum amount of work (especially in a group setting) and scrape by just fine, but if you really want to take away some of the bigger picture lessons it's going to involve you putting in some effort. To quote a former student's advice before I started the class "It's really easy to pass the class, it's almost impossible to get an A without really striving for it"
The weekly milestones for the projects have too much busy work. Besides that the class is solid. Your group matters a lot to how pleasant/manageable the class is.
Please do not be deterred by this class having CS in its title! This is a great course for anymore remotely interested in the application design process.
This class isn't hard so long as you're putting in some effort, and it turned out to be much more interesting than I had originally thought it would be. I recommend even if you're not a big design person, as I'm not either and still found the class interesting and fun to take.
If you've never taken a CS class and are curious about design, take this class!! It's a really beginner friendly introduction to HCI and product design, and you pick up some pretty useful skills! You become really familiar with Figma (you don't need to know how to use it before taking this course, all of it is taught in class), and you have the chance to work closely with your classmates on each of the three projects. The lectures themselves are a hit or miss; sometimes they're really engaging and provide pretty new and useful information (this was mostly in the beginning of the semester), but as the course progresses, the lectures themselves become a bit repetitive. I found the most rewarding part of the course to be in the projects themselves. If you're interested in a pretty light, but fun workload that requires quite a bit of creativity and teamwork, this is a great class for just that!
I 100% recommend taking this course! If you're interested in design or product development in any way, shape, or form, this course will give you a nice introduction to a wide range of concepts all while giving you lots of time to play and develop some interesting product concepts. You don't need to have any prior software engineering knowledge and will be a good first step in exploring the broader curriculum that Prof. Gajos has developed.
CS79 is a great course if you're willing to work hard for it. It's a different kind of hard work than a more theoretical/technical CS course — it's more process driven and is similar to being a product manager. If you're a work horse who excels at non–programming work at volume (think "Incoming Investment Banking Analyst," "Incoming Consultant," or "Incoming APM" on LinkedIn), then this is likely the course for you.

The material is quite interesting, the lectures are well designed, and the staff cares a lot about your success. But from the start, the manner in which Prof. Gajos talks about grading makes it feel somewhat arbitrary and capricious. Even after submitting all the work on time and receiving solid A–range grades, I'm still unsure about whether I'll receive an A or A–. Obviously not the worst problem in the world — but when every grade counts for Grad School / GPA honors, some certainty would be helpful.
It's an easy class in a sense that there's no conceptually difficult thing. Sometimes, interviewing people for milestones might be boring, but apart from that it's a great class for potentially discovering your interest in product management/ Design.
Assignments are tedious, time–consuming, and very redundant at times. Be prepared for quite a bit of busy work; however, the lectures are intriguing and course material otherwise is very cool.
Take this class!! Though considered a CS "gem", this course is extremely useful for future SWEs, PMs, Designers, or any tech–adjacent working person. Lectures and sections are fun and interesting and assignments are as fun as you let them be (you can kind of get creative). No CS experience necessary – many successful non–STEM majors take this course.
Go to lectures, engage with the material, and see how much from this class you can take with you to other parts of your life. Communicate with your team – you will grow as a team member and leader on a team.
Helpful for all CS students to take, at times trivial but still valuable. No coding work and very different from your usual CS class.
This class was great––take it if you have any interest in the design process and the user–oriented aspects of design. It is not a hard class at all (pretty frequent assignments but they are not stressful or difficult), and I found it very enjoyable. It is all group projects which can be frustrating, especially if you don't get a good team, but it's also nice to get to work with people and you accomplish more than you would alone. Especially for a creative class, having people to develop ideas with is useful.
The content is super interesting, but be prepared to put in a pretty significant chunk of work every weekend to complete the milestones (which involve a lot of interviewing, writing, etc.)
This is a very involved, team–centered class that will test your collaboration and communication skills. May seem like busy work, but if you are engaged in the content, you will learn a lot.
You get what you put into this class. You can probably scrape by if you want, but it's a lot more meaningful if you put in the work to learn what you're interested in.
Great class for learning about product design from a tech, design, and/or professional perspective, whichever you want to get out of this class. You get a lot of exposure to hands–on project–based learning. Tbh it just changes your perspective on observing how products are conceptualized, designed, and made in the real world, outside of tech and this class, and applicable to any type of creative field.

There is no coding so you don't really have to rack your brain that much compared to other CS classes, but it still is a substantial amount of work because they make you write up weekly milestones and present them in sections every week.
Phenomenal course! Definitely weeks where I spent far too long on Zoom calls with my groups, but overall worth it!
While this class may be known as the gem of the CS department at Harvard, it definitely isn't easy. You will be working a lot on several things on a weekly basis, it all depends on who your team members are. If you're lucky and get competent people for each project assignment, this course will be a breeze. If you are unfortunate enough to get people who are lazy, plan to put in extra work. The teaching staff are really receptive to feedback, so make sure to go to them if you have concerns/a bad team member. Overall it's a really great class and a nice introduction to Figma.
