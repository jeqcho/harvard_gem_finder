This class is just really annoying. Sometimes it's not even worth trying to understand, just ask for help immediately.
15c was a good course and I definitely preferred it to 15b. 3 hour labs are long but much more interesting than the previous lab components in 15a/b. Matteo is a good lecturer, Daniel's practical demonstrations are great and Kees ran one of the best sections I've had at Harvard (worth noting that attendance is required at everything excluding OHs though. Normally this would bug me as I often don't use section in classes, but I would've gone to this section anyway so no big deal really). Midterms were generally pretty accessible, but some of the PSETs were pretty brutal and I certainly wouldn't have survived without OHs and teamwork with friends.
fun course for a sophomore or freshman interested in physical phenomena but not studying physics.
Go to physics night
Very interesting course, but structure made it more difficult than it may have needed to be. There is no complete textbook, just a draft of one without practice problems. In–class demos are fantastic, made it really easy to stay engaged all lecture. Lab component is tough conceptually but really cool. If professor Mitrano is teaching, just be careful to double check the formulas you copy down. Also his office hours are the least helpful and may actually lead you to incorrect answers, so probably go to the CA or TF office hours for psets. Definitely recommend collaborating with your peers. The social atmosphere of the class was really great too.
Don't skip this class, there is a ton of valuable physics to be learned that you would just miss.
If you're reading this you probably have to take this course anyway. Just do it. It's more well–taught than 15b and makes a lot of use of real–world examples. Plus you get to play with powerful lasers! What could be better.
This is a hard class and you really have to put in a good chunk of time on your own to make sure you understand the material as you learn it because it all builds on itself.
If you LOVE physics and have a lot of questions, take this course! Matteo knew his shit and the in class demonstrations were amazing but this course was SO. CHALLENGING.
Even my ARC tutor who took the class last year found this years to be much more challenging. If all you've taken is 15a and 15b, you're in for a rough semester (unless you're some sort of genius idk)
Physics night and office hours definitely make it doable to get the psets done, but you need to have the time to attend them and be willing to work and collaborate. Make friends quickly in the class. The midterms are surprisingly reasonable, but the final is hard :(
Take the class, even as an elective. You will learn cool things about the world around you and the smaller size of the class make it so that it is very easy to collaborate and get to know professor and TF.
15c taught by Professor Mitrano is an interesting and rewarding course. It's certainly more difficult than previous iterations of the course, but it is worth it. I appreciated and truly understood the course content in way that I haven't in most of my other courses. Lectures are great; Professor Mitrano is a very effective lecturer who is engaging and organized. Section was great; our TF was kind and fun and section material often helped a lot with the problem sets. The problem sets were also great; they were difficult but interesting, and they required application of concepts in an interesting way. The lab component of this class was terrible, so for 3 hours per week, we were miserable (lab was unnecessarily long and slow, and we spent a lot of time just sitting around staring into space). The class in general was also rather inflexible regarding getting sick. For example, if you got COVID, and you told the lab professor that you had to miss it, he would simply not reply and it would affect your participation grade. Otherwise, this class was great.
