I'm conflicted about this class. I feel like it was trying to do way too many things at once. My favorite thing about this course was the lecture content –– learning developmental processes and stem cell therapies were interesting and exciting. Unfortunately, at its heart, this course is a genetic and experimental design class, which I didn't really know I had signed up for. For all of the tests, you don't really have to know any of the content taught in lecture, so I ended up not really learning the part of this class that I found most enjoyable. Instead, tests are almost entirely designing experiments and interpreting experimental data. This would be fine, if we were given any material to practice this outside of the practice tests. I did badly on the first test, because I was so confused when I showed up to the exam and none of it was on the content we had been learning and I had been studying. They also throw a metric ton of information at you during the 3 lectures a week, but none of it really matters in the end, and they gloss over tons of things. I would much rather have a deeper understanding of a few topics than a surface–level understanding of many. Also, there was a serious lack of resources to help you succeed. There often weren't any office hours up to two days before the exams, and CA office hours only happen before bigger assignments, like psets or papers. They are also horrible at communicating information, so I found myself showing up to office hours that had been canceled, or rescheduled, or conflicted with a section. TFs also often gave me conflicting answers, and sometimes didn't seem to be completely knowledgeable about the course content. The workload is really strange as well –– some weeks you do absolutely nothing, and other weeks you do absolutely everything. Because of this, I fell behind and had to cram before the exams. And for those exams, it's really a race against time more than anything else. Even if you understand and know the genetic systems, if you don't understand the question prompts on the first read–through, you're pretty much done for. There's no extra time to go back to questions or revise your answers. So, I wouldn't recommend taking this class unless you really have to. I admittedly learned a lot about experimental design, even if I didn't really want to. But I am truly sad that we didn't focus more on the rest of the content, which is what I was expecting this course to focus on. And my biggest frustration with this class! They're obsessed with the rhetoric that you don't need any memorization to succeed, which is simply untrue. To understand the genetic systems and the experimental results, you have to understand a lot of other background information, and you learn that content through memorization of the lecture slides. This class was hard for me, and maybe you'll like it more than me, but just manage your expectations before going in.
