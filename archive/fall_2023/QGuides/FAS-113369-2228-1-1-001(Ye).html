I haven't met anyone who recommends this textbook – John Lee's books and Loring Tu's books are helpful, and Frederick Schuller's lectures on YouTube are great.
230a is often more computational than abstract, and at many points feels unmotivated and not so rigorous
Don't be fooled by the fact that you don't require a lot of prerequisites for this class. If you have never seen any of the material here before, much of it will fly over your head like it flew over mine. Dr. Ye is a really nice person and fun to talk to 1 on 1, but his lecturing leaves much to be desired. He speeds through most of the beginning material to leave more time for the later topics on principal bundles and connections, which is quite a bad idea since by the time we reach said topics, we understand so little of what is going on earlier that there is a lot of difficulty understanding the later topics. The last two classes felt like absolute pop science to me, as he started dishing out many ideas from algebraic topology in addition to many modern theorems from differential geometry. I had no idea what I was supposed to get out of those classes except to "see" what the Yang–Mills equation was about. Furthermore, the textbook (Taubes) is really bad, and if you don't understand the material during lecture, don't even try to consult the textbook because it will definitely confuse you even more. Since he follows Taubes, trying to use another textbook (say Lee or Tu) proves to be quite challenging as well since the structure of the textbook is very different from the course material. Overall, an interesting class if you already know a bunch of differential geometry, but if you don't know jack like I did, be prepared to work very hard to gain very little.

Fan also provided no intuition for the concepts that were introduced, so it was not helpful at all to go to lecture. I came in very excited about learning differential geometry and I exited the course extremely disillusioned. The math department needs to start taking teaching seriously. Many classes in the department are so poorly taught compared to any other department.
