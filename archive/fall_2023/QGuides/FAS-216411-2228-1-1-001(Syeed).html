What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an amazing class for people with little to no experience in filmmaking but want the opportunity to learn, co–create, and expand their ideas of what documentary filmmaking can be! The instructors are wonderful and the class is inspiring and so fun.
Take it!! Musa is an amazing filmmaker and has so much knowledge and expertise that he is able to deliver to you at any stage of your work without scrutinizing it. This could be one of the best introductions to film, and it is a self–selective class which brings in some of the most thoughtful and genuine people on campus. Definitely worth your time, and treat it with the same effort as your other classes. It will make your end project feel that much better.
This is an excellent class to be exposed to a style and ethic of filmmaking that centers community members and does so in a way that is accessible to tools already available to you. If you haven't done anything with film in the past, I think this is a great introduction. You should do your best to watch the films suggested for the course and to just keep watching film throughout your semester because it really helps to build up your idea of visual language so you can then be more aware of what you want to film for your own project. And this class is better for students if you really utilize the office hours and bring in the shots or clips that you've taken to get advice on how to edit them, reshoot them, or build them into your broader narrative. Would completely recommend this class!
Great course! Musa has eyes like a hawk! You can really learn a lot!
This class was always the highlight of my week. Musa and Kathryn are really supportive and committed teachers. You will come away from the class having watched incredible films/stories and trying your hand at making one yourself
