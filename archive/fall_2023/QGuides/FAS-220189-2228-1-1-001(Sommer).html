What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great class if you are interested in the theory and philosophy driving a new and innovative form of pedagogy. However, it is more so focused on the philosophical aspects as opposed to pedagogy in practice, so tailor your choice accordingly.
If you want to learn about education as a matter of policy or a social science, this probably isn't for you, but if you're interested in the philosophy of non–traditional and arts–based education, you will likely love it! Doris is a great facilitator in seminar, and her interdisciplinary approach and background makes the class feel fresh and exciting. This class will force you to reconsider what it means to learn and to educate and will expose you to a wide variety of texts and thinkers. Readings are manageable and in–class discussions are always interesting, with some great guest speakers coming in. If you are interesting in education as a matter of the humanities, take this class,
