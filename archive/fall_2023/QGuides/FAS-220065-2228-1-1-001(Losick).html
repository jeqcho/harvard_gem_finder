What a fantastic class!!!
What an amazing class! Excellent lectures from prof Losick that blend analyzing papers with fun personal anecdotes and stories about the greats of molecular biology. This class really made me appreciate the knowledge and technology we have now, and what it took to develop it. It really is astounding how cleverly the experiments were designed in the absence of the techniques we have readily available today (e.g. PCR, sequencing, restriction cloning, etc.). The workload is not overbearing and the final paper is very open, which gives you the opportunity to study in more detail an aspect of molecular bio you find interesting. Also, Richard Losick has the best guest speakers. You won't regret taking this class!
I highly recommend this course; Prof Losick is super sweet and open to helping you, and always tells really interesting stories.
The course load is very manageable and the all the course staff are very easy to approach. Truly a gem
This is a must take class if you're interested in molecular biology–– Richard Losick is an intelligent and kind professor. That being said, the course material can be somewhat difficult so I recommend only taking if you have an understanding of molecular biology / the field broadly and want to deepen your knowledge.
ENJOY this course. Listen and soak in every word that Dr. Losick has to share. You will learn so much from his storytelling and expertise.
This course was FANTASTIC AND I CANNOT RECOMMEND IT ENOUGH!!! I am a junior, and MCB 149 was probably my favorite class at Harvard so far out of the 20 I have taken at this point. Professor Losick is such an engaging and interesting lecturer, and he has so many entertaining stories from the history of molecular biology. It is truly an unparalleled experience that you really won't get anywhere else – so much of this history happened in the very building (even the very room) that you take the course in.
Great course. Take it if you've ever been curious about the histories of the discoveries of concepts that we take for granted today: DNA replication, RNA splicing, central dogma, ... It is a chronological walkthrough of major discoveries in molecular biology, addressing 1–2 per lecture. Occasionally, the person who performed the experiments and authored the groundbreaking paper (and won a Nobel Prize) will give a guest lecture about their own work. Truly an amazing experience that you won't want to pass up.
I highly highly recommend taking this class! The only background you need is the LS series (as you need to be able to understand and read gels and other basic biology experiments), and Professor Losick is one of the sweetest, kindest, and most generous professors at Harvard! He is also SO incredibly passionate about the subject, and his 'buddies,' Nobel laureates and world–class scientists (as well as himself, too!) come to guest lecture... this is truly a once–in–a–lifetime experience. You emerge from this class so much more appreciative of the foundation of molecular and cellular biology as a field, especially learning much more about just how complex (yet also elegant) the experiments we have encountered for so long in textbooks alone can be.
Take this class!
I recommend this course for your MCB concentration requirement! The workload is pretty light until you start your essay and the readings are interesting.
I highly recommend this course! Professor Losick is an incredible educator and is extremely passionate about this material! Professor Losick presents a personal side to the study of the history of molecular biology that you will not find anywhere else. This is one of the courses at Harvard that every student should take! Whether you study science, history, or truly any field, you will find this course extremely enjoyable and interesting. Our society depends on a number of different developments that have come from the field of molecular biology. Professor Losick will guide you on a tour through the ages to study these incredible discoveries and learn about and meet some of the incredible scientists who made biology what it is today. For the final project, you will have the opportunity to research a scientist who made an impact in molecular biology. Professor Losick also helps students find scientists and stories that will be meaningful to them. For example, in my final project, I studied an Italian scientist of whom I had never heard prior to the course, but who epitomized resilience and was personally meaningful to me. Throughout the course and for the final project, Professor Losick is incredibly helpful outside of class and his office hours are always an enjoyable time where you will learn a great deal and get advice from one of the world's foremost scientists. I cannot recommend this course enough!
This is one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard! Rich is incredibly knowledgable, inspiring, and kind. Taking this course reaffirmed my desire to pursue a career in research. The course was easy, but I took so much away from the class. The best gem you can take!
Take this course if the subject matter interests you even a little bit! Rich is a wonderful lecturer and he puts a lot of work and care to the course. Also, we had some amazing guest speakers come talk to us which made the class even more engaging. I would recommend this highly just to get to know Rich a little better!
This is one of the most entertaining and interesting courses I have taken at Harvard!
This is a great way to learn the stories behind the famous experiments in biology! Professor Losick has lived through most of these discoveries and knows a lot of the figures personally, so it's always fun hearing his insights about what occurred. Definitely not the easiest class: the daily quizzes are often challenging and can take multiple hours, and the final term paper took a significant amount of time. At the end of the day, this is still a biology course and should be treated as one.
