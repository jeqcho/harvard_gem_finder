Gist of the class: This class is a very chill introductory physics class. It seems to be geared towards premeds who need to get their physics requirement out of the way. I am not premed, but I still find this class a good class to take if you just want to know the gist of physics without having to face really intense stuff. Physics is spoon fed to you, but not in a terrible way. In the lectures, the instructors help you derive certain equations. Then, for homework we have this thing call Achieve, which is just a bunch of practice problems that use those equations. And then, after those practice problems, we have a pset that is basically those practice problems and concept but a more advanced and critical version. The practice problems are a good gateway to the psets. The homework load is not bad at all. Each week, we have 20–30 practice problems on Achieve, which can take anywhere from 1–3 hours (depends on how focused you are) and then there are 3–5 problems on the PSET.

Teaching staff: The instructors are very nice, funny, and supportive. I had something that made me take a nosedive in the second half of the semester, and the instructors were understanding and helped me get back on track. The TFs are great. They know what they are doing, and they staff really long office hours that you can drop by whenever. The lab TFs are also very helpful during the lab times.

Resources: There is not a shortage in resources available. For exams, not only do we have practice exams and solutions, but we also have a walkthrough video that we can watch. I think that is incredibly helpful because sometimes solutions don't show the thought process of how the answer was found. If given the option of online and in person section, I think it is better to take the in person section. I took the online section, and what I realized was that if I had questions, I couldn't get them answered in the moment.

Lab: There aren't too many lab sessions during the semester, and while the block is like three hours for lab, I don't think my partner and I ever used up all 3 hours. That being said, the labs aren't always in line with what we were learning in class. There was a bit of a focus on understanding validity/accuracy tests instead of the actual physics concept. The guides make the lab easy to follow, and for anything we don't understand, lab TF is always there.
