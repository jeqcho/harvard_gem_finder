This class is a fantastic opportunity to criticality examine the nexus of various global resource issues as they relate to the environment in a practical data driven way. The class is very driven by results and discerning practical implications from theory, and accordingly there is ample opportunity to examine real issues as they are occurring.
Peter is an excellent lecturer and so knowledgeable about the subject material, which is super interdisciplinary and topical. Class structure alternates between lecture about a paper and lab, both of which are great. The readings are interesting and very topical, and workload is super manageable, as long as you are working on the semester–long group project evenly throughout. The teaching staff are incredibly supportive throughout the semester. The semester–long group project can either be wonderful or terrible depending on who you end up working with, so definitely start thinking about that early on.
Take this class, don't expect it to be super easy but it is entirely worthwhile. The final project is a great learning experience and it's worthwhile putting a lot of effort in during the first half of the semester because the teaching team offers a ton of support. This class is a supportive way to learn data science!
Definitely take this class if you're even vaguely interesting in the interdisciplinary nature of climate change. This is a great way to get your feet wet with various forms of modeling, teaches you how to rigorously and constructively read a paper, is run by an INCREDIBLE teaching staff, and has a great final project set–up that starts in ~week 3 that really teaches you a lot (and is how more STEM classes should be structured!!).
Take this class! Best I have taken at Harvard. It's a significant work load, but the topics are fascinating, assignments interesting and final project is really fun (make sure you have a good group though!). Professor Huybers is a wonderful instructor, who is humble about his work (which is a refreshing change of pace) and passionate about timely topics and making a change.
I would recommend you take this class if you have some sort of initial background in coding, do NOT take this class if you have never coded before or if you expect it to teach you how to code. Office hours were very useful for the coding homework and the TFs for this course were INCREDIBLE. Professor Huybers is also incredible and I really enjoyed the weekly meetings with him for the group project. If you are not a fan of group projects I would also suggest that you dont take this course as the majority of work and learning comes from the project.
You get in what you put out!!!! Caro and Lucas are incredibly supportive for coding help, and this course is truly informative and will open your eyes to so many different ways of thinking. I absolutely recommend this course; it is one of the best structured courses I have taken here. It is also a great introduction to research.
Overall, I learned a lot from this course and appreciate its practical focus on applying data to environmental questions. The week to week work was fairly manageable and interesting (roughly 1 weekly pset and 1 weekly reading response). However, the group project was a fairly stressful component of this course because it is done in a large group (4–6 students) over the course of the semester and is very open ended. Just something to consider if you aren't a big fan of group projects! I would also highly recommend that you do a crash course in Python before starting this course if your aren't a coder, as Python is used starting from pset 1 and you can fall behind if you're not familiar with it.
