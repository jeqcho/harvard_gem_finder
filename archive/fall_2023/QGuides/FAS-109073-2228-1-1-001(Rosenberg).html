What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

n.a
Graduate before it is required you take this nonsense again.
Be open
Although this course is an annoying requirement at times, I also think it can be a good opportunity to reflect more deeply about pros and cons of academia and hear from a wider variety of students their perspectives on the topic. By G6 fall we should already be doing this, but I feel many of the discussions set up by the course were not topics I would've thought as carefully about nor got as many different opinions on otherwise. Discussions were usually fun and at times legit helpful. This experience probably varies quite a lot depending on what group leader you end up with, but I'd advise trying to take the course seriously going into the semester and be optimistic about the value of the discussion sections. More concretely, I'd highly recommend Jeff Moffitt's section if you can. We regularly dove into much more interesting topics than the scripted course material.
It's more informative as a G6 than as a G2 (with 4 years of intervening experience to add context to what you will discuss). Keep an open mind both times you have to take it.
