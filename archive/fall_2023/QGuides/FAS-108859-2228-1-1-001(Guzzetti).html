What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great intro class – Alfred approaches it with a clear plan/structure for your learning so it doesn't feel like all at once, but also feels challenging.
a good introduction to videomaking
Worth taking, for sure. You will get out of it as much as you put it, but if you want to get the most out of it – be prepared to put in the hours when it comes to the final project.
This is a great class to take if you're interested in learning a lot about filmmaking in a relatively short amount of time. By the end of the semester, you'll have a finished product that you'll (hopefully) be proud of, along with a new way of thinking about visual storytelling. Be warned that it's not a studio class – we did all our filming on our own time and spent our class time on discussion/workshopping/watching films – but I believe they're planning on using more class time for filming next semester!
Take this course if you enjoy observational film. While it is an introductory course, learning to use the camera and editing software is partially the responsibility of the student.
