Take it! It's so fun and you feel fluent by the end!
Similar to the other CHNSE language classes, the teaching staff are great and really supportive. As for 130A, be prepared to learn a lot more formal Chinese and produce more professional/academic projects. You learn a lot about the societal issues in China and move away from more conversational/casual topics.
Fairly standard for a course in the Chinese department. Not too much of a step up from previous semesters, but you do get to learn terms which are more relevant to contemporary society (eg economics or politics terminology). Overall I enjoyed it; be prepared to spend a moderate amount of time learning vocabulary, but if you put the time in you will be rewarded.
Be prepared to consistently practice to do well in this course.
The Chinese program at Harvard is by far the most organized and effective in terms of language growth. While Chinese 130 is considerably more difficult than Chinese 120 due to the nature of the content, the teachers are very encouraging and helpful, and the course material is really interesting and relevant to current issues.
