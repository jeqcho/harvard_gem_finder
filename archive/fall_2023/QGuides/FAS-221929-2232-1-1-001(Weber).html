You get what you put into this course. The problem sets were moderately difficult and graded pretty generously. The main focus is on the research project, so if you are motivated/interested in doing a large project, then you can get a lot out of this course. Lectures were helpful to gain intuition, but doing the suggested readings and your own exploration is needed to really understand some of the content.
If this class is being thought by Professor Melanie Weber I would definitely take it. She made it interesting and always explained everything really well. The homework can be a bit complex but the TFs were always able to help.
Melanie is an amazing resource. She is engaging, gives clear explanations, and is always happy to help. She truly elevated this experience of an already enticing course.
This class is interesting and exposes you to lot of new ideas in Geometric Machine Learning which is still quite a new field. I think there is a lot of opportunity to do interesting work since this field is still quite niche. This class is also moderate amount of work (8 hrs/week or so).
If you do machine learning, especially for scientific applications, this course is a must–take.
Great course that covers many interesting topics. Melanie is well organized and clear in her teaching. The assignments were a good mix of building mathematical rigor and coding. Teaching staff were extremely nice and supportive.
TAKE THIS CLASS if you're interested in modern advances in machine learning. Course materials are some of the best I've seen, would highly recommend this. Melanie is incredible.
This course is a great overview of this new field. Taught in a ver clear and digestible manner. There is further readings for those with stronger math backgrounds should one want to take it further. The difficulty is appropriate so to stimulate you but not overwhelm you. For those with weaker math backgrounds, I would recommend stronger prep during the last third of the course in order to keep up with the class.
If you're a masters or PhD student, I strongly recommend this class above e.g. a computer vision or NLP course. As a choice, CV or NLP are probably your first instinct on what to go for. You might say "images and natural language arise more than graphs, and I want to work on key areas of ML". But there are so many interesting problems with inherent geometric structure to exploit! Just search on arxiv for the latest Geometric ML papers on molecule property prediction, shape search, or knowledge graphs. What CV and NLP were in the 2010s, geometric ML is in the 2020s. The upside is that there is plenty of low–hanging fruit in both the theory and application of geometric ML.
This is a great class to understand geometric structures in machine learning. The course project is a great way to apply these methods.
A pretty chill course that surveys some really interesting topics in an emerging field! Lectures are organized and informative, and the workload isn't bad (though assignments sort of bunch together towards the end of the course, so beware). All in all, would recommend if you want to broadly learn more about geometry in ML
This is a good overview about representation learning, GNNs, and optimization/ML on manifolds. It doesn't really get in the weeds though, the lectures are more of a surface–level survey. If you really want to understand this stuff you'd have to work through the recommended books on your own.
This class is good to learn more of the math behind many powerful tools in machine learning and really getting you to the forefront of this field.
Take this class! Melanie is fantastic, the assignments are fair but fun, and you learn a ton. Pacing was great––not too in–depth, but you get the flavor and motivation behind the methods
This course is an excellent introduction to geometric machine learning. If your research deals with graphs, manifolds, or any problem where there is an underlying geometric structure, this course can guide you through any mathematical foundations and practical implementations that you might need.
