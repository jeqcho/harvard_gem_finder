It is hard and sometimes a difficult to link topics together, but definitely worth taking if you are interested in active matter
This course is very difficult, but certainly worth it. Homework will be very difficult, and I would recommend students to approach this class very carefully. This class covers a vast array of incredibly complex and challenging topics.
If you're interested in biophysics, or generally applying physics to natural and biological systems, this is a great course for you. The guest talks are very worthwhile, and the problem sets have left me feeling very comfortable with the kind of math that is used in this field.
I think I would suggest that they brush up on some of their pde and fluids knowledge, but I would overall recommend it for people who are interested in mathematical methods applied to active matter
Really inspiring and interesting topics and guest speakers. Professor and TF were friendly and helpful to talk to both about homeworks and also for suggestions on our course project.
If you have the requisite background, this course is well worth your time. Even if you don't think you have the background, it is worth trying the course out to see how it feels. Working with active matter is really fun, and a substantial paradigm shift from the typical physics view of the world.
If you're interested in this kind of physics, absolutely take this class. It's hard but manageable, and you will learn a ton.
