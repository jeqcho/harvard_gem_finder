Professor LaBonte is amazing!!!!
This course gives you a whole new perspective on how health and diagnostics are deeply influenced by social, cultural and historical factors. The materials and assignments are really engaging and discussions are awesome. Anyone who wants to be involved with health or public health should definitely take this class.
If you have any interest in medicine and diagnostic technologies utilized in medical practice, take this course. Professor LaBonte is extremely knowledgeable and passionate about the topics of this course. The work load is very manageable and you will want to do the readings because they are very engaging. The reading response papers aren't very time consuming and help you to engage with course material. The podcast assignment was a fun final project since most classes are papers. I highly recommend this course to anyone in History of Science or interested in the many ways that diagnostic technologies influence medical practice, the physicians who utilize them, and the patients.
Definitely take this class! Michelle really cares about her students and provides thoughtful feedback. The readings are interesting, and you’ll learn a lot about different ways of thinking about the development of diagnostic technologies.
Take this class or another class taught by Dr. LaBonte. She's fantastic and truly cares about students. Classes were engaging, assignments were fairly spaced out, and readings were meaningful.
Amazing course! Highly recommend to anyone. Manageable and stays engaging throughout the semester
You must absolutely take this class. Whether you're pre–med, a HistSci concentrator or none of the above, Michelle is one of the best instructors here at Harvard and you will absolutely not regret taking her classes (even if it's not this one!). In this class, we discuss incredibly interesting topics such as how doctors come to a diagnosis via different tools in medicine, how diagnoses have changed throughout history, and the social/political factors that influence diagnoses. It's a seminar class as well, so you'll get to know your classmates incredibly well throughout the semester and Michelle helps foster such a unique, safe classroom space. I couldn't speak more highly of this class!
It's a rewarding course, and if you are interested in medicine or history of medicine, it will provide you unique insights into how medicine works, its challenges and social and cultural meanings.
This class is absolutely fantastic and you should absolutely take it!! Dr. LaBonte is very kind and understanding, and the content is super interesting and manageable. Definitely a great class even if you aren't in History of Science!! Assignments aren't too bad, and there is plenty of leeway if you have extenuating circumstances arise. The readings are fun and not too time–consuming, and though sections are long, they're really interesting (and Dr. LaBonte gives you a short break in the middle). Highly recommend!!
TAKE THIS COURSE!! (Especially if you're considering pursuing medicine / medical policy / etc!!!!) Course material is so so interesting and if I decide to go into medicine, I'll definitely put some of what I learned into practice. It's a great reflection on the medical system and historical trends; this was my first Histsci course at Harvard so I didn't need any special background in that area, but I really loved the course. Michelle is also lovely as a person, mentor, and professor –– she works hard to create a truly welcoming and inclusive environment in the course, and also to make sure everyone has a voice in discussions. There's a lot of flexibility in assignments and course work, which allowed me to put a lot of effort into this course without feeling stressed out. This was one of my favorite classes this semester and I'm so glad I took it!
This course is super interesting, and Michelle does a great job at creating a warm and welcoming environment where students feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and fostering fruitful discussion. Anyone interested in medicine or healthcare, I highly recommend this course; you will come out of it thinking differently about technology's and doctor's impacts on medical and healthcare systems.
