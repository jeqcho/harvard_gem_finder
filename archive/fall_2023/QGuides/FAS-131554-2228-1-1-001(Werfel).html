Make sure you have coding experience.
A really cool class – lectures covered interesting material, solidified by Psets, and labs which were so directly connected to the course content and cool!
No complaints, would recommend the class – Prof. Werfel, Haedo, Adam, and Caleb provided good support.
This was my favorite course at Harvard so far! I don't think the lectures are super interesting, but I think the material is super interesting and important for anyone who wants to go into the field of robotics. Every job interview I had this semester for a robotics company, I was able to showcase the theoretical knowledge I gained through this course.

That being said, YOU DO NOT BUILD YOUR OWN ROBOT. A bunch of mechanical engineers that shopped this course were disappointed to learn that 1– you don't build anything, but rather learn how to program, and 2– that the lectures are very theoretical (kind of similar to ES155 where it's basically applied linear algebra).

I think this course helped prepare me for job interviews and working in the robotics industry in general in the future. The HWs were fair, and not too easy but matched the material taught in classed and helped me understand the material. The labs were extremely helpful in providing students with hands–on experience and enough "proof" for employers that you are capable of working in their perspective companies. Taking the graduate version, the final project enables a hands–on experience in both writing mathematical reports and performing an individual project. Additionally, I thoroughly enjoyed the guessed lectures and learning from people conducting research in the field and leading it. Also, the book accompanying the course was great!
Prof Werfel is very communicative and is willing to make extra time if you ask him to. However he also really sticks to his own policies – unless you have a truly unavoidable emergency, it is unlikely that he will give you an extension, as he already has a late assignment policy.
Work on the labs as early as possible – the robot arms tend to get crowded in the last lab section. Also running on gazebo ahead of time is super helpful.

Also, Caleb and Haedo were awesome TFs and really improved my experience with the class! If you have different TFs you may have a different experience.
This is a very useful course and is very doable if you put work in for the psets and labs. Also get safety trained early for the robot! It will make your life so much easier when doing labs.
