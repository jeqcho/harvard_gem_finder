What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
You will have a lot of fun and get comfortable with reading Middle English! Chaucer is much funnier than you realize, and there is a lot of variety (in terms of genre and themes) amongst the tales––never a dull week. Professor Watson made the material come to life. In addition to the reading, there are a lot of small assignments but most of them are easy and not too time consuming.
This is such a wonderful class, and you'll receive so much excellent feedback on your writing and questions. I've learned so much, and I'm very grateful that I ended up in this course.
Give yourself time to sit with the tales after you read them! You find yourself much more fulfilled in class if you've had time to think about the tales and find the parts that interest you beforehand, rather than rushing through it in the hour before class.
It is initially quite difficult to figure out how to read, comprehend, and pronounce Middle English, but it becomes a lot easier after the first month! Keep on top of your reading schedule so as to not get overwhelmed, and make sure to study for quizzes and practice translation on your own time.
I LOVED IT. Super great class. Watson makes this a super enjoyable experience (with some goofily crude jokes along the way). This was a super entertaining course, moving from tales about marriage to tales about death to tales about farts. And Watson is always pushing and encouraging you to meet with him, he really makes sure that you are following the course and the materials of the course, and is super patient about learning Middle English, which was a lot easier than it originally seemed with Watson's help. BIG recommend for me.
Don't let the Middle English (or Chaucer's canonical status) intimidate you! This course is approachable and rewarding — the language and tales may not be immediately accessible (especially at first) but they are well worth wrestling with. You may just come away from the course feeling that you've taken a trip into a different world.
If you want to read the Canterbury Tales, you should definitely take this class. I assume there is not really a danger of your taking it if you don't want to, but there it is. Prof. Watson is quite good at making discussion both rigorous and interesting, and, at least in the version of the class I took, being surrounded by graduate students was super rewarding and a lot of fun. Grad students are cool!
