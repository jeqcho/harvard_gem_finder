honestly I really loved this class even as a non–concentrator. be prepared for hefty readings and if you don't like the brontes obviously dont take this
This course is a lot of fun, but be mindful of the heavy reading schedule (usually a novel a week, with the shortest ones being around 200 pages, and the longest ones being over 500 pages).
Take this class. I loved every minute of it. The Brontes’ novels are all amazing so the readings are guaranteed to be great. Professor Scarry has an amazing amount of knowledge and insights to offer, and the class discussion was the best I’ve had at Harvard so far — everybody was so engaged and thoughtful. The short form papers throughout the semester were a great lesson for concise writing and literary analysis. It is a lot of reading as we were doing about a novel a week, but it’s worth it. And the combination of the sisters’ novels, poetry, and their history provided a lot of new and interesting understandings of their work. It was my favorite class at Harvard by far.
This is a great class! Professor Scarry is very kind and compassionate, and also holds great discussions in seminar. The reading is a lot but worth it!
This is what a seminar class was meant to be. Professor Scarry was unbelievably engaged and accessible (she gave handwritten line edits on our essays and had amazing office hours), and the students followed her lead. The energy in this class, even at 3pm on a Thursday, was infectious each and every week!
This class is wonderful for English students seeking to complete the 1700–1900 concentration requirement! It does a great job handling the Brontes (powerhouses of the 19th C) as well as 19th C literary styles and forms broadly. Professor Scarry is clearly passionate about these unique writers and makes the class fun and exciting for newcomers to the Brontes. If you are not an English student, this is a very accessible class to get into 19th C lit/the English department at large. The discussions are welcoming and the close–reading essays provide creative opportunities for students to interpret the texts on their own. I really appreciate this class!!
Definitely take this class! Professor Scarry is so intelligent and kind—leads discussion really well, and actually cares to get to know the students and give good feedback on your writing.
This class was an incredible way to explore the works of the Brontës in a seminar style. Be prepared for a lot of reading each week, but each novel is so compelling that the reading goes by pleasantly, if not easily. Don't be daunted by the two hour seminar session either: this format works really well for having in depth discussions about each novel, and the flow of debate and the exchange of ideas helps the time pass faster than you expect. Plus, Professor Scarry brought food on multiple occasions, turning a two hour session into a really fun and engaging time.
