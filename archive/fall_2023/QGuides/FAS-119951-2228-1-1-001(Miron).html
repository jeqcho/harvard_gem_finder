Take this class! What a great econ elective. The class gives you a comprehensive overview of the libertarian perspective on various issues – abortion, guns, marriage, you name it. Your grade in this class is based on three things: the midterm, the final, and your attendance in sections of your choosing. You just have to attend 5 sections before the midterm and 5 sections after the midterm, and you're able to choose which times you want to attend. The midterm and final exams were all multiple choice and straight from lecture material and some research papers (you could answer those questions by just reading the abstracts and conclusions). Highly recommend – my friends and I all loved this class! And Prof Miron is awesome!
Make sure that you study for the exams and this may sound redundant but it is critical that even if you do not necessarily agree with the libertarian stance, you make an effort to at least understand the consequentialist framework enough to extrapolate it to all of the topics since the arguments may repeat themselves. Also make sure that you read the abstracts and conclusions of the readings. It gets memorization heavy so start early and keep up.
