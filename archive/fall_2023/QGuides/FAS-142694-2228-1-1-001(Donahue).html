What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The content is interesting but very niche. The professor is very knowledgeable, but the course is disorganized and lacks an overarching theme or structure. If you genuinely want to learn about Medieval English law, I recommend with reservations.
You should take this course if you want to have a deeper understanding of the interrelationship between ideas, institutions, and society, and a set of legal concepts that we non–lawyers may all too often take for granted.
This is a challenging course; you must be prepared to put in time to watch the lectures and complete the readings. Professor Donahue is brilliant and has a deep command of medieval history, and it will benefit you greatly if you talk to him early on. The material can be confusing, but if you read the supplementary texts you will be in better shape. If you dedicate your time to seriously understanding the texts and court cases you read, you will get a lot out of this course.
This course attracts a significant number of very conservative Christian undergraduates. They are generally nice but I didn't always have the best experiences with some of them outside of class, including being told that I was "morally debased" at a party by one classmate while another nodded along. This greatly diminished my experience in class and negatively affected my ability to learn in lectures and sections. Professor Donahue and Max, the TF, are opposed to such views and are excellent and compassionate teachers. That said, future students should know this class seems to attract students that hold views the larger Harvard community finds abhorrent. This was my favorite course until the party incident. I end the semester with it being my least favorite class in my four years at Harvard. Nonetheless, if you have thick skin I highly recommend taking this course. The course material and teachers are excellent and I've grown greatly from this course. I hope what happened to me does not happen to you and that you strongly consider taking this course while considering my reservations. If you believe you wouldn't be able to learn in a fabulous class that also attracts bigots, I recommend not taking this course. That would be a loss though, as the underlying course is excellent.
If you are an absolute fanatic about the law, I think you can enjoy this course; otherwise, it will be a struggle.
