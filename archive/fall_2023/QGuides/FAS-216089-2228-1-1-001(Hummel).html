This class was wonderful and I definitely recommend taking it!! Professor Hummel is a great lecturer and explains things so well, and the difficulty of the course is very reasonable. The assignments are also reasonable and ask you to apply concepts from class to the real world, which is honestly quite enjoyable. There isn't any complicated math or calculations for the sake of calculations – this course instead focuses on building intuition and understanding about these models. I definitely feel like I have a better understanding of how a lot of political systems work thanks to this course!

This course is one of the best courses you can take in the Gov department, in my opinion. I highly recommend people take it. The workload is manageable, Professor Hummel is super nice, the teaching staff is great, the readings are –actually useful= and applicable, and the knowledge of game theory extends beyond just the course into many other fields. I had the ideas in this class come up in another class I was taking in the Ec department. This stuff all connects. Furthermore, there's an actual discussion as to how these models fit into how we understand the world, so you get to grapple with the more impenetrable nature of the literature in strategic models and interrogate it a bit. This course wasn't meant to teach me how to read these papers, but it did by accident.

Would take 1015's sequel if one exists, great course.
I highly recommend this class. The models that Professor Hummel presents in her lectures are very interesting, and she does a fantastic job at applying them to real–world concepts and situations; the assignments are also effective at doing this, as they allow you to apply models to political scenarios of your choice.

I would add that the models and concepts you learn about will help you view politics through a more analytical lens, and these frameworks can even be applied to everyday situations. Lastly, the greatest asset of the course is its teaching staff: both Professor Hummel and Ms. Sung In Kim (the teaching fellow) were an absolute privilege to learn from; they were always extremely accessible outside of class and were very helpful at providing useful feedback throughout the semester.
