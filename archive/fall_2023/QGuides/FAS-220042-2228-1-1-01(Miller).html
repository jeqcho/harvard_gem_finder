The course is kinda all over the place and unorganized, nothing is posted in a consistent place, email responsiveness is pretty poor, but the content itself is interesting and it's good if there's any question at all that you've ever had and fairly easy to find a question once you get into discussion in the course
Quad Lab was INCREDIBLE! Everyone was automatically bought–in, yet Ian fostered such a beautiful community where everyone supported each other. I am friends with the people in this class! I liked the discussions and how we got to go all–in on the archives and meet with the archivists. Discussions were fun because everyone brought in their own Quad experiences. Really fun final project and the final presentations were so cool. Ian is awesome and very supportive and generates so much enthusiasm for the material. Since this is the first time teaching it, they were pretty disorganized with deadlines and assignments, but were super flexible as a result. If you're a history concentrator, there might be some repetition (you do go into the how–to–use–the–archives stuff so that everyone's on the same page and there were some repeat readings from previous classes). Hopefully in the future they straighten that out. What you put in is what you get out, and even though I took this as a gem, I got so much more out of the class than I expected. TAKE THIS whether you want it to be a gem or you love (or hate) the Quad or want to take a class with Ian in Cabot dhall!
One of the best classes I’ve ever taken. Definitely take this course if you’re interested in learning about Quad history and in going through the archives to write history yourself (there’s lots of support if you need it so don’t worry if you’ve never done that before). Literally the class I talked most about the year, and has helped me feel so much more at home at the Quad after learning about how the place came to be and the people who have lived here before it. Honestly the readings are great, Ian and Hazim are amazing, but the other students really made it. It was such a welcoming and down to earth seminar that I looked forward to (even if it was at 9am) — and everyone’s final projects were so cool that I felt like I learned just as much from the course from my peers. Such a cool opportunity to learn and write history about the place you live so take advantage of it!!
This is a great course! I would recommend if you are interested about the history behind the place we live––it is important history and one of the most applicable classes I've taken.

While I really enjoyed this course, I have a few comments. (Keep in mind this was also the first year this course was taught). The syllabus was often inaccurate (it was flexible, which I appreciated), but unclear when assignments were due (sometimes two different due dates were listed on different parts of the course website), unclear on where they were due (discussion board or assignments tab or email?), and sometimes even where the class was meeting. Overall, better communication, more organization, and faster responses on email will go a long way.

Would recommend, but hopefully if this course is taught in the future, some of these kinks will be hammered out! The layout of assignments was great (a couple papers, cool final presentation / project, lots of class discussion, and class time in the archives).
A GREAT COURSE! TAKE THIS CLASS EVEN IF YOU ARE IN THE RIVER!
This class is an amazing introduction to the history of Harvard, and of the Radcliffe Quad. Every week, discussions were rich and insightful. It was a class I was excited to attend and prepare for, even though it was early in the morning. Every small setback in the course was absolutely worth it for the valuable experience I gained (the major setbacks being confusion surrounding dates and readings, which I assume will be better with the next iteration of the class). If you are interested in the Radcliffe Quad, Harvard history, gendered history, or archival work, this is an amazing class to take and a wonderful way to fill the seminar requirement for history concentrators. Ian and Hazim were excellent, and it's an opportunity to get to know your professors and peers in a smaller, more intimate environment. I would highly recommend this course.
