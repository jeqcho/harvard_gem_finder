This class is very interesting and good preparation for other applications.
This course is very straightforward to master, and it is very useful material as an engineer. Problem solving is challenging at first, but once it clicks, the course will be a breeze.
If you want to do MechE, you're going to have to take this, and it's not a bad class. However it is chock full of formulas you're going to have to memorize or at least put on your cheat sheets, and you better get ready to do the same sequence of steps many, many times. You will love springs and dampers when done with this class, and you will probably understand why engineers like to pretend that sin x = x for small angles. TFs are generally amazing and you should go to their office hours all the time. Boris is less so, he's a good teacher but his lectures leave you wondering how exactly to apply any of it. Not a horrible class overall, it's well–taught and they want you to succeed, but not an easy class by any means.
The most useful engineering course Ive ever taken. If you are a Mech E you have to take this but you will enjoy it so much! You can understand basically any mechanical system ever after this class. Loved the experience and really enjoyed Boris. Just gotta put some time into it and go to class to learn.
A lot of concepts in the course seem difficult upon first learning, but after sitting down and going at those problems a few times, they will be understood relatively quickly. Going to office hours is something I often found necessary since there weren't any sections.
Probably only take it if you are really enthusiastic about physics/diff eq's or are an engineering concentrator. It's a good course, but pretty rigorous. Also, definitely go to office hours –– they are very helpful.
Utilize the teaching staff to your advantage. They are very helpful
I think this class is really well done. Boris is a great professor, and even though his lectures move quickly, the material is taught well. Definitely review the lecture slides after class to make sure you fully understand what's going on because he covers a lot of material/math in each lecture. PSets are a bit long and tedious but if you start early they're all pretty straightforward. Exams are fair as well as they're basically just HW problems.
Go to class, and pay attention especially during demonstrations, topic introductions, and example problems. Homework is straightforward if you ask for help in office hours. The class was a lot of fun!
Talk to Boris! He is a great person and a great resource to have for this course! And start on the psets early.
This is one of my favorite courses that I have taken at Harvard! It is well taught, and you will come out of it feeling confident about what you have learned.
