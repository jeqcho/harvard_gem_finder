Great movies. Great, great movies. And the course is not arduous. Do the readings and write as best you can, but it is sure to be a labor of love.
Professor Conley is incredible kind, supportive, and friendly. He creates an incredibly chill, nice atmosphere for learning. The selection of films is great. Would recommend!
Professor Conley is a brilliant and enthusiastic interpreter. However, this course was not a seminar as listed. The fact that students were scattered around an overly large screening room while Professor Conley lectured (welcoming and encouraging interruptions) meant that I came away unsure of whether he had even learned the names of all but the most vocal (and fluently French–speaking) students.
Take it! No questions asked. Conley is the best –– his aptitude, his compassion, his willingness to adopt the course to what students want, and his vast body of knowledge are all on full display here.
This class serves as a focused film survey course on the great, great films of the past. (a focus on French film and Italian film). Prof. Conley is exceptionally well versed on the great films and how literature and psychoanalysis meet cinema.

It's a course that is more loosely structured and lectures are not some kind of text–book like speech. Rather, lectures can serve as jumping off points for discussion or related discussions on adjacent films.

Write down all of these adjacent films that Prof. Conley mentions throughout a lecture and study them in the library, you've just made the best version of a film survey course at Harvard.

I recommend Prof. Conley's film courses to all students.
Definitely take this class!! It is a delight and very little required time outside of class
