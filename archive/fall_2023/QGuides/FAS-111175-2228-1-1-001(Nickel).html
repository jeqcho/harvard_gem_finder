Definitely take this class!! Philosophy is a transformative discipline & you might leave the class with a greater appreciation for the field even if you're not a philosopher concentrator. The teaching staff is fabulous at making tough concepts digestible + are very generous with their support infrastructure (offering abundant OH slots, being flexible with deadlines/grading, etc.). They teach with the understanding that most students are just getting into philosophy/may not have much exposure to college writing at all. You can truly tell that Professor Nickel + the TFs care about the students (they responded immediately, for instance, to constructive midterm feedback). I left the class with a strong foundation in various contentious philosophical debates and overall found the course fun, rewarding, and accommodating.

An excellent introductory philosophy class. Prof. Nickel clearly cares a lot about pedagogy and the course and the content is interesting. Workload is very manageable.
There are 4 main papers. Papers 1 and 2 are pretty short, and then you have paper 3.1 and paper 3.2 (which is a rewrite of 3.2). Then you have paper 4 which is the final paper. The soft deadlines come in clutch when you are taking harder classes alongside it. There is also a pretty long final at the end to also prep for. To be honest this class is definitley not a gem if you want a good grade (he is a tough grader on papers). If you are taking it pass fail then its definitley a gem since much of the stuff is just common sense so you don't really need to engage that much to get a B or something. Bernard is a pretty solid prof, he attempts to make boring content at least somewhat interesting, and, at times he is successful. If you are taking philosophy because you think it will be like the philosophy you hear about in books and online, be warned, this is not like that. Shortly after the add drop period the class becomes unnecessarily dense.
You won't get a more thorough introduction to philosophy anywhere else. The lectures are clear and help you make sense of the challenging readings you might encounter (every lecture had a sort of "aha!" moment where things started to make sense). However, be wary that the course moves quickly, and the writing assignments can creep up on you and give you some trouble if you haven't been thinking about the prompts/done the readings for the prompts.

Professor Nickel and the TFs are clearly invested in your success, though how much you get out of your section may vary based on the TF you are assigned. Lean on the teaching staff if you're ever stuck they'll do their best to assist you.

Overall, this course was helpful for me to know how I wanted to pursue philosophy further I know what branches of philosophy I'm not very interested in, and others I wish to learn more about. A fine gateway to the discipline and its approaches.

This is a great philosophy class for people not very well acquainted with philosophy! Nickel and the teaching staff did a great job presenting the material and encouraging discussion. The essays were surprisingly challenging, but there was lots of time to talk to TFs, and the grading was very fair. I think this course is an excellent introduction to philosophy. However, I would recommend that you have a base level of understanding regarding philosophical papers and how to construct papers. The professor goes through material exceptionally quickly and does not delve much into how to structure these papers, even though they are most of your grade. So while the professor may say you can take this class without ever taking a previous philosophy class, this did not match up with my or my peers' experiences. This was very stressful for some students, as one student even cried during one of the sections due to a lack of understanding. For instance, before coming into this class, I enjoyed philosophy immensely, but now I associate it with confusion and lack of understanding due to how fast the lecturer went over the material and how there was little to no focus on structuring papers. These papers are very distinct from other academic papers, so I suggest there be a bit more focus on this, especially since the averages were extremely low for the first few papers.
