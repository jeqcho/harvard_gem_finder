TAKE THIS COURSE. The Professor is incredibly kind and passionate about sociology ––almost every person in my class was motivated to want to take sociology classes in the future as a result. The discussions and readings were absolutely fascinating and will challenge the way you view Harvard as an institution and how institutions of higher education promote and sustain privilege. The absolute best class to take your freshman year if you're at all interested in issues of inequality.
This class is amazing! The professor is one of the best human beings I have met and is very open about having conversations about all types of topics. You will learn a lot in the class and will become a better person after taking it.
TAKE THIS CLASS. I think it will be one of my favorite classes that I will ever take at Harvard. Not only is the material interesting and the course work is light (at least lighter than other expos' I've heard of) but I think every student at Harvard should learn about how heavily wealth influences opportunity of education. It's especially relevant for first years at Harvard, and grounds you in navigating the institution. I think students from all backgrounds should take it, to learn about the experiences of others as well as the systems in place that have guided our own experiences before arriving at Harvard.
Take it!! I loved this class–––looked forward to it every day I had it. The readings were great, the section discussions were engaging, and Jim was such a helpful discussion leader and writing teacher.
This class was really nice, because it, for the most part, lacked the unnecessary readings, assignments, and busy–work that some other expos classes had. Every reading was necessary, focused, and specific to our papers and the course.
However, since it was a new class, the pacing of the class was rather unfortunate –– we had no work in the beginning of the year, and then were in a bit of a time crunch in the latter half the semester. Hopefully this will be adjusted in the future, though.
Give yourself lots of time to think over the prompt and evidence.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Dr. Herron is truly a wonderful teacher—personable, patient, and passionate about the topics discussed in this course! This was my first experience with the sociological perspective on writing and Dr. Herron really opened my mind to a new way of thinking and writing that has definitely impacted my path. Make sure you take the time to do the readings, which can be long sometimes but SUPER INTERESTING, because the class discussions were so intellectually simulating and perspective changing. This class was genuinely my favorite this semester; not many people have a great expos experience and I truly believe if you take this course, you will definitely have a significantly better time. Can't give enough praise for Dr. Herron too, such an awesome teacher who really cares about his students!
Dr. Herron is a fantastic teacher. He really cares about students and the subject matter, and offers really interesting insights into all the readings. He facilitates class discussions very fairly and well. I would highly recommend taking this course.
I cannot recommend this class enough to anyone interested in sociology or education. The readings are very engaging and enjoyable and the workload is very manageable. Jim is a very chill teacher and is very accessible and knowledgeable. The writing assignments (especially the final research paper) will definitely push you outside of your comfort zone as a writer, but in a good and necessary way that will help prepare you for your more rigorous future classes. Great discussions every class.
Loved this class! All of the readings were super interesting and even when we had a couple of chapters from a book I just wanted to read the whole thing. James Herron is an excellent professor. He cares about the process of the students and leaves room for discussion. I learned a lot in terms of writing and I think that all the suggestions and lessons pushed me to get better at it. Everyone told me that Expos would be the worst class of my freshman year, but I enjoyed it a lot. Totally recommend it.
