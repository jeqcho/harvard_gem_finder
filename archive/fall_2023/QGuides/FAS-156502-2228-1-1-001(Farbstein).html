IT IS SUCH A GOOD CLASS!!!
Regardless of whether you want to go into Human Rights Law or not, this class is worth taking! You will learn a lot about social justice and the small group setting allows for nice community building. Professor Farbstein is an amazing instructor and a very kind and compassionate person in general.
There are weeks when it may seem physically impossible to do all the readings. Don't sweat just ensure you're getting the main ideas from the events and topics discussed (google, look for more synthesized materials, etc.).
I would absolutely say to take this course, but something I didn't consider was just to be mindful that it is a specifically law–focused way of looking at issues of justice. If you're interested in these more broadly, the course still gives you a lot. Prof Farbstein is just a great person to know
I would say that this is a must have course in your Crimson Cart! It is an amazing and interesting course that you are not going to regret taking. The preceptor is engaging and excited to share her work and the content that she is teaching. Overall, the course load is light, the conversations are interesting and the relationships that you form will last a lifetime.
THIS CLASS IS FANTASTIC. I genuinely think it changed my life, in terms of career path and sense of self. Susan is a wonderfully empathetic and kind person who can lead a discussion effortlessly and dedicates so much care to her students. You get incredible access to law school facilities and Susan is so accessible to meet one–on–one or help you make connections with other HLS faculty and industry experts. Most importantly, though, you will learn so so much about human rights, international law, and how to be a good advocate. There is no one who wouldn't benefit from the opportunity to take this class. DO IT!
This course is amazing!!! 100% my favorite class. I know some people do not read the course material, but I would recommend reading it. Harvard is what you make of it, and this class is one to make the most of.
This course is an incredibly first step to ease your transition at Harvard. You will learn an incredible amount about the human rights movement with peers who are immensely talented, with a very manageable workload.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Susan is an amazing professor, absolutely make use of the opportunity to learn from her as an undergrad. The class is interesting and a light workload, and you gain an amazing community and connections. If you are interested in human rights at all, take the class––you will not regret it!
This class is really cool if you are interested in Human Rights/ International Law. Professor Farbstein is absolutely amazing and makes the class interesting. I will say that this class is two hours long which can feel like a lot sometimes, and the readings tend to be long and dense at times. But the discussions and content you learn in the class are worth it, and once again, Professor Farbstein is an AMAZING professor and person in general.
I would tell kids to take it if they have even the slightest interest for law. It is an amazing course with an amazing professor.
If you are at all interested in human rights law, this is a great class for you. The course will give you a good introduction into the human rights legal system, which, while depressing, is also pretty interesting. The instructor Susan, is incredibly nice and warm and makes the class 10x better.
