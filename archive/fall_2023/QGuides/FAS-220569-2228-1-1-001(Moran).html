What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Before this class, I knew nothing about Bernard Williams. After taking it, I am incredibly grateful to have been introduced to him. His thoughts on ethics are so different from what I learned in my intro to ethics class, and I find them incredibly compelling. He is now one of my favorite philosophers and writers. I have never taken a class in which I felt so passionately about the readings. I was unable to finish all of them, and I am committed to complete them during winter break (I have never done that before). It is a testament to how great the readings are that this was my favorite class, even though the seminar component was lacking. In a way, I do feel that I would have gotten almost as much out of the class as I did even if I had been unable to attend the seminar discussions and only had to read and write essays. Nonetheless, I'm sure the class will improve, and if just for the readings it is 100% worth taking.
