What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is interesting if you are remotely interesting in political philosophy. Make sure to do the readings.
I would highly recommend taking this course if you are interested in gaining a deeper understanding of political philosophy as it relates to the moral implications of scenarios involving groups. Emilio is fantastic, and the course is structured so that those with rigorous philosophical training and those who may be approaching the discipline for the first time are all able to learn. I especially found discussions of historical injustice and distributional liability fascinating.
I knew after going through the syllabus that this would be a course with topics that I'm very interested in. If you're also interested in these topics, this is a great course to take. Definitely carefully read all the readings and choose a topic that you're very interested in for the final paper. Office hours are also very helpful.
If you have interest to topics in political philosophy and/or are looking for a possible approach to the same, this course is very rewarding and horizon–expanding. The professor is very helpful and flexible in every way.
With the right mindset, one can benefit greatly from the learning environment and perspectives offered – both academically and personally.
