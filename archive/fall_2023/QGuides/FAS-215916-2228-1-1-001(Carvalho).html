This class isn't very difficult but you won't take anything away from it. Don't take it if you are actually excited about the material. All of the discussions are very theoretical and hard to apply. The lectures are also hard to follow.
This was the first time this class was being offered, and it suffered for it. Nevertheless, it has a lot of potential. I felt that the weekly course structure of the material and the assignments were well thought out. The material is super interesting. Yet two points where the class suffered was lecture delivery and lack of synergy between section and lecture. Regarding the latter, my section felt like either a repetition of the readings, a reiteration of lecture, or both, just in a smaller setting. This was coupled with lectures sometimes feeling like a barrage of information. On lecture delivery, two professors teaching at the same time is not it. Fix these two and you have a really good, worthwhile, and frankly chill–ish GENED, all in one. My advice: take it, if there's evidence of improvement on these two issues.

The assignments are fun and creative. The final project is somewhat intensive (10–20 pages writing), but you learn a lot about urban planning by doing it. Take this class to learn more about what it means to be a city and what challenges cities face (e.g. housing, transportation, etc.).
