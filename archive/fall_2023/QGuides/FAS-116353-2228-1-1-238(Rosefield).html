What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Be prepared to be graded relatively harshly compared to other expos classes. This class seems to be more difficult than other expos classes because of this difficulty in grading. Make sure you get a head start on your essays in order to ask the preceptor as many questions as possible.
Be prepared to work, but this course is really helpful in boosting your academic writing abilities. It's also really enjoyable if you get a topic you're interested in.
Classes are extremely engaging, engaging, and entertaining! I cannot stress how much more interactive and fun attending classes are compared to even a freshman seminar. There is also minimal amounts of reading required (and no books, which is a plus). However, Hannah is definitely one of the tougher graders among expos preceptors. Only one or two As are given in the class, and even A–s aren't too common to come across, as far as I am aware. If you're up for the challenge, definitely worth taking this. If you're looking for the easiest way to get an A, take something else.
I would tell future students that it is an excellent course to take to improve as a writer and the classes are a lot of fun. I did not wake up dreading to go to Expos, and while some of the essays demand a good amount of time, they weren't a drag to write. Unless you have a strong attachment to the source material of any other course, I would definitely recommend this course over the others.
Hannah is a really sharp, kind and flexible preceptor who will really be there for you to help you work on your writing. While you may not go that deep into the subject matter of the course, the readings and discussions offer some really good food for thought which you have the space to think and talk about in a chill environment. And for the final paper, you get to write a research paper on any film of your choice. Overall would recommend!
This is an awesome class! Hannah is great with the readings and lets class discussion flow wherever it goes. The material is awesome, especially if you like movies. It is a good amount of work to write the essays but its worth it for your development as a write. Hannah is really good about deadlines and wont be a stickler if you need an extra hour to submit something.
