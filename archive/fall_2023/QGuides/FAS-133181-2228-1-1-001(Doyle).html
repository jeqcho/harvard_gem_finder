What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Doyle is very smart and interested in the material, but he is an abysmal lecturer. If you plan to take this class I recommend getting started on the essay's early and coming to office hours. You should probably only take this class if you have an interest in philosophy and are prepared to do a fair bit of reading each week about it.
This course is very fulfilling in that you do gain a broad understanding of the history of philosophy in general and ethics more specifically. The readings are interesting for philosophy readings. They can be dry and long, but I ultimately believe they are worth the effort. Lectures can be dry and boring, too. I recommend finding a buddy who can keep you awake during lecture and who you can keep awake during lecture. If you haven't done philosophical writing, there is a bit of a learning curve to it. I would recommend starting essays early, and bugging your TF and/or professor for help as much as you think you need to.
The course is great as an elective. Professor Doyle is very knowledgeable, although do expect him to go off–track rather frequently. I personally don't mind since the things he said are very interesting.
Would not recommend
I highly recommend this course; it is definitely the most influential class I taken, and it has considerably deepened the way I think. The texts and lectures were fascinating, and the assignments were invaluable and necessary for a firm understanding of the material. I do wish that Doyle was a bit more organized with his grading, as our papers were returned weeks after they were given, and with his lectures, during which he can often find himself meandering down some tangent, but I feel that the benefits of this class and the amount I have drawn from it far outweighed these technical issues.
The content of the class is extremely interesting if you put in effort. Sometimes covering each reading in full analytical detail is not necessary/useful, be tactical in what you can tackle. Start early enough on the essays and do not hesitate to reach out (to both Prof. Doyle and the TF) for help w.rt. the writing format and structure of a philosophy essay. The assessment/assignment portion of the class is quite back–loaded (mostly in the final weeks); again make sure to be proactive. Go to Prof. Doyle's office hours, even just "to be a fly on the wall!"
