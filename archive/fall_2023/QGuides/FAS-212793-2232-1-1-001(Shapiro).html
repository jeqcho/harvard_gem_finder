Be patient and open to new concepts
This course is usually praised as being a science class for non–science concentrators. Even as someone who had a great science background, this class was a little difficult for an introductory course. The math component was difficult to grasp. The midterm was very difficult, many people did poorly. Problem sets were difficult toward the beginning, but became more manageable as we started focusing on the history of the Earth. I think that this class has great bones and that the professor is a truly wonderful educator. The student teaching staff seemed a bit disorganized, but I think this may have been due to the fact that they weren't expecting the class to be so big. Hopefully they will be more prepared in the future. You should still absolutely take this class! It is really going to teach you so much, it just may be a little more rigorous than you would expect based on its advertisement.
This course was great if you put independent work into understanding the concepts. Irwin is the sweetest man ever but not the most effective lecturer, so I often felt like I relied on Youtube videos to understand material. However, the homework was super manageable, and if you just study a bit before the exams, the class is a great "rocks for jocks" option.
Interesting stories about science. Really accessible. Great way to fulfill the science divisional distribution requirement if you're a humanities/social sciences concentrator who needs it. Not too much time commitment + the math is really simple and accessible to anyone who has a basic grasp of algebra and a very basic grasp of trig. Professor Shapiro as well as TF Ana and CA Kim were also great!
This class might be fun if it is in–person, but over Zoom, I didn't find it particularly engaging and I thought the organization was poor. Be prepared to learn about other fields, as it's not really an astronomy class. There are parts (the first half of the course especially) that are pure astronomy, but the second half of the course is very random — and, in my opinion, pretty poor.
Irwin is amazing and is worth taking a class with
If you feel unsure about your background in math and science, this might not be for you. But if you're excited about the material, it could be a good experience.
This class is great if you want to learn more about various science phenomena. The staff are amazing and very helpful. Professor Irwin is an amazing professor and the TFs were just as amazing. take this class if you want to learn really cool stuff. ***
