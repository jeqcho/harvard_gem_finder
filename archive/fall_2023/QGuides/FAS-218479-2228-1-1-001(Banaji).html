What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class!!! It's probably one of the harder seminars, but Professor Banaji is extremely knowledgable, and really wants to get to know /help her students. The readings are fascinating, and the writing assignments force you to think about how to analyze them.
This class is very scientific; weekly assignments consist of reading and summarizing 1–2 psychological studies. This course allows students to build a strong foundation in implicit bias and the science behind this mental phenomenon. I recommend this course to students interested in brain science and developing new studies but not necessarily to students primarily interested in current events involving implicit bias. The workload is manageable and very interesting for students interested in psychology. If you are considering psychology as a concentration, this is a good seminar to evaluate whether the subject is a good fit for you.
I would recommend to students to know that if they want an easy freshmen seminar as just an extra class to get to know people that this is not that kind of class. It serves just like any other class, just without tests but the weekly workload is no different. It is demanding but very rewarding towards the end.
This is a great class. I'm not very interested in psychology as a subject, but this class has changed the way that I view and interact with the world around me. The work is very manageable and Professor Banaji is a great resource: you can tell she loves what she does.
I feel lucky to have learned from such an accomplished professor. I do believe I look at the world differently since taking this class. If you are interested in implicit bias, I strongly recommend you take this class and learn more about it and how it affects the way we interpret our world (without us even realizing it).
