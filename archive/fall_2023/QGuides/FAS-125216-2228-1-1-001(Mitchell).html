If you're searching for gems, do NOT choose this class. The only people who liked this class were Folk and Myth concentrators, and even some of them hated it. Super boring and didn't learn much
This class is a great way to fulfill the A&C GenEd requirement. Make sure to go to lectures – they are very helpful for the final exam.
This class is listed as a GENED but really doesn't follow a typical GENED format. It seems to be more of a hardcore folk and myth class than a true GENED. You definitely need to pay attention in class and do the readings to do well on the assignments and on the final. Professor Mitchell definitely cares about the students and the class but it was not super enjoyable to hear him lecture for an hour straight every day. This class could definitely be made better if there was more interaction and if it was less about the nitty gritty of folklore and more about the broad cultural implications.
Honestly, this class wasn't for me. If you enjoy learning about Scandinavian/American folklore this is the class for you, but that wasn't me. The assignments are pretty easy, though they can feel unstructured at times. The trips were really fun and make the class stand out. Not quite a gem but definitely take it if you are somewhat interested in the material and don't want to much work. Also, only do the readings necessary for section the others are way too long.
This class is a great introduction to folklore studies. Though it can be hard to stay engaged at 9am lectures, they often contain funny videos and really out–there topics like
"necropants" that make lectures entertaining. Readings can be a little slow, but looking over them helps prepare you for class. Folklore collections are definitely a little difficult to compose at first because most students haven't done anything like them before, but they really help you gain an appreciation for Harvard Folklore. This class is a fun choice if you want to learn about how narratives and story–telling reflect and shape cultures.
This course is great! I really enjoyed it.
It's fun, lectures are a bit boring if you aren't interested in the material on your own.
I would definitely tell future students that this course is an excellent choice for those interested in Europe.
