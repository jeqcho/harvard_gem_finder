This is a great course! If you're considering concentrating in astrophysics I'd definitely recommend taking this class as a way of helping you decide if the subject is for you.
Take Astronomy 17! The material is fascinating. Prof. Eisenstein is amazing. The TFs are wonderful.
As for some advice:
– Do the readings! You can get by if you skip, but you will get so much more out of the class (+will be able to participate better) if you do.
– Get into good note–taking habits, especially as you go through the modules. There will be a lot of material to review (or learn!) for the final exam.
If you like astronomy take the class. It's very interesting, and it's a rather manageable intro class.
This is one of the best classes Harvard has ro offer! It is very well–taught and very well–structured. With little effort, you can learn a vast amounts about cosmology. The math/physics required is minimal and the content is more qualitative than you wold think.
I would make use of the hours at the tail end of the week as they are helpful in digesting the content better and helping you get through the problem sets. Be attentive in class because you may miss content if you are not putting your full attention forward.
This is an amazing class! Eisenstein is an awesome professor, and his lectures are super engaging and interesting. The psets are totally doable, especially if you start them early (I was able to complete them mostly on my own throughout the week before checking answers/finishing up during the Friday section) and the class has weekly quizzes instead of a midterm, which makes it a lot less stressful. There is definitely more emphasis on qualitative concepts in addition to knowing how to solve quantitative problems, so make sure you have a solid understanding of both going into the final and you'll be fine. This class was super fascinating and honestly one of my favorites classes here at Harvard –– I would recommend it over Astronomy 16 if you're interested in trying out an astronomy course!
Please read the textbook. If you fall behind on readings it's hard to fully try to catch up on understanding fully the material, I thought you could probably take a lot away from this course just from reading the textbook.
The content of this course is fascinating! Professor Eisenstein is a good lecturer, and sections are helpful and well–taught by the TFs. Structurally, though, this course has some annoying aspects. The problem sets do not always reflect what is most difficult conceptually, and sometimes feel like busywork. When studying for the exam, we also are given very little guidance of what information is the most relevant, and what was simply interesting and mentioned offhand, and we were told at some point or another that almost every resource we had (problem sets, study questions, quizzes, etc.) was not necessarily indicative of the content or difficulty of the final exam.
This class starts with manageable and enjoyable subjects and quickly accelerates to a level of difficulty that disincentivizes individual learning and leaves students behind. Professor Eisenstein is very open to questions, which is wonderful. One issue that frequently arose with questions, however, was that when a student posed a clarifying question, Professor Eisenstein would reply with an answer that did not clarify the situation, but only added more layers of confusion onto it.

The workload is manageable, the TFs are wonderful, and the qualitative part of this course is engaging and interesting. The difficulty of the course, however, is very noteworthy; few Harvard students (especially freshmen and sophomores) have the skills and experience required to truly thrive in this class.
The lecture component of class tended to be a regurgitation of textbook and module material. The course is definitely difficult and without there being a midterm, you will have to study a lot for the final if you dont want to get blindsided. There is one pset a week/ one quiz, and textbook readings. Definitely recommend taking this course and keeping up with the textbook readings. If you do this, you should be fine.
This course is fascinating, and I loved learning about cosmology and the ways that we currently understand the expansion of the Universe. The materials build on each other, and so more complicated concepts follow naturally from concepts learned earlier in the course. The problems can feel somewhat repetitive or over–simplified, but overall a great class and I would recommend it!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Eisenstein is a wonderful professor who explains concepts quite well. He is very open to answering questions throughout the class and is very humble. This is one of my favorite classes that I have ever taken at Harvard, and is a gem in my opinion– the course content is beyond fascinating. You learn about everything from black holes, quasars, dark matter, dark energy, the expansion of the Universe, before the Big Bang, etc. (again, pretty much everything) The course will be what you make out of it. I highly recommend reading the textbook chapters and modules every week and taking notes on them. Try your best not to fall behind, and definitely don't skip the readings/modules– they're the key to doing well on the final exam and actually understanding what is going on in the class.
Very interesting subject/class with not too bad of a workload if slightly standard lecture style classes. If you take the class, make sure to keep Friday office hours open in schedule as may need for help on homework
It is not a good class. Lectures are not engaging, and you have to do a lot of work reading the textbook on your own if you really want to understand things. Pset's are not that hard, but they are often weird questions that are sometimes impossible to do without clarification from a TF. They also do not really teach you python, so it is helpful to know beforehand otherwise you have to be carried along. I would not recommend you take it, but if you do, be prepared to put a lot of work in on your own time OR not understanding things very well and get a lot of help.
GOOD
– Daniel is basically the pioneer of DESI and BAO, which is what you will learn later in the course.
– You learn some astro material, including cosmology done in a quantitative manner (eg accelerating expansion of the Universe)

BAD
– The lectures are ineffective and attendance is part of the grade. Most of the learning is done reading the textbook though.
– The assignments and exams are quite basic math and physics. If you are a serious physics concentrator, this course is mainly an annoyance than something fun. I only find the content after Week 8 interesting.

ADVICE
– Ask Daniel questions to make the most out of this course
– If you are not concentrating in astro, take some high level astro classes instead or fun ones like celestial navigation.

MISC
– This class is a requirement for Astro concentration or secondary, though there are some gov or econs people taking it for fun. I am not sure how fun they found this course, but you definitely emerge with some cosmology knowledge and a better understanding of the Universe

CONCLUSION
– This has potential to be a great class with promising content. They just need better lectures and more rigorous content.
