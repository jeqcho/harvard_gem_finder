What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Don't expect a satisfactory teaching of (time–dep and time–indep) perturbation theory.
Prior to starting this class, it would be a good idea to brush up on undergraduate quantum mechanics and learn the basics of group theory.
You cannot rely on lectures for your learning of the course material, but Professor Vafa is really nice and don't be afraid to ask for clarification or ask questions.
I would strongly recommend to participate in classes since there are much to get from Prof. Vafa's explanation that cannot be reached through lecture notes.
It's a good class, but I recommend supplementing with outside reading, such as Sakurai's quantum mechanics text. Both the lecture and the Weinburg text can be hard to follow at times.
