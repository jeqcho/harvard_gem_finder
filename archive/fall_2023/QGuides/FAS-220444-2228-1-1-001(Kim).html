Comments
Take this class if you're interested in East Asia, Southeast Asia, science fiction, or literature. There's really good breadth to the material covered even though this is still the first iteration of the course, and it'll likely teach you something new about a region that you already knew, or shed light on a region that you didn't know before this. The readings are extremely varied, and though they take up a decent amount of time, doing them is genuinely a really interesting and engaging experience.
I LOVED THIS COURSE!! The syllabus was really interesting and spanned across short stories, novels, TV shows, and films (both older and very modern) and generated a lot of great discussions. I learned a lot in this class and really enjoyed the close–knit culture & having Adhy as a professor.
This was a great course to take to learn about a variety of Asian history and its role in literature, international opinions, and media. The class itself could get a little boring as it was a 2–hour discussion based class and we didn't always get breaks. Having a background in literature/literary analysis is certainly helpful, but not necessary and it was easy to participate regardless of background.
This is a great seminar especially if you like science fiction!
Take this class! The readings are fantastic and super fun. You'll learn so much about this niche topic. Plus Adhy is a very good lecturer and does a good job facilitating discussion in the course.
