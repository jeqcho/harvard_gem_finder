What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Try to get some hands–on experience at CNS before the final project season rolls around. Just email the TFs for some one–on–one time. It will be well worth it when you can use all the CNS tools fluently and not have to worry about compressing CNS equipment training and final project fabrication into the same few weeks.
This class gives you great experience in Harvard’s clean room spaces and you not only learn about MEMS, but you also get to design and fabricate which has been a lot of fun!

You will have to work in teams throughout the entire semester (only one assignment was done individually) so if you are a lone wolf when it comes to studying then this is not the course for you. Fawwaz has a conversational style of teaching which requires student participation, so be prepared to ask questions. The final project did take a lot of time (think 5 hours every day over 4/5 days) but most other weeks were only a few hours a week.

Other than that, this class has been lovely to have in my schedule and I think this course will improve greatly as the teaching staff seemed self–aware of how the class was perceived throughout the semester.
MEMs is a very hands–on course. It has a very specific application, so what you learn seems a little disconnected from what you're learning in other engineering classes. However, I really like all the group work and collaboration involved in and out of lecture.
This course is great for getting some introductory cleanroom experience, however really make sure you know how to complete all of the design steps INDEPENDENTLY in the labs so that you are prepared for the final project.
