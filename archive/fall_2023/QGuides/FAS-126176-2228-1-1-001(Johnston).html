What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class gave me a lot of fundamental insight on the world of stable isotopes throughout Earth history. I came into this class knowing a little about isotopes, but came away from this class with a different view of how dynamic isotope systems can be especially when considering deep time. I'd highly recommend this class, it was a fun experience and allowed for me to hone my science communication skills too. I can say that now I have a basis of knowledge to apply in my future graduate studies –– scientific and otherwise!
Nice class overall! Lot's of opportunities for collaboration and great discussions!
This class is really useful if you have experience with stable isotope geochemistry and are interested in the Precambrian. If you do not have a background on stable isotope systems, you may struggle a bit in this course (or have to spend the first few weeks catching up). The final symposium in Colorado at the end of the course was really fulfilling. It was great to meet the CU students jointly taking the course and get immense feedback on our final projects.
This is a fun class with the potential to learn a lot if you have the goal to do so. The instructors are also helpful and encouraging, especially when it comes to the very open ended final project.
