What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a wonderful Gov 94 that eases you into the research process and how to start formulating your own empirical research. The class has a lot of support for you to navigate reading political science papers effectively, developing research questions and hypotheses, and creating a long–term plan for a research paper. What I love is that you can choose to do a research prospectus instead of having to do your own research, and you still learn loads about research design and methodology. And the concepts are super interesting! Lots of issues in democracy are unique to or more prevalent in the Global South, and the readings introduce you to major issues that will pop up often in the field. Overall, highly recommend!
Definitely take this course. Julie is amazing and you'll learn a lot about research design in political science. The readings are amazing and she sends a reading guidance with them which makes it easy to read them. She also doesn't really care if you read the entire thing all the time and focuses on reading effectively and actually just getting the most important things from a reading. The final project is super flexible and you can basically do it on whatever you want within the topic. Great course overall and would really recommend. Even if you're not a gov concentrator tbh, if you're interested in democracy in the global south, take the course.
I can't recommend this class highly enough! Julie is an amazing instructor who makes in–class discussions very accessible and pressure–free. The assigned readings were a lot of fun to read and each addressed very interesting research questions through different research methods. One could really learn a lot about political science research thanks to the readings. In addition, the in–class discussions also touched on larger themes and a little bit of theory as well, so class time was not just spent on summaries of readings. Grading seemed to be quite student–friendly.
If you are interested in gaining knowledge about democracies that you have likely not learned a lot about then I highly recommend this course.
Professor Weaver is amazing. Not only Is the course content so interesting if you are generally interested in learning about politics in the Global South, but the readings are manageable and Professor Weaver provides guiding notes to help you with them. She was always available to chat about the research memos and overall it was just a lovely time. Really recommend.
