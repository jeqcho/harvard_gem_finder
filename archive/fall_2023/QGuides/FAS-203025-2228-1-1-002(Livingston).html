What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you've taken Latin far in the past (if you've taken Latin before you should be taking Latin 3) it would be a good refresher/might be a little repetitive, depending on how well you know Latin. For those who have never taken Latin before, the course moves pretty quickly (3ish grammatical concepts and 20is new vocab words a week) so be prepared. There is a little quiz almost every week, but if you keep on top of your assignments and know the vocab you'll basically be fine.
For the most part, this is a non–Latin–speaking course. The teaching staff is great, providing interactive lessons. Dr. Livingston provides visuals to help visual learners understand Latin through storytelling. As someone who is not good at languages or studying Classics (I had no Latin background and it's been a few years since I took a language), learning this language was hard. I would study everyday or else you will fall behind. If you speak Spanish or French, Latin may come a bit easier. There are a lot of grammatical systems and words to memorize. In terms of difficulty, I would probably rate it as difficult in terms of the exams. The examinations really test if you have mastered the material. Again, don't fall behind: do your work and study.
Work hard, have fun.
