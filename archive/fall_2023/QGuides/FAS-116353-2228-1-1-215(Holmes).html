If you were recommended Expos 20, but still have reservations – I encourage you to take Expos 20, not just because it's a one–semester course, but because the intensity and vigor at which the course paces itself will drastically improve your writing overall.
As far as EXPOS goes, Climate Fictions is a great section to choose. The subject matter is fairly straightforward as compared to many other EXPOS sections, and the readings are definitely doable. Most importantly, Eliza is such a lovely person, making section time a lot more fun and engaging than it potentially could be, with hands–on activities taking up a fair amount of in–class time. She provides useful feedback in writing as well. However, as EXPOS tends to be, if you want a good grade you'll have to put in the work.
Climate fictions is a great choice for expos– the topic may at first seem boring but there are so many different perspectives to look at climate change and this course really explored them all. Writing assignments have a lot of freedom in them, which makes them much more enjoyable to do. The final paper is basically just writing about whatever form of art you like, which was a very fun experience for me.
This course is great. Readings are interesting and you get lots of freedom for your graded papers.
Make sure you don’t procrastinate in this class! You will fall behind.
Expos 20 is widely considered one of the most difficult freshman courses, but I actually think that that can change a lot depending on your level of interest in the course and the professor's engagement/enthusiasm and kindness. Eliza was a very supportive and reasonable professor and the course itself is incredibly relevant globally right now and getting to examine climate issues through the lens of novels, movies, creative mediums, etc was really special.
Eliza is an amazing teacher! Rank this class as your 1st! YOU WILL NOT BE DISSAPOINTED I PROMISE YOU – WE LITERALLY WATCHED PRINCESS MONONOKE WHICH WAS AMAZING!
This class was one of my most enjoyable classes. I loved the people and felt so comfortable with Eliza and all my classmates. It was very easy and comfortable to participate. Eliza's feedback was the most valuable to me; she truly put in a lot of effort to give helpful comments. It is evident how much work she put into helping students and wanting them to succeed. It is also very relevant to today in the sense that learning about climate fictions is very important.
Would not recommend. Preceptor is very kind, but assignments are lengthy and frankly unnecessary. I feel that my writing has gotten worse, as the class prioritizes quantity in words over quality in content. Class (mandatory attendance) is a waste of time, and I could spend the hour doing something more productive (but this may be true across all EXPOS sections).
