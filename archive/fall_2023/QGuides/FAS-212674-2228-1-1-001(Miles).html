What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class. It is not too time–consuming but will help you continue a study of Black life.
This class is the most rewarding class I have every taken at Harvard. It gives you an opportunity to directly see the impact of your work in real time, which is an experience that will stay with me long after I graduate. The class is challenging, but it is in such a warm and welcoming environment that makes it feel easy. I highly recommend this class to anyone interested,
Professor Miles is a generous, engaging professor who is truly a leading expert in this field. You will learn a lot about the challenges and responsibility of communicating painful histories to a variety of publics. I would recommend this class to anyone who is interested in working in the public sphere, whether in history, government or education. We all have a responsibility to be considering how the history of slavery impacts our work in the world.
INCREDIBLE CLASS! TAKE IT!!!
Tiya Mile is an incredible professor. In undergraduate and graduate programs, I have never had an instructor as attentive to, thoughtful about, and responsive to students' needs as Professor Miles, especially in coursework with difficult subject matter. Readings were thought–provoking, in–class discussions were rich, and assignments did not feel like hurdles or busywork, but like essential components of the work of the course. Site visits were powerful experiences. The success of the course owes much to Professor Miles' gift for making students feel empowered to work through facets of a challenging topic together, and with the mutual support of their peers, professor, and teaching fellow.
