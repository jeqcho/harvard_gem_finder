This course definitely encourages discussion amongst peers and encourages you to consider the ethical implications of research while simultaneously imagining the far reaching impact of the groundbreaking field of study: regenerative biology.

This was a terrific freshman seminar – TAKE IT!

Paola and Amie are wonderful, and it really is a great opportunity to learn about some very exciting topics. Lectures were always interesting, and we had some very cool labs as well (seeing human brain organoids and an Axolotl lab). If learning how to turn human skin cells into electrically active human brain organoids, discussing brain plasticity, learning about brain–machine interfaces, talking about the bioethics of chimeras, etc. sound interesting to you, you'll love this course. And how can those not sound interesting!

Paola and Amie were so nice and are really skilled at what they do. They care a lot about the course, and the students in it! They aimed to make it an engaging course, not only being lectured at but with students actively participating in the conversation, and I think they succeeded quite well.

One thing to keep in mind – you'll get out of this course what you put in. You didn't need to do any of the readings to go to class, which was very nice, but all of the suggested readings and resources are incredibly interesting, and well worth the read. I am sorry I did not spend more time engaging with them, and I still got a LOT out of this course without, but I do intend to go through all of the readings when I have a chance, because the topics are just that interesting.

So, this course really is an INCREDIBLE offering, everything a freshman seminar should be, and then some. Take it if you can, you will not regret it.
