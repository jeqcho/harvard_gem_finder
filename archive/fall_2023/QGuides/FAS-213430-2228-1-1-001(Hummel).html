A very straightforward Gov elective, likely appealing especially to those with comparative/IR focuses. Lecture twice a week led by the prof, section once a week with the TF, and 4 assignments throughout the semester based on syllabus topics that are on your project group's dictator. 2 of these are group submissions (hopefully will be de–emphasized in future) and 2 are individual assignments. One timed midterm (short essay writing) and one final (a report with rubric on your case group, will probably include some content from the previous group assignments). Late policy (72 hour bank) very reasonable), lectures range from interesting to slightly repetitive. Definitely a political theory class (what factors make regime more durable, what are the biggest threats to a dictator, etc) and not a content class, so don't come in expecting to learn about the history/context of different regimes around the world in depth. Definitely one of the easiest Gov electives I've taken in four years, with really decent payoff in terms of learning new/interesting material at low time investment.
