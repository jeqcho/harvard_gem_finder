What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class if you look at the syllabus and think at least 3–4 of the films will be pretty interesting to you. Professor Erspamer clearly has a ton of knowledge both about the films themselves and about the context of Italy at that time. His presentations go into lots of the intellectual background worth considering. The class meetings themselves early on consist of mostly him talking but it's quite interesting. That being said, if you don't find a particular film interesting, it can turn into a bit of a drag. You don't get any feedback back on the response papers you write, but they're a good exercise. Overall a solid class for interest, especially if it counts for something (Italian citation, etc)
I learned more about film and literature, facilitated by Professor Erspamer's deep knowledge of the subject and enthusiasm and passion for incorporating student feedback during class discussion. I learned more both about Italian cinema and the course improved my writing concision with the response papers. Awesome, low–stress course that I'd encourage anyone, especially those with Italian background to consider!
Take this course! The Professor is, and I say this without exaggeration, one of the brightest minds not only at Harvard, but out there. He is someone who will inspire you through the humanities and his way of seeing the world to look at life differently and to ponder upon the questions that truly merit thought. I am a senior now and will truly miss the chances of being able to sit and hear his thoughts, to be with him has been one of the greatest privileges of my time here.
Watch the movies as properly as you can, on a large clean screen with good sound in a dark space and with no interruption. Also, readings are helpful and should not be ignored. This is how to get most out of this class. Participate as well! Its a class I really enjoyed
