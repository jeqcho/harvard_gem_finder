One of my favorite classes at Harvard! Having never taken an English course before, I was really nervous at the start, but it ended up being very welcoming and fun. Highly recommend!!!
Engaging, thought–provoking course with a very reasonable workload and a fantastic syllabus!
Definitely take this class if you have the chance! Professor Kim and the TFs are all incredibly kind and have made the experience unforgettable. They make the material very accessible and cultivate such a welcoming learning environment. You leave the class with so much knowledge and appreciation.
TAKE THIS CLASS. As a stem concentrator who's taken their fair share of Harvard English classes, this is by far the best one I've taken. Don't be scared of the lecture format –– the class is engaging and discussion–based & Professor Kim and the TFs know everyone's name!
This course is equal parts ethnic studies and literature, so if you are interested in both, this course will be good for you.
This course was probably the most impactful courses I have ever taken at Harvard. I cannot say enough about the lecture format, connections to Asian American identity, and beautiful choice of works for the syllabus that made this class unforgettable.
Overall a really good course! The selected texts are interesting and Professor Kim does a really good job of providing historical context to all of the works that we read. There could definitely be more room for discussion and certain texts are emphasized more than others, but the course provides a very broad sample of Asian American texts, so it's understandable that we can't spend too much time on any one piece. Assignments are very reasonable and readings are also doable (albeit a bit heavy for novels vs short stories) as a STEM concentrator, I'd definitely recommend this class to anyone looking to take an English class with a very reasonable course load.
I cannot recommend this class enough. Professor Kim is absolutely incredible, and is a delight to listen to lecture in class. She is also great at encouraging discussion in class even in a lecture format. The class is so well–organized and expectations are super clear, so even though the syllabus might seem daunting, it's actually super manageable and ended up being one of my more chill classes to manage. The readings are all so interesting and discussions are great. I loved this class!
This class has quite a bit of reading and moves pretty fast regarding content, but it involves takeaways both in the sphere of Asian American studies and in English and literature studies. Assignments are relatively light, and I found the course content and section genuinely engaging. If you're at all interested in Asian American studies and organizing, I would definitely recommend this course!
This is an ABSOLUTELY TRANSFORMATIVE course: perhaps my favorite class after seven semesters here. Professor Kim is incredibly understanding, knowledgeable, and is a great person. Her lectures are very well–organized and eye–opening, and the syllabus is very interesting and relevantI was just so happy sitting in lecture! The course is also very welcoming to students of different backgrounds in many ways, and I always felt supported and never looked down upon for not knowing something. I have a much deeper understanding of the Asian American narrative over the last couple decades (and myself and my own identity as a result), and have learned so much about reading/understanding/analyzing different texts. A caveat I must add is that of course, you only get out of this class as much as you put in: definitely do the readings, as it makes lectures more understandable. I wish every class was like this. PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS!
Great class! Prof Kim is amazing. The readings are great, and it's really cool to see Prof Kim essentially do a lovely close reading of the texts. Realized what people actually want out of a English paper by listening to her analyses. Prof Kim is also just a really interesting person and super warm and kind. I wish I could have gotten to know her better, but limited time and energy is my excuse.
I really enjoyed this class! The professor and teaching staff are so great and really want to see you succeed, and the readings are super interesting. The assignments are also very chill and there is an opportunity for extra credit at the end. I took this class without ever having taken an english class, and I found it easy and interesting, so I would definitely recommend, especially for anyone looking to fulfill the second semester english for the premed requirement.
I had rarely had the chance to read Asian American literature. This class allows you to do just that. I do have to say that lectures are mainly Prof Kim lecturing all the material at you, I personally wish there was more discussion even in lecture and not just section.
take this class, if you're interested in asian american literature! all the texts are really interesting to read.
Take this class!! I personally learned so much about not only Asian American literature but Asian American history as a whole which was extremely fulfilling for me as someone who had very little exposure to Asian American Studies before taking this class. I think there's something really special about seeing yourself wholly represented in literature, and I definitely felt a special connection to the texts a few times throughout this semester. Plus, Professor Kim and the TFs are super understanding and knowledgable, and genuinely want students to succeed. 10/10 experience overall!
This is a great class to take if you are interested in reading the texts, and it's also a great option as a first English class since it's super accessible and many of the students are not English concentrators. Professor Kim and the tf's are also super supportive and encouraging. The only downside I found in this class was that it would sometimes buy into the kindof Asian American monolith and lose track of the level of variety in Asian American experiences, which I sometimes found to be somewhat alienating.
Grading is dependent on section leaders. Even if you don't do that great on your essays, you can still get a good grade! There are extra credit opportunities and an essay revision opportunity. Even if you don't write essays often, you can still take this class! Close reading is a unique form of essay writing for the English department/ field of study, so it's fine to have no prior background. Even if you write a lot or a little, you will learn a lot from this class.
