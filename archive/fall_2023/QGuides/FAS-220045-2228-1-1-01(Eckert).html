What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is an excellent class but requires you to be thoroughly interested and knowledgeable in both Korean and Japanese history at an academic level. Historiography can sometimes feel arcane and deeply academic, so you must enjoy the content or it will be difficult to get through the readings/classes. As much as this is true for all classes, it is particularly true for this one. Most students in the class are masters (RSEA) or early PhD students. The course material and discussion quality reflects the calibre of its students. Hence the readings are long and hard and you will very often feel lost but by the end of the class, it will feel worthwhile in that you will have accumulated a very deep understanding of the different historical arguments surrounding big topics in Korean/Japanese history. That is what this class teaches you: to differentiate between historical arguments made by different authors on the same topic. It teaches you to become a more discerning and critical reader. But to achieve this, you must have a strong foundation in both Korean and Japanese history. Some experience in historiography would be helpful too. If you are interested in doing a PhD / going into academia, this is a good class to take.
This class is really a helpful and nice course!
If you have any interest in this area and have the chance to take this course, do so. This is not an opportunity that comes often. Having had the privilege of studying with each instructor individually, having them together in the same class was extraordinary. The reading list is excellent and varied. Discussion is very engaging.
One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. Professor Gordon and Professor Eckert are so kind and supportive. Will come out of the class learning a lot about Korean and Japanese history!
Japan and the ROK are closest neighboring countries. Learning recent histories / societies in both countries at the same time would be a great opportunity for you to broaden your understanding of historical/cultural contexts within them. Also, if you are interested in historical issues between them, at the final class, you would get so much excitement for discussing them seriously with bright classmates!
