It is very refreshing to have someone who is as focused on the act of teaching as Louis. This likely the first physics class you will take that is not grounded in your physical intuition; that is scary, but Louis and the rest of the teaching staff do a good job of introducing the reasoning behind the concepts as well as the mathematical machinery. Also, this course is required for anyone doing physics, so you likely don't have a choice in taking it. With that said, there is absolutely nothing to be afraid of!
It is, objectively, one of the classes with THE MOST SUPPORTIVE TEACHING STAFF I've ever taken here at harvard. Take this class with Louis. There are so incredibly many opportunities to ask questions and make sure you understand every single concept presented in lecture (literally like 5 hours of office hours EVERY DAY!!!). Some people complained about the class going too slow or being too handwavy, but DO NOT listen to them. This class will prioritize conceptual understanding over EVERYTHING else, and that is what matters. You might not rush through Griffiths as some people want to, but you will have a very solid understand of the theory, and you will be more than well prepared for any other higher level physics class (also, don't get me wrong, the psets do take some time – approx. 8 hours). Quantum Mechanics is SO COOL, you should learn it from some one equally cool. And there is no one cooler than Louis Deslauriers.
I would highly recommend taking this class! Especially if Louis is teaching it. The material is very interesting if you're interested in mathematical physics. It's taught at a pace that is somewhat fast but still reasonable. Louis is a fantastic teacher! He explains the material well, and you can tell that he cares about you and your learning, which is great for helping you stay motivated throughout the class. He gives very fair homeworks and tests, and he has a great sense of humor.

Louie is amazing very dedicated to his students, generous with his time, and really passionate about the material.
Take this class! Louis is an awesome instructor, and for the most part the material is presented very clearly and with a very helpful conceptual basis. The subject material is really beautiful, and brings life to both physics and the mathematics it relies on.

You should know linear algebra well before taking this class! We had little "introduction", and for the most part linear algebra was treated (appropriately) as a prerequisite. You will not understand the material nearly as well if you do not thoroughly understand how to compute an eigenvector or change a basis, and having experience with i.e. Fourier series really will help your understanding a lot.

Watch out for the last few weeks! I found them significantly more difficult conceptually, and there are a lot of subtleties that are hidden away. In particular, watch out for the Bloch vector! It's very easy to convince yourself that you understand it well, even though it's far trickier than it looks.
I love Louis' philosophy towards teaching.
