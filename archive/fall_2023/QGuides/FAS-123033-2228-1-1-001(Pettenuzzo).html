Ec 1123 is definitely not as hard as people make it out to be. I got worried when I read the previous year's Q reviews, but it was definitely not as difficult as some reviews made it out to seem. You definitely need a really strong statistics background (either Stat 100/104 level or AP Stat, if you remember enough and are willing to spend some time at the beginning of the semester reviewing the stats concepts — note that Stat 110 won't help you at all here). Don't let the Stata element scare you away — it's definitely not too difficult to learn, and you only need it for one question per pset. If you have questions about Stata or any material, make sure to go to the TF office hours (especially Salvador!! (I'd recommend Lucas too but he's graduating this year)). Prof. Pettenuzzo is also really helpful, and is very willing to explain any concepts/pset questions if you ask.

Maybe the best advice I can give is to read the textbook!!!! I read through the first half of the textbook before the midterm and the second half before the final, and found that it really helpfully covers all of the material from lecture while providing a bit more reasoning and packaging the material slightly differently (so you can improve your understanding of the content).

Oh and also go to section!! It's not required, but you'll find that it's really helpful because it does a great job of distilling the lecture material and giving you the content that will be the most important for the psets.
For anyone debating on whether to take Econometrics in the fall or in the spring, TAKE IT IN THE FALL! Professor Pettenuzzo's teaching style was incredibly engaging, as he went through note taking with the rest of the class rather than most professors' style of reading off of powerpoint slides. The teaching staff are always available for students who would like support and are enthusiastic as well. While the material can be difficult at times, if you put in the work you will get a lot out of it. The assignments and exams are very fair and reward hard work. This was one of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard and I cannot recommend Pettenuzzo's EC1123 class enough.
