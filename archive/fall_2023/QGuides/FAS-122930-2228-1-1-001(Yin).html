Students were literally laughed at by the professor and fellow classmates when they asked questions. The professor was condescending, often referring to certain proofs that required pages of complex analysis as "trivial" or "elementary."
xi yin teaches the class in a more theoretical way than a typical QFT course, but you will come away with a deeper understanding of the material.
This class is not impossible as an undergrad. Find a pset group to study with and spend about 20ish hrs per week and you’ll be fine. Don’t let people scare you away from it—it’s the culmination of every single math and physics class you’ve taken, and it’s worth taking. Be prepared to work hard but in the end you’ll learn a ton.
Quantum field theory is very deep. If you wished to be mystified (in a good way), you won't regret taking it.
This class is hard, and it at times has the feeling of a math class which can be hard to follow for people trying to gain physical reasoning. The final project is time consuming, prepare for that.

The course is great! You can learn a lot about quantum field theory in this course. The assignments are very helpful to better understand the materials. Some familiarity with undergrad–level complex analysis and working with indices for tensors would be helpful. With Professor Yin, we also had the opportunity to do a final project to explore something interesting related to what we've learned in the course and it was a great way to learn how to approach a theoretical physics topic and very helpful for future work whether you will do theoretical physics or not because you would learn how to think deeply on a scientific issue and question yourself and keep pushing yourself to learn more.
