What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're interested in social theory, I would recommend this course. Although it is listed in the History department, this is definitely more of a philosophy class. Wonderful if this is your first exposure to Benjamin.
This course was a lot of fun and both Professor Gordon and Michael Mango are excellent at leading the class through some very tough material. Some weeks will be more confusing than others, but the ones that are illuminating are REALLY illuminating. The assignments are quite flexible and office hours are great.
Jumping ahead of all technological possibilities, Professor Gordon and Walter Benjamin, working in careful conjunction, will in a snap of a finger transport you to Paris for free every week. This is a class in which you will get a chance to dive deep into the recesses of European literature and philosophy and temporarily see the French Second Empire resurrected and examined so many years after its fall. It is a seminar that will never fail to give you beautiful and stimulating readings followed by conversations that will then help pierce the surface of the many delightful, allusive texts. Truly, truly a marvelous, wonderful experience, at the crossroads of so many sources of adventure!
Very interesting material, the Michael Mango Peter Gordon combination is a match made in heaven. They are a delightful pair. Benjamin is an experience – don't expect to 'learn' in the traditional sense. But enjoy the ride.
Wonderful seminar — less a History class and more similar to Social Studies work. The texts are quite dense, but Benjamin is a must–read, and Prof Gordon and Michael eloquently articulate the nuances of his theory. Only 1–2 assignments but you need to come to class having read and prepared to engage in discussion.
I would recommend this class for an in–depth study of Walter Benjamin and his writing specifically on Paris. It is fascinating stuff, and a semester only begins to scratch the surface.
Take this class! It's a great way to make friends with a bunch of weirdos who like talking about Marx only slightly less than they like making fun of other people who like talking about Marx too much. There are two camps of people in this class: 1) aesthetes who vibe with Benjamin's cultural criticism + literary writing; and 2) people who rock with the proletariat revolution. Together they create banger discussions. But in all seriousness, Benjamin is a great voice to have in your head when trying to make sense of the late capitalist phantasmagoria, and if Michael's still the TF, that's reason alone to take the class.
