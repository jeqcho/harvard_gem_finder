It's a lot of busy work but if you have to take it you have to take it, 3 psets and group projects made for a lot of hours spent at MQC but still doable at the end of the day
Math 1a is a good class to learn calculus. It definitely takes up a lot of time and discipline in order to keep up with the 3 psets a week. It can be hard if taken in the fall because during sections you won't have the typical classroom dynamic, but you will work more with peers to learn the concepts.
It's an introductory math class. Nothing life–changing, but well–taught with friendly and helpful instructors, and if you need this for other classes or QRD, it's a good option to choose.
This class is hard!!! If you have not taken calc with integrals and derivatives you will struggle and always be at MQC and Office hours (but make sure you go to them).
They should be ready to work hard
You probably have to take this course for some sort of requirement, so the following points may not actually influence your decision to take it or not. However...
1. The PSets are rough. They aren't too long at all, but they are due every Monday, Wednesday and Friday. You always have math you could be doing and that is sort of horrific. Additionally, many of the PSets I was unable to complete by myself: I will never get back the hours I spent from 7:30pm–10:00pm in the MQC for so many nights this semester, feeling miserable. Some of the CA's are great, others are really moody, not super helpful and talk to like you are sort of dumb.
2. The projects are not that related to course material at all. I wish they were: it would make the class better, however, they are not. No one in any of my groups did any work and I ended up doing a lot of it.
3. The way the sections are structured can be pretty bad. The first half of the semester, there was no demonstration or lecture at the beginning of class to show you how to do what you were supposed to be doing. You were expected to stumble through a worksheet with a group of fellow students (many of which had completely different learning styles than I did) and "figure it out." This was miserable. This got a lot better after the midterm survey went out because they began to incorporate some form of lecture at the beginning of class: but be forewarned.
4. Your experience in this class will greatly differ depending on who your CA's and TF are.
This course is an amazing introduction to calculus, and the instructors have taken the time to ensure that the course is accessible even for those that do not have any previous experience with the material.
Go to MQC its a life saver and pset grade saver! Try to go start the Pset at office hours then finish it at MQC.
Use the worksheets and psets to study and actually look at the objectives for the exams. Make a checklist of the objectives and make sure you know how to do every part of each objective.
This class is very easy if you have an AB Calculus AB background. I had this coming in and it made a huge difference, especially with how I approached section. The class teaches very basic calculus concepts in a very overcomplicated way and because of this, it is easy to get lost even if you already have prior knowledge of the course. I had friends that did not know any calculus and they definitely struggled a little bit more. 3 PSETs a week gets old, but most of them are pretty good in reaffirming concepts. Make sure to do the practice assessments before every exam because it gives a good indication of exam difficulty.
