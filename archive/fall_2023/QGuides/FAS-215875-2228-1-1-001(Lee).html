The course is definitely full of important content that I think everyone should learn. I would recommend the class if it was not for the huge change in the syllabus that was made right after add/drop period ended. The reason I decided to take this course was because it seemed like a relatively light workload while still teaching valuable information. In the beginning the main components of the course seemed to be the healthy living challenge (which I figured would hold me accountable and help form healthy habits), a midterm, cpr training, and weekly reading discussions. I liked the idea of having an overarching project as the main workload as to having weekly assignments in the course. However, right after add/drop period, the syllabus was edited so that the healthy living challenge was only 2 weeks long and there were weekly reading summaries that were to be submitted. I think reducing the healthy living challenge reduced its main purpose which I would assume was to help students stay balanced. Furthermore, before taking the course, I was unaware that there was going to be a THREE hours final. It was not on the syllabus and it wasn't even on the 3 hour final course list when it came out. The final was added on very late into the semester. Having to study for the midterm and final were more stressful than it should have been due to the fact that there was little guidance on specific topics we needed to know ("everything was fair game"). The course is interesting but it was not at all respectful to the students enrolled to change the syllabus that much, especially after add/drop period.

However, be prepared that if you want to actually get a good grade, the 2 nights before the midterm and the 2 nights before the final are going to be kinda hellish because you will need to squeeze all of the content of the class that you likely don't remember anything from into your poor little brain. Lecture was at 9 AM this semester and I do NOT like waking up early, but going to his lecture just started off my day in the right way because he radiates so much wholesome energy and really just wants you all to be happy. I definitely recommend this class 100%. AND, you will actually end up learning a ton about how to keep yourself and your family healthy–– like seriously, you will likely end up living a longer and healthier life if you take this class and go to lecture and take it seriously. You also get to learn how to do CPR which is so so cool.
