What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a wonderful introduction to Sanskrit, but is certainly not an 'easy' class. By the end, you will learn a great deal of elementary Sanskrit, and feel comfortable with the script (Devanagari) and be able to read and understand basic prose. Nell is a fabulous instructor who cares deeply about her students. However, be prepared to work hard. The language is difficult, and Nell definitely expects a lot of her students in regards to putting in the work to memorize verbal conjugations, nominal declensions, and vocabulary. Because everything is additive and builds on previous things, I highly recommend not skimping over the basics like sandhi or the meaning of each declension class. But, all in all, I recommend if you are interested in Sanskrit and are willing to work hard. If you want an easy A in a language class, skip.
Sanskrit is memorization intensive.
Be prepared for a lot of memorization and just when you think that your head is jammed full of grammar rules, Nell bounces in smiling with another handout full of them. Translating is like a big puzzle, and if you can find the joy in working through it, it is very worth the effort.
Sanskrit is a beautiful and interesting language, and Nell is such an amazing teacher!!! If you’re genuinely interested in putting in that work to learn Sanskrit, this is a really good opportunity. That being said, it is a very difficult language that requires lots of dedicated time and attention to detail so definitely be aware of that going on — you’re going to learn a lot, but it won’t be easy!!
Excellent course. Requires hard work and a passion to progress, but if you want to learn Sanskrit, you're in the right place.
I would like to tell beginner students that Sanskrit will be a difficult course and takes time out of class to actually learn. The tests will actually require you to at least put in the effort to study.
