What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is great! Although it is listed as a graduate–level class, the material is very accessible to upper–level undergraduate STEM concentrators. I would particularly say that any senior studying physics is very qualified to take the course. The material is also very engaging. The course covers in great physical detail the processes that govern climate, weather, atmospheres, and oceans on Earth. This course is definitely a must for anyone considering graduate school in Earth/Climate Science, especially from a physics perspective. Professor Kuang is extremely knowledgeable, does a great job of showing real–world examples for all of the topics covered, and overall generates lots of enthusiasm for the material. I would highly recommend this class!
This class is hard, but rewarding in knowledge. Don't be afraid to ask for help from the instructor or TA and the content will become much clearer.
I would recommend taking this class if you are interested in learning about climate!
Zhiming is very knowledgeable about the material and enjoys discussing it with his students. He encourages a conversational class style, so take advantage of that to ask questions and make observations. It makes for a much more fun class.
I thought that the small class size really made the class super engaging, as I was able to ask Zhiming questions comfortably during the flow of the class. I thought the content was very interesting and explained in a very organic way. It was a very interesting, yet challenging class that did not require too much of my time.
