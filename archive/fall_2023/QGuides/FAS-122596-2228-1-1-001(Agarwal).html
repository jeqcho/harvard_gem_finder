Comments
Amazing course!
It is an interesting and accessible way to get a breadth of knowledge on a variety of diseases from numerous standpoints.
This is a great course if you're interested in biology and medicine! Not too much work, but you get a lot out of it. Good speakers and papers.
I would tell students to make an effort to truly read the assigned publications and participate in the in–class discussion! I found the discourse throughout the class to be the most enriching aspect of the class.
To get the most out of this course, read the papers and reviews before class and prepare questions for the speakers in advance
This class is a great grad school class. The amount you learn depends on the amount of work and attention you put into it.
This is such a great class!!!!
This class is amazing as someone who is passionate about medicine, either pre–med or from a research standpoint.
As an undergraduate, I definitely was out of my depth several times in a class geared more towards graduate students, but the course does a good job of bridging gaps in knowledge as long as you're passionate about the subject material and willing to be diligent with reading the papers.
I would recommend this course wholeheartedly for graduate students. I was impressed by the line–up of lectures and just how much I learned over the course of the semester. If you are curious about translational research, or the role of a physician scientist, this class is for you. I observed that sometimes undergraduate students maybe were not as ready for some of the density of the papers, but they still came up with great questions and weren't held back by this!
