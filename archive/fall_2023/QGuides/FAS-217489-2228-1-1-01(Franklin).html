TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! You learn SO much about immunology, and it's fascinating! Prof Franklin is fantastic––she is such a great lecturer who explains extremely complicated concepts very clearly. This class provides a strong foundation in immunology and teaches very interesting applications. She and the teaching staff are super approachable and knowledgable. You don't have to memorize any complicated mechanisms, so you get to focus on the big picture.
great class – great mix of technical concepts from immunology with a really fun final project! Probably my favorite class I have taken thus far at Harvard; Ruth is absolutely amazing and you should definitely take it if it is offered again!
This is a great class! Professor Franklin is amazing, course content is well structured, and no assignments are ever too stressful.
If you have any interest in human medicine or disease or biology at all, take this class. The immune system play a role in way more than just host defense, and I didn't realize just how many roles it does have until I took this class. Super cool! And the teaching staff is so much fun. The workload is relatively light and definitely manageable, and there are no exams, so no boring rote memorization, yay! Time is spent on much more interesting things like journal clubs and reading relevant science communication pieces for discussion in section. And Professor Franklin is so cool!
This class rocks! If you're genuinely interested in learning about the immune system, this is definitely the class for you. Sometimes STEM (especially biology classes) get super jargon–y and you have to spend hours memorizing pathways and molecule names, but this class truly emphasizes understanding and grappling with the bigger picture so that you have a more foundational understanding of the immune system and its many functions. Pacing of the course is great, PSETs are super fair, and the final project is really fun. Teaching staff is super passionate about the material, and they make themselves available always. Can't say enough good things.
Great class with great assignments if you are interested in the immune system and have no background. The beginning might feel slow if you already have an immune system background, and it might feel fast if you have none. Prof. Franklin is great.
This class has no exams which makes it veryyyyy stress–free! However, the lectures are very dense and takes a lot of effort to follow. The psets (there are 3) are also frustrating because by the time you get to a Pset, you have most likely forgotten a lot of the material that is on it. The final project is super fun. The teaching staff is amazing and really wants you to learn though, which makes up for the lecture structure. I would definitely recommend this class over the other immunology introductory class.
This is a great advanced SCRB course. The material is advanced, but the class format is chill. Tests are replaced with three challenging problem sets and some journal clubs. I highly, highly recommend downloading or buying the textbook and using it on all of your assignments. It will offer much more depth than lectures.
Take it I would recommend
