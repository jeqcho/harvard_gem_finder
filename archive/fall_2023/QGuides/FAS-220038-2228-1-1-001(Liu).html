This was a great course, if you want to practice your language skills while also being exposed to Chinese philosophy, I would highly recommend. The workload is manageable, the course staff is really friendly and wants to see you succeed. I did not take this course along with the Gened though, and I would probably advise against that to be quite honest.
Take this class!! Honestly it may seem intimidating at first (especially looking at the original language of the texts) but the teaching staff works with you and walks you through it and you will honestly learn so much. You're grasp of more formal Chinese will increase significantly and it will give you much more confidence in being able to speak on more serious issues in Chinese (but also honestly English too since I really do believe the class help me think about and understand modern issues better in general).
Take it! The course staff is super nice, and there to help you in any way that they can. It may be a bit tough at times if you haven't had a crazy amount of exposure to Chinese language outside of a language classroom, but it's still super rewarding and crazy fun.
Course content is hard! Especially at the beginning. I took 123xb before this and I think i was a little underprepared for the rigor of the vocab. Also, I'd recommend taking the gened at the same time, otherwise it can be hard to keep up with and understand content.
If you want a quick introduction into Chinese philosophy, then this is it. Unfortunately, if you want anything deeper, this class will likely not suffice.

This class is quite burdened by its Gened counterpart, which is fast, sensational, and shallow. There are some nice, more unbiased portrayals of the philosophy presented in the Chinese portion (which I found quite more valuable), but as it stands it's not contentful enough to make too much of a difference.

Remember that this is also a Chinese class – for better or for worse. There will be an emphasis on genuinely learning Chinese. But this may take away from how much philosophy you will learn.

It's a fun and quick class, and the texts are wonderful. But don't expect anything deep.
Take this class! It's more flexible than the normal Chinese classes since lecture is only once a week. The teaching staff is phenomenal –– they're helpful, approachable, and very enthusiastic about the course. They made my experience wonderful and motivated me to continue in Chinese at Harvard. You learn a lot about Chinese philosophy, but the course is created in a way that allows you to read material about modern Chinese culture as well. You also learn a great deal of vocab through the readings. If you're choosing between a traditional Chinese class and this give 136 a shot! Highly, highly recommend.
Take this class! It's a lot of fun, all the Professor Liu and the rest of the teaching staff are all so engaging and responsive. While the classical Chinese can be tough, I learned a lot especially about Chinese culture (and how philosophers apply to present day thought) and was offered many opportunities to practice my Mandarin. I thought it linked to the concurrent GenEd course pretty well too.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! The content is super interesting and you learn a lot of relevant vocabulary. The vocabulary quizzes were difficult but there are many opportunities to get extra credit!
Do the readings thoroughly and sign up early for presentations. If you also need the requirement, take the gened course with it because you'll basically be taking the same course in two languages so it's a two–for–one deal
Would recommend, especially if you're interested in Chinese philosophy or Classical Chinese! The instructors are friendly and very supportive.
