What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are a first year graduate student in the EPS department (in general) or a CCB student (specifically pursuing an atmospheric chemistry Ph.D.) – this course is for you. The problem sets are what you will spend majority of your time on in the current version of this course. The best part is that there are ZERO exams. No midterms and the final exam is a powerpoint presentation on a course topic of your choice. Out of the four graduate leveled course I have taken, this was my favorite in addition to being very relevant to my PhD studies.
EPSCI 200 is a great class to take! Anyone who might need basic or slightly in depth understanding of atmospheric chemistry should take it. Note it is NOT a climate and climate change class, which some people seem to think when they sign up.
Take it!
The class is detailed, relevant, and fun. Steve is an especially dynamic lecturer and supplemented the class material with his own personal expertise on different topics, field campaigns, and environmental policies. I highly recommend this class.
