Comments
If you want to learn about contemporary Central Asian politics through the perspectives of multiple countries, this is the course for you.
This course is great if you are looking to learn more about how Central Asia fits into the politics and interests of surrounding countries/great powers. While there wasn't a lot of feedback on the assignments, the readings are generally interesting and pertinent to that week's discussion topic. Professor Kassenova knows an amazing amount about the region and is willing to take questions during every class.
This is a good class that I wish I took earlier, it got me very interested in the REECA program
This is a GREAT class to take if you are really interested in the politics of Central Asia. Even if you aren't specifically interested in the political aspect, it is still a class that you would be able to walk away from having learned A LOT about the region. Professor Kassenova is a true expert on the region and the guest speakers are wonderful. The class might feel disorganized a lot of the time though.
This course is an excellent introduction to Central Asia in a geopolitical context. Prof. Nargis is very chill, friendly, and approachable; she is always willing to help, and is very receptive to student feedback. The readings and other course materials were also pretty engaging, but sometimes lectures were characterized more by dull chronological rehashings of historical facts, rather than analytical insights into diplomatic strategy or foreign policy. And while Prof. Nargis is a generous and fair grader, prepare well for the midterm; she can still grade harshly. As long as you go to class, turn in assignments on time, and show that you are trying your best, you will be fine (I hope).
