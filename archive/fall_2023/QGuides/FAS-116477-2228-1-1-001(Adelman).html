Key class for graduate school and to begin thinking about experimental design.
This is a great class for anyone looking to (1) learn how to use a toolbox of experimental techniques to test hypotheses, (2) understand how wet lab scientists think, and the different ways they interrogate a system (biochemical, mechanistic, etc.) and (3) get an overview of common experimental techniques that any biologist will come across regardless of their specific area of expertise.

It's definitely accessible to computational scientists so long as they understand the basic biology of the central dogma.

It's not a class that will emphasize rigour or novelty in experimental design.
This class is an excellent introduction to molecular biology and the various tools available in the field. The recorded and live lecture session as well as the small group discussions and sections with the TAs make it easy to ask questions and give students different contexts to learn the material.
To gain the most out of this course, it's important to watch lectures closely, so that you can fully participate and contribute in live session.
