It is a wonderful class. However, the class is a commitment – one that requires you to do the reading, write essays, and come to class prepared with thoughtful comments. If you are looking for a professor to meet you where you are at as a student, then Dr. Fisher is perfect.
If you like literature, like to think, and like to write, take this course. It was the most academically exciting two hours of my week every week, and what you put into the essays you will get back in Professor Fisher's feedback. That said, if you dislike reading, this course is not for you and will be a waste of your time. There is no point in taking a course on Ulysses if you are not planning on reading it.
This seminar is an excellent first–semester course to take! The students in this class are incredibly smart and insightful, and Professor Fisher is very kind and supportive; the overall classroom environment is friendly. Do be warned, Ulysses is really hard –– it's a struggle to get through it, even with the help of your peers and your professor and UlyssesGuide.com. Also, don't be intimidated by this class if you aren't a Humanities person! I'm a STEM major who wanted to take this class to diversify my course load. Even though I had less background in reading literature than my peers, I still learned a lot from everyone and enjoyed all the discussions. In fact, I would especially recommend this class if you're not 100% an English person but enjoy literature, since the classroom environment is so nice and the assignments are low–stakes. Highly recommend you seize this amazing opportunity!
I loved this course! It was definitely one of the best classes I have ever taken––up at the top of the list with my very favorites. Professor Fisher was eloquent, intelligent, brilliant, and clearly knew the content better than I imagine any other individual could; the class was stimulating, exciting, and enlightening, and the first place at Harvard that I really experienced the brilliance of Harvard.

I literally walked in, and felt like I was at home. The brilliance of the people and the professor around me hit me with an exciting wave, and I had more fun in conversation than I'd had the past day of orientation activities.

The discussions were the best I've ever had, and I loved listening to Professor Fisher speak.

The one thing I will say is that we did have to write weekly response essays, which were a bit grueling and difficult to keep up with; every weekend, I'd end up having to stay in for a good portion of the time to nail out the reading and essay.

All in all, it was a ton a work, but one of the best course I'd ever taken. Choose the rest of your classes wisely when you take this course, love literature –– and this will be a favorite for you, too. I would HIGHLY recommend this course, apart from my caution on the workload.
Phillip Fisher is one of the BEST TEACHERS I have ever had. TAKE THIS CLASS. He takes the time to read through each student's essays, provides specific feedback (alongside his own analysis), and even mentions it during class discussions. His insights about Ulysses and Hamlet are thoughtful, thorough, and brilliant––I always find myself trying to write down everything he says. He's also kind and caring. Granted, reading Ulysses is difficult––but that experience really brings the class together. If you take "Ulysses and Hamlet," you must buy into the seminar experience by doing the all of the readings and participating in class. Yes, this seminar has a lot of work (typically two chapters of the text and a short response paper per week), but that's part of what makes it so special. If you like English or literary analysis at all, this class is worthwhile.

I genuinely think it will be hard to find another class that matches the amazing experience I had in this one. Take it, take it, take it.
Be willing to commit to the reading! It's what makes the discussions in class possible. Also, listen attentively to your classmates' points in class, since sincerely thinking about and responding to them makes the discussion much more interesting.
I imagine that the remainder of my Harvard journey will confirm my hunch that Professor Fisher must be one of the best professors on this campus. He is incredibly insightful, articulate, and most of all passionate about what hes teaching. (If he needed to, Im sure he could talk about Ulysses for the entire two–hour class without hesitation, repetition, or deviation.) His feedback on response papers (500–600 words each week) is specific and personal, and hes just overall a lovely person.

Ulysses is undeniably a great work of literature. But be warned: it is a very difficult read. Its dense, long, and at times downright unintelligible. And because of the weekly response paper, you must do (at least some of) the reading. So if thats not your cup of tea, I would recommend taking another seminar. But if you are a literature buff and have the time and bandwidth to fully engage with the text, then theres no better person to help you navigate it than Professor Fisher.
This is an extremely insightful class. You will learn about two extremely important works of literature in a comfortable, yet in–depth, way. You will get out of this class what you put you can coast if you want, but I strongly suggest that you put serious effort into your essays and do the assigned readings so that you can appreciate the works of literature for what they were.
