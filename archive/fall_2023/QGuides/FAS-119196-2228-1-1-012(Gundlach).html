Multivariable is tough but 21a makes it accessible and feasible. Assignments are fair and lecturers are incredible. Team–based learning is awesome. Freshman–heavy, but not solely freshman. No one really takes for fun, but it can be if you engage and try.
A very well–structured course that you will do well in if you put in the time, but it does add up to a lot of time over the course of a week
This math course is probably one of the better–structured and better–organized math courses you will find at Harvard. The course materials are ample: from worksheets and solutions to online readings to extra practice and practice exams, you'll be well covered. The practice exams prepare you very well for the exam, and the course study schedules are reasonable and meant to help you succeed.

There are also plenty of opportunities to get the help that you need. MQC is highly collaborative and a great place to get PSET help, but it is very very crowded and not necessarily an enjoyable experience. Office hours give much better one–on–one help. There aren't really bad office hours to go to either... everyone is super helpful and good at explaining the material. Feedback is very thorough even for exams, so you'll always know exactly what you did wrong!

Have a group of friends that do the PSET around the same time as you and don't be afraid to ask for help. This is one of those courses where as long as you put in the work, you will see good results. There is a lot of work – 3 PSETs a week is no joke, but nothing is unmanageable or even unreasonable. You also have the opportunity to learn extra late days... use them!!
21a is a solid class, grading is fair, and you will learn the multi you will need for the future. It meets four times a week (3 sections, 1 workshop) and has three psets a week, but if you show up to section and stay on top of the homework it is quite manageable. The sections feel like they waste 30 minuets and the workshop is totally useless, but the commitment here is not overbearing. If you did well in AP Calc BC or a similar course, you are definitely prepared to take this class. Overall, I felt it was a great introduction to multivariable calculus.
If you don't have a strong math background you will struggle in this course. There is a lot of content and a lot to take into account when solving a lot of these problems, and it's hard to take into account things you are learning for the first time/don't already have intuition for. However there are some good TFs who are willing to sit down with you and make sure you really conceptually understand the material. Just dedicate the time.
This course is pretty much exactly what it advertises on the packaging, introductory multivariable calculus. It's very straightforward, and if you just took calculus in high school without any intro to multi like I did, you'll do fine. 21a feels very much like a high school math class in terms of style, where you go into class 3 times a week, sit down, learn new concepts, do problems related to those concepts, and repeat. There are 3 PSETs a week, and you generally have 1–2 days to do them so it's important to stay on top of the deadlines, but honestly, I had roommates in other similar math courses with only 1 PSET a week and I wouldn't say I spent more time per week on homework than they did. Going to office hours and/or the MQC or having a group of people you can work also definitely helps, but I wouldn't call it necessary to get by. Take this if you like math, need it for a concentration requirement, or anything else, it's not bad.
Definitely go to the Math Question Center if you've got the time. A study group is also essential.
3 PSETs are rough, but it's definitely a fair course.
Wouldn't take it for fun, but if you have to take it, make sure you're ready for a lot of Psets
Well taught with good support, has a lot of psets, but is definitely manageable and you come away with a lot
This is a very reasonable math class where you will learn multivariable concepts and formulas well. For many people with a solid single–variable calculus background, it will probably not be too challenging. You may be able to pick up some of the underlying derivations (although this isn't necessary to do well in the class), but overall, this class does not explore proofs or higher–level reasoning. The three psets a weekthough frequent and occasionally tediousare never overly time–consuming; They help establish a consistent schedule and even out the weekly workload. Lastly, whether office hours or the MQC or pset groups (try to find some people to work with early on!), resources for help are readily available.
Be wary if it is 3 psets a week (budget more time than you are expecting and get ready for the inconvenience to your schedule), otherwise interesting content that is definitely worth learning!
Math 21a is such a great math class. It is taught in small sections and you learn through doing math, not listening to lectures. They really emphasize understanding the concepts and being able to apply them, which helps the information stick. The problem sets seem daunting (three a week!) but they are actually very manageable and it helps that they are regular because it teaches you to understand new concepts quickly. Overall this course was a fantastic introduction to multivariable calculus and I would strongly recommend it!!
