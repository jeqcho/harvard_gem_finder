I really recommend taking this course! This semester was the first time the course was being taught, but it far surpassed what I had expected. This course is everything you would imagine an environmental science and engineering class to be: we learned a lot of science, we learned about what actually goes into solving environmental issues, we talked about the limitations of current science and engineering–based solutions and considered ways to improve them, we had labs and field trips that combined science and engineering, we discussed public perception vs attitudes held by scientists/engineers vs implementation vs legal/economic complications, etc. It was a perfect combination of quantitative and qualitative course content with a hands–on lab component. We learned a lot, and Bryan is a really good teacher so it didn't feel like too much. And if there was something that you found to be less interesting, we would move on quickly enough. In addition, the course was very manageable as there wasn't too much work, and the work that we did have paired nicely with what we had been learning about. The final project was super open ended (although Bryan might change that in future years) so it's a good idea to start thinking about it or brainstorming in advance.
This is a great class. If you're an ESE or EPS student, definitely take it before you graduate. Bryan is an INCREDIBLE professor, he really cares about his students and is a pretty chill guy. The lectures are put together incredibly well, any time a student asked a question it would be directly answered on the next slide, like Bryan is a mind reader or something. Seriously, the material is really interesting and I would actually look forward to coming to class every day.
