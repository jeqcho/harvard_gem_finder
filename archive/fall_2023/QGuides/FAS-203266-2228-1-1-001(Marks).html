What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is one of the best classes at Harvard. Take it if you get the opportunity. The time commitment and course load is extremely reasonable. The assignments are always interesting and relevant, and are most of the time something you'd do for fun anyways. The professor (Sam Marks) is incredibly engaged and engaging. He's very knowledgable and cares deeply about your craft.

I learned so much in such a short period of time. I also made a bunch of new creative friends through the class. I'm so glad I took it.
Take this class! It's a great way to get to know the technical aspects of writing a pilot and is a fantastic opportunity to write and produce your own work.
This is a helpful class to learn how to write a pilot and especially worldbuild on a larger scale. Since planning out a season of TV takes a lot more time than other mediums, I would say that in order to get as much out of the class as possible, you should work on assignments early and watch a lot of TV in addition to what's assigned to get inspiration and get a sense of pilot structure. I don't see a lot of courses on how to write for TV so I'd definitely recommend it; even if you've never written a pilot before (like me!) you should definitely apply.
Wonderful class, 100% apply to it and take it if you get in. More than worth the time.
This class is a great introduction to TV writing for any interested students. I really liked the workshop format of the class. I really learned a lot from discussions with my peers in the course. However, I found the pacing to be pretty slow, and wished that each student had more opportunities to present their work.
This class is super accessible to anyone who is interested in writing scripts, even without background knowledge. However, you might need to put in a little extra effort to keep yourself organized since the class will not do that for you.
