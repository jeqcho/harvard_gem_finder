What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are placed into Korean BX, take it. As a senior who never considered taking a language at Harvard, my biggest regret is not having taken this course earlier because there are very few classes that will teach you material that you will carry with you beyond graduation.
The course is a lot of work and fast–paced, but rewarding. If you are willing to put in the work and keep yourself accountable, you will be able to see noticeable improvement in your Korean.
This is a great class for any beginners with Korean heritage. As someone who came into the class with virtually no speaking experience (and thus lots of trepidation), I now feel much more comfortable with the language! The course was also genuinely lots of fun. At least half the battle is getting over worries about making mistakes, since that's how all of us learned throughout the semester. (One practical note: Do skim the textbook once a week or so. It helps with the fast–pace of the class.)
Bx is a fun experience and you'll unlearn your improper speaking/writing/grammar habits and learn the proper fundamentals. Class feels like a community, Lee SSN is funny and down to earth. The pace is very fast and there's a lot of weekly work, make sure to keep up. But it's not anything unfair or too difficult. There are weekly HW packets, dictation and vocabulary quizzes, speaking assessments during section, and 2 short essays that
you present on, a final essay/presentation, and a final exam.
Definitely take this! It's an incredible intensive introduction to the language. Teacher Lee is so nice and supportive. She has high expectations for the class, but that really pushes you to grow. She cares a lot about her students. She even did a letter–writing exercise where we sent our families letters in Korean, and it was honestly so sweet and thoughtful of her to encourage us to apply our learning.
