This course brought us to the frontier of research in quantum information. This is pretty literal: some papers that we dove into in the presentations were published only a couple weeks before their presentation in the last week of class, and I went to some talks where the speakers presented substantial portions of work that I had reviewed in papers for our presentations. This course was very helpful and inspiring to me. Thank you to Professor Lukin, Can, and Nishad!
It is a must if their research is anyhow connected to modern quantum optics.
This course is truly amazing course that you can develop both understanding of fundamentals in quantum information but also the skills on reading and presenting cutting–edge research papers. I strongly recommend this course to anyone (even though their primary interest in physics is not in quantum information science) who wants to develop these important skills to take this course!
This class teaches you a broad and pretty detailed overview of quantum technologies. It is a great class if you want to improve your paper reading skills, presentation skills, and learn more about some quantum technologies you might never otherwise work with. This class has a considerable amount of work, and it definitely requires a bit more background than an intro to QI course such as Phys 160. Prof. Lukin's effort in listening to everyone's presentation closely is also admirable.
It's an excellent, very unique course! Take it!
Loved this class. It taught me so much and helped me get a solid foundation on so many topics I had only heard about previously. The homeworks and presentations are interesting and fair, and the lack of tests is a big plus. 10/10 would recommend
It's a great opportunity to build comradeship during the course.
The contents about quantum information theory, entanglement, and various implementations of quantum computing are all very interesting. However, one need to work through the sup–optimal lecture (it is at times disorganized, slides feel like they are hastily put together, and Misha goes through slides materials a bit too quickly sometimes) in order to absorb the contents. The problem sets are very helpful in making sure one understands the materials.
A big portion of the course (around half of all lectures) is student presentation; this is a double–edged sword depending on how you prepare your own presentation and how well the other presentations are prepared.
This class is good to get a broad sense of modern experimental quantum information fronts. Misha does a good job providing a structure in which to think of the field, and the presentations are good practice in clearly presenting material that might not be from your specific sub field, as well as collaborating with a large group of people (5–6 people/presentation on average). The homeworks were reasonable and directly related to the presentations, although there were a few instances when they were quite long and tedious. Would overall recommend!
It is a very good class to get exposure to key ideas of quantum information science. You will definitely get a great deal out of it coming in with a strong quantum mechanics and atomic physics background. I now have the tools to teach myself about QIS research independently, which I did not before.
Take this class! Misha is a wonderful professor!!
difficult to do course alone. many students know each other already from working in one of the qc groups.
