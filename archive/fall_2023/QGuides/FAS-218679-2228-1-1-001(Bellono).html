This class is super chill. There is no real assigned work besides 2 presentations and a 2 page proposal and a take home test in the beginning. The material can be pretty dry; it is all about ion channels and that's it. Easy to slack, so you get what you put in.
Take this class for a chill way to meet an upper–level MCB requirement. Try to go to lecture and ask questions as participation is a big part of your grade. Reach out to course staff early about the final proposal!
It was somewhat difficult for someone with no college science experience whatsoever. But a fun way to branch out into new subjects at Harvard.
Take it
I would highly recommend that you take Prof. Bellono's course. Really great structure. A wonderful opportunity to learn about the diversity and complexities of ion channels, which serve such fundamental biological functions, without undue stress to think or do a certain way when it comes to assignments. Exceptionally kind and understanding course staff. Assignments are structured to keep you engaged throughout the semester with a few larger assignments that open up the opportunity for you to pursue specific areas that pique your interest. Exciting guest speakers interspersed throughout the course. If you have the chance, I think you would enjoy this course.

this class will give a broad introduction to ion channels and the work being done in the field.
I highly recommend this course! Professor Bellono is an incredible educator! This course will teach you about ion channels and cover an incredible number of applications. Beyond explaining how ion channels are used in cells, Professor Bellono will teach you the techniques and give you the tools to understand research on ion channels and study ion channels yourself! To this end, the final project is to create a research proposal in which you have the opportunity to apply all of the incredible techniques which are covered throughout the semester. Professor Bellono is extremely helpful and makes these complex systems understandable. The final project will also allow you to study how ion channels relate to your own interests. For example, I am very interested in bones and Professor Bellono helped me think through how to study ion channels relevant to bone biology. This course is a must–take for anyone interested in experimental biology! Also, you will get to see some amazing creatures in Professor Bellono's lab from octopuses, to sea robins, to piranhas, and even sea anemones. The lectures are extremely interesting and you will learn about so many different topics in this course! I cannot recommend this course enough!
If you're looking for a chill way to learn about cell physiology, you should take this course!!
Absolute gem of a MCB course. Would highly recommend to anyone looking to lighten up their semester while still fulfilling their concentration requirement
