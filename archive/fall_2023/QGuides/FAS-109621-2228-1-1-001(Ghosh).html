I would highly recommend this class to anyone who is interested in modern Chinese history. It was so interesting to take the course while following news in China which drew intriguing parallels. The lectures are quite dense but in a good way. The TF James is so nice and encouraging and makes the sections fun and inclusive. One thing to improve is the lecture slides, which could make revision easier if had more information written on it (currently it’s mostly pictures of maps or graphs). This is not a very difficult course, but you certainly learn a lot from it!

But other than that, still a really fun and easy course to get that requirement done if you need to! And also an interesting class to take in general, I definitely didn't regret taking it even if it was a mistake. Dr. Ghosh really knows his stuff and his lectures are really engaging, and you'll learn a lot of cool stuff about a country that had such a diverse history during the 20th century. The class is not too difficult, I found going to lecture generally adequate to keep up.

James and Professor Ghosh are really really amazing teachers and if you have any interest in the class content you will enjoy the learning you'll do.
This course is the greatest! Honestly, it is one of the best classes I have taken in Harvard. I learned so much about Chinese history (with basically no background), and Professor Ghosh and James are some of the most engaging, passionate, talented educators I've ever seen. I didn't find this course especially challenging, and it doesn't require much time investment outside of classes (readings are pretty short and there are no reading responses), but you get incredible bang for your buck in terms of knowledge gained. If you have the chance to take this class, you really should.

The lectures in this course require attendance unless you are sick because you can only access the recording if the professor gives you access to the recording. Additionally while the slides are posted, they contain almost no lecture content and no other lecture materials are posted. Furthermore, the lectures are not on the readings so the readings do not cover lecture material you missed. Therefore, if you didn't take notes on something or remember it, it is very hard to recover that information in this class.
Take this course! Ghosh is an amazing lecturer, the way he presents China is nuanced and engaging. The course is really made by James, the TF, who you will spend the most time interacting with. He is so supportive and helpful, really took the stress out of everything and lets you learn the course material. Tests were VERY fair, my biggest piece of advice is to choose which secondary sources seem like they’d be helpful for writing short essays each week and do those, if you’re crunched for time, you definitely don’t need to do all the readings to get a lot out of this course but you do need the readings for the midterm and final
