What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a very demanding course (not possible to overstate that part), BUT if you want to do cell culture or bioengineering or anything like that then this is definitely the course to take. You will get such significant experience working with cells, and likely learn more applicable skills than pretty much any other course at Harvard. Professor Parker also has some good life / career advice and he is a great resource.
This class is difficult –– but it will all be alright in the end. You are disciplined many times but that's just how he is. Eventually you'll learn the ways of a good scientist and be thankful, because he does care after all. Science is just hard and things will go wrong, but again –– you'll be alright at the end
This year there were fewer students so everyone gets more time and resources in the lab. That means you can learn a lot but also need to put significantly more time into the work. You get want you paid for and the outcome is rewarding. Professor Parker, Dr. Hancock, TFs (Albert for this semester), and other visiting instructors and guests present an absolutely worthwhile experience if bioengineering hands–on work is something you seek.
