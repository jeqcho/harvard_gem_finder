A really cool course! Came into this having zero prior linguistics experience (wasn't even sure what it was). A bit more math–y than I expected; there's a good amount of calculus–type notation and very basic proof writing if you're into that. Also a good amount of philosophy thrown in there. I personally loved how it showed me the computational side of language and at times this course blew my mind. Also, super laidback grading, and lots of materials to help you succeed on assignments. Would recommend.

Absolutely take this class if Professor Chierchia teaches it again; he is incredible.
This class is definitely hard if you don't know anything about linguistics. Don't take this class if you don't have much experience with linguistics/semantics or CS as you won't be able to make connections between the fields and you will struggle to understand 90% of the material. The PSets will still be doable, but they will be more challenging. The good thing about this course is that the class slides are very helpful to do the Psets.
This a great class for anyone interested in linguistics/logic. Super nice professor and TF, Professor Chierchia really cares about everyone in class and is very approachable. The workload is minimum.
Semantics allows to us to think of language differently, and much more analytically. Be prepared to loosen your beliefs on the structure of language and explore how certain structures across languages manifest in our mental semantics. It's very interesting and sometimes draining, but you have a lot of support. Above all, I would recommend having someone to work with on psets so that you can struggle together, and attend section!
The course is highly dependent on the professor. Prof Chierchia is a joy and very enthusiastic about the material, and Hayley was an incredible TF. The problem sets can be tricky, but if you start them early so you can bring questions to section and/or office hours they're very manageable. The overall workload was pretty chill, just four problem sets and a take–home final. If you have any familiarity or experience with logic (or better, predicate calculus) the basics will be easier, and if you don't then it might take a while for things to make sense. There's a lot of notation, but you can take a lot of what you need directly or near–directly from the class notes.

The problems were a blast to work through. They had so many neat nuances and challenged me in the healthiest way possible. Professor Chierchia is an absolute gem –– dynamic, caring, approachable, self–aware, patient, and warm. And yes, the homework is tough at times, but there are SO many resources at one's disposal: office hours, class notes (with solutions), section notes (with solutions), etc. Also, the TF and the professor give highly useful feedback on the homework.

Both the professor and the TF are VERY knowledgeable. Getting to ask them questions was always a delight.

This class is also super conceptually rich; you'll get to engage in some very cool debates!

Though all that aside, Professor Chierchia is one of the most special people I've met at Harvard; simply getting to know him makes the course so valuable to take.
