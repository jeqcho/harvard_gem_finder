TAKE THIS CLASS. Best class I’ve taken at Harvard hands down. Just take it.
This class is a great way to get an overview of what is happening in the music, health and education sphere. If you are interested in any of these topics, I highly recommend. It is a fun class with an awesome professor and TF.
I would highly recommend MBB 980P! The class community was outstanding and hearing from the guest lecturers every week was so valuable.
This course is super fun and really fascinating! The seminar formatting makes it a lot easier to get to know one another in the class, and discussions are always really interesting to listen to and participate in. Regarding workload, there are two relatively informal presentations and a final paper, in addition to short weekly discussions, so it's definitely very manageable. All of the guest speakers (and the teaching staff) are truly wonderful as well! If you have any interest in music, science, and/or healthcare, I'd definitely recommend this course :)
This is a fantastic class for any musician who is premed (or just interested in healthcare). Dr. Wong and Ayelet are knowledgable, kind, and warm, and everyone in the class brings so much diverse musical talent. Among the best classes (if not the best) I have taken at Harvard. Workload is generally light (and readings are interesting) and the guest speakers are all excellent. Highly recommend.
Actually practicing music (specifically Western classical music) is not necessary, but the class catered more towards people who have had some sort of formal musical background (dance was also common). The class was so fun, and you will get the most out of it through participating and actively listening to guest lectures and student presentations.
This was genuinely one of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard so far. Not only were Dr. Wong and Ayelet amazing instructors, but the format of discussion + guest lecturers meant that every class was interesting and relatively fast–paced. I've also come away with a lot of inspiration for future research projects in music/neuroscience. This class really helps with that if that's of interest to anyone pursuing a thesis/looking for a lab in general.
This is a wonderful class for anyone who loves music.
This is hands down my favorite class I have taken at Harvard. Workload is very manageable – weekly readings and discussion posts and a final paper. Dr. Wong and Ayelet were both amazing. I loved hearing different perspectives from weekly guest speakers and how music affects the brain. Would highly recommend this course.
This course is a therapy course in and of itself in a way. A lot of us who came into the course came into the course with prior relationships with music, but admittedly, not the most healthy nor productive one. We came in with a love for music, but also with a lot of questions as well, about exactly how music will continue to play a role in our lives moving forward. I'm so glad I took this course. There is still a lot more unlearning and learning to be done, but this course was the turning point for that. If music means a lot to you and you care not only about realizing and re–realizing how much it adds to your life, but if you want to be inspired to work towards pushing the boundaries of access, the survival of music as a field in an increasingly commercialized, technological world, if you care about these questions and issues, you have to take this course.
