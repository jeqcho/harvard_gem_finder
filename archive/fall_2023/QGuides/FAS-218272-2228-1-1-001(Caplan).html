What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Hands down my favorite class that I've taken here at Harvard. I learned soo much from this course and genuinely enjoyed every minute of it. It allowed me to think about music and entertainment in entirely new ways and the assignments are actually fun! There's always a creative option. I will say that a lot of the success of the class is contingent on whether the people in your section are engaged, and I would say I feel very lucky in that regard. It was one of the few sections I've been in where almost everyone is eagerly participating because they have so much to say.
I really enjoyed this class! The readings are interesting and include books, movies, journalism, songs, and academic works. I also loved that each student did a creative presentation because I think it helped us get to know each other better.
This class is SO good. Lucy is amazing at guiding discussions and the course material manages to cover a huge range of regions, genres, and theory without feeling spread thin at all. Take it take it take it.
This was one of my favorite classes that I have taken at Harvard. It provides a perfect intersection between music and activism. It helps you understand the music around you and its impact on politics and more. It is a decent amount of work and requires you to come prepared with readings; however, it will change the way you think about music and the world around you.
Take advantage of the available office hours!! Although this course is a seminar, it would be very beneficial to get to know Professor Caplan because she is happy to help with any troubles you are having with assignments or to share ideas.
