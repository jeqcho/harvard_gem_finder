What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The class is very good and provides necessary knowledge about introductory calculus, which may be needed for various concentrations. The teachers and CAs are very attentive, the class format is fun, and it is considerably possible to get a very high grade just by attending the classes and doing the assignments with dedication.
The psets in this class are not made to be worked on individually. The MQC is a great place to solve the problems together and ask questions on topics you might not have understood well. It is a very collaborative space that is also helpful. Making sure you fully understand the topics from section and doing practice are the best ways to do well in the class.
Do not assume that you can slack off in this class just because you've taken Calculus before. Also, don't be afraid to ask for help. Office hours and MQC (Math Question Center) will be your best friend–––you can literally get A's on all PSETs if you utilize these precious resources. Unfortunately, I didn't practice what I preach, but I at least hope someone else can benefit from my misfortune by taking that advice. Also, don't wait until the last minute to do the PSETs, you will overestimate your ability to work under the pressure of a time constraint.
I would tell future students that this class goes at a steady pace that is easy to follow. I would recommend it to anyone who is weary about their math skills and knowledge coming out of high school.
Go to office hours! It is a great way to get to know professors and to help consolidate knowledge.
You will have three psets a week so the workload takes up your time. Be prepared to make time to attend office hours and MQCs. This year we had a portfolio as a final project so please do not procrastinate. If you have a final that is an exam, the ARC is your best friend. Get a tutor and work with them on skills in calc you struggle with
Work hard to review on quizzes and don't put the class on the back burner! You get what you put into the class.
Very well–structured, enjoyable, and a good amount of challenging. There's three p–sets and quiz almost every week but it's super manageable. P–sets might take an hour or two at most, and the quizzes are very similar to p–sets and what we learn in class. This class was fun and very applicable to real life. Really made me consider a math concentration.
This class can be a good intro to calculus and it doesn't have a final exam but it's more work than you may think it will be.
