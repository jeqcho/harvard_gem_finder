PLEASE, PLEASE, PLEASE do not be afraid to approach Dr. Tucci and Dr. Matchacheep – they are the loveliest people you will ever meet! LPSA is a phenomenal introductory course to chemistry and biology, which everyone should take regardless of their background in these subjects. Coming into this class with only knowledge from AP Biology, I initially felt overwhelmed with the amount of chemistry I was learning, but with time I became accustomed to the workload and looked at science in a way I never did before. It is important to note that this course is not heavy on memorization (even though it may seem like it at times) instead, it teaches you how to apply the knowledge from class to word problems and questions you might encounter in a scientific setting.

The layout of the course consists of three lectures (MWF 9:00 am–10:15 am), a section (1 hour and 15 min long), and a lab component (3 hours max although most labs only take 1 hour). There is one PSET due each week on Monday at 9:00 am, a pre–lab quiz due on Monday at 9:00 am, and a lab report due on Friday at 9:00 am. This all may seem daunting but to be honest, the workload is VERY manageable. There are three exams and a final exam and I can't stress this enough but DO ALL THE PRACTICE EXAMS. The tests are very fair and if you do the practice exams you will know what to expect since they are of the same format. They host review sessions every week before the exam and have a multitude of help rooms you should attend.

The course for the most part is chemistry (2/3 of the course) and then switches to biology (1/3 of the course) at the end. Both Dr. Tucci and Dr. Matchacheep interchange, so one will teach a few lessons, and then the other will take over for some time.

Dr. Tucci:

–He is an enthusiastic man that makes this 9 a.m worth waking up for because he brings so much energy into the room.
–He always encourages you to shout out your questions and will take time to answer them!
– He tries his best to get to know you personally and will always greet you in the morning.
–He holds one on one meetings that you can sign up for if you have any questions that aren't answered in class or after class. These are short thirty–minute sessions but he tries his best to get through everything that confuses you.

Dr. Matchacheep:

–Many people often approach Dr. Tucci for questions, but Dr. Matchacheep is always there to answer questions as well. Both are chemists and are knowledgeable in their own domains (fun fact she also teaches organic chemistry in the summer, so if you have questions regarding chemical interactions this is your woman!)
–She will walk you through all questions you have and will always make it very interactive. She works each question step by step with you.
–She is also willing to meet with you in person to explain concepts you might have not grasped (I spent an entire hour with her reviewing problems until I finally got them)
–She walks around the lecture during learning catalytic questions, so even if you are sitting in the back you are able to ask any question you have.
–She is very kind–hearted and will always respond to your emails with kindness, so please approach her!
