This is a great class! Madhu is an excellent lecturer and very well–organized, the weekly assignments are short but very helpful for reviewing material. The psets are generally well–designed and not too strenuous, and the final project is quite reasonable and in line with the philosophy of the course. You should be wary of taking this class if you do not have a large amount of CS theory background; e.g. at least two classes at the level of CS 121/124 or grad theory.
This is a very good class; it really surveys a wide spread of information theory and its uses in theoretical CS. It moves quickly and there's basically a new topic every week.
This is an overview class on the field of information theory. The material covered in lecture is very hard, but Madhu stops for questions very often and you can tell that he's very committed to helping you understand what he's talking about. Also, there's no exams and just a final presentation, along with 4 biweekly problem sets, plus you have to write scribe notes for one lecture, so overall the workload for the class is not heavy at all. However, if you put in the effort to really pay attention and try to understand lecture, you will certainly reap the benefits.
Take this class if you have a strong interest in the material.

This course is quite challenging, and a lot more work than I expected. The problem sets, though biweekly, are very long and challenging to complete without help from office hours. It gives a rigorous overview of concepts in information theory, and Madhu's teaching is clear. I do wish I knew about how much work this class would be though.
If you want a grad level theory course where you learn a great deal, this is the class. It's an old school course (chalkboard lectures and problem sets) but Madhu is an excellent teacher with incredible knowledge of the field. Class encourages questions, so even if the material is tough, you can get through it. This is a challenging course and the problem sets are definitely not easy and require significant time and effort. But they are very rewarding and if you have a good group of peers to collaborate with, it's very doable and enjoyable. And you definitely will come away with an understanding of info theory from this course.
This class starts out quite fun, as you begin seeing a lot of the cool things entropy can do. I think towards the middle/late middle arguments felt a bit tedious and homeworks a bit handwavy (the middle portions on communication complexity, and passing through various lower bounds). I think I was excited again at the end, the quantum information theory lectures and extension complexity portion tell really interesting stories about what people have done in the past, beyond just the actual theorems themselves.
This is a great class if you're interested in CS theory, and want to go deeper than CS121/124. The proofs can be a bit more abstract since they are often non–constructive, but if you enjoy that kind of stuff then you will love this class!
TAKE ANYTHING THAT MADHU TEACHES! That's it. Amazing lecturer, amazing person, just incredible. Madhu is exactly what a professor should be: extremely knowledgeable, brilliant lecturer, kind to the student, and cares deeply about educating the next generation. I admire him for being a true leader in the theory CS community, and for the amount of care that he puts into making sure that theory CS is a welcoming space for everyone. A lot of male professors will use empty words and say that they care about gender diversity; but in practice, they do nothing and continue to alienate minorities from their fields. But Madhu, unlike most of these professors, actually takes action: he makes sure everyone is comfortable in class, he encourages questions from the students, he always responds to these questions with a smile even if they are elementary, and it was so fun to go to his office hours. I always looked forward going to class and I only wished they lasted longer! I wish I could take so many more classes with Madhu –– he is such an inspiration and someone I really look up to. He always generated enthusiasm for the class material and it made me so excited about continuing to study theory CS. Thanks for everything! You really are bringing change to the next generation of computer scientists.
