You will learn a lot about human behaviors. Psychopathy is an umbrella term so there is a lot of room for investigation and finding what intrigues you the most. This class is unique and makes you really think about what you want to learn more about. For me it was the philisophical side of psychopathy. It was awesome. Coolest Professor out there and literally the funniest human being.
This course is so interesting. I liked how well the topic connects with the philosophy of responsibility and real–life situations. It definitely is a gem, and I could not recommend it enough! The assignments are really fun too!
If you are lucky enough to get into this course with Professor Fersch you should certainly take it. Professor Fersch is a student's dream in that he is reasonable, knowledgeable, entertaining, and genuinely cares about each of his students.
I recommend this course to everyone interested in psychopathy! It is a wonderful way to explore such a mysterious subject through open discussion.
This class is such a gem. Ellsworth is a great professor and will keep you laughing the whole time – even though a lot of the class is talking about intense topics.
To get the most out of this course, you should definitely bring your own interests in psychopathy/law/neuroscience to the table, as it really helps with choosing readings and project topics. However, keeping an open mind about what you'll learn also helps when engaging in class discussion. Professor Fersch fosters a very low–pressure environment, so don't be afraid to speak your mind!
Amazing class! One of the most engaging I've taken at Harvard. Professor Fersch has a vast array of knowledge to share, yet despite this is so open to hearing the ideas of other students. The class facilitates learning from one another and interacting with your peers. The students in my class were also so open and intelligent, I really enjoyed learning from them! Overall, a greatly manageable seminar to supplement your other academic endeavors!
This class was great. The topic is a really interesting one, and you get room to explore whatever aspects of psychopathy you find interesting as well as become interested in new aspects (biological, social, behavioral, legal, etc). The class is also extremely non–stressful––the work each week is reading 2–3 papers and writing a one paragraph discussion post, and then there is a short case study of a psychopath and a final paper on any topic. It's really more about the class discussions than any assessments/grades. The class discussions are usually on interesting topics, and the professor is amazing and hilarious.
Take this course!!! Professor Ellsworth is an absolute gem of a human being, and the discussions that come out of this class are so incredibly unique. Do yourself a favor and petition into this class.
This is a great course! The readings are all engaging and quick enough to get through and the weekly discussion posts are super short. Dr Fersch tells lots of stories about patients he's had which adds a great dimension to the course. Definitely would recommend!
Best professor you'll have here.
