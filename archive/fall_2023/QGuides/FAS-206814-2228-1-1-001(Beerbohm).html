What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
A wonderful seminar all around. Eric Beerbohm is a marvelous teacher and mentor throughout the seminar, and the weekly conversations were enjoyable, informative, and transformative. Reading is a tad heavy at times, though Eric recognizes the burden and makes sure to ease up over the following weeks. Assignments were very straightforward as well. Overall, highly encourage everyone to enroll in the seminar!
This class was wonderful! Eric is such a kind and thought–provoking professor and it felt like he really created a community in the class. It's a class that can be a little heady at times, but it was also accessible to most people including multiple people in the class who didn't have a very strong background in political theory. If you're interested in this class, I can't recommend it enough!
Eric is *such* a wonderful seminar leader. He's so sharp, and so generous, and does a really admirable job of synthesizing students' interests into a larger discussion. This is a graduate seminar, so you have to be ready to do a lot of reading and think on your feet, but if you're adequately prepared for that, this class is absolutely worth your time. You also get to read so much cutting–edge literature, so there are real professional development gains to be had as a grad student taking the course.
This course is a fantastic survey of many different thinkers in contemporary political theory. Professor Beerbohm does a great job creating an engaging class discussion that allows everyone to glean the insights that are most useful to their own research. This class requires significant background in political theory.
