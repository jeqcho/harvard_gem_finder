DO NOT PROCRASTINATE. Go to every single section, you will be lost if you skip class. Office hours in this class are sacred. I got almost 100s on every single PSET and understood every single PSET, plus did well on exams just by going to office hours. I had section Monday, Wednesday, and Friday and I went to every office hours offered on those specific days. Office hours get packed on Tuesday and Thursday because people procrastinate. By going to office hours 2–3 hours to finish my PSET every MWF, I avoided going late at night to Math Question Center (MQC) which also got packed. Start the PSET early and finish the day of. Do all the practice tests for exams, and redo PSETs.
Go to the MQC. At least 3 times a week.
This class is definitely a time commitment with three psets a week, but if you stay on top of your workstart working on it the day it gets assignedand make a good pset group, you'll be fine. The content is paced pretty well and there is a lot of a support with MQC.
Start your p–sets the day they're assigned so you have time to ask about any questions you may have.
The course is not structured to support you and your success. The grading mechanisms, absence of opportunities to show learning growth, and unhelpfulness of some TFs at office hours demonstrate little to no desire on the part of the staff to watch you succeed. You really have to fight for yourself and work hard on your own, and hope for the best as well as that it is not extraordinarily unfair when it comes to exam time.
If you take Math 1B definitely go to MQC and office hours! Lectures aren't always super clear and it really depends on the TF you get.
Do your psets as early as possible. They are long and often you will have questions for your peers, CAs, or TFs. The content is manageable if you seek help immediately when you are confused. The exams are difficult. Good luck!
There is not much background that can help in this course. Many people –– including me –– took this as an "easy" class, since we thought it would be a repeat of AP Calculus BC for our math requirements. I took math up to AP Calculus BC previously, scoring 5s and the highest grades possible in all of those classes; however, Math 1b was still somewhat difficult for me. The material is taught very differently, and requires you to rethink/relearn everything you've learned before. Additionally, it is a lot of work. The psets take hours of your time; this class was the biggest time sink of all my classes this semester, and it was a poor choice for freshman year 1st semester. If you need to spend time in your freshman year adjusting to the new environment, don't take Math 1b.

Overall, the format of the class is somewhat disappointing. There were no lectures; just "experiment with these problems and find the answer". The CAs and MQC saved us. I do wish they did more teaching in this class.
This was my single biggest time commitment.
The course is challenging but not overwhelming, as long as you put in the effort you will do fine.
