The readings are fascinating and carefully interconnected –– *at least* skim all the readings each week.
This is a great seminar on the U.S. criminal justice system. The readings are excellent and there is basically no busywork – all assignments build to the final paper.
Great class especially if you have any interest in American history or incarceration. Bekemeyer is a super approachable and helpful professor.
I think this is a great class if you really want to learn and be engaged in the subject matter
This course is fantastic and Aaron is the best professor. It's the most interesting course I've taken at Harvard, challenging in the best way. The content is amazing and class discussions are a great way to digest them. The cumulative research paper is a great way to explore any topic of your choosing that interests you in depth. I'm not a history concentrator, and you're interested in anything vaguely related to capitalism, law, history, or crime, you will get a ton out of this class.
