This was one of the greatest academic experiences of my life. Ross' class is challenging, but he is a uniquely supportive and excellent teacher. I came to Harvard as a prospective math concentrator and left this course eager to enroll in American government courses. I recommend this course to anyone, and maybe especially to those who don't plan to focus in areas of law or government
Personhood and Constitutional Law was my favorite class of the semester! The assignments can be demanding and the reading can be very dense, definitely not a simple class. However, you are interested in the Supreme Court or big social issues, I recommend this class. It requires a lot of reading and out of class work, but the professor, Dr. Ross Martin, is amazing and very supportive throughout the whole semester. If you are willing to put in the work, there is so much to learn from this class!
The professor is truly one the best ones I had this semester; he is there to assist you and wants you to do great. If you are a deep thinker interested in constitutional law, I deeply recommend it.
If you are interested in Constitutional law in the slightest, I would take this class. That isn't to say, only students who plan to study constitutional law again should take the course, in fact, quite the opposite is true, and I think many of the students who loved the clas the most were those who were not otherwise interested in persuing this subject; however, some interest in the topic will make the class much better. Other than that, it can seem daunting as a class, but it improves your writing significantly (talking to peers, seemingly much more than other expos classes). Beyond that, Professor Martin is a saint! He wants you to succeed. That alone is a pretty good reason to take the course.
Take this class! Dr Ross Martin is an amazing professor and I learned so much from him.
The discussions are super interesting to listen to, but if you're not a constitutional genius (which somehow almost everyone else in the class seemed to be) and your parents aren't lawyers who can bounce around ideas about case law with you, you might be like me and not really have much of substance to contribute to these discussions. But even though I mostly stayed quiet, class was very fun and interesting. Ross grades pretty harshly, and this is definitely NOT an easy expos. If you want an easy expos, don't take this class. The weekends before essays are due you likely won't be able to go out, and much of your Thanksgiving will be spend doing research (unless you're a constitutional law/writing genius of course which you very well may be). HOWEVER, it is a very rewarding class, everyone is super super engaged so the discussions are fascinatingly insightful and not forced, and you do learn a lot. It's good to take if you're pre–law. This class is very hard, but it can certainly be worth it.
This course may have slightly more work per week than the typical expos, but its fully worth it. Dr. Ross Martin is a fantastic instructor, and the cases are interesting to analyze. You will become a better writer and a better informed citizen.
It is a difficult Expos in terms of getting a pure A, but it can be done and you're not going to get worse than a B. You don't have to be "Pre–law" to take the class, but just have an open mind to Supreme Court decisions, personhood, etc. I think it is definitely worth taking to 1. understand the Supreme Court / the law / personhood in the US better and 2. to improve writing
Dr. Martin is an incredible teacher. He truly cares about every student and will work with you on a 1–on–1 basis to improve your writing skills. The class has an interesting selection of court cases – if constitutional law does not interest you or technical language will bore you, this might not be the class for you. The assignments are definitely challenging, and honestly, I think all expos classes are hard, but the way Dr. Martin structures the assignments (analytical question/thesis, draft/extended outline, and revision), and the feedback he gives on each part helped me get through this process. It's a lot of work, and expos is known to be painful. But having just gotten through it, I am happy I took Dr. Martin's class. I initially chose it because I thought it would be more of a history–type class rather than an English class. It is definitely neither of these types, but rather a class where the goal is to improve the writing of the students.
I could not recommend this class enough. It's definitely on the more difficult side, but class discussions are so engaging and rewarding. I definitely left with a changed perspective of the American legal and political system. Dr. Martin is also incredibly attentive and caringhe is flexible with assignments, gives super valuable feedback and insightful analysis, and really works to foster a fun and exciting curriculum. As a STEM major, this was still my favorite class this semester.
The conversations and discussions are very fruitful and rewarding. They have tremendously helped me in the writing process. I am very appreciative of the classes when Dr. Martin guides the discussions, but I also appreciate when he takes a step back and allows the students to have their own discussion. The writing assignments are wonderful, especially if you are like me and have an interest in this topic. Dr. Martin is extremely knowledgable and outspoken when it comes to Constitutional/Supreme Court history and his critical reflection of student writing only makes the experience so much better. I would totally take another class of his if I could!
The name of the course sounds long and scary, but it was honestly the best course that I took this semester. Ross is an amazing instructor and the content of the class is really interesting. The assignments are not unreasonable––and neither is the amount of required reading each week. There is no question that if you take this class, you will grow exponentially as both a writer and a person and I guarantee that, by the end of the semester, you won't want it to be over.
