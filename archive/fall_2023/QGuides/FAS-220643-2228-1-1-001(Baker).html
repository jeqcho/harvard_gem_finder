Comments
Highly recommend Dr. Baker's course for individuals interested specifically in history as well as all students in the sciences to learn more about their discipline. The readings cover a breadth of material and range from accessible to challenging, but Dr. Baker does a wonderful job synthesizing and contextualizing the work in class.
This class had super interesting material and I really feel like I gave me new context to understand the ways scientists talk about their jobs and responsibilities. It gave me a lens to understand science discourse through. I really enjoyed the readings each week and did not find the week–to–week workload burdensome. Sometimes the in–class discussion could be lacking. Erik had a lot of great knowledge about the topics and was very good at talking about them, but wasn't always the best at leading a discussion and engaging the class in conversation. This was his first class and I have a lot of trust that it'll only get better from here, so don't be afraid of taking this class!
Highly recommend this class! The readings are some of the most interesting I have ever done in college. The workload is relatively light (weekly discussion post, midterm paper, and final paper) but very enjoyable and allows you to dive deep into subject matter that you are interested in.
It is a good class but a lot of readings, and difficult if you do not have a strong background in history of science. Mostly geared towards upperclass students.
One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard!
Pretty good course. The reading responses were doable and the readings themselves very interesting. The book review assignment was a bit boring if you weren't interested in science/military involvement, but the final paper was really fun. Erik was clearly passionate about the subject matter, but our discussion could get a little bit dry / quiet at times. I think this might have been an artifact of this being the first time the class was offered though –– I'm confident that it will be even better in future years. The class was also pretty early, so I think folks were still waking up.
