This class is absolutely incredible! Professor Jasanoff is an amazing lecturer, and will always have your interest. Lectures are organized, informative, and have a variety of different sources/kinds of materials to keep you engaged. The structure of the assignments is great as well (not too long weekly readings, weekly quizzes in place of a midterm or final, and three papers). The papers help you practice important historical writing skills and are not too long, you have a long time to work on each of them. The teaching staff is very helpful and will always make time to meet with you and help you at any level you are at. The actual information that you are learning is also really significant and will help you to better understand how our modern world has been shaped. I would say that this class is a GEM. It is very possible to do well, no matter what your historical knowledge is beforehand.
Absolute no–brainer class. Super interesting and important material to understand world history. I wish it had spent a bit more time on things like WWII, but it's a great survey. Jasanoff is an engaging lecturer. The readings are interesting if you want to do them and to have engaging discussions in section. The essays do not relate to lectures that much, but you should go to lecture so you get the relevant background info. Essays are not a cake–walk to do well in but if you are thoughtful and go to OH you will be fine. I think this is a perfect, interesting, not too challenging course. I wouldn't call it a gem just because you have to do some work to get an A, but it should not be too bad.

But the class was very interesting and Professor Jasanoff clearly knows so much about the British Empire.

THE PERFECT HARVARD CLASS: manageable (<6 hours of work per week, always), incredibly interesting and relevant (it's modern British history, after all), very well balanced ideologically (lectures and short assigned readings focus on the darker sides of Imperial history, whereas the textbook highlights Britain's consensus views), with a very strong course structure (with very detailed, documentary–like lectures; only three papers, all of which are capped at five pages; weekly quizzes that cover almost surprisingly surface level material from slides; and a section component that is little different from other courses).

I took this class for my social sciences distribution, as a Computer Science concentrator going into finance and investing after graduation, and I could not have had a better experience. The very, very manageable yet incredibly interesting work load every week, for someone like me where much of what we covered was new to me, was a breath of fresh and the highlight of my nearly every week.

Professor Jasanoff is not out to surprise or alarm anyone who takes the course: It really feels like her passion project and everything about the class feels structured in a way that attempts to be reasonable. Taking this class is an absolute no–brainer for anyone looking to fulfill their social sciences distribution, a history elective for a secondary or concentration, or even a fun elective more broadly.
