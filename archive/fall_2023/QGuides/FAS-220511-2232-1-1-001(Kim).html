What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class has changed the way I see, the way I think about photos, even though and particularly since we consume them everyday. Dawn, Claude and the class was also hugely supportive, and I enjoyed myself and learnt immensely.
This is an amazing class!! It was the first AFVS class that I took at Harvard and I loved it. It's a small group so there is a lot of in–class interaction. You will also end up learning so much about using a digital camera, printing and photography critiquing. Understanding how to make good pictures was just one part of the class but by the end of the semester, whenever you look at a photograph or a sequence of photographs, your brain will automatically come up with so many thoughts on its possible meanings and composition. The assignments are also really fun and at no point do you feel like the class is burdening you. There are also a bunch of guest lectures that are very insightful and will make you think about photography in new ways. Hands down one of my favorite classes that I have taken at Harvard.
I would highly recommend taking this class if you're willing to put the time and effort into growing as an artist with your peers and be uncomfortable at times. It's hard to take pictures in spaces you're not used to having a camera in, it's hard to share those pictures with initial strangers, and it's hard to talk about them. But if you take risks and open up and buy into the class it can be an incredibly rewarding experience and bring you closer to your creative drive and other artists from various parts of Harvard and the world. I came in with no experience in photography but I had a great time and learned so much and this is a great course for people with any genuine interest in photography as an artistic medium and at any level of experience.
On the first day of class, we each got a camera. Dawn told us: “Spend the next few days with your camera. It’s your responsibility to _be curious_ about it since it’s what you will be using to create your work.”

Wow this was an incredible class. I've never felt so warm and appreciated and welcomed as in that small community of 12 students, Dawn, and Claude. I didn't even know anyone in the class beforehand, but they're all people who would smile at me now when I see them around. Taking photographs teaches you a lot about yourself and the world around you. My life feels fuller. Guest lectures from artists were fantastic. Exploring art galleries and bookstores and an audio walk in NYC as a class was also wonderful. I will never look at photographs the same way. I feel like I will never look at people the same way, either!
I would like to tell future students to take this class! While it could sometimes be time–consuming, it was an incredibly introspective and creative process in which I felt I learned more about myself and got to explore the nuances of photography. Overall I would highly recommend it!
Take this course. It has absolutely nothing to do with what I am studying but it was so enriching and I learned a lot about reading art as well as making it. It definitely takes up a lot of time and energy, but it is well worth it.
This course is definately a gem. With any previous background in photography, it is a worth–taken class to fundamentally transform your understanding of photography as an art form and a way of creation. Dawn is a sharp and thoughtful instructor and pushed me forward a lot. By the end of this class I really see my self making substantial progress. Claude is the nicest TF ever.
It has made me consider concentrating in Art, Film, and Visual Studies because of how Dawn and Claude helped us improve our individual work. I think that the insight of my classmates was also very helpful because I learned how to improve my work by watching and critiquing theirs. It was an incredibly fun class that encouraged self expression in many different ways, and it is something I valued a lot.
