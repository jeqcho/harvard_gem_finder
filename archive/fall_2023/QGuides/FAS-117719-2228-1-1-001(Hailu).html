What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a really great introduction to particle physics! It's a topic I had always been interested in, so I am super glad I took this course because I had such an enjoyable time! The course is really focused on maximizing what you can take away from it and having fun as opposed to grades or really rigorous assignments, so the relief from that pressure gives you more confidence in diving deep into the challenging concepts of the course. Girma was a fun professor since he was so excited about the material, and Alexis was a fantastic TF who was really devoted to helping you understand the material and led useful sections. The course assignments were all very fair. Overall a really fun class that I absolutely recommend if you're looking for a low–key, yet productive introduction to particle physics!
I enjoyed this course a lot, but I would say you should go into this class with an awareness that this is meant to give you only an overview of particle physics. It definitely became apparent in the class that if we really wanted to understand everything we touched upon deeply, we would need to take quantum field theory and physics 143b. If this is going to bother you because you want to understand every tiny piece of the subject, then this is not the class. Nevertheless, Prof. Hailu did an incredible job of picking out the most important and interesting parts, showing us tedious mathematics when it was illuminating, and leaving it to gloss over when it was not. I think this course did exactly what I wanted it to do for me and more: be a relatively low time commitment class that gave me a good background in particle physics and made me really excited to learn more! Alexis was also a great TF. She was always responsive to emails and worked hard to make section as useful as possible to us.
145 is tough at times, but go to section and ask questions –– it's really rewarding, and a great way to get exposure to a range of topics in particle physics. The final presentation is particularly cool.
Take it if you are interested in particle physics. Materials is very very challenging, however the assignments are mostly doable. Being familiar with some group theory, further quantum mechanics in the level of 143b, and electromagnetics or tensors in the level of physics 153 would be helpful. Also concepts from 16 and 151 also come up. I took this class after only taking the 15 series and 143a. I was lost in many lectures but I believe I did decent in the course. Professor Hailu, although may not be the best lecturer, is a great person. He will help you get through the course.
Go to Girma's Office Hours!! And don't feel nervous about asking stupid questions!! Review the lecture notes, use the textbook, and look up things online (online supporting materials proved VERY helpful for conceptual context), but also don't feel bad about just going to Girma during a p–set or after a lecture and explaining that you're confused and want help understanding something. He may explain it quickly at first, but that's just because he's one of the most brilliant and enthusiastic people you might ever might; he's also one of the kindest people you might ever meet, so just ask additional questions if you need to.
Also, you should take this course even if just to experience Girma's lecture style. He clearly loves the material and wants to share its elegance and quirkiness with the students; this leads to some incredibly energetic lecturing + the sense that he is really willing you to understand the material (without worrying about the details).
