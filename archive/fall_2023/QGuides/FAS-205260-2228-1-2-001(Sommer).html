What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Only enroll if you like a highly demanding course based on self–learning. Professors don't explain readings. The assignments require a lot of time and effort that aren't worthy. Also, the game pedagogy could be hard and confusing.
They can take this class without restrictions.
You really get out of this class what you put in, and it is such a once in a lifetime class that I would recommend taking it a semester when you really can put your all into it..
This is a relatively heavier time commitment during the semester so be sure you're ready for that! I spent ~4 hours on the weekly writing assignments, ~4–5 hours on the readings (which are long, dense, theoretical texts). Plus you have to create an activity for one week (extra time going in depth on the readings, ~1.5 hour meeting with the professors, creating and gathering materials with a partner) and write modest length final paper at 7–8 pages (written as a "companion piece" to another paper so you don't have to come up with a new idea). I think this time commitment was proportional to the rewards I reaped from this course––but I do think it's helpful to be informed about how much this class will demand from you on a weekly basis.
This course is a good way to get a sense of the broad scope of theory. However, it's not a good way to deeply engage with the material. Doris and Nicole have students facilitate the majority of each session, and while it's fun to come up with a creative activity that helps people understand the text, it sometimes doesn't allow for the kind of traditional close reading of the text and discussing its meaning, and I found that some theory texts do really need that.
