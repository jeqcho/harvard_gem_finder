What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class! It was mostly a review/consolidation class for me(I knew most of the material except for certain uses of the subjunctive, like noun clauses and conditions), and it was great at fulfilling that role. We do most of our learning through translations of text, which is really effective at teaching the language and increasing your translation confidence. Overall, if you did Latin in high school but are a little unsure of your skills, this class is a great option to take.
That Latin can be a genuinely fun, engaging language.
Latin 3 is a good continuation of Latin 1 and 2. The course underwent a reconstruction to match it with Latin Review for incoming students. The workload was light. It is a good course to keep up with Latin, preparing you for Latin X.
Like any language class, it takes work, but it's worth it! I'm sure that this is probably a class that varies based on who is instructing and in the class each semester . . . but we were blessed with the best group of people. It made coming to class a highlight of the day.
Towards the beginning of the semester, I was in between taking Latin 10 and Latin 3 but eventually chose Latin 3 because I wanted a more solid foundation for grammar, even though I did well in AP Latin. I have no regrets and felt this class prepared me extremely well for reading "actual" literature.
If you are active in class and truly try to participate during class translations, this course will not be a time–consuming or stressful one. However, if you do not try to translate in class, the midterm and final will likely be an unfortunate surprise.
Consistency is key! Staying on top of the material enables you to assimilate it such that you can sight read more and more fluently.
It is well taught! If you are interested in Latin and want to cement your basic skills, this is a great class for that.
