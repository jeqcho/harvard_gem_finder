Comments
This class is a lot of work, but really rewarding. The material is really relevant and is so applicable to environmental issues. Waste is something so overlooked in climate and environmental conversations, but this course gave a great introduction and analysis into the mechanisms of managing it. There are at least two weekly assignments a week in addition to readings so that is something to keep in mind if you have a heavy semester. However, Professor Thomas is incredibly engaging and invested in our personal development and work.
If you have an easy semester, try it. If not, don't take it because you'll be struggling with completing things on time.
This is a very interesting class to take if you want to go beyond the climate–change oriented curriculum of the typical Harvard environmental science class. However, it is a lot of work –– homework, readings, quizzes, and weekly assignments that ought to be done well if you want a good final product. It is worthwhile, but know that an A won't come easily. Sometimes the assignments can be overwhelming, due to the volume of work (even if they are not too intense in terms of content). However, this is a good course overall.
I would recommend this class. I think there are weeks where it can be a little bit harder, but the assignments are always due on the same days every week and they are not too much to handle. I would also suggest getting to know Professor Thomas better because he is very helpful when it comes to understanding the content of the class and helping you with your assignments.
Pretty good and eye–opening class. The lectures cover many different topics, but not in much depth. While in the moment the lectures were engaging, oftentimes my classmates and I wouldn't really be able to clearly state what we learned after class. You have 2 assignments per week + readings, which aren't too bad, and prevent you from slacking off too much. One of the weekly assignments is to do research for the term project, so the work is distributed nicely over the entire semester. Ken is a kind and receptive professor, but cold–calls a lot. I also got to know my classmates better because it was a small class
if you are interested in any kind of ESPP, international environmentalism, economics/physics/cultural contexts of environmental issues, TAKE THIS CLASS! Ken is one of the best professors I've ever had, so amazing to learn from, and it's just twice a week with no section. Keep in mind though that he's not lenient with grading, I took this class kind of as my most chill class compared to 2 other pset classes and a high workload language class, and my grade suffered because of that. still worth it though!
I would only take this class if you feel very passionate about solid waste and are willing to put in the work to get a good grade. Overall, I learned a lot, but honestly, the lack of explanation in the assignments and the midterm exam, the high expectations of the Term Project, and general attitude of the professor would make me hesitant to recommend to a fellow student. Also, 3/4 of the midterm and several homework assignments were calculation–based, but there was not a single time in class where we were taught how to do these calculations –– I turned these assignments in with no idea if I did them right because we were never given an example or guide. I'm well aware that I could have contacted the professor and go to office hours to get clarification –– and I did –– but it's unfair to give assignments that cannot be completed with what was taught in class and the readings.

All things aside, the dinner party that the professor hosted at the end was wonderful.
