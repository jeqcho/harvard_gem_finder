Truly a fantastic course, one of the best I've taken at Harvard. Professor Fisher's lectures are great, and I always left lecture feeling inspired and wanting to read more. This course presents a unique opportunity to read some of the best novels all together, and I think that having comparison points deepens your understanding of each individual novel. Each work provides its own "theory of everything"â€”its own theory of family life, of marriage, of rationality, of what are the most fundamental human experiences. Seeing them together and thinking about which ones resonate with you can also help you learn a lot about yourself.
This is such a great class. Yes, you will have to read a lot, but to be completely transparent –– you only write essays on 3–4 of the novels, so you can skip one that falls during a busy period like midterms if need be (and just skim important chapters before section). But I actually loved reading the books and did read 4 of the 5 in full. When else will you actually read these super famous and incredible books, much less have engaging and interesting lectures to help make sense of them? Professor Fisher doesn't make you "close read" and draw out random meaning from tiny details like we are trained to do in high school English, instead, he takes the novels and their messages seriously and talks about marriage, free will, crime, family, science, and what the authors are saying about these topics. I loved this class and the novels so much and I had never taken an English course before and did well so you can do it too!
If you actually do the reading, this class is great. Professor Fisher has excellent insights that just will not make sense if you haven't done the reading. I had an easier time of this class than some of my classmates because I had already read two of the five novels on the syllabus, so if you already know you want to take this class maybe get ahead on the reading before the semester starts. I suggest Buddenbrooks, because that one is really not a page turner. But the syllabus for this class is excellent. The assignments really aren't that bad because you already spend so much time reading that Professor Fisher and the TF's take pity on you. If you want to take a novels class, this is the best of the best.
Definitely take the course if you want to have read these novels! They're really beautiful and powerful, and it's so nice to know the context around them. The essays aren't very difficult if you've been doing most of the readings and thinking about them and going to class.
Just be prepared to read a lot. Every novel except for the first one on the syllabus is extremely long but rewarding to get through.
This is an incredible class! You will read incredible books and get so much out of them. I am not an English concentrator, but I found this class to be very accessible as long as you keep up with the readings. I highly recommend if you enjoy reading long books.
This was my first English class in several years, and it was great. Prof. Fisher and the TFs are excellent, and the novels on the reading list are all really enjoyable. It is a lot of reading, so be sure your schedule allows for at least 7–10 hours per week of reading, as well as some time to write the three papers for the course. It is worth the time investment though, and I would recommend highly for anyone interested in reading classic works of fiction.
If you like to read, take this class. You will become a better reader with a much better understanding of what the novel is and its unique contributions to human life.
Be ready to read a lot, but this class is really incredible in broadening your understanding of a fascinating stage of the novel.
If you love reading and don't mind devoting a lot of time to it, this class is for you.

The only reason I would not recommend this with enthusiasm is because you should only take it if you enjoy reading. This is a wonderful course. Professor Fisher has acquired so much definitive wisdom in the course of his career and he shows it off in his lectures. Often times I just sat there gobsmacked. There is a lot of reading (of great books) so make sure your course load allows you to focus on this class. If you do, it pays off!
I'm a realist enthusiast, but the course proved difficult because the books were SO long. I don't know a single person in the course who *finished* all five books we read. Professor Fisher was an interesting lecturer, but because it was so hard to keep up with the readings, I didn't feel like I got very much out of them. If you have the time to dedicate to reading all of the novels slowly and deliberately (perhaps by starting over the summer), this course could be really enjoyable. But, mostly, I just found it stressful to be 400 pages behind on books, despite putting in a lot of reading time during the week. Otherwise, Professor Fisher gave a lot of really interesting insights to the novels that I wouldn't have otherwise considered and section was a lot of fun. Grading was very quick and fair. This is a good course and it is clear that everyone who takes it ACTUALLY wants to be there, but you should be fully aware of the time commitment before signing up.
One should pick their courses in the humanities at Harvard either based on the Professor or the explicit reading list and this course offers both of those things excellently. No course will cover more important texts and few professors are better served to handle such a task than Prof. Fisher.
Cannot recommend this class highly enough! Classic books, interesting lectures, fun and engaging sections – this course has it all. I had so much fun working through these novels with friends that I could hardly believe it was my schoolwork. Would encourage anyone to take the class – you do not have to be in English/humanities to enjoy it or succeed (I study CS); I'd say it's a great fit for anyone who wishes they had more time to read in–term.
