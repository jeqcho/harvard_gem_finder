What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This was a great class! Michael was an amazing section leader, although unfortunately he will not be returning next year to TF for this course. Unger and Stauffer are clearly brilliant thinkers, and the connections and ideas expressed in the class were incredibly interesting. Because the lecture format is essentially just a discussion between the two speakers, it could at times be difficult to follow along and conversations could get a bit off track. However, section was very helpful in contextualizing and organizing all of the information given during lecture. The reading load was very heavy (100+ pages a week), but section was useful in explaining the main ideas and important details of each text. The only assignments in this class were 3 essays, however they do take quite a bit of time to plan and write. The final paper (15 pages) was definitely one of my more difficult writing assignments since I've been at Harvard, although there was a great deal of creativity in your topic selection.
Definitely go to lecture. It is difficult to sit through at times, but it is more difficult to watch online outside of a class environment. Without attending lecture, you will be very lost in section and assignments because you cannot simply deduce what lectures will be about based on readings.
Definitely take this class if you like writing and reading.
