Comments
Psychology PhD students: take this class! Don't think you need psychometrics for your research? You're wrong! You absolutely do! If not for primary results, AT LEAST for cultivating a more complete understanding of your data in your early stages of analysis.
As someone coming from outside the Psych department, it was really interesting to get an insight to how psychologists study and analyze data.
As with all of Patrick's classes, you get out what you put in. It's very easy to get away with minimal work, but if you pay attention to all lectures and engage with the assignments thoughtfully, you will walk away with a great working understanding of several statistical tools and models and how to apply them in practice. Patrick is always very available for questions and feedback!
Patrick is the best! Take this class and you will have no regrets.
Highly recommend. One of the best classes I've taken. Patrick has made a really excellent course that focuses on practical applications of a broad array of psychometric methods (factor analysis, structural equation modeling, network analysis, item response theory) with no math background required and just some introductory–intermediate statistics required, but still briefly provides theoretical/mathematical aspects for those interested. He provides a lot of code to get started, and it's all the code you need to do the exams which are straightforward. He's available for office hours and collaborations and encourages discussion. He's also really an expert on the topic, having written an excellent textbook himself, and has a lot of insight on different additional methods to try out.
