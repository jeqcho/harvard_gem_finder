What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is a lot of math and is difficult but rewarding
This is a great course, even for undergrads! If you enjoy econ theory at all (think 1011a) and are curious about finance, then this is a great option. Sam and Adi are amazing lecturers who are clear, funny, and engaging. The class is very light on work, so while the problem sets are challenging, there really isn't too much work overall.
I took this class as an undergrad. It definitely took more work than an average class, but with a strong interest in finance I found the work to be highly rewarding and great preparation for graduate studies. I recommend that if you decide to take this class as an undergrad, you should be highly comfortable with microeconomics, econometrics and mathematics, and I believe some background knowledge of topics in corporate finance is also needed.
This is a really good, and fun, class. You learn a lot about economics and economic “storytelling” and intuition (as opposed to just crunching math as in other courses). The problem sets are very long and are way harder than the class lectures.
