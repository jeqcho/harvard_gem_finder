If you have ever been curious about Russian literature, this is a phenomenal course for both introducing you to another world of literature and deepening any existing interest for Russian culture and literature. Russia and its literature can be such a mystery, but there's something uniquely honest, soul–gripping, and of course, strange about it that's hard not to appreciate.
This class has a weekly theme in which the works we read or movies we watch revolve around. We would meet twice a week for small group (~8 students) discussions. I really enjoyed the focus on discussion where we, the students, could push the conversation to whatever subjects piqued our interests. It's an added bonus that the intimate nature of the class allowed us to get to know one another very well, and Professor Sandler is fantastic at facilitating discussion. She's like the tour guide that leads us through these different works and her expertise and openness to interpretation was always appreciated as it can be difficult to dissect "strange" texts. It was gratifying to see how our initial thoughts were developed throughout the discussion and by the end of each class, we came away with these profound insights. It's a big credit toward Professor Sandler and my awesome class that we could develop a diverse variety of interpretations, find the connections, and come away with an understanding that you just can't get when thinking alone. Additionally, the majority of the works we read were short stories and poems. You will definitely come away with at least a couple new authors you want to keep reading in the future! In terms of assignments, we had response/creative papers of varying lengths (anywhere from 2–5 pages usually) that were given every couple weeks in addition to a final paper. To me, this system worked since the papers would help reinforce each couple weeks' worth of material. They were also a nice opportunity to take a deeper dive into a works that particularly caught your attention.
Overall, I'm grateful to have taken this course and it was always a pleasure to go to discussion and get into the readings each week. Professor Sandler is brilliant, empathetic, and passionate about what she teaches, it's easy to see, and I think you could take any class with her and find it to be an immensely rewarding experience. Not to mention that she is so kind and supportive of all her students. In retrospect, I think exploring Russian literature through strange writers is perhaps the best way to get into it. You'll see what I mean if you take the class. Finally, Russia can often be misunderstood especially because of its rich and complex history. This class was integral in helping me navigate the biggest themes related to Russia, learning how to make sense of them, and ultimately, I gathered enough of an understanding to feel capable in continuing to explore Russia, strange writers, and Russian literature in the future. It's a great feeling. I'm thankful to have been able to take a class like this as it has added to my appreciation for a culture that I probably couldn't have made sense of on my own. Definitely would encourage those curious to take this course!
