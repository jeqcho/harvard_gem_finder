This class is really fun and absolutely worth it if you are remotely interested in learning more about Borges. The readings are super interesting and you really do take a deep dive into his literature with the world's leading scholar on the subject. Class can drag on at times and it's easy to get lost, but section is great at reeling you back in and going over everything we do in class. Try to participate and engage with the course material to get the most out of it. Would definitely recommend.
great course. mariano is an engaged lecturer and cares about borges, in particular. not a lot of reading, but it can be dense. i often went to class confused on what borges was even trying to say, but mariano did a great job of providing an interpretation of most texts. great intro to borges! definitely taught me to think differently about literature, translation, reading, etc.
Take this class! It really wasn't that hard week to week, and the readings are very cool.
It is fun and not too challenging, as long as you know enough Spanish.
This class could be an excellent or terrible experience all depending on your Spanish level and you interest in South American Literature. If you are not fluent in Spanish you will greatly struggle to do well in this course, as the texts require heavy analysis and multiple revists to understand even a couple sentences. If you are fluent and like literature, this is a great way to fill a requirement for an elective/citation/concentration. The class does not have a heavy workload so you can manage it with other harder classes. Also, the professor is a great guy (friendly and wants the students to do well)
I would tell them to take it if they have time to read the texts, since they can be quite challenging.
