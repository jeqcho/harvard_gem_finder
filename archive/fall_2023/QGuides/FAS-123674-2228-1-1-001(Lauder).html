Absolutely take this class. So so interesting and cool, engaging lectures, worthwhile lab. It assumes very little prior knowledge, although the class will be easier if you know some biochemistry/evolution. This course provides a very vast yet thorough introduction to how and why the human body functions as it does. The material covered in lecture is very comprehensive and interesting to learn, and the labs are very enjoyable and always track very well with the lecture content. The course staff is very supportive and will be willing to meet one–on–one with any students to provide guidance or clarification on content questions.
While this course is difficult, it is not as hard as some students I talked to made it out to be/its reputation. The main difficulty of the course is the information volume: with 3 dense lectures a week, each midterm covers 12 lectures and a ton of material. However, tests are very fair, mostly just asking for recall of lectures. I would recommend a platform like Anki to help with memorization. Additionally, since there are no psets, there is no motivation to keep up with material until the exam rolls around, which is hard. Ideally, you would study a little each week, although I didn't do that and just crammed in the 3–5 days leading up to the exams (wasn't ideal, but not impossible to do well even like that). Lab component is also great for reinforcing things learned in lecture, and the materials provided by the preceptor + going to 1 lab office hours are all you need to do well on practicals.

Overall, this class is my favorite that I have taken at Harvard in terms of content. You truly learn so much about the human body, which if you find it interesting, is amazing! Course staff are also very helpful and provide lots of office hours/study groups. If something presented in lecture is confusing, they are great at rewording it so you get it. Definitely got a lot more out of this class than some of my friends in other ("easier") bio w/ lab courses for premed reqs. Grading is also fair, and anyone who performed above the median in exams was said to be in A/A– range by course staff.
