This is really hard course. Work together. Support each other. Find help ASAP. This class will eat up so much of your time. The sooner you find your support group, the better it will be for your mental health. And don't blame the professor. It is not the professor's fault. They care about you. It's how the course is structured and the material being taught. People have complained about this course for YEARS. Literal years. And nothing has changed. Don't expect it to change. Hate the class, not the prof.
This class will be very difficult for some if not most MCO students, but learning to appreciate what you are learning will be very valuable.
I would tell future students to attend office hours and TF hours as much as possible, since the instructors were very helpful, and I'd tell students who didn't have prior coding experience or upper level math background to reconsider taking the course if it's not a requirement for them
It may be a learning curve depending on your background and approach to quantitative questions in biology, but the course presents handy mathematical and computing tools that (I believe) are handy for the modern molecular biologist.
This is a challenging but rewarding class. Attend the sections and student hours and start the homework early.
