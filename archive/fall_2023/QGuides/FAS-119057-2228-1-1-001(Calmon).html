Comments
I thought the course was cool! But be warned that information theory gives some limits of communication systems, but doesn't often inform how to achieve bounds. This made the subject less rewarding for me at a personal level as an engineer, as it felt more like a math class. But still material that I appreciated.
This class is a pretty thorough introduction to the main ideas of information theory and gets you to the point of reading papers and completing substantive projects! The course staff are wonderfully supportive.
This class is hard but rewarding. Flavio is an amazing lecturer and an understanding professor. You will get a new perspective with which to view your work as well as the digital technology surrounding us.
The course is fantastic, just make sure that you have a strong probability background. The more math, like analysis, the better.
This course may be offered by electrical engineering faculty, but it is not an electrical engineering course. It is a mathematics course. If you are not interested in advanced theoretical mathematics and are not comfortable with measure theory and convex optimization, do not take this course. That said, it is a well–designed and well–taught course that will seriously improve your math skills if you choose to take it.
I think this is one of the best taught courses I've taken so far at Harvard –– wish I'd taken it earlier! The problem sets do take a while, particularly toward the latter half of the semester.
This was both my favorite and most difficult class this semester! Flavio is legitimately a star professor and he is probably the best lecturer I had out of the six lecturers I had this semester. I can easily tell his expert knowledge on information theory and adjacent fields like signal processing, machine learning, and communications. He was excellent at explaining things which is crucial in such a difficult class. I think this is a gem if you're interested in the subject matter and willing to put in the effort. He is also very flexible and willing to give you more time if necessary on a problem set, which he understands very well are very difficult.

This is a mostly pure math course but there is no doubt that this course is applicable to a vast area of engineering, computer science, and applied math. Machine learning is a common application and reason why people take the class which is good but Flavio also covers a lot of other applications like statistics and communications.
