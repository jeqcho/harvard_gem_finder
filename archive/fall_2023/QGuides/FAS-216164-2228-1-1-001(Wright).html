What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course – in its current form – tries to do too much, resulting in a somewhat scattered and unfocused result. There are a lot of readings assigned each week (too many to cover in class), discussions are not super effective (the class tries to be more student–led but ends up lacking direction), and a lot of small assignments throughout the course (care journals, audio explorations) that are supposed to help you with your engaged project but end up feeling more like busywork. Although I really enjoyed my engaged project, it is a fairly self–directed and rarely discussed in class. The course itself is not challenging, but be prepared to dedicate at least a few hours to a service project over the semester. The instructors are caring and well–meaning and open to feedback – there might be some changes by next year!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!
This class will impact you deeply! However, I recommend it with reservations since it is a larger time commitment than the course staff makes it out to be. This class has care journals due every other week, audio explorations, a midterm interview, extremely long readings, and various misc assignments due throughout the semester––all on top of 3–5 hours of volunteering *every* week. It takes a lot of effort to keep up with the various parts of this course and also make sense of the vague guidelines that are provided for the assignments. With that being said, this class offers you the opportunity to think more deeply about how you want to orient yourself to the world and community engagement. Even though class discussions did not feel very productive at times (often dominated by a few students), the course staff is very supportive and just doing the readings/working with a community partner for the care project were really formative experiences for me. Highly recommend if you are thinking about going into a public service / nonprofit career!
