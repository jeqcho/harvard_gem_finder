This class is difficult– I know Prof Berker says it, but it’s the kind of class where by the midpoint you’ll be reading words without comprehending much. I would have taken at least one upper level philosophy class before this, in an analytic style (Heidegger or history of philosophy will not be the best prep) – philosophy of language would probably be best because it’s very closely tied to metaethics. There’s also a lot of work in this class – three essays, first one has a rewrite, and there’s a three hour final exam where the questions aren’t easy. The grading is also firm. Prof Berker is a fantastic lecturer as any of his groupies will tell you, but just know what you’re getting into.
Hard course content and readings, a lot of prior knowledge in philosophy is assumed. Taught wonderfully by professor Berker though. Challenging but worth it.
warning, this is a difficult course; proceed with caution. if you don't like getting into the nitty–gritty and nit–picking arguments, this class is not for you. It's not a high–level survey of metaethics (the course covers nihilism/relativism/expressionism), but spending a lot of time down in the weeds deeply analyzing a few representative positions and arguments for each. it's intellectually rigorous but stimulating and your philosophical thinking/reading/writing will definitely sharpen, but imo is very impractical for life outside the philosophy classroom. I personally didn't end up enjoying this class as much as I thought I would based on Q reviews, and in hindsight probably wouldn't have taken it.

readings are quite difficult, but Prof. Berker is brilliant, a good lecturer and provides great handouts. lectures have a lot of q&a and discussion mixed in, was dominated by a handful of the students who were more participatory, +1 on this quote from the previous Q guide: "These discussions also felt unnecessarily down in the weeds of issues that didn't feel relevant or interesting, when the material in general is very interesting." I think the class would definitely better be suited as a seminar. Berker interrupts students a lot. but plays devil's advocate very well. Ezra was a great TF, although section (open discussion) felt unfocused at times
This is a very engaging class with material that will push you further than an ordinary philosophy class. The material is harder than for other philosophy classes of its level but it is still worthwhile.
Push yourself to give as much of yourself as you can to this course; it's a great one!!!!!!
This is an intellectually engaging class. If you want your ideas challenged and want to be surrounded by smart people, take this class. Professor Berker is an excellent lecturer and simplifies complex philosophy very appropriately. The way he brings the rest of the class into his discussions in lecture is also amazing. You learn from other people in the class just as much as you do from Professor Berker himself. The TF this year, Ezra, was amazing: incredibly smart, very witty, and always helpful. Would recommend this course to everyone. Not the easiest class though; be prepared to work hard on those papers. But they teach you how to think critically about deep philosophical questions.
I think the format of this course is the best you could ask for for this type of material. Prof. breaks down information in an easy to understand way, and there are plenty of opportunities to discuss and ask questions.
This is a well–constructed but difficult and occasionally frustrating course. While the readings respond to each other in cool ways and Professor Barker is a great lecturer, the units get both philosophically harder and more esoteric as the semester goes on, moral expressivism in particular. The essays are graded pretty harshly. If you're a philosophy concentrator or someone who's taken a bunch of courses in the department, you'll be fine, and the material is always interesting. But be wary otherwise.
You need to be really into philosophy to enjoy this course—but if you are, you will have a good time. Teaching is excellent (Selim is amazing) and clear. He has these great handouts which tell you everything you need to know. At the same time, the material is conceptually tough and it might be hard to motivate the "why" of this course if you aren't independently motivated in this specific niche branch of philosophy
This was my first philosophy class (as a senior). I enjoyed it and would recommend! I took it pass/fail but it does feel like the course load is reasonable even if graded. Handouts and lectures are super clear, and Selim is great at engaging a class to ask questions during lecture. Ezra was also a great TF and gave a lot of helpful feedback on papers. I will say that the topic of metaethics itself can feel repetitive at some point; it's a lot of very specific arguments around similar topics.
Great class. Prof Berker is an amazing lecturer. Took it as an elective as a STEM concentrator and was able to keep up + got a lot out of it.
Definitely take this class! Learning from Professor Berker is a privilege. The material is tough but highly rewarding. Start the papers early and attend office hours and you will be fine. This class will push you to think about ethics and your life in ways you would not have before
Definitely take this class if you have an opportunity! It is definitely not an easy class and engages with many difficult and abstract questions, but it is despite that one of the best courses I have so far taken. Professor Berker is an excellent lecturer and communicates the material, no matter how difficult, with immense clarity.
