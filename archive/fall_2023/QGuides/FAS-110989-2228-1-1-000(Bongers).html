take math 21b. it is a very interesting course
This is a fast–paced course, but the supports are there to help you learn it if you put in the time and energy. Go to section, do the p–sets, go to MQC, and study for the exams and you'll be alright. Don't use up your p–set drops too early – I promise you'll want them later in the course.

The material is a lot of work, but the course is well–structured, well–taught, and fairly graded. And the material is very applicable for lots of STEM students!
Honestly, just take Math 22a instead. The 3 psets/week are soul draining in a way that you won't realize until it hits you in the middle of the semester.
I initially took this course because I had a poor background in understanding matrixes and felt very uncomfortable using them. This course helped me to become VERY comfortable in using matrixes now! Assignments are generally 3–5 questions long depending on the rigor of the material. GO TO THE MCQ to double–check your answers are correct and also to ensure you get a good understanding of the material. Create a PSET group chat to help. Assignments took me about 1.5 hours to finish, not including MCQ, and there's three PSETS per week. Grading is nice: several of your lowest PSETS are dropped at the end of the year, and more weight is given to your best Midterm I/Midterm II/Final exam. The TFs are generally great and want to help you. The class offers practice quizzes over the course of the year to prepare for midterms. Generally, I prefer to review the quizzes right before the exam because they are at times harder than exam material and will make me feel confident going into the exams.
Having 3 psets a week was definitely rough and time consuming. MQC literally saved me. If you like the more computation part of math, I would still recommend this over Math 22A. Overall, not bad, and you learn a lot.
Take some time after every two weeks or so to make sure you still remember some of the connections to previous material that you've already learned. This class really builds on itself so it's very important to understand all of the concepts
