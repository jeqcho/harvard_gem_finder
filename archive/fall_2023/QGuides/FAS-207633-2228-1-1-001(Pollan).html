What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS if you have the opportunity to!! The class is superb in every aspect, from the teaching to the guests to the assignments and readings themselves. Professor Pollan is at the top of his field in science journalism, and he generously shares his insights/advice with every student, from giving an insider perspective on how the journalism/editing industry operates to reading each student’s drafts and giving paragraphs of detailed feedback. We got the chance to speak with guests (experienced editors, writers, and scientists) throughout the course, getting detailed and diverse perspectives on every step of journalism, from reporting and finding sources to how to best craft a pitch. By nature of the subject, the class is very interdisciplinary and brings together students across the sciences and humanities in a very small and close–knit setting. If you are a student interested in both science and writing this is the class for you! We were exposed to breakthroughs and headlines in an enormous number of scientific fields, from agriculture to quantum physics. Simultaneously, we got to hone our journalism and creative writing skills through the “narrative” part of Narrative Science Journalism—careful thought must be put into constructing a person–centered story arc from the science. Apply to the class, even if you have little previous experience in journalism—as long as you have a strong background and interest in science and writing, Professor Pollan will make sure you are acquainted with all the conventions, strategies, and jargon of reporting/journalism. In addition, because it is a journalism class, be prepared to devote a lot of time outside of class to reporting: finding sources, conducting interviews, and traveling to nearby sites related to the story. The more time you invest in reporting and writing your story, the more you will take away from the class.
Please take this class if you get the chance––Michael is a fantastic teacher and editor, and we read so many good pieces of journalism this semester. Truly a pleasure being in the class.
If you have any interest in journalism and science take this course! You get to read a ton of great stuff and really learn and understand the journalism industry better. Even if you have experience at The Crimson or other journalism publications, you probably haven't had much exposure to the mainstream industry as a whole and this course really helps you understand that better. You also will get to really work on your writing as your pieces get workshopped. And, of course, Michael Pollan is teaching it. He's a really great teacher and gives really good feedback, and remarkably approachable and kind for someone so famous and presumably with a lot of other things to do outside of teaching this class. My main piece of advice to anyone who takes this class is, to the extent possible, try to make it the priority of your semester. You will get as much out as you put in. So try to go to office hours, spend a lot of time on your draft, etc. Don't waste the opportunity!
Michael Pollan is so cool and very honest, take this class and really use him as a resource because he can improve your writing a lot. Make sure to pick a good idea with what to write about, otherwise you'll get bored in the course. Good luck!
If you’re interested in science writing, take this class. You won’t learn the craft better anywhere else. Michael Pollan is fantastic, kind, and so smart.
It goes without saying that Michael Pollan is one of the greatest teachers at this school. Like many courses, you will get from this class exactly what you put in. You cannot fool Michael (or the class) by doing less work than you need to to produce a thorough piece of writing by the end. Through this class, I learned how to write, edit, and be a good classmate. Take this course if you want a challenge and are someone who learns by doing!
I'd highly recommend this course to anyone interested in journalism. You will learn so much from a stellar collection of readings, guests from established media outlets, and Michael's decades of wisdom. This course is guaranteed to make you a better writer.
English CNSR defined my fall semester – it was the one class I talked nonstop to everyone, from close friends to relatives to strangers. Michael has been the most inspiring and encouraging writing teacher I've ever had, and he gives so much time and care in providing pointed feedback and guiding you in putting together a piece that you are truly proud of. Every class and office hour left me refreshed about all the exciting possibilities that my piece could take me, and a large part was due to the incredibly helpful suggestions from our class workshops and Michael's comments. The small class lend itself to forming a tight–knit community that went on occasional dinners together or movie nights. If you have a chance to take this class, please don't hesitate to take it!! This class nurtured my curiosity in a way that few classes do at Harvard, and I left the class a better scientist and writer.
Pollan holds you to a high standard but he is, without a doubt, the best teacher I've ever had. He'll give you invaluable insight into whatever field of science you choose to cover (what's hot, what's been covered, what angle you should take, etc.) and of course you'll become a better writer. He is as good a teacher as he is a writer, which is to say he's incredible at both.
You'll spend a lot of time on this course but you'll get out of it what you put in. The small community of the course is fun as well.
