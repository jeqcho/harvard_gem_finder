Take this class!!! It was so powerful and rewarding, and Hudson is the absolute best. You'll find a community, gain a new understanding of how to write effectively, and learn about an extremely important issue in the US. Even when it was difficult to understand and discuss, Hudson was so considerate and supportive. It'll be a lot of work sometimes, but it's all worth it.
Please use this course to prepare yourself for future classes at the college. Expos 20 is meant to challenge you, so the least you could do is pick a topic that you have a passion for. This class is truly all about the effort you put in, so please make the most of it by submitting assignments that exhibit thorough reflection and incorporates any feedback. The preceptor makes themselves available to his students, so make sure to ask any and all questions!
Complete assignments on time. Make sure to attend office hours so that you can make sure your drafts are on the right track. The first draft comments are humbling, but they do make you a better writer if you give yourself time to apply the feedback.
This class will push you. Sometimes, especially in the final essay deadline season, it might even make you question your relationship in writing. However, at the end of the day, not only will you come away with a community that you can always lean on, but you will also become a better writer than you ever thought was possible. In the hard seasons, you will have a professor who genuinely cares about you and wants you to succeed. He will work with you through the challenges, and give you grace when you need it. You will also meet incredibly strong and inspiring people who have themselves been through mass incarceration, giving you a better understanding for the system than any book or article ever could. To this day, I still get chills when I think about some of the stories our visitors shared. I find myself humbled at the reality of our system today, while also being filled with a sense of hope that maybe our generation can begin to help make a dent in ending mass incarceration, and bringing humanity to those behind bars so that they can one day come back into their communities and positively contribute to them, too. By the end of the course, you will understand the complexities of our system, and all of the injustice behind it. and, hopefully, you will come away wanting to do something about it. At least I did, and this course, through its social engagement capstone project, will give you a place to start. If accommodations and accessibility are concerns, rest assured that Hudson will work with you and insure that you have what you need throughout the course! I highly recomend taking this course. The work load is pretty light until you get to essay writing, but this course is so much more than just a writing course, and will teach you so much about mass incarceration and life itself.
Please keep in mind that the pace in this class is not necessarily fast but you need to pace yourself. I do recommend this course because it is a very important topic and Hudson does a good job of teaching you how to write.

This course is somewhat heavy and intensive but worth every minute. Especially if you're interested in mass incarceration, the Prison Industrial Complex, civil rights, or any kind of civil justice, I strongly suggest taking this course. Hudson is also one of the best preceptors and mentors I've ever had. He's very understanding and is completely capable of teaching students how to write advanced pieces of writing. If you're new to writing comparative analysis or more complex pieces, don't let that deter you from taking this course. You can pick it up along the way with Hudson's help, all while exploring mass incarceration.
This class is challenging but rewarding. "Are Prisons Obsolete?" forces students to confront harsh realities of the prison system in America, but for those who are interested in the topic, this course is incredibly transformative. The course also features visits from speakers who allow students to get glimpses into the real–life effects of mass incarceration, from formerly incarcerated people to heads of prison education programs. This course's final project gives students the opportunity to delve deeper into any facet of mass incarceration that interests them, which I particularly appreciated. While the workload is not light, and grading is not easy by any means, Hudson is generally flexible and will help you get assignments done to the best of your ability. I recommend taking this course if you have an interest in incarceration and are prepared to be challenged in a constructive manner.
