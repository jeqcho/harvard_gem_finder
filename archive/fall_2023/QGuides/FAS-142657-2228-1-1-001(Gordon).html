This is a great class if you are interested in learning about the Japanese experience in world history. It explores in detail the interactions between Japan and other parts of the world, even in prehistoric times. Because of the span of the course, there is a lot of freedom with the assignments. There is always an opportunity to explore in more detail the parts that interest you the most.

TAKE THIS CLASS. Gordon, Howell, and Gene are the best teachers ever, you get a comprehensive understanding and knowledge of literally the entirety of Japanese history, in terms of how Japan became Japan and how it interacts with Asia and the world. My favorite lectures, my favorite section, my favorite people. This class is the reason I'm concentrating in history.
This is a really great class. I wouldn't call it a gem because I think you have to do most of the readings and attend most lectures but I don't think it's impossibly hard by any means. It's a bit more of an intimate lecture which allows you to interact with the professors and the material more.
Future students should know that this class moves quickly given its wide timeline and span. However, it is still manageable if you get on top of everything.
