What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Read the texts in the order they're listed on the syllabus, don't read them at random. And definitely read them through twice, even three times
David is an inspiring teacher whose enthusiasm seeps into every aspect of his seminar. His selections are always fascinating and a pleasure to read. And his own generous and measured, though always playful, approach to his materials and his students is exemplary. If you are interested in learning about the history of Comparative Literature as a discipline and understanding the key debates at the heart of its field, this is the course for you. Be warned though, unless the course is reformed in the future, there is a lot more theory to be compared than literature!
This course will certainly make you better readers of both literature and theory. It's a real eye–opener, so don't miss out on it!
