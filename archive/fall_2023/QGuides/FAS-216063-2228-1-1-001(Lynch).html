The quality of this course is very dependent on which Professor you are looking to study with. That being said, Professor Lynch is WONDERFUL. If you get a chance to take any course with her, you should DEFINITELY take it. Kathrine Horgan (our TF) was equally as amazing: she has changed my essay writing permanently and under her advising and teaching my writing has improved tremendously this semester. You will definitely like some of the texts in this course more than others, although it is one of those classes where you get out of it what you put into it. If you don't do any of the readings and don't participate in discussions, you will be bored. However, even if you do most of the readings (nothing crazy, just enough to know whats going on) and participate in class, you will have an amazing time. Form is incredibly important and its enlightening to see how it can work with content to enhance meaning. Its not very difficult either: very flexible and easy to work into your schedule. 10/10 would suggest to anyone.

This is a class that gives you so much, if you are willing to put in the work. Set aside time to do the readings, and to read attentively. Start on your essays early, so you can get feedback on your ideas and refine your writing before the time comes to submit. And go to OH with Prof. Lynch –– she is so helpful, and genuinely cares about advising you and getting to know you.
This class is hands down the best English class I have taken. Prof. Lynch and Katherine say the most brilliant things and it makes you want to keep a commonplace book (which you'll learn about) to note down all the cool things they say. They teach you how to read, write, and just think. A fabulous intro class for English concentrators.
This class is a really great start to an English concentration. It provides a great foundation in the field and was honestly really fun to engage with. Discussion was encouraged and not intimidating, and I gained a lot of appreciation for the many genres I don't usually read.
I would not recommend this class for non–English concentrators just because there are some technicalities and a lot of focus on historical forms. But for English concentrators, try to join Professor Lynch's class! I loved her enthusiasm and passion, and she is so lovely to talk to in office hours. Some readings were better than others, and some felt like a "oh okay I guess this is required," but overall I enjoyed the class and there was a very reasonable workload.
This course was probably one of my favorites at Harvard. Professor Lynch and Katherine are both INCREDIBLE and I genuinely enjoyed all of the readings. The focus on form was an extremely useful framework that I will certainly take along with me into other courses. The essays were very (very very) difficult, but Katherine was an immense help. I came out a far more capable and confident writer.
Deidre Lynch is an absolute treasure! Every lecture blew my mind. The essays were graded pretty harshly, but I felt I achieved a greater understanding of literary form throughout the course, which I guess is the whole point.
English 20 with Professor Lynch was a really wonderful class. Professor Lynch and Katherine are both extremely passionate, extremely knowledgeable, lovely people to learn from. The readings are not too dense and the class discussions are generally very engaging. The course is a little lecture heavy because you need to learn about the forms, but there is generally some time for interesting conversation near the second half. The creative assignments are extremely fun. Katherine bends over backwards to help you with your papers—she makes herself so available to brainstorm and think that a very intimidating essay becomes much easier.
If you’re considering doing an English concentration/secondary AND Professor Lynch is teaching it, take this class. I learned a ton about genre/form and how literature has changed over time (plus, like, the human condition and all that).

I didn’t love all the texts (though there were bangers), but the teaching always made up for it. Professor Lynch would sometimes make a comment so mind–bendingly insightful that our graduate Teaching Fellow would gasp and look around at everyone like "did you just hear that?!"

It’s a very low–stress class not because it has no work but because it's very well–structured. The reading is super manageable (and I read slow and annotate a fair amount). The two papers weren't bad, largely due to the help of Katherine, our TF, and the only other grades were very easy one–page creative assignments.
