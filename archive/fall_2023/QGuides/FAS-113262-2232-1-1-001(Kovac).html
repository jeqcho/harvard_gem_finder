What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Pros:
The most rewarding class I've taken at Harvard College – cannot recommend enough. This is exactly what I joined the Astrophysics concentration for – the chance to do research. I found my project very hands–on and the stuff I did actually mattered – the questions I was answering did not have a canned answer I was supposed to arrive at, but rather I was exploring questions that had not been explored before.

Cons:
Unusually difficult even for an astrophysics course. The work you are meant to do is basically to condense two 3–month–long projects into like 6 weeks each. Also, standards in this class are very high – your papers basically need to be grad student level.

Overall:
TAKE THIS COURSE, but make sure your other classes are easy, in order to make room for the high workload of this class!
Very large courseload. But, you learn a lot. Try to take this course knowing that you will be dedicating most of your time to it. Structure other courses around this one.
Definitely a great learning experience, but be prepared to set aside 20+ hours a week during midterm/reading period. There are 2 20+ papers that you'll write for each project, and they're usually due right after the final presentations so be expected to allocate a lot of time for that. This class also goes on a trip to see the Bell Horn which was pretty cool.
This class has high expectations – two research projects in one semester, each with an accompanying presentation and ~20 page publication style research paper. However, I think it is worth taking if you want the opportunity to dive into two fields of research that are likely unfamiliar to you at the beginning of the semester. The Bell Labs field trip was fun.
The class workload should NOT be underestimated. This class is literally insane. You write TWO twenty page research papers that often go like 10–20 pages over 20 pages, and there’s not a lot of support along the way. I think it’s definitely worth it, but be prepared for a LOT of hard work that can feel hard, directionless, and frustrating.
