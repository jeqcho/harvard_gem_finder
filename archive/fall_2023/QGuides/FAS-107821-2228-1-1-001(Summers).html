For background, I took this class while enrolled in Ec10A and had very little exposure to economics coming in. If you are in this boat, being concurrently enrolled in ec10 will be helpful, but not having a lot of economics exposure will make the course more difficult and require extra work, especially since a lot of the people in this course are economics concentrators with much more experience with the course material. However, this class has made me much more interested in taking future economics courses, and it has shown me how economics is important in my own life. It is definitely a class that provides you with a greater understanding of the world. The policy simulations are particularly unique and provide an enlightening experience. The guest speakers are also INCREDIBLE as are the professors.

As far as workload, you will have to watch asynchronous lectures weekly, as well as work on two policy memos. Start the policy memos early!! The policy memos will be graded fairly harshly, so starting early is crucial. The policy memos will significantly increase the workload for this class around the time that they are due. Also, don't be afraid to go to office hours and ask your TF for advice about the memo. This was extremely helpful for me, especially since I had not written a memo before. Finally, I'm not sure how they'll look in future years, but know that there might be reading quizzes (that test both the reading content and the asynchronous lecture material). These were made easier as the course went and weighed very little on the final grade, but they could still be fairly challenging.
