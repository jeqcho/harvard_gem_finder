This is the best GENED I have taken at Harvard. It only has a midterm and a final, which count for 30 and 50 percent of your grades respectively. The remaining 20 percent is section attendance and participation (so you do have to go to section). No homework. The readings are great, but you can get away without doing them, or at least without doing all of them. I still recommend reading some of the Supreme Court opinions which are required readings in the second half of the course, particularly Justice Holmes's dissent in Lochner. Prof. Sunstein tries to do justice to both sides of a debate, so you will hopefully come out of this course having a better understanding of the opposing views on many issues. This is especially true of judicial philosophy and contentious Supreme Court decisions, which I think a lot of people, including many in the media, misunderstand and/or misrepresent.
