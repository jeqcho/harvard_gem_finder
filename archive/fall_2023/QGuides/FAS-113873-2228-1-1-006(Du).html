This class is extremely well organized and generous when it comes to grading. So much of the grade is effort based, but that being said it can be very time consuming for those who have no background in Chinese. I am a student with a decent about of previous Chinese background, but there were students of varying backgrounds. As someone who has taken Chinese before, the class was not time consuming at all, yet I am glad that I did take it because there are many grammatical lessons that I had never learned before, and specific nuances that they teach in this class that were not taught in my high school. I probably would have struggled in Bx, but BA was a bit easy, but I think BA was good because it solidified my foundation and recorrected some bad habits.
This is the best language class I've ever taken (and one of the best classes I've taken in general). The teachers are AMAZING and so nice, and they genuinely try to get to know the students. I've felt really supported and encouraged by them. The lectures are really good and actually pretty fun/interesting, and the homework is a lot, but actually teaches you the material. A few things to note: (1) it's a lot of work, way more than the other language classes (from what I've heard), (2) If you have 0 background in Chinese at all, the first few weeks to a month will be really overwhelming. I considered dropping it after about 2 weeks because I felt like a was really behind (there are clearly some people in the class who have a bit of background in Chinese), and I had to put in a lot of work the first month in particular. I think this class took more effort than any of my STEM classes at first. However, after you get used to it, it does get a lot more manageable. This ended up being one of my favorite classes this semester and was incredibly rewarding.
Take this class! It is a very in–depth but relaxing introduction to Chinese.
Study every day a little bit at least

This is truly one of, if not, the best class I have taken at this school. The teachers are absolutely amazing, and they are truly here to help you learn Chinese, regardless of where you begin. They will teach you, make the class comedic and fun, applicable to real conversational Chinese, rather than conversational, and just makes the entire process of learning this language so fun, comfortable, yet still extremely productive in learning a large amount of material. No matter how overwhelmed you may get, there is plenty of practice and help provided by the course that ensures that you will catch up and be up to par with your classmates, of course assuming that you do actively participate in class and overall materials. You build a great community, as you do see them every single day of your semester. All that being said, please do your work, study the material, keep up with the class before it is too late, this class is what you make of it, so with just a little bit of effort, you get such high rewards. Truly, if you are at all interested in learning Chinese or Chinese culture, or just to truly have fun while learning something extremely useful, take Chinese!

I am a senior and this is the best course I have taken in college. We play Kahoot games, charades, etc. The instructors are young and full of energy which makes this course incredibly engaging. I highly recommend that you take it. It is crazy how much you will learn in a single semester of Chinese. Having said all of this, I would offer the following advice. This is challenging for the beginner, but highly rewarding. The content taught is highly relevant for everyday conversation.
If you have a lot of time to dedicate to studying, it will be a very rewarding class for sure. You will be required to learn a lot of vocabulary words per week and be expected to write 20 characters biweekly, so I would suggest starting to learn the new words right after your last dictation of the week if you have time. All of the lectures are so so sweet and nice, so they make learning fun. The main downside is that this class meets everyday when you combine sections with lecture, so it gets to become a lot. However, you literally cannot fail this class based on the way it is set up (attendance/participation makes the bulk of your grade). You will definitely learn a lot this semester. P.S. take notes in class. They advise against it but it makes doing the homework easier because you don't need to navigate the long slide decks.
