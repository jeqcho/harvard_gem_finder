Interesting material and occasional interesting clinical connections. Super jargony and requires lots of memorization. Few assignments = not a lot of work, but midterm / final felt high stakes. Lectures are harder to take notes on than most STEM classes –– no slides, just very messy drawings that are not helpful to look back on. Shiv's enthusiasm and knowledge are clear, and I appreciated his appreciation of the history behind the science.
be aware of psets, it is hard to gauge what they are actually looking for you to answer. read the textboo and take notes, make study guides every week to consolidate information. don't want until October for midterm to study.
Teach yourself use the lecture as homework.
Take this class if you are excited or curious about immunology, and be smart about understanding big topics as opposed to specific pathways. Take your time on psets, start early, and seek help often. The midterm is not too bad, just make sure you review HMX prior.
Great course on basic immunology!
This course provides a solid overview of the field of immunology that should prepare you well for further research in that field. The lectures can be a bit surface level and quick, but the sections are quite useful for solidifying the material.
If you don't have a background in immunology, this class is hard. If you're not a grad student, this class is hard. If you don't have a good TF, this class is hard. All in all, there are innumerable obstacles to feeling equipped while taking this course. Although it's definitely possible to do well without having extensive experience in the field, it can often feel like you're flying by the seat of your pants. Resources are scarce, lecture is difficult to tackle (the acronyms that Prof. Pillai throws around only make it more difficult), and the standards that you need to meet are often unclear. That said, the course staff generally tries to be helpful, even if there are issues with accessibility, and the content is absolutely fascinating. Once you're done with the class, you'll realize how much you learned and appreciate the level of detail that you had to command in order to succeed, but in the moment, it's not a walk in the park, and I'm not certain that there aren't other ways to learn just as much as you do in this course that don't require as much guessing and uncertainty.
This class has a lot of graduate students! This means that for a lot of people, this isn't their first encounter with the material. Don't let this deter you. Compared to the first immunology course I took, this one was better structured. Immunology is never going to be a cake walk, but if you go to section and make an honest effort in lecture to listen then things will fall into place!
Basically a college level class. Not very suitable for graduate students who already have some background in immunology. Also, IMMUN 201 lays emphasis on experimental design in its quizzes, which is very useful but generally not covered in MCB 169.
Shiv is a great professor and the content is hard but it's worth taking to know what's going on in immunology
Immunology is so important to the field of biology as a whole and taking this class will ensure you are comfortable with the material!

This is a great course and Shiv is a great professor. Definitely would recommend!
This lays the ground work for future studies
Take this class if you want to really learn and understand immunology! Materials and feedback regarding exams are a bit vague but the most important thing is to keep up with Professor Pillai's lectures.
This class is very difficult, which a lot of material that is covered in a short amount of time. You will be expected to remember specific cytokine names and other minutiae, and will be tested on it. To avoid high amounts of stress before the final, please keep up with the course material and study using active recall. The course also has no office hours for problem sets, and there is little guidance as to what is expected for the assignments. Be prepared to do most of the learning on your own. That said, immunology is a beautiful field and Shiv is a captivating lecturer, so if interested in learning immunology, 169 is the way to go.
The class is a lot of self–learning, so you must be self–motivated to learn immunology and seek other resources.
This course is incredibly interesting, and is increasingly important for every biologist/future doctor to understand. You should know that a lot of the course will feel like rote memorization, but memorizing the terms early on will make it much easier to learn the more challenging concepts later in the course. The section component of this course is absolutely vital in your understanding.
Shiv is an excellent professor and I cannot speak highly enough of him. His lectures are engaging and he is so willing to interact with students. That being said, there is a huge disconnect between him and his lectures and the rest of the course. The TFs handle all the course logistics, and they are generally mean and unhelpful. The sections are super variable in what you learn, and so it is incredibly unclear what content is important and what isn't. There is no common place to find material and the TFs are incredibly unhelpful. They do not hold office hours and cannot help with PSets. The final exam was INCREDIBLY unrepresentative of what had been taught and emphasized, asking niche questions that the answers are literally not even certain among the scientific community. The class is minimal weekly work, but it is not worth the stress.
I did quite enjoy this class, although I spent much of my semester very sick and didn't feel like I could really appreciate it as I needed to. You really need to stay on top of lectures and the HMX course – it piles up SO fast, since there's a massive amount of content to learn and details to know. Know the names of all the proteins and cells, and do properly study for the exams. It's really worth taking if you have an interest in immunology. I learned so much about how immunology works, and I did enjoy the process. Shiv is obviously known for his poems in class, and they're good, but they're not really something that adds or detracts from how understandable the material is. I didn't super vibe with his lecture style, since I'm not a super visual person, but section was extremely helpful for understanding.
This course is overall so great! Shiv is an amazing professor and spends the last ten minutes of each class theatrically reciting poems he wrote about the day's lecture (AND he has them fully memorized!). It is so clear that Shiv loves teaching and truly cares about his students–– he is such a special professor. The material is so fascinating but the course organization is poor. Definitely READ THE TEXTBOOK! I spent the two days before the final reading about 700 of the textbook and it really helped tie all the concepts together. I don't recommend doing this, however. Instead, keep pace with the course and read one to two chapters each week––it will really help you understand the lecture material and get more out of the class. The midterm exam was not too bad; however, it was only one long question so if you got one part wrong, you lost a significant amount of points. The final was really hard, honestly, and touched on concepts we didn't learn in section or lecture. Study well in advance and know as much as you can for this.
Do not take it if you have previous experience in immunology.
This course is a good course! You will learn a lot about immunology and the load is very appropriate. The only thing to be cautious of is that the TFs really shape a lot of the course and that resulted in a great deal of variability between students in their success.
It is a great class that I highly recommend students to take. Make sure to keep up with all the material and to focus on the main ideas that are often repeated throughout the semester. In studying for the midterm and final exam I would highly recommend to focus on the topics and material reviewed in section and to make sure to create detailed and clear notes that you can go back and study on throughout the semester!
This class is certainly not for everyone. Be warned going in that Dr. Pillai's lectures are more free–form than other science classes and there are very few course materials provided. The responsibility for learning is entirely on you, which can be a good or a bad thing. If you like the freedom of having fewer assignments that are each weighted more, then this is a good class to take. However, don't expect anyone to hold your hand and walk you through the material: you're going to be mostly on your own, and there's a bit of a sink–or–swim mentality present. If you swim though, you'll find this course is actually fairly easy and requires nothing more than attentive participation in class and the ability to quickly process lots of new information.
While this class provides a fundamental overview of immunology, the lectures can be difficult to understand and there are very little assignments to prepare for the final. While I generally liked the class, it needs some fundamental tweaks and changes to truly become an excellent biology course that doesn't suffer from teaching pedagogy issues.
