The readings are not light by any means –– about 80–100 pages a week. Besides that, the field reports and lectures/sections are fun and engaging. The final paper assignment got changed to a 5–7 page reflection on what we learned in the course, if it hadn't don't know how well I would have done in this course given that the readings were so dense and I skimmed the majority of them. This would be a good course to take pass/fail imo.
This is a good broad overview to the topic of income inequality in the US. Different weeks focuses on different subtopics which keeps things interesting, but it is definitely more of an intro course as we didn’t dive into any topic very deeply. The lectures included a lot of examples from the field which was an engaging and informative addition. Overall I found it to be a pretty chill and easy course.
TAKE THIS CLASS. such an amazing experience and such great teaching staff

I believe that everyone should take this course. The content of this course changed my perspective on many of the institutions and systems we come into contact with on a daily basis. Professor Wooten is also an incredible teacher.
This class is great if you're looking for a general introduction to systems of poverty in the U.S., but make sure you actually want to do the field reports. Having to go out into the world to answer a specific research question is not easy and can be uncomfortable at times. It's a good learning experience, but for me that didn't outweigh the annoying, frustrating, and sometimes uncomfortable process of having to figure out what you're going to do for your fieldwork and actually doing it. Aside from the fieldwork, though, I really enjoyed this class. Tom is approachable and kind, and he cares a lot about teaching. The lectures were interesting and illuminating, though don't expect them to be super in depth since this class is basically a survey of a bunch of different topics. TL;DR: interesting and easy intro class with good teaching staff, but make sure you want to do the field reports.
