Be on top of the reading and assignments. Really try to understand the subject matter. Come with tons of questions and challenge Professor Langer on what she teaches––she loves to be challenged and it only goes to deepen your understanding of mindfulness.
Langer is wonderful as a human being, but I regret taking this class. If you are actually interested in psychology, you would benefit from taking a more rigorous course. In general she made a lot of sweeping statements with very little empirical evidence (or supported by one study, or anecdotal evidence) and every lecture was very repetitive – the same concepts repeated in different permutations every time. If you want something with very little work where you can just talk in a chill environment, then this is nice, but it isn't very life–changing at all, and at times dubious. It does make you think differently about how you approach negativity in your life, but at times these arguments are taken almost hilariously to the extreme (she has interesting opinions on sexual assault and grieving for victims, not necessarily backed by empirical evidence or very accurate or reasonable). One thing I really like about this class, however, is that the conversations are very laid back and Langer encourages students to disagree with her (which a few students actually do every class, which is interesting).
This class is very interesting and can help you improve your mindset, especially if you're someone who overthinks a lot or cares about others' opinions. For example, you get to learn about accepting uncertainty and finding positivity in negativity. Applying what you learn in this course can reduce unnecessary stress and help you view life in a more open–minded way. It also doesn't have a heavy assignment load, so it's a great way to learn while allowing you extra time to get adjusted to the college life and environment.
This course will open your mind to new possibilities and ways of thinking to help you live the life you want. Some concepts take a minute to wrap your head around and things are repeated but it has a lot of beneficial knowledge. The instructor is super fun.
Do not take this class if you are not interested in psychology. It is a lot of work for a freshman seminar, and if you are not interested in this topic, it is a pain in the ass with all the readings and papers.
you have to be open to extracting life advice out of this course, sometimes it can be a little hard to grasp but there is really great advice entangled in professor langers work and how she thinks
This class is a total gem :) The workload is very light and class itself is quite relaxed. However, it is structured as a purely lecture–based course with lectures that become more and more repetitive over the course of the semester. A few weeks into this course, it may start to feel like you're learning the same things over and over again. If you need an easy, light–workload course to take this is a great one, but there are definitely other courses out there that will be more engaging and educationally stimulating.
This class is a great class to take that is stress free and will help your life beyond the classroom.
