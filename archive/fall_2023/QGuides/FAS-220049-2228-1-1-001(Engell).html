What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class was excellent: a well–curated selection of literature from a time period that seems to be quietly departing from Harvard syllabi, thoughtful discussions, and above all Professor Engell's instruction –– both in leading the discussions and in offering feedback on his students' writing. I'll emphasize the second: barring extremely poor writing, teachers at Harvard will often ignore an essay's style and focus solely on its content. But feedback on style is critical to becoming a better writer. Students in this class know to expect writing marked thoroughly with black pen, from the familiar brackets (meaning something like "are these words necessary?") to suggested rephrasings or thoughts on punctuation choices. These are not minutiae: such small improvements to prose add up to significantly better writing. I'm grateful to have taken this class for the discussions and readings, but also for the opportunity to improve my own thinking and expression.
