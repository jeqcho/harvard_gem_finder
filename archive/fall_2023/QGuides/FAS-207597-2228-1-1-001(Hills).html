I really enjoyed this class! It was definitely challenging in its content, but everyone in the class is there to help and support you. Professor Hills cares deeply about his students and guides you through every step of the journey. He is really approachable, and I really appreciated when he stayed back (even sometimes past midnight) to answer our questions and help with our learning. The TFs and the CAs also cared about our learning, and it was a really welcoming community. Definitely a super rewarding experience!
ES 152 was great, especially because of the staff! Gage, Georgios, and the whole team really brought this class to the next level. I'd say the staff is what makes or breaks this class, and Gage is definitely the one you want!
Come in expecting a lot of work since beautiful circuits and a good understanding takes time, but have fun with it and go straight to Prof. Hills or a TF if it starts getting frustrating. It can get hard, but the staff is there to support you and more often than not have a nice way of explaining things so that they make sense.
Make sure to go to office hours! Extremely helpful – everyone in the course staff wants you to succeed in the course.
This class is mainly for Electrical Engineering majors. To understand all of the material is not easy, and so I would really only recommend it if you are majoring in electrical engineering or are really interested in the topics.
The biggest thing determining wether you like the class or not is the love for what's being learned. Otherwise, the amount of work can be overwhelming if you don't love it.
This course is an amazing circuits course for EE concentrators; however, it is a ton of work. Be prepared to be spending the majority of time in your semester on this course to thoroughly understand the material. The labs are well designed, but take a long time to complete. Professor Hills really cares about his students learning which makes the class very fun.
