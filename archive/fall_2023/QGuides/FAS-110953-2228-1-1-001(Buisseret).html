This class can be very conceptually difficult. It's important to trust the process and do the best you can.
This is an excellent class. I think you should only take this if you really want to engage with formal work, but if you do, you will definitely learn a lot.
From my experience there is a wide range of workload for this course depending on how much experience you have with math. I'm an applied math undergrad, which meant I had a decent amount of experience with probability, real analysis, finding extrema of functions, etc., which helped a lot with the problem sets, particularly the early ones. Experience with games and thinking strategically is helpful as well.
This is a great course to take as an Applied Math in Gov concentrator. It's fascinating work, and I really left the class feeling like I could tackle interesting and complex problems.
