This is a really great class that does a deep delve into Ukrainian history obviously, so if you're interested in the pre–amble for the Russo–Ukrainian war or just Ukrainian studies in general it's a great background course. It also explores a ton of other things though that you can think about more broadly– nationality and culture, imperialism and colonialism, European studies beyond what you'd learn in an American high school (i.e. France, England, and WWII). It's super interesting and the professor is very sweet and always delighted to answer questions.
This course is an excellent way to learn about Ukrainian history and to think about questions of nation.
I really enjoyed the class! Prof Plokhii is wonderful and obviously extremely knowledgable about the material, so I was able to learn a lot. There is a lot of information and readings to get through though, so keeping up with that was a little bit difficult at times. However, if you're willing to put in the work it's definitely very rewarding and very important knowledge!
This is a fantastic class for anyone who even has a little interest in Ukrainian history or really the history of Central and Eastern Europe in general. It will change your understanding of world events by looking at them from a perspective not often covered in traditional history books and will open up your understanding of the history of the region. Interesting readings and discussions, as well as fascinating lectures.
I would say that the course is not necessarily naturally engaging and requires a certain amount of individual investment. It’s definitely possible to get a lot out of it, but the setup of the class as well as the professor’s style of teaching allows a lot of students to be pretty disengaged and can make the discussions and classes feel not very dynamic.
DO THE READINGS!!! Professor Plokhii is great and definitely participate. Ask questions when you are confused.
I think this is a very interesting course, but it is certainly not like other History courses. There are only two exams (unless you choose to write a paper), so the reading is more for your own interest and is not assessed at a detailed level, and there are no weekly assignments. However, I really think is challenging to learn in this class if you do not the reading (or at least learn about the week's concept), because otherwise you will be lost in lecture. Professor Plokhii has a wealth of knowledge, but his lectures can be a bit rambly, and are challenging to follow at times. This class is I think best geared to people who want to really engage with the material and Professor Plokhii, and not those looking to dabble in History.
Very difficult, do not recommend unless you already have a good understanding of Ukrainian history.
Take this course! Plokhy is the best and he will help you learn a ton about a very important country at the gates of Europe!
Do the reading with an eye towards understanding the big picture, you will not remember every detail, but it's crucial to know broad strokes in order to get the most out of class.
