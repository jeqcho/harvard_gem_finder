This class was interesting and I feel better off for having taken it, but I have my reservations. Prof. Sag–Parvardeh was really great and kind, and the entire teaching staff was really supportive. The psets were easy, short, and infrequent (too easy in my opinion) and this class was definitely a gem. This class achieved its goal in covering a broad range of introductory linguistics topics. However, this course definitely had its problems. The pacing of this class seemed really off. Lectures were attendance mandatory but they crawled by so slowly. The content that we covered in 2–3 classes (2.5–3 hrs) was covered just as easily in a 1–hr section. In fact, section felt like the most important part of the class. Additionally, there were often questions that arose during section or from psets that garnered confusion among the majority of students and never actually had a clear answer.
I learned what you would expect to learn in an intro to linguistics class. This information would be useful were I to continue pursuing linguistics, but since I took it as an elective, I find that the information is not particularly important to me and the content didn't intrigue me enough to justify taking it. The PSETS are very short, but some questions might take some asking around/figuring things out. I would probably only recommend taking this class if it fills a requirement.
I would tell future students to take this class. I think that this is a really good class for people who are interested in humanities but also like concrete material to learn. There is very little subjectivity to this class, except in the morphology unit. I like it a lot.
The work is not too difficult and the concepts are so cool! The class itself can be a bit dry but the actual material is fascinating even if it's not something you want to focus on.
A light and decently done course. You don't go too deep into anything (it's a little frustrating) and you don't get into the philosophy of the thing, but by the end you'll know all the foundational words in linguistics. Plus, the professor and staff are nice.
Take this class! Yagmur is a great professor. She's enthusiastic about the material and also really wants to get to know the students in the class. She takes as much time as needed to make sure everyone understands the content. It's not necessarily a gem, as the content can be a little confusing at first, especially if you've never taken a linguistics class before like me, but it never gets so confusing that you're struggling. Go to office hours! They're kind of essential in order to really understand the PSets. But if you have Yvette as your TF, you'll honestly look forward to going to office hours. She's really friendly and great at explaining anything you're confused about. One office hour per PSet is plenty to completely understand everything. Overall, the courseload is extremely fair. I never spent more than 3 hours working on a PSet, and most weeks it was closer to 1.5 hours. Above all, the content is very interesting if you're interested in the building blocks of language, and what makes language what it is. This class convinced me to make linguistics a secondary! Highly recommend.

The course staff was relatively present. I would definitely recommend the class to anyone who has a passing interest in linguistics but doesn't want to spend much time doing work.
