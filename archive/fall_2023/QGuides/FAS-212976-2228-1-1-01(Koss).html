What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This class is great if you’re interested in modern Chinese history and don’t have a background. Sometimes the lectures feel unstructured, so I recommend having a clear timeline of the major events in your head.
This course is an exceptional tour–de–force teaching the history, theory, important milestones on the Cultural Revolution. The Cultural Revolution is not an easy material to teach, and Professor Koss skillfully planned the materials and presented a very helpful blend of narratives about the Cultural Revolution and key historical backgrounds, as well as first–person accounts and primary sources that helped me gain appreciation of the experience of the Cultural Revolution from a variety of perspectives. We have analyzed important events from the perspectives of political elites, mid–level cadres, grassroots students and workers. It showed a picture of the Cultural Revolution that goes significantly beyond the traditional simplistic depiction of this important historical event. We've also discussed the contemporary significance of Cultural Revolution and its enduring presence in Chinese political discourse, including in the neo–leftist movement.
Easy elective to take for gov concentrators. Nothing exciting or worth writing home about, but an easy enough class where you won't really mind falling asleep to the professor's lectures.
Having an academic foundation about the Cultural Revolution is paramount to developing a legitimate understanding of China's political/historical landscape. Professor Koss puts together a great selection of readings and lectures and splits up the time period to ensure you get a great comprehension on its precursors, major events, and aftermath.
Take this class, it was my favorite this semester! The readings are totally manageable and you should do them to participate in lecture (which are really interesting!). Besides 8 weekly reading responses, the only assessments were a quiz and an analysis of a primary source which you can use in your final paper. Professor Koss' lectures are always great and the TFs were wonderful.
It’s a great course, Professor Koss is wonderful. Would recommend.
Take this class! You will undoubtedly enjoy it. It is worthwhile not only for any East Asian Studies major, but also any Harvard student who hopes to be able to engage with modern China in the future with an understanding of its history in hand!
Regardless if you are interested in the Cultural Revolution, you should take this class to gain an understanding of one of the world's largest rising powers. It truly helps you grasp the political and historical dynamics of Modern China and learn about something that still so deeply concerns many families. Do the readings and start your final project early. You will be amazed at your transformation by the end.
This is a great class if you're interested in the intersection of political science, history, and China. Although the title of this class is the "Cultural Revolution," the material covered in lecture actually spans from early communist China to the beginning of the Reform and Opening Up. Lecture can sometimes be boring, especially if you're not particularly drawn to China. Professor Koss, however, is an excellent lecturer and wonderful person. Definitely go to his office hours. The most important element of this course (grade–wise at least) is the final paper. There is lots of autonomy in choosing your topic, and you can basically write on whatever you want as long as it relates to the Cultural Revolution.
