This is a fantastic course and Chuck's enthusiasm for plants through a holistic lens is contagious. If you like or love plants, take this class, and if you don't already like plants, this class will make you like them!

Take this class. Manageable workload with great instructors and fascinating exploration of plants and their significance to people. (Especially to knock out science requirement!)
This was one of the most fun classes I have taken at Harvard (as of my senior fall). Chuck Davis is a saint, and puts in a ton of effort to make this an entertaining (as well as shockingly informative) class. Great field trips and reasonably enjoyable sections, and the actual assignments aren't too taxing. While the focus of the class is mainly botanical, it's very accessible to humanities folks as well. For OEB types, it's also a great introduction to the Herbaria.
This is a really fun class – labs are really hands–on and involve everything from flower dissections to touring a chocolate factory or brewery. Lectures are also really informative (though guest lectures can be hit–or–miss) and there's something for everyone.
This class is a fun introduction to the world of plants and their uses, and the course is good for developing fundamentals in botany and understanding modern science. The lectures are interesting and engaging, and the labs every week are real highlights of the course.
Don't underestimate the "quizzes" (actually midterms) because Chuck (Prof. Davis) likes to make them difficult. Take full advantage of review sessions and make sure Chuck provides sample questions before the quizzes because they were extremely useful for the second two quizzes. Don't try to cram for this class; make sure you're asking questions along the way so you don't have to relearn everything right before.
This course was definitely fun and engaging. I took this class with an interest and background in plant bio, and I appreciated the fact that this class introduces you to the fun side of ethnobotany and economic botany, such as how psychedelic plants work and the wonderfully weird ways in which humans have used plants since the Holocene. The professor (Chuck) and the TFs (Sophie, Justin, and Yan) were all very friendly and approachable. Beware, the quizzes are no joke; often they asked very particular questions that I felt were sometimes unnecessarily technical and only tested my rote memory skills, rather than how well I engaged with the course material. The scheduling and timing of assignments was also a bit disorganized; I remember that sometimes the schedule alternated between having no assignments one week, then having a quiz, two writing assignments, and a lab the next week, for example. I definitely recommend this course; it's the right balance of challenging and engaging (never overwhelming), but sometimes it can be frustrating as well.
This class is great!! The curriculum is a bit all over the place, however, it covers really interesting areas in plants, food, and culture. I would highly recommend it if you are even remotely interested in plants! It's also a great distributional requirement because it is not TOO science–heavy for those who may not be as well versed :) We went on some really cool field trips too– and the TFs and Chuck are an awesome people to just chat with.
The section/lab is really helpful for understanding the materials brought up in lecture. The writing assignments every week get annoying, but they aren't difficult. Chuck is amazing, and really loves teaching about this topic, and the TFs are stellar.
I absolutely loved this course. The teaching staff was truly fantastic, and Prof. Davis is perhaps one of the most down–to–earth professors I've ever had the pleasure of learning from. The course material is fascinating, too, and highly relevant to many modern issues we face (invasive species, sustainability/output in agriculture, regulating medicinal plants, etc). If you are at all interested in the intersection between plants and humans, I could not recommend this class more. You also have the opportunity to develop your scientific writing technique, which is a skill far too often forgotten or de–emphasized in other science classes. The final project is a lot of fun (especially if you plan ahead), and the teaching staff is extremely helpful both in and outside of class.
Take this class!!! I learned a lot but even more than that ~~~ the course instructors generate such a genuine enthusiasm for the content that made it so enjoyable. Definitely recommend.
