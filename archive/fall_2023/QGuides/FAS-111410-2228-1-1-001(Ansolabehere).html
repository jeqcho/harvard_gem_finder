What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This was a great class and one of my favorites I took this semester. The professors were very attentive, knowledgeable, and committed to the course and students. They facilitated great class discussions. The course is an interesting and fast introductory survey of the main important topics, theories, and readings in political science. There were a ton of readings but most of them were useful to understanding the theories and being able to participate in discussion. The weekly response papers were helpful to digesting the large amounts of information in the readings and preparing for class discussion. The final paper was a lot of work but a great exercise in doing an analytical literature review on a topic related to political science of our choosing. Highly recommend this course for PhD students interested in an immersive introductory political science course.
Honestly, don't take this class. It was really demoralizing and useless.
You must be extremely careful in taking this class. So much of it depends on who the professors are and how they set the syllabus. If you are not an American Politics student, I would not advise taking this field seminar unless the teaching staff changes and the syllabus is reworked. If you have an interest in American Politics, you might be better off taking classes at MIT to prepare for generals.
