Having engaged with human studies 100% of the time throughout high school and college, I wanted to deviate from human exceptionalism and engage with non–human behaviors – and to some degree – cognition. This course epitomized that understanding and enlightened me to the complexities of behavioral development. I now have a clearer understanding on primatology, biology, psychology, and neuroscience questions and will better apply broad concepts to my understanding of humans and myself. The class itself is not difficult if you put the energy into it. The teaching staff is wonderful, and you will have no problem adapting to the methodologies of primatology.

The course is a comprehensive introduction to primate social behavior and scientific methodology more broadly, taught by some of the nicest professors at Harvard. The lectures covered a vast amount of material and were approachable for someone who has never studied HEB. The regular quizzes helped keep me engaged and I loved the assignments. I highly recommend taking HEB 1330 for those who are interested in primate social behavior or if you are just looking for an introduction to real–world science.
