As far as I know the difficulty of 131 can vary across years, but this iteration with Prof. DeMarco went smoothly and relatively easily. The class closely follows Munkres, which is a good companion; however, I felt as if there were some additional topics that the course could've covered, particularly with respect to algebraic topology (case in point, Prof. DeMarco was away the last week for a conference). Psets and exams are manageable and reasonable; the final was take–home which was a big plus. Overall, this was a basic but fun intro course, and made topology more or less the most fun it could be.
Many people describe topology to be very “abstract” and “difficult”––although I do believe that many of the things that I learned this semester were one of the most difficult objects to comprehend in my math career, I think professor DeMarco in Math 131 did a really, really nice job making these topological concepts very intuitive and almost natural.
I took this class after having taken Math 25a/b, which roughly covers material from Math 121 (Linear Algebra) and Math 112 (Real Analysis). Since this course teaches topology from the very definition of a topology and builds from there, I think the only hard prerequisite is a proof–writing course, like Math 22a+/100–level math course. But I HIGHLY RECOMMEND having taken real analysis––having a level of background dealing with the topology of R^n will allow you to appreciate the course a lot more, since you will be generalizing these concepts to a lot of wacky spaces (for instance, what does it mean for a function to be continuous between two topological spaces which aren’t necessarily metric spaces?). I also think it’s difficult to realize the significance of topology without having seen it in action in real analysis. As for the group theory prerequisite (Math 122), I’m unable to speak much about it since I have not taken a group theory class yet. Hefty group theory is only used only in the last quarter of the course, so I think it’s the softest prerequisite. That being said, I feel that my understanding of the later topics in algebraic topology are a little superficial, so having a background in group theory will definitely help you appreciate topology at a deeper level.
The class is very well taught, and professor DeMarco provides extremely intuitive descriptions for topological concepts––which I would’ve not understood just by reading the textbook. Unlike previous math courses that I’ve taken, there is a very significant portion of the class where professor DeMarco explains topics using drawings, instead of writing out a long proof on the black board. Professor DeMarco really emphasizes the understanding of the subject, which is great for a subject like topology that is difficult to comprehend, but she does expect us to read the textbook outside of class so that we get acquainted with the language and the formalism that is used in the proofs. Some proofs in class might be a little abbreviated, or even skipped, so it’s important to refer to the textbook for details.
The topics you learn in this class are extremely useful and general––and will appear everywhere in math, especially if taking analysis/topology courses in the future. I highly recommend taking 131 with professor DeMarco to fulfill your Geometry/Topology requirement, not only because it’s the most foundational topology course, but because professor DeMarco teaches you to think like a topologist, and will equip you with a very strong understanding of topology.
This is a very fun math class! If you enjoy mathematics and want to explore a new side of mathematics that will play with your brain and thinking in new ways, take this course!
Overall good course. The material was interesting and useful, and Prof. DeMarco is super smart and cares a lot about the students. All star TF roster as well. The midterm was super easy, and take home final was not bad at all. My primary complaint is that the psets were a bit rough around the edges, many times there was a problem that was unreasonably difficult and that even the TFs struggled to answer. I wish that either the professor or TFs looked over the psets before releasing them to avoid this sort of issue. Also, expectations were often unclear for psets, since I was never sure if I needed to rigorously prove something or if I could just draw it.
I would absolutely recommend this class! The material overlaps a little with ideas from real analysis and abstract algebra (first and second halves, respectively), so a little background there would be good. I found Professor DeMarco wonderful – she was both extremely enthusiastic and extremely intelligible. On the whole, I felt that the pace of the class was pretty good – I would go to class, lose the plot halfway through, and then grasp the material the next time. I would recommend reading ahead. On the whole I am really happy to have been in the course.
Hard and lengthy psets, but overall an excellent class. Topology is cool! I wish it was a little less grindy, though.
I thought this class was extremely challenging, but if Professor DeMarco is teaching it again, take it –– her office hours are invaluable! I would ask really dumb questions until I understood what was going on, and she was not only incredibly patient but also amazing at pinpointing my misconceptions. If you don't know anything about topology, it would be worth watching some YouTube videos or reading through sections 12 and 13 of Munkres before the first lecture because you will be bombarded with new definitions, and you can fall behind quickly. At least for me, definitions were not immediately intuitive, so I had to spend a lot of time studying. It's a challenging class but not totally unmanageable if you ask for help when you need it.
