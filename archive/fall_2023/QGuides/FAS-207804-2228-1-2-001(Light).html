This is easily the best class I've taken at Harvard, hands–down. The class content is paradigm–shifting and transformative, Professor Light is brilliant and warm and makes the space so generative, and the small seminar format meant that I developed very close relationships with my classmates. Everyone in the class really wanted to be there and was highly engaged every week, and so it was easily the most intellectually productive and generative class space I've been in here. It is definitely a fair amount of reading, often challenging to understand, but it wasn't a huge workload for me –– just weekly discussion posts, a short essay, a take–home midterm, and a (very generative!) final project. This class changed the way I think about care, family, love, work...basically EVERYTHING, and it's already changing the way I live my life. I can't recommend more highly.
This class is fantastic! Don't take it if you can't commit to doing the work/readings––it's definitely a heavier load because it's a high level seminar. Professor Light is brilliant, so great at community building, and an over all great teacher. She supports everyones growth and is clearly so glad to be facilitating these conversations.
Take this class! Professor Light is so caring about her students and so smart, you will learn a lot and grow a lot academically. Plus just a really cool woman to spend time with and learn from– and she's accessible outside of class. I have taken a couple of classes with Professor Light and will continue to because her teaching and the academic spaces she creates never disappoint! The class is very student–directed and Professor Light takes into account what students want and shapes the syllabus accordingly, something I really appreciated in her class this semester. Additionally, our class itself had a great sense of community I have not found in other academic spaces. It will also totally change the way you think about labor and the content is super interesting.
take this class!!! the course does a good job balancing more concrete issues and theory, and it's the first class i've ever taken that has made me genuinely interested in theory in any sense. plus our classroom community is so beautiful and wholesome, and prof light is amazing. the course readings are also so interesting that i've recommended many of them to family & friends.
This is a great course and a really critical foundation if you are interested in labor or the social relationships of capital. I would definitely recommend this both for the readings and for the classroom environment and the kinds of discussions that can occur.
