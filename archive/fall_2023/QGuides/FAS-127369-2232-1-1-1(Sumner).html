Discussion is important to this class! If you're willing to do the readings and participate, you'll enjoy this class
As someone concentrating in a field that has very little in common with Celtic folk/myth, I believe that this is one of the most important and worthwhile courses I've taken during my time at Harvard. I have grown so much as a writer and as a critical/analytical thinker. The class readings and discussions were some of the most fascinating and thought–provoking of my college career.

Additionally, Professor Sumner is without a doubt one of most remarkable scholars and teachers I've ever worked with. She cares so deeply about her students and about the subject matter. She is also one of (if the *the*) foremost experts in her field within Celtic Studies—learning from someone like her is one of those "only at Harvard" opportunities. This course reminded me why I love learning so much.

I would recommend that you take advantage of all the resources and opportunities that come with this class. Go to office hours. Read some of the optional readings. Chat with the grad students. Participate in class. Engage deeply and critically with the course material. You will take so much away from this course (and have so much fun) if you really dive into the readings and stay curious.

I truly believe every individual would be better off for taking this class and for learning from someone like Professor Sumner. I think our world would be better off if our future leaders took part in a course like this. I wholeheartedly recommend Celtic 106 to any and all prospective enrollees—you will not regret it. You will become a better student, thinker, and person. You will engage with absolutely fascinating material and learn from one of the best (in every sense of the word) professors at this institution. This class is the transformative Harvard College experience we were promised; courses like Celtic 106 are the reason I came to Harvard in the first place.
You will have an engaging, thought–provoking, and often delightful experience delving into the world of Gaelic Scotland! This class was a powerful way to both learn about a time and place different from my own context, and reflect on my own ways of being and knowing.

Professor Sumner creates a welcoming and engaging environment, with different ways to explore folklore stories and practices.
Such a fun and unique course that is a great option to take as an elective
Take this course. It's fun, interesting, and easy.
This course was SO fascinating and enjoyable. While it was really interesting to analyze folklore for the first time, most of all this course helped me realize just how much I love learning about new cultures, and it was incredible to hear about the traditions of Gaelic Scotland. The readings were interesting and the professor and TF were such incredible resources!
Celtic 106 is a phenomenal course! I am not a Folk and Myth student by any means, but I took this course after having it recommended to me, even though it meant taking 5 courses in the semester. It was absolutely worth it. Professor Sumner is extremely kind and knowledgeable, and the readings are truly so enjoyable. It was such a neat experience reading fairy tales or traditional songs or a specific hex and then examining it in class and discussing it with a more scholarly approach. There were only three essays along with the class readings, and these were fun essays. I never felt like it was an assignment, but more a chance to academically write about my hot takes on fairy tales (the dream). All in all, I would recommend that people take this course, even if you are not a folk and myth student.
