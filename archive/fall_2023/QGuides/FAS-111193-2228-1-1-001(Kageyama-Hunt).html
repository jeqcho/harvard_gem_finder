What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
While Japanese BA may seem intimidating as a language that is completely different than English, it is an engaging, and fun course. The class moves quickly, but the professors are always available to help if you need it. I feel accomplished after the course, and I absolutely recommend it.
Take the course! You won't regret it. Excellent teachers and materials make this a worthwhile experience at Harvard. Plus, you'll be improving your fluency at an astounding rate!
This course is a fantastic introduction to the Japanese language, taught by very attentive and kind Harvard instructors, if you can commit sufficiently to the course load.
It's a really fast–paced class, and sometimes the amount of vocab / grammar introduced can feel overwhelming but the teachers are really good at making sure you understand it by the end of the course and the grading is really really lenient so overall it's a wonderful class.
All instructors are so kind. They pace the class well but give students enough time to take in new material. I would highly recommend!
Take this course!! This course is absolutely incredible. The teaching staff are so patient and helpful, make themselves accessible outside of class, and seem to genuinely want every student to have the best time possible learning Japanese. You learn a lot throughout the semester, but there are SO many resources to help you –– from extensive office hours to the well–organized, resource–full course website. You receive so much helpful feedback on major assignments (which always have preliminary drafts), so you're never on your own with those. There are also a bunch of fun enrichment activities (like making onigiri) that make this class really special. Overall, it is one of the best classes I've ever taken, and I highly recommend it if you're even thinking about learning Japanese.
This course helps you to improve Japanese at an impressively fast pace. It is always fun. Every class is highly participatory, and you are never lectured to for long periods of time. It starts from the very basics and as long as you attend class you won't get lost.
I would highly recommend Japan BA if you are interested in learning Japanese. The class is particularly diverse in the students who take it. My class came from a variety of the graduate schools, and even undergrad students had diverse interests and reasons for learning Japanese. I came in without having learned Japanese before, and although the learning curve initially is a bit higher than those who have been exposed to the language previously, I truly did not feel hindered or at a disadvantage. This was the most well–thought out, well–planned, course I have ever taken. You will be amazed at how much you learn each week. The quality and joy for learning that Kageyama sensei and our awesome TFs nurtured throughout lecture and section was truly incredible.
Another important aspect of the course to note is that there is an abundance of additional resources that perfectly align with the course on the Canvas page. Take advantage of them! The textbook and accompanying app for audio (you can download it for free) matched lecture perfectly and were excellently prepared as well. I have never felt so supported in a course and unlike other courses where the book or reference material doesn't match class, all the units are very systematic and if you ever get confused or need clarification, the book is guaranteed to have the answers to your questions. The course staff also holds "Nihongo Chat Hours" every week if you want more practice speaking or just one–on–one time to review.
The course is one you need to dedicate time to, but it will be a rewarding and fun experience. You'll see how deeply Kageyama sensei and your TFs are dedicated toward giving you an authentic, rigorous, and eye–opening experience in learning Japanese language and culture. They are some of the most professional and caring course staff I have had the pleasure of working with, and it shows in how much all of us in class felt we wanted to do our very best for them. Overall, taking Japan BA was an invaluable experience and it's one of those courses that has deeply impacted my continued interest in Japan and for other cultures in general.
