The exams are not easy. They require you to have a very good grip on all topics covered in class, and they are closed–book with no notes allowed. You have to remember a lot, so start reviewing the material early. You should expect to spend at least 1 full day studying for the midterm, and at least 2–3 full days studying for the final.

Problem sets are of moderate difficulty. Sections and office hours are a good resource, and there are a lot of time slots for these, so it's easy to get help. The late day policy is also generous; you likely won't have to worry about deadlines.

Knowing how to write proofs is an absolute must. The course provides some basic introduction to writing proofs, but it's not nearly enough if you have no prior experience. You should also know some basic Python for the coding portion of problem sets.

Overall, the class is not great and it's still being polished. But you have to do either CS 120 or CS 121, so just take CS 120 and get it over with (again, unless you care about theory).
