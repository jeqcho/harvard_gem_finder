Professor Segel really cares about this class and has put a lot of energy into making it what it is! The format of the class involves bringing in an outside speaker (or several) for each session, including Harvard administrators, professors at the various schools, and outside experts and business leaders. We went on several field trips (including to the Seaport District, the Arnold Arboretum, and Houghton Library). Readings include theoretical texts, background material, and Harvard Business School cases. The class will probably be interesting for anyone who is curious about cities and urban development (and the interdisciplinary issues associated with them), but it is geared toward a real estate/business approach. The workload is light, but make sure to do the readings so you're ready to take advantage of and learn from each week's seminar!
