Comments
This class was great!! I chose to take this one instead of the FAS developmental psych because I was looking for a less intense class. I still learned all of the concepts while having minimal works (just 3 essays). The TF was generous with grading and gave really good feedback on the assignments. You can really get as much out of this class as you choose, readings and lectures weren't really mandatory besides when drawing on readings for the assignments. Overall really chill and interesting class and cool to take class at the Ed School. Would highly recommend if you're interested in the topic!
This is a really great course. Low stress but you learn a lot. There were three papers that do require thought and effort, but other than that just manageable levels of reading. The professor and teaching staff are great and class is very enjoyable. I also learned a lot about children––this class will definitely affect how I interact with and understand kids. As an undergrad, this class is very doable for undergrads.
This class content is very interesting and the three papers are manageable. Overall, I would say this course is not taught in the most engaging manner, but it is still a great way to fulfill a psych or MBB credit.
If you are interested in child psychology and the experimental design of psychological studies, this is a helpful course. The readings are interesting, although not very necessary to complete outside of class since they are all reviewed during lecture. The lectures can feel a bit dry sometimes, but Harris is definitely knowledgeable and enthusiastic about the subject material. The essays are actually very interesting to write, and allow a great deal of flexibility and creativity. Feedback is also very thorough. The professor and tfs are very accessible, and also offer opportunities to engage with research. There is no section component, and overall the workload is light and assignments very doable.
