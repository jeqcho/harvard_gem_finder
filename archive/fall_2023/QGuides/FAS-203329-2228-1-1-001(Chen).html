This class is challenging, but you will learn a lot. The professors really want you to understand the material and will work throughout the semester to improve their delivery to maximize learning.
This is a course required for PHS PhD students, though students can elect to waive out of the coursework and just sit for the exams. I would encourage everyone to take the full course rather than just the exams. While this means you will have to complete problem sets, they are extremely helpful for the exams and also help you to get a deeper understanding of the course concepts and ultimately the concepts underlying modeling that you will do in your research. The problem sets are not that much work either in comparison to other courses, such as the Epi sequence. The exams themselves are fair – they can be long, but you are well–prepared to do well on them if you spend time with the course material. In addition, you will get great exposure to a truly wonderful teaching team. Never have I experienced such a group of professors and TFs so strongly dedicated to teaching.
