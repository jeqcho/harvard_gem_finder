TAKE THIS SEMINAR. IT IS THE BEST COURSE I HAVE EVER HAD, MAYBE THE BEST COURSE I EVER WILL HAVE. You get many chances to explore the herbaria and the museums of Harvard, and they are awesome chances, because we have so much in the collections. The professor is incredible, and discussions are often extremely interesting. I took this course because I worked at a museum and because I'm interested in museum work in the future, and I do not regret it at all. The workload is manageable and the content is pretty digestible. This class is a hidden gem.
I would fully recommend taking this class. I really enjoyed it!
Take it!! This course was so interesting and also quite fun. We spent a lot of time exploring the collections of the Harvard Peabody museum, the herbarium, and the museum of comparative zoology. It was such a cool experience to see the behind–the–scenes of what goes on at museums because it's something that most people never get to see. Chuck is a great professor and was always willing to listen and hear our ideas. He knows so much about the class content and gave us some really fun, low–stress assignments. One of my favorites was a homework where we made a specimen collection of a plant anywhere near Cambridge, then identified it and made mounted specimens. Overall this was definitely one of my favorite courses. It's low–stress, super interesting, and has an amazing professor!
Chuck is amazing and so knowledgable, and weekly classes were really engaging and interesting. Activities included pressing our own plant collections and touring the Natural History Museum's exhibits and some behind–the–scenes areas. There was a bit more of an emphasis on plants than I anticipated (Chuck is Curator of Vascular Plants so it is his speciality). I do not have much experience with plant biology, so some scientific papers that were assigned for homework were a bit confusing to me (but a good challenge). Overall, I would recommend this class. I learned so much in a hands–on way, and the final group project (creating our own Natural History Museum exhibit) was a perfect way to end the class.
Take this seminar!! I was looking for a fun seminar to ease into my freshman fall and I have absolutely no regrets taking this seminar. Chuck (Professor Davis) is an absolute gem of a professor. He is so kind and passionate about what he teaches. I looked forward to having this class every week. The discussions are quite engaging and I loved the classes we would explore other museums on campus. Could not recommend a class more.
Take this class if you have any interest at all in ecology, evolutionary biology, conservation, museums, cool animals or plants, or anything of the sort – this class will be an absolute joy. Chuck is an incredibly knowledgeable and very nice professor and was a pleasure to work with for a semester.
I 100% recommend this course. Due to the lengthy freshman seminar block, it's important to choose a course that is interesting and dynamic. This freshman seminar is hands on and collaborative. The amount of work assigned is optimal for understanding!
