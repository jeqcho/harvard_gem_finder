I wish I had more time for this class, because it was go great. That being said, you can also take this if you have a packed course load–– you'll just get out a bit less. I was taking this completely for fun as a CS concentrator, and I'd wholeheartedly recommend it as an easier course that's still extremely interesting (instead of taking a gem). The lectures are recorded and amazingly clear and interesting, and the homework does not require OH and is very straightforward and interesting. There is no busy work that isn't interesting. The course structure is a good balance between learning and application.
I'd recommend NEURO120 with reservations. The workload is minimal, barring the final project at the end of the semester, which requires time but can be done in a group. The quizzes after each class (graded by completion) are sometimes tedious and easy to forget, but they are short. Although the lectures are, at times, not super engaging, the course material is itself very interesting for students interested in computational neuroscience. However, to take full advantage of it, you will have to study the material outside of class using the resources provided. Ultimately, I'd recommend this course to students who want a lighter workload and a foray into comp. neuro. Note also that computational neuroscience covered here is largely about analyzing firing patterns of neurons and systems neuroscience problems; if you're interested in translational neuroscience, this isn't covered much in the course.
I loved this class! Professor Batty is great!! She is enthusiastic about the material and always happy to answer questions! You should definitely take it if you are curious about the use of computational models to understand the brain and vice versa! The material is very interesting and engaging. There are also a lot of resources if you are less familiar with linear algebra and python.
This is a good course for students who want broad exposure to the field of computational neuroscience. The HW assignments are based in Python programming, and involve completing code to analyze datasets from actual computational neuroscience problems. The mathematics are covered in enough detail to illustrate the concepts, but the math is not the central focus of the course. I would recommend it for anyone interested in comp neuro but unsure if their ready for the more advanced courses offered. The workload is very reasonable, and Ella is a good teacher.
This is a quite nice course. The material is good for an intro course but Batty spent a bit too much time on the math background knowledge. Most things are explained rather well but you will still find optional reading very helpful. As long as you are familiar with basic programming, the homework would not be very hard. Overall, this is a nice course to take.
The course is super informative! Things gradually get more difficult throughout the semester though, so just make sure you are understanding the material.
This class is a great intro to comp–neuro. Lectures are fun and engaging, content is broad and applicable to many interests including generative models and machine learning. Ella is a great professor who is approachable and helpful if you have questions
