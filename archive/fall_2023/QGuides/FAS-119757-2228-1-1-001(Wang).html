What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The entire Chinese language program at Harvard is fantastic, and I really recommend taking Chinese classes, even if you feel that it is a large time commitment! The program is well–structured, and you truly do learn a lot.
I had taken every Harvard Chinese class up through 140b, and 150a definitely felt like a big jump up in difficulty and in coursework. This class is a lot of work (preparing for dictation 3 times a week, homework assignments, tests every two chapters, at least 3–4 presentations throughout the semester, plus also the final research paper). I really liked the final research paper assignment – it's broken into 3 chunks over the semester, so it's really not intimidating and was easy to get done, while also helping me grow a lot as a learner. Class time is mostly spent in free–form discussion about course material.
If your are really interested in learning Chinese, seize every opportunity you can on campus to do it, this course included! It's a strong course.

People make 150 seem super hard because you have to write a Chinese "thesis," but it's actually just a 3,000–character essay that you work on throughout the semester. The teachers also give you feedback on it, and overall, the class is super flexible. I thought it was comparable time–wise to Chinese 140 and was maybe even a little less because it only meets 3x a week.
