What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Neoliberalism is so taken–for–granted and pervasive as an ideology that it can be near–impossible for the average person be lucid and objective about this matter without taking a course like this. For me, ANTHRO 1716 was truly eye–opening and constituted a turning point in my own thinking about the world and social sciences. The only downside is that the revelations can be kind of depressing at some times.
Best part of course is in–class discussion, which does require doing all the readings. You won't be devastated if you miss a week of things because they're mostly standalone, with the exception of some of the more foundation stuff early in the semester. Really informative class and exposes students to big picture ideas that other courses gloss over because of limitations of big lecture style courses. (Also keep in mind this is not an economics or social planning course and does not offer fixed solutions to perceived problems).
Make sure to get started on the readings early, they are amazing but you pretty much have to read a book a week so you're going to need time!
The professors are fantastic.
This course is so extremely intellectually stimulating and rewarding. Take it if you want a thoroughly fulfilling academic experience. The teaching staff are so knowledgable and invested in every student and their opinions. The reading is quite heavy but you get out what you put in to this course. Cannot recommend it more highly.
Take this class–– it was one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. It's pretty much all readings and then a twenty page paper at the end, so the workload throughout the semester is flexible: you will get as much as you put in, and if you do the readings carefully this class will actually change the way you think about the world. If you are scared of the paper at the end don't worry. You can write it on so many different topics it will 100% be a meaningful experience to think through something you care about; 20 pages is not as long as it seems.
