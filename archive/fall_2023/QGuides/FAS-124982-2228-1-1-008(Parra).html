Ask questions, practice the course content with purpose and fulfillment, and seek various ways to practice Spanish outside of the classroom (speak to your pets in Spanish; practice ordering a meal in Spanish; etc.). While the course content in instructed in a phenomenal and professional manner, your ultimate ability to learn a language rests upon you and your own intrinsic dedication. If you enter the course with the desire to LEARN Spanish, you will do extremely well.
Be careful about the section attendance. The class punishes students really severely (half a letter grade) for each absence beyond 3.
good introduction to spanish, moves at a very fast pace. Highly recommend setting time for studying and memorization, if you do that it'll be easy. It's not a class you can never study for.
This class is not a gem, but it's manageable. It's four days a week, so it's not a small time commitment, but you get used to it over the course of the semester. Also, having a good preceptor for this class is so important to having a good experience. If you don't like your preceptor after the first week you're in the class, switch if you can. My preceptor was wonderful, but without that being the case, this class would've been much harder. Also, practice out loud in class. Participate. I know it can be nerve wracking or sometimes you just don't feel like speaking, but you'll honestly get so much better and more fluent the more you practice out loud. And definitely dedicate ample, actual time to studying for the quizzes. Don't go into them thinking you'll just know it because you've practiced 4 days a week during class. You have to study the content on your own time, just like any other class, because the quizzes have a lot of writing. It won't go well if you don't study the structures and the different words you need to build sentences related to the content.
This class was probably my favorite class of the semester, although it didn't start out that way. In the first week, this course made me absolutely terrified. I came in with no prior Spanish knowledge, and the Spanish professors speak exclusively in Spanish (from day one)! Be prepared to be challenged linguistically, and remember that YOU decide how much you get out of this course. You can probably do fine (and maybe even get a better grade) if you stick to the simplest sentence structures, vocabulary, and grammar, but you will learn a lot more if you push yourself to use more complex structures and vocabulary, even if it means you might miss a few points on a test or interview.

The work in this class is not overwhelming, but you should probably write down or at least make note of all of the due dates at the beginning of the course. They are not evenly spaced out, so you might find yourself having no work outside of class one week, but having work outside of class every day in another week. Also, if you aren't sure that Spanish is right for you, you might consider how much time you're willing to devote to learning the language (well). The Spanish department requires a 95+ to get an A for the GPA, but this is not the same for other languages (93 for an A is Harvard "norm," and those 2 points really can make it all the more challenging to get an A). Keep that in mind.

Above all, the small class size (about 15 students) and amount of time spent (four days a week) has made this course feel like a family. Our instructor, Andrea, has been nothing short of fantastic. I really admire her for all the work she puts into this course (because she is also incredibly busy outside of this course!). I feel confident that I could communicate effectively in a Spanish–speaking country or community after just one short semester of Spanish. Be prepared to work hard, but remember that the end goal is simple: learning as much Spanish as you can in order to communicate with other Spanish speakers! Don't get too caught up in the homework or exams, and remember that this is a new language. You aren't meant to know everything from the beginning. I got really frustrated a few times this year, but something has really clicked in the past few weeks and everything has really started to make sense. This class has inspired me to pursue a Spanish citation, and I've also been able to find some really amazing Spanish music that is now always on repeat while I study or just listen to music for fun! Buena suerte!
