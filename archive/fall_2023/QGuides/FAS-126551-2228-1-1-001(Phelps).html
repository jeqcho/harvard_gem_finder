The actual content of this course is really interesting, however the lectures are dry/boring and the exams are very nitpicky with wanting you to know specifics of specific experiments. I overall would recommend this course if you want a broad overview of cognitive neuroscience or want to do research in the field, but I would warn that the lecturers aren't very engaging which is a shame because the information is really cool.
Take this course!! If you're debating between psy14 and neuro80, I highly recommend psy14. I loved going to lectures for this class and always found them to be very engaging. I think psy14 is better to take over neuro80 if you're more interested in the behavioral/psychology connections in neuroscience over the more biology–intensive elements.
This class is hard. preparing for exams ahead of time is necessary if you want to do well. There are no trick questions and the content is pretty straight forward but there is a LOT of it.
This class was super interesting – make sure you study adequately for the midterms and the work will be very manageable.

This course is very interesting and will help you learn about the neural pathways underlying cognitive processes.
Content: I found the material super compelling (at least in the first 8 weeks). Much of the material we covered seems to be the subject of active research, so the material is not as beautifully packaged as it would be in an undergrad CS or pure biology class where the results are pretty old and at least well established. I found this to be a really cool thing. The profs and TAs are in the midst of the experiments which end up on the slides. As someone with no psych background nor any related long term goals, I found this very fun. Sections were great and the recordings basically contain the exam solutions.
Workload: There was a short assignment (20 min including reading) where you proposed a question for the author of a current research paper which cumulatively less than 10% of the grade. The bulk of the workload is a few hours of studying for each of the three tests (including a final). One cannot do well without studying (i.e. memorizing the lecture material). There is also a short writing assignment. I found the tests useful and necessary for my internalizing the material.
This is a good course that provides interesting ways of considering cognitive neuroscience. The lectures are interesting and although the readings can be long, there are opportunities to ask authors of papers questions. The TF this semester (Emma) was fantastic and went above and beyond to make sure that students understood the content, being very available for questions, and recording review sessions for students.
Please attend section as extremely helpful sample questions you might see on midterms or exams will be provided. This component is a life saver that's helped me tremendously to study for assignments.
Not the most engaging or life–changing class per say, but really interesting material; worth taking if you want to expand your horizons or are interested in neuroscience!

This course is fantastic. I took it Pass Fail and was not able to devote much time to it, but it was still one of my favorite courses and I learned a lot. If you are good at memorizing, this course will be easy. Tests consist only in answering questions about the material covered, so if you could remember all of the content (and it is not too hard to learn the content and remember the general facts if you study), then you will easily pass. You learn a lot, and even more so if you do the readings. It is material that everyone should learn at some point in their lives, and you will not regret taking it.
