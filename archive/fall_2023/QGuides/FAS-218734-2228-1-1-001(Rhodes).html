The readings are dense but they are so rewarding. You will learn so much!
This course was simply life–changing. Discovering the various ways that race has been historically constructed and thus molded into the hierarchal system we see today is a concept that ALL students should immerse themselves in; not just Government students. Additionally, the knowledge which Dr. Rhodes possesses is inimitable. HIs lectures were clear and concise, and he consistently allowed for students to share their thoughts at any point. Simply stated, I cannot recommend this course enough.
This class is fascinating and will really change the way that you see the world. I do advise that you take this course. I really enjoyed it. Just know that you may not receive the grade that you want. This course is not easy. The criteria for getting an A are rather harsh and you will not get assignment feedback in a timely manner. Nevertheless, the content is so interesting, that it can still make it worth it.
Discussions in class were always a lot of fun and interesting. Dr. Rhodes facilitates good discussions and creates a really welcoming atmosphere in the class. While it is listed as a Gov class, it definitely feels more like a Sociology/Anthropology class, so be sure that that's something you enjoy. The papers are pretty light, which is nice during the semester, but if you're looking to write a senior thesis, a different seminar might be more helpful for that specifically.
This course will challenge your idea of race–– Professor Rhodes always leads an engaging discussion, although readings may be a bit difficult at times
A fantastic choice for a Gov seminar!! The readings are meticulously organized, and there are four essays (three 5–page essays and one final 10–page essay) instead of the usual big final 25–page research paper, which distributes the work of the course much more evenly. Dr. Rhodes is a sweetheart and keeps seminar very engaging with lots of discussion about every reading. (So do make sure to do the readings, participation counts.) Feedback can be a bit slow but overall it's a great class. You will learn a lot about race and race history from all over the world.
Take this class for a pretty chill Gov 94 with super interesting topics! The is a lot of readings that are dense but if you pace them throughout the week it's quite manageable. Dr. Rhodes is a great discussion facilitator with very interesting and expert takes on the subject matter so you will learn lots and he really engages with students' ideas personally.
