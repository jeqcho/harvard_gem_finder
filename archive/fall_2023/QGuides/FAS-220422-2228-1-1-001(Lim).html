Comments
It's a great course –– take it if you get in, no question. Dennis' pedagogy and the range of course assignments/activities are wonderful, and the opportunity to not only see Lynch's work in full, but also watch the common myths about one of our most important auteurs shatter before your eyes is a total delight.
If Julia Sharpe is your TF, proceed with caution. Also – do the readings and watch the films, you'll get much more out of the experience of them yourself than you will in discussion.
Take this class! It's so rare at Harvard to find a course dedicated to a single artist, and this was an incredible one. Professor Lim is as close to a Lynchian expert as it is possible to become, and the film and reading pairings are wonderfully generative. Regardless of your familiarity with Lynch (mine was limited), you will learn so much about his work and about the nature of art/film in general.
This class is amazing. The course materials truly allow for an understanding of the colours and flavours of Lynch. However, the class itself was, as many AFVS classes are, a bit pretentious. And when someone said something that the course leaders did not agree with, it was very palpable. The class felt very tense, to say the least.
You will get the most out of this class if you're interested in film theory and if you're a fan of Lynch and Dennis Lim. If not, I would avoid.
Here are some benefits I got from the course:
– forced me to watch every David Lynch movie in a theater
– some insights into how to think about fuzzy terms like weird/real/unsettling
– got a chance to make a short film at the end of the course

Reservations:
– the productiveness of group discussions (the whole course is group discussions) are largely dependent on fellow students, so some classes are more fruitful than others
If you enjoy David Lynch or more "artsy" types of films, this is a great class for you. Some really interesting discussions and you get to go really in depth. Dennis is great.
This course is so cool. Dennis and Julia are great instructors who challenged every student to think deeply and differently about the films we watched. I never knew where class discussions would lead (in the best way), and I left every class feeling 1. like I learned something and 2. so excited for class the following week. This course was rigorous, but when I put in the work and time to watch the assigned films (even the ones not screened for us), it was one of the most worthwhile courses I've ever taken. It's clear that Dennis and Julia put a lot of time and care into the syllabus. If you have the chance, TAKE THIS CLASS!
I've taken a wide range of classes at Harvard and this one lands solidly in my top two favorites. The way the course is set up allows you to think about Lynch's work without the distraction of meaningless assignments. Professor Lim places a lot of faith in the students to come to class prepared and having done the necessary work, and I will say that I think students should only take the class if they're willing to do that work in good faith. This means doing the readings, watching the additional films, and meeting in your small groups. I took it during a fairly busy semester, but at no point did the classwork become an annoyance—it was always something I looked forward to. Fantastic class and a must take, especially for Lynch fans / film studies students.
