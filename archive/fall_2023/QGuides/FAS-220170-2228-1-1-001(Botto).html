This course was poorly done. Professor Botto is very nice and seemed to be trying her best, but the course did not meet my expectations. The lectures were often poorly thought out, and a lot of the content lacked depth. To understand the content further, like the psychological roots of morality, the content would have needed a much deeper dive or to go into seminar form. Instead, the content was kind of brushed over without a lot of depth and didn't really provide a lot of answers. I often found myself biting my tongue as an upper–level psychology concentrator because concepts were poorly presented or brushed over. The quizzes, having to memorize paper findings, were not helpful for my understanding. The reflection papers were a waste of time and applying the results to my real life often felt contrived. Overall, I appreciate Professor Botto's enthusiasm, but this course needs severe improvements.
