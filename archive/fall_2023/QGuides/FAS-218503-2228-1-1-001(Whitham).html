Take this course!!! You will have such interesting discussions, and will take away so much in a low–stress environment. Mr. Whitham is an excellent professor, and our class grew very close over the course of the semester. Put this as your first choice seminar!
Will is awesome.
I would take the class if I were you, but I would ask you to come into it with an open mind. Everyone's viewpoint at one point or another is going to be challenged, and I think that will be a good thing. 10/10 class.
If you enjoy discussion–based courses and want to contemplate terrorism and power, take this class! It is fun, low–stakes, and engaging. Dr. Whitham has a great sense of humor, and you will always be welcome!
Definitely take this class. I myself am a stem concentrator, yet found this class very interesting. Not only did we learn about all these politically violent organizations, but we also learned about their reasons, their organizational structure, and beliefs. Ultimately, this class teached empathy towards others by showing the other side of terrorist organization to see them from the inside through their own members. The class and instructor do not lead you towards a certain belief. Do come with an open mind and ready discuss matters with other students.
Please don't be afraid to speak your mind about issues you care about, especially when you feel like your perspective is not being heard in the classroom space. Dr. Whitham is understanding and approachable; he's very willing to understand your perspective and make every student feel like our voices are valued. Sometimes, this class will test your emotional capacity as these discussions are difficult yet necessary, but don't be afraid to speak your mind or take a step back.
You'll get out of this class what you put in. Try to do the readings and reflect thoughtfully. It can be a very rewarding experience.
I would strongly encourage any first years to take this seminar!! It's very low on workload which makes it easy to manage alongside other courses, and any work you do sets you up for the discussions in class. The class is 2 hours, but as long as you are interested in the topic it will fly by. The other people in the class always have amazing experiences to share, and as long as you come to the class with an open mind and willingness to share your own perspectives, you will truly get so much out of it... definitely a freshman seminar gem!
I highly recommend this class especially if you are interested in any forms of political violence, colonialism, terrorism, etc! Definitely brush up on a few historical events before though to be able to experience the readings to their fullest.
PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS. Dr. Whitham a fantastic instructor and the readings for this course are carefully picked to develop interests in political history and cover a wide variety of viewpoints that are challenged and debated really well in class. Dr. Whitham never attempts to impose any viewpoints upon his students and teaches how to appreciate diverse perspectives and cultivate empathy very effectively. You will also find great friends with similar interests/drive as you!
