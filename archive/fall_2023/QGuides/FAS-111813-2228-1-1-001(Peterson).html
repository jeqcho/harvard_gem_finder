Take notes on the videos before class, and you'll be golden. Honestly, I didn't open the assigned books a single time throughout the semester because the videos are so in–depth, and really are all you need to succeed. Follow the syllabus closely so you don't accidentally go over the wrong information for the following class. Also, make sure you are proficient in the class's court case (which should be italicized in the syllabus). Reading a quick summary should be all that you need for class. Remember that this course opens with the idea of American exceptionalism, so it makes sense for it to close with it. Ponder this idea throughout the semester and especially toward the end. Having a history with comparative government will help.
If you are a student looking forward to learning about the US politics in the US college, this is the perfect course! I am an international student who did not know anything about the US politics, and this course really helped me a lot to learn about the history, structure, and unique characteristics of the US government! The Professor and course TFs are also wonderful people, the best people I have ever met in the school perhaps, so no worries about the workload or assignments for this class! The everyday quizzes could be a little stressful, but as long as you watch the videos beforehand, you would be fine! The best course I took in my first freshman year.
