That the readings are difficult but the assignments and class activities are extremely engaging.
This is such an interesting class, I absolutely recommend taking it! Dr. Love is the best and guided us through so many amazing discussions about classical literature and art, race, ancient figures, philosophy, and so much more. The projects are really interesting if you pick something that you want to learn about, and the group presentations are so fun to listen to.
This class answers the question in its title in several different ways. A good background in the ancient Greeks and Romans is helpful. It's not a lot of work if you plan ahead (only 3 major assignments in the term), and the seminar structure was great for facilitating discussions.
Super fun and Professor Love is great! The class is both thought–provoking and engaging without being overly burdensome courseload–wise.
Take this class! Professor Love is an amazing teacher who leads incredibly interesting discussions every class. A large chunk of the workload is reading, and some of the readings are confusing, but Professor Love goes over the readings in class during discussion if any of the texts were too unfamiliar. The four pass–in assignments are not very difficult. If you are looking for an intriguing, discussion–based, intro into classics I would highly recommend.
