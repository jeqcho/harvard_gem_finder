Highly recommend if you are at all interested in political philosophy ! Lectures are super interesting, Professor Risse is great at engaging students with the material, and papers are not too difficult to get through.
This course will be more work than you expect.
This course felt super relevant to better understanding where influences on our political world have come from. I didn't have any experience going in, so it was a bit challenging and not part of my main interest, but I still am really glad I took the course. The assignments and final exam were super fail and though I took it pass/ fail, it is manageable. There were a lot of readings assigned however.
I would recommend this course to future students. There are three fairly short papers to complete throughout the semester that help you think deeply about the subject matter from lectures/readings, and although philosophical writing may be challenging especially for STEM concentrators, it is a useful thought process for formulating your own opinions on the most just economic setups for society.
There's a lot of really important information and knowledge that can be gained from this class. There is both engagement with the Western philosophical canon (Smith, Marx, etc.) as well as the premier thinkers in the area of economic and racial justice (Du Bois, Coates, etc.) Some of the readings are dense, but Mathias makes them very digestible through informative lectures. It may seem intimidating to take an economic philosophy course, especially as a GenEd, but it's going to be easier and more straightforward than you expect.
Definitely take it if you need to satisfy this gen ed rec and you're interested in topics about government and society.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Seriously, it will change your opinions on how the American economic system is organized and expose you to new, traditionally marginalized perspectives. The essays are designed to help you think through the material, and the readings are manageable. It will help you figure out where you sit along the economic thought spectrum. Lectures are interesting and tie into real–world issues. Can't recommend it enough!!
I really really recommend this class! You learn about very relevant topics in political philosophy/economic thought from Marxism to classical liberalism which are topics that have much misinformation around the internet, so it was very useful to have it all broken down and understand these topics well. The professor is also incredible.
Economic justice is a mental jerk off fest. The conclusion to the course is that is no solution, and really everything on the matter has been said and done, with every "new" idea just being rehashings of old ones.
TAKE IT, TAKE IT, TAKE IT!! Best course I have taken in 3 years at Harvard. You will learn about things that are actually important and useful for the rest of your life.
The course is a very fulfilling and rewarding course. Especially if you are interested in political and economic structures and theories, this course will introduce you to different perspectives and solutions that people have to tackle economic injustice to reach economic justice. After this course, you will definitely think differently about your circumstances and the circumstances of others. However, the writing component of the course can be fairly difficult. If you have little to no experience with writing in philosophy, I would recommend taking an introductory philosophy class first (though it is not necessary!). I would recommend reaching out to your TF for help with writing if this is a concern for you. Despite the difficulty with the writing aspect of the course, it is an amazing class and I do not regret taking it.
This course covers a large range of thinkers throughout history on the subject of economic justice. If you love reading in this area and generally the topic of economic justice then you'll enjoy this class.
I highly recommend this class for anyone who values justice and seeks to make a positive change in this world. The readings are dense and the course is challenging, but is is extremely rewarding.
