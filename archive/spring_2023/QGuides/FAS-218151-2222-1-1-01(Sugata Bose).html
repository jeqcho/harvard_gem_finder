This is a very well–taught and accessible class for people with any interest in Southeast Asia. No prior background in history or serious knowledge of Southeast Asia is necessary coming into the course. It also gives you a good chance to read some Southeast Asian literature and a good deal of flexibility with the final project.
This class is challenging, but fascinating. I learned so much about an under studied area of the world. If you have any interest in the politics, history, economy, or culture of Southeast Asia definitely take this course!
Savor every moment in this class! Professor Bose & TFS are truly unique, compassionate, intentional, and understanding. They have a passion for history, particularly emphasizing the visibility of SEA on a global scale, such a pleasure and an opportunity to have learned from them.
I strongly recommend this course. Professor Bose is very passionate about the course and he infects students with that passion. Also there are not a lot of similar courses at Harvard, so this course is a gem!
Excellent introduction to Southeast Asian history!
Take this class. You will learn a lot about this understudied region. The course content is engaging, as we study colonialism, national development, independence movements and more across Southeast Asian countries. The professor is great and and the workload is not hard. This class is truly a place to learn and widen your perspectives about the world.
I would encourage everyone to take this course
If you have any degree of academic interest in the Southeast Asia region, this course is essential. You are not likely to find a better course that will give you the introduction to the essential history of the region you need before you move on to more specific work.

Reasonably interesting and chill class on a cool subject matter not taught elsewhere at Harvard. Definitely the kind of class where you get out of it what you put into it

This course offers you the opportunity to understand the broad movements that unfolded in the region from colonialism to modern day. It is particularly important for American students to take this course and understand their own country's impact in the region and colonization of the Philippines, an issue that many Americans do not know about in my experience.
