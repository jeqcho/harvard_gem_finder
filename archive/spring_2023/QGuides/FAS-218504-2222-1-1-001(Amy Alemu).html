What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a fun and engaging exploration of how archives tell stories. There are a lot of very cool class visits to museums and libraries on campus, where you might get to see materials such as flyers and articles from student protests at Harvard in the 1960s. Seeing these materials puts current social justice issues in perspective and helps me to think about the present as something that will soon be enshrined in history. The final project was super interesting and fun to work on. I am inspired by my classmates' projects to pursue the creation and study of art in relation to my other academic interests.
Definitely take it and you’ll want to be an activist too
Dr. Alemu is the absolute best. She's so passionate and intelligent– being able to learn from her was one of my best experiences at Harvard so far!
Take this class!!! not only is it an amazing class for people interested in activism or social justice movements in general, but it is an amazing exposure to Harvard's on campus buildings and resources –– especially for future papers and if you are interested in potentially writing a thesis. this class was definitely as great as it was because of Dr. Alemu (but unfortunately I do not think you will get the privilege of having her because she is moving to another school), but if you do decide to take it, I hope that the next professor will hold true to the intents of this course and regardless, I think this class is surely a great decision.
It's very fun and you learn a lot. One hundred percent worth it if you're interested in activism and social justice.
