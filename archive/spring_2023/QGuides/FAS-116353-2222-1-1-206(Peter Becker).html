What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The material in this class is difficult but Dr. Becker has tremendous expertise. He will hold you to a high standard but is also very understanding and listens to his students. The books you will read in this course are amazing.
This is an incredible class that will challenge you to improve your writing and analytical abilities.
As a black student, I was apprehensive about taking a class on Slave Narratives taught by a non–black person, but Dr. Becker approaches the texts with such respect and genuine care for the authors of the narratives, the characters of the novels, and the students living with the repercussions of slavery. It never felt like he was explaining the black experience to me or anything like that.
This expos is certainly one of the more difficult ones that you can opt for although I found it to be manageable if you adhere to the central themes and adapt your literary analysis to what is focused on in class. Be prepared for reading four entire novels and copious literary analysis that you will find similar to rhetorical analysis in AP Lit. If you are interested in more about the history of slavery or synthesis based argumentation, then this class is not necessarily geared towards you. There is not a limit on the number of A's that can be awarded to students although that does not mean it is easy to achieve. If you put in the work for this class and actively participate, you will likely grow in terms of your literary analysis capabilities.
Immerse yourself in expos, you will grow and thank yourself in the future!
I would recommend students to take this class if they are interested in history, literature, slavery, and slave narratives. It is not overbearingly difficult and Dr. Becker does a great job guiding and helping each and every one of the students and is always available to meet if we needed assistance.
It is a challenging class, but the knowledge you gain makes it worth it.
Be prepared for a rigorous writing experience and a rewarding learning experience.
I would greatly recommend this class to any incoming first year, but be prepared to read 4 narratives/novels and incorporate the reading time into your schedules. Writing and argumentative skills are sure to improve!
