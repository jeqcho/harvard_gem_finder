What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Iain is really great. He is available for discussion, open to any topics, and really inclusive at any moment. Also, the papers are well selected and this class provides students with a great opportunity to survey the field.
I highly recommend taking this course, especially if Iain is teaching it. As a PhD student from outside IR, I was not sure how much I would learn from the course or how difficult it would be, but I found that the workload was manageable and that I had a lot to learn from the theories and models in IR for my research on domestic politics.
This was a nice introduction to international relations. Iain is a fantastic and extremely kind professor with an extensive knowledge of the field. The assignments and reading load are very reasonable. The topics overviewed are a bit scattered, but this likely reflects the overall structure of the field. Iain's mini–lectures and slides on select aspects, subfields, and histories of IR were particularly useful. Overall, I would highly recommend taking this course with Iain to fulfill the Government Ph.D.'s new field seminar requirement or if you are interested in conducting research as a scholar of IR.
