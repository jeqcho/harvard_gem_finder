Really cool class! The material is interesting and Professor Mazur is excellent.
Professor Mazur is great! You should take a class with him if you have the chance — he really focuses on the main ideas, letting the textbook and homework fill in some of the details. I thought his style of teaching gave me a better overall understanding of the course material without sacrificing any precision. He supplements the lectures with great lecture notes, which come in handy when reviewing for the final. They're very clear, and much less dense than Marcus (the textbook). Also, don't be worried about the in–person final — I actually think it's better than a take–home exam. It forces you to review the whole course without being super stressful, and as a result I think I learned the material a lot better!
By the end of this course I came to realize that many of the theorems learned are very interesting and deep and that algebraic number theory is fascinating. However, this Math 129 was badly structured and that prevented the course from being enjoyable at all. First, we were assigned weekly reading from Marcus' "Number Fields" textbook. I don't have anything against reading some pages every week, but the fact that lectures covered the same material as the readings did (sometimes slightly less, professor Mazur often skipped details, focused on irrelevant concepts, or just presented too slowly) made going to lecture basically useless. It also didn't help that Marcus was an EXTREMELY BORING read. I believe that the course could have been structured in a way that avoided the reading assignments and instead present in class only the key results without going into all of the boring details. Second, homework was badly designed: everybody complained about the length of it and I found many exercises to be there just for the sake of maintaining the assignments at a certain length. On the bright side, the presentation part of the course was not bad: I learned some very interesting applications of algebraic number theory while doing research for my presentation. Overall, I would recommend the course for its material, but I would avoid taking it with professor Mazur, unless he changes the course structure. It was a shame that the course wasn't on par with Mazur's expertise in the field.
Mazur's great. Algebraic number theory is hard but by the end I felt I understood it. There is a sit–down final which is unusual for the department but it wasn't that bad.
Challenging yet rewarding class. We follow Marcus's Number Fields quite closely and almost all of the homework problems are textbook exercises, so going through the book will give you a pretty good idea of whether or not you'll enjoy the class. Mazur teaches the class pretty well.
The material is great. But I am not sure if I would recommend this class in its current form. The class in general was rather disorganized, and the lectures were not very good. Prof Mazur's teaching is rather like swiss cheese: for the most part you follow along the solid bits, but every so often you'll run along a gaping hole that is just never explained and completely glossed over. At first glance you'll feel like you'll understand the material, but dig any deeper and you'll realise that there were so many portions of a proof that were just never explained properly or completely missed. This would be fine as an occasional instance, but this happens many times every lecture, and at some point these holes just make the lecture very hard to follow properly.
A good class! However, a surprising amount of prereqs; a pretty solid understanding of rings + fields + Galois theory + a little complex is very important. Barry is pro.
