Take it if you can! It's a bit more work than other seminars but it's interesting and you can get a lot out of it if you're interested and you put the effort into the readings. Dr. Schacter knows a lot and class discussion was often interesting. You do two research papers which can be interesting if you choose a topic you like. It counts for the Psychology concentration and it is very low stress which is great.
I would recommend this course if you are interested in learning about memory, but not as an introductory class for psychology.
This seminar was extremely interesting if you’re motivated by questions of what it means to be human, cognitively, and how we come to be functional beings. I gained a fundamentally new POV on the role and functions of memory, and actually have a much deeper appreciation for its function despite examining “sins”. The subject matter inherently hits home sometimes but is very interesting for that reason. Schacter is SUPER knowledgeable and fun to talk with!
Take this seminar!!! The workload is incredibly manageable and the book Professor Schacter wrote and based the class on is both accessible and interesting. He is so knowledgeable about the field of memory and the seminar was truly such an enjoyable experience.
