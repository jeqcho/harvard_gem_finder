What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is less about the ecology and more about the practice of conservation, so it is worthwhile to take if you've already taken OEB 65 or have experience with conservation biology, but expect to learn more about history and strategy than about on–the–ground conservation techniques or wildlife. The workload is reasonable week by week, but be aware of the long final paper and start in advance! Prof. Foster is very willing to meet outside of class and even go to the Harvard Forest, so be sure to take advantage of that.
I appreciated the open–ended nature of the final assignment and the course gives you room to explore your own interests. At times, the material can feel specialized, but overall the course provides a useful perspective on New England as a region.
Great for anyone with any interest in the environment, opens your eyes to new career fields as well
