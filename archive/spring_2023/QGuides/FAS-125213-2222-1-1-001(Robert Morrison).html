What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an absolute must–take if you are considering studying music at a higher level.
Please take this class!!! If you're remotely interested in taking a music theory class, TAKE 51B. Landon is AMAZING and he really engages every single student. His enthusiasm and attitude toward certain topics are just wonderfully contagious. Kate, the TF, is brilliant and truly cares about every single student. Yes, this course is a bit of work, you'll spend time stuck on assignments or confused on certain topics, but it's super rewarding when you finally get it. Practically everyone in the class seems to do well grade–wise (A/A– range), so it was nice to just focus on the actual learning instead of being preoccupied with fighting for a grade. The teaching staff definitely makes sure you succeed!
This is a great course for all sorts of people. If you have less of a musical background, you might have to work harder and struggle a bit, but if you take advantage of the instructors' office hours, you'll do just fine.
Take this class! The homework assignments can seem like a lot, but truly enrich your learning experience. The class does a wonderful job of engaging students beyond written music theory and teaches them skills such as improvisation and composition. Also, the Parker Quartet!!!! performs everyone's compositions at the end of the year, which is a once in a lifetime opportunity. The professor and TF made time for each and every student, and the course was extremely welcoming and enjoyable :)
51B is a great option for those who feel they have some theory knowledge already and want to dig a bit deeper. It seems to be the point of departure for topics that go beyond straightforward roman numeral analysis and voice leading. The workload is significant but still very manageable (one assignment per week). The content is predominantly classical, but also examples were drawn from a variety of genres. Overall I would highly recommend it!
