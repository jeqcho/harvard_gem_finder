Would 100% recommend writing a thesis in the History department – they give you amazing support, and will make sure you meet your deadlines and don't have to rush everything in the final week. All the instructors and TFs are extraordinarily generous. Well worth your time, it will be something to look back on as a major achievement during your time at college!

THESIS!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!! This was one of the most rewarding experiences I've ever had, and while it's hard, it's SO worth it.
The thesis process was the highlight of my college career. It was the most difficult, most rewarding, and most transformative experience in my time as an undergraduate. Having a clear idea of your topic or argument at the outset is unimportant; rather, desire to think critically and become expertly acquainted with some general area of interest are the only requisites. If you keep these in mind, you will not regret writing the thesis. The seminar is extremely supportive and useful as you progress, and the instructors will answer any and all questions. The History Department puts its full weight behind your enterprise, including providing resources and flexibility to focus on the thesis, in order to ensure your success. I would highly recommend this class to any student that feels serious about some academic interest and wishes to experience the full rigor and richness of academia.
Definitely use the resources they provide for you, whether that's the professors or your peers. Also can be super stressful; practice a lot of self care when you're writing the thesis.
Your mileage may vary depending on the peer group you end up receiving (I think this year a bunch of people in one peer group won the Hoopes and literally nobody else did) but overall I really enjoyed the thesising process. If you're reading these Q reviews and deciding whether to thesis or not I would certainly advise you to thesis (and the relationship with your advisor is much more important than how the numbers in this class shake out).
