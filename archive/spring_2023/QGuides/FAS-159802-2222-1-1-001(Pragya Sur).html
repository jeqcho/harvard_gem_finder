What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The pacing of the course is quite off; Pragya goes too slow for most of the semester and decides to speed up near the end. Otherwise, if you are an undergrad, this class teaches you a lot about whether you should care about grad school.
Stat 213 is a very rigorous and mathematical treatment of statistical inference –– it is far more mathematically intensive and detail–oriented than 211. To me, the topics didn't feel super interesting or important, although I appreciate that they are foundational. There are also no course notes or recordings, so keeping up with lectures is important.
Fantastic, well–run course in all aspects.
To those considering taking the course:
The concepts in this class are harder than Stat210 or Stat211, but the problems on the concepts are less about noticing the trick, per se.
To those planning to take the course:
Go to sections! Because there were only 5 psets, it becomes hard to gauge your understanding of the material solely by going to class. By going to sections, you can both improve your understanding and realize where you are possibly lacking.
Good continuation to Stat 211. Helps bridge you to modern research topics. Strong mathematical preparation in measure theory, real analysis and linear algebra is recommended
