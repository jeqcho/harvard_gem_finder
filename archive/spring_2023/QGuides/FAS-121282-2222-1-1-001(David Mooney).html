Make sure you save the time to commit to this course as it will be very worth your effort – highly recommend.
If you're considering this course, you're most likely a bioengineering concentrator who has to decide between this course and BE 121. If that's the case, you should absolutely choose this course over BE 121. I learned so much about the current state of the art in tissue engineering from this course, and the lab experience has been super valuable when connecting with future employers in biotech. There are no weekly psets (!) and the exams are all open Canvas (!!), so it's much much less stressful than BE 121, and the lab doesn't ask you to sell your soul to a serological pipette like BE 121 does. The course staff are all amazing people who are so dedicated to the course and helping you succeed in laboratory and on the exams. If you're at all interested in tissue engineering or bioengineering on the cellular scale, you should absolutely take this course.

The course load is very heavy, especially if you opt for the laboratory component of the class. There are 2 Midterms, Journal club reviews every week (which are critically graded), laboratory deliverables every week (also critically graded). You have to present a journal club paper once in the semester and have to evaluate all the others. The midterm examination is quite challenging and requires critical thinking and a very good understanding of the topics taught in class. Be prepared to devote a significant chunk of time to this course if you are planning on taking it. The takeaways will be that you will get a good sense of the breadth and depth of the tissue engineering field, and which direction the field is now taking.
