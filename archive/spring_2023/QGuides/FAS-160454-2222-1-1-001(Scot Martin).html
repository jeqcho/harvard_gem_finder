This course is incredibly personal and engaging. I highly recommend taking this class as an introduction to engineering. If you are studying the natural sciences this is a great engineering class to take to learn about technological implementation of natural sciences. Professor Martin is incredibly kind and made the classroom setting very comfortable and fun.
You will gain a strong intuition and thermodynamic mentality towards the technological world, but it does not imbue very strong quantitative thermodynamic skills if that is what you are looking for.
This class is a very approachable introduction to thermodynamics, and the workload is never too overwhelming. I would definitely recommend this class.
TAKE THIS COURSE. It is so much easier than the other thermo and you still learn everything you need to know in practice. The exams are take–home and open book and the homework is generally doable (though I would highly recommend going to OH for them to check with peers and get help on problems that require some outside knowledge). Elizabeth was incredible and really made the course understandable and less stressful.
Take this class!! ES 112 is enjoyable and a supper interesting and applied way to learn thermodynamics. Prof Martin is great and generates enthusiasm for the subject. However, you get as much out of this class as you put in––it is worth it to spend time looking over lecture notes and doing the readings, but there is so much of it it's hard to keep up. The actual weekly assignments in this class do not take long, though they don't relate well to lecture notes.. Also, the final project is a great way to consolidate everything we learned in an interesting way.
This course isn't too difficult (wayyyy easier than ES 181), but I didn't learn much. Lectures are not taught very well but sections are amazing! Elizabeth made this class do–able. Psets were so different from lectures but section taught the necessary knowledge for psets.
This course is definitely a relatively low–stress look at classical thermodynamics! The community within the class is great, but I'm not sure how much thermo knowledge I ultimately gained from the class.
Take this class if you don't want to do ES 181 but don't expect to leave with a strong understanding of thermo.
This course take you from no knowledge to a solid foundation of thermodynamics. The teaching style is easy to follow and the lecture notes allow for easy review of the lectures. There are no surprises. What you learn is what you use which is a help in validating what you are learning. The friday sections were a nice addition to see how what we learn applies to the real world.
