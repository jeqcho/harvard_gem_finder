Comments
Take this course! If you are not as into theory, this is the course for you. It really shows you the range of what social studies can cover. Everything is super topical and the readings are so interesting! Professor Adatto really cares about helping you further your thesis topic through this class. It's also helpful that we read examples of social studies theses. I feel a lot more prepared to write my thesis.
Highly recommend the class!
Please take advantage of office hours, Professor Adatto is a really helpful resource for advice and prepping for your thesis
Recommend this class! Lots of current examples of themes, constantly changing syllabus, so engaging! Professor Adatto will be there with you every step of the way.
You will absolutely love this course! Use it to your advantage to write a chapter of your thesis.
This class is excellent. The readings are very interesting. Professor Adatto does a great time of leading discussion, prompting everyone to participate but never with any pressure associated with it.
This course is great for people who want more exposure to the types of art in the world, as well as new ways to think about them. People come with lots of personal experience and anecdotes. It may be difficult, though, to figure out what to talk and write about if you haven't already formed at least some art–related thesis or paper topic. There were weeks when I had no idea what to say because the week's topic was not one I was directly familiar with. So, I might not recommend this course to someone who wants to experience/discuss political art and speech for the first time, because it might be overwhelming and intimidating. The professor is super nice and eager to help, though! And if you're passionate about the subject, you'll love the course.
This class taught me so much about not just art or politics, but also the nature of humanity itself — you will be so glad you took it! I learned so much from both the professor and from my peers alike. The 2 hours really flew by each week, since the conversation was so dynamic and lively.
This class singlehandedly changed the course of my thesis and inspired me to focus on the topics I love and am passionate about. By far it is the best tutorial/small discussion based course I have ever been in. The professor (as well as every classmate) was ready to have amazing and deep discussions on topics, and always provided helpful feedback and suggestions for our papers. After giving my presentation on my final paper, I truly believe the feedback I received from both students and the professor helped me put together a better paper. Words can't express how amazing this class was. Not to give myself away, but is one of the few classes where I always made sure to do EVERY readings, because that's how interesting the topics were. It was two hours long, but the pace of discussion and the enthusiasm of my classmates never made it feel like that. One of the few (if not only) classes were a five minute break halfway through; there was always something else to talk about. Take this course if you want to truly learn how to have freedom and creativity with your thesis. With Professor Adatto, every topic is possible!
This is an amazing course for students interested in some combination of arts, culture, and society and contemporary affairs related to the intersection of those topics. Its a great opportunity to dive into some theory but stay rooted in relevant examples, and a fantastic place to advance your own research interests and develop a stronger understanding of your own theoretical or social approach to a contemporary research topic. Definitely take this tutorial!
