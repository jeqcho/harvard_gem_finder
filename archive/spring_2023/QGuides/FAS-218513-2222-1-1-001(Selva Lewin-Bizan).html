Take this class!! Whether you're interested in psychology, education, policy, law, healthcare, becoming a parent one day, or even something else, this course will benefit you. The course load is quite light, and the discussions lead by Dr. Lewin–Bizan alone are enough to make this class worthwhile. Such a fun, engaging, and interesting lecture, lead by such a wonderful professor!!
If you are interested in being introduced to the ways in which society shapes a child's development, then you should take this course. The assignments are light and flexible, Dr. Lewin–Bizan is kind and understanding, and the class discussions are often thought provoking. The lectures are also set up in such a way that you can still participate even if you did not have the opportunity to review the readings for that week, which is really nice.
This was a great, but not terrific class. I really enjoyed the book club component of the class, as well as the documentaries that were shown in class. However, lectures tended to run pretty dry. There are 4 assignments, and some have flexible deadlines, so try to spread them out over the semester instead of cramming at the end. Overall, I would recommend it as you get really valuable insight into the mechanisms in systems that contribute to adverse developmental outcomes (e.g., education, child welfare, immigration).
If you are at all interested in the development and wellbeing of children, you should definitely take this class!! Professor Lewin–Bizan is the BEST. I was always excited to come to class every week which struck a perfect balance of lecture and discussion.
Great class. A gem for sure. All of the assignments were feasible and relative to course content. You always get good feedback, and discussions were interesting. A lot of flexibility and independence in terms of assignment deadlines, and every assignment was straightforward.
