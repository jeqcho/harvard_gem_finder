What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Amazing class!! Definitely take it if you're interested in Tibetan art. There is a lot of work throughout the semester but it is not insanely hard or time intensive. You will get a lot out of the class by engaging with the materials and putting time into the final project.
Go ahead with the class. You will learn a lot and enjoy it.
Super challenging course, requiring background knowledge for almost any discussion in class. Prof Kim is just so smart and passionate about the material, but the material itself is so complex for someone who does not have a preexisting background (and there is no pseudo–introductory course offered at Harvard), that it is hard to really get at least 25% of the material she covers
Try your best and don't be afraid to ask for help. The material can be pretty intimidating, but it is so fun to tackle and the art is absolutely incredible. Professor Kim is there to support you and loves to do so, so ask her for help if you need it!
Overall would recommend! Not a gem, but still a very reasonable time commitment. Not to mention the course material is incredibly interesting and likely to cover a lot of information that you've never learned before. Anybody with interests in Asian history, art, and/or culture will get something from this course. Readings can be quite long, but pace yourself and don't be afraid to mind your own limits and you'll be fine.
If you are at all interested in Himalayan art, I would definitely take this class. It exposes you to a wide range of art, culture, and history from the region in a very accessible way.
