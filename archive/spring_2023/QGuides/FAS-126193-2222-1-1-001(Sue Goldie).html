I think this is a great GenEd to take: you learn a substantial amount, but the workload is very manageable (under 5 hours a week, usually). The assignments can actually be fun, because you get to write about the topics that you're interested in (i.e., for the policy brief you can choose any country and any health issue; for the final project – you can literally do anything: a podcast, a short video, a policy brief, a poster). The only drawback of this class is the section: I don't think I learned a lot from it, since it usually was just an unstructured free discussion, where students simply shared their thoughts on pretty much anything.
Professor Goldie really cared about her course and her students' learning. If you want to get an overview of global health, seriously consider taking this course because you will be surprised about how much you can take away from it if you put in enough efforts. That said, if you are just interested in a particular area of health problems, the course content may often feel not deep enough, so you may consider taking a more specialized course instead.
