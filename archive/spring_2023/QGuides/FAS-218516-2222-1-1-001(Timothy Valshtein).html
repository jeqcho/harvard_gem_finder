This was probably the best psych seminar I've taken at Harvard. You learn about romance and romantic relationships in both personally applicable and more societal/systemic contexts and every week the discussion becomes more interesting than the next. If you have a chance to take a class with Tim (Dr. Valshtein), DO IT. He is so smart and knowledgeable but also extremely supportive and a little spunky. The readings and assignments for this class were all extremely productive and thought provoking. Take this class if you can.
professor is really understanding and accommodating. workload is reasonable.

Definitely be prepared to learn about relationships without a filter. If you feel like you might be "putting on the blinders" in your own relationship, this course will take them off.
Come prepared with the readings! Professor Valshtein does a great job of stimulating and guiding conversation, but hearing classmates' diverse and thoughtful insights really made the course experience for me.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Tim is knowledgable, very passionate about the material, and cares about the students so much. You'll learn such important stuff about being in romantic relationship. It isn't hard, but you'll get a lot out of it. I really can't recommend it highly enough.
This is a prefect class for filling the psych elective requirement. Tim is a knowledgable and kind professor. 10/10 recommend!
The class topic is interesting, I just feel that it was slightly miss handled with the current syllabus. Power and Society in Romantic Relationships should indicate that the syllabus deals with more of the societal structures and ideas that shape power dynamics in relationships, but that was not the case for this semester
This is truly a phenomenal course. It is enjoyable, educational, and genuinely rewarding. It is clear that Professor Valshtein cares more about you learning as opposed to testing your knowledge. Consequently, it fostered an environment where, as a student, you don't need to worry about anything except engaging with the course material as the course intended.

Outside of the classroom, I quoted material I learned from this class regularly in my everyday conversations with my friends. I think it is representative of truly how valuable and applicable the knowledge from this course is.

With that said, it is one of the rare courses at Harvard where you get out what you put in. Not intended to bad mouth other classes that aren't as rewarding, but truly in Psych 1527, it does a phenomenal job of offering lots of value if you engage with the course material and every discussion. If you come into class with a good attitude and a curious mind, it won't take long until you can appreciate this classes's beauty.
