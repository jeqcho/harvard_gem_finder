taught by a kind preceptor who will give you lots of great comments on how to improve your paper (though she definitely has a stronger opinion on right/wrong arguments than some other teachers, so revisions tend to be a process of figuring out what she may want to hear). overall good class for strengthening your writing skills, but would avoid ranking this class if you're mostly just invested in the subject matter of American Money. this is much more akin to an english class than to a seminar, so many lessons on things you may have learned in high school (how to cite, what makes a good thesis, types of evidence, etc.).
Take this class if you want a more laid–back (at least, from what I can compare with other expos students) class, but this doesn't mean you shouldn't take your writings seriously. Dr. Rosefield will give people an A if they deserve an A (as opposed to others who have a curve/quota of grades), so make sure you're prepared to improve your essays when taking the course. You don't have to worry about excessive readings for this course, but they may be on the less engaging side, but I liked watching "The Big Short."
Take this section, you won't regret it at all. This was my favorite class, and I'm a STEM major (which is a bit troubling lol)

This class is not graded easily. The readings are quite interesting (many are related to money in very tangential yet relevant and captivating ways), but you'd need to put a lot of work in the response papers and essays.
Take American Money!! 2 out of the 3 essays that you write are based on movies (one is The Big Short and the other is a film of choice) so they are super enjoyable to analyze. The class is a bit more focused on developing writing skills than on talking about the content matter. Hannah is the sweetest person ever and will definitely be there to support you through it all. Not too intimidating of an Expos class!
