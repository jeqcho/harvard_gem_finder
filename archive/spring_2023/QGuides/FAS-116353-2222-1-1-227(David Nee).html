What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great Expos and I would highly recommend! David is a caring, thoughtful, and very professional preceptor, and runs an organized class with a professional, academic tone. However, he is still very easy to talk to and is obviously invested in our development as writers. In Unit 1, you read the Saga of Laxardal (an ancient Icelandic saga), which I quite enjoyed, but you must be prepared to actually read it and dive into it. It is definitely **old** (written in the 1200s) and can be a bit of a drag at times, but if you want a solid essay you must commit to reading it. However, the quantities of reading are very reasonable and if you're actually invested in the plot, then the essay and class discussions will be engaging and fun. Unit 2 is centered on the show Finding Your Roots (very fun to watch in my opinion) and lens theory. This unit generated some interesting discussion, and there wasn't really much work outside of class for it, but was definitely the least engaging of the three. In Unit 3, you pick your own research topic, so I would recommend finding a book or TV show that you really enjoy, and then the paper will be quite a breeze to write! If you've never done research or used Hollis before there are some in class sessions about it; though if you have, then it will feel very redundant. Overall, the workload for this class was quite low––outside of the essays, there was maybe 18–25 hrs (just a guess) of additional work for the whole semester (like writing response papers, reading, etc.). David gives great feedback on the essays, and I would definitely recommend meeting with him one–on–one to discuss your work. In my experience, he was very clear about what he wanted me to change, and it worked out well for me. The downsides of the course were few. There were times that I didn't feel discussion was that engaging; I think there was potential for us to have really interesting conversations and the vibe was often very academic and stiff. However, if you're considering an Expos, I would definitely recommend Family Sagas!
This expos is an highly underrated gem! Take it, I loved all the material that we had to read/watch for this class (Icelandic Saga, Minari, etc). Its very low–key and extremely interesting as the topic of "family trees" applies to each and every one of us.
This is definitely a course worth taking! A very painless way to go through expos, and David gives such helpful feedback. Definitely take advantage of the time you have with him and go into conferences prepared to discuss and take notes on the things he says. The texts are mostly quite interesting and not too difficult to read, and he's very very understanding. Highly recommend for fulfilling the expos requirement!!
While somewhat a harsh grader, the subject matter of this class is for the most part fun. And also Nee is an extremely nice professor.
