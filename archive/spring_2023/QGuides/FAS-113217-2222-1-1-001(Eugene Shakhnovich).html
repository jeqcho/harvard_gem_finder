Great course to learn statistical thermodynamics in a low stress environment. Grading scheme is very favorable, lecture notes are very helpful.
Don't take this class if Shakhnovich is teaching it, I promise you will regret it. The class isn't too difficult and the grading is very lenient, but you will literally just learn nothing from Shakhnovich.
Lectures are pretty useless, but sections are very clear, and the psets might leave you floundering but TFs are responsive.
Cool class. Lots of complicated concepts that you can have the flexibility to explore, because the grading scheme for the class is really flexible. Not sure if it'll change, but I really appreciated having exams where you could choose questions to attempt and have those counted. The prof and TFs are devoted for our learning, but it's just fundamentally hard concepts to learn.
A little disorganized, but overall good material and interesting class. Really low commitment, and the grading is really generous.
A decent math background is a must. The course is fairly intimidating but also very reasonable with assignments and exams so you can focus on actually learning the material.
I would not recommend taking this course if you can avoid it. While Eugene is very smart and knowledgeable and is very approachable and nice, I found his lectures very difficult to follow and often felt unprepared by the lectures for the problem sets. I would definitely urge future students to attend sections and to ask questions because the problem sets are challenging, and it is easy to fall behind in this class. This is probably the hardest class you have to take for the chemistry concentration, so I would recommend being prepared to put in a lot of work to keep up with it.
The course material can be interesting, and it is not hard to do well in the class given how the grading works. However the lectures were quite boring and I felt like I learned nothing from then. It was only by going to section that I learned the material, and even so the problem sets and exams were very challenging.
Go to section and office hours, problem sets are very very hard without them.
This is a tough course but you will learn a lot during it.
Please only take this class if you are very interested in thermodynamics or statistical mechanics AND you have a strong math background. The problem sets are the biggest component of the course (half of your grade) and where most of your effort will go towards. Thus, if you have not taken all your math courses yet or need to refresh yourself on anything including multivariable calculus, the class will appear harder than it actually is. However, if you do well on all your problem sets and how to solve every problem, you will be more than set for the exams. The in–person midterm was very straightforward and the take–home final exam asked very fair questions – the only down fall of the final exam is that its very long but you at least get three days to do it. Overall, great class but you have to want to take this class and be ready for a lot of math.
