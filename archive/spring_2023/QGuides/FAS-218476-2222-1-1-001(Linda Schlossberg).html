This is a great survey course and introduction to LGBT literature. I found that as the course went on, the discussions focused less and less on the readings we were assigned for class. If you did not read the book that week, that is helpful, but it can be frustrating if you were looking forward to talking about the book in class. Professor Schlossberg is an engaging lecturer and is genuinely excited about the readings. If you are an older student in the humanities, the course can be a bit basic and frustratingly focused on close readings as opposed to larger themes and looking at the books in their historical context. Overall. if you're looking for an easy and enjoyable class, I would highly recommend it.
If you are queer, DEFINITELY consider this class. As someone who is STEM, I was cautious with deciding whether or not to enroll in this, but I loved it. The texts are insightful and include a variety of gay, bisexual, lesbian, and transgender experiences. There should be more inclusivity of other identities (asexual, intersex, etc.).
Many assignments are based on completion and contribute a large portion to your grade, which is very relieving. Make sure to take advantage of office hours to review papers and drafts.
Start reading ASAP because it is easy for one to fall behind.
Lectures are a MUST, especially for the first half of the class. The midterm covers specific texts, and the analysis is all provided during lectures. If you take notes during lecture, read (or at least skim) the text, and write it all down in the midterm, you have a guaranteed A.
For the second half, you are more flexible with what you can do. Instead of doing a second analysis paper, you can do a creative project, like a memoir, reflection personal essay, short story, etc. and I highly recommend. It is a great chance to talk about your own queer experience and connect it to certain texts.
One thing I noticed about the class (though it is not necessarily related to the content but more to the demographics) is that there was a plurality of white students. It is definitely something to consider, but the texts read are also from Black, Latinx, and Asian authors, which is extremely important to include!
