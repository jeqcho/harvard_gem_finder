This really won't matter because the current professor only taught it to fill in for a professor in sabbatical. In short, this year's heat transfer was horrific because the professor taught the class as if we were grad students (i.e. we had already assumed much of the "basics"–– which FYI aren't really basics), gave really bad explanations about certain concepts, and had very chaotic lectures that were pure of lengthy derivations. However, if this class were taught well, it should give you a good intuition about heat transfer in your daily lives. The course content is not too much, compared to thermo (you only cover conduction, convection, and radiation), and if the syllabus stays the same there are only 4–5 pset assignments, one take home midterm, and final project. I don't know how the class will be taught next year, but if it is taught better (which it probably will), it may be worth taking this instead of thermo because the content in this course seems more interesting and the courseload is not that heavy compared to thermo (even with this year's iteration).

Most of your learning will come from the readings/psets/section, lecture can be useful for gaining info about derivations and where the physics of heat transfer comes from, but often doesn't go over more practical information/problems.
