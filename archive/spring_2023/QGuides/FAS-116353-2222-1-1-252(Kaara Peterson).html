IF YOU ARE INTERESTED IN LITERATURE IN THE SLIGHTEST, TAKE THIS CLASS! Even if you are not, take it!Prof. Peterson is an amazing preceptor. Feedback is always extremely useful and she puts a lot of effort into understanding each individual argument. I would say it is a tiny bit more work than other expos classes (certainly more readings), but the assignments are so interesting that you will not even notice it. Building up to the draft of each long paper, we had to write a short response paper on a similar prompt, as well as a thesis paragraph. Prof. Peterson gave feedback on all of them, so one could easily see what arguments worked and what arguments did not for the longer paper. She is also just an amazing person in general, extremely passionate about the topic and wants to pass her passion on to all of her students. Before I took this class, I thought literature was extremely subjective, but she taught me that as long as my argument is well–proven, it creates the basis for a very good paper. Highly, highly encourage you to rank this first in the expos lottery.
Only take if you're really interested in detective fiction as it's a lot of that, if you're bored by over–structured assignment prompts and plot–based class discussions stay away from this class, the feedback on essays is actually quite good if you take it seriously, many of the theoretical readings are interesting but class often flat
I would highly recommend this class because I genuinely enjoyed all the readings we did. There were books we read that I actually could not put down because I wanted so badly to know the ending. I was not expecting to derive such genuine enjoyment from an expos, but this class certainly delivered.
This class requires a lot of reading, so be ready for it. The prompts for the essays are somehow both constricting and uncomfortably open–ended. If you like classic detective fiction, the first two units are good, but the last unit is basically a piece of Gothic literature that you have to write an essay about to argue that it’s a detective story. If you like contemporary detective fiction, be aware that Agatha Christie is as contemporary as this class gets.
This course is a lot of work, but it is so much fun. The stories you get to read and watch are gripping, exciting and intriguing. You will be pushed as a reader, thinker and writer but your skills will be all the better for it. But be prepared to work smart. Start your essays early, to give yourself time to revise and rethink. Put in a lot of work on your drafts, to make sure the comments you will get are as detailed and effective as you want.
Expos is a bad class across the board – it is unnecessary if you know how to write and you will find it incredibly tedious and redundant, especially if (like so many, myself included) you take it in the spring having already taken an essay class in the fall. Try to make discussions about the text rather than about your works cited or the MLA format, or you'll go insane.
I would strongly recommend this expos (Detective Fiction) to those who prefer to write about literary analysis. The readings are very fun and engaging, and that makes them fun to discuss in class as well. The writing workload is also spread out consistently. This means that although there will probably be writing most weeks, if you put in the effort it won’t eat too much time when essays are due. Additionally, Dr. Peterson gives very clear and thorough feedback. She is very kind and always willing to meet and discuss ideas. As someone who normally doesn’t like writing, I am very satisfied with my choice of expos.
