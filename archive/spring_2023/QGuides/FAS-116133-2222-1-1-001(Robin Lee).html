This class is great. I really like Professor Robin. The readings are not too taxing (max one a week) and the psets are really great study materials. The exams were fair and if you apply yourself and keep up with the class, it is actually very interesting. He does not do what every other ec class does…he actually talks about and addresses the imperfections of society and explains how the theory is only a simplification of the real world and why. Definitely take this class if you don’t want a ton of readings and prefer psets like me! Even if you don’t, psets are very manageable if you go to office hours and get help.
This is a great econ elective!
TAKE THIS ECON ELECTIVE!!! Robin is a fantastic and engaging lecturer, and this is genuinely the best econ class and among the most enjoyable Harvard courses I've ever taken. Psets are challenging but interesting, exams are fair, and you'll start to see traces of industrial org theory (which is fascinating, if you've ever wanted to learn how to win an auction or dip your toes in a manageable way in other concepts like market concentration and platforms) everywhere in life.
Amazing course. Lots of very interesting real–world applications are discussed. As you are planning your semester, keep in mind that the 4 weeks when psets are due will have a much higher workload than other weeks.
This class is quite typical of an Economics elective, in the sense that the content is somewhat accessible and an intermediate amount of depth is covered. If you are interested in the topic at all, then definitely take this class. However, if you are just looking for any Ec class, keep in mind that this class is good but nothing to write home about.
I definitely recommend the class. Great professor and TF and interesting material applicable to real world.
I think this is the most interesting/useful economics class I've ever taken! Very applicable to the real world. But make sure to do your readings and start your problem sets early!
