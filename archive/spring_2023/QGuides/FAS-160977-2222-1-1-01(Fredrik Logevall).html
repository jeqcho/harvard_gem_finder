Overall, I really enjoyed this class. Professor Logevall is an interesting and engaging lecturer, and time generally passed quickly while in his class. He engages with student contributions will, both through the weekly briefings and live in–class, and he has a wealth of knowledge and connections about U.S. Foreign Policy. The class readings were manageable, and the assignments were engaging and not too time–consuming.

The only (potential) drawback to this class is that the lectures are much more about discussing themes, motivations, and counterfactuals in the history than about the actual nuts and bolts of what happened. I thought this was great, but if you're not familiar with the major events in American Foreign Policy from 1914–now, you will have to get that information from the readings or from outside sources ... not from the lectures.
Professor Logevall is a great professor, and you'll learn a lot about the big moments/themes in American foreign policy from WWI onward. Although the class is not structured like a traditional College class, it was great to get out of my comfort zone and learn how to speak up in a 100+ person class. You'll also learn a ton from your classmates. There are a lot of cool people who take the course, including auditors, HKS National Security Fellows, etc.
Take it! You meet so many cool students from HKS and Logevall is awesome.
This class provides a great overview to how American foreign policy has developed and the major debates/challenges that American leaders face when it comes to foreign policy.
Take the class to learn about foreign policy from a true expert.
Professor Logevall delivers great lectures and the class discussions are always incredible. I loved the readings and feel like I learned a great deal about U.S. foreign policy over the past century. That being said, I received virtually no feedback on my work in this class and I have no idea what my grade is. The lack of feedback really stressed me out, so if you care about your grades, maybe avoid this class.

Definitely take this class. Logevall is incredible, and this is one of my favorite classes I have taken at Harvard.
This class is a good overview of events in American foreign policy, but that understanding is going to come from you doing the readings. Professor Logevall lectures for about half the class and then facilitates a discussion (frequently on a philosophical level or counterfactuals). Because the class was cross–registered with the Kennedy school, this was interesting, but I didn't feel like I got a great understanding of the events we were supposed to be describing. Also, I received very minimal feedback in the class, to the point that I have no idea what grade I might get. We completed two simulations — one was well–planned and had clear guidelines, the other did not — but I've heard back on neither. The entire directions for the final paper could be found on the syllabus. I would have appreciated more guidance as to what to do in this course, but it was certainly an interesting experience.
History concentrators, this is a great class to take! Logevall is a great lecturer, and the readings are accessible and easy to understand. Logevall LOVES when students talk, so the bulk of class will be given to discussion. This can be a pro or a con, as I'd have liked to hear from him more and had a little more historical analysis. That being said, no regrets: he's an incredible professor, the assignments are not overwhelming, and I definitely have a much better understanding of American foreign policy history. Bear in mind, this course covers some very contemporary material, so it becomes a little less "historical" as the semester progresses.
Professor Logevall is a deeply engaging lecturer. The learning I did in this course could not have been done by simply doing the readings–to me this is one aspect of feeling good about the decision to take a course.
Take this course! It's a safe bet to take any Logevall class, but this one in particular is a masterclass.
