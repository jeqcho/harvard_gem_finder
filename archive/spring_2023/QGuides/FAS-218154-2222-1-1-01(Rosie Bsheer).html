What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Quite literally the best class I've ever taken at Harvard. The other students were fantastic, really interested and incisive in their analysis, and clearly did the work every week. Prof. Bsheer is brilliant – she pushes students to be as good as they can be while also being a very kind and genuine person. I really, really loved this class, even as someone without very much background in Middle East/South Asia studies, and could not more highly recommend it.
Be prepared to dedicate a significant amount of time to the course readings and weekly responses.
Take it, if you can! Before you read each work, try to get a basic sense of who the author is and how they relate to the subject matter. Think not only about what your response to a given text is but about why it might have been assigned. Try to see the what is useful or good in each work or approach, alongside what is incomplete or invites criticism. Don't be afraid to do a lot of basic background research and googling, especially at the beginning.
