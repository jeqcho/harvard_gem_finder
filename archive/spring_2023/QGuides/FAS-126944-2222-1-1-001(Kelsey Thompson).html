the assignments are very complementary to the lectures and fun to complete. You are given sufficient time and the teaching staff are very helpful.
Eric and Kelsey are great teachers. There are a bunch of similar classes on data analysis but they are truly the most effective and skilled educators.
Great course, you'll learn a lot about the meat of what bioinformatics is done for, along with some coding. Girls that code together, stay together––you'll make some great friends along the way.
It is a great introductory course for people who are interested in bioinformatics. It provides a good starting point for you to explore the field. However, additional training are needed if you want to expert.
Good course for a very brief overview of various topics if you are completely new to STEM. However, not recommended if you are already deeply in the field of bioinformatics.
Biological or genomic background is crucial for this course.
The course does not explain the details (e.g. how BLAST algorithms work exactly and quantitatively), but provides a good overview.
You need to spend a lot more time just participating in the class, including coming to the class in person. People will feel frustrated when more than half of the class is not showing up.
Carefully designed course that will provide you with a toolkit of genomic data manipulation methods.
Most valuable time to take the class is probably during the first year––lots of practical information that can benefit your grad school trajectory!
