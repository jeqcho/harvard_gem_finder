What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great class! It is definitely not intro level, however, so make sure you have some economics or economic anthropology knowledge before entering the course. A book a week is a lot of reading so make sure you do not underestimate, because the readings will be heavily discussed.
You will leave questioning everything that you thought you knew (or didn't realize you needed to question) about the modern social, political, and economic order –– exactly what a transformative education ought to do.
Fantastic class with a fantastic reading list. The discussions got a little repetitive – I wish we had focused more on the books for each week rather than talking more generally about what neoliberalism is and isn't because the discussion on that started stagnating.
Highly highly recommend this class if you're interested at all in equity or learning more about hypercapitalistic values/society. Class is all discussion based. Assignments are manageable and include presenting and leading a discussion on the weekly reading, responding to a presentation, and then a term paper due at the end of the semester. The workload is a lot because we read a book per week but the readings were excellently curated and I enjoyed most of them. The teaching team is PHENOMENAL!!! They really cared about us as students and learners. They also made it very clear that this was a collaborative learning environment for everyone and really helped foster that.
Take this course!!!! Salmaan and the teaching team are wonderful. Syllabus is excellent. You won't regret it!
I gained a really good understanding of neoliberalism m its history and how it shows up in all aspects of society. fantastic discussion and i learnt a lot from my peers. this course definitely challenged me but I think I am a better student for it – I was probably under prepared going into the course but I didn't drown and was able to takeaway A LOT. The professors are amazing and I would love to take another course by Salmaan Keshavjee, he is one of my two favourite professors at Harvard. Reading a book a week was difficult but also a nice challenge and the books were all very interesting – especially the ones which demonstrated neoliberalism in different ways (technology, race, public health, pharmaceuticals)
Everyone can benefit from taking this course. It reveals, bit by bit, many of the interlocking systems of oppression that reproduce injustice today and have for a very long time. It empowers students to tease apart problems that may seem unrelated, but are in fact linked together by a strong but often invisible system of ideologies that treat humans as economic units rather than as people. The course helps unpack colonialism, racism, and many other forms of violence and contextualizes them with great case studies. This course is eye opening both for people who already think a lot about oppression and neoliberalism and for people who are new to the topic.
