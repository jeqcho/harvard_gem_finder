Really great class! A bit more technical than what you might be expecting, more of an emphasis on theory and big–picture things.
It can definitely be a little intimidating at first, being surrounded by so many graduate students and reading complicated material. But as the weeks go on, you'll slowly adapt to it. In the end, it is truly a rewarding course for those who persist, as the final paper really feels like you've capped off an entire semester of research in a field, that most people don't typically focus on when studying China.
Very insightful course with amazing teaching staff and students!
A really important class to better understand China and stop treating it as a monolith. You will learn a great deal about the internal politics and geography of China and reevaluate the extent of control the CCP has on its citizens. Probably one of the classes I have learned the most from content–wise. Great in terms of building skills too, as you learn how to use map–making software (QGIS) and create an interesting final project. Prof. Koss is a good lecturer who always ties in his own experiences and shows actual pictures of what he's talking about in China. He's a bit of a "scattered" lecturer, but very passionate and the lessons are always clear.
