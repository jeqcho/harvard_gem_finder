Pay attention during lecture! The readings are a bit much but as long as you take note of the passages mentioned you'll be okay. The two midterms are a bit fast paced so if you aren't great with timed essay writing this might not be the course for you. The midterm review sessions are really helpful for preparing for the midterms.
Be strategic about how much of each reading or movie you are watching, often you will not need too much for assignments and only need the full content to comprehend lecture, which is often distilled in section
This is a truly insightful and well–planned GENED, with lectures and section featuring the depth of analysis you would expect from any humanities course. Professor Scarry truly cares about all her students, and she has the most interesting random thoughts about facets of daily life that she weaves effortlessly into lectures on the texts!
Take this class – don't be intimidated by the readings! They are fun to look through if you have time, but they also go over all you need to know in lecture and section. I loved that the class had a small enrollment and everyone who was taking it wanted to be there, even if they might not have done all of the readings. This is actually one of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard, largely because of section!! (ET and Emma are great, if they are TFing again!)
This course is definitely worth taking! It will change how you think about consent, and especially, how you approach interdisciplinary studies.
please do not take this class unless you're a philosophy concentrator or are otherwise deeply interested. it is taught much more like a law lecture than an early undergraduate survey course.
This is an incredible class.
Not quite a total gem but pretty easy. It's essentially no work every week (there are readings but it's like any other gened). If you study hard for the exams it's definitely possible to learn everything the night before after taking no notes all semester. If you're interested in consent definitely take the course. If you're thinking about it, look at the syllabus and course readings and if they seem interesting, take the course.
This course was amazing. It caused a ton of good reflection over the course of the semester in a way that no other course over my time here has. I really loved the course material, and was exposed to a few of my new favorite pieces of literature/film/essays/plays! Please take it and also definitely talk to Professor Scarry when you have the chance!
