It’s a great class, but I wouldn’t take it unless you need it for CPB or you’re really interested in the material! It’s disorganized and very hard to prepare for the exams / know what’s expected of you for the lab report and even answering some of the questions on psets and exams.
This class is very difficult for many reasons. First, there is a ton of material to cover. In addition, understanding the material really well does not mean you will automatically be able to solve the types of problems on exams and LPS assignments because they require a certain type of thinking/familiarity with the formulas that you need to develop. If your grade is what is most important to you, I would recommend dedicating more time to doing practice problems than trying to understand every detail in the lecture notes. Also, try to do as well as possible on the first exam because that one is the least challenging. The grading on LPS assignments is not particularly forgiving and sometimes the question can be looking for something different than what you think, so you need to go to office hours (even if you are not the type of person to go to office hours usually). Make sure to go to office hours for LPS1 to make sure you are writing your critique correctly and including the right information and to get feedback on your final paper. Having prior research experience (at least a few months or previous classes with lab, this is essential) will not only help you with lab but also help you with the type of thinking that is needed to do well on exams and the final paper. Make sure you know the basics of how to use excel/google sheets. Also spend a lot of time making your cheat sheet and getting to know it. Do practice exams and section handouts. I think this class is doable if you have the patience, drive, and time. If you put in effort and carefully look over the notes, you will be able to learn a lot, especially if you care less about grades.
This course is a lot of work and is a large time commitment, but if you stay on top of the lectures and problem sets it is very rewarding! The teaching staff is also incredible, and are very invested in your learning.
Go to office hours for the LPS assignments. Seriously. Do it. Also, don't wait until the day before the lab report is due to put it altogether. Seriously.
Definitely not an easy class (really only taking this if you need the Pchem req). Make sure you do the practice exams; they are definitely the most representative of what you'll see on the exams and the best way to prepare.
This is still very much a biology class, and suffers from trying to straddle experimental and quantitative approaches. When I was looking back and reviewing the content, it actually seemed interesting, but the course fails to execute and deliver the content in an interesting manner. You also just have to spend so much time on the course – 3 classes a week, lab, homework, pop questions – that it is just genuinely exhausting. There's an unfortunate amount of memorization expected for a physical class, and it's especially frustrating when you have to recall a random line from the slide on the exam. The exams in general are just not very reflective of the way the content is presented, though there are practice exams available to help prepare. This is definitely one of those cases where you get out what you put in, though – take this class if you can motivate yourself to remain interested even when the lectures are difficult to remain focused in. The lab portion is actually quite nice, though you should definitely start your lab report a week or two before it's due if you can – the course staff will tell you this, and you should listen.
This course is hard and there are a lot of components to juggle, but the content is really interesting and the course staff are amazing! If you keep up with the course materials and ask questions when you need help, it's totally manageable.
Not going to lie, this course is a lot of work. If you're good with statistics and probability, you have nothing to worry about, otherwise, you might struggle. Teaching staff is great however, so always go to office hours! The lab portion of the class was the most interesting for me.
