What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a lot of work! You learn 80–100 vocabulary words and 20 grammar structures every week, and you have two vocabulary quizzes each week, one for each lesson that you cover in a week. Before taking this class, you definitely need to understand that this class is not a joke (unless you're already fluent), and you will have to put work in in order to do well. However, I think that this class was still so, so rewarding! I learned so much, and the class itself is really chill, especially if you become close with your classmates and instructor. All of the work you do in the class is so that you can improve your Chinese skills, and it doesn't feel like busy work. Definitely recommend!
I would like to tell future students that this class will require a lot of outside class time learning the words and vocabulary, but if you are really passionate about honing your Chinese and become a multilingual global citizen, this class is a must.
After taking Bx, this is a fun class to take. There definitely is a noticeable step up in terms of vocabulary with an emphasis on more formal communication, and expect to spend more time on this class than Bx.
If you're a heritage speaker, take this class!!! Your Chinese will improve A LOT, especially reading and speaking. Basically the class is just talking to your classmates in Chinese, so you get to meet more people and really bond with your classmates. The caveat is that it is every day and the walk to Vanserg is pretty far (~10 minutes from the Yard), so the class does require quite some time (though in my opinion it was definitely worth it)
Class is very enjoyable – fun environment.
fantastic, fantastic course for people who are looking to improve their conversational Chinese my parents have remarked on my improvement from our facetimes :) and the teachers are so lovely
Be prepared to practice regularly.
Although it was annoying at times that class was everyday, in the end it was so worth it! This was one of my favorite classes and I loved the close–knit community and how the lecturer and section leaders tried to make it fun.
