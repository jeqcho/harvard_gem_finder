This course is a lot of work. Mainly, there are a lot of readings, although the readings were really interesting. I think the class is interesting and informativeand, in particular, relevantbut it is a time commitment, and I think you should take it pass–fail if you are interested. I had trouble completing all the readings with my STEM schedule, so I was quite often stressed during lectures to be cold–called and section to make a meaningful contribution (attendance and discussions are graded). The Professors and Teaching Staff are knowledgeable and engaging, and I learned a lot from this course on US–China relations as it relates to the current state of affairs, but I would have taken this class pass–fail if I could go back. Also, be prepared to do a lot of public speaking in addition to a lot of readingthis course is heavily discussion–based. The assignments at the end of the course are extremely interesting and meaningful, as I learned a lot from my final essay and debate. But, the work is also concentrated at the end of the semester, so be sure to start early and budget time closer to the end of the semester if you choose to take this course. With that being said, I want to reiterate that the class is interesting, and if you're interested and have some passion or experience in politics, economics, US–China relations, and foreign policy, you should definitely take this class!
