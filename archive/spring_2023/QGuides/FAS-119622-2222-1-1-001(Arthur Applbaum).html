TAKE. THIS. CLASS. I freaking loved it. The tight–knit setting of this seminar is INCREDIBLE. Class was dynamic, engaging, and the camaraderie was through–the–roof. Applbaum himself is brilliant, and his philosophy discussions are full of humor and wit. You will NOT be bored (unless you don't do the reading and don't know what he's talking about...then you might be).

I seriously looked forward to this crazy, 2 hour 45min class every week! Not only will it be highly entertaining, but you will also benefit from learning TONS about social contract theory, somewhat esoteric French history (at least for the avg American), and you will come out of the course confident in your ability to begin to comprehend the work of HARD philosophers, i.e. Kant, and not sound like a bumbling idiot while you're at it. Full–send it with the readings and discussion posts every week, and have a ball in actual class with the back–and–forths. It'll be great.
This is a worthwhile class, but you must be willing to read. That's the biggest barrier to entry and it inhibited some of our discussions this year when people didn't read.
This is a fun class, Professor Applbaum is super knowledgeable and very fun to talk with in class. Pretty cool readings and interesting coverage of both history (French Wars of Religion) and philosophy (Montaigne, Hobbes, Locke, Kant).
This is a wonderful class that takes dense texts and makes them digestible to students of all backgrounds. Arthur is a gem – super patient and funny. The workload is not insignificant, and you might get confused at times in the readings, but class discussions help to clarify the key points of the readings. Great class atmosphere with discussions.
