What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Teaching:
The teaching in this class was fantastic! Kim SSN is so funny and talented and makes every class equal parts informative and entertaining. I can confidently say that I had the most fun in Korean Bx + 123xb out of all the classes I took this year.

Workload/assignments:
The workload can get pretty heavy at times, so if you are interested in taking the class, be prepared for a lot of assignments. Based on my experience, most of the assignments did end up being worthwhile but it can definitely feel like a slog at times. A typical week of assignments consists of 2–3 chapters of grammar homework (1–2 hrs depending on ur speed) and a couple short essays based on narration passages from the textbook (another 1–2 hours). However, three times a semester you will be required to write a 1.5 page essay and then memorize and give a 5–minute presentation based on that essay. On those weeks, I probably spent between 8–10 hours on Korean outside of class. As some consolation, there is no grammar + composition hw on presentation weeks.

Community:
I love the community that formed around this class! I found that it was easy to make friends since as Korean Americans we had many common experiences to bond over. It was also fun to have a group of people around the same level as me in Korean who I could converse with on a daily basis. Some of my favorite memories with my Korean chingus this semester were hanging out for an hour before most classes in NW basement and planning a group 노래방 trip together!

Preparedness:
I would say if you are trying to figure out if 123xb is the right class for you, taking the placement test is definitely the best option! After you get your initial placement, you can schedule a follow–up meeting with one of the Korean professors to get a better idea of where your specific reading, writing, and speaking skills fall relative to the classes. My one note for 123xb and the heritage track in general is that since most of the students speak Korean at home, they will be able to process and speak and process the language pretty quickly and comfortably even if their grammar isn't 100% correct.
