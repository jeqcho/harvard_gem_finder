This course was great! This class really broadened my worldview and taught me so much about mental health and trauma in the context of crises and disasters. I love how the course syllabi broke down the different weekly topics (i.e. violence of the state, violence of capitalism, etc.). Professor Abi–Rached was a great lecturer, and it's clear she's passionate about the course. I loved the addition of documentaries and guest speakers/lecturers! Molly was an exceptional TF that made sure everything ran smoothly; between her flexibility/accommodation and feedback on assignments, she made sure everyone was in a good position to succeed in this class. Course material was interesting, but Perusall was a slight nuisance at times. I think the requirement for Perusall engagement should be lowered in the future! That way students aren't stressed about a comment limit and can engage with the sources as normal. The written assignments and final project were unique and allowed for students to tap into their creativity. I appreciate that. A rubric would be even more helpful since there was a lot of confusion for me when approaching the change over time essay and final project. Nevertheless, I enjoyed HISTSCI 1472 and would recommend to my peers!
This is a truly amazing course that really taught me about the history of psychiatric care and mental health from a global perspective. This is the first HistSci class I took, and after this, I am so excited to take many more! Dr. Abi–Rached and Molly (our TF) are truly incredible and caring people, who take the time to meet with you to discuss assignments and your interests outside of the course. I enjoyed doing all of our readings and loved getting to choose a research topic that I was excited about for the papers!
This class is incredible, and so is the teaching staff. Im not a History of Science concentrator, but I still walked away feeling as though I had learned so much. I definitely recommend taking a a class with Professor Abhi–Rached if you can!
You will learn a ton and talk about things that you likely do not think about on a regular basis
Even if the title is about trauma, the content is very interesting and I learned alot.
This class is really engaging and will leave you thinking about its content for a while after. It changed the way I understood and think about mental health, and I really enjoyed how we used a fairly global lens throughout the course. TAKE THIS! An amazing class with amazing content. Honestly, did not know what I was going into, and I'm glad I tried it out. Dr. Abi–Rached is absolutely an incredible teacher, and her expertise is crazy. The TF Molly was also really helpful. Really really loved this structure and class.
Do the readings early! They are long and sometimes not as easy to read due to the antiquated language. While this class may not be super intense, remember that you get what you put in. You can really learn to speak eloquently about these topics and learn something invaluable if you put in the effort.
This class was one of the most interesting classes I've taken at Harvard – definitely recommend!!!
This class discusses so many different issues from a mental health perspective, and you'll get to see just how much mental health and trauma are integrated into times of crisis. You'll also get to learn how to think about these issues from a historical perspective and create arguments as to change over time! Professor Abi–Rached and Molly were very kind and supportive, and they made both lectures and sections very interactive and engaging, making lecture something I looked forward to. The assignments could be difficult at times, but they let me write about topics I was really passionate about and brought out parts of myself I hadn't expressed much before. Therefore, I highly recommend this class!
