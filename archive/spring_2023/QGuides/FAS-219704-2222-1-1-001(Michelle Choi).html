This is a wonderful class and great introduction to Anthropology. Dr. Choi is a fantastic instructor: the class was well–organized and operated at a predictable pace. I would recommend it to students from across departments who are interested in developing a more critical perspective towards how people engage with the future, and how our ideas about the future inform the present. In general, it offers a fun and intriguing survey of "futurist" trends, which will expand your imagination.
Take this class! The class community and discussions were great. The instructor is kind and helpful. The work is manageable, and the final project basically allows you to write about whatever you find most interesting.
Dr. Choi is the BEST! So kind, thoughtful, talented, and great at sparking discussion
This is honestly such a phenomenal class. Even if you're not an anthro concentrator, take this class! You don't have to do the readings (or you can skim them), Michelle is the sweetest human possible, and the two–hour class just flies by because the discussions are so interesting. The workload is fairly light for a high–level seminar.
This is an amazing class for students of any background and is no doubt relevant to all. Michelle is all you could ask for in a teacher—brilliant, caring, engaged—and the syllabus covers a wide range of topics, perspectives, and geographies. Great content aside, if you are at all curious about anthropology/ethnography, a seminar setting classroom, or improving your writing skills, consider this course. It is a low–stress environment where you learn a ton.
This course is incredible! Please take it, the subject matter and discussions are super interesting, and the final project is super flexible.
