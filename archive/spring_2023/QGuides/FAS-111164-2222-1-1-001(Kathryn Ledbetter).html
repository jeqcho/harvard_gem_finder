If you have no physics experience and will be taking the minimum prerequisite math class to take this class (presumably Math 1b), read this:

I'm gonna keep it real with you. If you are a party animal, do not take this class. If you are doing 20 hours of research a week and two other pset classes, seriously think about your decision to take this class. If you aren't seriously interested in learning physics, pleaseeeee reconsider taking this class! If you for sure need all A's for pre–med, etc. don't take this.

However, if you–like me– meet the criteria in the first line and want to prove a point to yourself while also being genuinely excited about learning physics, then THIS IS THE CLASS FOR YOU! The first weeks will be the hardest. You MAY OR MAY NOT be under your desk in the fetal position at 5am because it's just a lot. But stick in there. I say this, but when this was me, I had the opportunity to drop and still didn't! I needed extensive help at first to complete every. single. problem. on any assignment, any lecture practice problem, any section problem... (And by at first, I mean for the first month, at least.) As I progressed through Math 1b, lectures started to sound like English, when the first few weeks they had sounded like Dutch (which I don't speak, but it vaguely sounds like English sometimes). This class actually gave me an edge in Math 1b, and just made me wayyy more confident using math to problem–solve in general. Suddenly, around the 6–week mark, my eyes opened, and I started being able to do problems (or at least try something) by myself! I cannot stress enough how incredible this felt. Professor Mundy and the TFs are all awesome, by the way. Once you can catch yourself up enough to vaguely understand what's going on, they've got the rest. By the end of it, I was solving problems by myself, and truly understanding concepts.

This class was not easy, but it was such an incredible learning experience. This has been the most educational class I have taken in my life– not just because the content is so important but because of the immense gain in my confidence in my problem–solving capabilities. Take it. Be warned, and buckle up, but prepare to be a much better scientist as a result. :) Big thanks to the teaching staff.
