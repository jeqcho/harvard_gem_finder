What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is a really cool and unique opportunity. You will see cool books, you get to see inside a lot of Harvard libraries, you hear from some really awesome speakers. However, the lectures aren't always the most interesting, and the class feels very disorganized at times (especially for 2h45 long class meetings). If you have an interest in books/manuscripts/their history, this class will probably be great for you especially if you have some background already, but if you are taking this as more of an elective and aren't sure if you are interested, it may be more of a mixed bag––a lot of cool experiences + some less interesting lectures.
This was the best course I have ever taken at Harvard. Professor Stern is passionate about the material and every week presents a new opportunity to look at amazing material. The workload is very manageable and the readings are fascinating. TAKE THIS COURSE.
TAKE IT!!!!!!!!!! TAKE THE CLASS!!!!!!!!!! DO IT NOW, COWARDS!!!!!!!!
Do you want to turn the pages of the Gutenberg Bible? Do you want to look at (and touch!) some of the most consequential books in history? Then take this class!! The History of the Book is seriously the best class I've taken at Harvard. No other course singularly makes use of the resources here.

The workload is relatively light, too. You should expect to spend at least 2–3 hours on it outside of the (once–weekly) class meeting. Each week there is a group assignment in Houghton, which takes usually 1–2 hours, plus readings that you don't strictly need to do since they will often be covered in class.

Professor Stern is an amazing teacher knowledgeable, enthusiastic about the subject, and incredibly kind. If the books and the light workload weren't enough to convince you, it's worth taking this class for him. I really cannot repeat myself enough this is an AMAZING class!!
Take this course. This course has fundamentally changed my approach to literature/literary criticism. There is now a clear pre–NEC 107 and post–NEC 107 division to my studies. I can no longer think about literary texts in the absence of their material features/where they fall in the history of the book. I now really believe in the book (particularly the codex) as a sophisticated piece of technology and a site of extraordinary, history–making innovation. This has easily been one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard.
Take this class. You will learn so much about books, Western history, and art all in one.
This is one of the best courses I've ever taken at Harvard and will certainly be my most memorable one. If you love books and the thought of going to Houghton every week to read rare and old books excites you, this is the class for you.
This class is amazing! I touched a book from the 11th century, the Gutenberg Bible, and a book made entirely of tin. What other class at Harvard let's you do that? Seriously, though, this was one of my favorite classes and always looked forward to it during the week!
