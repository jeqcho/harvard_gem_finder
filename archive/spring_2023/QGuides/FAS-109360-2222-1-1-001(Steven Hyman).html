Overall, I liked the class, but I would not have taken it if it did not double count for a gen–ed and an upper level concentration course. Lecture had interesting topics, but largely fell flat because each slide took 20 minutes to get through. There were four papers and a midterm, so there was definitely a decent amount of work. I felt like the papers were interesting though and the readings made it easy to write. In section, there was one activity that was upsetting regarding the stability of personal identity (a case in which a “changed person” should have a lesser sentence or not— they committed a hate crime which ended up leaving the victim in a vegetative state). As the answer should be obvious, it seemed like there were much better scenarios to talk about. Although the other sections did not have to do it, there apology was half–hearted and felt more of “I’m sorry you feel that way”. Other sections were fine, but after that a lot of people did not care to be actively engaged. I think there were a lot better ways to have us interact. In general, there was potential for it to be an interesting class, but it did not end up being noteworthy.
Do not expect this course to be a gem. I came into this thinking that this would be my easy fourth class in an academically challenging semester. Boy was I wrong. The teaching staff for this course are actively trying to combat the notion that all geneds are easy As. This is extremely annoying when everyone knows that geneds are not supposed to be the most challenging courses. The course is not a huge amount of work, however the essays and midterm exam are graded extremely harshly. Points are taken off on the midterm exam for literally no reason (some of the TFs even agreed). The essays are essentially all ethical writing –– not a great choice for science concentrators if you don't like writing ethical/philosophical essays. This is all pretty unfortunate, given that the topics covered in the course are actually interesting and relevant and the professor is very accomplished and established in the field. Section is also hugely unfortunate –– it is a massive waste of time and includes the same activity with a barely–changed prompt every single week. Would recommend taking pass/fail if you really want to take this.
