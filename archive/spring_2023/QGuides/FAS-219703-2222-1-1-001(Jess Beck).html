I took this class purely out of curiosity and it was 100% worth it. Hands–on courses like this one are the best courses at Harvard, in my opinion.
This class is one of the most interesting classes I have ever taken! You will learn so much in a real way; no cramming is required because the hands–on classes after every lecture really help you absorb the information. If you have any interest in the human body or archaeology, take this course for sure! Also, Dr. Beck is one of the most knowledgeable professors I've ever had and is so great! Only caveat is that it IS difficult and grading is oddly weighted. But, it is over by the last day of classes– no assignments during reading period or finals which is great!
While there are some sections of the course that is feel very fast, it does get broken down in the hands–on components very well. The lecture slides are posted, so you can use time in class just to absorb information.
Take this class! It's pretty steady work but it's a very unique class and I loved it! Would be great for both pre–meds and non–pre–meds. There's a hands–on component where you get some experience with real bones and skeletal remains, which is always very cool.
It might seem overwhelming at first, but once you get used to the pace of the course, you will find it very fascinating. I would've regretted it if I had not taken this class. The course material is just so interesting, and Dr. Beck explains everything very clearly and was very helpful in my learning experience.
This is a fabulous course was a great instructor who has tons of experience in the field of bioarchaeology. I would highly recommend this course if you are interested in anthropology but especially if you intended to pursue a field in the health sciences.
Take this class! It was definitely one of my favorite courses that I have taken at Harvard. A lot of studying is needed to do well, but you are somewhat forced to space this out due to the quizes. The studying is not so bad as long as you have an interest in osteology and develop good study habits early on (highly recommend Anki or some other way of doing flashcards). The practical component of this course was super interesting because we applied knowledge from lecture directly to real bones and/or casts of real bones. Harvard can be very draining academically, but this course has genuinely reinvigorated my academic spirit. Overall, super highly recommend!!!
