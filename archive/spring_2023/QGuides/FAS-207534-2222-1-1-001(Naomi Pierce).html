This course is probably the best way to complete then foundational neuro requirement. Lectures were very enjoyable, and I found the section papers quite interesting as well. As there is a lot of material, and some of it can either overlap or be relevant to a particular topic, making very clear subsections/divisions in my notes was very helpful for studying. Don't fall behind on studying! The material itself is not difficult to understand. It's still quite a lot to keep fresh, and you don't want to be memorizing the particular details of that one example you are still not comfortable with the night before the exam. Investing time in the rough draft of the written assignments is probably the best strategy. Peer reviews are not always helpful, and giving yourself that extra time to discuss something in your draft with your TF can make the final draft just a one–hour improvement session.

The class itself is very interesting; you'll learn a lot about different aspects of some parts of the neurobiology and evolutionary biology fields, and the case studies they present is also informative. The lectures might be a bit dry, but the material they go over is intriguing enough that you'll do just fine if you already had a prior interest in these subjects. The grant proposals they make us write might be a little stressful, but it really helps that you'll get to experience the peer review process. Exams are a bit brutal since you'll have to rehash a lot of very specific knowledge (so taking extensive notes help, and so does paying attention in lecture AND section!) but otherwise, especially because lectures are also supplemented by a lot of videos so it won't become too repetitive nor dull, this course is pretty cool!
