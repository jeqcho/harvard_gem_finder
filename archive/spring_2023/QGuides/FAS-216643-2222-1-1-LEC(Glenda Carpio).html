What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Prof. Carpio is indubitably a brilliant and astonishingly well–read scholar of Black and African–American literature and indeed lit a fire under our class in most every discussion. A huge recommendation to all to take this class and get a real deep dive into the canonical 20th century AfAm lit, and if not possible , take a class with Carpio!
Take this course to gain insight into the subtle resistance contained within literature that is guarded from commodification and hidden away.
Professor Carpio is a brilliant reader and teacher. The syllabus is great, and she connects all the texts to each other & places them in U.S. history, showing their influence not only on the world of literature but the on the world in general. She shows how the writers of these works are not just authors, but theologians and politicians and comedians as well. Most of the course work is reading with a few short responses, which can be creative so they can actually be pretty enjoyable! Even if you've read some of the stuff on the syllabus, it's definitely worth it to read through them again with Professor Carpio– she will definitely open your eyes to some things you've never noticed before.
Take this course!! I cannot recommend it highly enough.
