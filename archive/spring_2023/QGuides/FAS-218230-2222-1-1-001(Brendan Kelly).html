Take this course. This was the first year the course was taught and you could see how much it improved during the semester. The course will teach math in a way that is applicable to the real world. Each math topic will overlap with a current issue the world is facing. The teaching staff will often bring in experts to talk about the issue they are facing and how math was used to solve the problem. One project that stood out was a gerrymandering project. Each student analyzed data sets from their home state and created mathematical models to decide if their state had indicators of engaging in gerrymandering. This project was directly applicable at the time because most states across the country were redrawing their district maps and getting ready for upcoming elections. The section concluded with a special guest from an independent redistricting committee discussing issues they are facing.
I cannot recommend this class enough. Coming to Harvard, I was nervous to take math, but Brendan Kelley is the best teacher I've had in my entire life.
