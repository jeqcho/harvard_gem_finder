take this class if you want to learn excel and be super comfortable with doing simple calculations on it and using Excel formulas. The 3 hour, once a week lectures are pretty painful to sit through, so if you do go to every lecture, make sure to read the Ross textbook because it'll save your life. the textbook fills in the gaps in your understanding that may remain even after lecture, so it's super helpful to read it and annotate the lecture slides while reading it. The homeworks are graded by completion, and since they're so simple sometimes, they give you a false sense of security about how the exams may just be that simple as well. But they're not. Make sure to do the practice exam beforehand, because the actual exams are nothing like the homeworks—they're so much harder and more complex!!
I would like to tell future students that this course provides a very broad overview of financial markets. The weekly psets and exams are taken in Excel, so it helps with skills on that front, but there is not constant practice or use of Excel to help with skills.
This is a great class. You will get comfortable working with financial data in excel (financial statements, DCFs,...). Helps develop a tool in the tool belt for finance internships/jobs. Not super demanding with work. Midterm and final are tough but its tough for everyone. The class is curved. Bergstresser is dope too. Super laid back. I recommend it.
