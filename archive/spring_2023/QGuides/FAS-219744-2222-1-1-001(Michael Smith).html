As the course is named, this is a beginner CS course that emphasizes a computational thinking approach. For those of you who are considering CS50 or CS32, I believe CS32 has a much more natural and supportive method of teaching a beginner how to code. I came into the class without any prior coding experience. For years, I've wanted to learn but there's always been this sense of intimidation in addition to feeling overwhelmed in navigating programming on my own. Your first introduction to coding can shape your entire perspective, so I wanted a course that focused on fundamentals and did them well. I can say that CS32 gave me a solid foundation in python, helped me develop a problem–solving mindset, and gave me a new sense of confidence in my capabilities. Each class focuses on a problem to solve for the day, and we follow along as our professor breaks down the problem and solves it with code. This is not a class that formally teaches syntax. You learn the syntax by doing, kind of like learning a language by living in the country. For me, and I believe for many of us, we really struggled with the first and second pset because there is so much that is new. For instance, downloading python, setting up your computer, and then of course doing the actual coding can be extremely intimidating at first. Please know that this is completely normal, and while it's not fun in the moment, that challenge and discomfort will pay off at the end of the course. Instead of a final test, we have a final project and I didn't see how we would be able to design and solve a problem on our own after 3 months of coding. You'll be surprised because all of your work over the semester comes together in amazing ways in this final project. The final project was a highlight of the course for me. Application is key in learning CS. You won't always understand what's going on in class. You can feel lost, but when you work on the psets, you start to make those connections and by the end of each assignment, you'll have learned so much. For that same reason, a final project directed and made by you is the best opportunity to apply your knowledge and it will be incredibly rewarding to see what you are able to accomplish. Most importantly, the manner in which we learn to dissect a problem is the essence of programming and establishing this mindset early on will put everything into context, no matter how dry or difficult, in the future. Professor Smith and the teaching staff were very considerate of the pacing and they truly tried to give us the time, resources, and encouragement to guide us in this journey. A huge reason why the course was done so intuitively and naturally was because Professor Smith and the teaching staff understood what the perspective of a beginner is like. They eased us into CS and the first pset in a supportive way, and it's sometimes overlooked how important the awareness and dedication to the student experience is. Doing CS for the first time can be scary, but the CS32 staff definitely has the right mindset and will care about adjusting the course to best suit its students. What may surprise you is how much confidence you gain by going through this course. Coding can be frustrating and even the smallest mistakes could cause you to be stuck for hours. When you force yourself to sit there, make yourself understand every line of code, brainstorm different approaches to solving a subtask –– ultimately, you learn to have patience, be resourceful, and you'll see that you are able to solve any problem. Sometimes, it's better to resist getting help immediately because when it's just you and the code, you know you are going to have to be the one to solve the problem. And yes, you actually will be able to solve every problem –– it will amaze you. I went from being intimidated by coding to creating a final project that solves a real world problem on my own. Not just with programming, but in all respects, I have a great sense of confidence in myself because this course pushed me to realize there's a solution to every problem. If you want to learn how to code in a natural, problem–solving approach, CS32 is a great choice. The staff cares about making this transition as painless as possible for you. Focusing on python instead of learning multiple languages eases off the intimidation as well. Overall, if you're a beginner and you want to understand the spirit of programming, this is class will be a great experience for you.
