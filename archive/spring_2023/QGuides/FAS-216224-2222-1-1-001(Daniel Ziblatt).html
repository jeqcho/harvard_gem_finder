What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Fantastic course. Ziblatt is a phenomenal lecturer, and the course content is cohesive and compelling. The course is especially good at bridging theoretical conceptions of democratization and case studies. I've felt like I've gained more knowledge from this course than any other I've taken at Harvard so far. Note that the course is mainly historical and only spends about three weeks on modern democracies.
Take this class! One of the best professors I’ve ever had and extremely interesting course material!
I HIGHLY recommend this course! It is rooted in fascinating historical cases, so I never felt bored or bogged down by abstract political theories. The readings are interesting, the work load is manageable, and lectures are very straightforward. Professor Ziblatt is an exciting lecturer who spreads enthusiasm for the topic, evident in the eagerness of people to bombard him with questions at the end of class.
Great course! Ziblatt is a really accessible and engaging professor. very relevant material for current political climate too.
I enjoyed this class, particularly in the way that it blended government and history. I really appreciated how Professor Ziblatt really took the time to consider really relevant historical case studies and how they apply to our contemporary political moment. The structure of the class works very well and intuitively, as you progress from democratic breakthroughs in the first half of the semester and then to breakdowns in the second half. Professor Ziblatt is quite funny and engaging and it's a real treat to be able to take a class from him.
This is a great class to take if you want to a historical background to contemporary democratization. The teaching staff are wonderful and workload very manageable.
This is a really great class for understanding why democracy comes about, how it breaks down, and how it is backsliding today. Great survey of case studies across the world and also offers many opportunities for creatively applying the theory we learn.
This course feels like a history class that is structured within political science theories.
