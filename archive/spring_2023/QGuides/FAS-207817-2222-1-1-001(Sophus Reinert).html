This class is so awesome. Professor Reinert is both insanely knowledgeable and super chill. The HBS cases are really diverse and interesting; we read about everything from the birth of capitalism in Italian city–states and the Ebola epidemic in Liberia to climate change in Bangladesh and the future of US–China relations. I'd recommend coming into this with a background in economics; you'll get by fine without it, I think you get a lot more out of the cases if you're already somewhat knowledgeable about macro (e.g., took AP Econ in high school, are willing to read a basic intro Econ textbook before term, etc.).
Such an amazing course. Sophus is an absolutely incredible professor– funny, clearly a brilliant thinker, and an unmatched speaker, not to mention he seems to have led the coolest life of anyone I've ever met. He led really thought–provoking and fascinating discussions, and every class went by so quickly. I will say that having a decent background in econ/politics seems to be very helpful with regards to getting fully engaged in these conversations; I came into this with relatively little knowledge on the topics being discussed, and thus had less to contribute, but still learned so much and had a lot of fun.
see above.
This course is a mix of history, economics, and international relations. They are not all contemporary topics by any means, but draw lessons across different centuries. Come with a bit of economic background knowledge and you'll learn much more. Professor Reinert is a very engaging teacher, honed through years of experience, and adapts his teaching style to the classroom. Pretty fun class content–wise and discussion–wise.
If you’re interested in learning about capitalism as a history and a power for change, take this class.
Please take this course. The professor was incredible and any freshman would be lucky to get into this class.
This course was LIFECHANGING! Professor Reinert is the absolute best; he engages everyone in the class and is a phenomenal professor overall. The HBS cases were all so different but equally interesting; I learnt a ton! Take this class even if you don’t have an interest in pursuing an economics concentration; it will be the best decision you ever make!
