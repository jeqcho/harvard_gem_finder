Take this class if you have any interest, at all, in Russian literature! Prof. Buckler is wonderful and committed to ensuring the experience is worthwhile. The actual course texts are fantastic and you will find at least one new favourite author.
If you are looking for a professor who is genuinely interested in the success of her students, passionate about the texts and the subject, and want an environment in which to read and fall in love with Russian literature, this course is for you. Your insights from the course will stay with you for a long time!!! It was an absolute joy to take.
If you want to read several of the greatest works of Russian literature in one semester, take this course.
I cannot recommend taking this class enough, even (perhaps especially) for those who have not yet taken a literature course at Harvard. It is accessible, without losing any of its depth for accessibility. The buy–in amidst the students was remarkable, and is driven by Professor Buckler and Brett –– they know how to elicit insightful discussion, and balance providing context on the texts and their own literary comments with actively drawing on student contributions. You will want to spend time on this course; not necessarily because it demands onerous amounts of time, but because Professor Buckler and Brett will make you want to give the texts and other materials in class their due, and to immerse yourself in all they have to offer. I would go to pains to include this in my class schedule; you will come out of the class with an appreciation of some of the greatest works of literature, as well as a solid grasp of the historical and personal context which informed them.
This is perhaps the best course I've taken at Harvard. If you are at all interested in Russian literature, I would highly recommend that you take this course. Dr. Buckler does a great job explaining the texts and placing them in their proper sociohistorical contexts. The reading can be a bit heavy at times, but it is always extremely engaging , and the teaching staff is very understanding about other deadlines and outside circumstances that might affect class performance. Again, if you are at all interested in Russian literature, I would highly recommend that you take this course!
This course is the ideal elective at Harvard — a rich syllabus, thoughtful instructor, flexible assignments, and amazing works of Russian culture. With no previous background in Russian literature or arts, I took the course out of curiosity and can say definitely that it was one of the best classes I’ve ever taken at Harvard as someone with eight semesters behind me.
This class has been one of my favorite classes here at Harvard, and I can't say enough good things about it. I've been wanting to gain some exposure to Russian literature for a long time now, and this class definitely helped me do that. Not only does the array of materials give a very good introduction to Russian literature, but I also really enjoyed each of the pieces we got to explore. Prof. Buckler and Brett are also absolutely lovely – it's very obvious that they're passionate about the course, and they make the classroom a very welcoming and engaging space for everyone to explore this material, regardless of their academic background. Ultimately, if you're even remotely thinking about taking this class, I would highly recommend that you do!
TAKE THIS CLASS! This class has been my favourite class at Harvard so far (and to be honest I can't imagine it being beaten) and I cannot recommend it enough. Professor Buckler and Brett are both incredibly knowledgeable and passionate and it comes across in both class and section. Each class is meticulously prepared by Professor Buckler and she will wow you with the sheer amount of fascinating information about each book – background information on the author, the time, other writers' quotes about it, eye–opening close analysis. It is also a discussion based class which makes it very easy to engage with. I was GRIPPED every single class. Brett is also amazing and runs such an interesting and lovely section, that I was always excited to go to. I looked forward to this class every day I had it, and I felt like it just opened up my world. It made me realise just how amazing courses at Harvard can be. The deep dive into Anna Karenina was just unbelievable. TAKE IT TAKE IT TAKE IT!!!
I would tell them that they should appreciate every moment of it. It's one of life's great joys to study literature with people who love it deeply and know how to teach it, and Dr. Buckler and Brett are two such people. I hope that they enjoy it at much as I did.
TAKE THIS CLASS. This course is one of my favorites at Harvard. You learn/read a lot but never does it feel overwhelming. The workload is very manageable if you're willing to pace out the reading. Our syllabus originally had a midterm, a final project (usually a paper), and a take home final, but Professor Buckler canceled the final and distributed the weight elsewhere. She's great, and very accessible. She's very excited to teach the class and interact with the students and this only improves the classroom experience. She is also extremely willing to move deadlines for people if they communicate in advance.
This course teaches you a lot about canonical Russian literature in a really interesting way, but more importantly, it makes you appreciate great literary works and encourages you to read more. It's very good for your personal development and mental health. The professor and the TA were very friendly and helpful, and I learned a lot in a fairly relaxed manner.
This course is an absolute treasure (not an easy gem, but a truly great course that you won't regret taking). It does require some work, but it's totally worth it to read some of the best literature in the world. Prof Buckler is incredible, don't miss the chance to take a class with her. The overall classroom environment was incredibly welcoming and fun. It's great to know the names of all your classmates, and Prof Buckler and Brett really facilitated a positive classroom energy. Fantastic readings and course materials, including film, opera, and ballet. I learned so much! One of my all–time favorite classes at Harvard and one that I know I'll look back on positively for years to come.
