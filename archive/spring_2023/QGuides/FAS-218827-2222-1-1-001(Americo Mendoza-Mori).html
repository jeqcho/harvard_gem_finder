Might be smart to take the intro to Latinx studies course first but otherwise, the courseload is manageable and the content is interesting!
You should take it if you are looking for a manageable course; however, there is a lot of tedious busy work and corny material.
This class does not require many hours per week, but the grading felt arbitrary at times.
The professor is great but the class is not
If you are a Latinx students or you are curious about this community, please do not hesitate this class! You will not regret it!
This course is extremely important for anyone interested in Latinidad, media, Latinx people, and culture in general to take. I'm grateful that it exists, and I deeply enjoyed absorbing its content and applying it to my assignments. I felt very connected to it, and am ready to carry what I learned with me. The downside is the way that it was taught, as there is certainly room for improvement. The expectations for students of this course were sky high, which is acceptable only if they had told us about them. Once our first few assignment grades rolled in, we were hit with rubrics and expectations that hadn't existed a few weeks before. I felt lied to. This only continued as assignments that were not on the syllabus were popping up, sometimes just 24 hours before they were due. In a college course, clear communication, expectations, and following a syllabus at least somewhat closely is crucial for the well–being and learning experience of the students.
Take this class! You can learn a lot about the topic and the teaching staff are very genuine people that are great to reach out to outside of class.
Look, will you do well in this class, yes. Will you be totally engaged the whole time? Probably not. The class gets very repetitive and you can grow very frustrated. But the teaching staff is very nice and honestly a lot of the videos you watch in class are very interesting.
It's a good class but be prepared for the unknown for better and worse. The Professor and the teaching staff are all very insightful on the subject. However, the classes tend to be disorganized and tended to go overtime. For example, the professor would prompt conversations with classmates only to give you 2 minutes, which was never enough. However, the materials and readings are pretty good.
Definitely take this course! It teaches you very interesting things about Latinx media and what representation actually is. The assignments are few, and the class usually consists of videos, music, guest speakers, and conversation. Prof. Americo is also very kind and enthusiastic about the course. It's truly a gem.
I appreciated the class assigned relatively shorter readings and videos and only expected biweekly discussion posts from us. I felt the course had structure until about halfway throughout the semester, when lectures became the professor introducing a topic and then having about five classmate breakout sessions to round out the lecture. In addition, we were not told a midterm would happen in the class until about a week before the exam. All of this said, the entire teaching staff was kind and open to discussing the content outside of class.
