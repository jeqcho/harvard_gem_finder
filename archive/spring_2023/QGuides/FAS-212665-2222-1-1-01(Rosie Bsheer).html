It’s a good course, and very manageable as long as you show up to lectures and take notes. Definitely get the Gelvin textbook, because the course pretty much exactly traces it, so it’s really helpful.
phenomenal course all around. you will learn so much and deepen your understanding of the making of the modern middle east from a historical perspective but you will also develop a critical lens to understand 'international relations' and see modern issues in a more critical light. Bsheer and Hanna (tf) are fantastic and super knowledgeable and make this course a must–take
I took this class to gain an overview of contemporary issues in the Middle East, however, it was too historical for my liking. I would have preferred a course just on the 20th century (maybe the late 19th, but nothing too much before this). The style of this class is very much for history students, not those interested in contemporary politics/policy. It is also quite a high workload.
Whether you're middle eastern and have grown up there or don't know where it even is on the map, this course is great. Fantastic overview of the history of the whole region over time.
This course is a wonderful way to build a basic background in the history of the modern Middle East in an accessible, informative, and engaging way, through an interdisciplinary lens. It is an important foundational course to understanding the history, politics, and society of the modern Middle East.
Really good course overall! The content is so important given how the Middle East is discussed in the news. The reading load is a bit high, but I thought they were all chosen well and with direct purposes.
This class is a fantastic introduction to the study of the Middle Eastern history. It won't be the right fit for everyone since the readings are intensive, but I really learned a lot.
I would definitely recommend they take this course. It is a bit challenging in terms of the material, but you will learn a lot!
Very interesting class. A truly Middle Eastern prospective on Middle Eastern history (which is extremely hard to find at Harvard). Very enlightening discussions.
This is a really great class! Take it to learn way more about the Middle East than you probably know now and dispel the conventional narratives you've been taught about the region. I've learned so much about the region and now feel like I have a much better understanding for various aspects of the modern Middle East. Pay attention in lecture because Prof. Bsheer goes very fast, but you'll learn a lot. Go to office hours with both Prof. Bsheer and your TF!! You'll learn so much because they both have so much knowledge that they just don't have time to share in lecture/section. Be prepared –– the class has considerable assigned readings (usually 120 pages/week). You can get away with not doing some of them, but it's definitely in your best interest to do them (and you'll learn much more by doing them). Other than readings, the course isn't too bad assignment–wise –– I had to spend a lot of time studying for the midterm and final, as well as for preparing a section presentation, but other than that, my only work for the class was reading. Also don't feel intimidated if you've never taken a history course before –– the course is structured so that no prior knowledge is needed, and even though we were in class with grad students, I never felt out of my depth.
This class was fairly disorganized in terms of the lecture material—we jumped around a lot from one week to the next, without sufficient context/background sometimes. Workload is manageable, but keep in mind that there are weekly discussion board posts which tend to be tedious. I liked the section component, where one student would present on the readings weekly. The clas has a definite political agenda—which I don’t mind, but I do think some important facts/context were left out because of it.
Professor Bsheer is very passionate about what she teaches, and always allows around 10 minutes or more at the end of class for a Q&A on her lectures. Something I didn't expect from the course was how deep we went into the historiography and power dynamics behind knowledge production in the Middle East, which was incredibly interesting.
Professor Bsheer is fantastic and you can really learn so much in this class! Additionally the topics are super interesting, so you will want to learn! Some weeks there can be a lot of readings, but usually the readings are pretty engaging as well. The exams (midterm and final) are worth a big part of your grade, so you can't afford to do badly on those, but there aren't really other assignments besides the weekly readings, so its very manageable to balance this class with the rest of your courseload.
You should take it to understand the modern Middle East
