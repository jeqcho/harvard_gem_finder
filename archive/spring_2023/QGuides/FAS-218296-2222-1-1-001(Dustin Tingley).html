This is good class. I took it in its first ever year, so there were definitely some hiccups and learning moments, but the material is interesting and applicable. Topic areas range across all of environmental policy, and if you have any interest in the field at all, you should take this class. I wish there had been a little more guidance on writing policy or investigating what actual legislation says, but I think the broader context is more welcoming to a wider group of people. There should be more courses in this topic area across all departments and the professors seem to be aware of their responsibility to set a precedent in teaching an important topic in an accessible way.
This was the first year the course was taught so my class was the first to experience and be the guinea pigs. Let me just say that this course needed some major updating. I felt like they said we don't need any background entering this course, but I would disagree. I was very lost on a lot of the readings assigned (and they were very many and very long). And when they were discussed, mainly in sections, it was not in a way that helped me understand them. For assignments, we have two policy memos assigned. The first one was the worst because they did not even let us know certain expectations for the memo, and did not show us how to write one correctly. The prompt was also very vague and unspecific that it became hard to know what the TF's were looking for. The second prompt was the same way, but all the sections complained to the TF's and they made it a bit more detailed which made it easier. But we made the professors add a third optional memo because many of them (including me) did horribly on the first one. But the organization of the course was mediocre at best because there seemed to not be a lot of understanding between the professors and TF's. The whole class was also based on the Boston Project, which is a semester–long thing, and I felt like for the most part, this part was ok, and they had the little things planned out (except for when we had to conduct interviews, but that was not really their fault). One good thing about the course is that they are very welcome to feedback, and most of the things people brought up that could help that class, it was addressed. They definitely knew that this course was not perfect and was willing to fix some problems that arose.
This class is very interesting, but is also a newer class and has some things to work out. If taken, professors are extremely receptive to the class, so communicate what is or is not working.
