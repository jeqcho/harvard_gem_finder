What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The best philosophy course I have taken at Harvard. I highly recommend it!
It's a lot of logical analyses; if you are a math person this might be a great class for you, but if you are more of a Continental philosopher, stay far away.
Lectures can be a bit dry (every lecture is just one long monologue), but Professor Goldfarb is a pleasure to listen to and the material is interesting as long as you're invested and interested enough in the discussions. The course quickly turns into a course on the philosophy of language, which I did not expect and definitely caught me off guard.
This class is worth taking if you have the opportunity. The content is tough but engagement with it is well–rewarded.
If you have any interest in logic or the development of analytic philosophy, then this course is for you. Professor Goldfarb is truly an expert on the subject and every lecture is lucid and enlightening. I was initially drawn in by Wittgenstein's name and though his section of the course was interesting, it was Goldfarb's comprehensive treatment of Frege that was the unexpected highlight.
