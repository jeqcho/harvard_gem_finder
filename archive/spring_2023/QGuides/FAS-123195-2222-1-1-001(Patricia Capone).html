Anth 1130/1131 is a great yearlong introduction to archaeology! Even if you have no plans to study archaeology, you should still take this class for the experience, and I'm so glad I was able to do so. It has a very manageable amount of work, as well
You have to take this class if you took anthro 1130 and it is totally well worth your time! The assignments can be a lot of work, and both they and the class/lab work can be very time consuming, but as long as you are somewhat interested in what you dug up last semester, this semester will be a blast. Also, if you studied hard for anthro 1130's final, it makes this class much easier when it comes time to catalogue.
This is a fantastic class! No need for any experience in archaeology. If you're looking for a non–traditional classroom–style class where you can learn hands–on, you should definitely take this class! The teaching staff is amazing and you'll definitely build community over the course of a year in this class.
If archaeology interests you at all, TAKE THIS CLASS. It’s like a mini field school that you don’t have to pay an extra $2000 for!
I highly recommend taking this course, regardless of your intended field of concentration. It is a fantastic course to learn more about Harvard's history, and you will have a very unique opportunity to connect with and learn in Harvard Yard.
If you have the privilege to take this class, you would be a fool not to do so. Genuinely and truly I cannot emphasize enough the amount that you will learn in this class and the relationships that you will develop with not only your fellow classmates, but also with the incredibly teaching staff. This was truly one of the greatest highlights of my year and of my Harvard experience.
As a senior, I can say that this is the best class I have ever taken at Harvard. Along with its Fall counterpart, you learn so much in the classroom and you look forward to both class and section! The teaching staff genuinely cares about you and wants you to succeed and by the time the class is over, you actually make an impact in the field of archaeology through your interpretations of what you excavated. Truly such a unique opportunity and one of my favorite times at Harvard!!
This class is really fun! You get to excavate in the yard for the fall semester and process all of your findings in the second semester. Don't be afraid to get dirty and go learn some archaeology!
