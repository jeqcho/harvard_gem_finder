What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The pre–class quizzes were a strange experience – a month into the term, I (and many others) were doing the readings during the quizzes, instead of before them. I definitely did poorly on some later quizzes (especially the math–oriented ones), but most were conceptually oriented and straightforward. Based on this course, I now believe that RL is a fairly repetitive field, and probably not for me.
The classes themselves were pretty entertaining. Professor Murphy is really dynamic and clearly loves the content, and makes the class very conversational. Breakout sessions are much less intimidating than they sound, and just involve talking to the people around you about a short, conceptual question (though it may still be very difficult). Sometimes, people from the Murphy group would come and present to get feedback, which was again very cool, though it would often be Professor Murphy and the TFs turning it into a pseudo–lab meeting.
The project is basically the entire grade. I would definitely suggest putting substantial work into it early and making something you're proud of, since this defines your experience in the class. There is support from the course staff to help you along the way. The poster session and writeup are just opportunities for you to show off the work you've done.
Keep up with the readings! Try to let them sink in over the course of several days as opposed to the night before
I LOVE SUSAN!! Compassionate professor that genuinely cares about your learning over the semester.
Have fun doing the project and start early!
Very good class but very theory–based and intended for mostly graduate students. No homework to help you check your understanding and relies mostly on your individual reading and understanding the material. Start thinking about the research/survey paper early to check with the teaching staff.
