What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a lot of fun!! I really enjoyed my time in class with my peers and with Amr. I think one of the most fabulous things he did (especially compared to other Arabic AA/AB profs) is incorporate Egyptian dialect / amiyya into the course, both in listening exercises and the language he used in the classroom (which was a blend of fusHa and amiyya). He also tries to teach various elements of culture from the Arabic world, including traditions, food, holidays, greetings, expressions, etc. As far as the work, you really need to study Arabic a little bit every day, between homework and reviewing vocab. But if you do, the course really isn't that much work––you just need to do it consistently.
This course is challenging but it is worth every drop of effort that you put in. The homework will eat up hours of your day and the tests will be challenging but, at least in my opinion, it is worth it. It is worth it when you are watching a tv show in Arabic and can understand it. It is worth it when a tourist asks you a question about Harvard in Arabic and you are able to help them. If you want to truly transformative foreign language experience this is the class for you.
You should be ready to put in the work. There's homework every night, and especially if you have no background in Arabic, it's not easy. But the language itself is fascinating and incredibly rewarding. I recommend it highly.
You will not regret taking this class! You will make a lot of friends and learn an amazing skill in speaking Arabic
I can only really speak on behalf of students walking in with an Arabic background, but for this individuals this class is probably going to not be as challenging –– which could be a good or a bad thing, depending on what the goals of the student are. As someone who walked in being able to speak and understand Arabic decently well, but very limited ability to read or write, I would say that we did not practice speaking as much as I would like, the listening exercises weren't really challenging at all, but I did significantly improve my reading and writing skills.
The Arabic language courses at Harvard are very well taught.
