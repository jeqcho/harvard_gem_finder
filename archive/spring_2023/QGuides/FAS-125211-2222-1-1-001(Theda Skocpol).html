Comments
This course was one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard because the discussions in lecture actually made me think about inequalities in America and what structures are allowing this inequality to persist. It's the first class I've taken in the government department that has actually talked about the why regarding these issues instead of just describing the what. Professor Skocpol is amazing and I would highly recommend taking a class with her if you can!
This is a great course if you like small seminars and close interaction with a prominent political science professor.
The class at times feels intense, lots of reading and high–level discussions. But I learned A LOT
Lots of reading but valuable insights from Professor Skocpol, that make it worth it
Take this class. Was great to have insight from Professor Skocpol. Very interesting discussion
Professor Skocpol is an icon. Incredibly knowledgeable, gives great feedback on final project deliverables, and invited us over for the last class to have dinner at her house. This class is pretty readings heavy (ranging from 80 to 200 pages of reading in a week), but they're really interesting readings, and I do recommend making the time to actually go through them properly rather than just skimming (though you can likely get away with skimming). I feel so much more informed about American inequality/polarization than before. You have to write four memos throughout the class, but it's nice because the first two memos are front loaded, and that you can choose the weeks in which you write the last two memos. Overall great vibes, strongly recommend this course!
