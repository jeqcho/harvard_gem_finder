Comments
Do not take this course with Bamberg. He has unreasonable expectations and will put your wellbeing below his desire to experiment. This class, though billed as a Geometry class, turned into a Memorization and Classical Greek class instead. AVOID THIS CLASS.
this class generally entail learning things on your own. Much of the material is taught via recorded YouTube videos or simply involves you reading Euclid yourself, and then presenting proofs in class
Avoid this course. There is no focus on concepts, and you will spend every week stressing out over the undecipherable homework problems, lecture notes, and other materials. Moreover, Bamberg assumes a base knowledge of Greek morphology and R coding, which are not listed as requirements– no time will be spent actually teaching you either of these, but you will be heavily graded on your ability to utilize them on the frequent in–class presentations (for the Greek) and homework problems. The geometries being studied are presented without any motivation, there is no structure presented for studying these geometries– most students were stressed out every week because we had no idea what was going on, and had to rely heavily on the CA to help us figure exactly what was being asked. The questions on the homework were almost always easy, but never provided deeper insight or understanding into the concepts being discussed. This class was the biggest waste of time of my Harvard career.
This class was very time–consuming between homework, flipped classroom self–teaching, preparing math presentations for the class, preparing Greek translations for the class, and projects. And there wasn't much of a break from work –– often, I would be at dinner and I'd get an email telling me I had to give a presentation on a new topic the next morning. I appreciate Paul's enthusiasm for (and knowledge of) the material, but I did not enjoy Math 130.

And to this day, I don't understand why such a big part of the class was learning Ancient Greek. It was not at all essential for learning the math. But if you like Ancient Greek, you will love this class...
