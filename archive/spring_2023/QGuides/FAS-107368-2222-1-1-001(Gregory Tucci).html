I'm writing this feedback as someone who took this course because I'm genuinely interested in the topics presented. I'm not a pre–med student, but I really wanted to learn about the underlying chemistry behind climate change, energy and batteries (for a potential concentration in MechE). However, the course managed to turn the materials that I'm genuinely interested in into something I dreaded throughout this semester.

The reason is because of the absurd amount of requirements and rigidity of the course. Personally, I found the material quite easy. I essentially self–studied 3 weeks worth of course material in the 3 days before every exam and averaged at 97 (for the midterms). The conceptual basis of all the units is pretty intuitive to understand (as someone with an honors chem background in high school), and then the rest of the work is just plug and chug.

HOWEVER, this course expects you to spend essentially 3–4 hours every week just doing plug and chug. Add onto this the 2 hour lab sessions, where you essentially transfer a few liquids, write down a few numbers and just wait around. Add onto this MANDATORY lectures and sections, in which I quickly learnt it was more useful to spend my time doing other work (since I could learn the same material in 1/10 of the time afterwards anyways). The PSETS are long and annoying. You know that feeling when you spend 10+ hours solving a really difficult physics or CS pset and feel really accomplished afterwards? Don't expect to feel the same way about PS11 psets. The only skill you'll be honing is using your calculator.

I understand that this is a personal perspective and that there are students who might have appreciated the extra practice and explanation. However, the course should make this extra practice OPTIONAL, and not make all students sit through the grueling sections that they might not need. Personally, I also don't learn well in large, in–person lecture halls, and it's ridiculous of the course to presume that this is the best learning method for all students. If lecture was not mandatory, the students who genuinely benefit from this style of learning can come, and the rest can learn the materials through other methods. As long as they LEARN THE MATERIALS, which should be what matters most.

For the course instructors—once again, I really appreciate your work (special shout–out to Lu Wang, I essentially learnt most of the materials through the textbook + your problem solving sections, and the way. you explain things is great). The content is quite interesting, and the general progression of the course neatly links the topics together.

However, please seriously consider my above feedback and have more faith in students' ability to decide for themselves how they learn best. If you want students to spend a lot of hours on the course regardless, maybe consider designing more conceptually challenging problems/bonus options, etc. for the intellectually curious rather than having a one–size–fit–all approach.

For students considering the course, my two cents is:
– If you're pre–med and looking for an easy grade boost (aka you have to put in hours of mind–numbing work but will probably get a good grade), sure, this is the course for you.
– If you're genuinely interested in learning the material, just get the textbook and skip this course. There are probably more useful things you can do with your time.

Section is the most useful thing ever. Go to every section, take notes, and do the practice questions you were not able to finish in section. The concepts are summarized in such a nice way that you can copy them down on your cheat sheet when it is time for an exam.
