What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Adeline was a phenomenal professor!! Just wish she could stay at Harvard full–time :((
Luckily, the class is designed in such a way that if you're having trouble understanding the material, it's not too difficult to get by. I really wish, and this applies across methods classes at Harvard, that we could try more to focus on understanding a few concepts very well, instead of a trying to master so many concepts, and inevitably failing. I often found it difficult to follow what was going on in class after the first 15 minutes.
I'd probably recommend students really dive deeply into the topics that were of most value to them and be comfortable staying at surface level for the other topics.
Taking a course in machine learning will be extremely useful, no matter what field of social or political science you work in. This class exposes you to a huge variety of techniques and gives you ideas for tackling a diverse range of questions using machine learning, including new data sources such as text as data.
This was a very fun, low–pressure introduction to machine learning that covers a good deal of Hastie, Tibshirani, and Friedman's "Elements of Statistical Learning" along with some more modern additions from text and network analysis. Adeline and Sooahn are fantastic instructors and people. I would recommend the course!
