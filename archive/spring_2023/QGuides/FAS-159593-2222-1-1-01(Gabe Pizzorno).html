I would highly recommend this class to those that are interested in the subject – it can be a bit of a hurdle to get involved as the material is so far into the past, and the readings are often quite dense on the topic matter.
This is a straightforward class that is very clear about its requirements and while the content can be a little bit dry at times, the teaching staff does their best to make it engaging. If ancient history is your thing, this class will be exactly what you expect and want from it.
If you like History I would recommend taking this class. Professor Pizzorno gave very impressive engaging lectures and Sama made section so much fun and so helpful!
This class is a little dry, but it was cool material to learn since it was all brand new.
It was a pretty life changing class –– it changed the way I view society/civilization/empires and also the way I think about how we think about the past. I took it pass/fail so it probably meant I wasn't burnt out from trying really hard on readings/essays, maybe it would have been worse if I had been more preoccupied with that. I just got to enjoy lecture :) But maybe it would have been better because I would have read more and written more. Who knows. Anyways, take this class! Learn more about the side of ancient history you hear less of!
This class was a highlight of my college experience. The material is generally interesting, although given that so much material is covered, everyone will have some parts that they like more than others. The papers are also really fun to write, and the final paper is particularly fun given that you can choose any topic that you want as long as it's somewhat related to the course material. Gabe is a fantastic lecturer, and he clearly put a lot of effort into the slides. He was also extremely accessible, and was always willing to meet and discuss things. Sama, the TF, was also a highlight. She was always incredibly helpful, and more than willing to meet and help with assignments. In other classes, I've always found section to be a waste of time, but I actually looked forward to section in this class every week –– it was by far the best section I've had in any class. Overall, take this class if you want an interesting, relatively chill elective with a dedicated professor. My one hesitation in recommending it is that not everyone is as interested in the ancient near east as I am, but I still think that many of the lessons of this course (how states develop, how propaganda is used, imperial ideology, etc.) are universal.
You WILL be inspired to become a historian from this class. You WILL learn something valuable each and every lecture. So many good things to say!!!!
