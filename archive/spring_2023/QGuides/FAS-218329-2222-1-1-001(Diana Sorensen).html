What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I might have given the class a higher score if it didn't have a section (and I feel quite strongly about this). Seminars most weeks usually went very well, and we ended on a positive note. The five–minute breaks were helpful. Then we had the beast that was section afterward, which was totally unproductive. In fact, this was one of the worst sections in my undergraduate career, not only because everybody was dead inside after already having spent two hours wading through complex Spanish literature (Julio Cortazar, while worthwhile to read, is also not very easy to follow, and unfortunately many of the texts we read erred on the confusing and complicated side) but also because we rehashed material that had already been covered in section to the point of dull repetition. In fact, Mauro insisted that we cover so many topics for the second or third time that we almost always left material hanging for the next week (and the words "lo que queda pendiente" will forever haunt my memory). In addition, I felt that the class wasn't very coherent: I believe parts of this class came from a previous class, Spanish 88 (Things in Literature) but I can't find its syllabus to compare with. Anyway, it felt like some things were taken from Spanish 88 and smushed together with new parts. There were certainly many texts that I enjoyed (El zumbayllu, Neruda/Borges/Mistral) and others that I didn't, but on the whole, I wish we spent more time drawing throughlines between each week's themes, and my god, spent less time in section.
I do not usually enjoy literature, but the teachers and other students generated so much enthusiasm. I came to enjoy the analysis and thinking about the different relationships that exist to objects. Overall, a lot of work but enjoyable.
Prof. Sorensen is a wonderful, funny, insightful professor. Her class was engaging every week. I cannot recommend this class enough!
Latin American literature has an extremely unique style which is well–worth the readings.
Jot down discussion questions in the margins as you read
Take this course! It is a time commitment, the course readings can be long, but Mauro and Profe Sorensen create a great class environment and really help every student on their journey.
This course has such interesting readings and is an excellent primer to consider the material world.
