What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class provides a great community while you're going through the thesis–writing process. Take advantage of your connections with your peers. I found it really helpful to meet with a fellow student also writing in medical anthropology ~5 days before the thesis was due. We spent a couple of hours sitting together reading each other's full drafts, and then we provided each other comments. This was so helpful, it it shaped how I devoted my energy in the final 5 days of revisions. Also, your course head and section leader are fantastic resources who will provide indispensable feedback on your draft chapters if you simply reach out to them. Don't wait until the last month to seek feedback from the course head! Just because he/she won't be commenting on your submissions to Canvas, you can still reach out to him/her to seek feedback.
This is a requirement so you will have to do it if you are writing a thesis! Definitely be prepared to work hard – try to get as much as you can done early to get ahead of things!
Anthro99b is a great class and you will not only get closer to your peers but also to the teaching staff who has been with you, willing to support you as you continue your thesis journey. The spring thesis writing is difficult especially if you saved a significant portion of your thesis until now, but it is possible!
