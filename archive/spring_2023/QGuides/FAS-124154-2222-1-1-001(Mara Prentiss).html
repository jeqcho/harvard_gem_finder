I really enjoyed 15c, and there are lots of reasons to take it (there were concentrators in physics, astrophysics, engineering, etc. in the class). Don't be too scared of the workload, but you should be prepared to learn new math along with the new physics (which is different from 15a or 15b). In total, though, the workload is not too bad, and there is a lot of physics that is widely applied to the world.
Take 21b before taking this class!
Professor Huth is a pretty good lecturer. My main problem with this class is that the practice tests given out were from a different year with a different professor, and the format of the actual exam wasn't the same as what we were given. If they could fix this, the class would be better.
If Mara is running lab that's a good sign. The class honestly isn't too much work, I hadn't taken Physics 15B though so in the last third of the class I was pretty lost. So take 15B unless you already know a lot about circuit stuff
This course is unnecessarily difficult, and the material frankly isn't interesting enough to justify the headache. Once you understand a topic or concept, it's usually actually super simple. The only reason this class is difficult is because of the way it's taught. I found it very difficult to learn the course material. Lectures move fast and often breeze through difficult or confusing concepts, and there isn't a good textbook. The lecture notes are not enough to understand the class. Every week, I'd spend hours on the internet trying to learn the necessary material for the course using other sources.
This may be one of the intro physics classes, but you might be surprised by how much you learn! In the beginning, the pace gets off a bit slow and seemed like a review of a lot of previous classes, but after the midterm topics got a lot more interesting.
This is a well–taught and interesting class that you should take. However, understand that this is a "real class" in terms of workload; do not assume that you can use this as a padding course while your main energy is directed elsewhere. This is especially true in light of last year's course reviews, which (in my opinion) understate the commitment that the class requires. This is not to say that it is a ridiculously hard class; rather, it is an average–difficulty STEM class that deserves the same attention and respect that any other average–difficulty STEM class does.
The grading is too harsh and the lectures seem unorganized. Professor Huth is a super cool and interesting person but his teaching style is not the most effective. The final exam was also very difficult and not at all like the practice exam which was the exam from the previous year. Dr. Prentiss is amazing though and the only reason I liked labs.
Lots of fun and a really cool way to learn about physics
You're probably taking this because it's required anyways... It has the least hand–holding of all of the 15's, but wasn't too hard. In my opinion it was easier than 15b but harder than 15a. I wouldn't take it as an elective – the exams were difficult, even the take–home final! Huth is a good professor, and pretty flexible, too. His demos are great and keep it fun. Otherwise I feel pretty neutral about this class, it didn't cause stress but I definitely couldn't slack off.
