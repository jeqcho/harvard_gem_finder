If you’re at all interested in the military or any particular wars or regions, this is a great 97. Definitely do the readings and be prepared to discuss the major themes. The tutorial model is really good for receiving critical feedback.
Take an easy class load to balance, but really buy into the tutorial experience. You can get a lot out of it by leaning into the hours and the assignments, and get close with your Professor. Take Penslar's class!
This class is a lot of work but will be very rewarding. I recommend military history with Dr. Penslar if you are interested in this subject. Make sure to keep on top of the deadlines because you will likely have an essay due almost every week and you do not want to get behind.
Make sure to work very hard on the papers and participate in class because those are the only assignments all semester.
This class is a class not in SUBSTANCE, but in METHODOLOGY––which is its purpose. Don't approach this class thinking you'll learn mere facts and dates––rather, you'll learn how to write historically. Pay attention to the prompts––don't merely write argumentative essays that you're used to from previous (gov) classes. It's a wonderful class––it's what you make of it.
I would tell them that it's a great way to become a better thinker and writer
Do NOT wait until the last day to do your papers. You're probably taking 97 because you have to, so take whichever topic is most interesting to you. Assignments are the same. Not a huge difference, but from talking with others, it sounds like this is one of the more strict sections of the 97 offerings. Meets for full 2–hours–45–minutes. Classes feel long sometimes, but the course content for military history is super interesting.
