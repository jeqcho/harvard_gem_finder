The course is a lot of reading for a gen ed, and the lectures can be a bit dry. I also found the assignments a bit confusing in terms of what the teaching staff wanted. I did learn more about the region and had a good intro to poli sci theory, but I think the course could have been executed better.
You will write a 4 page, 6 page, 12 page (in a group of 4), and a 15 page paper individually throughout the class. There is a lot of reading, most of it I did not use for my papers. If you do the readings, lecture doesn't teach you anything. if you don't read, then you have to take very close lecture and section notes.
Just be warned that the reading can really pile up if you don't stay on top of it. Honestly this is probably closer to a regular government class than a Gened (definitely not a GEM). As a neuroscience concentrator, I'm not sure how much easier the class is for someone who concentrates in humanities. You can definitely recover if you get behind. You'll learn so much content and have a much better informed view of the Middle East for sure after taking this. But you definitely have to be willing to put in a lot of work to do well.
