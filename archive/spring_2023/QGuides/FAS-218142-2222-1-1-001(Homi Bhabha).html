What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Bhaba is a highly esteemed figure in his field, but his seminars will not be appealing to many students. The reading is challenging to understand, let alone theorize on. More often than not, seminar turns into a lecture on specific lines of the text with few openings for students to contribute. Though Prof Bhaba frequently asks students to disagree with him or provide suggestions for the course, the space does not feel particularly welcoming to either of those options. The class does not encourage multiplicity in readings because Prof Bhaba is quite insistent on a particular reading (not necessarily the popular one) of the figures we cover. He can be a bit dismissive of arguments/interpretations he doesn’t agree with, and he makes this clear in class. This is understandable, considering he is a well–acknowledged scholar who presumably has strong scholarly opinions on the course readings, but quite a departure from other seminars where instructors entertain dissenting thoughts. The course is 'guised as an open discussion but is not one in reality.

It may not be his intention, but his seminar is not a comfortable place to speak. It’s a heavy, intense space that feels as if any contribution needs to be a well–developed argument with a conclusion. Initial thoughts or small commentary do not have a place in the seminar, especially because student contribution interrupts Prof Bhaba’s lecture. Conversation often takes an unexpected turn, which makes preparing for the seminar challenging.

Some students will not mind, and some students might actually prefer this environment. Out of my seminar, I've talked to at least half of my classmates who feel similarly as I do about the course's shortcomings. It is an interesting exploration of theory, but not a particularly welcoming or well–structured one.
Only take this course if you know you love theory and are open to sometimes vague/general/highly esoteric discussions. If you think you might be interested in theory and want to explore it, this is not the class for you.
it's a great course where you get to study a lot of different theory and literature from different themes
This course can feel intimidating at times, but it is worth it to get through. Professor Bhabha can also seem intimidating, but he's incredibly personable and hears out everyone who wants to speak. If the syllabus attracts you, it will be worth it to encounter these texts with Professor Bhabha!
Definitely take this course! Participation is half the grade, so as long as you do the readings and show up intentionally, you'll be good. Professor Bhabha is brilliant and says something illuminating every class.
Very interesting class with productive discussions. However, definitely felt dominated by those who had theory/philosophy/social studies backgrounds. Prof. Bhabha talks a lot too, but it is interesting to learn from him nonetheless.
Take this course if you can! The theoretical work you do is rigorous, and challenging, but Professor Bhabha, Paul, and your fellow students are working through the issues with you with a remarkable patience and grace. You will learn a lot of frameworks and ideas and ways of thinking which'll be applicable to other theoretical texts you pursue, and also to your growth as a philosophical and historical thinker, even though this is an English course.
