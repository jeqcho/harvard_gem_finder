What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Richard ji is the funniest, eccentric professor with a great depth of knowledge of the language and culture. It's truly a treat to learn with him and I am consistently encouraged to continue through, even when I feel like I'm at a dead end. All he cares about is our learning in the most authentic of ways.
Richard ji is a fantastic instructor, and you will improve your Urdu reading and writing greatly in this class. However, you should only take 102B if you're very interested in Urdu, or the assignments will feel tedious. This class was significantly harder and more time–intensive than 101A/101B, something you should keep in mind when deciding to continue from Intro to Intermediate.
You will find a community of friends who are all equally passionate about learning, and engaging with, Hindi–Urdu who you will stick with outside and beyond this class. Richard ji is a phenomenal mentor and facilitator of this community as well, and through just engaging with him alone, you will gain a lot of cultural understanding of the language and its modern transformations/applications.
Attend class as much as you can, get to know your peers, and ESPECIALLY attend the weekly chai–and–chaat hosted by the Harvard South Asian Studies department. So worth the time!
