This is a great class if you are interested in music and music theory. Dr. Jake is an excellent professor and does a great job combining theoretical explanations with tangible musical examples. The section component is great as well, as it helps to reinforce concepts Dr. Jake covers in section. I'd highly recommend this class!
If you want to learn how to work with sheet music and be more conversant in basic music terminology / common practice, Music 2 is the course for you. If you play an instrument and would like to use this as a platform to deepen your understanding of introductory music theory, you may want to reconsider. Music 2 is by no means a bad class, but it was not what I had expected, as it involved lots of tedious homework assignments that felt like a chore more so than a stimulating exercise. This is just the nature of the course content, as the only way to really learn scales, for example, is to write out a ton of scales. The course's teaching team is supportive and excellent, regardless of your prior music background. And if this is something that you want to learn, the teaching team will enable you to do so.
I would tell future students that the content given in the actual lectures should be considered separate from the textbook. They will overlap largely but putting all of your attention on the textbook and not on the lectures will put you in a position to be blind sighted later in the course.
This class has time consuming workbook exercises each week (a few hours long). If you do not have a background in music, this class will be quite difficult. The pace with which the material is taught is very fast. Professor Jakubowski is a great guy and puts together lectures well. However, there is often too much content to cover.
Definitely learned a lot, but pretty difficult if you have no previous ear training / music background
Super interesting class! I think that it is managable if you put in the work and effort. There are some repetitive aspects to the class and there are times where it moves a bit too fast while there are other times where it moves a bit too slow, but overall if you want a preliminary exploration into music theory this is a great class for you!
I think the best part of this course was section. Overall, I think if you have music theory experience, you will find this course easy. If you don't, you might find it challenging. Lectures are ok.
I think that if you have a significant music theory background (even if you studied it on your own) you might do well to check out music 51a. As a person that had a pretty solid music theory background coming into this class, I found that its definitely geared toward beginners (as its supposed to be) and class can be a bit slow toward the beginning. However I think there is still a lot of practical value you can get from the class even if you have a music theory background. I understand arranging in classical music better now, and I feel like I got good chances to apply my knowledge in the projects. The projects were also pretty fun and not too burdensome in my opinion. Overall I think it was a well run and fair class.
This course is great to balance out a schedule while being low stress and actually learning stuff. Grading is also chill as long as you do the assignments with care. Overall really good course and the professor is super nice!
Take this class if you are actually interested in learning music theory. If you are, this class will teach you A LOT in a very short amount of time. You see the connections between many different genres of music; it is definitely not just classical. That being said, it can be tedious and boring at times, but that is how music theory is. If you just like listening to music and even playing a little of it sometimes, that might not be enough. You will be learning all of the fundamentals. They are fascinating, but they also require memorizing and practicing things which can get quite annoying after a while. The professor is great, the TFs are very helpful, and the projects fun. Does not require a lot of time. Probably no more than 2 hours per week to complete the simple, repetitive workbook exercises. In that sense, this is like a gem. You do have to work for the projects, but they are very fun, and likely more creative than difficult.
This class is definitely low stakes for those who are interested in taking a music theory class.
Great way to fulfill the arts and humanities divisional requirement. Super easy especially if you have previous music experience.
If you're interested in music theory, then you should definitely take this course to strengthen your foundation. If you're just interested in music period, this might not be the course for you. It sort of assumes that you understand how to read music and/or have heard of these concepts before. Every concept is explained in a couple of minutes during the lectures and, if you're section leader doesn't mention it, it might not be brought up again. Homework can be really tedious, but at the very least, it is good drill practice. Otherwise, it's definitely fun, but you have to know what you're getting yourself into.
I came into this class with absolutely 0 music theory experience, unlike the vast majority of the students. This did make the course challenging, since everything builds on itself and so much of getting good at music theory just involves a ton of practice hours, hrs you're unlikely to have during the semester. However, I very enthusiastically recommend this class to anyone wanting to learn music theory––it provides a great foundation and resources for a topic otherwise hard to learn on your own! I learned SO much, the course staff is all very kind, and it will be a great experience as long as you don't mind feeling like you are super behind most of the other people in the class. All the assignments are still doable, just tough/can take some time if you come in with no experience.
Section heavy course, but lots of fun and engaging. You have to be able to engage with the material on your own too.
This course will be easy and very helpful if you are just shy of being prepared for Music 51a.
Take it! You seriously dont need any background. MUSIC 2 is remarkable in that it takes people who dont know how to read music and turns them into arrangers.

As an instrumentalist, I found the beginning of the course to be quite slow, as it teaches students to read music. But I got a TON out of learning about harmony.

Also, the homework is generally pleasant to do (though tedious on occasion) and very rarely feels like busy work.

The projects are GREAT. They tie all of the material together so nicely and are a challenge in the best way possible. Such a joy to do and something I genuinely learned from. I loved all the creative liberty we got as well.

The problems on the final were difficult but very interesting.

Section was super dry, but lectures were great. Dr. Jakubowski taught us a lot of theory and exposed us to lots of great, new music. And he always made sure to tie in concepts we were learning with real–world examples (i.e., songs). He not only showed us a new lens with which to look at the world, but looked through it right in front of us.

Would recommend for musicians and non–musicians alike.
I would warn future students that a basic understanding of theory is not enough to help coast through the semester. Chord progression/analysis/Nashville Number analysis got quite tricky at the end.
I think this course would be a great introduction to music theory for people who have never played an instrument seriously before. If you have experience playing an instrument or arranging music, you might prefer to take music 51. Sections were great –– they were assigned based on experience, and they were a great way to get to know other people interested in music; we were able to focus on our areas of interest and activities we enjoyed. It was not a challenging course, however.

Also, don't buy the textbook! It's available for free online if you google it.
