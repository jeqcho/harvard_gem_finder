This is an amazing and unique course that is the closest to hands–on experience you can get in the social sciences classroom. It's a great way for regional experts and newcomers to broaden their understanding of conflict dynamics, negotiation skills, and regional knowledge
This class is all about learning through doing and self–reflection. You'll gain a lot of confidence in a variety of life situations but expect to put in the heavy lifting of learning yourself rather than waiting for external feedback.
I recommend taking this course (given that you are ready to devote three Saturdays to negotiation session) if you want to learn negotiation principles and skills in a very engaging and entertaining way. It is also a great opportunity to learn about the region from a new perspective and get to hear first–hand accounts from guests who are experts in the issues that we covered in class.
This is a fantastic and very practice–oriented course. What I really liked about the course was its focus on skill–building: The theoretical constructs from the readings (especially those on negotiation practices) can be directly applied to the negotiation exercises. The course helped me a lot to understand and work on my own weaknesses when negotiating. The guest lectures were interesting and some of the content actually really useful from a negotiation perspective (especially the talks by Alexander Hug and Danny Stoian). The memos help to actively reflect on one's negotiation strategy and to integrate (and thereby better remember) the main take–aways from the readings. Through the negotiation sessions, you also get to know your classmates much better than in other courses. The readings are very helpful and def worth reading, but I often had the impression that it was fine skimming some of it since they are not the main predictor of doing a good job in the negotiation sessions. Arvid knows how to make the class interesting and entertaining, and Alex did a fantastic job when organizing the negotiation sessions. Both of them, and also the guest lecturers, are genuinely helpful with any questions you might have and keen to help you improve your skills. Overall, I feel like I learned a lot and met super interesting people.
This class is amazing!! There aren't many classes at Harvard that are as applied as this one and that help you grow as a person like this one does. Don't be scared off by the Saturday classes – they actually fly by and are pretty fun, plus you become friends with your classmates during them and it's good bonding. Take this class if you want to improve as a negotiator and be plugged into U.S.–Russia relations!
This was potentially the best course I took at Harvard, and I say that as a second–semester senior. I've used the skills I learned for everything from communicating with family to negotiating grad school stipends. It was fascinating, applicable, very well taught, and the rest of the students were incredible. Big time commitment, but very worth it, and it was nice to be done by April. Seriously, Dr. Bell is a standout professor, and the team he puts together is awesome as well. My only regret is not learning about the Negotiation Task Force earlier!
This class was super helpful for anyone looking to get into policy. Getting exposure to seeing many different sides of a conflict gave me better insight on how I would approach any policy I work on in the future. While it is a pretty big time commitment, especially for a select few weeks, it is worth it.
One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. Offers a truly unique and engaging method of experiential learning through the strong emphasis on simulations, leading to both a detailed understanding of the perspectives of multiple sides of a conflict and of yourself as a negotiator. Being able to take the course alongside graduate students with deep knowledge of Eastern Europe and National Security Fellows with vast professional expertise in the field is deeply enriching.
Let me preface this by saying that most of the students in this course are concurrent masters students or HKS students, as neither of those, this course was definitely outside of my comfort zone but all students should be pushed out of their comfort zone when picking a course schedule. This class seamlessly blended negotiation with foreign policy and allowed students to immerse themselves in foreign policy situations. For anybody that has Saturdays free and wants to role play being in the State Department, this class is for you.
This class is so fun, please take it. Do not be intimidated. If you have a lot of speaking and personality skills– you will truly love and enjoy this class.
