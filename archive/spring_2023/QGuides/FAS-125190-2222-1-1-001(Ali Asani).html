The bi–weekly write–ups may seem like a lot, but they go pretty fast. Professor Asani is one of the sweetest and wisest professors I have had here — so much to learn from. Overall, this is a great course for deepening religious literacy (which in the US, I think we all need) and appreciating different aspects of Islamic practice and expression! I tried hard to like this course but I did not enjoy it going into it with hope for Islamic art & art history content. What you end up getting is theology – dense texts of religious literature and essays on ideology, which I personally hated. This course has ~120 pages of theological readings weekly (but sometimes you need to finish a whole book in a week, lol) for lectures and sections, 3 mandatory 2–hr film screenings that happen at night (or write a long essay for each as penalty), and 2 small assignments due PER WEEK. The same coursework applies for HDS 3627 (its graduate level title), so yup you're doing the graduate level work for a GENED. Also, the art perspective of Islam is kind of this class's false advertisement – lectures will touch on written arts (such as music and poetry), but never fully examines visual art. This course did demystify Islam for me, but I wish I could take half the class, because the other half is too much theology, too much readings and work for a GENED.
