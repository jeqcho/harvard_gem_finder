What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Please take this class.
This is a fantastic course, and I hope that it is implemented in other programs beyond CS. It teaches a variety of skills that are very helpful for succeeding in the PhD specifically (e.g. reading a paper, critiquing a paper, reading theory papers, communicating science) as well as skills that are helpful for doing well in grad school and life (e.g. mental health, imposter phenomenon, debugging unhealthy self–expectations). There are a variety of faculty visits throughout the course, and they can be very engaging, but I would say that the true gems are the student panels where students ranging from G2 to G6 weigh in on a variety of topics (e.g. how much do you work each day, how to talk to your advisor about vacation). These panels made me feel like I'm not alone in what I'm experiencing. Can't recommend this course enough!
You will get the most out of the class if you are willing to engage with other students and share your challenges and struggles openly.
This class should be a MUST for every first–year PhD student. Hearing other students' perspective and experiences about the PhD is incredible helpful to understand your role as a PhD student and to overcome the struggles you may have during the first year.
This class is the only reason that I did not feel lonely and isolated during my transition into life as a PhD student. I also learned a lot of research/communication skills and have a better understanding of the expectations of PhD students. I am going to miss taking the class a lot next year!
Awesome class, great way to get to know your cohort, and learn what it means to be a researcher in Computer Science!
