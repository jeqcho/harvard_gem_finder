What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course was okay. Not good or bad. Just okay. The office hours were very helpful in learning content but the lectures were very boring and difficult to understand.
Make sure you find a study group, and if you are actually available go to office hours!!
This course is difficult and hard to follow at times. The real world connections aren't always visible and I found that made it hard for me to keep up at times. The course material also had some frustrating typos that took a while to figure out.
I very much enjoyed the class and found it interesting. But make no mistake– this is a math class and it is reasonably difficult. It is well grounded in engineering applications, especially towards the latter half of the course, but much of it is straight math, and spent on systems and Fourier analysis. I would recommend it because I appreciate the skills taught and I enjoy math for its own sake. (As of now, it is required for EE majors.) However I think people who don't like math will be less enthusiastic about the course from what I heard from my classmates. It is one of the less popular electrical engineering cores. Nonetheless, this class teaches a lot about systems and it is a good thing that EEs have to take it. Go to lecture and office hours and you'll be fine. The last thing I would add is that the exams are not just repeats of homework or lecture; you have to actually think on them.

Todd is great and very happy to help, although a bit strict about deadlines. Didn't take it with Flavio, but he seems great as well.
Signal processing is SO INSANELY COOL! The math it uses is so simple yet so clever (a common theme in EE), with applications in communications, AI, and physics. Make sure you connect all the concepts of the course because it gets dense after the midterm
After every lecture REALLY make sure that you understand the presented contents inside and out. It will catch up to you for midterms and finals if not. Go to office hours also!
The way I see it (and I've never written a bad Q review, so I'll try not to start now), the course will be different with Flavio back at the helm. This year was especially weird where things were less accessible than they should have been. Bad office hour timing in the middle of the school day, general inaccessibility shrouded in flexible policies but an arbitrarily harsh syllabus, and widespread knowledge of students being told to "withdraw" rather than providing support. The content is difficult and taught a bit too fast. If you lose focus for even one week, you will pay. The coding exercises and final project can be cool if you start early.

Exams are tough, but maybe it'll change to take–home again. If you're taking the course, it's probably required for you, so buckle up.

Many EEs find this to be their favorite course, and I respect that and their mathematical maturity. I did not feel this way.
