You will learn a lot about birds, and you'll probably have a really good time. If you like your classes to have any sort of structure, this probably isn't the class for you, but if you're willing to sacrifice organization (and some early mornings) for great field trips and a fire spring break, you won't regret taking it. The two final exams + final project make the end of the semester a bit of a grind, but the tests are pretty fair and the final project is (sort of) a group project, so that's fun. As with a lot of classes, what you get out is based on what you put in, but OEB 190 definitely gives you a lot of bang for your buck.

The class forms a great community, and a lot of this comes from the field trips. Fair warning, the exams and lab practicals are hard. There is a lot of material, if you can start studying early, do it. Try not to have other exams on the same days as the birds exams. They can be easier if you study with other people in the class, but they're not anything you can do without a lot of studying. Regardless, the exams are doable and the rest of the class makes them entirely worth it.

This is an excellent course for the avid birders out there. You'll learn a lot, get hands–on experience in terms of identifying, but be prepared to study rigorously for the exams as well. Lectures aren't recorded, and the lecture slides are sparse – you need to come to lectures in order to learn anything comprehensive for the exams, and even then it might be tough to remember what all those species' names pertain to in terms of history, etc. There's quite a bit of fossil history you'll need to know about, and the dissection labs aren't just a joke – you'll be expected to memorize anatomy and rehash that knowledge on both the lab exams and the lecture exams. The field trips are fun, but that's just one aspect of the course – make sure you have time in your schedule to focus on exam preparation before taking this course! Otherwise, all the assignments besides the exam are intuitive, and you'll likely be able to do well as long as you've payed attention in class and taken good notes. Good luck y'all!!
