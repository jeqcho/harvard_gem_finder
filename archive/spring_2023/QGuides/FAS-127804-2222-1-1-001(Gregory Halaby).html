What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Prof. Halaby really cares about his students and is dedicated to each student's success and advancement in Arabic.
I would recommend others to take it
This class does require commitment to doing the assigned homework, studying the vocabulary often, and reviewing vocabulary or concepts learned in the past as to not fall behind. This course is very helpful in expanding your knowledge of grammar in order to refine your speaking and writing.
I felt that I had to take this class to progress on my Arabic language trajectory. However, it was difficult for all the wrong reasons. Learning a language is hard, it stretches you in so many different ways. But, you should feel able to be confident in your teacher's ability to chart a course through these troubled waters, and I lacked that. I felt that class time was often misused, which led to difficulty learning and applying core concepts. I felt that I was working very hard, and still falling behind on expectations and material. I would still encourage someone to take this class to progress their knowledge and plan of study, but to also be aware of its challenges.
This course is very well taught. It is heavier on the grammar side, but your writing will become a lot more sophisticated. It is a time commitment, with daily homework and exams about every two weeks, but it is a worthy investment!
This course is awesome! Definitely recommend taking it (especially with Ustaadh Greg and Phoebe). There is a lot of work––but it is all necessary. Arabic is a hard language with lots of nuances, and you will spend time on the assignments, however it is so rewarding! You will learn and progress so much!
I couldn't recommend the course more highly! It is difficult at times, but Professor Halaby ensures that you are very well guided through the process.
