the content of this course is really, really good; but professor wang's grading is a bit unfair towards viewpoints on historical state formation that he doesn't agree with (especially the important role of economic or social explanations, in contrast to just the Tilly external–war–makes–the–state view). he is a really nice professor. but probably come into this course expecting a specific historical explanation (not a large variety of them)
This is an excellent course to take if you're at all interested in political development. Over the course, you are introduced to a wide range of scholarship in the field and are not limited to studying any one region. It is also one of the better classes for practicing critically reading scholarship and assessing the arguments made by authors. Professor Wang is excellent at encouraging and guiding in–class discussion while helping students consider the course materials in new lights. I would highly recommend this course to anyone.
