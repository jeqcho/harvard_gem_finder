The best thing about this course is the other students in it. The course is basically all discussion based, so conversations are based on what the students find most interesting. I learned the most in this course from listening and learning from my fellow peers. All of my peers were so interesting, thoughtful, and from very different backgrounds with different perspectives.
Please take this course! It is by far my favorite class I have ever taken (at Harvard or otherwise), and I cannot recommend it with more enthusiasm. The work does not feel like work because it is so enjoyable, and the class discussions are so fun, engaging, and eye–opening. I can say with certainty that you will come out of this class as a more enlightened, critical, and passionate version of yourself. I would take it again if I could.
Take this class. Take this class! You'll learn SO SO much. The discussions in class are so good, Dr. Noll is great at fostering discussion. I learned so much from listening to my peers, and you'll hear so many diverse perspectives that will truly change the way you think. The readings are so applicable and Dr. Noll does a good job of unpacking the denser ones. The weekly logs are short and the interviews and projects are really good ways to talk to people and do some social justice work. Dr. Noll has office hours and you can talk to her about truly anything. TAKE THIS CLASS! This was the best class I've taken in my time at Harvard and everyone should take it.
Dr. Noll is incredible – she really truly cares about her students, and you can tell how this affects even the assignments given. Each piece of work you do is meant to help you learn and reflect (advocacy project, interviews, weekly reflections). You will learn a lot about the experiences of women and girls. If you have a strong background in WGS already, you might not be exposed to many new topics, but you will be exposed to new perspectives and opinions. The class ended up being more discussion heavy than I expected, so be prepared for that.
