Class is interesting and gives different perspectives on how we should live.
Really loved this class. Format was a weekly two hour class, where Unger/Puett talked for 60–90min with the rest for Q&A/discussion, along with a weekly section. I loved the more modern take on religion and philosophy, thinking less about God or trolley problems and more about actual philosophy I'd consider meaningful or interesting today. The readings and classes partly focused on what today's versions of Christianity, Buddhism, Schopenhauer, Emerson, Nietzsche, Confucianism, and Hegel look like, if you take what was truly radical about them in their time (for example, Christianity's love + anti–hierarchy) and try to revise and apply them today. But those were a backdrop to the most interesting part of this class which was trying to synthesize all of these together into two branches of philosophy – "revised self–fashioning" and "maximal connection", as two ways one could approach the conduct of life, and also how they combine into one interesting approach to politics. I don't think the philosophy Unger proposed by the end of the class was completely right, but I think the approach getting there will really stick with me, making it much easier for me to think hard about what matters in life and how I should approach it going forward. I loved the sections although they could be a bit quiet at times, though that changed by the end of the semester as the focus shifted from discussing specific readings to thinking about various problems in life and how we might approach them.
