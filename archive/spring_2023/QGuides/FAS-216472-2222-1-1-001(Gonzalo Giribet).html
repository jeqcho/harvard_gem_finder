What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
A fun, low time commitment class if you like arthropods. Gonzalo and Shoyo are fantastic teachers.
This is probably the best class I have taken at Harvard. Lectures were incredibly organized and well–presented, with the most important information about each group being easy to find. It was also consistently presented across topic areas. Interesting and crazy examples of different phenomena provided an engaging way to interact with the material. Having research presentations pushed me to find the topics I was interested in and investigate those, while offering a less stressful arena for testing my knowledge. Lab was very helpful in putting the conceptual and theoretical understanding of these animals into the physical world and letting me figure out how they operate. Fieldwork is as always such a rewarding way to put the knowledge into practice. Gonzalo is an amazing professor and so obviously cares deeply about these animals, which really invites you to get invested in the material as well. A really great introduction and investigation of an often mischaracterized and misunderstood group of organisms that are so amazing.
This is an awesome course!!! It's super super low stress (one of the chillest classes I've taken), but you'll learn a lot. Gonzalo and Shoyo are both great, and create a welcoming, casual environment where anyone feels welcome, regardless of biology background. If you're at all curious about invertebrates, you should take this, and you should ESPECIALLY take it if you're at all apprehensive about Biology of Invertebrates or Biology of Insects, both of which are more intense than this course. You'll also get to go on a fun field trip where the students all get much closer to one another, and you'll get to learn about arachnids and myriapods hands–on. All in all, 10/10 must take if you think this stuff is even remotely interesting! and you get to learn about all aspects of their biology, so it's really comprehensive.
