What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
While there are a lot of readings, this course provides so much great knowledge about economic inequality and the politics surrounding it. I think Professor Remington is an incredible facilitator and has great insights into this topic. In an era defined by rising inequality, I think it is really important for all gov concentrators to take a course on this topic.
By all means take the class! You will learn a great deal, not just about countries you're familiar with but also about a range of others. Inequality and the politics behind it are both very rich areas, and Prof. Remington does a great job at guiding students into them.
Take this class because inequality is a very relevant topic in today's world.
This is a great class, but you should be aware that there is substantially more writing than in other similar courses. Most Gov94s have either a research paper or a midterm and a final with several small responses throughout the semester. This class has a full research paper, a midterm, and a final. While the papers are rewarding, the end of the class is a LOT of work because you have to write a 20 page paper and then turn around and write another 12–15 pages for the take–home final a week later.
