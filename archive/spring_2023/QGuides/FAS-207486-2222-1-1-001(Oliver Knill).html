Oliver Knill seems like a very interesting professor who generates a lot of enthusiasm every class. He uses props sometimes that can be fun, and includes lots of visuals in his lectures and gives good lecture notes with the proofs of all the theorems (for the most part). The homework and tests themselves do not focus so much on proofs, but rather the application of theorems in a computational setting, which is significantly different than Math 22A, with Professor Dusty G. I think the class does a good job, however, especially towards the end––introducing the student to broader applications of calculus both in the discrete and not. The section on the Fundamental Theorem of Line Integrals, Greene's Theorem, Stokes' Theorem, and the Divergence Theorem really trains you to recognize their interchangeability and applications in complicated situations. Apart from that, the homework is simple computation, and the midterms are exactly the same format as those you can find on the professor's website. Workload: about 10 hours a week if you're pretty good at math, maybe even less.
Professor Knill certainly has a personality. I found it somewhat entertaining, but I can't say the same for everyone.
5 problems per pset, 3 psets per week. They're mostly computational problems, so it's not too much work.

The concepts and theorems of this class are actually pretty simple. I watched a lot of Khan Academy videos to understand what Oliver was talking about in lectures, and comparing that simple, intuitive content to Oliver's meandering, often way too high–level explanations was pretty striking and instructive. Oliver is a really good mathematician and he knows a LOT about the interesting applications of the math. I think this class is probably worth taking, especially if you're good at math and want a challenge. And I think that it's good to work to conquer the learning curve. But it's only really worth it if you know you have the time and energy to keep up with the 3 pset/week workload (don't be fooled into thinking the psets are short and easy — THEY ADD UP), and to do well on the tests. If you're unsure about that, or are taking a few other tough classes, I'd go with 21B.

If you choose this class, be prepared to supplement with things like Khan Academy and do not expect content to be spoon–fed to you (sometimes, spoon–feeding is necessary to get a good handle on math basics). And obviously, start your psets early — everyone says that for everything, but you actually need to do that for this class.

That being said, if Dusty is teaching this class, and you're not taking an insane workload, GO FOR IT!!! Dusty is a great lecturer and you will likely get a lot out of the class without having to make up for the bad lectures. But if Oliver is teaching this class, just be ready.
