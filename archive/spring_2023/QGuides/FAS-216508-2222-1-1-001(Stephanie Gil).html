Do not take this course, especially if the hardware component is still around. The hardware component was not well thought out, and I wasted countless hours trying to debug hardware issues that were simply not possible for students to debug. It is a waste of time to constantly be in the lab, and just be stuck on issues. Also, the course is poorly organized such that assignments are frequently overlapping, and it is very overwhelming.
This class can be challenging at times but is a great introduction to robotics and multi robot systems, especially if you don’t have any background in the field. Be prepared to spend long hours in the lab working on the final project, but otherwise the assignments and mini hacks are manageable.
What a time sink. Material is interesting (to an extent) but the reading and the hardware component are so excessive getting through becomes your only focus. Terrible class structure too. The TF’s are amazing but as a class, there is still a lot of fine tuning to make the experience a bit better for students.
Good survey course of multi robot systems and algorithms, but would have been more fulfilling if theoretical and technical concepts were taught more in depth.
If you would like to learn how to deal with robots, this could be a good place to start. Just make sure to start working on your final projects early.
The class is very enjoyable and you will learn a lot if you take some time to do the readings and engage deeply with the material. Also, start the final project early since the project will be the bulk of the work for this class.
I did more all–nighters working on this final project than for all of my other courses at Harvard combined. The average workload for the course was near 10 hrs/week for me, but the two–to–three weeks for the project were well over 40 hours each. I barely slept for two weeks.

That said, if you want trial by fire, this course is a good one to learn ROS and get hands–on experience with sophisticated sensors on robots. Start the final project before the last of the assignments are all finished.
I was incredibly excited to take this course because of the interesting material. But unfortunately, this was one of the worst course experiences I've had.
– The hardware component of the course was new. But if it was going to be new, it shouldn't have been such a large, time–consuming component of the course. The hardware was incredibly complicated (not just something you can Google), forcing students to sit in the lab for 5–12 hours at a time, hoping to get a response on Piazza just trying to get very minimal components of the mini–hacks / project to work. My peers and I would joke about there needing to be a hotline service for the course, but honestly, this was the extent to which this was so distressing. The number of times I had to walk home in the freezing rain from being in Allston so late, the number of times I spent 12+ hours in the lab and regressed in terms of progress, etc. was incredibly frustrating.
– The support for the course is honestly unsatisfactory, and it's not even the fault of the TFs. Although every single tiny problem is something you need to either spend 10+ hours at a time in the lab for or get a TF's response on Piazza, it wouldn't make sense to expect that TFs are available at every moment. But in order to complete assignments in the way that this course was constructed, you need this help constantly. Another example: in the first week or two of the class, we were told that students with M1 Macs would not be able to complete programming assignments on their personal computers. Yet, the support for this was so minimal, leaving students with the only option of going to Allston whenever they wanted to work on any of the assignments.
– The papers that we read in class were incredibly complicated and not well explained in class. It was really difficult to follow and gain any information from. I genuinely learned very little from this course, unfortunately.
The subject of very interesting but we ended up spending a lot of time ironing out hardware kinks. During our final project, we spent ten hours a day for two weeks trying to finish our project while being set back by random hardware/software issues
This class was a lot of work..especially with the hardware component of the final project. However, with that being said, you will learn to become a better computer scientist. This class taught me how to read research papers for the first time, and how to work with Robot OS (ROS) and work with physical robots for the first time! Overall the class was a good time, and would definitely reccommend to motivated undergrads to take this class!
If you have any interest in robotics, you should absolutely take this course. Dr. Gil is one of the best lecturers I have encountered at Harvard, she explains things super well and is super friendly with all the students, truly a gem amongst the Harvard faculty. Give yourself plenty of time for the minihacks and psets.
Great class! I loved it, genuinely was one of my favorite classes in Harvard.
The hardware component of this course was a little difficult to navigate and did not have enough support, probably because it's a new aspect of the course. The programming assignments were very fun and interesting. Overall would highly recommend if you have a good programming background and want to learn about robots.
Start the final project VERY EARLY. It requires an insane amount of work.
This class is a nightmare. It’s not particularly challenging in terms of course material; however, the course logistics and introduction of a new hardware component made this course incessantly stressful and time–consuming. Professor Gil seemed unaware or indifferent to student concerns and was largely removed from the hardware work that the students were doing. If you take this course, be prepared to spend many late nights in the SEC debugging seemingly simple code because you are not given the proper information in order to be successful.
This class is kind of a lot. If you are new to ROS (and even maybe if you aren't), expect to spend a lot of time on the minihacks and hardware portions because there is simply too much to understand before anyone can be comfortable with it. Thankfully, the course staff is very helpful. Also, at the start of the class, psets and other assignments were occasionally had small mistakes that made them difficult to understand, but this course is newer so I don't think this will happen again. Readings are very frequent (ie. before every class), challenging, and time–consuming; however, as long as you can get a general idea, you'll be fine. The class can get stressful at times, but don't worry too much as there seems to be a strong focus on rewarding effort.
