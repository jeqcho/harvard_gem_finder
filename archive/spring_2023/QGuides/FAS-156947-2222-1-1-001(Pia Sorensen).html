Great, fun course that I got a lot out of. Pia is a great professor, and super kind and passionate about food fermentation. I felt like I made a lot of decent friends, and also got a great glimpse into an exciting field. Food fermentation is not entirely established, and it was really cool to apply our backgrounds to what feels very much like a "young field." It blew my mind how many unanswered questions there are that are relatively simple to solve, so much that we could do it for a final project. The final project got stressful towards the end, but just make sure to pick good teammates and it should be more than fun. Overall, great course that I learned a lot, but not in the super stressful environment that is typical of most Harvard courses.

You don't need any previous knowledge for the course. Take this class!!! This is one of the most niche and AMAZING, unique classes you'll take, and you'll be greatly inspired to be more creative and confident in your scientific pursuits. There is so much opportunity to take this course as far and as deep as you would like to go, so there is less pressure to "meet" a certain structure and more emphasis on enjoying the scientific process of exploration and curiosity. This is a perfect capstone for science majors, especially, as it is one fo the only opportunities that you can be so free and explore other disciplines while still diving into the nitty gritty of fermentation if you want.
