This class is a gem! Super easy and exposes you to a lot of beautiful poetry within the interesting theme of borders. I skimmed through the readings and showed up to lecture almost every week and it was a breeze. The assignments are very easy and fast, they took me max 1 hour each. The teaching staff isn't looking for specific interpretations of poems, so I never felt pressure to respond a certain way – any analysis goes, which takes the pressure off. If you like poetry and are looking for an easy gened, this is a fantastic choice.

This class has an incredibly compelling syllabus, and it provides a great environment for anyone (having experience with poetry or, like me, having none at all) to engage with poetry regularly—but perhaps the most striking aspect of the course is the great lengths that Professor Sandler and the teaching team go to in order to create a sense of community, of support, and of encouragement within the class.
This class was planned to be for 30–40 people, but had over 100 people enrolled. In spite of that, the course was really well run. The poems, both the ones on the syllabus and the supplemental ones, were excellently chosen and well varied. The lectures were also really well done, and somehow managed to center course discussion despite the size of the course. There were aspects which didn't work so well, like the "poetry pod" discussion groups, but for the most part, the staff made a serious effort to make the course accommodating, and the course as a whole was enjoyable because of it.
This is a very manageable course which you will learn a lot about poetry from if you take it. Don't take if you dislike poetry obviously, but if you are openminded towards art in general, this is a great course
