take this class! Dr. Quigley is an amazing teacher
You should start your papers early and make your first drafts as detailed and fleshed out as you possibly can. This saves you work for the revision!
Dr. Quigley is the absolute best, literally take this class just for her. She is so kind and compassionate and you will learn SO MUCH from her. She encourages you to step out of your comfort zone and is so inviting. Her class truly felt like a safe space to learn and grow. The class content itself was engaging, interesting, and intellectually challenging and stimulating. You will learn so much about gender and its intersection with psychology and mental health.

I had a really heavy course load this semester and she was so understanding with deadline extensions and just in general. I was initially worried about not having any experience in psychology as I never took a psych class before, but I never felt like I couldn't express my opinions or share my thoughts on psych topics. It truly is a safe space for everyone.
Go into Expos with an open mind! Many people dread this class, but your experience highly depends on your professor and what you make of the class yourself— Gender and Mental Health is the way to go! I learned so much about psychopathology, historical power dynamics, and how psychology is applicable to our lives today.
I know that the idea of another required course may be daunting, but expos was actually one of my favorite courses! I believe that as long as you engage in a topic and dedicate time to improving your writing, this class can be very helpful. It can become one of those classes that you are actually looking forward to throughout your day.
This course is phenomenal. Dr. Quigley is the professor you never knew you needed. Her ability to create such a welcoming and forgiving classroom environment made this class all the more enjoyable. Discussions are always so insightful; come prepared to have your mind challenged by your classmates. The writing is definitely not easy, and it will push you to think in new ways, but with the help of Dr. Quigley and your classmates, this class is not a burden. If you are interested in gender studies and/or mental health and psychology, please take this course!
