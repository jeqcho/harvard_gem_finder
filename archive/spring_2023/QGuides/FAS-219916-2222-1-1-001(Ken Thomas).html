Take this class!!!! It is a gem. One of my favorite classes I have taken at Harvard. It is a very interesting subject without being stressful. Professor Ken is incredibly kind, funny and supportive. You feel that the professor genuinely cares for you. The weekly homework assignments were very easy and the term project is super reasonable.
Too many assignments and coursework. Otherwise a great course if interested in this field. It makes you job ready in this field.
I would highly recommend this class to anyone interested in water issues in developing countries. Although the term project was a lot of work, the work is spaced out well throughout the semester so as to not overwhelm you, and the end product (i.e the final report & policy proposal) is so rewarding, both because of what's been accomplished throughout the semester and from all that has been learned. Overall, this is a very interesting course, and Ken's enthusiasm and expertise generates even more excitement from students.
This course was amazing and I'd enthusiastically advise any undergraduate with some level of interest in sustainable development, environmental justice, engineering, or climate in general to take it if it was offered. I learned so much every week about topics relevant to my final project and water resources in general and the lectures really complemented the weekly developing country case studies in each section. No one region's water resources and needs are the same and Professor Thomas does a really impressive job of covering a broad set of complex topics in a really engaging and interesting way. The term project was a good deal of work but it was pretty evenly spread out in the weekly assignments so it was easy to divide work and plan around uncertain circumstances throughout the semester. I learned so much from my classmates in this course, each of which had a unique background and was studying a different developing country. The organization of the class in terms of assignments, lecture, and section, really enabled this learning from my peers and exploring new topics. I'd advise undergraduates to enter this class with an open mind and some genuine curiosity about the topic so that they can make something really interesting and valuable out of their term project.
lots of time and essay writing + hw assignments but overall fine class, teacher is great
Take this class! Whether you are in ESPP or not, this class is a great introduction/overview of water resources. Unlike a lot of classes at Harvard it focuses on the developing/non–US world which I appreciated and learned a lot from. Loved it!!! And you get to do interesting research into a country of your choice and interview NGOs working in the water sector. That was super empowering and fun.
This is an amazing class *because* Professor Ken puts in the work to focus on his students. Do not take this class if you're looking for an easy A, do take this class if you wish to become a much better policy–writer.
I cannot recommend this class enough. Ken is an amazing professor, who is so purely enthusiastic and dedicated to his work. I won't lie, the class is a bit difficult at times, but there are more than enough resources to do well. It's the type of challenging that teaches you how capable you are as a student and as a person. This class also deals with many different disciplines: climate change, health, inequality, a little bit of environmental engineering, and much much more. It's truly a class for everyone, and water is something that we all need and affects all of us! Please take this course!!!
