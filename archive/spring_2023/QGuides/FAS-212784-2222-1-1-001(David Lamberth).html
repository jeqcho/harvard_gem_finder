Comments
Don;t get behind on the readings and try to start early on them because they can take a while
Professor Lamberth is extremely knowledgeable and fantastic professor. However, there is way too much reading for the course, and I would love it if we could have far less reading and more focused discussion.
Professor Lamberth's seminar is an interesting one, but definitely one that will be more rewarding if you really commit to putting the time into doing the readings. The reading load is relatively heavy, and can be quite dense at times, but it is always interesting and thought provoking by the end. The professor really cares and is very passionate in explaining topics in the class, and will offer lots of insights from his study of the readings presented by the class. Class discussions really make or break the seminar, and the discussions that we had in our class were usually interesting. It's necessary to make an effort to contribute to discussions in order to make the class worthwhile
There will be reading to do, so if you are looking for a seminar that you kind of just exist within don't do this one. The reading is important to get the most out of the class but you don't have to put more than 2–3 hours in a week. However, for some seminars that is a lot too ask for so just be prepared. Also, my class only had 7 people so be ready to discuss.
Very interesting, wonderful professor, lots of reading for a freshman seminar but all the readings added to my understanding of the course topic
