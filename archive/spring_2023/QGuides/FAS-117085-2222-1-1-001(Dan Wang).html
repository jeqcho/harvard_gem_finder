What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This Chinese course is quite different to others in the department. Instead of a lecture+drill structure with frequent revision of vocab/grammar, there's more of a focus on learning applicable Chinese and weekly homeworks consist of mini–projects in which we apply what we learnt. There are also no exams. Unfortunately, this meant that I didn't end up retaining too much as, the week after learning all the vocab and grammar, we would move on to something completely unrelated, never to see them again. Also, instead of using a textbook written by the professors you're using one from 10–15 years ago. I would also be prepared for a very uneven work load. Some weeks you'll barely have anything due, the next you'll have 5 assignments due in the same week. Another thing to note is the quality of this class is highly dependent on the students you take it with. Towards the end of the semester, students start to realise they can complete their preview homework without actually doing the readings or learning any of the vocab and that becomes painfully obvious in class.

That being said, I think this was an interesting opportunity to study a very different type of Chinese and the professors were wonderful! I don't feel I learned as much in this class as other Chinese classes, but perhaps that was senioritis.
The teachers are great! Like all Chinese classes at Harvard, I really enjoyed my time in this class and learned a lot. However, the course structure is different from other Chinese classes and I felt like not having tests or dictations hampered my ability to remember all the vocabulary.
