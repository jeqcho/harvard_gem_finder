What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Make sure to start psets early
I liked this course and think it's a really good alternative to the other math classes at this level (Math 21b, 22b, etc.). Fewer people take the applied math track, so the class is smaller, everyone knows each other, and you're more accountable for things like attendance. I thought the workload was really reasonable, but I'm still leaving with a good grasp of basic multivariable calculus. I do worry slightly that this course is a bit less rigorous –– not many proofs in the homework, for example –– so if you do want to get hardcore with math later in college, do consider taking a harder class. But especially if you're a freshman, this is a nice class that will challenge you and teach you what you need to know but not totally overwhelm you with impossible psets. I'd solidly recommend this and would take it again!
If you're set on going down the full AM 22 track, take this after 22A. There's more manual computation than 22A, and it gives a great introduction to different topics in multivariable calculus (breadth over depth, as it should be). The homework was straight from the textbook and was supplemented by solid slides. PSETs also weren't that bad and there were a couple weeks were we didn't have one. Exams were not that bad either if you prepared. Then again, the professor is leaving next year so take all this with a grain of salt.
I sort of regret not sticking with regular 21a since I feel like this course lacks a bit of structure and I didn't feel like there was as much to ensure you learned the content until the test. I also didn't feel like we touched much on the "applied" side of things which is why I initially chose the course instead of regular 21a. Unlike AM22a, we did not work much on Matlab and didn't see any particular perks of taking this over 21a.
This is a wonderful class that covers a parallel curriculum to its pure math equivalent, but which allows more individual connection with professors due to its small size.
Take this course!
