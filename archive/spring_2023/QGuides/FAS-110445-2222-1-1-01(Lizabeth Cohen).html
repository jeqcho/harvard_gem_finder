What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Based on what I heard from friends and others in the other HIST 97 tutorials, this seems like one of the best ones. The content was really interesting and Professor Cohen is great. It's a good amount of writing assignments which can be a lot but it's totally doable because they're spaced out and it's super useful because they all build up and contribute to your final paper. Ione was an amazing TF and also made tutorial so great.
In my opinion, the hist97 tutorial is "make or break" based on your professor and TF. Professor Cohen and Ione were fantastic, so Hist97h ended up being fantastic also. The class will teach you many of the skills you need to write history, and will walk you through the step–by–step of writing a research paper. Be sure to find a topic you're interested in, that you are willing to spend the semester working on. I found the course really helpful, and really rewarding.
Meet the deadlines!
This is more than just a required course! Take this course as an opportunity to really hone in on your writing skills and explore a new topic.
Really prioritize this class and make sure you have a schedule that allows you to prioritize it. It's a lot of work, but try to buy into the assignments as much as possible, because that's how you get the most out of them.
This course moves at an ambitious but manageable clip of writing assignments, and is structured entirely around writing, as opposed to readings or discussion sections. What's really helpful, though, is that each assignment builds off the last, and your skills and understanding of the topic you choose to pursue during the course are developed during the papers and in class. This is a great course for nailing down the principles of historical writing and growing as a writer.
