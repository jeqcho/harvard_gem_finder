I would tell students to choose an expos topic that they are interested in and stay on top of the assignments instead of procrastinating
Take Expos 20 The Uses of Horror with Dr. Zecca! She is such an amazing preceptor, and is one that I really do plan on keeping in touch with during the rest of my time at Harvard. She is so kind and smart and so helpful with writing assignments. Expos 20 is not a fluff class, and really does take time and effort to come up with! So be prepared for a significant amount of work.
Take The Uses of Horror with Dr. Zecca! It's definitely a pleasant way to fulfill the Exos requirement. I feel like I gained a lot from this course because the short stories were so interesting and Dr. Zecca is the best!
This class is phenomenal, I hate writing and reading, but Dr. Zecca created a course that is so engaging, that I did not realize I was doing work.
You should take an expos based on a fun topic. You are going to enjoy writing papers so much more on a topic you enjoy reading about, rather than feeling like you have to take a non–fiction based expos to learn something. My writing got so much better because I loved the short stories, books, and movies we absorbed in this class, whereas my friends had to trudge through a miserable paper because they took a different expos.
You should absolutely take this course. The readings are very engaging, and in my experience that is very hard to find in a writing course.
I would tell future students that choosing an Expos topic can probably make or break your experience. Pick something that genuinely interests you. :)
