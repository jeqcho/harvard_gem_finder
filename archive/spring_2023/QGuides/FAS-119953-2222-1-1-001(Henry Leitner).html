What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The lectures in this class are often not necessary, not because they can be found online, but because the material covered in class is often so elementary compared to PSETs, exams, and projects, that it's almost offensive to waste your time going to lectures. That same time can be spent doing something useful, like actually taking the time to do the PSETs.
Section and office hours are really helpful. psets get harder after the first few but most of the work is pretty reasonable. It's really helpful to have some coding experience if you're planning to take the class for a grade.
Unless you have a reason, take this course Pass/Fail. The first five weeks (prior to the p/f) deadline make the course seem super easy but it jumps very quickly especially if you have absolutely no CS background. Taking it pass/fail allowed me not have to worry about exam, psets, etc. which is the only reason why I actually learned something in this course.

Lectures were pretty confusing towards the end. Go to section every week if you want to comprehend, even though they make it optional.
Professor Leitner is awesome, it's a great intro to CS. Get started on the PSETS early (there aren't that many) and you'll be fine. More generous and gentle than CS50.
Extremely glad I was taking this pass/fail, okay intro to python, difficulty of psets sharply increases at the end. Julia was amazing though!
This class is not CS–50! This is computer science for those who do not want to do CS, but want an experience with coding. It really sets you up to continue learning on your own or stop where you are. I recommend!
Consistently going to section and office hours will substantially help with understanding content.
