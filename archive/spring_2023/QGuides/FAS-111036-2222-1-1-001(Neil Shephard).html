I am a non–stem concentrator who took Stat 110, really enjoyed it, and assumed Stat 111 would be more of the same. Here are the ways in which I was wrong:

1. Stat 111 is much, much more difficult than Stat 110. Just to give a couple of examples, the derivations behind some of the concepts we use are too complex to understand intuitively, and the steps to solve many of the problems require pretty intense algebra/differentiation. I had to invest far more time into understanding the material in Stat 111 compared to Stat 110 and, even with more studying, did not understand everything by the midterm or the final. So, take it from someone who took Stat 110 and did extremely well: how well you understood Stat 110 material might not be how well you understand Stat 111 material.

2. Stat 111 is not as "fun" as Stat 110. In Stat 110, you got recognizable results that you could more readily map onto cool world examples or hypothetical situations, such as a deck of cards or a guessing game in Stat 111, you learn about stuff that's absolutely crucial to the field of statistical inference, such as the consistency of an estimator for a particular estimand, but stuff you probably will not find interesting or useful unless you plan to do statistical work.

3. Having said that, while Stat 111 is not as fun as Stat 110, it is much, much more practical. Stat 110 is fun for solving cool math problems but Stat 111 is for taking actionable steps to make a reasonable guess about something based on data, which you will be doing a lot of in a variety of fields.
