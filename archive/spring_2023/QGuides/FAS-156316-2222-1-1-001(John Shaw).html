Take this class! Super interesting subject area and amazing teaching staff. This is what every GENED should strive to be. 10/10
This class does not go into the specifics about the social impacts of energy resources and how they impact the environment, so if you're looking for that I suggest you look elsewhere. John Shaw has a lot of energy, but that didn't save me from my disinterest in how different types of powerplants run and other technicalities (it's very important you know this stuff). I thought environmental science would be interesting from a sociopolitical standpoint, but it's harder than it looks and this class implements more chemistry and math than one would think. Some assignments, like the labs, felt over–complicated. The class covers a ton of content and the tests are memorization based. If you miss one class, you miss a lot, because he covers a lot through talking.
This was a very insightful class. It is a tough GenEd though. You do need to score 90s on the exams to get an A, and this does require a lot of time to memorise information. It isnt tough work, it is just time consuming work
This course is not super easy, but you get a lot out of it. If energy resources or climate issues interest you, this class is definitely worth taking
Definitely take this class! It's so vital to learn about the energy market –– and you will leave with a deep understanding of energy resources and why we need to understand these resources to address climate change.
Great class! You'll learn a ton about different energy options, and it's not much work.
Great class that teaches you very relevant information about energy and the environment. Not a lot of work.
The exams cover very specific details from class
Take this course.l loved Robert's teaching style and John's lectures were very interesting. The labs help understand the concept, however do take a lot of time
This is a great class for anyone looking to learn more about how the world's economy works and its implications on the environment. I would particularly recommend it to anyone interested in climate policy as it provides a great understanding of what considerations are at play in current energy decisions and will allow you to build a more robust perspective of the challenges at hand.
Take this class if you are sure/are seriously considering going into an energy–related field. It's a lot of information and it will challenge you. Don't take this class if you're looking for a GEM.
super interesting class with not too much work. id recommend reviewing material regularly or else you'll be swamped for exams. the exams are quite difficult and memorization–focused
This is a great, well structured class, and you will learn a lot IF you pay attention in lecture and participate in lab ie don't rush just to get it done. Prof Shaw is an incredible professor who delivers 40 slide lectures with no notes, completely from memory, and who knows an incredible amount of detail about energy resources and its environmental impacts. The labs were well thought out. Honestly, this GENED has been running for a pretty long time so the structure and expectations of the class are quite clear.
Overall this GENED is everything a GENED should be: great, informative content, fantastic course staff, no work outside of lecture/lab. Indeed the exams can be challenging, but only because there is a decent amount of material. The exams are fair and if you took good notes and review methodically, you'll be fine.
Highly recommend.
