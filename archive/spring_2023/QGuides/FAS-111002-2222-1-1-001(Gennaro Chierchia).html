I would tell them not to take it. They should wait for the junior tutorial if they can so that they can develop more in their linguistics knowledge. The course was boring and uninteresting in the second semester so I would avoid it especially if it was taught by the same tf.
Workload is not very demanding, and content is genuinely interesting –– not sure where else I would have had the chance to explore these topics in a casual academic setting!
It’s really a hit or a miss. The first half of the course made me want to bang my head against a wall, but the second half was great. The first half taught me a valuable lesson: don’t try to design a curriculum around a passion project that does not appeal to a wide audience. (So I guess that is to say, don’t design a course around a passion project.) The department tried to do this with writing systems, and consequently, eleven people were bored silly. I will say, though, it opened my eyes to just how many layers there are to writing systems, and I enjoyed designing a writing system of my own (which was like doing a fun puzzle). And the TF put a lot of effort into the course. The homework was also fun.

But the second half was indeed great. Learning how to use Excel was valuable, and I loved writing a critical review. Learning to read papers carefully and thoughtfully is crucial to succeeding in linguistics, and the TF did a wonderful job of teaching us how to do this. Also, OT is very cool. I had fun doing the OT problems the TF gave us. Lots of effort on her part as well.
This class is a requirement to study Linguistics and feels just like a requirement. Nothing sparked my interest like crazy, and the work just felt like another task to complete. It's not too hard, but was not the most fun part of my day.
This is an interesting opportunity to branch out of the typical subfields of linguistics.
This is a great tutorial! It's split into two different topics taught be two different tfs, so if you don't like one of the topics then you only have to do it for half as long as a normal class. Our tfs were both great, and since the class is just taught by grad students, they were so much more realistic than professors are with the amount of work we had to do
