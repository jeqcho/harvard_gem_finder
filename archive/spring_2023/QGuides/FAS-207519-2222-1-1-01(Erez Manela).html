This class is hard. It will make you work for it. But it is so worth it. I grew so much as a writer this semester and I am very grateful to the TF and the professor for all their help. Definitely recommend it, but be ready to work.
Make good use of the course support structure. My experience was one in which both my TF and my professor were extremely willing to help on any questions related to course work (or to studying history, more generally). Meeting with my TF an additional time after the required tutorial meeting, after I'd edited the draft I'd submitted for the tutorial according to the guidance I got in tutorial, but before the final draft deadline, was very useful.

Also, the course is introduced as very official and difficult, and, I think in part as a result, some students try to sound especially knowledgeable and formal when they make comments in discussion. I'm a shy person, and this environment made me nervous about speaking at first; however, it was important and helpful to recognize that those students were mostly just a little insecure about wanting to seem "good at history" in a course that's been labeled as the central "how to do history" course for concentrators.
