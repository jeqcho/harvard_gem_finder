This course definitely requires some background in electrical engineering. The first few lessons will be very challenging without any previous background. Problem sets were very long and challenging, but they are doable if you know how to manage your time. The quizzes weren't as bad as I expected them to be, but they were definitely challenging and time–consuming (took me 6 hours without breaks to do each quiz). The course definitely covers a lot of things that may be of interest to some students, like Op–Amps, logic gates, circuit components, and breadboarding. However, the course doesn't go too in–depth in any of the topics, and they go over several topics very fast which can make it hard to learn.

ES–50 is a hands–on class that gives people a great introduction to electrical engineering. The labs are great even for non–STEM majors. The teaching staff is incredibly kind and helpful. Take it if you have always to be in labs and work with cool electronics.
Do your best to make it through lecture, and try to enjoy lab. Also, always work with a partner in lab, it will make life so much easier and more fun. As far as the project goes, you probably won’t start early no matter what I say (I didn’t), but prepare to spend four to five trailhead days and nights at the SEC.
If you have an interest in electronics, you will love this class. If not, you will struggle quite a bit. The projects at the end made it worth it.
ES50 does not feel like an intro course at the beginning, which may discourage you; inadequate time is spent on covering the fundamental concepts of electronics (e.g. current vs voltage sources), making the subsequent, fast–paced lectures hard to follow. If you have no background, expect to invest a lot of your own time in teaching yourself fundamentals without a lot of course resources available, because the course does not provide a textbook or recommended video resources. That being said, the TF team is INCREDIBLE. One TF even stayed behind for an hour and a half beyond lab hours to walk me through a pset I did not understand. In addition, the course instructors are truly invested in helping you understand material—the Slack is very active, and the lead professor walked me through quiz solutions on a one–on–one Zoom call. If you do take this course with little background, just know it will be quite a bit of time commitment, but to not be afraid to reach out to the TF team/course instructors, who are extraordinarily generous with their time and help.
