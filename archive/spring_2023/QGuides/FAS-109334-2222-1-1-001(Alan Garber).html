TAKE THIS SEMINAR!! The lectures were amazing, engaging, there was lots of active participation encouraged, the topics were fascinating, incredible class–wide discussions, and Professor Garber overall is such a great teacher and has so much expertise! You will learn so much about health, economics, medicine, clinical research, the US health system and the class–wide discussions are phenomenal. I was always so excited to come to class each week. You will learn a lot if you do the readings, engage with lectures and discussions in class, and choose a final paper topic that really speaks to you. Loved the flexibility in what we could research, and the encouragement throughout.
You'd be an idiot to not apply for this course. I couldn't have taken a better freshman seminar.
This seminar is fantastic, and certainly worth taking if you're considering a less conventional path in healthcare or looking to understand the economics of health systems and insurance. Dr Garber's breadth of experience unmatched and there's so much to learn from him. You also get the chance to work individually with him and get personalised feedback at multiple stages on your final paper, and he pushes you to think critically in class discussions. The workload is extremely reasonable– just a final paper and presentation. The readings are dense but worth looking at— they aren't pivotal to class discussions but are extremely informative as added context. The SAT?UNSAT nature of the class also allows you to grapple with more complex ideas in a low stress environment, and the intimacy of a freshman seminar is a welcome break from big lecture classes. The class ends with a dinner at the Faculty Club— it's a great way to talk to Dr Garber about his extended time with the College and all the experience he has.
You will learn life–long important info, worth it if youre intrested in med, econ, public health, or anythin related
If you are at all interested in the health care system and health care economics, take this course! You will learn a lot about the challenges that the U.S. health care system faces, and if you take the time to read the readings each week, you will learn even more about health care economics. The only assignment is a class project, and the project is very enjoyable to complete because you get to choose your own health care proposal.
The lessons are a bit dull and a lot of talking rather than discussion
I would tell them to make sure to do the readings – especially if you have NOT taken EC10A – but also to not let that be a deterrent in your decision to take this class. I would also say that you should make sure that you have an interest in less of the bio/medicine and more of the legislative side of health care.
If you are interested in healthcare or public health I highly recommend this class, it is really interesting and I think it's not an exaggeration to say that I walked away from each seminar feeling pretty mindblown.
