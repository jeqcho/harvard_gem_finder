What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is really great if you are interested in philosophy (specifically Epicureanism) but if not it would be pretty boring. Don't take it if you're just looking to take any Latin course. However, I really do recommend it if you like ancient philosophy. It is a good amount of work, but not unreasonable.
This course is a great introduction to Classics at Harvard for any student with a strong background in Latin beforehand. For advanced undergraduates and graduate students, Dr. Philbrick's language pedagogy and discussion curation are excellent sources of inspiration for further reading and research.
Use the class to notice what Lucretius is talking about in nature. See how we use our senses and how we can be deceived by them. Find examples of science and art working together in union. This class helps you see the world in a more beautiful way.
Please know your Latin grammar and vocabulary well!
