What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Only take this if you’re prepared to completely guide your own project, and don’t come in expecting to be given technical assignments or learn new technical skills unless you teach yourself or specifically ask for it. I was expecting more learning how to draw, so was a little disappointed. If you’re not expecting that, it’s nice to have the space and time to work on an art project.
I'm glad I took this class. It expanded my conception of what it means to make art, and it helped me surround myself with artistic people and be creative in a way that I wasn't able to access before. My biggest issue with this class is the frequent 3 hour critiques, where we were expected to just sit and talk about people's work rather than using that time to make our own art. It was a fun class overall, but it was frustrating to have so little time to work in class.
This is an excellent class with excellent professors. At times it may feel a bit inaccessible and conceptual, but just speaking with the instructors and trusting the process will get you a long way. the class allows you to do literally whatever you want, which may be a bit daunting but if you just trust your gut and keep producing art it'll all work out
I would like to tell them that they should be ready and motivated to push themselves and find their own project, a lot of freedom necessitates a lot of self direction
The course staff is wonderful and the vibes are very good, but unfortunately the class is so disorganized and expectations are so unclear that it’s very difficult to get anything meaningful out unless you have a very clear vision and are ready to be entirely self–motivated. It is a “drawing” course in name only, there is no clear link to drawing and you can most likely do any sort of art you like
