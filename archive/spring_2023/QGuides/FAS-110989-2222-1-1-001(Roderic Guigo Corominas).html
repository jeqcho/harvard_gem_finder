PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS. The sense of fulfillment it will bring you is exceedingly difficult to find, and taking this class is a beautiful experience.

I came into this class after having taken math classes earlier in college and struggling to the point where I became disenchanted with them. If you’re in these shoes as well, I beg you to take this class. You will fall back in love with math. It might even change the path you take in college. It’s changed mine.

I’d heard linear algebra was useful and widely applicable, but I had no idea just how much that was the case. And I can safely say that there were many moments where I sat in class and simply marveled at just how powerful the material I was learning was.

Is this class hard? Yes. But you will have so much support from so many exceedingly patient and helpful CAs and TFs, and you will have so many resources at your disposal. If the textbook doesn’t help you understand something, lecture will. If lecture doesn’t, the videos posted on Canvas will. If the videos don’t, the course staff will. So on and so forth. There are so many office hours as well. You’ll never need to worry about not having enough in–person office hours to attend. And come finals, you’ll have review sessions to attend. Janet (the course head) even sent out articles on how to overcome nerves around taking tests, how to answer true–false questions, etc. YOU WILL HAVE SO MANY RESOURCES AT YOUR DISPOSAL. The course staff sets students up to succeed.

Also, Janet basically designed a system where we could benefit from learning. For example, we got a pset drop for every three quiz sessions we attended. (By the way, at quiz sessions, students take NON–GRADED quizzes and talk through them with CAs as a full group. Very helpful!) And we were given another option re: to how to weigh the midterm grades if we filled out a survey where we assessed how we could learn the material more effectively. So you’ll be encouraged to learn and to learn to learn effectively. In fact, you’ll be rewarded for it.

And the problem sets set students up to learn effectively. They tie everything together so nicely and always have a clear purpose. Also, they are REALLY fun. There are even extra credit problems for those who wish to gain a deeper understanding of certain material just because.

Do yourself a favor — whether you like math or just want to become a better student and thinker, PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS.
