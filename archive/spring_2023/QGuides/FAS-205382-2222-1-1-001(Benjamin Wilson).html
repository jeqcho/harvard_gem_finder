What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The course is reading heavy, but if you do the readings well you can get a lot out of the course overall.
Take this course!!! Professor Wilson is wonderful and cares so much about the course. Also you get what you put in – so do the readings
I would tell them that they're going to be doing a lot of reading and that they'll come to enjoy it. Even when the readings pissed me off, they left impressions on me, and I appreciated the opportunity to unpack them with Professor Wilson and peers from all sorts of backgrounds and perspectives. I believe any student would benefit from taking this class, as the subject really touches nearly every conceivable part of life from the 1940s onward. You'll come away caring about things you never expected to have opinions on and understanding the way that the Cold War shaped development of myriad sectors in virtually the entire world, with a legacy that continues to this day.
This is a great course with a kind and unexpectedly brilliant professor ('unexpectedly,' because he doesn't rub it in your face.) Take this course if you are interested in how the modern era was shaped by Cold War intellectuals.
