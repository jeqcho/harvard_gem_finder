This was a very solid course with a great professor and pretty cool material. You'll learn a lot about ocean waves and some climate–related phenomena. Be aware that this class is very quantitative and requires some coding skills (which you can easily pick up, though). I found the problem sets more challenging than expected but still fair. Eli is funny and if you pay attention in class/try to really understand the material, you'll probably really enjoy the course.
It’s a good class but the material can get confusing sometimes. It requires a deep conceptual understanding and sometimes that’s more assumed than taught.
This was ultimately a fun course. Strong emphasis on clarity and intuition in doing derivations. It's a survey of different oceanographic phenomena involving motivation, mathematical derivation, and physical intuition. The notes are also really good and problem sets are generally very reasonable but sometimes confusing.
This class is truly an amazing EPS course. You come away feeling like you understand so much about the ocean; Eli is a great professor and the TF (Camille) is also super accessible and great! The teaching staff has your best interest at heart. With all that being said, this course is definitely mathematically rigorous conceptually, even though psets are infrequently. If you are on top of your work, though, it is super doable.

This class is relevant to understanding how the oceans and climate work and is def worth it.
