What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It can be difficult to get an A on assignments, and the later half of the course is a bit of a book club since we read through the entire textbook of "Take Back the Economy." It was also cool to read work that J.K. Gibson–Graham wrote, but it also meant we did not learn a lot of other perspectives.
I would tell future students to take this course while Professor Gibson is still at Harvard!! I thoroughly enjoyed this course and will be taking this vital knowledge throughout my journey at Harvard in WGS Studies. Since Harvard doesn't have a geography department and this material is most conducive to that non–existent department, this material usually is never thought of (especially not by a world–renowned feminist author), so I definitely would take this course while Prof. Gibson is still here to teach!
I wish more people could take it! This class gave me a new lens to look at the world through. In other WGS classes, I've been frustrated with the high–level theory and lack of obvious applicability, but this class mixed both! I was taking Ec10B at the same time as this class and I'm so glad I was exposed to these alternative economic ideas. We take so much for granted about the economy and the world around us, I really really liked the diverse economies approach.
