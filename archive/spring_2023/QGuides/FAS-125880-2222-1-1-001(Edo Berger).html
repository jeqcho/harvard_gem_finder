What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You get to work really hands–on with the techniques and concepts that you learn in the lecture component of the course, and there's lots of opportunity for in–class practice and 1–on–1 help since it's a smaller class. The spring break observing is a fun time and it's really cool to be able to handle the telescope control and make your own observations! (hopefully future students will actually be able to go to the observatory if covid permits!) The course is definitely more focused on being able to walk you through the different processes and techniques of observational astronomy, so there's not a heavy pset workload and the project during the second half of the semester is definitely manageable if you use your time in class to work on it.
Only take it if it's going to be your 4th and final secondary requirement. It's not very difficult but you need to stay on top of your work. Outside of that, not very enjoyable at all. (All of these suggestions are null and void if you actually enjoy and have done astro observations before– you'll probably love the course if you have experience with that and know you enjoy it.)
The class is pretty cool, especially if you get to go to Arizona (we didn't rip).
