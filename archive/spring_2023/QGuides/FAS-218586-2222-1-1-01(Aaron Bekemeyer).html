This course was alright. We went through history from WWII until the modern era, which is different than most history classes that do not discuss today's history at all. I do wish they had spent more time on the modern era. But it was easy. The readings were not too bad, and you could manage to get away with not doing them. But the midterm requires you to know a lot of terms and concepts discussed throughout the semester, which can be hard to study for. But the professor was very nice and the TF was very attentive.
Really good class for students interested in how history affects the present. Easy assignments and fascinating lectures.
You will love this course! Professor Bekemeyer goes above and beyond to make this your favorite course.
I would strongly recommend this class to anyone who is interested in developing a deeper understanding of the history that has most recently shaped our current society and political context. Most of my US history classes in high school didn't make it beyond the 1960s so this course gave me the opportunity to dig deeper into more recent events and develop historical skills in a way that felt useful and relevant.
Good survey course of U.S. history – does exactly what it tells you it will do on the syllabus. Aaron is an excellent lecturer. Thomas is a good TF, but section might suffer if no one does the readings. The course is very manageable, just make sure to skim the readings again before the midterm and exam.
If you want to really dive into the specific events of the Cold War Era and beyond, this course doesn't really do that. It covers many of them to be sure but takes a much broader view of post–WW2 history, which was actually very helpful in reorienting my perception of this time. If you like (and already know a lot about) this time period, this course may still be very useful in exposing you to new info and ways to contextualize historical trends and patterns with a broader scope.
This class is absolutely amazing. It fills in many gaps that may not have been covered during high school American history and is extremely enjoyable. The readings are all useful and interesting and they will not feel like a waste of time. The papers are also helpful and there are not many of them. Professor Bekemeyer is so engaging and intelligent, you will learn so much from him. Everybody in the course staff is extremely helpful and will always make time to meet/discuss materials.
Professor Berkemeyer is my favorite lecturer so far at Harvard. He is extremely organized and gives thoughtful responses to any question that is fielded to him. The arguments that he makes in each lecture are clear, to–the–point, and efficient with the limited time we have each week. Lectures are a straight 10/10.

This class was ultimately eye–opening and it challenges a lot of the conventional beliefs people have about the Trump, Obama, and Bush years. I appreciate that it's rooted in evidence and goes much deeper than other American history classes. Even if you think you already know a lot about American history, you should take this class.
This is the best history class I have taken at Harvard! Incredibly engaging lectures that give a complete, nuanced view of American history over the past ~75 years.
Pretty standard structure for a history lecture course, but very interesting and relevant content. Manageable course with a light amount of writing for a history course. Writing assignments were clear and manageable, too. Solid foundational course for first years that will teach you interesting content while pushing you engage in different types of assignments (essays, exams, etc.). Content felt very relevant to modern day, which made it a lot more enjoyable than other history lecture courses.
Take it!
