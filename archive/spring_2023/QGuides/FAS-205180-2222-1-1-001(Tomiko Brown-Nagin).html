The readings may seem like they are a mammoth load, but they generally contain a summary either at the end or the beginning. Read and familiarize yourself with the court cases and glance over other materials. Engage with your peers in class and make an effort to get to know them outside of it as well. I promise it will make your experience so much better.
Dean Brown–Nagin is a phenomenal scholar and professor. And the people who enroll in this seminar are all fantastic and lively. In the end, however, I would be hesitant to recommend this course. I think my potentially washy feelings about the course come from my expectations. I expected us to be diving into constitutional law and really dense material. At times, this was the case, but the course more broadly was definitely more focused on social movements rather than the law. It is a great course, and a great opportunity to take advantage of â€” but it wasn't quite what I was looking for.
I would recommend taking this seminar as an easy supplement to your freshman course load. You learn about the theory of social movements and then get into real–world case studies such as Me Too and Black Lives Matter. The final presentation on the social reform movement of your choice was also pretty fun. Dean Brown–Nagin is one of the smartest professors and legal/historical minds I've met here at Harvard, so you may feel intimidated at first. However, it's a great chance to engage with her and talk about current events and issues in American society. The class gets a lot better once you loosen up a bit and have fun with the discussion at hand. All in all, it was a good part of my Harvard experience, and you can't go wrong taking it.
This class is definitely interesting, but can get a little bit dull at times. It is truly a discussion–based seminar, so there is a lot less input from the professor than you'd expect. If you're interested in both legal theory and social movements, then this course is definitely for you. But, if you're only interested in one without the other, you might be disappointed by how the material is framed.
Take this! Dean Brown–Nagin is wonderful and so intelligent, she's a wonderful lecturer.
This is a fascinating course if you're interested in law and social justice. The course is almost completely discussion based and you will learn so much from your fellow students. Prof. Brown–Nagin is very knowledgable and presses you with questions and ideas to get you to really think about your points, but it's a positive quality and you will learn so much from her!
