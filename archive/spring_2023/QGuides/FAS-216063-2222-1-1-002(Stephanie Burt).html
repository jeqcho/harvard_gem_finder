What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take it! It's super fun and you'll learn a lot!
This can be a bit of a chaotic course. Partially because of the course content itself –– English 20 has a core of five books that are taught in each iteration, and the remaining readings are chosen by the professor. I think the professors tend to choose readings that align with their own research interests, which don't necessarily make a cohesive syllabus when combined with the required five books they have to teach. The professor for this particular English 20 class can have a somewhat chaotic teaching style; discussion often derails and sidetracks. Thomas is the best TF though. I learned so much from section. This is a required course for English concentrators and secondaries, and it's still overall a good course.
Take eng20 if it is a requirement, skip it otherwise. It's a good class for basic exposure to lots of different forms, but I didn't feel like knowledge about really any specific form grew greatly as a lot of the class is just student–driven discussion so if you've never analyzed a form before you can feel a bit lost. While I really liked the teaching staff, it did feel pretty chaotic and disorganized most of the time. This class was the most "english" class I have taken so far — nearly every other sentence was a reference to a canonical piece that I had not read so I often felt pretty out of my wheelhouse. That being said, it was an environment very open to questions which was nice. The structure of the class was to bring a prepared question to class and that will be the jumping off point for discussion. So often class was just talking about random (although interesting) things that weren't always super central to the text. The assignments were pretty flexible though — up to two creative assignments out of four total. Overall, a perfectly fine class but not my favorite, especially since I often felt kind of ostracized from not having the same background knowledge as everybody else (but that could just be specific to the group I was with and not the class).
Be ready to read a lot, though Professor Burt is very understanding of lapses.
Take this class!
A fun, not–too–hard English course. Stephanie is a bit eccentric but very approachable. If you need every lesson to be structured, this is not the course for you. It felt extremely disorganized at times, but I found that to be part of its charm. The discussions and even the readings evolved around what the students wanted. I'm not sure what I learned, but I'm glad I took the class.
I loved this class, lots of material, lots of good discussion, lots of heart.
Keep your past readings from the course in mind as you move forward –– the course truly is designed to help you see these connections across the canon, and I think it's worth your while to keep the plot of Beowulf in mind while reading Persuasion or remember Bishop's poetry when reading King Lear.
