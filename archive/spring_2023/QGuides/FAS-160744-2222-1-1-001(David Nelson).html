What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Nelson is an expert in the material! If you are interested in the statistical physics of fields, coarse–graining, phase transitions, and renormalization––I highly recommend.
If you want to learn what renormalization group is from a statistical mechanics perspective, this is the best course I can recommend.
This class is a great choice once you have a good understanding of the basics of condensed matter theory. Since it's not as standardized as, say, the 295 a/b series, the exact form of the class will depend a lot on the professor teaching it, and the topics they choose to talk about. David Nelson's lectures were very clear and accessible, and the topics captivating and surprisingly subtle. Assignments aren't always very easy, but if you start them on time and put in some effort, they will work out. Overall, great experience.
