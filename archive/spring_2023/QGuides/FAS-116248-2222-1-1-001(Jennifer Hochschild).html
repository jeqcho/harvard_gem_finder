Find another GenE to take! I would NOT recommend this course! The teaching staff for a course on RACE should consist of members who have first hand experience with the issues of race (I.e not taught by a white professor and white TF). This is not to sound shallow or crude but rather a critique that I, and many other students in this course, hold about the way this class is instructed. The instructors being people who have not had to experience racial discrimination themselves but attempt to educate others on the topic is problematic and is a prime example of how the history of race in this country is not taught by those who it has affected most but rather by those in positions of power who control the narrative. Harvard, this needs to change! Also, the grading of your essays is very very weird. Even if you follow the rubric exactly, it's very unlikely that you'll receive full credit. HUGE GRADE UNCERTAINTY towards the end of the semester...like it actually gives me anxiety thinking about how I STILL DO NOT KNOW MY GRADE! This is not an "easy" course and be prepared to read and write A LOT. The teaching staff tries, but it's just NOT IT for reasons I mentioned above. Be prepared for awkward sections where no one read the readings and even more awkward one on ones when you receive a horrible essay grade! If you are knowledgable on the racial issues of America and know even basic American history regarding its racist roots, then you don't really need to take the course because you won't learn anything else! There's no emphasis on solutions to our racial disparities, and this course caused me more annoyance than anything else!
