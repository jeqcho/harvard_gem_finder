What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is offered for students who have the intention of conducting graduate level research in control and reinforcement learning. Many reading materials are very technical, which requires a considerable background knowledge to understand. Therefore, the class can be intimidating if you haven't taken a introductory course in systems and control. On the other hand, if you are a graduate student interested in doing research in control theory or theoretical reinforcement learning, this course is a great oppotunity to deepen your knowledge in these fields.
Knowledge in control theory is not necessary, but you should know something about RL. I only knew a few basic definitions of control before this course, and it does not affect my understanding, since Lina had several lectures introducing the basic models. As for RL part, I think it presumably requires students to be familiar with basic RL concepts. So one of my friends who knows almost nothing about this topic dropped the course... Overall, I think this course is perfect for: (i) grad students completing their course requirements (workload is light compared to other numerical courses) (ii) students who have read some RL papers or textbooks, and would like to know more state–of–the–art works.
This class is fantastic if you want to do research in control theory, RL, or where they intersect. If you want to understand the current questions of interest in these fields, this is the class to take.
