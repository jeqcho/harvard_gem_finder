High emphasis on translational papers and gene therapy treatments
Really good advanced neuroscience course for undergraduates at the college. The topics are interesting and the course staff are some of the greatest in the field.
Take this class if you like research and are interested in medicine. Super research heavy though you don't need to retain much from the lectures. The hybrid format made it super difficult to foster discussion. However, I appreciated the teaching staff's general overall enthusiasm for the course. Wish there was more time to chat with the teaching staff (office hours or general one–on–one check–ins or meetings). Some of the TFs were also a bit checked out... but overall the content and structure was unique. You get to talk to patients, physicians, and researchers. It's super disorganized but you will learn a lot. You will need to do the learning yourself though.
If you're premed or interested in a clinical career this is a great class. If you're interested in preclinical research and critical discussion of primary literature, this class may not be for you.
I wanted to love this class but didn't. The patient presentations and guest lectures were extremely interesting and insightful but everything else felt very chaotic– there were only two assignments (a presentation and take–home final). The presentation was pretty straightforward, although we got zero feedback on it (no grade, comments, etc). The final exam was supposed to take three hours but took me far longer than that as I had no idea what the expectations were in terms of length/specificity/etc. It felt like the course instructors were putting in minimal effort to the course (makes sense since they are all very accomplished). Would not recommend to college students for course credit as it caused more stress than it was worth. If anything, I wish I had audited on the patient days and not taken for credit. Keep in mind that the trip to Longwood can be long, especially on the 9:30pm ending nights.
Take this class if you are unsure about whether you want to pursue medicine and/or love learning about the pathologies of diseases. The class was extremely insightful, but the commute and late–night lectures can be a hassle.
At its best it is a fun and interesting discussion of diseases of the nervous system, with a focus on patients and treatments. At its worst it is a badly managed rambling discussion about uninteresting papers. Expectations and time input are low for the most part. It is very clinically oriented, often to the detriment of understanding basic facts about the disease. Probably best suited for medical students or pre–med students.
This class literally changed my life. I am a senior and it honestly made me sad I dont have ore time at Harvard to take advantage of the info/labs/connections here. This class was so inspiring and interesting. My friend group would literally wait until I came home so I could tell them the stories/info I learned in class.
