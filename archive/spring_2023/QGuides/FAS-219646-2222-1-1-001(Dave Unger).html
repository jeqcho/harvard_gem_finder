This class is worth more the more you engage with it! Don't be afraid to share your thoughts!
Take this class.
You will learn about multiple alternatives to traditional hierarchical organizational structures, profit–driven corporations, and individually–owned private property that you may never have learned in any of your core education. These things are real and they are valuable to know about if you are interested in shaping your world to function with a more cooperative and collaborative ethic. The course helps you both in understanding the background on this topic and in envisioning future worlds where life is different. You'll also get to have many wonderful discussions with the brilliant Dave Unger.
It’s a long class and feels like it drags on a little bit at times but overall manageable and easy to understand

Absolute Gem!! Only one paper and one big group project, class once a week. Would advise you to go to at least a fourth of class and speak up, as participation is 33% of the grade!
This class is very focused on Co–ops and Commons, and feels scattered at times. The 2 hours and 45 minute session is very lengthy and it's very focused on discussions. Feels like it is made for a small group, but instead was 50 people. I think there are much more engaging and interesting courses in the HistSci department, though this one is very easy.
Dave is really cool, and his courses are too! In my experience, students treated this course like a gem and as such we didn't get as much out of it (collectively) as we could have. Do the readings and find the (few) other people who do, too. Engage as deeply as possible, especially with the modules, and you'll get something out of this course.
This is a low workload, more hands–on class. In addition to class discussions, we did a lot of activities in class (building structures with pasta, "fishing" with candy, etc.) which made for a more fun experience. There are very few assignments throughout the semester – just one paper and a final group project – other than weekly readings. Dave is a really kind and approachable teacher!
Love Unger. Two things you should know: 1) he is a sweet, sweet guy, who will try to have your back and involve you in the class, and 2) in that sweetness you can get away with a lot if you so desire. If you're looking for a class to passively engage with, this is a great choice. If you're really interested and passionate about these issues, there may be something to found in the readings, but the class itself is likely to leave you disappointed.
Dave had expected to run this class as a small seminar, but he ended up with 40+ students, so naturally I think the course had some design flaws this year. (I imagine it will be better in the years to come.) This year, though, it was a complete gem; I probably did less than one hour of work each week, and I did still learn things in class. Take this class if you're looking for something to fill up your schedule, or if you're really passionate about the subject matter. If you're looking to learn a ton though or be pushed in any way, it's probably not the best call.
