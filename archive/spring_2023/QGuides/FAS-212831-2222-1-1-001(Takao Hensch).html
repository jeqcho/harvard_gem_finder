Definitely take it if you're at all interested in the molecular aspect of neuroscience and the causes of brain development.
Amazing class with a great professor.
Take this class! Dr. Hensch and the teaching staff really care about your success in the course and are very approachable. The course content is really interesting, and you will come out of the course learning about important experimental techniques used to study neuronal plasticity and critical period development. The guest lectures are also a really nice component of the course, and the workload is very manageable.
The sections were great, you got to lead discussions on the papers and really get into the figures. Lecture was kind of unrelated to the coursework (which is just to do reading responses and then make a grant proposal), so it was kind of hard to motivate to attend lecture / engage in lecture.
I loved this class! The lectures and articles were very interesting, and you definitely learn a lot. Takao is a great lecturer, and Patty and Jingting were amazing TFs as well. The only downside is that assignments generally are not graded and returned. As of right now, I have only received 3 grades out of over a dozen assignments completed, so I honestly have no clue where I stand in the course. I knew this going into the class and still decided to take it because I thought it would be worth it (and it was!). But definitely take that element into consideration during course registration!
This course is very much based in scientific literature and is fairly light assignment wise (no psets or exams). However, this puts a lot more weight on participation, section discussion, your presentation, and the final grant proposal. Make sure to seek out feedback as much as possible, especially for the grant proposal. Also, do the readings well enough to think of good discussion questions & to be able to speak confidently on the paper in section.
Definitely a super cool course that teaches you so much about how much experience truly impacts brain development! If you are interested in this area, or even just a little bit curious, take this course! Also get started on your final grant proposal early! Don't be afraid to ask for help and ask questions to finalize your aims.
This is a very interesting class with many positives and is not difficult. The only downside is that I never really knew how I was doing, because the feedback is very minimal.
this course has a lot of potential to be a relatively stress–free way to complete a neuro requirement, but if you don't know/remember/feel confident about a lot of neuro or biology concepts, take some caution. if the course is anything like it is/was in spring 2022, i got two grades/comments back on discussion questions even though we have several more sets of discussion questions to submit throughout the semester. i also didn't hear anything back on my grant topic turned proposal even though that comprises the majority of your grade. most of the class content wasn't too bad to digest and i usually didn't feel worried about this class, but man, the sparse feedback is a tough obstacle to work around
Pros: (1) Work is extremely light –– read a paper and write a short response to it every week. (2) The final grant proposal (2 pages single spaced) is a very fun and rewarding project. It's very low stress and you get to actually learn a lot about something you are genuinely interested in. Takao is extremely supportive throughout the process. (3) Takao and the TFs are super chill and genuinely want you to succeed in the course without giving you stress. Takao himself says that the final project is meant to be fun and enjoyable, and it really is. (4) Section is a journal club format which I personally enjoyed. If you don't like giving/listening to presentations it might not be for you though.
Cons: (1) You get almost no feedback on assignments throughout the course. It's not a super big deal because the course staff are chill, but if you're the kind of person who likes to know where you stand at all times it can be a little annoying.
Note: Highly highly recommend taking NEURO 80 BEFORE taking this class. I did not, and I was extremely confused for the first few lectures, until I caught myself up by reading a neuro textbook and Wikipedia, and then I ended up fine.
This is a great course! Put a lot of time into the readings and go to office hours. Think about how this area of neuro research can relate to your other passions for the final grant proposal. The teaching staff is great!
This course contains extremely interesting content. You spend a lot of time reading papers, which is great if you're looking to cultivate your critical reading skills for scientific publications. Takao also got lots of cool guest speakers for you who are experts in the field. Low stress, but still feel like I learned quite a bit. Definitely take it for your adv neuro req!
