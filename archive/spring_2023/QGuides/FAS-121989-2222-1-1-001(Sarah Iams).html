The grading is a little weird. Sarah says you can choose your own grade but then 'reserves' the right to it herself. This scared me a bit and I never really knew where I stood.
Be prepared to do a lot of the prep work on your own. Make time early on for office hours. Learn some mathematica beforehand.
This is a really great course if you put in the work. Since you are judging yourself, it provides you the chance to decide how much work to put in certain assignments, especially if some assignments seem to take up more time or are something that you can work on in future references.
This is an interesting class. You will learn a lot of material that you probably have not been exposed to. Since it is a flipped classroom it can be time consuming at times.
Great course across the board. While the videos are long, the textbook is really good and helped me understand the material a ton. Sarah's teaching also is outstanding and really helps make sure you understand all the skills as you keep progressing forward.
This class is awesome! The flipped classroom format really keeps things engaging, and contrary to what some might say, it does not entail that much extra work outside of class. There are a lot of different assignment types, which means no single thing is overly stressful or make–or–break for your grade. I took this class not knowing what to expect and now looking back, I can honestly say it has been my favorite math class. You should give it a shot. Also, Sarah is the best — would highly recommend taking at least one class with her, especially if you are an Applied Math concentrator.
Don’t slack off! All the resources are there and its really up to you to turn your assignments in on time
This class is a lot of work and composed of a lot of different moving parts. Make sure you have the time for it and make sure you start early on final project!
This is one of the most rewarding math classes I have taken at Harvard. I really like the flipped–class room aspect – it really forces you to learn the material when you always have to actively apply it in class. Sarah is an incredible instructor and very accessible outside of the classroom. Further, the content that you learn in this class is really applicable to every other field – I saw immediate applications of the learned materials to the other classes i was taking concurrently.
I would steer future students away from this course. This course was arguably the most demanding course of my Harvard experience so far. There were far too many minor assignments (pre–class assignments due before each class, in–class skill checks before each class, weekly problem sets), which made the course very difficult to catch up in if you happened to fall behind one week. Sarah is a great professor, but almost every assignment felt very tedious and stressful rather than helping me actually learn the necessary concepts. While the idea behind deciding your own grade may seem attractive, the amount of work and stress that this class causes arguably makes it not worth the hassle.
This class can be a lot of work hours wise (its flipped but you don't work on hw in class so you end up doing pre class assignments and psets) but its all very low stress because of the grading. Sarah is great and there are a lot of resources for the course.
This class gave me a real appreciation for Applied Math. If you are debating the purpose of math, of this concentration, and whether you love math –– take this course. Sarah is a fantastic instructor and the class is small, making it tight–knit as well. It's highly collaborative and exposes you to what it means for math to be applied than any other course I've taken at this school.
If you liked learning about nonlinear dynamical systems in Math 21b, you should consider this course. There are flexible deadlines and opportunities to redo assignments. Additionally, Sarah is a fantastic teacher and great at helping the students understand the material. The class does not have grades, so you "assign" your grade at the end of the semester. In theory, this sounds cool, but in practice, it can be difficult to decide which grade you should receive. Although the class was great, I have one reservation: it is A LOT of work. There are many assignments/ tasks on top of the pre–class work.
This class has a lot of components that require time and effort outside class but are very rewarding. Sarah is super flexible and an amazing instructor and really focuses on your understanding of the knowledge. Definitely a good choice for satisfying the Differential Equations req for AM
Although you nominally 'choose' your grade, don't let that be the reason you take this class. To justify giving yourself a grade of 'A', you will need to spend around 10 hours per week on the class. The material is not too challenging conceptually, but there is a lot of work to do.
Assignments are well–constructed with good skeleton Mathematica files, and the course is structured well. The coursework prepares you quite well for the 2D system analysis and the final project. Be aware that final project topics can vary significantly in difficulty and scope.
I recommend the class and think Sarah is one of the best in the Applied Math department.
I would tell future students you are getting your money's worth with this class. Although because of the flipped classroom style there is more homework, it really does add to your learning.
This is a moderately high–workload class in a low stress environment!! I definitely recommend, and Sarah is a really good teacher.
