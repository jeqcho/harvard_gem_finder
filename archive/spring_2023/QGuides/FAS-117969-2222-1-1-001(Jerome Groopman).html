Comments
Dr. Groopman speaks frequently of "generosity of spirit" in the books we read in seminar. I don't think I'll ever come across a person who embodies that more than he. Take this course (!!!!!!) and try to soak up as much of his wisdom as possible. The readings were all super interesting, and it is clear that over the years he has nit–picked the most valuable stories from literary canon of this genre. Essays are pretty low–stakes and low–pressure, but it's definitely better to turn in something good because he line–edits everything and gives in–depth feedback, which is either a blessing or a curse depending on the week.
Dr. Groopman is a wonderful professor to learn from. The atmosphere of the course can feel intense with his style of calling on you and asking if you know some obscure city in Europe or what a word in Latin means but the very obscure nature of these questions means that no one really knows until he tells us so there's no fear in saying "I don't know" or asking Dr. Groopman to clarify something. The readings can sometimes be lengthy so starting them early is important. The writing assignments seem to be critiques harshly but there aren't that many of them and the harsh critiques really do wonders to improve your writing if you implement the suggestions you're given. Dr. Groopman covers a variety of topics in the syllabus and the readings span a lot of time periods which is interesting and keeps the course engaging. He also brought us cookies to class (since you can't learn on an empty stomach!) and that was much appreciated. I highly recommend this course if you're pre–med (and not deathly afraid of doing some writing) or if you're into journalism/writing specifically in a scientific/narrative context. You really get out what you put into the course, and because of Dr. Groopman's career and accomplishments, people usually tend to put in a lot of effort, just so you're aware of the "standard"/ the attitude your peers will probably have towards the course.
This course is the ideal of what a freshman seminar is supposed to be. Amid the typical set of relatively large, impersonal, and at times uninteresting entry–level STEM requirements you will likely take if you are a prospective pre–med, it is incredibly refreshing to have a class like this in a small group setting, where you deeply engage with the "why" behind the material, contribute to active class discussions, and get the amazing opportunity to work with such an accomplished professor, physician, writer, and researcher. Dr. G genuinely cares about all of his students, who he handpicks from a competitive application pool for this class (so rank this one as your first choice if you want it), which is evident from how he conducts class by involving everyone in the discussion, the detailed feedback he gives on written work (he will line–edit your writing), the cookies he brings to every class, and the way he gets to know all of his students (you show up on the first day of class and he will know your name, where you're from, and where you went to high school, and he will remember everything you talk to him about). Prepare for a relatively consistent and reasonable but not trivial workload (on average probably 80–100ish pages of reading per week plus some interesting writing assignments that are on the shorter end – nothing >1000 words – but which you will want to put your best foot forward on), show up to class prepared and ready to learn, and this will be one of the most rewarding academic experiences you can find during your first year at Harvard.
Take this class!!!! Dr. Groopman is truly amazing and anybody would be SO lucky to learn from him. Yes, this class might be slightly more work than an average freshman seminar, but it is so worth it. He is honestly a genius and will transform your thinking about medicine and healthcare. Plus, he brings cookies each class! The format of the class is unlike anything I’d ever experienced, but you’ll adapt and learn to love it. This class is a wild ride, but a worthwhile one.
This course has been an absolute highlight of my semester! I've never found a course that better combines my interest in medicine and literature/writing. The readings were incredibly interesting and allowed me to see into the decisions and perspectives of physicians and patients. My favorite part of the class was Dr. G, who integrates his personal anecdotes with the lessons from these readings; his thought–provoking questions and insights always reminded me, in his own words, that "there is always more to learn." I came out of every class a better thinker as well as a better idea of what it means to be a good physician/person, and I would highly recommend anyone interested in medicine, literature, or anything in between to take this class! It's truly amazing!!
