What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Very math heavy
I loved this course! I learned the theory and application of causal inference rigorously. I found the textbook to be clearly written, lecture was helping in building on the textbook material, and homework was concise (a management amount) and a good balance of conceptual questions, simulations, and proofs. Professor Murphy is enthusiastic and shares insights and examples from the research world (health, economics, social sciences, etc.) during lecture. I also really appreciated the homework group structure; I made some new friends and felt comfortable having collaborators on each homework; in–class discussions were interesting.
This class is poorly organized and not well–supported. Once I scheduled a meeting with the professor to ask a few questions about the course, during which she blatantly told me that she had no idea how the course would be evaluated. On another occasion I scheduled a meeting with the TF since I could not make his one weekly office hour; a medical situation arose which caused me to arrive roughly 2–3 minutes late; finding an empty room, I messaged him, only to receive, 2 hours later, an angry email describing an intense period of waiting, which ferociously culminated in the TF departing without notifying me... c'mon man.

After these interactions and others like them I received a zero on one of the twice–weekly quizzes. After receiving neither comments nor an answer key, I basically had no motivation in the course. I switched to it pass/fail, and learned very little. Sometimes I would skip lecture for a week, come back, and realize that I could follow the material perfectly well... so what did I miss? The world may never know.

I was taking Stat 98 at the time, and did my final project on a causal inference topic. I learned more in those 10 days of independent research than in the whole semester in 186. I would say definitively that there are better ways to spend your time at Harvard than this class.
