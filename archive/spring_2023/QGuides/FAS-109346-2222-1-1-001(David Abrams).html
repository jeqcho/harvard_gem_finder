What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you want to properly learn how to make circuits, please take this class. I enjoyed the breakdown of the class being half lecture and half lab; David Abrams is a good instructor, though he will not hesitate to go very in detail regarding specific topics in class and office hours. Pedro was an amazing TF, and he helped me a lot in making sure that I was comfortable with the material, and was getting through the labs. Be aware though, this class takes up a lot of time: on top of the two four hour sessions, pre–class readings took me about an hour if I wanted to properly go through the material before section (when I was able to go through it, they were sometimes helpful). Even though David estimated 2–3 hours per homework during the week, normally it would take me double that amount of time, if I wanted to do the homework well. TL;DR, this class was hard and time–consuming, but if you're interested in electronics it's definitely worth it!
It's difficult but a lot of fun.
I would highly recommend this course. Although it is a lot of time spent in class (4 hours x2 a week), it is well worth the time and effort. I have learned a tremendous amount about both practical circuit design and helpful frameworks for designing code to interface with the circuits. Additionally, just from all the time spent in lab, my debugging and hardware/circuit building skills have improved greatly. I have also picked up a strong appreciation for data sheets and now know how to look for relevant specifications that will help inform component selection in future designs.
Good starting point for FPGAs, microcontroller part is only useful if you clearly know why an Arduino won't work for your project/research/life.
