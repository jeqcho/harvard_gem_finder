TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Not only is it a gem but it really truly teaches you about the importance, relevance, and implications of stigma that will change the way you view discrimination and stigmatic interactions. If you are a person interested in solidifying the reasons for why people may stigmatize,/discriminate even unintentionally and the potential solutions and health implications, this class is a definite must. TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Everything about the course is amazing. Professor Hatzenbuehler's passion for reducing stigma is contagious. He structures the course in a way that we are able to learn about stigma in a very unique way. I particularly liked the research component of this course; we get to apply the theories we learn about in class to a stigmatized identity of our choosing. By the end of the semester, we truly know so much about how stigma affects that group of individuals and what approaches could be taken to reduce this stigma. It also has a really light workload, which is definitely a huge plus. An absolute gem of a course.
Great course. You should definitely take this class if you are at all interested in stigma, health, discrimination, prejudice etc.
PROS:
– Incredible professor, the very best kind at Harvard – very active in conducting research in this field, truly an expert; very passionate about the material; generates enthusiasm among the students; truly cares and seems invested in all the students; great lecturer; encourages everyone to attend Office Hours, generous with his time.
– Good balance of psychological and sociological approaches, and considers problems at different levels (individual, interpersonal, structural).
– Issues discussed felt important and concepts were applicable to the real world.
– Engaged peers, good discussions.
– Good variety of activities in lecture (lecture, discussion, videos, debates, small group activities).
– Interesting assignments.
CONS/THINGS TO CONSIDER:
– NOT a gem. The application tasks were actually a lot of work (~15 hours a week) and the rubric that was introduced felt unattainable within the one page limit – I would suggest not including as many requirements in the rubric or increasing the page limit so we had more space to hit the requirements.
Definitely take the course but be mindful that the weekly application tasks require a lot of time if you are hoping to achieve full marks.

This is an incredible course. Prof. H is amazing, and he's really knowledgeable about stigma. I learned a lot and the concepts are really applicable to life. Sections are ok, but lectures make the course outstanding, so be sure to attend them! Definitely take this course.
Absolutely take this course! This is the most rewarding course that I have taken, and I am a senior. Professor Hatzenbuehler is really an eminence in his field of research, and it's such a precious opportunity to get to learn about stigma directly from him. He is also super well–spoken and the nicest person in the world, and the teaching staff is amazing, which mean you will most likely have a very positive experience. The issue of stigma as it relates to health has more recently come to attention, and it's perhaps a lot more complex than how it is simplified in popular media. There are so many different motives and processes that can underlie stigma, and if we don't understand them well, well–intended interventions could only backfire. This course will teach you the complexities of stigma processes, and ultimately give you hope that things can change. If you want to contribute to that change, or are curious in any way, this is certainly the course for you to take.
