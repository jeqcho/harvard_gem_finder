A lot of this class can get dry really quickly—despite Dr. Kumekawa's very enthusiastic lectures—if you're not engrossed in the material, so as obvious as it sounds, just make sure you're ready to dive into a lot of challenging abstract readings. The midterm was graded harshly, relative to typical essay exam courses, and the most frustrating thing was that we received zero written feedback when the exams were returned (so we didn't know why any points were deducted). I also can't recommend taking the class if the group project component remains and you don't have friends in it with you, as it is difficult to do well if you end up in a group with less engaged students. As for the final paper, just make sure to plan in advance as it's very self–driven; it's nice that the topic can be anything you want.
If you are looking for a class that covers economic history across a massive timeline and allows you to deep dive into the primary sources behind these theories, I would highly recommend this course! I really enjoyed the class and I learned a ton about the development of economic theory.
Such a good course! Prof. Kumekawa is awesome, perhaps the best lecturer I've had in college
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! One of my favorite classes I've taken. Dr. Kumekawa is the best lecturer I've had at Harvard – his passion and knowledge of the topic shines through. Salmaan is a really good TF. You will learn so much in every single lecture – this class covers a lot of material in a really engaging way. Readings are concise and to the point. Assignments are all fair and will let you apply what you've learned in class. Take this class if you have any interest in how economic concepts have developed over time. IMO, this should be a required class for all Econ concentrators.
The content is interesting and Professor Kumekawa makes lectures exciting because he's so excited about the material.
