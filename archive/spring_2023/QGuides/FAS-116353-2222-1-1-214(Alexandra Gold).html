DR GOLD IS A GIFT PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE TAKE THIS COURSE TAKE THIS COURSE. DO NOT BE A FOOL AND TAKE THIS COURSE PLEASE. As a dense stem major who thought that writing 2–page papers were nearly insufferable, I came into expos resigned to the pain this class would cause me for the entirety of the spring semester. I accepted the hit to my GPA and that I was about to endure and just hoped for the best based on the topic and q guide scores. BOY WAS I WRONG. I left this course fully prepared to change my major to either English or comp lit because Dr. Gold is such an incredible teacher and will genuinely help your writing. Her comments on drafts are thoughtful and useful and she is SO supportive if you reach out for help. She wants you to succeed and to make you a better writer and IT SHOWS. The class itself is as enjoyable as it could be. Some classes are spent discussing readings and the people this class attracts (by nature of the topic) make the discussions insightful and inclusive and will make you want to participate. The classes when you go over the technical parts of writing are expectedly less interesting but Dr. Gold is such a wonderful person to be in a room with and does care about communicating the information so these classes are not that painful to sit through (and most of the time we did activities that allowed for social interaction which was maybe marginally useful for understanding the material but engage with the info in the Gold Guide (which is goated btw) if you tune out during lectures. The class is a fun experience even if you hate writing or struggle with it –– it was my favorite class this semester. Even if you aren't really sure if you have much to say on the issues of feminism and WGS, the research paper is super flexible and you can write about just about anything related to social justice or activism or social media or art –– not everything is about gender specifically in the class and it touches on broader topics of activism that are enlightening. I thought I hated writing (which I still lowkey do but I think everyone hates writing on some level) but this class has made me love it in a strange way and once again (because it can't be said enough) Dr. Gold is an incredible preceptor. Do not be stupid and do take this class. Take this class even if you are not a woman or don't feel you fully identify with the me too movement/experiences because the class is so much more than just social issues. If nothing else take it for Dr. Gold. Obviously, your mileage may vary, but expos was a fundamentally (positive) life–changing experience for me (which is a sentence that I don't think gets said often). I have no idea what they pay Dr. Gold, but I guarantee it's not enough.
This class is truly a gem expos class. Not only are the course materials and texts engaging and interesting, but Dr. Gold is an incredible instructor who gives excellent feedback to help guide students' writing.

This is the best Expos course I could've taken! Though it is not always "easy," all of the work serves a greater purpose and is interesting enough that you'll want to do it. You will never do "busywork," and you will have so many opportunities to learn from people with various perspectives. Dr. Gold is so knowledgeable and passionate about her field, so its really fun to learn from her. At the same time, she gives credence and respect to student ideas, helping to spark genuine discussion in group settings and in one–on–one settings. As you will learn, feminism is very broad, which means that there is a lot of flexibility when choosing what you want to study for your Expos research paper. No matter what you choose, though, you will be supported and encouraged to have fun with it. This is such a great Expos class: I highly, highly recommend.
