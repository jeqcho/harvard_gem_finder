This class is very interesting but beware. The grading sucks. The problem sets were way too hard, harshly graded on random details, and the proposal was also graded very harshly. Participation scores they gave were so bad. The professor I think wants to give everyone As but the grading is so unfair.
This class is super interesting and relevant to an area of research that some of us may not have thought of if not for this class. It is worth taking for the topics themselves, but hopefully the class will be restructured to be more accessible and provide a more fruitful academic experience.
Ryan is a good professor who clearly cares about the class and his students. This course does not have a lot of work, so it's possible to get by without understanding all the material. I had a hard time comprehending the material, so I'd only recommend to people who know/remember RNA and DNA molecular biology well and to people who are very interested in the nitty gritty molecular/chemical details. Definitely not a typical SCRB class, this way way more molecular focused.
This class is very rigorous, with a strong emphasis on the professor's work (for better or for worse). It's graded pretty tough (especially as there's a good number of grad students) so I wouldn't take it if you aren't willing to put the extra effort into the psets and project.
This is not a SCRB course.
This course is great! I feel as if I learned a lot of relevant information from this class, and I recommend taking it if you're interested in learning more about RNA and RNA–based therapeutics! I didn't know too much before this course, and I feel as if I learned a lot!
Office hours aren't very helpful. TF said they wanted to challenge us on assignments and would just point us to lecture slides instead of explaining concepts.
Take this class!!! Professor Flynn is so knowledgeable about RNA biology and you will learn a lot from him. This class is especially helpful if you have any interest in RNA research, since much of the course focuses on the methods which enabled novel discoveries in RNA biology. After this course, I feel much more confident in designing my own experiments and reading the literature. The week–to–week workload is minimal, so it's a good class to balance out a busy semester. There are two problem sets and a final proposal, which each took a decent amount of work, but besides that, all you need to do each week is read a paper and come to class. Overall, a great course with a great professor. I would definitely recommend it.
