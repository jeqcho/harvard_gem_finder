What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
First and foremost, this is a math–heavy seminar. You don't necessarily need a super strong math background, but be prepared to do a considerable amount of math during class.
Given that you are looking for a math–heavy seminar, this class is amazing. The content is super interesting, and covers a huge array of fields. Professor Williams is a superb lecturer, and also a really nice person. Get to know her; I highly, highly recommend going to office hours even if you don't have questions. The amount of homework is also perfect; enough to be interesting, but not enough as to be overbearing. The final presentation and paper are also pretty open, which is nice.
Professor Williams is amazing!! She is a great lecturer and it is wonderful to get to talk to her in a smaller more informal setting. A bit more work that I would have expected from a freshman seminar, but especially if you are considering majoring in math I would highly recommend.
This freshman seminar is a significant investment, with weekly homework for the first half, a final presentation, and a final paper (5–10 pages), but trust me when I say it is worth it. Prof. Williams will take you on an incredible mathematical journey – in the middle of lecture, one student leapt up from their chair and proclaimed that the result on the board was the coolest they'd ever seen. This course rekindled my interest in combinatorics and got me excited about fields of math/physics I hadn't ever heard of, in addition to introducing me to some of my closest friends here at Harvard. Sadly, I don't think this course will be offered during the 2022–23 school year, but take this class if you can!
This was a great seminar. The content was very interesting and Prof. Williams is great. The lectures were mostly very clear and organized, and we would have informal chats at the end of class about life in the math world, which was great. Also, the other students were always incredibly engaged and I feel like there were a lot of really interesting people in this class.
Definitely take this class! It was so much fun, and Prof. Williams is a great teacher! The topics presented in this class are very niche, as is the learning environment. This class is a really special opportunity––I wouldn't miss it!
Professor Williams is so nice and helpful. If you are interested in combinatorics, this class will give a general view of the subject, putting on display the goals in the community. However, if even if you are not wanting to do combinatorics, this seminar is a must for any future mathematicians here at Harvard. Expect to spend some time on the problem sets though!
