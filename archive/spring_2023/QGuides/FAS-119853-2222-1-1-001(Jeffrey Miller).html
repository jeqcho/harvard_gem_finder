Participate in class!
Great course! The workload can be a little bit heavy at times (there's a good bit of coding involved in homework, and there were 3 required class presentations), but I think it's worth it, considering the amount of material covered.
This is a really great course if you're interested in Bayesian statistics (which you should be). It's a good amount of work, but it's worth it. If you have the chance to take this with Jeff Miller, do it! He's one of the best profs in the BST department. He's a great lecturer and has great materials.
This is a fantastic class that has changed the way I think about statistical and inferential questions. Dr. Miller does a brilliant job tying new frameworks for approaching uncertainty and statistical concepts with the frequentist approaches we are all familiar with, helping to ground Bayesian approaches in what we are already familiar with.
Take this class if you're at all interested in Bayesian methods or computation. The homeworks take a while (<=8 hours), but you have plenty of time to do them (> 1.5 weeks usually) and they teach you a lot. You can likely do the assignments with a passing grade without paying much attention to lecture and just reviewing the slides later, but the lectures are very interesting if you choose to attend –– you get out of this class what you put into it. Also, there are no exams, only a project, so you can really tailor your learning to whatever topics you're interested in.
