Super interesting course with a very manageable workload – highly recommend if you're interest in bio. I originally didn't like writing very much, but actually enjoyed writing most of these essays – however, the caveat to this is that you must must must start early on your essay. Writing your entire essay the day before it's due is never going to be fun, no matter how good the course is. Other than that, Emilie is a joy to be in class with and is super helpful and accommodating to her students
The class is very manageable if you do the readings and participate.
This class is great if you want to learn to write about scientific concepts in an English context. You should not take this course if you want to do a lot of close reading. You also should not expect exceptionally rigorous scientific discourse; however, you wouldn't find that in any Expos class. This class has a good amount of reading (reading that is very dense: scientific papers and Nietzsche) but generally otherwise it is not that hard compared to other Expos classes. Emilie is a fantastic preceptor; except to thoroughly enjoy class.
Engineering life is a great expos class to take if you have any interest in biology, specifically the ethics of synthetic biology. Dr. Raymer is a great preceptor and is very willing to work with her students to achieve their writing goals. She also provides great feedback on assignments while encouraging engaging discussions in class.
If you plan on going into science then take this class it's very useful.
This is a great choice for Expos if you are interested in the bleeding edge of biology and the various ecological, moral, and scientific dilemmas that have arisen as our technological capabilities blur the boundaries between nature and feats of human engineering. Should you take this course, you will become a miniature expert on the debates surrounding genetic enhancements, de–extinction, and another topic of your choice within synthetic biology, and hopefully, learn how to delve into these controversial issues with depth and tact.

I really enjoyed the fruitful in–class discussions we had about these topics, so if this topic interests you, I will wholeheartedly recommend this class! It is structured pretty similarly to other Expos classes where there are 3 units each focusing on a different topic. At the end of each unit you are expected to write a research paper which enters an ongoing debate within synthetic biology or compares the argument of one author through the lens of another.
Great class, especially if you are interested in biology, ethics, philosophy, or any combination of the three! The topics were super interesting, and Dr. Raymer is a great teacher/lovely person. Would highly recommend it. Just be wary of a week or two of heavy reading at the beginning of the semester, but then it becomes a lot less
Engineering Life rocks! The readings and paper topics are genuinely super interesting, and Emilie is such a sweet and understanding person.
Some weeks there are a lot of readings, but if you are at all interested in synthetic biology this is the expos for you! Our section had extremely engaging discussions and the materials are often fascinating to read.
