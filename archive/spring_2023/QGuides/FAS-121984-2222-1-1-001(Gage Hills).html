This is fantastic class that teaches you how to make VLSI circuits and systems. You are taught enough that you can continue to build things in industry. While the course has a good amount of work, I did not find it to be stressful. Most of my time was spent working on the problem sets which did a fantastic job of teaching me how to use the VLSI design tools.
This is an excellent class. Interesting, funny, not too work intensive, etc. If you get the chance to take this class, do it. I recommend taking CS141 AT THE SAME TIME, because this class basically functions as the applied side to what you learn in CS141. I think taking this class without CS141 (either at the same time or as a prior class) would be unnecessarily hard and that you would lose out on the beauty of the material.
I learned so much from this course!! I'm a graduate student working in a completely different field, with no electrical engineering experience, but this has been my favorite class at Harvard.
This class is great to take when you need something to lighten the load of a heavy semester. It pairs perfectly with CS141 so if you are an EE concentrator, I would definitely recommend taking them at the same time if you enjoy the device side of EE. The class has a great vibe and its a great way to meet grad students and understand what grad school is like.
Take this class if you are concentrating in either EE or CS! You will learn so much about how actual chips are being made (and have the opportunity to make one yourself at the end of the class)!

He is insanely smart, supportive. funny, and passionate about education and VLSI, which helped create a very collaborative and enjoyable learning experience.

He also spends a lot of time crafting lectures and our design labs, and it shows because they are detailed and well made. Would recommend if you have even a passive interest in VLSI just for Gage.
