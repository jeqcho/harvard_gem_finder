Take this class if you want to learn how to quantify and model problems using mathematical tools.
This is a great AM class. Definitely take if you are an AM concentrator. The professor is good and very reasonable, the work is reasonable compared to other AM classes and honestly very interesting. The projects are a fun way to synthesize the work
I had a ton of fun with this course because my group chose to do very fun projects.
I originally took this course to get honors without a thesis, but it honestly turned into one of my favorite AM classes at Harvard. This course was by far the most applicable math course I have taken, and I actually wish I had taken it my junior year, as I think I may have been more confident in writing a thesis with the experiences from this class under my belt. There are so many interesting topics you learn in this class, and my favorite part is that if you don't like one, the material moves quickly, and you don't need to apply any one given concept to your final projects. This class honestly changed the way I think about how math interacts with the real world, and I understand why it's a critical class for AM concentrators.
I will say, it's rather important to get a good group for your project. I had a very dedicated, strong group, but I think if my group was less hard–working and cohesive, I may have liked this class less so. Overall, the concepts are super engaging, the material is useful, the professor and TFs are really kind, and I couldn't recommend this class more.
Finding a good group to do the group projects with really shapes the experience in this class. If you're all on the same page, the more you put into this class, the more you'll get out of it.

It’s a very rewarding class! Zhiming is a wonderful person and professor, and in the end I learned a lot this semester. It’s engaging, and you’ll find yourself learning things that you know you will bring to your career one day.

This class is superb! One of the best in the Applied Math department! The materials used and given to you are very approachable and make getting started very straight–forward. The projects are fun explorations and opportunities to deep dive appropriately! I think that this class is really nice if you have friends in it, but also you will be able to formulate strong teams. You will learn a lot in this class and it is a great way to synthesize years of applied math together!
Really great class, I would definitely recommend. Professor Kuang is the best, and I found the material super interesting throughout. There's a lot of flexibility with projects, but it's important to find a good group to work with (I don't think you have to be a senior to have a good group, just make friends!). Note also that you have to turn in labs throughout the semester (sometimes around the final projects) and this can feel like a lot some weeks––but make sure to use the in class time well, because you can get most of the labs done if you focus!
