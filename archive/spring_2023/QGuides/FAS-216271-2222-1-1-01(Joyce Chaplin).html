Definitely a gem. Readings are super digestable (1.5hrs to read, max), the writing assignments are spread out and fairly straightforward, and Professor Chaplin is fantastic!
Prof. Chaplin is great, and has a really ambitious vision for the possibilities of transformation at Harvard. I would recommend this course, it's manageable and engaging, and you get to design something that will physically exist— pretty cool.
The class is oriented around a fun project, and very light.
Take this class! If you have any interest in the concept of wilderness, landscape history, GIS mapping, or nature more generally, you will enjoy this class. I had absolutely no experience (or interest really) in GIS mapping before taking this course, so don't be intimidated or turned off from that aspect. Prof. Chaplin is wonderful. She is extremely thoughtful and wants this course to be rewarding for her students. She's very willing to make amendments to the syllabus or assignments if it will benefit the students. This class, while extremely rewarding, is not at all stressful. Assignments are more than manageable. Readings can be hefty, but you surely don't have to read everything every week (though they are super interesting). Class can be a bit long, especially if you aren't interested in the topics. This class does have a lot of group work, but at least from my experience, everyone in the class is really interested in what we're working on, so working in groups was very pleasant. If you are on the fence about taking this class, I really do recommend it. Plus, it's incredible to have a true impact on the Harvard community. I can't wait until our little mound is planted, and we get to see our work in action. It was also a really cool experience to work with representatives from the Business School –– it was a window into what real life is like and what presenting pitches is like. Highly, highly recommend!!
