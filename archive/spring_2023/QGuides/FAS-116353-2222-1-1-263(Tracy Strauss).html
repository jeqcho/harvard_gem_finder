What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Please take this class with Professor Strauss. She was incredibly compassionate, understanding, and patient, and she pushed us to improve our writing significantly. The material was fascinating, and I feel as if I've learned much about not only how to write academic papers but also how to live my life with more agency and through a more analytical lens.
On the material: While it engages with some really great works and writers, this course lacks an intellectual core that makes it cohesive. Beside commenting that the works do, in some way, pertain to the self/self discovery, no discussion will EVER go deeper than that/ investigate what an author is saying or how they are saying it. Essentially they get you intellectually hungry (again, because the works are so great) and then dont let you eat!

On improving your writing: perhaps this is the nature of all expos courses, but it felt as though all pleasure was drained from the writing process in that it became totally driven by arbitrary parameters you had to hit. ZERO emphasis whatsoever on actually saying something interesting or worthwhile, just concerned with if you mentioned all four 'craft techniques' per paragraph as you were supposed to. I felt my writing became worse –– less organic, more formulaic, less lucid –– as a result of this class.
Select a course you are interested in but also have heard good things about the professor.
I really enjoyed this course. The materials were really interesting and felt very relevant, and it was really neat to be able to write about a topic that I genuinely cared about.
