This class, to me, really didn't feel like a neuro course – it's an mcb course at its core. As a psych concentrator interested in neuro, I was hoping to gain a more biologically grounded view of the mind, but that's not really what this course is. Instead, this course is about a collection of genetic and molecular systems that also happen to relate to behavior, and most of that behavior will be related to sensory systems rather than more complex behaviors like parenting or habits. (Speaking of which – if, like me, you're most interested in the latter half of this course, be aware that the topics don't go that in depth. The second half of the course is like speed dating a bunch of very complex topics.) I did learn a lot about genetic methods, which is super super helpful if you're going into neuroscience and want to have more scientific literacy. But if you're taking this course for the behavior component, I'd say that in my opinion, genetics/molecules are too far removed from behavior for them to interest me.
