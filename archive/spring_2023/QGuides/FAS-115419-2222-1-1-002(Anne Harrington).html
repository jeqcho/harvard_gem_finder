This class is really well taught. It gives you the opportunity to take a class with Professor Harrington which is phenomenal as she is the best Professor I've ever had. Overall, you will learn how to think. Yes, the material is interesting but the bigger emphasis is the skills that you apply to the new material each week. Between the presentation and the papers you will have the skills for history of science that you need. The thing that I love about this class is it doesn't feel like much work at all, but at the same time I feel like I've learned so much.
this class is a great opportunity to gain a more enriched understanding of histsci methods and, if you haven't already taken another histsci class, shows you how to take a research interest or curiosity and turn it into a full–fledged project. in addition, through section and through the sharing sessions, you will leave this class with a wonderful community of histsci peers, so don't be afraid to make friends (with other students as well as with professor harrington and the other amazing tfs)!
Anyone who said this class was easy–going and interesting lied. Between faculty panels, sharing sessions, and section, this class was a 5–hour time commitment. Mind you, this is a sophomore TUTORIAL. Additionally, there were 3 large writing assignments and a 10 page mock research proposal we had to complete for HS97. All of this was so excessive and stressful on the behalf of the student. None of these assignments helped with my historical analysis or writing skills; it honestly was just busy work! Section was boring for me, but that's likely because of my TF. And the course material was also quite boring some weeks (for reference, you learn about many random niche topics like the atomic bomb or Scopes trial or the vaccine). Overall, this class felt like such a drag because of (1) the excessive time commitment between class meetings and time spent on the papers and (2) the redundant conversations we would have during sharing sessions (that ran over time every. single. week). I believe this is the first iteration of this course structure, and I have to say I did not enjoy it at all. This class did not change my life and it actually diminished my enthusiasm for this concentration unfortunately. Hoping junior tutorial is better than this :(
I would warn future students that the class can be very chaotic at times (very unorganized canvas website, rotating topics each week). But, I would l also tell them that the class can be extremely rewarding if you lower your standards and relax a bit. Also – prof. Harrington seems to really care about her students and the contribution she is making to our acadmeic experinece. also the TF's are extremely engaging as well and i even spent time working with Tfs who were not assigned to my section.
The essays are a bit of a pain, but the class itself—and sections—are really interesting.
Stay on top of the readings and pay attention and you’ll be fine!
LOVED this class!! you will have the pleasure of meeting the course faculty, who care very much about their students and preparing them for writing their own research papers.
It is a very neutral class, nothing too hard or too easy.
Newly redesigned this year, the sophomore tutorial was very well planned and not too difficult. Readings are interesting, but beware of the 2 hour sharing section, and do not under any circumstances plan a lab session immediately after it.
This class is required so there's not much to say. It's not too difficult, but it's not interesting either. Although the class says it's 2 hours and 45 minutes long on Canvas, prepare to be in class closer to 5 hours every week because there's a section and faculty panel that's not obvious ahead of time. The class really depends on how good your sharing session discussion is, so if you have a good class, you might get more out of it than I did.
