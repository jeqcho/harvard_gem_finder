Take this class
I recommend this class! Anna is incredibly supportive and knowledgable, and you will learn a lot and change the way you think about science and knowledge creation. The readings and workload is manageable (while still engaging and intensive). Everyone in the class had different concentration backgrounds which made discussions super interesting.
Absolutely take this class. Dr Jabloner is a brilliant lecturer. It does not take that much time but you certainly get a lot out of this class. This class is especially interesting if you are coming from a scientific background. What you get out of this class is dependent on your effort. You can skim the readings and survive but the readings are actually wonderful. I remember planning to skip the readings and then pulling all nighters because it was too interesting!
This class is a must–take, no matter your academic background! If you have any interest in the intersection of science, technology, and society, you'll gain a lot. The readings are a bit heavy, but are so worth it, as I appreciated how in every class Anna and Nayanika opened up really fruitful and eye–opening conversations, tying together the texts, student comments, and current, relatable events. This was my most enjoyable class this semester, and I'm looking forward to taking more classes related to STS in the future because of it.
This class is one that you will really get out what you put in. If you do the weekly readings and participate in class, you can have engaging and interesting discussions that make you leave the class with a changed perspective. The readings can be quite long some weeks, but they are overall pretty interesting. For the two papers, they provide you with topics so you are not lost on what to write (especially if you are not a humanities/social sciences person). The two papers are not long and definitely should not deter you from taking the course. Anna is typically great, however she had a TF this year that did most of the grading/meeting for office hours instead of her.
Do not hesitate, take this class. Professor Jabloner and Nayanika are both extremely knowledgeable and great at leading productive discussions. Seminar style classes are a classroom style you have to experience at least once while at Harvard. The readings are not hard, the workload is light, and the profound effect on my worldview during the around 2 hours of assigned reading per week will be with me the rest of my life.
This is a great class to take that is a good balance between interesting and not too challenging. I felt like I learned a lot, though I definitely did skim some of the readings when they got a bit dry. Anna is a good professor who strikes the balance between lecturing and fostering discussion quite well, as I always felt like I had enough background to jump into discussion.
This is a wonderful course that will make you think critically about the relationship between science and society, about how science can be a source of good, but can also perpetuate injustices in the world under the banner of objective truth.
The world at the moment seems a chaotic place, where the authority of science on things like climate change is increasingly questioned. If everyone planning to major in a STEM subject had to take a class like this, then I think this would be one way to start to change things for the better.
If you are at all interested in learning about the ways that science, technology, and society interact – and how knowledge is produced – I wouldn't recommend taking any other class. Professor Jabloner does a phenomenal job leading discussion and asking critical questions about how we think, and think about thinking. The assignments themselves are not very hard as long as you are engaged in class. The readings can be dense at times but eventually illuminate many aspects about the scientific society and how we arrived at the facts that we know to be truth today.
The readings may seem long and dense at times, but you will always be thankful you did them come class time. Also, although the class period takes up a two hour block, it is important to stay engaged and make use of the short time you have together per week.
This class was very interesting in that it really brought how social factors influence the facts that we learn in school and in academia in general. Great conversations and workload. Readings can sometimes be pretty dense but as long as you get the gist from each one that is enough to be able to actively participate. Got a little repetitive and boring in the second half of the semester but overall really enjoyed this class! Anna is amazing and a great facilitator of conversation.
