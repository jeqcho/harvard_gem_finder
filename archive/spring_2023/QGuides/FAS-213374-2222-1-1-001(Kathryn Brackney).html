This course offers a fresh, enjoyable way of looking at a relatively familiar historical period, mostly through film. Definitely recommended.
Do not take this class. Professor Brackney is a tough grader. I know people who genuinely tried in this class and were still receiving low grades on their essays. This class seems like a gem because you just watch movies, but it actually ended up being the hardest class I took this semester because of the hard grading. I put in 50+ hours into my final essay, and I still feel like I won't get a good grade :(
I would say, take it! It's a really interesting subject matter and you learn a lot about how to approach comedy. However, make sure to set aside a good chunk of time per week for the readings and films. There is a lot! Also don't blow off the weekly assignments because Dr. Brackney really appreciates and notes when you put time into them.
This class is an in–depth seminar that works best for students who are primarily interested in European modern history––it is more difficult to engage in this class if that is not your main course of study
I think if you're genuinely very interested in comedy or modern European history then you can get a lot out of this class. Just be willing to put in the time of watching the films / doing the readings because otherwise you won't get much out of it.
