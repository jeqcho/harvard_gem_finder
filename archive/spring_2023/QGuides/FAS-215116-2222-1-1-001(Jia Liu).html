You should definitely have a strong electrical engineering background if you want to be able to understand everything that goes on in the course. You can still take it if you do not have such a strong background, but be prepared to not understand some things, especially in the middle/later parts of the semester. Professor Liu is generally happy to answer questions after class or during office hours, and the homework and lab assignments tend to be interesting and not overly difficult. Some things covered include Neuralink and optogeneticsâ€“both very cool topics. Would recommend for Bioengineering concentrators or EE concentrators with an interest in medical devices, electrophysiology, or basic neuroengineering.
This class is not a lot of work, but I also don't feel like I learned so much from it. If you want a chill class that fulfills an engineering requirement this can be it (but warning that lab starts halfway through the semester and is 3 hours a week), but I don't feel like it helped my understanding of engineering/bioelectronics. Coming into it with more electrical engineering background could be helpful for that
The course has interesting material, but it is hard to follow. The teaching is often unclear because the professor speeds through the lecture material. The professor assumes that we know more than we do and often made me scared to ask questions because he often said that we already learned what I asked about. If you feel comfortable with electronics, then this will be a great course for you! But if you are still learning, I don't think this course will offer the support you need.
I think the class is most useful for 1st or 2nd year graduate students that are looking to start engaging in research. The class expectations are set reasonably for graduate students with a background in electrical engineering or instrumentation, but if I didn't have that background I'd probably be lost.
It is not work–intensive but it has very interesting contents to learn and the several presentation requirements makes the course very interactive.
