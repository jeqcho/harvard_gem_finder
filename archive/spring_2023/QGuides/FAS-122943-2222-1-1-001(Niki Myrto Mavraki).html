This is a great class for people who took 21a/b and are looking for a transition into proof writing. The material was mostly new (and entirely unrelated to 21a/b) and required almost no math background. It seemed like the most useful skill for this class was the ability to figure out the edge cases for any mathematical statement and show why they were or weren't true – everything beyond that was just notation. The material itself was about as difficult as 21b although this class required a fair bit more creativity, but the workload was about half that of 21b. The exams were the most challenging part of the class, but proper preparation and a decent mathematical intuition was sufficient to perform well.
This course is a bit of a higher–level mathematics course focused on proofs in addition to other mathematical concepts. It is more conceptual rather than computational.
