What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

The class is pretty straightforward, meeting once a week for less than the entire 3–hour block. The class itself is mostly discussion, so make sure to do the weekly readings if you want to engage. About half the time there would also be a slidedeck presented to us with supplemental information. 4 very short assignments (less than every other week) besides than the final paper help dig into practice with IR style theories and hypothesis building. Take if you want to learn more about the very topical U.S.–China relations from a historical, security, Taiwan, theoretical, and race relations standpoint. Final paper is pretty hefty (35 pages) so don't take it randomly.
I feel so much more confident in my understanding of China, US–China relations. I can speak intelligently with people about things like Taiwan, the economic relationship, etc. that I've always enjoyed but never fully understood. I also feel way better about thinking theoretically for research papers and beyond. As Prof. Johnston stresses, theories are important even in policy/politics. The reading load is reasonable (and the pieces are interesting). The earlier assignments can feel tedious, but don't stress them.
Professor Johnston is amazing and brought our class cookies multiple times throughout the semester. The presentation portion of the seminar can get a little boring at times, but the material itself was very interesting. Make sure you get started on the paper early. It's 30–35 pages, and the assignments throughout the semester don't do a great job of building up to it, so you basically start it with a month left.
Be ready to write a very long original research paper (i.e. at least 20 pages), but there isn't any other graded work & it is truly a rewarding and meaningful experience to have completed the course
Would highly recommended this class. It was one of the most memorable classes I've taken at Harvard. Don't let the 30 page paper faze youit's really not that much considering you only have to write one thing for the semester. Professor Johnston is a really engaging lecturer (and he provides quality cookies each class). He's super knowledgeable and very laid back. Grading isn't harsh at all, and he really emphasizes the learning part over grades. We also had amazing speakers (one of whom was a retired Major General of China!), so take this class before you graduate! You don't have to be a gov concentrator to get a lot out of this class.
This class is an excellent Gov 94. Professor Johnston was great to learn from he was super knowledgeable, flexible with assignments, and clearly cared about our learning and development as scholars. The course topics were broad and engaging, and after taking the class, I felt I had a deeper understand of each sides motivations and historical perspectives on the issues that animate the relationship. I also appreciated the fact that the course was a bit more structured than the typical GOV 94; Professor Johnston had slides for each class and made an effort to ensure that we got a representative overview of the topic (unlike some Gov 94s where you only learn about what the loudest voices want to focus on) while still facilitating robust participation in the class. Professor Johnston's connections also allowed him to bring in some really interesting guests that deepened the learning opportunities.

The course assignments were fairly short and all built up to the final paper. This course was an excellent research practice course in addition to the obvious focus on U.S–China relations; I felt like I was a better researcher after taking the class. Though such a long final paper can feel intimidating, Professor Johnston gives a research design assignment due in April that forces you to do significant work on the paper before reading period and finals, and he's always willing to help students along the way.
