TAKE THIS CLASS! Matt Liebmann is hands down the BEST professor I have had at Harvard. He is so incredibly funny and passionate about archaeology and anthropology. His love for the fields is contagious and makes attending class so exciting. I never felt overwhelmed with the class assignments; they are so evenly spaced out and balanced that they are insanely manageable. On top of that, both the professor and TF are willing to provide feedback on quite literally anything and everything – they are very accessible. And, you learn so much and get so many hands–on experiences both in and outside the museums. For one the sections we even got to throw atlatls. I signed up for this class kind of last minute because I desperately needed a fourth class. It ended up being one of my favorite classes ever taken at Harvard, and I most certainly plan on taking a class with Matt again before I graduate next year!
TAKE THIS CLASS! An upperclassman here, and it was one of my all–time favorites! Professor Liebmann is the coolest. We learned so many fascinating details about history during his class — and I developed an appreciation for archaeology and anthropology because of his teaching. We also did fun stuff like throwing atlatls and visiting the Peabody Museum's archives! The research paper was fun to write and the exams are fair and doable if you pay attention in class and take good notes.

This class is WONDERFUL! If you are curious at all about early American history, first contact history in the American continent, or Indigenous Studies, this class is for you! No experience needed, interesting lectures and readings, amazing professor! Section with our TF, Jess, was also AMAZING but she will not be teaching at Harvard next year. This class is really so great, my one piece of advice is to make sure to take notes!! When exam time comes around you have to study broadly, and there will not be any content notes on the lecture slideshows (only images/data/etc.) This class will not be your most intensive of the semester, but it will be interesting and fun! Highly recommend!
For me, this was a perfect course, and I'm definitely regretting not taking a class with Matt until my senior spring. While the course is not a "gem" in my book (discussions during lecture mean that you are responsible for doing the reading), it is a very manageable workload each week, and I think the assignments are spaced well. Matt is an engaging lecturer and brings many funny stories about his fieldwork & personal experience to the table. Pretty much every week there is some sort of artifact brought out and discussed, and I very much enjoyed the opportunity to go inside the bowels of the Peabody to look at objects in a closer context (an opportunity you don't get in many other contexts). Exams did require studying but were straightforward and fair (and I might even say fun to take). Jess was SO sweet and took the time to connect with us on a more personal level. Definitely a highlight of my senior spring, and of my four years at Harvard.
