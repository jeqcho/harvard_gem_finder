What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS. This is such a good class that by the end you realize you have gained so much! Not only do you learn how to evaluate and critic research papers, you learn how to write effective papers that avoid pitfalls. Reading 3 papers a week is not a lot but it is effective in introducing you to several fields in data visualization. The papers have allowed me to gain much more respect for the field and understand that a lot of theoretical backing is involved in what may appear "intuitive".
This course gives a great overview of the papers in the research field of data visualization.
CS271 is a great class if you are interested in working on a visualization research project. I did not take CS171 but I had research/work experience with visualization, and this was fine for the project. Johanna is an excellent and enthusiastic teacher who was respectful and helpful throughout the process. The workload was manageable, and everyone in the class was super nice, and it seemed like everyone wanted to be there.
Great class if you are interested in reading a lot of data visualization research papers and want to write a short paper of your own. Expect this to be a largely discussion and presentation–based class with little to no lectures from the professor.
This was my favorite CS class I have taken at Harvard. Don't be intimidated by the fact it is a graduate level course, the workload was very manageable. This class is great both for visualization experts and people who don't know what visualization really is, and you see that from the diverse skillsets of people taking the class. Great readings, incredible teaching staff, and a fun project based approach. More CS classes at Harvard should follow this course's example.
I suggest taking this course if it's offered with Johanna. She is very friendly, and the course is structured in such a way that you will learn about the vast field of data visualization within a semester. The readings are great, the research you will do will be fun, and the experience as a whole was a good time!
Highly recommend if you're interested in data visualization or paper discussion.
The course is extremely well–organized and the instructors really want you to succeed. It's a relatively light time commitment on weeks you're not presenting, but you need to be on top of your project. Still, the course is kind of structured to encourage you to stay on track.
This course engages with the frontiers of what it means to see. Visualization is playing a hugely important role in many academic and scientific fields in bringing new horizons of massive datasets, multidimensional data, and dense 3d data, closer to our ability to intuitively parse and understand. This course explores the technical details of visualization research while covering a huge array of topics where visualization research is making massive strides.
