This is a fantastic junior seminar – and definitely something to consider taking if you are interested in writing a thesis. Not only do you gain background into behavioral economics – an emerging field – but you are supported in creating a final paper on a topic that interests you.
This is a fantastic class that spans almost all relevant topics in behavioral economics. Tomasz is a great professor who is easygoing in his lecture style and the class feels like more of a conversation than a high pressure tutorial. Awesome freedom to study what interestes you and develop your own unique final project while not getting bogged down by too much work. A great experience overall.
This class was a great way to learn more about behavioral economics and also economics research. Although the assignments and workload can be a bit tough at times, the grading is very forgiving and the course staff is extremely patient and supportive.
The class is divided into genius pre–PHD economics students and normal economics students that are still considering writing a thesis. The course content is difficult, but the workload is very manageable and there is lots of support on the few assignments that are difficult, so don't be intimidated at all. Highly encourage to people who want to knock out both writing and prereq in an economics elective.
Take this class if you want to learn from a top researcher in behavioral economics who is genuinely one of the nicest people in the department. This class gives you good exposure to economics research and graduate school!
This is a great junior seminar! It offers a good mix of theory and experimental evidence on psychological biases. Be prepared to do some math/statistics, though!
