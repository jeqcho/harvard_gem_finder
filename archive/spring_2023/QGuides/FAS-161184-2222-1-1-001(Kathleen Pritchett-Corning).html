This is one of the best OEB courses you could find. Professor Pritchett–Corning is the best.
This class is a treasure!!! As a humanities student who cannot do math and was freaking out about what I would do senior spring for my science distribution requirement, this class probably is the reason I can graduate. Incredibly low stakes but incredibly engaging. This class was exactly what the distribution requirement should be: no crazy assignments, loads of homework, tests or quizzes, but instead a really engaging change to learn about something way outside your own field of specialty. I will never think about science, research, (or mice!!) the same way again. It was fascinating to make the connection between best practices of animal welfare and the best scientific results: the two go hand in hand.

Dr. Pritchett–Corning is absolutely superb!!!! Kind, thoughtful, funny, wicked smart, empathetic, and really, really knowledgeable and passionate about what she teaches.
Definitely take this course!! It is one of the best courses I've taken on campus because not only are the lectures enjoyable and to the point, but it really makes you feel like you're opening a new world of science. I have such a better attitude towards mice now that I feel like a lot of people don't have the privilege of having. There are no assessments in this class, which actually makes it much easier to learn because you're not constantly bombarded with the pressure of remembering everything that's coming out of Dr. Prittchet–Corning's mouth. Take this course!
