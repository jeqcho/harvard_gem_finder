This class is amazing! The lectures go into more detail on some of the important concepts discussed in Neuro 80, and the problem sets are pretty straightforward. The lab component is definitely worth it (you get to actually perform patch clamping and record action potentials!) The teaching staff is incredibly nice and approachable, and the workload is manageable. So overall, this course is definitely the way to go if you want to fulfill the foundational neuro requirement.
take this class! Ryan is a great teacher and so is Katie. If you do take this class, the psets are really helpful to understand the material and they can be a little confusing but if you go to office hours they provide so much help and you can get the grade you desire. It was a moderate amount of work compared to other biology courses I have taken at Harvard. I wish there was a part 2 of this class because I liked it so much. Definitely take it for your foundational neuro course if you like more micro or quantitative perspectives on science topics.
Definitely take this course!! It was one of my favorite courses of the semester. Katie and Ryan are awesome and great at explaining the material, and you get to learn how to patch–clamp neurons in lab.
This is a great course! It is definitely difficult, but rewarding!
If you are interested in the underlying basis of neuronal function, take this class! It goes pretty in depth on a cellular level, so if your interests are more systems/macroscopic I would say that I would still recommend this class because it gives you a great foundation, but it may not be the best foundational course for you. You have to be willing to review your notes a couple times before the information really solidifies, but I personally think this class is worth it. The course staff is super supportive and lectures are pretty solid.
