What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Interesting course and you will see that literature written a long time ago is very relevant to who we are today.
This is an excellent course to introduce you to early English literature. Having gone in entirely opposed to the Medieval period, I was surprised to find Professor Niles' instruction and engaging nature extremely enjoyable and rewarding. It is extremely helpful to have an Old/Middle English background, but I do not think it would be entirely necessary for most. The only reason I do not recommend with enthusiasm is simply because I still do not find myself enjoying the content or period itself, but I am entirely and extremely satisfied with the functionings of this course as a requirement.
This class provides the perfect introduction to Medieval and Renaissance Literature—it recognizes, then interrogates, the several threads that run through historical texts, and seeks to understand the ways in which writers and readers alike have imagined others, defined variously along colonial, religious, racial, gendered, and class lines, amongst others. Despite the fact that we are reading texts written hundreds of years ago, the class remains engaged with political issues that feel strikingly relevant, and whose effects and afterlives we are still seeing unfold today. This is a wonderful, engaging class—take it!
definitely recommend it if you want to learn about the history of english literature and get some good readings in while youre at it
