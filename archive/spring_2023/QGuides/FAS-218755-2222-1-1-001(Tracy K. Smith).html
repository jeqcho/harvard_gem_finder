take it, it is super interesting and important
TAKE THIS COURSE!!
By far the best way to fill your poets requirement for English––or just a great elective! Professor Smith is so thoughtful and such a good lecturer. Lots of opportunity to do open–ended creative projects. GREAT syllabus. And amazing guest speakers!!
Amazing class – learning from Professor Smith was one of the highlights of all of Harvard. I didn’t write any poetry coming into class, I read a few poems before and was moved but didn’t have a lot of formal tools to analyze poetry. Getting feedback from Professor Smith and Wyatt was also such a blessing, and helped develop my poetry and essay writing skills to the next level. This class taught me how to appreciate and sit with poetry, that poetry isn’t about analysis but about the feelings and effect it invokes on the reader. I have such a deeper appreciation of not just poetry but of words that people say in day to day conversation. Take this class, you won’t regret it.
An amazing experience –– genuinely looked forward to each lecture and section. You'll learn so much about not only the Black lyric but gain a sense of what might become your own lyric.
Do not be intimidated by Professor Smith’s prestige, she is so warm and inviting. She really does want to here everyone’s thoughts and opinions to create the best environment.
This class is truly truly special. Professor Smith is a brilliant and engaging lecturer and her thinking and frameworks for teaching these poems make them feel super accessible and meaningful (for someone who has never studied or written poetry before). The assignments to write imitation poems pushed me out of my comfort zone and I am so grateful for that—it's rare in classes at Harvard that I feel like I'm taking a risk and trying something that new. I am now interested in continuing to read/write poetry and am not intimidated by it anymore. I cannot recommend this class enough.
One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard!
This is a great course with a very light workload! You'll learn a lot without having to devote significant hours each week. Probably best if you've taken at least one other poetry class before –– I hadn't, and felt I didn't have the formal knowledge base to work from.
Take this class, y'all. Tell all your loved ones to take it. Tell everyone you know to take it. It's magic magic. Getting to sit with these Black poets and artists and thinkers and getting to hear Tracy K. Smith's profound wisdom and stunning lectures. You will be grateful forever.
I loved it and strongly encourage it.
Amazingly rich and generous course on Black lyric poetry and art in the US, spanning a wide history of identities and artistic traditions. Professor Smith is a wonderful and necessary addition to the Harvard faculty and is brilliant and caring in lecture and discussion. One of the absolute best courses I took at Harvard, would recommend to anyone at all without reservations. Even if you’re not an English concentrator, this is a great course to take as an introduction to poetry and Black arts movements—and it resists the dominant structures of white, imperial, heteropatriarchal western canon that many other Harvard English courses are pretty tied up in or don’t work as hard to critique.
This is a wonderful course for students who are looking to learn more about the black American experience, poetry, etc. The course pays a lot of attention to close reading, and there are opportunities to try writing poetry as well. The workload is generally light, mostly reading. It's definitely a course where you get out what you put in, and students who are invested in the course content will learn a lot! Prof. Smith is wonderful.
