What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an incredible course! It pushed me out of my comfort zone in terms of reading/writing/discussion but it was a small and supportive environment for engaging with complex histsci issues and was not an intimidating environment. Professor Abi–Rached is passionate, incredibly intelligent, and thoughtful about how to approach the readings and writing/research we did in this course. I really enjoyed the readings and appreciated the chance to pursue research independently as well.
This class was one of the best and most rigorous I've taken at Harvard. You will think critically about each source and read for both information and calibrating your own understanding and interpretation of events in the history of global health. Concentrators and non–concentrators alike should take this course. It is especially great to take before embarking on your thesis!
Please take this class if you are passionate about global health!! I learned more from this class than from all of my global health classes because of all the various perspectives. Joelle is truly the most wonderful professor for deeply caring about students (even when she had her own family emergency) and challenging all of us to go beyond the surface–level analysis of our readings. Her wonderful pedagogy really honed my love for the class material
