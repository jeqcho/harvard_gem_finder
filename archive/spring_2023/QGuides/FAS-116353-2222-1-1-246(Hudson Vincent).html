Comments
Hudson is wonderful in creating an inclusive environment conducive to learning and unlearning about writing and creating a community within your class. As with other EXPOS courses, the class is difficult, but if you put in the work, it will change you as a writer and as a person. Not only is the topic so salient, but also the process of writing your essays will help you learn about what it means to be a good writer and self–reviser.
This class is great! I ranked it as one of my top choices for EXPOS sections, and I was not disappointed. Hudson is an amazing teacher, and I learned a lot through this class. You end up interacting with your classmates a lot through all the class time spent on revising in groups, so I guess this part can be kind of hit or miss, but the other people who take this class are also generally quite knowledgeable, as they also elected to take a class about prison abolition. The grading can be kind of harsh, but that's to be expected of any EXPOS class. I would generally recommend to take an EXPOS class about something you're interested in, and if this topic is interesting to you, I would recommend taking this course.
Rank this expos as your first choice!!! You will not regret it. Hudson is what truly makes this class and is very understanding. He makes expos as bearable as possible but still pushes you to become a better writer. Regarding the work, all of the assignments build up to the major essays and is structured to make the writing process easier. Though, what you put into the course is what you get out. One thing that makes this expos different is the engaged portion built into the course which involves several guest speakers who are directly involved with the issue at hand. All of the speakers were great and I enjoyed listening to the work they've done to help solve the mass incarceration crisis in the country. Overall, this was one of my favorite classes of the year because of the interesting topic and people.
TAKE IT! It's not much work and it's really interesting...but the grading is difficult.
Take this expos. So interesting and meaningful. I never really felt like I was doing work because of how much I enjoyed the readings and writings.
I cannot recommend this class enough to future students. Hudson is a phenomenal teacher and this class was easily the best course I took at Harvard during my first–year. The readings are extremely engaging and changed how I thought about the criminal justice system. The papers are clear and as long as you keep up with the readings we cover in class, you will be amply prepared to write the papers for this class. We also had class visits from leaders of criminal justice organizations and formerly incarcerated individuals that were incredibly eye–opening and a one–of–a–kind experience to have as a first–year. Finally, Hudson provides extremely robust feedback that will genuinely improve your writing skills and he is a very understanding, generous, and fun professor. It was an honor to take his class this semester!
Great course. Definitely take it. Rewarding.
An amazing course to learn from well–founded sources about mass incarceration. It is a very emotionally–charged class, so I would recommend to think carefully if you are sensitive to the topic or not. The lecturer is so passionate about the topic, and he is a very supportive person that will do his best to see you succeed.
The professor and topic of this course are excellent. I cannot recommend this course enough. The workload is reasonable, and the material is genuinely interesting.
TAKE THIS CLASS. This is honestly the best expos. The workload is extremely manageable. There are only two essays and a capstone project. Professor Vincent is extremely passionate about his teaching, and every second that you spend in his class, you are laughing and forming really close connections with your peers. This class will 100% help you reach new heights in terms of writing, and you'll have a blast the entire time you're in it.

Hudson is amazing and so engaging, he really makes you want to improve your writing because he's so passionate about writing and has so much confidence in you. I've talked to many peers about expos and none of them liked theirs as much as I liked this class. I very highly recommend.
