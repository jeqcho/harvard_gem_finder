What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Make sure you think about the research proposal early and ask questions. It could be hard to come up with a question at the end.
In general, I would not recommend this course unless you know you have a strong interest in chromosomes. The class was disorganized, had unclear expectations, and did not really inspire interest in chromosomes for me. There is no one better (in this world) to teach about chromosomes than Prof. Kleckner – however, I personally did not find my learning style to be very compatible with her teaching style. She is blunt and straightforward, which in itself is not what I found off–putting, but I felt that this did contribute to a fear of being wrong or naive in the class, which was hard to deal with as an undergraduate with little exposure to chromosome biology.
Take advantage of the fact that there is a low course load. This means you can focus on reading the paper for the presentations and speak with Nancy more closely one–on–one if you need to.
The class really gives you a lot of freedom to choose your topics and to present papers that are interesting to you. Considering the bulk of the workload is mainly based off of these presentations you give every 1–2 weeks, it is definitely a very reasonable workload.
