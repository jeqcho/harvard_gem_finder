AVOID THIS CLASS AT ALL COSTS. CS51 is by far the most difficult class I've taken at this school.

Problems I had with this class:

1. Lack of structure

This class only has 5 lectures, and none of them were useful. Literally all they talked about was how beautiful programming is. What does that even mean? Like please I just want to pass this class.
Lectures in NO WAY prepared you for assignments or exams. The only time any useful information was given was during code review (section) which is led by TFs, who are literally sophomores. Instead of lectures you would go to lab, where you would be assigned a random partner (this partner changed every week). I do not think this was conducive to learning because a lot of times your partner was just as lost as you so you’re both just sitting there and can’t figure anything out. Then you try to call over a TF and they tell you you’re doing everything wrong. And then you’re like, “How would I have known that?” Guess what. There’s no way you would have known that because the professors don’t lecture.
In addition, the textbook for this class is NOT well written. The majority of the exercises provided in the textbook have no answer key, so you have no way of knowing whether you’re on the right track or not. The textbook is also so theoretical. It is very confusing and hard to read. I would say reading 10 pages of that textbook takes around an hour and a half, if you actually want to understand.
Thirdly, the grading system in this class was very arbitrary. If you completed the assignment exactly how you were asked to, you would get a 90%. The only way to receive 100% was to code so well that you were on the same level as the professors. Let me ask you, how would I get to that level without ANY real lectures from said professors??

2. Impossible exams

There are two exams in this class and I failed both of them. I have never done so bad on an exam before in my life. The sad thing is, when I was taking the exams, I thought I was doing pretty good. However, the way the questions build on each other makes it very easy to get a low score if you mess up the first part of the question. Additionally, some of the exam questions were so vague that I just completely misunderstood what I was being asked to do and as a result received like 0/12 points for that part of the test, even though I was somewhat competent.
They release all previous exams to students as study material, but NONE of the previous exams have answer keys because apparently the professor and TFs used to just grade them as they went WITH NO ANSWER KEY. I’m sorry can you literally do the bare minimum and make an answer key to your own test??? Ridiculous.

3. No support for students

All office hours for this class are overly crowded. Office hours were run from saturday–wednesday, and PSETS were due on Wednesday night. I have major issues with the way CS51 office hours were set up. For starters, we all know the CS department is loaded. Why then, are there only TWO TFs are each office hour session? In LS1b, there are literally 20 TFs staffing one office hour session, and you hardly ever have to wait to ask a question. In CS51 office hours, I had my hand raised on their virtual platform for over an hour before a TF came to address my question, and by that time office hours were about to end. Please hire more TFs so office hours are not so busy. I truly felt bad for the TFs in office hours but at the same time I was so desperate for help that I had to be aggressive and literally beg them to continue answering my questions before moving onto the next person in the office hour line. When we asked course staff to add more office hours earlier in the week so we would have to rush to finish PSETs, they suggested that we simply work on it more individually. If I was capable of working on the pset individually, then yes, this would solve the problem. Unfortunately I was so lost that I actually had no idea how to begin each PSET. Like I’m not a bad student. This should not be happening.

One of the more useful components of this class was the Ed platform that people asked questions on. That being said, the reason it was so useful was that there were two genius students in this class that would just answer everyone else’s questions. While I do love them, that should not be their job.
