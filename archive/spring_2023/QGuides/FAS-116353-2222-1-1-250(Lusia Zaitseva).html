What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is very interesting, but if you're not too politically inclined then you might find the second unit hard to digest. Dr. Z is amazing and she's very lenient with things if you let her know in advance. She's very detailed in her feedback too, which helps a lot when it comes to revising your essays. Discussion–based participation isn't a requirement, which is great for students who are shyer than others, but participation is always encouraged and Dr. Z makes it a welcoming environment.
Be ready for a decently heavy workload
The teacher will put in as much effort as you do.
Attend class and do all of the response papers on time! They will really help you when it comes to writing the graded papers.
The more effort you put into the essays, the better writer you will become. If you complete assignments passively, you will not gain much from the course.
Be prepared for class, so that you get the most out of class time––it was usually the case that my expos section had activities that were based on a reading you had to have done, etc., so make sure you schedule a time to do the assigned readings/exercises.

If you are nervous about the course, don't hesitate to make use of the writing center from day 1, and to ask your preceptor for help––writing center can be really helpful in getting you unstuck if you're having trouble with anything (defs ask for help instead of being stuck by yourself for a long period of time.)
