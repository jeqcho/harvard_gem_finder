Pretty easy, interesting material, fun assignments
Take this course! Super great, accessible intro course to personality psychology that has some very memorable and applicable insights into a number of personally relevant matters. The assignments and overall course structure is also extremely well designed — it feels like just the right amount of work and you get a chance to apply your knowledge in a bunch of different ways, which is very refreshing. Was my first psychology class I’ve taken in college and has made me intent on taking more, even though I’m not concentrating in it
Pretty standard psych elective, in that it was not overly time consuming but very interesting! Add in reasonable (and even fun!) assignments, a caring professor, and neat readings, and overall I would definitely recommend. (though keep in mind it is a pretty general course! in case you're looking for something a little more focused)
I would tell students to take very careful notes in lecture as very obscure questions might appear on exams.
Even if you are not interested in directly studying the psychology of personality, many of the topics/concepts that you learn in this course will be relevant to other psychological areas as well as life in general. While this course had a few hiccups, they were largely growing pains that can be attributed to its status as a new course, and these areas can easily be improved in future iterations.

Its boring, more complicated than it looks, and its one of the worst (if not the worst) classes I have taken at Harvard.
There is nothing good about this course: the content is boring and not well–structured, there is no section component to discuss what you learned in lecture (have fun only having powerpoint slides to study for a very complicated set of "quizzes" which are essentially midterms), and you never meet the person who grades your coursework.
Take your time with the class, and if you need help go to office hours. Make sure you are taking good notes as well.

This class is really interesting. At times the assignments can feel tedious, but all were manageable. Quizzes are a bit tricky, some questions tend to be a bit unclear. The workload was relatively light because of how it was all spaced out, and all assignments tied directly into course material. If you're looking for a fun/creative psych course, I definitely recommend this one! Also, Professor Jones provides reviews, office hours, recordings of assignment explanations, etc., so you always have what you need to do well.
