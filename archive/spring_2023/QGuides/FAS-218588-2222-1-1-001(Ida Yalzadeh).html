What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course changed the way I approach intersectionality and how I understand interlocking systems of oppression that maintain the world we live in today. The overall concepts that the course introduced me to are extremely salient to my conception of the modern world and how we can situate current events/occurrences in it.
Take this class!!! You will not regret it! It is such a laid–back and SUPER relevant class that will shape or re–shape your perception of how capitalism, racialization and the US empire come together and its effect on marginalized people within and outside the nation. The class discussions are amazing and the classroom space is very safe and open (also depends on your classmates). Prof Yalzadeh is such a sweetheart you will love her! Don't miss out
I would highly recommend this class to any student interested in Arab American and/or Asian American studies, or in ethnic studies more broadly. This class will be manageable and beneficial to student of all concentrations and grade levels, so long as they are committed to participating in seminar and completing the readings, which are very fun and interesting!
If you have the opportunity to do so, please take this class! I learned so much over the course of every week, and it's honestly what's inspiring me to go into academia or scholarship. I learned so much not only about the history of the Asian and Arab American communities but also about reading academic works and journals. Professor Yalzadeh also is compassionate and understanding unlike anyone else.
TAKE THIS! This is the best course I have taken at Harvard. Professor Yalzadeh is an incredible lecturer and also just the most incredible person ever. You will learn so much from this course, I promise you.
