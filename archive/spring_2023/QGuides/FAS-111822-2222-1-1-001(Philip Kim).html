This course, while in my opinion very hard, gives a really in–depth over–view of EM. The homework takes a long time to fully complete, but the lectures were really good, and Professor Kim and Henry were nice professors that would be willing to talk outside of class hours to help be more familiar with the content of the course. Psets are especially important to work on with other people, or in office hours, please please please don't be afraid to ask for help in this class!

probably best physics class I've taken so far in terms of teaching staff
This is a more in–depth dive into electromagnetism than 15B, so I would recommend it strongly for Physics concentrators. Professor Kim is an amazing lecturer and the example he presents are very instructive. Overall, great class with fair workload.
This is a rather challenging class, but much better than 15b. It gives you an appreciation for what EM can really be beyond the simple examples in 15b for example. You see the math, and from that the theory takes shape, which is nice. It will take some time to get used to vector calculus, but by the first midterm it is fine.
Overall, I really enjoyed this course, and I would definitely recommend the course in general and especially if you like math computations. Professor Kim and the teaching fellow Henry are both incredibly helpful individuals and will take the time to answer your questions. The class follows the content within the Griffiths textbook closely, but the PSETS are challenging, worthwhile, (and pretty fun). Exams are tough, so study psets and lecture examples carefully. The course material might seem to go fast at times (especially towards the end of the semester), but it can always be understood with the help of the textbook. Office hours are extremely helpful and worth your time. At least try the course out, I think you’ll like it! :)
Professor Kim goes through many examples during class. I would recommend studying those examples and putting them on your cheat sheet when studying for exams. Also the final exam was very similar to the final review that Professor Kim did.
