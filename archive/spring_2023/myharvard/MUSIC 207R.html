Description:

A Theory Toolkit: Key Concepts in Sociocultural Theory. This seminar provides a focused, critical introduction to selected concepts and theories from across the humanities and social sciences.  The study of expressive behavior has become steadily more interdisciplinary over the decades, but the typical graduate curriculum has not kept pace.  Students are faced with an ever-expanding vocabulary drawn from political economy, literary theory, cultural studies, science studies, material culture studies, the environmental humanities and others without being able to explore each of those fields in depth.  This course is an attempt to remedy the situation by equipping the student with a basic conceptual toolkit for interdisciplinary study.

We will start with certain fundamental concepts (including ‘culture’; essentialism; the semiotic sign; agency/structure; power/hegemony/governmentality/resistance; habitus/embodiment), and will explore their applications and extensions in current debates (over “strategic essentialism”; structural/systemic racism; intersectionality, decoloniality, et al.).  These topics, though relevant to the study of the performing arts, are also broadly useful for scholars of the humanities in general, as well as the social and behavioral sciences.

The course’s modular design will allow us to follow student interests, adding concepts directly relevant to student projects (such as affect, affordance, epistemic violence, genealogy, ontology, performativity, or wherever else the class prefers to go).  In every case, we’ll adopt a dialectical approach, bringing diverse and/or conflicting perspectives into dialogue.
