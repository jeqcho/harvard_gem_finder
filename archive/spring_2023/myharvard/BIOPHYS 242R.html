Description:

Biophysical topics emerging from special interest research not normally available in established curriculum. The 2022-23 year's course (Spring 2023) is focused across 4 topic blocks:  Making Sense of High-Dimensional Data; Structural Biology and How it Relates to Function at the Molecular Level; Single-Molecule Biophysics; and Special Topics in Neuroscience.

Course Notes:

Lectures, Problem Set, Research Papers, proposal writing, and potential lab components.

Class Notes:

Focuses on topics in four areas of Biophysics emerging from research in faculty laboratories, in each professor's area of special interest; topics which would not normally be available in the established curriculum.  Each Biophysics graduate student must take this course at least once during the early part of their time as a student in the program.  The Spring 2023 year’s topics are: Making Sense of High-Dimensional Data; Structure and its Relationship to Function- Seen Through an Atomic Lens; Single-Molecule Biophysics; and Special Topics in Neuroscience.

Class Meets on the HMS Campus: Building C, Folin Wu Room (Bldg. C, Room 137), 240 Longwood Avenue, Boston.
