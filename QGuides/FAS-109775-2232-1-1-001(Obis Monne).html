This class is really good! Take it if you want to know more about the city as well as the historical, social, and cultural influences over time.
This class is a great way to get a background on Catalan culture and Spanish history in general. The course focused more than I was expecting on literature and art, but it was very interesting and I really enjoyed it. Aina is the best– she is so encouraging and enthusiastic, so she really makes the classroom feel like a welcoming, comfortable space. I took Spanish 40 before taking this course and feel like I was adequately prepared.
This class was great— Aina is super nice and welcoming. I will say that the readings can get a bit dry and long sometimes, but generally the class is not too stressful and you’ll enjoy discussion!
This is a great course! Aina is a great teacher, and you learn so many interesting things about Catalonia and Barcelona. In this course, you really get out what you put in; the more time you spend on the material before class, the more you will learn and get out of class. I would highly recommend to anyone looking for advanced classes for a citation or secondary.
As an advanced–level language class, course readings are challenging but manageable, lectures/discussions are stimulating and low–stress, and it's a great way to practice Spanish without the rote memorization/grammar/vocab structure of easier Spanish courses. This course was not very challenging but always interesting. I had no knowledge of Catalan, the history of Barcelona, or its relationship to Spain, and I feel that I've come away with a really thorough understanding of architecture, art, moments of decline, moments of triumph, and literature of Cataluña. I also didn't have much interest in Barcelona (I took the course to fulfill a requirement), but now I do!Prof. Monne makes the most of Harvard's resources: our visits to the Fogg art museums to study Miró and our visit from catalan experts (the MIT professor and the poet laureate who spoke on sexual meanings of language) were highlights of the semester. It was so much fun to study history and culture through multiple different lenses: art, architecture, poetry, politics, etc. Discussions got really deep and stimulating, it never felt like we were just summarizing content we read for class. I can't wait to visit Barcelona and see what I've learned in person. I strongly recommend this course.
