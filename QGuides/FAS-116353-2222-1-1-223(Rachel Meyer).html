Overall a decent Expos section. Expect long and not particularly relevant discussions as well as front loaded reading, but if you do the reading and write the drafts it really is not that bad. Don't overthink it. Rachel Meyer is a good teacher and seems to care about her students, even if she's a little annoying in class sometimes. If you want to learn a bit about the workforce environment while taking Expos, this is a good course for you.
I was planning on switching out of this class when I was placed into it but I stayed in the class because I thought professor Meyer was funny. I regret staying in her class. some things you should know about this course, that I didn't know before, are:
– the readings are very long compared to other expos classes (120+ pages for each unit) and the readings are pretty dull and boring for the most part
– the timelines are really weird. some units are suuper fast and the last one is super long.
– the course is not as hard or as transformative as she says it is (I think she said that to freak us out)
– she doesnt take feedback well during the course
– the course assumed that the work force looks the same for all workers when we were actually just studying what the workforce looks like for white men. As a women of color, it was really hard to stay calm about this.
– unlike other classes, Professor Meyer refused to give examples of theses, counter arguments... etc. this makes essay writing really hard.
It is worth taking this class! While it is true that expos might not be the most exciting course you will take at Harvard, Dr. Meyer is a great preceptor and I have always found her classes enjoyable. Although I mostly took the class because I tried to avoid having to read literature (I did not have an interest in labor), the sociology we discussed was very interesting and widely applicable to anybody so I am very glad I got this section.
While there are only studies that you have to read, you still need to go through about 60 pages every unit which might be more than what other sections ask. The class is quite fast paced (we might have a revision due when other sections were just beginning their drafts) but this also meant that we had plenty of time before each revision and, in particular, before unit 3 revision. Dr. Meyer has been teaching this class for a long time which means that she knows very well when students tend to start their essays so she therefore always has office hours a few days before the deadline. Moreover, she knows what mistakes students tend to make in every unit so she is able to put more emphasis on those topics. Finally, she grades fairly and is a great resource for any sort of advice.
Highly recommend this section!
Dr. Meyer really makes you a better writer if you put in the work. This is my first sociology class, but the readings gave a very substantive background on the topics and the topics discussed in class were super interesting. She really knows students' timelines and concerns for each unit's assignment, and she also has a very firm pedagogical approach. The class flow and environment really don't impede your learning at all, so you get what you put in. Her comments can be very harsh if you don't put much thought into your argument, but if you can overcome that, you'll emerge a much better writer.
Be prepared to always have a deadline coming, this course moves super fast. I enjoyed the content, some people say it sounds boring but it actually isn't and it's super relative.
I loved this course! TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Rachel is the best and she will guide you through nailing down things like topic sentences, thesis, and question formation, and she will teach you a lot about sociology. She is also very understanding and enthusiastic! Professor Rachel knows a lot about sociology and her field, it was really awesome learning from her. Also, there are only 3 units, each one gets harder as the semester goes on. Read the sources right away do not wait until the last minute work on your drafts and put a lot of effort into them because it will help prepare you for the final paper!

This is a solid Expos (not a Wastelands–type expos, but not Shakespeare either). It was intellectually stimulating and my writing definitely improved. The readings are reasonable in length. As a grader, Rachel leans strict but is fair. Would recommend if you are tired of analyzing novels in high school and are interested in the social sciences. Put in the work on the drafts so that you receive helpful feedback from Rachel. My class dynamic was a bit awkward (quite serious all the time, discussion didn't always flow), but we bonded over the dread of writing.
This class was pretty difficult, and I would urge students who are not the greatest of writers to think of taking another class. I would say that the readings are long and overwhelming and happen once in the beginning of units. Dr. Meyer is a great professor who is easy to approach and talk to. I think that sometimes though she goes off on tangents and you don't really get much done in class as she is speaking most of the time. I would say she grades relatively fairly maybe slightly on the harsher scale, but you genuinely do improve as a writer. The topic is somewhat interesting especially looking at worker accounts of things, but the whole sociology aspect behind it was a little too much, and I think most students who are just trying to get expos done aren't trying to open that can of worms. I would just say to be very wary of what you're getting into as this class is no joke.
