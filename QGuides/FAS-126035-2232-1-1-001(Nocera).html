Please take Chem 154. The exams are fair, the psets are a little hard, but the material is very interesting and you'll expand on the things learned in Chem 40. However, maybe more importantly, Dan is an awesome professor and scientist and I think everyone should meet and learn things from him. His stories are incredible and his accomplishments in Chemistry are very inspiring. Plus, I think this has been the funniest and most enjoyable 9am so far (you will be able to explain the symptoms of being a vampire using Chem 154!). I never missed a class.
Most of the lectures were confusing but the notes were informative.
Very good course. Interesting material. It's just that the lecture style might be high level and requires extra efforts to understand
Take this if you can. Dan is amazing
This is a great sequel to Chem 40. You learn more about the physics/math behind inorganic chemistry (basically why you do the things you did in Chem 40), but you aren't tested on this. Some of the psets were very challenging, so go to office hours! Dan's lectures are super fun and made taking a 9am chemistry class totally worth it.
I highly recommend taking this course! It is extremely useful and relevant for anyone interested in chemistry, especially inorganic. Professor Nocera is a brilliant lecturer and does a great job introducing concepts and answering student questions, which he seems to take into account when preparing lectures. This has been one of the most compelling courses I have taken at Harvard, and I feel like I now have a much more in–depth understanding of fundamental concepts I encounter regularly in chemistry. This course is challenging but manageable, and Professor Nocera and Sam did a great job supporting students throughout. I would certainly recommend this course to future students!
Take this course!! Dan is a phenomenal lecturer and you really get a deep and quantitative dive into inorganic concepts. Though quantum mechanics and some linear algebra is used to explain concepts like SALCs / MO theory, the rigorous math won't show up on exams (which are very straightforward and fair) so don't worry if that's less of your speed!
