What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course gave me a much better fundamental understanding of calculus and just more intuition of how specific mathematic concepts worked in general. Although its a lot of work, it definitely is worth the effort to gain such a better fundamental understanding of how to even approach simplifying and finding better numerical approaches to approximate the answers to different math concepts.
If Sumit is teaching this class, consider taking it! He is a very passionate postdoc who made the classroom fun and inspired me personally.
This class satisfies your AM Master's requirement –– the course content is not super interesting if you're not into physical mathematics, but it's not too difficult and the course materials are fair.
See if Brenner is teaching it. After watching one of his lecture videos, I could see why this class is required for the AM masters and appreciated what we were doing a lot more. It's honestly really cool math, you'll learn how to make approximations and make progress at solving impossible equations. You'll also learn why the approximations can be useful rather than just blindly following numerics.
Light coursework (4 psets, take home midterm that is pretty easy if you understand the psets, and final project). Sumit is hilarious and does a good job explaining hairy theoretical concepts. The theoretical part of the class feels a bit irrelevant (why do you need to know how to approximately solve x when we already have scipy / wolfram alpha / etc.) but the psets are mostly implementation of said numerical methods. 9am lecture kind of sucks but it is what it is.
This class is great, and it has changed the way of how I look at math. However, it is very hard to understand (I am a sophomore so that might be why, most people in the class are Phds or seniors/juniors at least). But Sumit and Davide were very patient and answer all of our quetions.
This is useful if you're planning on future research in any math–adjacent STEM field, just be prepared for a relatively uneven distribution of work: it ramps up hard towards the end of the semester.
Take this class. You won't regret it. Course head is amazing and so is the content. You will learn so much not just about math, but about the philosophy of problem solving. Great class.
While this class is doable without the pre–recs, they really do serve to motivate the material being studied. This is a true applied math class in the sense that it combines analytical approximations with numerical solutions fully, so if you genuinely like applied math this course is for you. If you don't enjoy engaging with equations alone (e.g. without a word problem) then I'd be hesitant about this course. Overall, problem sets are very manageable so that shouldn't be a concern.
