TAKE THIS CLASS. It completely changed how I view happiness and deeply improved how I choose to prioritize it. Literally life changing – I recommend taking it senior spring so you can take its lessons away with you as you enter life after college.
Take this class! And take it pass fail (if you can).

The material in this class is arguably the most important that you will ever come across in college and maybe even in life. The class is genuinely about learning how to be happier, and not in a preachy or self–help type of way. The homework assignments are practicums were you implement new interventions in your life each week and write about whether or not it made you happier. The sections are very chill and you get to debrief the practicums / learn about other people's experiences. This is helpful because you get to figure out what works for you and what you can implement in your day–to–day life.

The lectures are also super interesting. I have a really difficult time paying attention in my lectures, but this was one class where for the whole 75 minutes, I was engaged because the material is so important and interesting. You may not agree with everything Prof. Mitchell says, but it gets you thinking. Lectures are not recorded and the slides get posted but they're not super detailed, so you have to pay attention in this class and take good notes.

Also, take this class pass fail! That's a huge part of the reason the class was not stressful / very enjoyable for me. The exams are easy to study for if you've taken good lecture notes. You do not need to do the readings (unless you're taking the class for a grade and are aiming for an A). The final exam is not cumulative and focuses on material in the second half of the course. The final paper is also fun. You get to basically create an intervention that you think will make you happier and run an experiment on yourself.

Absolutely take this class before you graduate, especially if you're a senior. It makes you think hard about your priorities after graduation and how you want to live your life.
