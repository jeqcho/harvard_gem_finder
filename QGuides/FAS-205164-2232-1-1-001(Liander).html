This is an amazing class, perfect for anyone looking to improve their Spanish (if they are already near fluent). The readings can be a bit long at times, and there are several spontaneous projects, but overall an amazing class. **
This is a great class if you want to learn more about Spanish cuisine and culture. It's definitely not a class that goes over grammar and the more rote aspects of language, but you do learn a lot of vocabulary. Some of the readings may be challenging to understand if you do not have an advanced level of Spanish, but it just might mean that you have to be more careful to understand when reading. If anything, Profesora Liander will typically go over the readings in class (although she will still expect you to share what you understood from it). The cooking sessions are also fun, although note that sometimes you might miss lunch from the dhalls (assuming that the course continues to be offered fro 12 –2), and the dishes you make are not necessarily going to fill you enough for lunch. The course is challenging in a number of ways, but it is also very engaging and doable so give it some consideration.

*This class is great! I had such a good time taking it and honestly learned so much. If you really enjoy cooking, youll like this class a lot!
In terms of speaking/communication, you should be comfortably proficient–– I took Spanish 50 last semester and did fine here. Overall, 81 is a great concept, and a lot of fun at times, but not quite as good as it looks on paper. You'll sometimes get hit with a sneakily hefty assignment that wasn't on the syllabus a few days before class... good example was a 5–minute presentation assigned on the Thursday before having class the following Tuesday, in addition to already–assigned reading. The teaching team is also pretty hot and cold... either very buddy–buddy or kind of confrontational / weirdly on your back if you mess something up. In keeping with the not–on–the–syllabus assignments, it's also just generally disorganized–– random assignments might come via email the day before, and you won't have any grades in Canvas the whole semester. However, the cooking component is almost too good to pass up (6 cooking classes throughout the semester–– you'll make great Spanish food), the material is interesting on net, and it's not a huge amount of work. So, overall, if you don't mind logistical inconveniences and some wacky moments here and there, it's a solid course.
