What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Tough course if you do not know much about medieval times, but Professor Hamburger is one of the smartest people alive. It is a great pleasure to work with him.
This course is incredibly interesting and can change your perspective on Medieval manuscripts. I came into the class not really sure if I had any deep interest in religious manuscripts and their illuminations. But throughout the course, Prof Hamburger really engages everyone in class discussion and the material can be applied to so many topics that are important in writing, history, religion, and art. There is a fair amount of reading in the class and it can be a bit challenging at times, but you don't discuss the readings too often during class. The Houghton Library visits are incredible ways to engage with the material and are also a lot of fun. Overall, a great course!
I would tell them to take this class if they want time to develop their own voice as a scholar. Professor Hamburger is a brilliant educator and mentor, but perhaps the best thing about his courses is how strongly he prioritizes the creative process of writing seminar papers. One always has the sense that he is playing a much longer game than just the course itself––rather that he is using the course as an armature for supporting the emergence of scholarly work in a way that is narrowly tailored to both the student and to the field.
This class will change how you think about manuscripts! You also get to view actual medieval manuscripts in Houghton, which is a treat and something everyone should do during their time at Harvard if they get the chance. The fieldtrips to view the collections are a highlight. Professor Hamburger is also an incredible instructor who offers his keen insight on manuscripts and encourages us to put forth our own hypotheses on what they mean. The research paper will allow you to pursue your own interests within the class, and students come from a wide range of disciplines (sciences, humanities, etc.) and interests.
This one is not to be missed!
The readings are helpful. However, the illustrations and PowerPoints are more important.
