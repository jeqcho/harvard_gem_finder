It was an amazing course to take to gain a much deeper understanding of how intertwined nature and nurture truly are. The coursework includes reading a paper (and an optional review if helpful) to discuss in section, submitting your questions for review, and a final mock grant proposal. The course is incredibly manageable while also being very enriching. I definitely recommend this course as your upper–level neuroscience requirement or even as an elective.
The workload for this class is super manageable and allows you to really benefit and learn from the amazing lectures you'll attend and papers you'll read. Interesting content and minimal workload. This is a great advanced neuroscience course with more specialized content and in a much less stressful environment. The course is set up to actually learn and care about the course content instead of just completing assignments and getting a grade. The weekly articles that are assigned are modern applications of the concepts learned in lecture, which is super interesting to learn more about neuroscience research. The guest lectures are awesome as well because you get to hear from amazing neuroscientists and their revolutionary work.
If you like reading papers and discussing neuro–related topics in general, take this class. The discussion posts are relatively low–stress and definitely help with developing discussion topics for section. You also have an opportunity to meet and discuss with all the TFs, they're all super friendly and helpful!
** This is a great course if you're looking to fulfill your advanced neuroscience requirement! You meet twice a week: once for lecture and once for section. The section component of this course is the best part of the class; it's set up so you review a new paper each week, which allows you to engage not only with the study's findings but also it's methodology, motivations, and missed opportunities. You'll learn a lot from Takao and the teaching team, not only about critical periods and early life brain development but also about how to write and design a study.
This course does not require a lot of outside–of–class work but expects a high quality of work. Weekly we are only expected to read one scientific paper and post 2–3 questions on it, but the questions are expected to demonstrate critical thinking on the paper, so I would take those seriously and not rush through them. The most stressful part of the class is the required section presentation that is about an hour long and I would recommend preparing for that a week or a few days in advance because it is a lot of work and is the only big assignment before the final (if you've taken Neuro/MCB 125 it is similar to that but is a solo presentation). It's nice that the final is only a two–page single spaced creative grant proposal, but again, the standards for quality of work are high. You'll spend most of your time stressing about revisions instead of stressing about finishing the paper. Also, the grading is super unclear, so I think I'm doing really well, but I'm not sure. I hope the make the grading more clear for you guys though!
