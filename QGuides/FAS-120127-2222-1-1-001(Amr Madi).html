The class is challenging, but if you are willing to work with the Professors and understand your mistakes, you'll be able to do well. The most difficulty comes from the 4 x weekly class meetings because you have a significant amount of daily homework to complete and it is probably pretty time–consuming.
It's a lot of work but it is incredibly rewarding! I would only take this course if you are trying to get a language citation and/or get closer to achieving fluency in Arabic because only a lukewarm interest in the language will not carry you through this class.
This class is amazing! The preceptor takes time to provide feedback individually on every assignment and ensures that you are both comfortable and improving in your language skills. It is incredible to see your skills grow throughout the semester! I can't say enough how great the teaching staff are in this course, learning a language can be intimidating and difficult but they ensure that you are supported and able to improve in the language every step of the way.
Put in the work and stop complaining. Language courses take consistent, daily effort. If you're not willing to accept that, don't bother.
But if you ARE –– you will find this course tremendously rewarding. You will feel your fluency improve week by week, you'll gain exposure to the Arab world with material from the Arab world, and most importantly, you will get to learn from a truly class A language teacher who is deeply passionate about his students and his work –– and who makes you want to keep going even after a rough day in the classroom. :)
This class is a huge time commitment, but is VERY worth it if you are invested in learning Arabic.
You should definitely take this course! I had been off cycle in the Arabic department when I entered and definitely felt that Professor Madi was exceptionally good at addressing my level of Arabic specifically relative to the course. Moreover, he is an exceptionally welcoming and attentive professor; of all the courses that I took to complete my citation, this one was *truly* the most rewarding and fun! If you're debating continuing because you're not sure of your experience with the first two years of Arabic using Kitaab al–Kitaab, this course –– especially with Professor Madi heading it –– is a refreshing departure that really will boost your confidence and ability tremendously.
Take this class! It is incredibly challenging but you will learn SO much in a year. The instructors (Amr and Hany) are incredibly supportive and challenge you just enough to make you feel that the class is worthwhile but not too much that it's unmanageable. You will need to have an extensive background in formal Arabic, but you may speak dialectical Arabic if you wish.
