This course is a must–take. It exposes the hidden world of toxicants and forever chemicals–––topics so ubiquitous that the class will change the way you perceive the world. Elsie (the wonderful prof.) imbues you with the understanding and tools to analyze and understand the environmental world around you, as well as a deep passion for making regulatory change. More generally, too, the teaching staff are very responsive to suggestions and were willing to alter assignments when they were too burdensome; and their excitement for the subject is infectious.
Take this class if you are interested in contaminants to our environment. You will receive a wide–ranging perspective on the environment and our relationship with it. This course does tend to focus more on mathematically determining where chemicals are located and setting appropriate levels of risk rather than chemistry or biology, but even if the latter pique your interest more than the former the course probably will still be rewarding.
I highly recommend the course based on the teaching staff and the critical thinking skills you acquire from the course content. There were also really interesting speakers.
Some of the most important and applicable material in the ESE department if you plan to pursue a career in environmental science or engineering.
Great survey class for intro toxicology. Elsie is at the top of her field, but the class was a bit disorganized.
I really liked this class. It blew my mind to learn about just how common and widespread chemical pollution is and how little is done to prevent it. The knowledge is really valuable. If you are even remotely interested in environmental science/engineering/protection/conservation it is worth taking the class (you do not need to be in the major to take this class). The assignments were so reasonable and such a low–stress course in SEAS. Kind of a gem in my opinion.
This class is an interesting introduction to the field of toxicology with professor Sunderland, an expert in both science and policy. The class covers a lot, which contributes to it feeling somewhat disorganized.
easy and really interesting and informative class, if you are interested in this topic, you have to take it
This class was ok. I expected there to be more about the mechanisms of toxicology, but it was more about the physical dynamics of toxins in the environment. A lot of the assignments seemed disorganized. Many times we were given exercises to complete without being fully taught how to complete the exercises – they were just graded on effort, but I think it would have been more effective to be taught beforehand rather than try to figure it out ourselves. Additionally, the material covered on assignments and exams did not always accurately reflect the time spent on those topics in class. That being said, grading was always fair to generous. Elsie and the TFs were helpful and available outside of class.

Everyone should take this class. I learned so much about the chemicals in the environment and in products that will weigh on decisions I make everyday. It scares me how little I knew about toxicology and the fact that there may be others in my same boat that are making policy decisions that effect so many people.
It is an interesting course!
