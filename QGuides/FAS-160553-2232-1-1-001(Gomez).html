What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Since a thesis is a very self led endeavor, this course is more of how much you make of the material than how well it's is as a class itself. Some things that were a plus was the project based sections, those were definitely helpful and a highlight for many students. Some of the lectures that taught core concepts like defining technical specifications, understanding testing and analysis, as well as technical writing were all very helpful. I kind of wish we had these topics covered much earlier, like almost during sophomore forum, or ES 96. It also would have been much more helpful if more skill based trainings from the active learning labs were available, such as pcb making, machining for cnc, solidworks, comsol, etc instead of some of the lectures, and these workshops would have been very useful for people who do not have these skills, or need a refresher to use on their own senior thesis. Overall though, the section leaders are incredible, Ben and Elaine, thank you for making this such an incredible year, our thesis would not have been as fun and as much of a learning experience without you all. All of the active learning lab staff are incredible and should be given so much more credit for the time they spend as section leaders and as support for all the es 100 projects.
ES100 is actually manageable if you set deadlines for yourself every week and try to stay at least 1 week ahead of the course deadlines. I think that I learned more in this class than I did in any of my engineering classes since I was the only one working on the project and was forced to devise solutions. It was really hands on and helped me apply what I had learned in my other classes to the development of the project.
Good luck and take advantage of the resources you have with ALL and ES 100 staff. It's a rough but rewarding experience.
Seriously try to think about defining your project during junior year. So many people waste the first third of the course trying to refine their problem statement. It seems silly, but just try to start the course with a solid problem statement and stakeholders in mind and you'll be way ahead of the curve.
Try to be on top of deadlines
This class is really what you make of it. Nobody really wants to write a thesis and its certainly a grind, but if you start working on your project build as soon as possible (IN THE FALL) you shouldn't be in a bad position over the spring. If you leave it all for the spring, you most likely won't have a fun spring break.
Start at a good pace then keep up with it, it will pay off in the end
Choose a project you actually want to work on actively.
This class is a requirement for SB concentrators, so there is little choice in the matter of taking ES100. The best advice I can give is to figure out your project during the preceding summer and familiarize yourself with the project's background and motivation. There will be crises of faith and moments of abject frustration with the ES100 course structure, but overcoming them will bring a sense of immense achievement come April. Best of luck!
