REVIEW:

As an FGLI student, the social problems discussed in class were extremely interesting to me. I want to say that the lectures consisted of findings that Prof. Chetty and other researchers have found. During lectures, we would learn of the design of a specific experiment. The lab that week would then make us practice using that concept with an example. You will be working with actual US data and you will be able to analyze the inequality you see in a region of interest to you.

This class, I believe, is the best introduction to the economic research side of economics. There is almost no discussion of interest rates or monetary policy. The topics discussed are thus more human and down to earth. You will understand the effect of neighborhoods, schools, teachers on kids' income. You will learn how to analyze the effect of class size on kids. How do you assess the risk of patient? When creating a program, how do you analyze the benefits and drawbacks of your method? How can you tell if your policy succeeded?

No background necessary. However, as a CS grad, I found the class very easy. For those with technical skills: realize that this class is more about the content than the technical skills. This course will not teach all of R or stata and you will only get a brief overview of many subjects. This is great for an intro survey class (the aim of the class). But, if you want depth, then this is not for you.


In terms of assingments, every lab is easy. In the beginning, when you are learning your language of choice, the labs will take upwards of 5 hours. Gradually, it will decrease as you get more and more accustomed to the language. After spring break, there is a little bit of python that is introduced and it is basically you going through each line of a preprepared python document, trying to understand what is going on. If you don't care, you can literally run the code with minor changes and your lab is done. There are two significant assignments. The empirical projects (part 1 and 2) take a while and since the class is generally very laid back, this assignment can catch you offguard. If you look at my time spent, you will notice two extreme increases in time spent. These are the weeks that the project was due (parts 1 and 2 respectively). Also looking at the data, notice that the empirical project in total took around the same time as the total OF ALL LABS COMBINED. Do not delay emp. project like me and do it as soon as possible.

Midterm was easy. Quoting Dr. Bruich: "The mean was 24.4 out of 30 with some students scoring 30/30. The standard deviation was 3.2. The median was 25 out of 30. There were 63% of the scores within 1 standard deviation of the mean. There were 97% within 2 standard deviations of the mean." Hope this helps.


Ultimately, if you are interested in social equity, education policy, health policy, and solving social problems with data, this is for you. For humanities oriented students: this is also a great way to dip your toe in a semi quantitative class without it overwhelming you.
