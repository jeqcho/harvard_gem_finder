This class is a lot of work, every week. There are no exams or big assignments, but you have to do a lot of work every week. However, I have never attended another class that lit up my brain every time I attended lecture. Krzysztof is truly amazing at what he does and the weekly assignments expose you at the independent group design process. Be ready to take risks, lead with no authority and spend a lot of time thinking about design outside of class, including running user interviews with friends or strangers. But, but but... if taken seriously, this class is truly superb: I am now considering a career change because of this amazing class and staff.
This class is more work than it seems (not super technically difficult, but a lot of time goes into the projects). The section that you are in makes a HUGE difference since those are the people you are working with and the TF that you interact with the most. I don't think I learned that much about product design in detail, but it was a good introduction to design concepts and considerations.
If they're still requiring 3 projects and randomizing groups, just skip this class. There's not enough time between milestones and no support at the start of the semester. If you get stuck in a bad group, then too bad. You're honestly better off taking an online course in your free time.
