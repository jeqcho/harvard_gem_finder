This class is worth taking as you get a lot of more nuanced historical, sociological perspectives on the way our economic system came to be. The cases are extremely relevant in understanding some of today's biggest global challenges (i.e. climate change in Bangladesh, communism in China, etc.).
If you are interested in history and economics, this course is great for you. Professor Reinert is extremely engaging and the class is really a one–of–a–kind opportunity for freshmen.
Great topic with a great professor!
I genuinely grew as a critical thinker through this course... Professor Reinert is a fantastic lecturer and this class has been the epitome of a Harvard course, in all the best ways.
Great seminar! Case studies are interesting, and Prof does a great job in facilitating conversation and posing interesting questions. A little econ (and econ bro) heavy. Professor is pretty busy person too.
This is the best class I have take at Harvard so far. It is one of the few opportunities you will get to engage with HBS faculty, materials, and learning styles as an undergraduate, and the community you build with your classmates in the seminar format is unmatched. If you are interested in learning how our world works, this is a great perspective to engage with.
This is one of the most thought provoking classes you will take. It will challenge you to see sensitive issues from many perspectives –– including those that may seem ridiculous compared to your own. You will also be required to reanalyze your views on economics, wealth, inequality, and fairness. All the case studies are interesting and enable you to see a different face of capitalism.
If this taught by Prof Reinert, this is a must take. Easily the most engaging class I took all year, not only is the workload very manageable, but the content gets you thinking about the state/history of the world in a whole new way. Great, great class, high praise for Prof Reinert.
Absolutely take this seminar if you are interested in the topic! The professor is wonderful. He really made this course special. Everyone I talked to enjoyed this class and was sad it was over. The workload is minimal (reading the cases and a flexible written response) and yet I got so much out of this course.
