What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I wanted to take a Hist–Lit seminar and this one looked really interesting. The professors are wonderful, accessible, and choose materials that were very helpful in understanding the topic of the week. My biggest issue with this course is that it is incredibly one–sided. Every class felt like an echo chamber and it seemed like everyone in the class had the same opinion. This is not to the fault of the professors, but there was very little engaging discussion. There was so much unanimous opinion that it felt almost like an unsafe environment for people to share varying opinions. I'm not sure how to change this in the future, but I think it would have been a more intellectually stimulating class if there were varying opinions represented in the class.
I would tell students that this class is incredibly engaging and requires a lot of thinking (both within group discussions and outside the classroom). I would also say that the class appeals to many people's interests as it covers many different topics. I think this class and the discussions we had in the class were particularly meaningful because of our class's willingness to engage and contribute to the group discussion. I would also say one really nice thing about this class is that the requirements are very clear and the class structure feels very intuitive based on the discussions and materials assigned.
Take the class! The sources you will read will introduce you to perspectives you might've never taken the time to consider before, and you will learn a lot regardless. The professors are absolutely amazing and foster great discussion as well.
Take this course!!! It is an incredible class with incredible professors who are easy to talk to, kind, and fascinating. If you are all interested in the topic, you will love this class.
This was an incredible course! The professors are both awesome and facilitate great discussions about the material. The readings for each week can be long, but are all super interesting and are almost entirely covered during class. I have rarely been in such an engaged discussion as I was every single week with this class, talking about some of the most prominent social/cultural issues in the US.
This class is fantastic –– Lauren and Steve are wonderful instructors.
TAKE THIS CLASS. This was easily my favorite course of the semester. Lauren and Steve are amazing professors who always know exactly what to say to keep the conversation flowing. They are also super kind and understanding, and created such a stress–free environment without compromising high–quality discussion. I had never had an opportunity to learn about the 80s and 90s, so this was great, in addition to the fact that the culture wars are still currently raging around us and learning about their origins was both upsetting and fascinating. We read amazing books, watched amazing movies, and had amazing discussions, every single week without fail. Imagine 2 hours of riveting discussion with no dead space or awkward pauses. This fantasy can be yours if you take this class! Perhaps part of that was a particularly engaged group of students, but I will say that a lot of it was also definitely due to exceptional course materials and the deft guidance of our professors. As a Hist–Lit concentrator, I can say this is some of the best Hist–Lit has to offer: the best Hist, the best Lit, and the best of both worlds. While this class is not a gem, and you should be prepared to work pretty hard, it is rewarding, interesting, and fun. I unequivocally recommend this course.
