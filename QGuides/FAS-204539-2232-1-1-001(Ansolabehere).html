What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class was a great introduction to public opinion and featured a lot of practical application of what we were learning. If you want a start on a publishable paper with good data while also learning a lot about how attitudes are structured, this is the course for you. It's not always the most efficient class in terms of discussion, but it was a pretty enjoyable way to learn about the literature.
This is a really fun course with a great, chill, supportive classroom environment that covered wide–ranging topics and methods in public opinion survey design and analysis. It gives you very practical knowledge and experience in survey development and results evaluation. The survey project that you do throughout the course is a really cool way to design a real survey collaboratively with the professor and other students, problem–solve real issues, do your own analysis, and end up with findings that are relevant to you which can be written into a publishable paper. As a PhD student, it's great to walk away from a class with a project that generated interesting novel data written into an actual publishable paper that aligns with your research interests. With the readings and course topic discussions, I learned a lot about public opinion survey design and analysis as well as was able to reinforce political science knowledge relevant to my field of study. Steve is a very approachable professor who really welcomes differing viewpoints and experiences into his classroom for everyone to learn from each other. He's incredibly knowledgeable and experienced in the topics and methods and also creates a very welcoming and fun classroom environment. Previous experience with survey design and already having research questions in mind about a short survey you would want to do is very helpful to being able to dive right into the course and get the most out of it. Highly recommend this course if you have interest in public opinion research and digging into generating novel survey data for your own project!
