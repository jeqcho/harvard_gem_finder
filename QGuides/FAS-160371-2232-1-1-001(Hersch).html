If you are interested in computers and history, this is the perfect course for you. The course content goes from the origins of communication and technology to modern day computer culture and issues, and it's overall really, really interesting. Prof. Hersch is a quirky and engaging professor who's hilarious and helps you understand the history of computers really well. Would def recommend if you want a course on this topic that's not that hard and really interesting!

Take at least one class with this professor!!

CS majors, if you want to satisfy the Social Sciences divisional distribution, take this course! It's heavily STEM leaning and involves 2 essays, a programming assignment, and a final exam. No weekly problem sets nor midterm.

I personally enjoyed their class on military technologies slightly better. I found the early lectures for 1825 a little more CS based (still no CS experience required) but still a fantastic class where you'll learn a ton about the digital revolution from Ada Lovelace to Steve Jobs to AI.
This is a great class to learn about the history of technology, especially the early history of the computer/Internet pre–2000s (which you really won't find anywhere else at Harvard). The readings are good but make sure you go to lectures since there are no lecture notes and it is important for the final exam.
