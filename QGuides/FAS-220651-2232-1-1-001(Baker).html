Overall this is a super interesting course to learn about popular science in culture. I personally liked the first half of the material more, but it's always good to have a survey of information. I did like the analytical–style essays as I think this is one of the more useful types of writing and it's fun to be pushed in it. However, grading was very reasonable too.
This is a fascinating course that lets you learn about science in a modern–day setting. I'd recommend it to anyone who is curious about how science exists outside of the classroom or laboratory.
I would tell them that this is a gem, take it if you can.
Lectures are interesting but boring and was hard to stay engaged. Nonetheless, you watch many interesting movies and think about other representations of science in popular culture with a critical lens which is a ton of fun.
Professor Baker is incredibly passionate about HISTSCI, and his breadth of knowledge is reflected in his lectures and engagement in roundtable discussions. This seminar made me realize how nearly all developments in science discovery and popularization are artifacts of their time. Jurassic Park and Ex Machina were a highlight for sure :)

I highly recommend this course!
