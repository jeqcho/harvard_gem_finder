What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The nice thing about this class is that the rubrics are pretty clear and that each assignment is one stage in a paper–writing process, which means you don’t have to do everything all at once once the final paper rolls around. The bad news is that you should probably have a familiarity with the basics of R or an equivalent statistical programming language if you’re going to take one of the sections that involves methods. If you’re taking one of the political theory sections, then knowledge of key thinkers/philosophical argumentation/basic historical context are all musts. Don’t think taking a political theory section is an easy alternative to a section that asks you to crunch numbers!
The readings are dense and sometimes complicated to get through, but ultimately incredibly rewarding if you take the time to really dissect them! Sophie is an amazing TF who leads interesting discussions.
Tough but you learn a lot
I learned so much in this course, not just about political science but about how to apply it outside of class. Much of our section and papers were focused on deeply understanding one's theory and thought and less on giving our own opinion. That, I think, is unique about the section on Dignity and Indignation. It is a great class for anyone interested in getting an overview of political thought, with a focus on European and US political thought. I would say one of the more difficult GOV 97 sections, but 100% worth it if you are curious and willing to put in the work.
