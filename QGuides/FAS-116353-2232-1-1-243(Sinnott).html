What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The topics covered in this EXPOS were very interesting. There is a good amount of reading but if the topic interests you I would totally recommend!
Even before I arrived at Harvard, I had the impression that Expos was notoriously difficult. Furthermore, I felt that I had done my fare share of writing in high school, and I was dreading having to take yet another writing class. My spirits rose slightly when I realized that there were different topics from which to choose, and I conceded that I could always use a bit more feedback on my writing, but it wasn't until I started taking this class that I realized that my dread of Expos was out of proportion. The assignments could certainly be challenging, but they were by no means impossible or unreasonable, and our preceptor was always there to support us when we needed any sort of assistance. Even when the course was most difficult, the readings and class discussions always kept me engaged. I would highly recommend this course to anyone who is remotely interested in privacy and surveillance and who is looking for a truly beneficial Expos experience.
For future students of the course, I would highly encourage them to try as hard as possible, but don't expect to end with an A in the course. In the course, grading is harsh (although mostly reasonable), but will bring students a lot of emotional and academic stress if they are very GPA–concerned. Regarding office hours, know that only ONE 15 MINUTE VISIT is allowed between the rough draft and final revision, so you must prepare for the visit carefully. Overall, the content and topic of the course definitely makes it very interesting, but the fact that it is near–impossible for students to make an A makes the course questionable.
