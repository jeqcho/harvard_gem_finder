What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great methods class, and honestly should be the first one in the sequence. Very rewarding, especially if you already have some prior background on quantitative methods but want to really understand what's going on. Matt is an excellent instructor.
The course and the lectures move fast enough that it is imperative to pay attention during lectures and ask a lot of questions the second things start getting murky – otherwise you'll end up the day before the final not knowing what a plug–in estimator is. Because Matt is a great lecturer, it often will not become clear to you that you really don't understand something in the moment of lecture, so be sure to keep up with the readings and really question whether you are fully understanding all the concepts. Start the problem sets early if you can and don't worry about the linear algebra review – you won't really end up needing it. The STAT 110 videos on YouTube are also really helpful. Try to make a problem set group if you can, or attend office hours – problem sets are a big part of your grade and there's a lot you can do to check your answers and ensure you do well.
This course was really helpful for gaining a stronger understanding in statistics and how to use them both responsibly and effectively for political science. As an undergraduate, I recommend this for those willing to invest time into the course and really immerse yourself in the material.
I think this was a great class that was well–taught and I feel a lot better about my grasp of regression now. It's probably a good idea to have some basic working knowledge of statistics and R before you take this course, but otherwise it's pretty much an intro class. Matt's a wonderful and patient teacher!
The class starts slow but picks up very quickly after the first few weeks. It's a very valuable course for anyone intending to use statistical inference in the social sciences.
