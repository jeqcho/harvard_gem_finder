What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is extremely difficult, and I do not recommend taking another language class simultaneously. However, you will learn a lot, and the class is rewarding in the end.
If you are a strong Latin student and you know that you want to concentrate in Classics, take this class earlier rather than later. It will give you a good overview of what is out there in terms of Latin literature, allowing you to delve into the fun stuff (whatever particularly strikes you as the fun stuff) later.
This class is challenging. It requires a large time commitment in order to stay on top of the readings, actively engage in class, and do well on quizzes and self–assessments. You will learn a lot about the connections between authors, individual texts, literary genres, etc. There is a higher emphasis on secondary literature and thematic analysis than a typical language survey course, so if you're trying to get through as much Latin as possible with little background noise, this isn't the course for you. There also isn't a great emphasis placed on historical/social/political context. You will receive some information, but not enough to completely analyze authors and works within the greater milieu of Roman culture.
This is an intense course, but it is worth the effort you put in. The forest of Latin becomes more clear, but you also get to enjoy the beautiful individual trees and their foliage.
This class is an engaging introduction to the second half of Latin literature. It is still a substantial work commitment, but no longer as scary as 112 was rumored to be in the past. Take this class if you want to grow in confidence as a Latin reader across a variety of genres (I really did!!), and don't be afraid to face all kinds of different texts: a highlight for me was falling in love with Tacitus.
Professor Peirano Garrison is a thoughtful, unfailingly kind, and very engaging teacher. Rebecca (the TA) was also fantastic.
