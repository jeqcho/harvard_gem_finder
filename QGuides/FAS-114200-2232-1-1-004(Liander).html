The difficulty of this course varies so much by which professor you get. I loved the material and thought it was super interesting though. And very helpful in developing my Spanish skill
This class is an excellent way to increase both your fluency in Spanish and your knowledge of various Latin American cultures. If you took Spanish 20, I would venture to say that this class is a bit less labor–intensive, even though it is definitely a step up in difficulty as far as language use goes. I was initially annoyed that I had to take more foreign language classes at Harvard after completing the equivalent of three years of Spanish in high school, but now that I have fulfilled my requirement, I am very glad that I was required to do so, and my Harvard Spanish experience has encouraged me to continue pursuing Spanish outside of school.
This is a great class to continue your spanish learning. Definitely more challenging than Spanish 20, but only as much so as is doable and appropriate. I was definitely expected to speak more (all cold calling), but this really helped to improve my comfort with speaking. The materials were mostly short stories which I enjoyed and generally found to be interesting. The Units focus on essentially reteaching you all the Spanish grammar from Spanish 20, but through texts and topics that make it a bit more interesting.
Take this class! Take it with Prof. Mireles Cristino!
Israel is the best! He's so fun and kind, and if you really participate in class, that's all you need to be successful. It's a very achievable and fun class and I enjoyed it much more than the previous levels of spanish.
