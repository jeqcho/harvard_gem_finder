There are 3 problems with this class, which in my view make it one of the single worst courses I've ever taken in my time here. First, the lectures have no point to them. And by that I don't mean that they don't have content, I mean that they don't have a point. There is never a takeaway that we have at the end of any lecture. There is never any point when the lecture starts with a concept that was listed on the syllabus and finishes explaining it by the end of the lecture. It's always just a laundry list of random topics and a stream of consciousness with no real structure or point... ask anyone after a lecture "what were your 3 biggest takeaways," and they will list 3 topics that you wouldn't even be able to find in table of contents of the textbook. This is exacerbated by the fact that she treats a 100 person lecture as if it's a seminar, allowing constant questions in the middle... yes, this is nice if you're confused, but it's super frustrating because every class has that one kid who insists on asking some complex question that someone who's been studying economics for 2 decades wouldn't even be able to answer... this wouldn't be so bad, except Anne is so excited by economics that she gets caught up and ends up spending 25 minutes trying to explain that concept, and then never finishes the lecture. Second, Anne is too smart to be teaching this class. She is incredibly qualified and extremely passionate about what she teaches—which is what motivated me to stick to the class and not drop it after the first test (will get to this soon). The issue, though, is that Anne has a hard time explaining very simple concepts. She can explain a complex, nuanced economics issue with ease and elegance, but she has a tough time explaining something as simple as opportunity cost. Third, the tests are unnecessarily difficult. For no reason. And she acknowledges how difficult they are. The questions have almost no resemblance to what we do in class, they're worded like word puzzles (almost like you're completing a word search on a kid's menu at a restaurant just so you can de–code what it is that the question even wants you to do), and the averages are consistently in the D range.... without any actual exam curve. Anne has a "holistic curve" at the end, which is anything but transparent. At the end of the day, here's my thought process regarding this course: it's a good class with really cool concepts, and if you're interested in economics, take it! Just be ready for a LOT of confusion, frustration, and failure along the way.... but eventual success at the end! My better advice would be to just take EC10A on cycle like a normal Harvard student and just avoid this mistake of a course altogether......
