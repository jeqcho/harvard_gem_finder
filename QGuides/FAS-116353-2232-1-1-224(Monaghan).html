Take this class if you are serious about writing and like military history. Or even if you may not know what you think about military history. Or even if you dislike military history. This class, I would categorize as anti–war if anything. Yes, some cynics may implore you to "Take 20 because you get it over with and 10 wouldn't be worth it." Some fear mongers claim that "If you are a war history fan, I would take the class, but otherwise, I would not put it on your list for fear that you will get the class." But to quote the late and not–so–great FDR, "there is nothing to fear but fear itself." So enroll in this course. Tempt fate. If you are lucky, you will get placed into it. Otherwise, will be miserable in some other expos. Don't take these classes for granted. Don't dread expos. Try to appreciate it because, otherwise, what's the point of all this anyway? This was my favorite course of the semester, and hopefully it will be yours too. Manageable workload and interesting assignments, by the way.
This course is relatively standard for an expos course. Dr. Monaghan is very knowledgable, helpful, and generally a great writing instructor. However, this course may prove to be more work than the average expos course: there is slightly more reading, deadlines come at you fast and are not flexible. Grading seems generally fair, but it's probably stricter than other options.

I wouldn't recommend taking this course if you do not have at least a slight interest in military history and warfare generally. If you do, this course will be interesting though at certain points may stray more towards a home front perspective than you may like. Overall, this is a good way to get the expos requirement done. It is as engaging as possible for expos, very well taught, but may be slightly harder than other options.
There is very little slack cut in this class. You are expected to be on top of your stuff, all the time. Take it if you think you have enough time on your hands to make sure you always do everything the teacher asks and never have any issues, because she will not be forgiving if you approach her about them.
If you like reading old history papers and spending your nights worrying about using citations in Chicago style, this is the class for you.
I do not believe this to be the easiest option for fulfilling the expos requirement. But this is a must–take expos if you have any interest in military history. Dr. Monaghan runs a tight ship, but if you keep up with the deadlines and give an honest effort, you will get a lot out of this class.
Make sure to start preparing for BOTH drafts and revisions early. This is not a section of Expos where you can half–ass anything.
The content is pretty heavy, the readings are manageble, but the class is rewarding.
The material is quite interesting, but this class is A LOT OF WORK. Probably more work than many other Expos sections, from what I hear. Probably I spent more time than the average, but I think I spent on average around 15 hours per week on the class (some weeks less, some weeks more).
The Professor expects your true best efforts. Sometimes I think she thinks your abilities are greater than you think they are, but shes usually correct. It feels hard but idk I really liked it
