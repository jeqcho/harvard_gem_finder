This is a great course to learn about many different topics within astronomy. The requirements are not too strict, the course staff are excellent, and you will definitely learn a lot and have a great time working on the labs! Would definitely recommend as an elective or to fulfill concentration/secondary requirements!
The structure of this course is very exploratory and independent. We almost never had lectures, and the few we had were no longer than ten minutes. Given that it was the first year that the class was taught in this entirely group work format, there were a lot of kinks, but prof Johnson was very open to our feedback. If the structure stays the same next year, you will not learn very much new content, but the teaching staff is so supportive and incredible. I would imagine, based on how receptive the teaching staff was to feedback throughout the year, that there will be a bit more lecturing next year, though.

I would recommend this course if you are interested in unorthodox, more kinetic, approaches to education. I made and solidified a lot of wonderful friendships throughout the semester, and working on the boards every day made it easy to interact with my classmates and learn from them.

The workload for this class was incredibly inconsistent, though. We were required to post work in the form of blog posts, and it was very unclear when assignments would be due or posted. I believe that a lot of this was due to the new structure, though, and I think it will probably be more solidified in the next iteration of the class.

I know this class used to have a reputation for being incredibly difficult, and I have heard from several individuals that they dropped a concentration or secondary in astro because of it, but please DO NOT LET THAT STOP YOU FROM TAKING THIS COURSE. With the new structure, YOU WILL NOT FIND THIS CLASS TOO DIFFICULT, and you will be WELL SUPPORTED BY THE TEACHING STAFF.

The TFs pretty much carried my class, though, so idk if that means it would be a bit of a toss–up. Anyway, this class, 90% of the time, is ridiculously easy. This class is incredible. Professor Johnson is awesome; he's approachable, knowledgeable, and very skilled at communicating concepts.
This class is primarily composed of collaborative worksheets which makes for a very enjoyable and enlightening time if you are with a group that you love. Because of this, I highly recommend taking this class with friends.
The homeworks are slightly tedious (mostly just due to my inexperience with LaTeX) but very rewarding in terms of generating a strengthened understanding of concepts as well as the skill of working with LaTeX.
The grading is very fair and lenient and it is certainly possible to get an A even without the most advanced background in astrophysics.

I **highly** recommend this class to anybody, even those who don't plan to study astrophysics.
This course is not a lecture–based course. It is incredibly hands on. The majority of class time is spent working on worksheets in groups at the various chalkboards scattered around the room, and most of the learning that goes on in this course comes from working through these worksheets. The point of this is to teach you not only some of astronomy's foundational concepts, but also how to think like an astronomer. You probably don't have a lot of experience with this, so know that it is normal to struggle in the beginning, and to not know how to approach problems. You will build intuition as time goes on, you just have to buy in and stick to it.
It will not prepare you for higher level astrophysics courses, so make sure to take Astro 17 before taking higher level courses.
If you're new to astronomy like I was, then take this class! I had a great time. We spent our class periods doing problems on blackboards in groups after a brief introduction. This approach was very effective for my learning and it also made the class extremely fun. The in–class problems and homework assignments were designed so that we would learn a lot without being excessively stressed. I am surprised by how I can now thoroughly explain the cause of so many astronomical processes thanks to the impressively coherent and well–designed worksheets.
