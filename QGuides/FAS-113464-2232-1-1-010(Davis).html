This class was one of the most rewarding ones I took this semester. It can be challenging, but if you follow these tips, I am more than certain that you will have a good experience.

– Watch the videos posted under each topic before class and review the section notes right after to stay on top of it
– Get to know your section leader because the teaching staff is friendly, encouraging, and understanding
– Go to office hours. Even if you think you don't need to. Just get your problem sets done with a staff member there to guide you when things get confusing.
– Make a study group so that you have people who can explain things to you in their own words when you feel stuck or confused
– Do as many practice problems as you can when studying, especially for the skills checks
Take this course if you do not have a solid foundation on pre calculus and calculus topics. The Math MB team is very supportive!
The tests can sometimes get very overwhelming, especially with other STEM classes. But if you study beforehand, you should feel prepared. The majority of the TFs are very nice, so reach out to them; they are usually willing to set time aside to help you. Go to office hours and get to know other TFs; they are also very willing to help you. This class really does set your foundational math really well and makes you get familiar with the materials compared to other STEM courses.
