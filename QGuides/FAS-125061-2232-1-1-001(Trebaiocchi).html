TLDR: TAKE THIS CLASS! It has been my favorite class at Harvard so far and it has been THE ONLY CLASS that I have genuinely enjoyed going to every day.

If you've already taken Italian 10, Italian 11 is essentially the same class in a barely different font. I had Katie as my TF and she is AMAZING!! She was always so enthusiastic everyday in class, and that enthusiasm naturally passed onto me! The course itself is graded super reasonably––the class should rarely be overwhelming. In class quizzes are suuuuper reasonable, and its much easier to do well than poorly. THAs are very similar to the ones in Italian 10, these just involve some minor research and reading short articles/watching short videos. The online VHL assignments can get a bit tedious at times, but they're very doable, and a really great learning tool. The final project is SOOO reasonable, and is especially helpful when you're likely overwhelmed with other finals. It helped us end the class on a low–stress good note :) You will also probably form closer relationships with your classmates if you take Italian 11 since you would have taken Italian for almost a year together! I definitely recommend having Italian movie nights with your classmates towards the end of the semester when you have to watch the assigned film (La Meglio Gioventu)!
I would say that you need to study and make an effort to understand the material, since there are a lot of different tenses that will become really confusing really fast if you don't take the time to learn what each is and when to use them.
TAKE THIS CLASS!
Take this class if you've taken Italian 10! It really helps to get the basics of Italian down to a T. The class environment is so nice and all the students make the learning experience so great. There is quite a bit of homework, but it all helps a lot with comprehension.
This class is a lot of work but you will definitely learn Italian. Stay on top of the online assignments on VHL and you'll be fine. The instructors are all gems and they are very understanding and kind and enthusiastic about Italian.
This class is definitely a bit more intensive than Italian 10, as we learn quite a few verb tenses / conjugations in a short amount of time. There is also a lot more work assigned to you, but not necessarily more difficult. However, this semester places a greater emphasis on the oral aspect of learning a language, which is greatly useful. You get a lot more practice with speaking and you learn the verb tenses that you may have been missing with solely the present / past/ imperfect. I would definitely recommend taking this class, but this class will definitely not take less than 5 hours a week if you actually stay on task.
