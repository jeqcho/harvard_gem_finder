this is the best class ever regarding America and its legacy in the middle east
* I learned so much in this class about Middle Eastern politics and the US invasions, especially since I went in with very little information. The course covers a lot of material, and I got a little lost sometimes, but the big takeaways were clear. There's a decent amount of reading, but it's really interesting and definitely helped my understanding. I'm really glad that I took this course because I feel like this material is so important and not really taught in a lot of high schools.
Take this course trust me, assignments are great and the information is interesting and feels relevant.
Phenomenal course––detailed lectures and impactful readings that you will get a lot of out. Go to class because nothing is available online (slides, recordings nonexistent) and professor is hard to get ahold of. Other than that, it is a wonderful experience––loved it!
The class material is interesting but it was not presented incredibly effectively. Lecture was really hard to follow, which made the material difficult to understand and internalize and thus made the assignments really hard to complete. This made the class pretty stressful and not super enjoyable If lecture was better organized the class would be great though as the material is really important.
Prof. Sagha is amazing! He is obviously very knowledgeable not just in the politics of Iraq/Afghanistan but the whole region . He understands the nuances of regional politics (which is very rare among other Harvard faculty). This is all in addition to the fact that he is an amazing human being, very accessible outside of class, and very understanding!
Overall, truly enjoyed this class and would 10000% take it again if I could. It helped me better understand US policy in the region (even though I thought I had some background on this, I still learned SO much).
Doing the readings will definitely help you, but you will also not be completely lost if you do not do them entirely (you might be a bit for section though). Lectures are posted so it makes it very easy to go back to them if you need to reference something particular for the papers.
The assignments are straightforward and fair. The prompts that Prof. Sagha posts help you start thinking critically about the material and are very helpful in getting you started on the paper.
All in all, I do not have anything negative to say about this class. Take it if you are interested in learning more about US policy in Iraq/Afghanistan (which you should be if you are thinking of going into anything related to US politics!)
If you don't know a lot about the Middle East, you might get a little confused. However, the readings and lectures work together really well to clear things up. Take it. Great class.
