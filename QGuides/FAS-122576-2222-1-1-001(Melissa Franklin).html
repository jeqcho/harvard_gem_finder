I write this fully aware that it has almost no impact on whether or not you'll take this class since you probably don't want to and are just taking it as a pre–med requirement anyway. Regardless, if this class is taught by Dr. Franklin, try to avoid this class if at all possible. Here's a couple of the aspects of the course that made it an unenjoyable experience for me.

– Until Tim took over, the lectures were a mess. They essentially never finished on time, meaning that much of the lecture material had to be delayed until the next class. The notes were disorganized and hard to follow, especially if you either stopped paying attention for a few minutes or were trying to review them later on. The order of the activities themselves within a lesson often didn't make sense, seeming to jump around rather than following some sort of logical progression of building up from "obvious" facts to "interesting" results. Frankly, the most reliable aspects of these lectures were the inclusion of some off–color jokes, usually about pre–meds.

– Similarly, the class overall seemed quite disorganized, especially for one that has been running for so long. For most of the first part of the course, there wasn't even a consistent file naming system. This is especially important because there's no centralized resource for all the materials. There's module review notes and section videos, but neither are guaranteed to cover all of the concepts that you need to know. The most comprehensive way to find all the class material is by individually going through all of the lecture notes, and even then you have to hope that you can comprehend the annotations from the notes or that you have enough time to watch the recordings.

These points, as well as other small things that build up like the randomly nitpicky questions they like to ask on exams that seem to test more for if you read the question twice than for actual understanding, just added up to a less than ideal time in the class. I hate to say it but frankly, I'm someone who has generally always enjoyed physics, and this class has helped me realize why so many others don't. That's not to say it's all bad. The section TFs are helpful, the labs do a good job of reinforcing the materials without being needlessly long (and only 5 in the semester), and Tim's teaching near the end of the semester really helped to turn things around. Unfortunately, these redeeming qualities aren't enough to stop me from recommending against taking this class if you have the option.
