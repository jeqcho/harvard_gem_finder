What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class really emphasizes how vulnerable children are to their environment and, personally, led me to reflect on the ways I plan to raise any future children I have. Course material isn't too difficult, especially with a background in psychology. The professor sometimes expresses personal views that I personally disagree with, and sometimes voicing opposition to those views can feel difficult.
This is a really great and illuminating class to take. Though the content can be heavy sometimes, it is really critical to understand the systems that impact children and their development. An understanding of developmental psychology would not be complete without this course.
Was a cool class with cool topics. And it was pretty chill but learned cool things. Make sure you choose a good group for the book club because I was stuck in a pair (which wasn’t allowed because if the work load but she allowed it anyways) and I had to do all the work and was not given any credit.
I would recommend this course to students with an interest in the intersection of social issues and child development. I think students into social justice could enjoy this class because of our discussion of policies and social issues.
If you have ever had an interaction with kids (you have), take this course! You learn about almost every aspect of factors that influence the growing child, and the material is very practical to the real world. Dr. Lewin–Bizan is amazing and supportive; she isn't afraid to push you to improve, but she also wants to make sure you understand the material. Discussion during lecture is fascinating and nuanced. The class is not particularly onerous either. You have no reason not to take the course, even as a fifth class if necessary.
This class is a really good discussion class if you want to talk about the many ways in which underprivileged and disadvantaged youth might be at psychological and developmental risk in the United States. There are many difficult topics that the class covers, but Dr. Lewin–Bizan handles them with grace and is a really good facilitator. She also has lots of good personal anecdotes and invites people to share their own thoughts and experiences often. It feels almost like more of a sociology class at times as opposed to a psych class, but overall is a very engaging discussion–based class.
