Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS!!
if you like group projects this is the class for you!
This course is super laid back – you can really get away with doing nothing if you want to, but Yuval is very passionate about the subject, and if you put in effort, it can be really interesting
Don't expect too much of Yuval. Even thought the class is on public opinion, he teaches the class as if it's about his own opinion. He has a condescending demeanor towards students, especially if they give an answer that he considers incorrect, In addition, this may be only relevant to the specific composition of the class that I was in, but he jokes around way too much with athletes to the point that it distracts from the focus of the class (one could say he plays favorites). Additionally, he struggled at times to speak fluidly, (perhaps because English isn't his native language) which made it difficult to follow his arguments and reasoning. Do know though that Yuval LOVES to talk. Even though our class wasn't that big, our group presentations that were supposed to all be presented in one class session were never all presented in one class. This was because after every group presentation, Yuval would talk extensively (feedback is nice, but I do mean extensively and often redundantly) which took time from students sharing their work.
Take this class with friends!
Take this class if you're at all interested in public opinion! Yuval is chill but incredibly knowledgeable on the topic, and every assignment was a group assignment, which seems daunting at first but is very manageable. Bi–weekly presentations and two extremely short essays are all that is required in this course, and the readings actually capture a great overview of the mode of public opinion formation that is being concentrated on that week. A fun but rewarding class that I actually took something away from.
