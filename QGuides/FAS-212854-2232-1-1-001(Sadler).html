This course is very enjoyable. The teaching staff is great, super knowledgeable, and genuinely enthusiastic about the material. It is not like there is no work for the class (readings and a pset every week), but it is not especially difficult, and it is clear that the whole point of everything you do is to help you learn. You get the chance to interact with and discuss primary sources from the scientists who performed revolutionary experiments and then replicate those experiments (or analogous ones). My only complaint is that sometimes we did not get a lot of detail on the specific science behind the experiments we are doing. I did not always feel like I walked away with a full understanding of the experiment. I did always know a bit more about the historical background of them and how they did, in fact "change our world." Overall an interesting gened that is not especially difficult.

As a result, I would say that half of the course is quite fun and engaging; the other half may be a bit drier, but the readings are often skim–able for the answers to the reading questions.
