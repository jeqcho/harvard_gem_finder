This is a fantastic class with interesting and varied course materials: for our year, we read essays, comic books, poems, looked at art, and watched Princess Mononoke. Just as a note, this class truly is a climate fictions class, with emphasis on art and literature depicting and considering climate change; it is not a class based on the science and policy of climate change (although the final research paper is very flexible and can address some of these aspects). Also, Eliza is a wonderful, approachable teacher who provides helpful and insightful feedback throughout the writing process that will likely improve your writing. Additionally, she is very understanding and flexible with essay extensions and readings (which do get lengthy on occasion).

The expos course structure is demanding in short intervals: the three essays throughout the semester will feel like they occur one after another and will entail a lot of writing and during the drafting and revision phase. Nevertheless, the overall time commitment isn't massive since weekly reading assignments are fairly casual. The continual writing of essays and preparatory response papers sucks, but given that it's an integral part of the expos experience, it's largely unavoidable. Tldr: the subject, teacher, and course materials make this class not only very bearable but also fairly engaging.
