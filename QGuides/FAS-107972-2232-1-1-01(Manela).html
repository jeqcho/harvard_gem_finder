What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Invest everything you can into your seminar research paper! You will get out of it everything you put in.
This class is life–changing if you buy in. It will take up a good amount of time, no doubt, but you will get so much more out of it than any survey course. It is incredibly rewarding to walk away from the semester with something more than a grade to show for it. Highly highly recommend, but you need to be motivated to do the work.
Professor Manela and James are both great, and this is a very well–run seminar. The course content is very interesting, and the readings are well–selected to give you a wide range of perspectives. You also spend the first couple of weeks doing a "lay of the land" of both the geography and history of China and US/China relations, so you are able to fit the more detailed content from the latter half of the course into a clear picture of the broader history (this also means that you don't need much background in Chinese history to take the course!). You don't get any grades on assignments for the whole semester, but James gives very useful and detailed feedback. I really recommend this course!
Take this course if you're looking for a rewarding challenge. Writing a full–length history paper is something I did for the first time in this seminar, and the process was not simple but was incredibly meaningful, and I don't regret it. The readings are fascinating; you will learn a lot. And Professor Manela and James are incredibly knowledgeable and fun to converse with.
