Great introduction to political economy; would definitely recommend for AM/Gov concentrators! Reasonable workload, engaging lectures, and interesting content
If you want to take a gov/econ class and are good at math, take this class. If you are a gov concentrator who does not like math, I don't think this is the best class. I was the latter. This class is pretty interesting, but if you are not a math or econ person, it is easy to get lost in all of the models. The p–sets have quite a bit of excessive algebra ocassionally as well. That being said, Peter is an excellent lecturer and an all around great guy, and Sung In was a great TF!
I would take this course but definitely do not fall behind in lecture and pay attention!
great course! peter makes the content accessible and the assignments/exams are super fair.
People said that the exams were super easy – I think they do require relatively robust preparation and that you shoud review the pset solutions, slides, and practice exams pretty closely. I did well on the psets and froze on the midterm because I didn't practice enough. So definitely don't take it easy! You do have to work to do well.
The class is overall very chill and is quite interesting if you like political theory, especially the quantitative side of it. This year, the exams seemed extraordinarily difficult, but that shouldn't be too much of a reason to not take this class. If you are a government concentrator/secondary, you will find this class to be a refreshing divergence from typical political theory courses that are heavy on readings.
It is a very interesting course, and the professor and TF are amazing people. Beware that the class can feel difficult when you have very little economics and college experience overall. The tests were not a full check of what you learned (for example, the final test felt like it tested one topic we learned from the entire course, rather than different little concepts). It is very easy to feel like you are doing poorly in the class, especially in the younger crowd. As a freshman, I struggled to keep up with the pace of the course, but this was common among other freshman and not so common with the upperclassmen.
Lecture component not super useful for the psets. Exam questions can also vary a lot. Psets are good practice –– section TF is awesome. Challenging concepts and complex formulas. Not impossible though, and interesting material if you like game theory, political decision–making, etc.
This was the worst course I have taken at Harvard. If you are interested in politics or in understanding how policies our designed, this class will not help you. It is a completely theoretical course that has no real–world takeaways or applications. It is unnecessarily difficult and the professor does not know how to teach in a digestible way. He is outgoing and passionate but he does not know how to teach the concepts to people who do not understand it.
The class material is pretty difficult and theoretical.
This class had no real impact on me except for lowering my GPA (I think). Id say take it if youre interested in the concept and then be aware of the add/drop deadline. Peter is a really good guy and Sung–In is a wonderful TF! But the class itself gets complicated at times and most of the class was comprised of people with enough background in math and econ to do well in the class. As a freshman without any experience in those areas, I was frequently very lost. If you take this class definitely meet with the TF every week for psets because she will walk you through them in a really efficient and clear manner, and that will give you easy class points.
The best advice I can give you is look through the lecture slides before signing up for the class so you get a sense of its theoretical vs. empirical balance. The class is much more theoretical (more about models of different actors than real world examples in political economy) than I (and many of my classmates) expected going into it. For that reason, I unfortunately didn't enjoy the course as much as I would have liked to. That being said, if you want a course that gives you the foundations of theoretical models in political economy, this is the course for you. Peter and Sung In are both great, and are very knowledgeable and care a lot about their students. Also, don't expect this class to be a gem. It's certainly on the lower side in terms of workload (just one relatively short pset per week), but that pset is quite difficult (you will likely need to go to office hours to complete it) and the exams are extremely difficult (although there is a curve). All that being said, just know exactly what type of class you're signing up for and you should be good!
Lot of very interesting political economy models covered. Not in too much depth to bore you, but sufficiently enough to get you interested in exploring further. Enjoyed the lectures a lot.
This class is very interesting, but delves heavily into proofs behind the models; be prepared to learn some very technical material.
This class is so good!! The material is super interesting and relevant, and it is taught in a straightforward and clear way. The assignments are manageable and consistent. Honestly, I can't think of a single issue with this class. I've never taken an economics or government class but I really enjoyed this one :)
Overall, I think this class was portrayed as accessible for anyone but, in reality, required prior experience with economic models, high–level math, and economic concepts. As a government concentrator, I really struggled with the pace and math. This led to a lot of stress and frustration because I'm not sure if Prof. Buisseret or the TF Sung–In understood how challenging these concepts are to those who had never seen them before. They promised me help and a manageable speed but the PSETs and exams seemed to demand way more than just an understanding of the concepts. It was especially frustrating how much harder the exams were than lecture or the PSETS.
I recommend this class if you are a Gov or Econ concentrator and are curious about political economy. It has a strong teaching team.
This is a good class to take *if* you're willing to put in the work. Professor Buisseret is very passionate about political economy and he makes lectures fun with his dry humor and inclusion of relevant real–life applications of abstract topics. However, those topics can be difficult to understand, and the exams are sometimes more difficult than the problem sets. The problem sets tend to be simple conceptually, just somewhat time–intensive, especially as the course goes on. You will need to put in the work if you're going to properly understand the abstract theories discussed in class. If you do, though, it will be a rewarding and engaging experience.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It was one of the best decisions I've made in college. It's very manageable and super engaging. If you're interested in understanding political and economic issues through a quantitative lens, this is the perfect class for you. I took this class with absolutely no experience in economic theory, but Peter and Sung In were super helpful, engaging, and supportive so I did not feel behind at all.
