What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class has been top 2 for me of 3 years worth of classes. Recommend it to the highest degree to students who are Christian and battle with how their faith should be a lens and a lighthouse when thinking and discussing hotly debated topics. This class was so fun and everyone was entirely invested. I never thought about how much the reading was (which is not too much) because I was so genuinely interested in the material. Class discussion is the best; this was the only class where I really felt able to say anything relevant without having to worry about being judged or discriminated. I wish there was more class time per week. Professor Lockwood is spectacular and gives very useful background information so you also come away learning concrete material. He has wonderful input and always facilitates the best discussions.
Would recommend this class for students who may have grown up Christian but have not thought deeply about how Christianity interacts with the issues of today. The class isn't too demanding work–wise, and the readings were all really good. The discussions really depend on the students engaging, so be sure to do the readings or you won't get anything out of discussion.
Definitely worth taking! Chip is incredibly thoughtful and leads very rich discussion. All of your classmates will want to be there and will ask thought–provoking, respectful questions.
This is truly one of the most engaging courses I’ve taken as an undergrad, and especially coming from a Christian background and having lots of questions about the sociopolitical challenges facing theology, it was a perfect outlet both to learn from peers and superiors and formulate my own ethical posture on the issues discussed. Moreover, even when an answer isn’t obvious, you get to better understand the question. Not to mention that the nature of the course material requires that you get really comfortable with classmates, and we developed a sincere community! Chip (course head and PhD in the study of religion) is absolutely incredible as a facilitator of discussion, as a teacher (so knowledgeable), and he provided fantastic feedback both before and after paper submission. Truly a top tier class
Classes like these depend on the dedication of the other students and the size of the class. In 2022 Spring we had 16 or so people but the quality of discussion was very high.
This is a fantastic course that grapple with some of the biggest modern ethical questions in Christianity. Whether you are Christian or you are curious about the faith or have some qualms about it, this class was a welcoming space to discuss some incredibly deep questions that are often avoided in other classes. It's not a Bible study, but it does engage with Scripture in very constructive ways, read alongside theologians of diverse backgrounds and perspectives. The readings are not too bad nor are the assignments, and you could tell everyone was really bought into the discussions. I highly recommend this class even if you've never taken a Christianity or Religion class before and are simply curious about it.
This class was amazing. The readings and the class discussions were really interesting and Chip facilitated a really special classroom environment where we were able to explore really challenging themes and questions in a productive manner. I loved this course!!
Excellent course– fascinating, very enjoyable. Not easy, but never stressful. Highly reccomend.
I strongly recommend this class as it emphasized the impact Christianity has in modern society and how to tie in different theological perspectives into debating the ethics of these topics; it helps one better understand their own faith in an academic setting, and it is quite stimulating to hear the differing perspectives of Harvard College students on the matter. I highly recommend this class!
