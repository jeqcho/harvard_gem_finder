What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is SO FUN. Probably my favorite class of all time so far at Harvard. Learning a language is hard but the teachers are amazing
The Korean language department is always very lovely; I think Korean is one of the best languages you can take at this school. The class was a little bit smaller than the first course of this series, so I felt like our class formed a connection of some kind. 노 선생님 and 김환희 선생님 are great instructors. I really enjoyed coming to Korean each day and participating in class. You can tell they really enjoy teaching Korean, and I think that is partly why this class has been my favorite course of the semester.
This is a great class, and very similar in structure to previous levels of Korean. I thought it was noticeably harder than KOREAN 120a, but it's worth the time and effort if you want to improve your Korean.
I would highly highly recommend this class but I would also only recommend this class to people who want to seriously learn a language and are ready to put in a lot of hard work. A lot of homework, like in this class, might seem tedious but it is absolutely necessary to improve your abilities in these short semesters. Also learning a foreign language is always really rewarding.
There is going to be a lot of work and multiple deadlines every week. But your Korean does improve a lot from this course! If you have other major commitments and do not want to handle three or four deadline per week, perhaps you can think twice before taking the class; or at least you will have to find a strategy that works; But it is definitely a helpful course if you are committed to learning the language.
You can feel a drastic jump in the difficulty of the quizzes and assignments from 120A to 120B, but the end result is rewarding.
