YOU SHOULD TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Dr. Alvarez and Dr. Konkle truly created a one–in–a–million class. You can enter as a novice to both neuroscience / psychology and how the biological vision system works as well as a novice to computer science, and leave this class with an understanding of the highlights from each field. As machine learning and neural networks become more popular across all scientific and humanities disciplines, gaining a vocabulary and general understanding of how machine learning works is critical. Taking this class, you get to understand the pathways of innovation in machine learning research, and get a comprehensive overview of how key neural networks (some of which provided the foundation upon which stable diffusion ai and other networks revolutionizing art) were developed. The assignments of this class are straightforward, and you get useful feedback if you make a mistake.

The workload is also not too demanding

**This course is amazing and would highly recommend it to anyone who is interested in machine learning, AI or neuroscience. I came from a AM/CS background and I learned more about Machine Learning than I did in CS181: Intro to Machine Learning. This course doesn't focus on the derivations of the algorithms but rather the high level overview of the architectures, and does so in a thought provoking way by connecting it to neuroscience. The Profs. are super accessible and given how cutting edge these technologies are, it feels like we are all learning together which isn't something you get to experience as a student very often. Potentially my favorite course I've taken here and I'm a graduating senior –– I can't recommend this course highly enough.
George and Talia are great professors who can explain difficult concepts clearly to a variety of audiences. You learn a good deal about how human and AI systems work far beyond basic vision. Content is just the right amount of challenging and live discussion class helps to clear out confusion well in a fun environment. Would definitely recommend for anyone who wants to learn about how humans and AI systems are similar or different and what CS can learn from Neuro and vice–versa
I would recommend this course if you're interested in getting a glimpse into biological perception and deep learning, but want a softer intro into these topics without too much technical background. You spend two hours every week to talk in small discussion pods during class comparing two papers –– 1 on biological vision and 1 on some deep learning model. I personally didn't find the discussion pods to be all that helpful. I would've unconditionally recommended this class in its initial offering with a dozen or so students, but its newfound popularity resulted in 40+ students this spring. It's not a difficult class, but you probably won't learn as much as you would like if the class is as big as it was this year.
This course is amazing. The professors are fantastic, kind, and helpful. Seda, the TF is very knowledgeable and good at explaining concepts. Preparing for class through videos is a relaxed way of being introduced to the content. The papers we must read for class are difficult to understand more often than not, but the study guide and the class discussion will prepare you for the quizzes. The group discussion format can be intimidating, but trying your best to prepare for class helps you feel more confident and classmates will help fill in gaps in knowledge. Class time is a great time to interact with the professors and TF and get questions answered. There are only quizzes which can be challenging, but there is a chance to redo them later to show how much you've learned and grown. The group project is quite fun. The final project is very opened ended and gives a chance to delve deep into a topic on bio/comp vision.

Seda (TF) gives really helpful feedback too. I wish the professors were a bit more accessible outside of class though––they don't have office hours or anything.
The midterm and final project were awesome because they were really open–ended––you got to really pick something new and learn it and go in depth about a topic from the class.
Best class I have taken at Harvard, saying that in Senior Spring. They have found a great way to incentivize learning the material over just trying to get a good grade. You have a weekly quiz with short answer questions about big topics of the week. It doesn't matter what you get on the quiz because the midterm is you redoing your answers once you learn more and then get graded on the new answers (old grades go away). Both projects are interesting and straightforward. Both professors are so nice and helpful. You cannot miss this class.
