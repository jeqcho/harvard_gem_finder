This is really an excellent course. Shleifer is really amazing and cares a lot about his students, you will get a lot out of the class if you bring this same enthusiasm for your paper.
absolutely excellent – be sure you can write a good paper and be ready to spend a lot of time on that.
Take this class! Prof. Shleifer is kind and helpful—this is a rare opportunity to work closely with a world–class economist. He regularly meets with you one–on–one, and as long as you’re willing to commit to doing some good work, he’ll be there to guide you along. Even if you are not extremely experienced in econometrics, you will learn a lot from this class about thinking through economic projects, reading papers, and performing analyses. It’s not as stressful as it seems and Prof. Shleifer tries to relieve your stress as much as he can.
This class will be a good litmus test to see if you enjoy research in economics without being too crazy of a commitment.
This is a great class that focuses on a singular research paper of a topic of your own choosing (related to political economics), which was very rewarding to complete. Readings are relatively interesting but low stakes, as Professor Shleifer does a good job presenting them in class and facilitating discussion.
The class is good for giving you a chance to write a research paper that is supervised, though not very closely guided. Probably the hardest part of the class, which no one can help that much with, is finding an original dataset for your research idea.
This is an excellent class! Yes, the syllabus readings are a little dated and it doesn't feel like you're exploring cutting edge research. Yes, professor Shleifer combines lecture slides from talks given a decade ago and you write memos on readings that have nothing to do with econ. But that's not what matters. What matters is 1) you get to write your own paper, an immensely helpful experience for future thesis writers, 2) you get constant, frequent, and passionate mentorship from both prof. Shleifer and Suproteem (I don't understand how they devote this much attention to you, a lowly undergrad, but it's incredibly nice), 3) you get a ton of professional development in Econ if you're considering doing more in the department and possibly going to grad school, and 4) the guest lecturers that prof. Shleifer gets to come to class are amazing, they present some of the coolest papers I've seen in my life (often in prepublication stage) and patiently answer questions about daa sources and identification.
SHLEIFER is AMAZING!!!! Cares SO much about his students and this class. I honestly learned a lot about political economy and am grateful to have taken this course. Super nice that it only meets once a week, and the readings are honestly not that bad/long. A lot of the papers are seminal works in econ, so worth actually reading them. The books are interesting as well––I would recommend that with weeks with memos, you start early, as the response papers can sneak up on you. Final project is fun, would also recommend starting early if you can, so you're not scrambling to write the 20 pages, which the first draft is due mid–April.
This is one of the classics of the economics department, and I would really encourage you take it––even if you're not an economics person! I came from a CS background and took a lot away from the course. I learned a lot about economics research, the ideas that the economics world is grappling with, and what the forefront of the discipline looks like. It was definitely difficult for me to come up with and write the final paper coming from a background without a lot of econometrics experience (it's not necessary to have a lot of experience, but it would be helpful to have some knowledge), but Professor Shleifer, the guest speakers that come in, and the TF are all very helpful for thinking about and writing your final paper. It's a very rewarding experience to work on one paper throughout the term and come out with something you're proud of!
10/10

Take this class if you like doing research in economics or want to explore it. Having the opportunity to meet every other week with Andrei is amazing! He takes genuine interest in your ideas and provides helpful guidance throughout the process.

Advice to future students: start thinking of potential paper ideas early!
As you do the readings, you should read them with an eye toward how you would write something similar. People forget in seminar papers that one of the primary goals of doing readings is not just to get information out of them but to see models of how you should write. Also take note that despite the structure of the course, it is not as feedback–intensive as you might expect. So moderate your expectations in that direction, although the course is overall fantastic.
Professor Shleifer is amazing about meeting with students and developing a relationship with them, although lectures can be a little dry sometimes. The readings in this class are very interesting, and it's a great way of fulfilling the Ec concentration writing requirement. The Ec department is generally pretty impersonal, so this is a great way of having an experience that is not! Definitely get started earlier on with your draft and plan for the paper –– it's easy for the deadlines to sneak up on you, and the more you do each week the more productive / less bullshitty your meetings with Professor Shleifer will be.
