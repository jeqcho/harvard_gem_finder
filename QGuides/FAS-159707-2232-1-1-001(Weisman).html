LS50b is a worthwhile class to take in order to complete the journey begun in the first part of this course. Overall, while still challenging and needing a time commitment, this course is less demanding than LS50a! Regarding topics covered, take a look at the syllabus ahead of time; you'll need to do more coding and will be learning about fairly different things than first semester.
If you made it through LS50a, you will make it through LS50b. This semester has a lot more math, but it is not nearly as hard as the math–y sections of the first semester (no more stat mech!). However, while this counts as a math course, I still don't feel as comfortable in linear algebra as I would have if I took Math19.

In terms of new topics and what to expect from them, we had a computational biology unit that was very statistics focused, so you'll be better off with some prior stats experience, but you can definitely get by without it. Similarly, some experience with matrix addition/multiplication/inverses was helpful for the linear algebra unit but not necessary. The more biology focused units were similar to the first semester. As with last semester, there are frequent office hours that you should definitely take advantage of

At the end of last semester, previous students told me this semester would be easier than LS50a, but this was definitely not the case. The two semesters have similar workloads (although I did feel more prepared to get into a rhythm of pseting), so don't take more classes this semester thinking you will have a lighter LS50 load. If you do decide to take an extra class, remember to consider both time spent on work AND time spent physically in class. I forgot to take LS50's high amount of in–class time into account and ended up with 7–8 straight hours of class on Mondays/Tuesdays.
