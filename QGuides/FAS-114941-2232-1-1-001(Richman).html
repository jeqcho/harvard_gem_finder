What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The RLL soph tutorial is incredible. The readings and films are interesting and discussion section are low–stress. I feel like we read a fairly diverse array of works, although it could always use more diversification. Dr. Richman is an absolute icon, which makes the class that much better.
This class was a great foundational course for RLL. It's really nice to be with all the concentrators across different languages and then have a section to be able to speak/read in your own language. The atmosphere is pretty laid back and it makes for a welcoming dynamic. Dr. Richman is great!
This class is easy, but you shouldn't treat it as though it is. Recognize that flexible deadlines and relaxed conversations are a blessing, but take the readings and course material seriously. Do the readings and participate in discussion; that makes the class more fun for everyone involved. This is a stress–free environment to ask dumb questions (even though they don't exist), have interesting conversations, and leisurely explore some literature. The class is very french–heavy, which is a little annoying if you're a Spanish/LatinX–focused concentrator, but still informative and interesting. Do NOT be concerned about the language requirements for this course; you could probably survive with very little foreign language experience.
