EVERYONE SHOULD TAKE THIS CLASS WE LOVE PROFESSOR BLOCK SO VERY MUCH
This course was a highly positive learning environment, centered around a topic that drew on passion, personal experience, and curiosity. The teaching was world–class, and the content was engaging. One specific strength was the variety or resources used in the class such as panels, video/documentaries, and journal publications. Susan is the best teacher that I have had at Harvard by a long shot. She cares about her students, cares about the content, and brings a level of expertise that is unmatched. She makes students feel empowered in this course, and supports them through the more emotional moments of the course. I will say that several tears were shed over the semester in this course. Somehow, the community that was created in the course felt so safe and supportive, even with just seeing each other once a week. I would say that this is my favorite course of the year, and if you have any interest in the subject (or want to try something new) you should not pass up the opportunity to learn from/with Susan.

Susan is one of the wisest and kindest human beings I have ever met. Finally, Susan brings really delicious chocolate cookies to every class, brings her golden retriever Dante to class sometimes, and invites us to her house for chats.

This is a class to wrestle with some important questions on the good life and many existential musings that all of us yearn for to some degree. For example, we watched about 3 documentaries during the semester, and each time the class cried together and bonded through a shared emotion of sorrow, vulnerability, and empathy. The readings are interesting enough and the short writing assignments each week serve as a good reminder to jot down our thoughts.

TAKE THIS COURSE! One of the reviews I read when deciding to apply to this freshman seminar expressed a sentiment along the lines of "[t]his is what I expected from my Harvard education," and I cannot echo that enough. Susan is an absolutely amazing teacher and a beautiful human being, and I feel so grateful to have gotten to know her and the other people in this seminar over the course of the semester. My experience has honestly been too amazing to express properly in words, but I will attempt to do so briefly. This course has taught me to be a better support system to my family members as they experience illness and to my friends as their own loved ones experience health issues. It has allowed me to reflect upon and reframe my perspectives on previous losses. It has supported me as I have navigated the doubt that accompanies illness firsthand. It has taught me to think critically but also to listen. It has taught me compassion and humility, exposed me to new perspectives, and has made me a better person. It has changed the way I will approach my Harvard education, my life, and my career for the next three years but also for the rest of my life. It has been my favorite part of my first year, and you deserve to bear witness to the beauty and growth it could inspire in you.
