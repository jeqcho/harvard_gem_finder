What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I'm not sure this course will be offered again, because Anna is leaving (sad face), but this is a very worthwhile seminar if you get the chance to take it. I feel like I got a great introduction to the major problems and ideas of STS, and there are some crazy readings I have brought up in multiple conversations. It was fascinating to see how many scientific concepts we take for granted actually have been socially/historically constructed in ways that affect how we understand the world (spoiler alert: there's a lot of sexism and racism baked in, but it's not only that).

DISCLAIMER: a fair amount of the theory is very dense and I sometimes found it disappointingly useless. The class is a long morning discussion which was sometimes hard, especially if we were getting bogged down about the existence of a baseline reality. If those kinds of things don't bother you, however, or if the subject matter feels worthwhile regardless (as it was for me), then definitely take this class.
Take this if you want to learn more about the field of STS. The readings are great and insightful. The professor is also incredible.
Unfortunately Anna is leaving but she is a wonderful professor who is very knowledgeable about her field! Interesting class and I thought about a lot of questions I had never thought about before.
If you are curious about topics in science, society, technology, and in between, I would highly recommend taking this class. Anna does a fantastic job of bringing in a diverse array of readings that will challenge your assumptions regarding these topics, including how we make sense of knowledge itself (in more epistemological forays). However, there is much you can bring to class discussion regardless of what you study (and many students come from different backgrounds including STEM and the humanities), as the discussion often ends up taking many different directions. There is bound to be something of intrigue to you. The assignments also give you the opportunity to bring in ideas that interest you and to develop them into critical papers, which Anna and Eda provide excellent feedback on. Getting to work on these papers was thus extremely fulfilling.
this is a really great class to take, especially as a STEM student. you'll gain a deeper understanding of how and why questioning the scientific methods / thinking frameworks used across the sciences is important. you'll also learn about anthro theories used to understand scientific environments and more general processes like thinking, representing information, and how/why we think about objectivity and truth the way we do.
This was a great class on a really fascinating, important, and under–attended–to area at Harvard. I really think more classes should be offered that talk about STS and the production and use of scientific knowledge.
An amazing class that teaches you how to engage with theory! It is also excellent for STEM students to take in order to be more conscientious about their field and the impacts of their work.
Take it
Take this if you're interesting in talking about the relations between nature, culture, and language. The readings are sometimes very theoretical and without clear stakes ("OK, so you've diagrammed why objectivity is a myth in this way, now what?"), but surprisingly often they did seem useful to me as I'm trying to understand what science should do and what makes good science.

A low–floor, high–ceiling sort of class.
I loved this class!! I was already interested in STS, and I felt like this class gave me a foundational overview of theory. Class time is mostly discussion; I found that the class attracted a lot of engaged and open students so it was nice. Recommend if you are open–minded to questioning the culture of science, how knowledge is produced/validated, issues like how to make science more inclusive, etc. The workload was relatively light but rewarding (2 6–8 page papers and a weekly reading question).
AMAZING CLASS for ANY STEM concentrator or anyone who thinks/thought they might do science as a career. This class made me think more deeply about how the natural sciences aren't at all disconnected from the "social sciences". Amazing conversations with the peers in this class, I loved that everyone was from a different concentration/background and had different perspectives and experiences with science and the social sciences. Loved Anna's small group and large group discussion structures, especially I liked the small group activities for getting better acquainted with the reading before the large group discussion! Harvard NEEDS to offer more courses like this one!!
