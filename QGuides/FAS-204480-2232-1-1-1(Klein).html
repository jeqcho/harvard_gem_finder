What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Absolutely do not rely on this class to teach you skills for the PQE.
Be patient.
Do not let your PQE topic be restricted to the topics taught in this class, and do not delay coming up with a PQE idea based on this class schedule–– do your own external learning, because

1) having more time to hone your idea (not work on it necessarily, but think about it in depth) will be very useful
2) the class doesn't go over all quantitative methods that might be relevant for it
3) the class won't go into enough detail about the topic for it to be enough of a primer to use the method (won't help you implement it, except potentially ODEs)

Be your own judge for how much energy is worth putting into this class versus your PQE and your rotations–– personally, I put my rotations before this class because rotations/choosing a lab impacts the next 5 years more. Only near the end did it conflict with my PQE, so that was not a big issue, but I did once struggle to balance PQE final submission with the final problemset.
It's not a terribly difficult class, just get through it.
