What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course gave me a strong foundation in learning disabilities and their interactions with the educational system in the U.S. It was accessible but thorough; Dr. Gaab is excellent at unpacking the course material to make it understandable and applicable.

I highly recommend this course if you are planning on being an educator, a policymaker, a parent, or a community member who interacts with youth in any capacity. Understanding the science of learning disabilities and the policy that shaped how people with learning disabilities are educated is really crucial, and this course ensures you walk away with that knowledge.

Extremely grateful that this course was offered, and I hope it continues to be offered for future years. HGSE— and the university broadly— NEED a course like this.
A great 5th class to try something outside the College, especially if you're interested in education and disabilities.
This is an amazing class! Professor Gaab is one of the best professors I've had, and the class itself is really engaging. The assignments were not difficult but they really made me think more deeply about what we were learning about. I took this class as an undergrad but I didn't feel like it was too intimidating to be in a class with mostly grad students. I can't recommend this class enough!!!
This is an incredible class! Take this class! All of the material is super interesting, and there are so many engaging guest speakers. This course is also very engaging with all of the assignments/in class activities you do. It is also a great space to hear so many different perspectives of the educators, undergrads, administrators, etc. who take the class.
As an undergraduate, don't be intimidated by the fact that this is a graduate class! In fact, I would HIGHLY recommend you take it as an undergraduate student no matter what year you're in. The content is extremely accessible, and you will learn so so so much from your peers, especially those who entered graduate school from the workforce and have a wealth of experience to share. Dr Gaab and Eily made this course so welcoming and fulfilling, and I've feel like I've learned so much from this course that I genuinely could not have gotten elsewhere. It truly changed the way I view disability and ableism as a whole. If you have an interest in how individuals with learning disabilities see the world and how they go through daily life at all, this is one of the best possible introductory classes you can take on the topic. The assignments are also super fun!
