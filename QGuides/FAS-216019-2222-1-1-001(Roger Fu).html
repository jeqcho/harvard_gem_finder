What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is a super cool introduction to useful statistical techniques and data analysis skills in the earth sciences, but is also useful in other applied areas. This class gets you in the mindset of thinking as a better, more comprehensive researcher. However, you need an R background or coding background to succeed and find the problem sets less taxing.
This course is really cool if you are interested in data science or any type of statistical analysis technique used in earth science. The class is designed to really help you feel like you're grasping concepts well and growing in your abilities. It can also be really frustrating at times, especially if you're coming into this class with less coding experience or less math experience (21a isn't required, but taking it would have made my life easier). Roger and the TFs are really willing to help, though, and going to office hours frequently massively improves the course experience.
This class covers topics that are not covered in some other introductory statistics courses at Harvard, so it may be worthwhile to take especially if you are interested in learning more about common applications of statistical analysis in environmental research.
The largest strength of this course is that every lesson is tied to specific applications. The result is that you will walk away with a toolbox of statistical methods for environmental sciences. I would recommend this course to anyone in the environmental sciences fields, especially ESPP concentrators who would like a more rigorous statistical introduction than say stat 102/104 but want to keep it more applied to the field than say stat 110.

You learn a lot. The evaluative component of the course is fair and low stress (no exams really helps here). The course staff was very receptive to feedback, and provided additional help for students after a difficult problem set 1 really threw us for a loop. Overall, Roger and the TF's did an excellent job listening to student input. One critique: the late day policy was pretty unintuitive – late days kicked in automatically for assignments worth a negligible part of the grade (BCAs), whereas students had to request late days on psets. The result was that you could inadvertently blow your late days on BCAs.

The largest weakness of this course is that it isn't particularly engaging. I felt that the lectures were relatively dry, though I did appreciate whenever Roger tied the lectures to applications in his own research. Sections were more dry, and often it was unclear why exactly we were rehashing a certain topic or focusing in one area rather than others. The projects were pretty fun; I appreciated that students were given flexibility to research their specific interests. The course gets a little funky and quirky at the end when we talk about ML and Bayesian Statistics, that was cool.

If you need to knock out a stat requirement, this is a pretty good way to do it.
This class will teach you a lot of practical data science tools. However, this is not the most rigorous statistics class and often I found myself wanting to know more about the math behind these tools and why they work. Overall, the workload is very manageable and there are only three psets which is nice. A good class to take if you want to know more about methodology behind Earth science research.
