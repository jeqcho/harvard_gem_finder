What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class with Professor Blum! It's pretty fun studying *serious* literary methods through Dracula which is a very unserious text. Be prepared for some grunt work, though.
Even though this class is only for English concentrators, I highly recommend it. It's a great introduction to the many kinds of literary criticism, and it will make you feel like a much better critic upon completing it. Professor Blum is the absolute best and makes this class a joy to attend.
This class is excellent, I definitely recommend it. There is not too much material to get through and the course is really well paced so that you never feel overwhelmed. Also, Professor Blum is lovely, she is super enthusiastic and encouraging and she really knows how to approach each student in a way that best facilitates learning.
This class was very rewarding, and provided a lot of experience with literary criticism that I hadn't been familiar with before. I really enjoyed being able to focus largely on one book, really going into depth and exploring it from all different angles. Dracula was a great topic, it was fun and interesting, and had plenty of previous scholarship to explore.
This is a great class to fulfill the requirement. Beth's really available and a great lecturer. Would recommend.
This class offered me a new, exciting means of engaging with literature that I am so glad I was able to partake in. The big paper at the end might seem daunting, but by the time the deadline rolls around you will actually be so excited to write it – trust me.
Focusing on one text and its adaptations, related texts, and literary criticism gave me so much more insight into the world of literary criticism. I had no idea there could be so much discourse about just one text, and that authors could approach the same book through so many different lenses. I don't think I would want to take another class that is as narrow in scope as this one, but it was certainly a great way to introduce students to literary criticism, and helped open my mind to a broader world of literary interpretations.

I just wish we spent more time discussing the text of Dracula as a class. I feel like we didn't have very many in–depth discussions about the book beyond the first few chapters because our focus shifted towards reading academic critical work. Because this class has no section component, this meant that opportunities to discuss our own thoughts were quite limited.

Also — this is self–explanatory, but f you're squeamish about blood, I would not take this course.
You need to take this class in order to study English as a concentration. It's a good idea to start thinking about your final essay topic as far in advance as possible, as this really helped me make my essay the best it could be.
This is a really good course. Professor Blum is a passionate, engaging and kind teacher and 'Dracula' is a text that really keeps on giving!
