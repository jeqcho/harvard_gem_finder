Definitely make sure that you are prepared for it! The class is pretty fast paced at times and it helps to be comfortable in your knowledge of probability and models.
Fantastic course. Well organized and fascinating material. Not too much work (3 projects, 1 take–home, 4 homework assignments). Good for senior spring. Flipped Classroom.
I really enjoyed this class. The course is very interesting if you care about the intersection of biomedical data and statistics – it steps through a lot of introductory biostatistics methods with cancer biomarker data. Parmigiani really cares about all the students and you can tell he loves teaching the subject. **
This is a great class! You have a chance to learn about common models in Biostatistics, and to freely discuss Stats at a fairly high level with classmates and professor. Giovanni is a fantastic professor, very down to earth. Assignments are generally short, and straightforward if you understand the material, but at the same time they are somewhat open–ended so you have plenty of space of exploring cool aspects of the problem if you want/have time
Try to engage fully in discussions and sections by reading the lecture notes before lecture. The projects are quite fun to do with a group! Section is very helpful.
This is an okay class – I really struggled to learn the material. The material was taught by reading notes (which were very detailed) in advance of the lecture and then discussion of the concepts would follow during the lecture time. Maybe it's because my stats background wasn't super strong, but I really felt like I had a hard time learning the material from solely the notes and discussion. Section was very helpful for being more didactic and reviewing the material. Parmigiani can get lost down tangents (which are very interesting and a testament to his immense knowledge about the subject) but can sometimes result in confusion. Go to section for the code for the homeworks. Projects are slightly more difficult and require you to make decisions, but are useful for exploring/trying new things.
A great stats class, especially senior spring. Low total workload, but also very little busywork so you can get a lot out of it if you put in enough work. As a senior who has taken a lot of stats classes + compbio research, I felt like I didn't learn a ton in terms of raw content from the class (i.e. new models or estimators) but learned more about how to defend your work, describe it to people, and debate pluses and minuses of different modeling choices. If you need any other reason to take this class, it should be because of Giovanni. He is energetic, kind, and an excellent teacher who is very well–connected in the research community if you are looking for opportunities for senior thesis research (and beyond). Overall a wonderful experience!
GREAT class!! the best way honestly to fill a stats requirement. it's pretty painless. it sounds like a lot but the TF gives code out in section that basically tells you what to do, and even though TFs cycle through years Prof. Parmigiani is extremely caring about his students and his philosophy is that we shouldn't be stressed about school. It really felt that way during the class – I was never stressed about it, even when things got busy outside of class.
