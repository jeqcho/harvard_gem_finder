Very light course work; but dry lectures and unclear expectations regarding lecture participation and attendance.
Teaches you the nuts and bolts of virology, recommend if interested in the field.
In this class, you will be able to consider the interesting topic of (RNA) viral infections of (primarily human) hosts in a low stress way. Expect this course to be low workload and also not to cover as much content as certain other MCB courses.
This class will really teach you how to read a paper in great detail. The weekly workload is manageable, although the summary word count can sometimes feel insufficient to cover all the important experiments. You therefore really learn how to write concisely and how to identify the most important elements. The quizzes are annoying because the questions are sometimes tricky and require a bit of review before class, as well as some memorization. Overall, however, the class is worth it.
It's pretty much like a reading club which is fun! You get to read cool papers each week, synthesize the information into a summary, and then discuss in section. The lectures were good, but I felt like I didn't get as much out of it. You get what you put into this course, its a really great introduction into virology and I felt like I learned a lot.
This class will fulfill a requirement for you. The workload is very manageable. The lectures are not super engaging and sometimes confusing. You do get pretty good at reading papers through this class and also practicing presentation skills. Would recommend if you just need to get an MCB req out of the way.
Even though lecture quizzes were added to this course to make it more difficult, it still was straightforward and definitely a good class to take as a senior if you want an easy schedule. The subjects are interesting and I learned a lot. The weekly response papers can be cranked out in two hours and you should study some for the quizzes but they're not trying to trick you. Section is actually really useful as someone going into science after graduation and the teaching staff is great overall. Would recommend!
This class is okay work–wise, but lectures need some serious improvement. Expect to be teaching yourself lecture material. Section is very good however.
This course was amazing. For the first time in my life I feel like I can actually read and understand scientific papers, which is a super important skill for the future.
Definitely worth taking! It is one of the courses from which you get out what you put in, but education is somewhat better that way.
A great course that I would highly recommend for all students! You develop a strong skill set of how to not only understand scientific literature, but also how to write and present about them.
This is a fair course and moderate workload
Take this class seriously and you can get a lot out of it.
This is a FANTASTIC MCB course! Lecture is once a week, with the other weekly course block dedicated to a paper discussion. The paper in question you are asked to read and summarize beforehand for credit. The final was to summarize and present (in 10ish minutes) a paper of your own choosing: a very fair and doable final which is done before reading period! Overall, I found that I learned a lot in this class. Victoria (the professor) is a great lecturer and is super keyed in to the newest research happening in the field, which is super cool. Took it senior spring and did not once find it monopolizing my schedule. Would highly recommend to anyone in MCB or those on the bio side of chemistry.
