What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Very rewarding!
Start early and stay consistent!
Only write a thesis if you are sure about it and if you are willing to work hard. The thesis process is very difficult but it is also rewarding. It is one of the ways I learned the most about how to be a historian of science. I learned so much about my topic, about how to write in the history of science, about reading, about putting my work in conversation with others', and about how to use primary sources.
You should write a thesis if there is something in the history of science that you are passionate about researching and learning more about. It is a lot of work, so be prepared for that going into it, and make sure you are writing about something that genuinely piques your interest and you are prepared to dedicate the majority of your free time to.
Erik Baker runs a great thesis program, and cares a lot about his students
