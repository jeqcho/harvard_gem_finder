What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Here is some general information. Please consider the instructor and planned material before taking this class. The math department at Harvard is terribly inconsistent in regards to curriculum throughout undergraduate and graduate courses. To a degree flexibility is important, but often, it is clear that some instructors just don't care to teach a certain class.

If this course plans to teach out of Hartshorne, then you are ensured to have a somewhat decent experience. However, it is important that your instructor can help when you are confused or stuck, which will happen!
Algebraic geometry is hard. I have never heard of a postdoc/professor saying they felt like things truly 'clicked' in AG until at least the third time through. Chris is very good at teaching. He was always super available to talk about concepts, and to make the abstraction feel more concrete. Even for the technical things, he was very good at explaining the proofs and techniques. The CA Duc Vo was also an amazing CA. He helped all of us every Tuesday with difficult Hartshorne exercises. I think I learned a lot and still have maintained immense enthusiasm for AG despite this grueling process because of their support and encouragement. Thanks for a wonderful semester!
The exercises in Hartshorne are very difficult and you should work with others, but they are instructive.
Make this course the main course of your semester. This material is so cool if you want to go further in Algebraic geometry (and it can be taken multiple times )
