What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a very helpful class if you're considering doing research on culture.
Do not be afraid to speak up in discussion. First–draft thoughts and talking out loud are helpful for learning. The readings are complex so don't be afraid to speak up even if you're not entirely sure about the content.

Discussion leaders should also connect multiple themes across readings rather than reading summaries. This is a graduate level course so assume that all students have done the readings and jump into discussion quickly. Also, discussion leaders shouldn't be afraid to incorporate physical activities (e.g. moving in different parts of the room to gauge people's interest about how they felt towards the readings), soft calling (e.g. referring to people's discussion posts and asking them to elaborate), and creative activities (e.g. having students answer polls from their phones).
USE ZOTERO. Make sure to take good notes week–to–week! (There are many readings so it's easy for them to become blurred together.) Appreciate how fun the readings are. Be ready to engage deeply with readings/discussion––you and your peers co–create the class with the professor. Throughout the semester, think about how each week's themes/readings may apply/not apply to your final project.
I would encourage people who are interested in cultural sociology and are willing to do research in cultural sociology to take this course. It will be a rewarding experience.
If you have already discovered a passion for sociology and want to challenge yourself in this field, this course might be the best thing you can do.
This is a great class –– the amount of reading is intense, but you will gain a lot from it and from class discussions!
Take this course if you are interested in sociology/social theory! Prof. Lamont is amazing. The course is reasonably challenging and you learn so much. Highly recommend.
Expect a lot of reading, but it will be worth doing and discussing.
