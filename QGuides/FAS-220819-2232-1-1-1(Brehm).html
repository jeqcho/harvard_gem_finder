This class is great. Kate is a great professor, and the time commitment outside of class is pretty low. We even got to see Hamilton as a class for free, and think about how physical theater manifests in big productions like that. No prior acting experience required, and it's just a really fun time. Also definitely easy to take as an extra just–for–fun 5th class!
I don't think this particular method of acting was taught in an effective way. I think we learned a lot of very cool methods and practices. But I am still unsure of how to integrate this into my theater work. I think that more time should be spent on using what we've learned in a greater work based context. I think it takes a lot of guess work to get where exactly the instructor wants you to be. I think this is just a side effect of only having 3 hours a work to practice with someone who has done it for decades. But I think there is potential in this course. I think it can be very helpful in expanding your skill set as an actor and if you have more time it would be great for your portfolio and building your overall skill.
