What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take Darcy’s class – you’ll be so happy that you did. Everyone has stories to tell – you don’t need to be a “writer” or an English concentrator to tell them. This course will help you process what has happened in your life up to date, it will help you sharpen what you believe, and it may lead you to question what you believe as well as writing often does. Take it not only to be a better writer, but to become a more thoughtful human that is led to question what she/he believes when she has to put pen to paper.
Thank you Darcy for a wonderful semester!
LOVED THIS COURSE! For anybody who wants to write about themselves, their lives, or things happening in the world around them. Darcy is a great teacher, and he does a great job of fostering a welcoming environment in which everyone can be honest and vulnerable. The readings are so useful and a pleasure to leave, with great variation in style and content. You learn so much about narrative techniques, and emerge from the class as a better and more confident writer.
This is a fantastic and informal way to engage with and learn from some of the best nonfiction writing out there, with Darcy's thoughtful guidance steering the discussion. It's also a wonderful way to get to know a really cool group of people across all the Harvard schools.
Whether or not you enjoy reading or writing nonfiction, take this class. You learn so much about yourselves and creative writing in general and it's a joy to take
This course reaffirmed my love for and commitment to the value of the humanities in general and of creative non–fiction in particular. I learned from the carefully selected readings, from my perceptive and talented classmates, from Professor Frey, and from the extensive time–on–the–ball which comes with many hours of writing, reviewing and workshopping which comprise the core activities of this class.
I have been writing and publishing for years but without formal instruction. Although writing is largely a solitary pursuit, I appreciated the opportunity to engage with a small community of able writers. For college of graduate students committed to a lively engagement with the world by means of sincere attempts at non–fiction writing, who are determined to give of themselves in full, I could not recommend this class more highly.
This is a really wonderful class to take if you want to hone your creative writing skills and have an excuse to devote a quarter of your academic time in the term to crafting and studying personal essays. Prof. Frey is a super kind, conscientious and constructive professor, and every essay he assigned taught me something. Reading and critiquing peer writing was an extremely illuminating practice, and has informed my own writing. Highly recommend the class!
