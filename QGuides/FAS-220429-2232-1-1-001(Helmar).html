Make sure you are ready to spend some time on this class – it is fun and interesting and rewarding, but it's not an easy class you can fly through!
Take it. If you put in the work and, most importantly, MAKE THE TIME to go out, to do things, to make photographs of friends and people you care about, then this class will be life–changing for you. Patrice and Navid are a great combo. Couldn't ask for a better semester.
This class was an AMAZING introduction to photography. I came into the class not knowing anything about photography to wanting to be a "part–time" photographer. I've gained a new appreciation for the world through this medium. I now see the world in a different perspective because I feel inclined to capture everyday things to really look at them and see the beauty in them. I really enjoyed the freedom that Patrice gave us in having us submit 150 images of anything every two weeks. It really allowed the entire class to gain their own photography style and led to very fun critique days. I also really enjoyed the history of photography that we learned in both a hands–on and lecture setting, as we saw archives in the Harvard Fine Arts Library, had amazing in–class lectures, had guest speakers like Allen Frame, etc. Navid was also a great TF, who was very helpful in lab and with any question that students had. This class is truly the definition of both a hands–on and traditional academic experience.
