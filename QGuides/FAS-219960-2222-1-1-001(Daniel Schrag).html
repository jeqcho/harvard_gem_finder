What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Be careful about using this course as an opportunity to expose new ideas because of the pace and style of the conversation.
If Dan Schrag is teaching the class, definitely do not take it. Climate Justice is such an important topic, and it should be taught by someone who is at least well–read on the topic, which he is not. Schrag will act in offensive ways, which makes for an uncomfortable learning atmosphere. Additionally, there is no helpful feedback offered on your work for the entire semester, and the final had basically no instructions attached (we all received conflicting instructions when we met with him one on one as well). The course should definitely be taught again, but with a new syllabus and new instructor.
If this topic is taught again, I would recommend the course if you truly want to learn about the tensions and abstract concepts within climate justice. If you already have extensive knowledge or passion about the topic, it may be alienating to have the uncomfortable conversations in class that challenge the validity of the framework. Regardless, it is crucial that this topic is at the forefront of the climate action conversation, and that as many student can engage with it as possible.
