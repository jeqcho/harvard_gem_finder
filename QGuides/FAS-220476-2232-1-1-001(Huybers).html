This class is challenging but in a really good way. The workload is manageable, with most of the heavy lifting happening around the weeks leading up to the class that you'll lead and the final paper. That said, the readings require you to think critically to grasp the findings that are presented. I took this class my senior spring entirely as an elective and do not regret it at all. The content was engaging and interesting without being too overwhelming workload–wise.
This was an AMAZING class!! It felt accessible to people of all knowledge levels (those who are experts in agronomy, as well as those who don't normally take science classes). Professor Huybers is brilliant –– he is always willing to push you to think more critically, to question your convictions, but he's never harsh. I always looked forward to every seminar! What a highlight.
If you're looking for a 90 level ESPP seminar, this is a great choice. Professor Huybers is super friendly, while also being brilliant. You're going to learn a lot not just about the topic of the seminar, but much more broadly how to critically read and understand scientific literature. Huybers is in general one of the better explainers and synthesizers of complex concepts I've come across at Harvard, always a pleasure talking with him.
This class was so fun and informative! I never thought about crops at all before this semester, and now I feel like I'm equipped to intelligently discuss the future of agriculture as it relates to climate change and food security. Prof Huybers is super approachable and is really good at explaining complicated concepts in an understandable way. Giving a presentation on a topic of your choice was a large part of the work for this class, so be prepared for a ~2 week sprint and plan your semester accordingly. Overall, this was probably my favorite course this semester!
Prof Hyubers is an incredibly knowledgeable guy that you can learn a lot from! Take this class to better your presenting skills and read some really insightful papers.
taaaaaake this class if is offered. Professor Huybers is the best Professor ever and he is so knowledgeable about all topics related to food security that it is very empowering and exciting to talk to him about the topics we read about. The papers we read are interesting and the presentation/final paper is a cool way to learn a lot about a specific topic.
Great course to learn about scientific reading and writing, as well as food security and crops. Prof. Huybers is a fantastic professor who knows a lot about this field and is clearly passionate about it.
This class is a great introduction to the influence of climate change on food security. Papers were generally very interesting and the presentation and paper allowed us the opportunity to explore a topic in great depth. Discussions were very engaged and I emerged from the course with a newfound interest in the topic and greater appreciation for food security and climate science in general.
Huybers is great and really cares about each topic we cover. He is also incredibly knowledgeable on basically everything related to climate, crops, and food security. My only critique was the grade uncertainty. Everyone was very unsure of what grade they would get in the class because he didn't return assignments until the last minute. But still would recommend this course. 10/10.
The focal point of this class is a presentation on a chosen topic related to climate/agriculture –– if you are self–motivated and willing to devote a lot of time to this project, you will get a lot out of this class
