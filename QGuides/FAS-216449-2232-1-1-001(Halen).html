Jenn, the instructor, is wonderful. I wish I had more one–on–one time with her. She created a very welcoming class environment.

The CA, Tabitha, was very impatient and uncooperative in explaining her grading decisions. Borderline disrespectful. She validated all of her grading decisions in that she was using a rubric offered to her by the instructor, one that wasn’t also offered to students. I can’t say for sure that it even existed. She’d also claim that those details of the unpublished rubric were discussed multiple times in class, though they never were. She also waited until the final deadline to post all of our grades, so that only amplified my concerns. I was uncomfortable with my peer, a fellow undergraduate, grading my subjective materials and marking me down at her whim. I was also uncomfortable simply initiating a conversation with her about it because she’d entirely and disrespectfully shut it down, like my qualms were invalid.
Its a sensational class
This is one of the finest classes I have enrolled in at Harvard.

The greatest strength of Gov 94BF was its instructor: Jennifer Halen is one of the best professors I have interacted with at this school (and trust me: I have met a lot of them). Few members of our university's faculty are able to simultaneously provide students with meaningful support and share important lessons of great significance. Through her ability to provide valuable advice to students, dedicate considerable time to office hour meetings, facilitate important discussions in the classroom, and more, Professor Halen is a one–of–a–kind. Harvard is very lucky to have her in its ranks.

Another strength of the class was its TF, Tabitha Escalante. Tabitha was very helpful at replying to students' questions expeditiously, and she was effective at providing thorough feedback. I also appreciated the course materials: as an individual with little exposure to literature on policing, abolition, and the American prison system, I felt like I learned a lot through the readings listed on the syllabus as well as the classroom conversations we had about them.

In short, take this class!
