Take this course! It's a better alternative than a traditional Expos 20 class and helps you build a strong foundation in academic writing!
Firstly, Statistics:
I spent a total of 61:37 hours across 11–12 weeks on the class. This figure includes everything from writing, researching, lectures and more done for this class. I kept a strict time tracking schedule and these were my results for each week: (The data starts from Monday of each week and the first recorded week is the week of Feb 13. 5:29h means 5 hours and 29 minutes were spent during that week on this class. )
6 h, 4:25h, 8:53h, 2:30h, 8:20h, 0h (school break), 5:29h, 1:53h, 12:06h, 7:49h, 4:09h. I haven't finished the revision of my final paper yet, which will add another 8 hours or more probably. As you can see, some weeks are tough, others are a breeze. Be prepared for this.

28:29 hours were spent on writing, 19:13 spent on lectures, 5:21 on readings, 2:35 on the job cover letters, 3:35 spent on reviewing, preparing for class, and annotations, 0:28 on conferences. More time was spent on some miscellaneous tasks. My data on the conference times is incorrect, im not sure where I made a mistake.

Review:
I feel like this was this class was a disappointment. I believe we should have focused less on the content of the readings we did and more on the writing technique of the readings.

Although I had a fun time in class and discussion, I didn't learn much about _writing_! I expected more workshops and writing analysis instead of reading comprehension! For a majority of readings, our focus was on WHAT the author(s) were saying. I feel that we should have been focusing on HOW authors were conveying their thoughts. Am I mistaken here? Why didn't we analyze the O'Connoll piece (a piece about a doctor dedicating their life to helping the homeless) as an example of, say, journalistic writing instead of focusing on the content. Don't get me wrong, I cried during that reading, but now that I look back at my semester, I feel as though these were missed opportunities. How did that author manage to make me cry? Give me answers to THAT instead of making me write a random response and write some dumb thing on another classmate's post (That classmate, of course, also couldn't care less about reading my response). I would have loved to discuss how Crawford, an author we read for unit 1 on the value of manual labor, goes from discussing separation of thinking and doing in modern jobs to slickly including some Marx in there.


I was the wide–eyed freshman who wanted to improve their non–existant writing by taking BOTH of the Studio sequence classes. Boy was I wrong! I believe you should treat the expos class as a College requirement and just take one expos class and be done with it. You will not learn significant writing from this class. My complaint with expos 10 was that I didn't learn much and that we just hung out in class with nothing done. So, I thought maybe salvation lay in studio 20. I thought maybe they wanted to make a very gentle intro and that studio 20 would actually pick up the pace and teach actual writing. Nope.
The sad thing is this review will be buried with all of the SEPARATE reviews of the other sections of expos studio 20. Moreover, if you are looking through this review, that mean you probably have taken expos 10 already, so tough luck. You have to take another expos class. If so, then be a realist and take this class anyways. You paid the price of taking expos 10 by having to take two semesters of it. Now, at least benefit from that and make your second semester workload lighter.
