What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Start thinking about your final paper topic early on so that you can focus on the aspects of course readings that will be most useful.
I would recommend this class – it really is what you make of it. If you want to get a lot out of it, put effort into the readings and engage with the material.
I would definitely take this course as it had such a rich dive into political theory involving capitalism, with a deep focus on leftist thinkers that usually aren’t focused on in Harvard classrooms.

The reading, however, is very intensive and dense! I would recommend having taken a few upper level humanities courses before taking this one. I often found myself spending 10hrs+ per week to fully comprehend and digest the deep texts that Professor Forrester gave us. This is not a slight on the course or the Professor — just ensure that your reading and writing skills are to the level that this course demands of you, especially since it is open to both undergraduate and graduate students.

Professor Forrester is so insightful and I loved hearing her commentary every week on the texts and topics at hand. I also think this discussion was illuminated by the guest speakers we had. We had many guest speakers with 2 commentators — one PHD student and one professor — who applied a critique to the texts at hand. I thought this was especially interesting when analyzing the texts as we had multiple perspectives.

I thought the final paper was interesting. It was an open ended ~25 page assignment that had to touch on the course themes. I have not got feedback yet, but it is 80% of our grade so make sure that you are committed to writing a longer piece. The other part of our grade (20%) is course attendance/participation.

Overall, please take this course. I hope that they offer this course in other academic years as students deserve to learn the complexities of political and economic theory on this subject, especially with such an accomplished professor like Professor Forrester!
