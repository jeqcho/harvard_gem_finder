What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is difficult and in a sense accelerated given that it does not meet every day. You are expected to retain all the information from Latin 1, so after Latin 1 ends, don't forget Latin forever! If you have never taken a Latin course before, study over J–Term. Make sure to allocate study time every week for this course. I learned a lot in this course, but this isn't an easy language course. There are a lot of terms to memorize, and most importantly, understand the grammar. Simply memorizing rules will not suffice. Really try to understand the Latin language and morphology. Tests and exams are difficult, so please memorize all the terms to save yourself time in the days leading up to exams. Make sure to also memorize all the dictionary entries ––– there are a lot, and they build up over the semester. Essentially, don't slack off. You will learn a lot.
This class is not very good. If you're doing it for a language requirement, go ahead. If you actually want to learn Latin, you're better off on your own.
The class is graded pretty fairly and is probably one of the better options if you want to get to know the Classics or get your language requirement out of the way. As long as you pay attention in class, take decent notes, and study the vocabulary, you should be good.
Latin 2 is a fantastic class! It is taught with lecture and section, and Ivy is a fantastic lecturer who truly cares for her students. However, your experience is also pretty dependent on your TF; Vivian was not a fantastic TF and she seemed distracted sometimes, but she still mostly knew her stuff and cared for her students. If you've taken 1–2 years of Latin in high school, you should be well prepared for this class. For those who know a bit more Latin, we cover grammatical concepts up until (not including) subjunctives.
Don't take Latin 2 unless you need it for your concentration/secondary/citation.
