This is ***by far*** my favorite course of freshman year. Professor Hart's dry wit and incredible academic experience on this subject is truly unique. This course has persuaded me to study economics. If you are interested in topics like climate change, universal healthcare, or pandemic policy, rank this seminar first.
If you are the kind of person to participate actively in discussions, have strong (or at least vocal) opinions about government issues, and are willing to defend your ideas openly, then this seminar is for you. I really enjoyed the time we spent together, and have learned significantly more about how we can better evaluate government policy and what the role of government is in today's society.
Be prepared to engage deeply with course content that can be long but rewarding. Professor Hart has some great insights about the way that the economy works and you will learn a lot from this class.
Professor Hart is a great teacher, and you can learn a lot from the insights he shares in class. Long readings and weekly essay can be a grind, but in my opinion worth it if you enjoy discussing economic and social issues.
This was my favourite class I took freshman year! If you are interested in economics or government I would whole heartedly recommend this course and Professor Hart is a great professor. Definitely not easy work wise compared to other freshman seminars (normally 3/4 readings a week plus a 2–3 page response on those readings) however all the topics were really interesting and it meant that the class was normally a 2 hour debate about that topic so much more engaging than a lot of other classes.
Very good seminar class! If you are interested in economics or government, this is a wonderful course to take. Professor Hart is obviously an expert and is a great discussion facilitator, and the two–hour classes are always engaging and enjoyable. Discussions may involve either understanding applications of the theory behind, or (as expected) policy discussion regarding ideas either considered or implemented in the readings or in other countries (students are always welcome to and do share their own knowledge and opinions, which is a great way to expose oneself to new ideas). Both are very fun and especially policy discussion is a good way to increase one's breadth of ideas and learn from peers and the instructor.

Course content includes basic theory in econ, as well as different applications. The readings involve many papers published in economics journals, which is a good introduction to the literature in the field; also, there are many relevant real–world scenarios or ideas (e.g., the coronavirus, applications of behavioral economics, etc.). Also, we read some of Professor Hart's works in the final class (due to finishing previous class's material), a very cool conclusion to the course!
TAKE IT!!! Great class and a great professor!!!
I learned a lot from the readings and really enjoyed engaging with my classmates! Professor Hart is very funny and contributes a lot to the discussion!
