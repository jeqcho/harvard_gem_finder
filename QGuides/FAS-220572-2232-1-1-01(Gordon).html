What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Worthwhile course, especially to broaden awareness of texts in political thought.
These are issues and texts foundational to anyone interested in a rigorous, contextualized understanding of democracy, rationality, public discourse, and relation of individuals/groups to the state in the modern west.
This is a graduate course. As a undergrad, you should probably only take this class if you have a requisite background (which will probably look like having taken Social studies 10a/10b and/or some Continental philosophy classes). Know that your final paper will be completely self–directed.

That being said, I loved this class. Readings were eclectic but all tied back into to each other in some way. We read philosophy/theory texts but also hybrid works of sociology, history, etc... Hannah Arendt's Lectures on Kant's Political Philosophy changed my life. It's a good, good time.
