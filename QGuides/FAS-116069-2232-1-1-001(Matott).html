This class is very interesting and if you’re interested in anatomy or medicine its a great way to study those things. It is a good amount of work especially leading up to exams but if you start studying material as you learn it it is manageable
As you learn about the different anatomical structures, make it a point to study them on yourself and really pay attention to how you use your body! Helps you study and appreciate the intricate ways we function!
Be warned, this is a HARD class. You absolutely MUST STUDY EVERY WEEK for multiple hours, not just before the exam. I know a lot of professors say this when it isn't really true, but it is true for this course. I did very well in this class and I loved it, which are not unrelated – if you don't actively want to study the material, you will fail the exams, as it is entirely memorization based (I think I memorized about ~800 individual facts for the second exam alone). I got A's on my exams, but I also studied 40+ hours for each, because I genuinely found the material interesting. Also be warned that this class may consume your other courses if you let it, I wouldn't take it concurrently with another high time commitment class.

All that aside, this was such an incredible course. I was afraid to take it because of the warnings about the difficult exams, etc. but I am so glad I went for it. I learned so much about the human body and it piqued my interest in a possible medical career. This was easily one of my favorite courses so far at Harvard despite the time commitment. The teaching staff was also incredibly friendly and accessible – every lecture had a nice sense of levity and humor interspersed among the ~70 slides. Also!! Go to at least one cadaver lab, was a super cool experience and less scary than I thought it would be.
The class was incredibly interesting and worthwhile, but is a LOT of work. Future students need to know that you will need to start studying a week in advance and exam questions could come from literally any slide. Jo is a great lecturer but you'll need to dedicate time outside of lecture to sit with the information from each lecture, as there's a lot. Exams are weighted highly, the second exam is killer and needs lots of memorization but most exams are close to the practice ones. All in all, I'm glad I took it but I needed to spend a lectures–worth of time after each meeting to review what I was just caught and take adequate notes.
This course is what is advertised – an anatomy course framed by evolutionary concepts. I really liked the evolutionary component. However, this class was really difficult and had so much information to the point where sometimes we wouldn't finish the lecture content in class and had to watch videos. A lot of studying needs to be done outside of class –– I didn't plan well for this and I think my grade suffered. It's an interesting course for sure, but be prepared to put in a lot of outside legwork memorizing.
It’s a great class, smaller than LS2 while still being intensive and enjoyable. I learned a lot, and the exams are definitely very memorization based.
This is a very fun class if you want to learn about the human body. You learn about its evolution and development as well, and you see the anatomy in cadaver labs at the med school. However, do not take this class if you aren't prepared to study a lot! There is a ton of memorization, and might be the most challenging class I've ever taken.
This is a hard course that covers a ton of material, but it's great and has left me with a MUCH better understanding of anatomy. Also, the teaching staff is wonderful, supportive, and make the lectures fun.
