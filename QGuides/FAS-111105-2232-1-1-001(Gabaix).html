It is very mathematical and less conceptual. If you want to be a quant then maybe sure take it, but it's not going to be a class to teach you the basics of securities and investment strategies. It gets very detailed into bond pricing, option pricing, hedging portfolios etc
This class is tough, but the psets are fair. The psets definitely got easier as the semester went on. I took 10–12 hours in the begining of the semester, and by the end, the psets took 3–5 hours. The best way to study is to do practice problems. But keep in mind that the psets are slightly harder than the exams.
This class is cool and interesting stuff, but lectures are not taught the best. It is pretty mathematically involved, so having math 1b prep would be helpful. Tests are also pretty disconnected from the lectures, so make sure you do the practice tests so you know what to expect.
Lectures are painful to sit through: Pritsker is monotonous and Gabaix is all over the place
Stay on top of PSETs, attend lecture, and before exams do and understand all the practice tests and you'll be chilling
To be honest the lectures were not that helpful. You probably can learn better by watching YouTube videos yourself. But Professor Pritsker cares about student a lot! It's probably helpful to make use of the office hours and ask him questions.
Ec1723 was interesting even for me as a philosophy concentrator. The prereqs arent that important if you've had HS stats and ec10ab. Both pritzker and gabaix knew the material but were not great at teaching.My biggest qualm about the class is that upsets are never graded on time and are worth nothing and the final is worth 75%. So if you bomb it you're done...
Course content is great. Xavier in particular is a funny and knowledgeable instructor, and the course is much more interesting when he is teaching, but the first half with Matt is pretty disorganized. Psets are not always clear.
If you like finance and math this is a good class
One of the best finance courses on Harvard's campus – not too difficult, but not too simple either. Would highly recommend if you are interested in asset pricing/understanding the underlying mechanisms of all types of asset markets.
Class has very few support resources and is very dry. You don't learn a whole ton without already knowing about the material beforehand.
The class moves quickly, so if you are confused about something it's important to go to office hours or read the textbook to get a better understanding rather than waiting until the midterm to catch up. Make sure you meet the prerequisites, even better if you've taken linear algebra. Be aware this is not a finance course from the institutional perspective ; rather it's about arbitrage, portfolio construction, asset pricing, etc.
