This is a very very good class to take if you have room for an elective in your schedule. The content is extremely interesting and engaging, while not being too challenging. Professor Abbate has a very careful, intentional way of speaking, and everything she says during lecture feels extremely relevant and interesting. The lectures covered both the history of opera, as well as the effects of different stagings and analysis of short sections of opera. The assignments for this class come in the form of 6 listening responses and 4 portfolio essays. The listening responses were short (300 words) papers about a section of 1–2 minutes of opera music where we wrote about what we heard in the music and libretto, and how this tied to what we were covering in lecture. They were short and chill –– they had a time limit of an hour but I usually spent about half an hour on them. The portfolio essays were longer (1000 words) essays analysing a specific opera or about our personal experience writing opera. They were also nice, they usually took me 2–4 hours, and they were pretty fun to write! Other than that, there were about 30 pages of reading and 30–60 minutes of opera viewing assigned each week, but I could usually get the main ideas without doing the whole reading. I really enjoyed sections as well –– they were a good way to remember everything from lecture, discuss upcoming assignments, and have cool discussions about opera. All in all, I really enjoy this course, and I strongly recommend it to anyone with even the slightest interest in opera who has space for a chill and fun class in their schedule.
Take this class! It provides an excellent introduction to opera that requires no background knowledge and a great way to round out your liberal arts education.
This class was absolutely wonderful! There is no final or midterm, just a series of listening assignments and four, relatively short essays that are quite doable. The assignments are just enough to keep your understanding in check while not being overly mundane or redundant by any means. My appreciation and understanding for opera has grown immensely from taking this class, and I actively looked forward to lectures and sections because of the informative yet interactive teaching style. Professor Abbate is incredibly knowledgeable about the topic (an understatement honestly, as she personally witnessed some of the great operatic moments in the 20th century), and getting to regularly watch great operas in class, for assignments, and even free tickets for live performances outside of class was such a treat. Sections were also very fun – we were allowed to truly express any sort of comment or thought that came through our minds related to the opera, no matter how controversial or possibly off–topic it might be (we were literally told that going "on a tangent" was completely okay or totally allowed), and that free exploration of thought compared to a regular guided discussion actually helped me understand the operas much more, since I was able to get my specific questions answered and considered than a strict curriculum that might not relate to what most people end up not understanding. Truly a great class. If you have ANY interest at all in learning more about opera or classical music, I highly recommend :)
