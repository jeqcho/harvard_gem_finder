Great class but definitely not the easiest. The weekly readings and responses can be tedious but overall the lectures are super interesting, especially when they are peer–led. I’d recommend this class if you’re particularly interested in Buddhist culture or religion in general, less so if you’re looking for a traditional architecture class.
Take it! Especially if you have any remote interest in Buddhism –– taking the perspective of art and architecture is a very refreshing and interesting way to learn about Buddhist religion and culture.
If you are interested in Buddhism, TAKE THIS CLASS. However, if you are not, do NOT. It is not a gem in the sense that there is a solid amount of work, and the exams were on the harder side. Yet, the content was so interesting, and it was fun to learn for me – someone interested in this topic. The sections are super cool where you go to the art museum to see the artifacts irl. 8.75/10 recommend only if you are interested in Buddhism. ALSO, it focuses more on the architecture than I expected, which was not bad, but I wish we had gone into the religion itself more.
A great way to learn about Buddhism
There is so much specific knowledge and recognition required by the quizzes and pretty much every aspect of the class. It's not about the art as much as it's about the narrative these artworks convey
Take this class if you are interested in Buddhist monuments and ideology. The content is extremely interesting and you get to explore the history of Buddhist art across many countries and civilizations. However, it does have a bit of a workload. There are weekly response papers, 3 midterm quizzes, and a short final research project.
This course moves fast for a GENED and may be somewhat difficult to get up to speed with without background in Buddhism and/or art history. There is a steady flow of assignments — be aware that the three quizzes were scheduled OUTSIDE of normal class and section times, and the week where you serve as discussion leader requires a substantial amount of work. Outside of that, the reading responses are very open–ended and can be written quickly if you pay enough attention in lecture to catch on to the general themes of the week. Despite the somewhat heavy workload for a GENED, grading felt very forgiving as long as you completed the assignments. TFs Joie and Akarsh were great; I learned the most in office hours and highly recommend stopping by before quizzes — that'll make your life a lot easier.
I really really loved this class. It's not an "easy" gened in the sense that you have to work and study to familiarize yourself with the monuments and concepts, but you write only one response paper a week and the TF reads and responds to them all personally. The course staff is actually so wonderful. Take this class if you are at all interested in Buddhism and want to invest a bit of your energy to learn so so much.

Absolutely go on the field trip if it is offered again!
A cool lens through which to learn about history and culture and a glimpse into what art historians do. Can feel a bit too niche at times. Overall not a bad way to fulfill aesthetics gened. The course staff cares a lot about the course and the class is pretty small, so if you just want to hide and get the requirement over with it might not be for you. I found actually getting more into the material made it go by more painlessly. Weekly reading responses are always a pain, but overall workload is fair (only one longer paper at the end). If you just relax and listen to the lectures (the profs are great) and remember stuff fairly well you don't need to study for quizzes or anything.
This might be the best class I have taken at Harvard. I concentrate in something completely unrelated to Buddhism or architecture, yet this class was so enlightening, formative and fun that I would recommend it to everyone. I went from knowing nothing about Buddhism to having a good grasp of its basic concepts and its imagery. The professors and TFs are wonderful and I always looked forward to the lectures and sections. We went to the Harvard Art Museums very often and examined Buddhist art from up close. The classroom atmosphere was great. We went on a very fun field trip to a pagoda in Western Massachusetts; it was one of the highlights of my semester. Take this class. It is not too much work and it is very rewarding.
