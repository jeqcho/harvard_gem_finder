this is one of those courses that can literally only be found at Harvard – world class guest lecturers and amazing patient stories being told firsthand! truly changed my life and is definitely the most clinical facing class you can take as an undergraduate at Harvard. Len and David are great professors and very genuine/down to earth – would count this class as a "must take" before you graduate
Hands down, the BEST course at Harvard. Every week during lecture, a new physician provides an overview of our weekly topic/disease (which just so happens to be their specialty) along with a discussion of how stem cells can be leveraged to treat this disease. Afterwards, a patient with that particular disease shares their experiences living with the disease and how the treatment process is like for them (we also have ample time to ask the patient questions). In this way, this course covers a broad range of specialities within medicine and stem cell therapeutics in addition to the BEST THING THAT THIS COURSE HAS TO OFFER, which of course, is its weekly patient perspectives. As a result, class is SUPER exciting and you'll never want to skip lecture!
I've taken 41 classes at Harvard over the last 7 years and this was the best by far. Cannot recommend this class enough to anyone interested in medicine or stem cell biology!

Even if you're not going to be a doctor, the lessons you can learn from patients in this class will help you through your life. Such a special experience! One of the best science classes I've taken at Harvard! Could not recommend more.

I would 100% recommend this class to anyone who has any inclination to pursue a career in healthcare. The professors who lead the course are both physician–scientists and extremely accomplished in their fields. They are so interesting to learn from and bring such incredible passion for scientific medicine. In addition, each week you get to learn from a guest lecturer who is an expert about whichever disease we are focusing on that week. Then, the patient interview portion of the class is invaluable. You get to hear incredible stories of struggle, loss, survival, and triumph in the context of illness. It is very inspiring and made me want to be a doctor every time I left the class. The opportunity to develop a novel, testable hypothesis is so unique and challenging, but very manageable. If you're interested in medicine in any capacity, TAKE THIS COURSE!!

If you are interested in a career in medicine, translational science, or biotech, take this class. You will learn from a group of highly acclaimed, renowned leaders in several fields across medicine, and more importantly, you will have the chance to learn from patients who have been affected by diseases with promising stem cell therapies. Before I took this class I was not sure I wanted to be a doctor. Now, I am immediately applying to post–bacc programs when I graduate because I know for sure that I want to be a doctor.
Pretty cool format, with two leading experts in stem cell therapies leading the class. And they're both very personable, to add, which makes this class much more reasonable. A paper each week helps with internalizing scientific writing, and then you get to observe genuine patient experiences with illness. Grading pretty chill, too. Def recommend.
The good part of this class is that it is the only one in the SCRB department to expose students to the human side of the science we study. This is very important, but I think it could be done with more sensitivity—we received basically zero guidelines for what questions were and were not appropriate to ask patients, and what reactions were and were not appropriate to display during their talks.

Section is also strong, and gives useful experience in understanding and critiquing scientific papers.

Unfortunately, when it comes to assignments, the class does not set its students up for success. The professors admitted that they would grade papers on the topics they study more harshly (a student was told that he “picked the wrong topic” when he picked a topic that one of the professors studies). Also, they don’t tell you this, but you won’t see the comments from the professors (who are your primary graders) on your assignments unless you ask for it. I asked to see their comments on my midterm and one of the comments was literally the opposite of what was written on the feedback that I did receive on Canvas, and their comments also included several things that were not mentioned at all in my Canvas feedback.

Overall, I’m disappointed in this class, and I wish that it focused more on ensuring a fair and enriching learning experience for its students than on talking about the career accomplishments of the professors.
