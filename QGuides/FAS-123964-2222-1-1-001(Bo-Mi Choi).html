TLDR: You should only take this class if you are a Social Studies concentrator. It is much worse than 10A, despite the fact that the thinkers are much more interesting. This, however, is contingent on who teaches the lectures next year â€” I hope for the sake of this major that I love so much that it is not Jonathan Hansen again.

That being said, I have a few thoughts, since I really care about Social Studies and want to make sure people keep doing it. If you're looking at this comment, anyways, you're either a) really neurotic about this class or b) deciding if you want to continue in social studies:

1) The texts are a mess. There is no central theme to this semester, nor does i t really feel like we get to put the thinkers in dialogue with each other (and sometimes they write about such different things that it is just impossible). SS10b needs to figure out if it wants to be a serious class in analytical social theory, a historiography of its thinkers, or an introduction to critical analysis. I speak as a a queer person of color that considers themselves to be quite progressive: the course's goals are muddled, and my learning suffered a lot. Spending a week on Fanon, for example, is a bizarre choice to me given that we spent literally a single week on many thinkers that (imo) matter a lot more even to the decolonial canon.

2) Teacher has really suffered since 10A. First semester, I was excited to come to lectures by Armitage. This semester, it has been less–than–stellar lectures by Hansen where the entire lecture relies on 3 slides that have nothing to do with what he talks about (there was a picture of Hitler up for 20 min during the Nietzsche lecture), and a splattering of hit–or–miss guest lecturers. Many of my friends have stopped coming to classes, and I personally know people switched out of social studies in week 1 or 2.

3) The huge variation in how section policies are done is actually insane. Ii is incredible to me (and awful for the record of social studies) that one section has "estimated paper grades" for students and then allows them to turn it in for 3 days after the FAS deadline (ergo, the teacher has already assigned them a grade). Personalized tailoring. and attention is and should be a hallmark of this course, but given that we get arbitrarily assigned to sections, this makes the class experiences so varied. Make sure that you know you can switch sections if they're wack.

4) Readings need to be condensed, and there needs to be more time spent on each reading. Going through some thinkers in a week (with 200–300 pages consistently) is an injustice. Social Studies says that 10b should be our highest priority course, but it should also be realistic about what students can and want to accomplish. There are some ridiculously difficult texts in this semester (Frankfurt School, French philosophers, etc), and feeling like we have to rush through hundreds of pages is insane; many other classes devote a semester to a few chapters of these works.
