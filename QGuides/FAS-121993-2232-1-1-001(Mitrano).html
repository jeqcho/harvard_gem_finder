This class is not as terrible as people say, it's just a lot of work writing up the papers. Considering there are only 2 papers and a final presentation, it really isn't that stressful. The experiments can be fun and it's great getting to know the teaching staff more closely. I would just warn that the difference between the 'hard' experiments and the 'easy' ones is quite considerable. I would not recommend doing a hard experiment if you are unfamiliar with the theory.
If you don't look at it as a requirement, despite the amount of work it requires, you'll find it very easy to enjoy the class and have fun with it.
I highly recommend the ultrafast laser spectroscopy experiment. It is the perfect balance between insightful and nontrivial data analysis, and a well structured experimental setup that provides intuition for what the degrees of freedom are in an optical setup and what observables you can use to optimize that setup. Overall, the experiment was extremely enjoyable, had rich theory, and was a blast to work on!
This course will give you a new appreciation for the joy of experimental science (and I say this as a theorist).
You have to take it for honors, but I honestly recommend it for anyone who is thinking about doing experimental science in general. Really really helps with conducting experiments and writing scientific papers. Do leave your schedule empty since this class will take up a LOT of your time, with 8hrs of experiments (or more) per week, but definitely a for–sure recommendation. Instructors are super helpful and friendly so :)
This is a fun but hard and time–consuming class! Take it if you want to become an expert in 3 different sub–topics in physics, and if you are self–motivated but willing to ask for help. And start writing your papers early!
I wouldn't recommend this class if you don't have to take it, but it's probably a Physics honors requirement. Definitely take it with a friend and someone you work well with otherwise it is hell. There will be times when the experiments don't work well and you don't know why, you ask the course staff and if they have time to help you (usually they don't right away) they might also now know, so it can get frustrating.
This course is required for all physics students wanting to graduate with honors. You probably will take it if you're reading this. Some of the paper writeups will not be pleasant. With that said, the instructional quality (in the year I took it) was extremely high. The feedback (even when negative) on papers was extensive and thoroughly useful. You will have a much better idea of how to carry out experimental physics after taking the course, and you will be a better scientific writer. Even if you only care about theory, you will have the intangible benefit of understanding what the experimentalists actually do to validate your models.
it's a lot of work, and the physics is really hard
Due to the two 4–hour lab blocks per week, this class takes up a lot of time, even if you don't spend much time on it outside of the lab. That being said, it's an excellent opportunity to work with lab equipment in a high faculty–student ratio setting. If you take it with a friend, it's a pretty fun and instructive course.
With Jenny, Matteo, Jieping, and Joe, this course is one of the best Harvard physics experiences ever. They are all the nicest, most caring teaching team ever and really funny too :) I would highly recommend taking the course with them!! Just make sure that you have enough undergrad–level prep in physics so that reading papers from the 1950s and before (the pre–QFT era) is straightforward, so that you don't struggle with the theory. I would also say that you should be thoughtful about maybe taking the course with a pre–designated partner with whom you work well with, since that will be probably the biggest component to your success in the course.
Make sure you have a good partner.
