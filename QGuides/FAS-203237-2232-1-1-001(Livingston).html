Comments
it is an absolutely wonderful class and I highly recommend it. absolutely do all the readings and fully mark them up/prepare them and be very attentive in class especially to what's emphasized, as that is crucial to doing well on the quizzes
Definitely give it a try and do not be put off if you are in Classics/Classical languages just because it's Medieval! The instructor absolutely prepares you for whatever your next step in classics is, you just gain another dimension to your language study.
Very enjoyable!!!
It might take a couple to a few weeks to find what note–taking and studying strategies work for you. At first, I kept my notes in a notebook, not on the text, but this didn't prove to be all that helpful. Having a way to differentiate between the notes you took while preparing the text and the notes you took during class would also be very helpful.
I was originally planning on taking Classical Latin, but ended up taking Medieval – and that was a good choice. This course exposes you to a diverse range of authors, writing styles, ideas, and vocabulary that I had never previously experienced. It was a bit of a leap to leave the Classical authors I was familiar with by name, but I gained a lot by reading authors from a time period I hadn't previously thought a lot about. Aside from simple translation, this class discussed much about broad trends/a historical overview of the medieval world . . . again, something that isn't often taught, but is very good to know (and interesting!).
