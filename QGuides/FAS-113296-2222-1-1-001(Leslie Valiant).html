Lectures are very difficult to follow, probably because slides are just scanned copies of handwritten notes that are extremely difficult to read. There was not much flow between lectures and lessons and many times it was not clear how different lectures connected to each other. The problem sets were very long and had barely any connection with what we learn in lecture. Finishing the problem sets required going to office hours and reading the textbook (even then, we never learned the necessary strategies to solve such problems, or were given any examples to work through in class). The project had no concrete guidelines, leaving us guessing as to what is to be expected. Annie and Yash really did the most and were accessible and great. The class overall is pretty chill because there are very few assignments, but I found myself spending 20+ hours on each assignment. If you take this class you need to get started on psets early and go to office hours. I think there were 30ish students enrolled, but from the lecture recording it seemed only 5 people showed up. Take with caution, the material is honestly very cool and interesting, but this class did not generate any enthusiasm for the subject, and I don't think I actually learned much from this class
