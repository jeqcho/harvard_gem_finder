Make a serious effort to fully understand each lab and answer all of the lab questions.
TAKE THIS CLASS. It was top two of the best classes I have ever taken at Harvard
This is a great introductory electronics course for physicists looking to understand laboratory instruments for experimental measurements. For those who want to take Physics 123A/B in the future, but feel like they may need more preparation before taking a the more challenging course, I would highly recommend this class. While this is a newer course, Prof. Morii and Kathryn were great at making the learning material engaging and fun; this class is very much hands–on learning, and you will get much more enjoyment and reward from really trying to understand the concepts discussed and apply them to your own projects.
This class is a great way to get introduced to electronics, without being too overwhelmed. The lack of HW is awesome, as the course is designed to be self–contained in class time. I did feel that the exams were pretty hard compared to what we did in class.
GEM. You will learn a lot of useful skills and have fun, without having to worry too much about grades or anything. It is all about the circuits and getting to know them. Best class I took this semester.
You should ABSOLUTELY take this course if you want to be an experimentalist! It teaches you a lot of essential skills about the lab and how data is collected. It is also very helpful for physics 191. The small class size makes it very intimate and very easy to get help. There is no outside work but the concepts are very difficult and lab time is exciting!
