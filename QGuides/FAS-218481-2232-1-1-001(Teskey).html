Take this class for a good introduction to many different types of poetry across cultures and time periods. The course is not really as intense as a normal creative writing workshop but there are some creative elements to it. Overall its not too stressful and quite interesting.
Be ready to write your own poetry and complete your own translations of poems!
I'm very glad to have taken this freshman seminar. It was a great opportunity to become more well–versed in poetry, both as a creator and enjoyer. Our weekly assignments involved reading a dozen or so poems and posting prompted responses to them, with the occasional inclusion of translating poems in different languages or writing or own. It was not a heavy courseload, but easy to procrastinate until the end of the weekend. Overall, I'd recommend taking this seminar if you're in interested in reading and discussing poems of different cultures and genres.
This course is dense and does not do any poems we read justice. It is interesting for sure, but we never had any engaging discussions in class. We are also reading translated poems without know the original languages, which gives ways to so many misinterpretation and over–analysis of the translation. And we never got into translation theory. So it just felt very disrespectful toward the original poem and the author. I think I learned more about a similar set of poems in AP Lit in high school
This class is interesting and gives you exposure to a lot of different kinds of poetry. Not too much work but interesting to experience!
It was a good class, although the discussions were sometimes not that engaging and sometimes felt restrictive
