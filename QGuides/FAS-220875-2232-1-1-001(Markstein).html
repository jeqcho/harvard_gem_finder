A decently enjoyable class. I liked the subject matter and the logical way it introduced complementary concepts throughout the course. Also the weekly engagement with primary literature was a good way to get used to reading and discussing papers. Not a huge amount of weekly reading in general, and the problem sets were pretty straightforward and decently interesting instead of being unnecessarily hard busy–work. It was a new class this semester and as such was a little disorganized at times, but I'm sure this will be rectified in future iterations.
If you have taken MCB60 and want to elaborate on those concepts and apply them to cancer biology, this course is amazing!! I honestly enjoyed the lectures so much and the professors want you to succeed. We also had in class paper discussions and Prof. Kuhns was so sweet that he brought in coffee and pastries and always was extremely helpful. This class is honestly one of the best and most interesting that I have taken at Harvard and I highly highly recommend it as an intermediate biology class. It will also teach you how to read papers and find confidence in yourself as a biology student.
Honestly, I really enjoyed MCB 66. The teaching staff definitely is invested in your success, and they are always there to support you. The workshop part of the class is both fun and informative, and the exams are very fair (if you study and know the material, you will do well). In general, we also read some very interesting papers, and I’ve definitely come out of the course with a deeper understanding of a lot of the cellular processes underlying cancer.
Read the articles for the class earlier and annotate figures to be able to easily participate and follow along in class.
Take the papers seriously and finish them before the discussions. You will really enjoy the discussions and the lectures. Michele and Sam are very kind and amazing people. Do not hesitate to seek for help at any point.
It is just a very good course to take if you are interested in cancer research
This class was brand new this semester, so hopefully there will be improvements next year. There were only 2 exams, and no final exam. The weekly homework assignments were definitely the most challenging part and completely ruined my grade, but if you can do well on those you will be fine in the course. The exams were not too difficult, probably because you are allowed to have a double sided notecard.
Absolutely take this class! Michele and Sam care so much about their students! This is one of the few classes were you never get the feeling that the course wants to screw you over. Psets and exams are fair. The primary literature that is selected is very interesting!
Take this class if you want to learn about cancer biology! The coursework is pretty light and you learn from a very passionate teaching staff who are committed to your success. I have yet to meet such an amazing course staff at Harvard so far... plus you learn about the latest cutting edge research findings that can guide you to your own passion in reseatch/senior thesis as well. You should definitely have some background on cell biology, genetics, and biological research techniques/outside lab experience however. One more thing: they have yummy snacks in class and section all the time!!
I really loved this course and would highly recommend it to anyone interested in learning more about life science and cellular states and interactions. The content is incredibly interesting and gives you a great foundation of understanding that allows you to form connections between information from many different classes. In section and within workshops, you learn how to read, analyze, and critique research articles (which is a skill that I struggled with a lot) in a way that I found to be, if initially quite difficult, very engaging. Teaching staff for this course is amazing and definitely the key to what makes this class so great. In the end this is a class that you get out what you put in, and if you are willing to put in the effort I think you will really like this class and gain a much better appreciation for science from it.
This class is amazing! Learned a lot about reading primary literature. Lectures were well–taught. Assignments were fair and aided in understanding of the material. The professor, preceptor, and TFs were all super knowledgeable and accessible. Highly recommend if at all interested in pathological cell biology and cancer biology.
Very chill and overall easy class. The material is very interesting and the course staff is very nice and approachable. You'll learn a lot and have a good time if you put in the effort

Now though, I feel super confident about research papers!
