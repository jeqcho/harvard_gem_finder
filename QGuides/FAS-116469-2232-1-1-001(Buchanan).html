You're sadly never going to get to experience Bailey's class as we were her last, but if any of the other instructors are like her it will be an amazing course for you.
THE BEST CLASS AT HARVARD. I swear. This is my last semester and I promise you that this is the best out of all the 32 classes I have taken here. You will learn SO much. I would be confident going alone to anywhere Spanish speaking. The stuff you engage with is interesting too (lots of sustainability, migration etc.). Lastly, meeting every day at 9 with what will likely be a small group of people is such a great way to make new friends, and I'm really grateful for the people I met in this class. If you want to come out of the semester with a genuine tool that you can pick up and use, take this class. I literally could have written this whole thing in Spanish. That's how good the class is.
Learning another language is such a gift, and it's so rare to have the opportunity to dedicate intensive time to your personal and linguistic growth. Take this class!! It's a magical experience. You walk in knowing nothing, and walk about talking in Spanish about history, current events, and hypothetical situations. While it takes more dedicated time and effort than Spanish 11, the speed of the course and the focus from your peers is really unparalleled. I would recommend this course for anyone who *seriously* wants to learn Spanish, *yearns* to learn Spanish, and is unafraid to be challenged in a loving and exciting way! Nothing intimidating about this course or its professors at all. If you put in the work, you can make truly astounding progress.
You need to not only have taken a romance language before, but have taken that language **in a good program.** Meaning: if you took a French for four years in high school and got an A on all your tests, BUT you know in the back of your head that you couldn't speak a word if you went to France and don't have any real cumulative or comprehensive knowledge of the language, don't count that as fulfilling the requirement for the course! (i.e., "knowledge of a romance language" You will notice the difference during the class–– the kids who have a genuine understanding of concepts like the imperfect vs. perfect/preterite, subject/verb agreement, etc. etc., will be way ahead. But still take it if you're willing to put in the work.

Overall, you will finish this course speaking Spanish. It might be a bit halting, a bit hesitant, occasionally at a loss for words, but you will be conversational. You'll be able to stop and talk to a custodian who only speaks Spanish in your dorm (an experience several of us reported having as we took the class), and talking to other workers and people around campus/Harvard Square who only speak Spanish . This course taught me that the most important thing for a language is to use actively and daily. The course size is small so you get individual attention and detailed feedback, and you become close with your peers and instructor. It's your 9am–family :)

The instructor, Bailey, is the kindest and most supportive teacher you can have. She is strict about tardiness and turning things in on time (no substitute assignments or excessive extensions), but that won't be an obstacle to getting an A in the class if you put in the work.

Be careful: the first few weeks of the course are easier because you go slower and it covers all of Spanish 10. But, once you move onto the material of Spanish 11, things will pick up–– a project, a test, and a 30–minute Spanish conversation due every two weeks, on top of regular homework.
This is not an easy class that will serve only to fulfill a requirement. You should take it if you are really interested in learning the language and are willing to do an immersion in Spanish. I definitely recommend it and can guarantee that you will learn a lot in a very short time. If you already speak a Romance language, it won't be as intense either.
