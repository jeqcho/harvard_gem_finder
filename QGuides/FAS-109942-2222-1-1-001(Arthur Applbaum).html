What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The course is demanding but most definitely worth it. You’ll learn a lot about different philosophical modes of approaching big questions such as affirmative action or civil disobedience. In the process, you’ll be able to produce original work of your own (with plenty of support) that you’re proud of and meet students with different and interesting philosophies.
This class is mandatory for EJ Safra fellowship program, so really this is a review of the program overall. I think so far it's been a good experience and I would encourage people to apply! The class is not that stressful (as long as you participate and turn in the work you'll do well), the readings are on a wide set of topics in political philosophy and are interesting, and it's been nice to have a bit of a philosophy community with the other fellows as many of my other friends are in STEM. I think you get out of this program what you put into it –– pick a good topic for your paper and try to come to optional events and speakers and you will have a good experience.
I took this course feeling quite skeptical about moral philosophy––whether it was useful, what it was grounded in, etc. I find that after the course, I'm much more convinced that moral philosophy is important and useful. For my fellow skeptics, this course may provide some exciting challenges to your intellectual frameworks.
If you are looking at this, it is probably because you are interested in applying for the Safra Fellowship. If you are not a Safra Fellow, unfortunately you can't take this class (but you can apply in the fall!)

This class is wonderful, and the Safra Fellowship has been the highlight of my semester. I'll list you the bops and flops of this semester:

Bops:
– Arthur is genuinely one of the best instructors that I have ever met at Harvard, if not the best. He consistently takes time out of weekends and evenings to spend hours with us on revising our papers and helping us develop as philosophers.
– The curriculum really provides a broad survey of ethics, starting from individual questions of free will and agency to moral and political questions.
– Arthur + the course will push you to be more analytic, and he will not hesitate to speak.
– There is free food at the seminars. Good food that is better than HUDS. This should be at least one of the most important factors for why you apply.

Flops:
– It is 3 hours long.
– The class is very demanding, with lots of micro–deadlines throughout the week.
– You have a 25 page final paper that is about any topic in ethics. This, I will note, is half the length of a senior thesis in philosophy.

Not sure:
– This class is super Rawlsian. Arthur is a huge defender of Enlightenment–style thought. Neither are shoved down your throat, but be prepared to get some pushback on your util / social / post–modern thoughts.

All of this year's fellows are accessible on the Safra Center's website. Please do not hesitate to reach out if you have any questions about the program –– we will all vouch for it.
Take this course if you are social–studies oriented and want to meet a group of people who think about normative questions! The Safra Center itself is also a great resource.
Take this class if you're the kind of person who likes staying up until 2am talking with friends in the dhall about moral dilemmas. This class is incredibly interesting but you'll only find it engaging if you're interested in ethics. If you aren't interested in ethics, then you'll not enjoy the readings / class discussions. Arthur is one of the smartest human beings on campus – soak in his wisdom. The class is demanding but you won't have to worry about your grade – you should just try to learn as much as possible because this is such a unique opportunity. Try to do as much of the readings as possible, but there will be some weeks where you'll only have the chance to do 1 reading and skim the others (and that'll be okay because Arthur is pretty understanding).
apply to the fellowship!!!!!!
