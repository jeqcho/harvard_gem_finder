What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course was excellent. I feel truly sorry that this course will likely never be offered again because of the professor's retirement. This seminar is what comes to mind when I think about unique Harvard classes. Getting the opportunity to read Proust is amazing and Virginie understands the writing deeply and provides fascinating commentary. Since we can't read the whole work, the selected excerpts are well selected and feel coherent when looked at together. Reading about the the grail with Arthurian Knights was very interesting and the workload for reading was very manageable. The coolest part about this class is the moving classroom with each class in our quest being held in different locations. This is so cool. Taking this class guarantees breaking out of the Harvard bubble with fun class trips to Boston getting whisked around by curators behind the ropes of the city's best museums. I will be leaving this class with a lifetime of memories from seeing all the nooks and crannies of memorial hall to seeing Washington's Thomas Paine signed copy of Common sense at the Athaeneum. We also had a lovely time at the Fogg Museum and looking at Monet at the Museum of Fine Arts. The assignments for this class were fun and weren't overly time consuming. This course was an amazing exploration of liberal arts at Harvard and I hope it's offered again in the future.
Class won't be run again.
Best class out there! It made me appreciate the Boston area and the buildings that I see everyday so much more.
I can't think of anything Professor Greene could have done differently. I loved the course, was excited to come to class everyday, fascinated by our readings, and inspired by our discussions. This class provided me with a new lens in which I can understand art, Boston history, Western canon, and Christian mythology. I genuinely feel so bad for all the students in the future who might not be able to take this class if no one teaches it after Professor Greene retires. This class was genuinely a transformative experience, and I'm so grateful to have had it.
This is an incredible course at Harvard which creatively allows freshmen to really explore the options at Harvard. Reading Proust is a life changing experience, but takes time. Professor Greene knew so much she was able to truly break down the writing and made me feel at home at Harvard and beyond.
