Professor Matherne is genuinely enthusiastic about the content, the readings, and the philosophers we engage with in the course. All of the readings and assignments are doable, and there is even a fun creative assignment as part of the course that I really enjoyed! I have no complaints about the class, other than the fact that the lectures are not recorded, but I enjoyed everything about the class.
Take it! You'll learn a lot, and go to class often even if full attendance isn't mandatory. Ask lots of questions too, and go to office hours –– Prof. Matherne is so insightful in one–on–one conversations!
great class! prof matherne is a great lecture and teacher. she obviously cares about the content of the course and her students. the history of phil courses in the department tend to be hit or miss with TFs/grading just because most of the work is exposition/interpretation and everyone has their own way of reading the complex, ambiguous thinkers a course like this covers.
One of the best classes I have ever taken at Harvard. Matherne is an absolutely AMAZING lecturer. Truly so engaging. The questions you get at —what make life fulfilling? what role does art play in life? how can we live better lives? — are directly relevant to your life.

Even if you aren't super interested in aesthetics (which I didn't know I was) this is a great interview to some of the greatest philosophers of the 19th ce.
Very interesting class with a heavy focus on aesthetics. You will enjoy this class if you believe art is valuable and has meaning. If you are more of a science–y person, you might have a harder time agreeing with many of the philosophical ideas in this course.
Take this class! This is has genuinely been my favourite class at Harvard so far. Matherne is wizard who can understand and explain texts in the clearest way possible. If you are even remotely interested in the material, do yourself a favour and take this class.
This is an excellent course! The lectures are extremely engaging, and there are plenty of opportunities to ask questions or introduce thoughts of your own. The readings span many diverse thinkers, so you'll come away from the course with many perspectives. Overall, I highly recommend this course.
Oooh what a TOP TIER philosophy class, as Matherne's ALWAYS are!! Do yourself a favor and take it. You could do well if you've never taken philosophy either
Prof Matherne is awesome! Take a class with her at all costs! This course was great, but also recognize that it is a course about aesthetics, so if you are not interested in art and its role in the meaningful life, you might get frustrated that that is always the angle. Assignments weren't too onerous.
Take this course!!! Professor Matherne is probably the best lecturer in the philosophy department (you can ask a question in class and she'll fully just know, off the top of her head, the page number in the book that corresponds to an answer to your question). She writes everything clearly on the blackboard and explains the thinkers in depth, so don't be intimidated by the readings if you find them complicated because she'll walk you through it. The midterms are graded not that harshly and office hours are extremely helpful so make sure to go before you turn in the midterms. Most importantly, the class is super interesting and will actually help you lead a better life (unlike most classes here) helping you think through why art might be crucial for doing so.
This is a great class for anyone interested in learning about aesthetic philosophy, and if you're interested in reading some of the "classic" philosophers (Nietzsche, Hegel, Schopenhauer) in a classroom environment. Prof. Matherne is a great lecturer and makes sure that the class readings come from a variety of different sources. Overall, the class isn't that much work and the readings and lectures are engaging!
Take this class if you are interested in aesthetics and if you like philosophy. The texts are quite dense but it will be very rewarding if you get through them!
This is an incredible course that anybody with an interest in philosophy should take if they have the opportunity! Professor Matherne is an absolutely remarkable professor, very possibly my favorite in the department, and her deep knowledge and passion for aesthetics are clearly evident in the course. A background in Kant could be helpful, but any exposure to philosophy should be sufficient for you to get so much out of the class! This class would be worth taking for Professor Matherne alone, but the fascinating content and perfect pacing of the course make this a must–take in the philosophy department.
Prof. Matherne is incredible. She explains really deep, dense philosophy with such clarity. Definitely if she's taking this course, take it with her. I learned so much philosophy but also so much about ~how to think about life~.
Take this class!!! Prof. Matherne is great, the texts you read are great, and you learn a ton about the history of philosophy (and how this can be applied in your every day life). Don't take the class if you think you're going to skip lectures — you won't want to miss Matherne's lecturing, and there's so much to be gained from these philosophers.
very lenient attendance policy, engaging course materials, accessible TFs, reasonable assignments, cool vibes
This is an awesome class and I strongly recommend it, especially for those looking to dip their toes into the field of philosophy. Its not much work, its very engaging, you get out what you put in. You read some awesome philosophers (and artists!) and Matherne is so so captivating. I can't say good enough things about this class!
Prof. Matherne is an absolute delight who takes questions seriously and uses great examples. Her lectures are engaging and thought–provoking –– there were some lectures where I wanted to give a standing ovation at the end. The class goes by very quickly, and there is not much time to dig deeper, but I think it's a great rollercoaster of a ride.
Really wonderful course – Matherne is incredible and has put together a really fascinating exploration of aesthetics and 19th century philosophy here. I learned a ton and had fun along the way – would highly recommend!
Take this course! Don't worry about the course number—no prep is needed and it is quite easy to do well. If you're new to philosophy, go to office hours and/or work with the Phil Department Writing Fellow to understand how to write a good philosophy paper, but other than that you can get a lot out of this class if you do the readings and genuinely consider how these theories relate to your life. It's super easy if you don't do the readings too. Don't expect to spend a ton of time on this class outside of lectures. But definitely go to lectures because they will tell you everything you need to know.
