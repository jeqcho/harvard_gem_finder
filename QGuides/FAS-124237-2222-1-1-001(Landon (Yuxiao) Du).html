This is a great language class, and I highly recommend it for the beginnings of a major, a language requirement, or an elective. It's super well–organized, well–thought–out, and engaging.
This course will require a lot of work, but if you put in the work it's very manageable!
If you want to get familiar with Chinese and be able to understand the structure of the language/use a little conversationally, this is the class to take. Although the commitment is not insignificant, with class meeting for an hour every day and one homework/two dictation quizzes on a weekly basis (in addition to longer reading or speaking assignments every other week), there is no other setting in which learning a foreign language that has a lot of moving parts will be as enjoyable. The class culture/camaraderie amongst BaBb students, enthusiasm and youthful energy of the teachers, and just very well–organized teaching structure makes this a fast paced but fruitful foreign language class. I'm not the best at Chinese and struggled with speaking/grammar but still got a very good grade in the class because of how much participation, completion, and other small grades add up (as well as bonus point opportunities).
Studying a little bit each day is so much better than all at once for this class.
This class is an absolute joy! The course staff and material are amazing and enjoyable.
If you have any interest in Chinese, definitely take this course! It is a great introductory course that will teach you a lot while also not overwhelming you with its workload; although Chinese is a difficult language to learn for native English speakers, the instructors do a great job in making sure that you are following during section. If you make sure to study and put in a bit of effort, the course is very reasonable and doable and it is very difficult to do poorly.
Try to make time to follow their schedule –– you'll feel a lot more engaged in drill/lecture section if you revise the material they recommend you go over. It also helps you get stuff out of those drill/lecture sections. Also, as long as you dedicate a period of time to memorize the new words/characters that you need to learn every week, you'll be fine. If you feel like you've established a rhythm to keeping on top of your work after taking Chinese Ba, then (in my experience) Bb will be easier––and maybe even more enjoyable!
amazing! take Chinese. useful, fun, the best teaching staff i have encountered at Harvard. motivation for daily studying/class dropped off for me from Ba to Bb, but was still very enjoyable. Bb grammar definitely a step up from Ba difficulty–wise
I learned so much Chinese – it's weird to literally gain the ability to communicate in another language. I recognize that that's the point of a language class, but I think this is the first one I've taken that succeeded in this goal. By the end of the class, I'd walk out of section chatting with my classmates still in Chinese. It's crazy. I often bragged to my friends about how my classmates and I can *literally* speak Chinese now, like hear questions and just answer them without needing to manually translate word by word. Anyway, I loved this class, actually looked forward to it and felt energized after. It is a lot of work, but very worth it to me.
