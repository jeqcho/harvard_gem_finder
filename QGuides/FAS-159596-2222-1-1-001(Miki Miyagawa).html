What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you don't keep up with this class, it gets exponentially harder because your lack of understanding of vocab and kanji will hinder your ability to understand future assignments. Do whatever you can to not fall too behind.
This was a great course, as have all my experience with the Japanese language program has been. Through this course, you will learn how to effectively read, write, speak, and listen to Japanese, making you one step closer to achieving total fluency.
If you liked 120a, you'll like this course too, although it is somewhat more difficult. It's structured more strongly around short readings with kanji and vocab lists drawn from those readings. I'm not sure how it will change without Miyagawa Sensei at the head, but I have trust in the Japanese department.
