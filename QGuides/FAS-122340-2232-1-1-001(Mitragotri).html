Very easy
The course is very doable and can be integrated with a tougher schedule. The workload is fairly light especially if you start the assignments ahead of time, however the assignments took longer some weeks. With some drugs, a deep dive into the research was required to come up with a solid innovation and get full points, other times something would just click. The presentation is also not too difficult especially since it is in groups. These assignments were an effective way to learn about the drugs and understand the research behind them. I really enjoyed learning about the evolution of drug delivery and the novel techniques, the concepts and terms came up frequently in other places once I had been exposed to them. It is also interesting to learn about the markets behind drug discovery and delivery and how much of the field is influenced from beyond the biology.
This class is great! Dr. Mitragotri is super knowledgeable about the course material, and the course format is really laid–back and low–stress to allow you to engage with it as you want. The assignments are actually quite helpful in stimulating the creation of research ideas. Would recommend if you're looking for a chill engineering elective or 5th class.
This class is super interesting if you're interested in drug related fields. This is one of the few bioengineering classes about drug related topics offered at Harvard, and Professor Mitragotri is super knowledgeable and his insight into the field is awesome. You will get a broad overview of the drug delivery field. The assignments (2 a week) can be difficult to begin at times but are manageable and will took me around 3–4 hours a week each, but I was particularly slow and others took as little as an hour or hour and a half.
