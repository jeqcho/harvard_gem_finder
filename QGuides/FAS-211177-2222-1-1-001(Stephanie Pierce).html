I would say this class is very enjoyable! Your Inner Fish is an entertaining read, and classes are very interesting. Just be careful with writing your reviews because they will be critiqued harshly. Also the quizzes can be tough.
This is a fun class but this is no gem. You have to put in the work needed and remember that science communication is heavily stressed. Even if you know the material well, it doesn't really matter unless you can describe it well. Similarly, if you don't know the material well but you are good at describing things in short snippets or in corny analogies, this course is for you!
Pretty steady workload throughout the course. The book is fun to roast sometimes, and the hands–on lab component is quite interesting and eye–opening. I would take this course if you have space!
This was a super laidback and fun class, the staff was wonderful and I learned a lot! If you want to get more out of the topics discussed, I would suggest taking LS2 before this class — that introduced most of the concepts and created a foundation for this class to build off of. This is completely optional — this class really doesn’t need that level of preparation — but it was helpful that I knew what we were talking about before reading about it. The lab component can feel a littttttle bit long (especially for a Monday at 3 pm), but if you make friends in the class and contribute to the discussion, it goes by much faster.
