Before taking this class I thought it would mostly be discussing real world events regarding international relations. I should have known better since it is an introductory course. That being said, I still enjoyed the material and found professor to be very genuine and practical. He facilitates lecture participation and encourages each student to go a level deeper with core concepts and theories. He's also not a professor who keeps people until the end of class for no particular reason (those are the worst). If we covered all of the material in 45 minutes that day, then we were done. I really appreciated that. I would recommend this course with ONE caveat – if you are not necessarily into the theory portion of international relations and more so want to talk and learn about what's going on specifically in the present world, then I might not take it. If you want to apply theoretical models and analyze data for general claims that can be applied to the way in which international entities interact, then I would definitely take this course
