TAKE THIS CLASS PLEASE. This is my favorite course I've ever taken at Harvard and I couldn't recommend it more. Professor Shingleton is amazing and the discussions you will have in this course are truly eye opening. Eating disorders are so important to learn about and this class is a great way to learn about the science behind them, but also hear case studies, watch videos, and do assignments that actually feel meaningful. As part of the Mindich Engaged Scholarship program, we made infographics and presented them at a poster session at the end of the semester and it was really impactful to get to share our work with CAMHS/ECHO/Harvard community. Take this class!!!!!!!
This is an incredible and comprehensive course about eating disorders. Given the prevalence of disordered eating related messaging, this information is important and relevant for all. Rebecca Shingleton is an amazing person, so knowledgeable, and a joy to learn from.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! The content is incredibly interesting and Professor Shingleton is the best. The discussions are great and there are minimal assignments, so the class is very focused on learning.

This course was amazing! Especially if you have background and/or interest in eating/body concerns, Dr. Shingleton does a great job of creating an environment in which you can explore and discuss topics. She is so generous with her time (ex. loved the movie night she offered to watch a film related to coursework) and nurturing to her students!
Take this class! Not only did I learn a lot about eating disorder pathology and treatment, I also learned to be more cognisant of how my actions affect those who may be silent suffers or struggle with eating and body concerns.

This class is fantastic! By far one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard, and I highly highly recommend it. All of the assignments are very manageable, and as long as you take them seriously and get them done you will not have to worry about your grade. I thought the readings were always super interesting, and class discussions were really productive. You'll leave understanding eating disorders much more deeply, and will realize how often they tend to come up in conversation.
