What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I would tell future students to stay up to date on the work because all assignments build upon each other and culminate into the final paper.
take this class if you want to have the time to focus on a topic that really interests you. you can discover a lot about yourself by just taking a class that makes you do things that you actually like
The task of writing the final paper might seem daunting at first, but the class is designed to help you build to the paper throughout the course of the semester. Pick a topic in which you are at least somewhat interested, and it will be infinitely easier.
If you are considering this course, you are probably trying to fulfill the GHHP secondary research requirement. It is a tedious and sometimes boring class overall, but worthwhile nonetheless.
If you're reading this, it's likely because this course is a requirement for you and you probably don't have a choice. This course is good in that it gives you freedom to explore a topic of interest to you, and if you want to you'll learn a lot. However, the weekly section discussions were a complete waste of time (useless topics like, "how do you give a presentation?", "how do you cite?", etc.) which were obviously covered in high school / first year of college.
Everything is so drawn out and very boring. Would rather just write the paper immediately than sit through 2 hour sections talking about how to write individual parts of the paper which is something that everyone should have known at this point in their college careers. The requirement would have been more pleasant if it was more of an individual study set up where you just meet with the TF instead of going to long sections.
