It's a pretty tough workload, with 3 daily psets and 1 weekly pset on top of it. I did appreciate the small size of the class, so you get the opportunity to know the other students and work together on psets. Office hours was incredibly helpful in being able to complete the psets. The midterms were pretty difficult and there isn't really a good way to prepare for them, since there aren't any practice exams.
I would not take this class if there is the option to take math 21b. This class is very time–consuming as there are basically 4 psets a week, 3 daily and 1 weekly pset for every single week. The exams were also hard to study for because there were no textbooks dedicated for this course. The course also did not provide any practice test so the actual test came unexpectedly. This course also had a coding aspect where no coding was taught in the course, but it was expected that you learn it yourself for the psets, which was hard since most of the CAs for the course were unfamiliar.
If you want linear algebra, take 21 or 22. If you want statistics, go take a stats course. This course suffers from significant structural issues, course policies are unfair, and grading is harsh. It is too small of a class with graduate students, making for a terrible class environment. Students (even fellow undergrads) refuse to cooperate and collaborate because they want you to fail (competition for no reason), and there are no practice tests provided for small exams that just punish you for small mistakes. You cannot succeed in this course without having taken linear algebra. Lectures are non traditional and, in my opinion, not conducive to learning. Course materials are outdated, unreadable, and not open on Canvas when you need them for a problem set. Regardless of who is teaching this course, you should avoid this course at all costs. Harvard should separate 19b from 18b. It's a shame since 19a was so wonderful. If you took 19a, go take 21.
Strongly recommend! This was just an awesome course, and I thoroughly enjoyed it. It was a bit difficult, but nothing crazy. Everything we learned felt very important and worthwhile as a person in an increasingly–data–driven world. I had taken some stats before, but didn't really understand what I'd learned... This class was much more applied and helped me consolidate that knowledge, so it's actually helpful for me now in my daily life! MATLAB experience was good, too. I came in with none (but did have a tad bit of coding experience beforehand, nothing stellar...), and I still did OK. There were only a couple problems sets that were MATLAB–heavy, so you'll need to pop into office hours for those if you're weaker there, but it truly wasn't a problem. It was generally a good low–stakes environment to pick up some MATLAB skills, so could be useful if you're taking more MATLAB–intensive courses down the line. The support system was EXCELLENT when I took it the ratio of staff to students was 1:4. I think the small size of this class is a HUGE advantage and I can't recommend enough taking this class over 21b if you're less math–inclined like I am. I think it gets less into the weeds of linear algebra than 21b, from what I've heard (?), which for me is ideal, as I just wanted enough depth to feel in–the–loop about computational methods I'll use in my work which linear algebra forms the foundation of. The pace was PERFECT for me: enough to be challenging, but never enough that I felt left behind. There are a few tougher lessons than others, but all are highly–manageable, warning is given in advance, and support is bountiful. I love, love, love linear algebra after this course! Everything just made sense!
I think this course is very powerful for students who perform computational / statistical work in their main fields of focus. The concepts covered are very relevant. I would be wary, however, that the course may require more time than you expect, so budget your time accordingly.
TAKE THIS OVER 21B ** especially if you are more interested in bio applications. Much better structure and great teaching staff.
