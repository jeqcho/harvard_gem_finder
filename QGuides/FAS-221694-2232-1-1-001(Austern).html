Morgane is amazing! She always finds a way to make class fun –– and the material is quite interesting too. Occasionally the notation got in the way of intuitive understanding, but statisticians working on networks is a relatively recent development so it's understandably challenging to make a class that both operates at the frontier and is accessible.
This class is very awesome, so take it if you are interested in networks!
Great course — very interesting material and very applicable to real–life problems! Definitely should take if interested in graph neural networks/statistics regarding graphs
Great class. It was a bit chaotic this year since its the first year its being run, but I assume next year will be a little more structured. Morgane is a super super super nice professor, who is very willing to spend time outside of class to go through material, or to give academic/professional advice. The material is also super interesting, and is presented in a very logical way. Start from metrics on graphs, different graph models, and go all the way to embedding–based methods for graphs (based on random walks), an exploration of node2vec, deepwalk, to finish up with Graph Neural Networks which are explained super well by Morgane. Overall I highly recommend the course.
Really great course and very chill for a stats class. Interesting material and very doable with some mathematical maturity and either a CS or Stats background. Only 3 psets, a take–home midterm, and group project, so very light on the deliverables but there is a lot of material presented if you want to dig in.
Given that this was the class's first year, I thought it went pretty well. The content and material were engaging, and the pace was definitely manageable. The psets (of which there were only 3 graded ones and we had a pset drop) were pretty reasonable in length too. However, my major issue was with the take–home midterm, which was stated to take us "a couple of hours". We had unlimited time to work on it within a 3 day window, and I ended up needing upwards of 12ish hours to finish it. All three students I talked to afterwards also reported needing 10+ hours. It was a gargantuan undertaking and was highly unappreciated, especially since it was the week before spring break and I had midterms for the rest of my classes that week as well. Besides this first–year hiccup re: midterm length, the class was enjoyable, and Morgane is an enthusiastic, caring, and knowledgeable professor (but PLEASE shorten the midterm length!!)
Morgane is great: flexible, accommodating, and encouraging!
Chill stat class – very few assignments which don't take too long. Midterm was a lot of work but take–home and final project was fun. I got to explore graph neural networks and push myself to get a working model.
Course is a bit disorganized and Prof is a little rambly at times, but makes an effort to clarify. Psets don't necessarily follow from class so make sure to go to OH, but are quite interesting. Overall a good class to take.
This class was designed to be easy, although the material itself is actually interesting and somewhat challenging. There were 3 problem sets total, and only 2 actually counted towards the final grade. However, the problem sets were definitely not easy and took a significant amount of time to complete. The take–home midterm was HARD, like super hard, and it took me almost the entire time to do (3 whole days). The final presentation was not bad at all though. Overall, this course was interesting and a nice addition to other classes you might be taking that require more work. When the psets come out, I recommend starting them early and going to OH since they are non–trivial, but thankfully there aren't too many of them.
This class is very interesting as we interact with graphs all the time! Morgane is a great professor and she makes the statistical concepts very digestable.
Morgane is great. She really cares about students learning and is willing to put in the extra effort to make sure you are; she's a passionate, friendly, and smart instructor. The homeworks in this class can be a bit tricky though, its better to start early and go to office hours if you're stuck; Morgane is really helpful in clarifying things. It's sometimes unclear how the homeworks connect to the lecture or how you're supposed to start let alone solve the problems based on what was covered in class. Considering how quickly the field is moving and the fact that it was a new class though, it was well done.
