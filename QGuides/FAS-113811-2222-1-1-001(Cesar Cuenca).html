cesar is a very chill prof and while I was very frequently challenged in this class, I think cesar was entirely reasonable in setting the level of rigour. I wish I could practice probability questions and going for OH more consistently if I could turn back time. so definitely do that if you take this class!
This class provides a great mathematical foundation for probability theory, however that's not why most people take this course. Most people take this course because they don't wish to take Stat110, for fear that it is too hard, or at least harder than this course. If this is your worry, I highly encourage you to think twice about taking this course. Though on paper this course may be less of a time commitment than Stat110, there are other negatives to it that Stat110 doesn't have. For instance, there are very, very few people who take this course, so chances are you're PSETting and studying on your own. This class has only one TF while Stat110 has much more and all who are very committed to the class. Foremostly, Stat110 prepares you to take more Stat classes at Harvard and leaves the door open for doing a Stats concentration. Math154 course doesn't count towards Stats requirements.

As a disclaimer, however, I've never taken Stat110 – this is just what I've heard and seen from friends and people around me. But in summary: Math154 is a good course, but I suggest you take Stat110.
Don't take this class. Take stat 110 instead.
This class is pretty similar to Stat 110 in terms of material –– I took this class because I didn't take Stat 110 in the fall and didn't want to wait a year. I'm glad I did this route because now I actually know some probability! Depending on who you take it with, it could be more theoretical or more computational. With Cesar, the course was more computational. But we did some stuff with sigma algebras and a bit of measure theory, which you probably wouldn't touch in Stat 110.
A course you absolutely want in your math toolkit. Strikes a perfect balance between the empiricism of statistics and the rigorous subfield of theoretical mathematics. And the professor is a grandmaster at teaching the material.
