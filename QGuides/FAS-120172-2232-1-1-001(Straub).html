This class is a lot of work. There is about a pset every 2 weeks, which isn't bad, but they do take a long time. Straub was such an amazing professor. He made all of the models so easy to understand and was willing to answer any question. Changing professors halfway through the year was not ideal though. When Gabe began teaching it was a hard reset. His lectures were much harder to follow and he assumed that we knew a lot of the math behind the derivations. He also didn't really address questions well and just said he would address them later. Basically, don't let the first half of the semester lull you into a sense of security. Overall, exams were not too difficult, midterm 2 was way harder than midterm 1, but this course is a lot of work. Really only take this if you are AM EC.
This course is taught in two halves. The first half was taught by Ludwig, and the second was taught by Gabriel. I was quite interested in the material covered in the first half (we did growth models), though sometimes the models were trivial. When we started the second half, we did asset pricing which was a very interesting way to think about assessing the value of investments, projects, etc, but then the material got really dry. A lot of the lectures involved a lot of small technical details that were not very interesting, and I personally did not gain that much new economic knowledge from this class (compared to ec 1011a). A lot of the models are just applications of models from 1011a and the concepts are things you already know if you know some macro. 1011b is probably your best option for intermediate macro though, so there's not much you can do. I recommend the course, but it is not as interesting as 1011a.
