What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Taking this class with Kaighin was very rewarding – we covered a lot of topics and were very verse in the science by the end of the course. Referring to the textbook and course materials (especially section slides and formula sheets) is essential to working on problem sets and studying for the exams
I highly recommend taking this class! It was very interesting learning about freshwater resources on Earth and learning more about the intricate workings of the water cycle. Kaighin is an excellent professor and the TFs were also really great at explaining difficult concepts in an understandable manner. The teaching team was definitely a huge strength of the class! There is a decent amount of math and physics involved, so I'd recommend having some understanding of basic mechanics and differential equations. The workload is not bad at all (one pset every two weeks) and there are two midterms and a final (which you're allowed to bring a sheet of notes to). The exams were not too long but they were definitely harder than the problem sets, so I'd recommend studying in advance for those. On the whole, I thought this class was super interesting and definitely one I'm glad I took!
Lectures are pretty dry... I liked the people teaching the class, but the textbook carried me through most of it. Psets took me about 6 hours without reading the textbook chapters and 1–3 hours if I did read. It's hard to judge the average workload because psets were due every other week, so sometimes it would be a lot but other times it would be nothing at all.
I remember not liking this class while I was taking it, but in hindsight it wasn't that bad. Probably not ideal for an elective but if you're curious about environmental science it's an okay place to start. It's cool being able to answer questions about water because it's literally everywhere and very relevant to global issues
