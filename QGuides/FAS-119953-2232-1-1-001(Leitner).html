While the class advertises itself as being for people with no computer science experience, it is actually quite the opposite. Students who already have experience and knowledge of coding will succeed. But if you have no computer science experience and want to code, I suggest learning how to do that prior to taking this class. Throughout the lectures, the professor would bring up about how a certain procedure he is doing is further proven/explained in CS124. This class is not a beginner class for coding or cs.
This is a good introductory course that starts off relatively easy, but does get rather difficult at the end. Expect to devote more time and energy as the semester goes on.
Take this course if you need to complete QRD or SEAS, I guess.
If you are interested in learning CS, take this course!!! I'd also recommend spacing out the problem sets, but it's not impossible (though it is a bit stressful) to do them the night before.
Don't take CS1 if you want to go much further in CS.
Borders on fun, is accessible and interesting, appreciate Henry for the legend he is
if this is your first cs class ***
Don't be fooled by the easy psets in the beginning! This course gets harder towards the end, and you need to put in a greater amount of effort into your psets to finish the problem. However, this class is a great primer on computer science, especially for people who don't have any experience going in.
This course starts easy, but ramps up in difficulty. If you put in the work, you definitely learn the fundamentals of programming in Python, and you might be surprised at the end at how much you know how to do. Grading is a bit tricky because you aren't given an explicit rubric, so there are some mistakes you might make that you won't even know you made until after grades are back in. The exam is also tricky. If you truly have no background, I would suggest taking this class pass/fail.
If you need to hit the distributional requirement this is a great class because you can succeed without prioritizing it in your schedule, and you can always take it pass/fail which can make it much less stressful. Just make sure you attend section and don't fall behind on psets.
This course is very manageable, there is almost no coursework outside of the psets. Going to lectures and section is all that is needed to be well–positioned for the course.
Don't worry too much about this class. It's not nightmarish like you hear about other CS classes. It's super approachable. This is the only STEM class I've taken at Harvard and I survived. You don't need the textbook so don't waste your money. I took it pass–fail but probably could have afforded not to. Just go to office hours with specific questions and you'll be all good. Also Leitner really appreciates it when you show up to lecture so go!
