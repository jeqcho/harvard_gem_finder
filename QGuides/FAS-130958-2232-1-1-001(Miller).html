Great class for anyone interested in plays of theatre, no experience in theatre necessary.
To anyone with as much as a passing interest in theater, I'd strongly recommend this class. The workload is manageable—a few plays a week, discussion posts, well–designed presentations/assignments with clear expectations—and I found myself looking forward to class each week. It is rare to take a course that feels so natural, both in terms of flow and progression and in terms of the ease with which class discussion is led. Most weeks felt like a book club in which individual's viewpoints were taken seriously and developed and everyone was equally bought in.

Professor Miller combines rigour with a relaxed approach, adding fruitful insight into the conversation without dominating it, maintaining a serious environment while also fostering a classroom in which people feel comfortable speaking their mind. The syllabus was thoughtfully designed and dynamic and there was frequent opportunities for feedback and self reflection. Assignments were extremely well designed with clear rubrics. While Professor Miller's feedback on assignments was not extensive, there was always the sense that he was available outside of class should that be desired.

Couldn't recommend more, no reservations whatsoever.
Great class & great reading list. Don't take it unless you can commit to doing the readings because although you can get by without doing them, it's the kind of class where you won't get much from the class if you don't do them. Overall a solid course.
If you want to be exposed to the present state of American plays, this is the course to take! The syllabus is so diverse and remarkably current (to the point where it was difficult finding scripts for some of the works because they had literally premiered this year or last). I do wish we could've talked about the structure of the plays a little more, rather than just the thematic content, but overall discussion was very interesting and the two hours flew by. In fact, I would say my biggest complaint was that there was just not enough time to talk about everything there is to talk about with these shows!
It's kinda hard* but definitely take it if you love reading plays and talking about them—doesn't get more simple than that. Do the discussion posts on time and work ahead on the bigger assignments and you'll do fine!
