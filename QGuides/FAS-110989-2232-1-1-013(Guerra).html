It is not a hard course, but ready to put a lot of time into the psets! Exams are manageable, and you probably should try to go to most classes.
Get Jill as your section leader, you will be fine. Don't get Jill as your section leader? Watch her videos on canvas. Go to office office hours. Be friends with you TF. And you might be okay? Whose to say.
Only take the class if you need it! It is definitely going to be a challenging class as the course is content heavy and there are 3 (usually long) PSets every week. Also this course does not accept late PSets so definitely attend the quiz sessions which are super helpful in revising content (and gain additional PSet drops by attending them).
This class is taught well (as long as you have a good section leader) and structured well. The quiz session pset drop system is great, and there is a TON of support from the course in terms of office hours and MQC. The videos and handouts that are posted are also really great, DEFINITELY WATCH THE VIDEOS IF YOU TAKE THIS CLASS!

HOWEVER, this class is SO. MUCH. WORK. It takes over your life because it is 3 psets a week (I would say this class was 70% of my work). Some of the psets genuinely took me 6+ hours to complete. I got very frustrated by this class at times, and by the end, I was incredibly burnt out. When studying for the final, I genuinely started to get a headache every time I looked at a matrix. 3 psets a week really becomes very strenuous towards the end of the semester.
Also, the grading is kind of harsh and you have to explain a lot more than you think you do on exams.

I wish that this class would relax a little more with the psets, provide videos for every topic (instead of just for some), and just somehow make it a bit less work. If I were to redo the semester, I might take Math 22A instead just to have 1 pset a week instead of 3 (although I'm not sure, because I didn't want to do proofs).
Prepare to allocate significant amounts of time for the psets every two days.
