I highly recommend this expos class!! Dr. Napier is a fantastic professor, and as long as you follow the reading schedules, it's really not difficult to read and understand Austen. The readings are very entertaining, and I learned a lot about how to improve my writing through this course.
Wasn’t the best course but I think better than a lot of the other expos options; I did enjoy reading Jane Austen and the preceptor gave great feedback
I really enjoy Jane Austen, so I had a fun time in this class. If you are not a fan of 19th century novels – do NOT take this class, as this is all you do. However, if you are a fan, the course materials are engaging, and its interesting to write the essays because they're on a topic that you like. Not too much work.
I think that this class is a pretty self–selected group of students, so if you end up taking it, that's likely because you genuinely have interest in the subject matter. I definitely think that some people would not be particularly interested in this subject matter, but I am, and I really enjoyed the class. The revision process seems to mostly mean a major process of thinking over your claim, structure, and reasoning and sometimes starting the process over with a basis in your first draft, which can feel frustrating, but has ultimately been really helpful. If you are considering this course, I'd definitely suggest it!
If you are interested in literature, this course is perfect. Otherwise, this is probably not the course for you.
Don't beat yourself up if you're not the best at this , it's a process and supposed to make you stronger, not the best.
I would definitely recommend taking Austen & Us with Dr. Napier! The class attracts mostly lot of humanities concentrators, but there is still a mix of interests and backgrounds that make for interesting conversations on the text. I'd recommend scheduling office hours with Dr. Napier to make the draft/revisions process go more smoothly – he was always willing to give feedback and talk through my preliminary ideas which helped in the long run. Despite never having read Austen before, I loved being able to read Emma and compare it to the film version of Emma and Clueless. Both the novel and the films were engaging, making reading and viewing assignments easier to tackle.
if you are into Jane austen, literature, feminism, etc, take this expos!! I have so much more appreciation for Emma, Clueless, and Jane Austen after this course. Dr. Napier is really supportive and helpful with the writing process, and I like that he is really passionate about the subject. I mostly enjoyed the peer review process of the class because I made friends, improved as a writer, and got to see how creative people were (people’s topics for the research paper were so cool!). One thing I wish was done differently in this course is understanding perspective. I felt there was a level of depth, especially regarding the relation of Austen’s work to feminism and women in general, that we just didn’t get to dive into because we didn’t hear from female voices on the subject.
I recommend this course if you've had any level of familiarity with Jane Austen's work or read an Austen novel before but you in no way need to be an Austen buff to take this expos. However, it would definitely be easier to have familiarity because we dove right into reading Austen's Emma with a pretty hefty reading load. Draft and revision turnaround times can be kind of brutal especially during busy times in the semester so make sure to plan your time accordingly, but otherwise you have freedom to choose interesting paper topics and learn a lot to genuinely make your writing better.
Definitely more work than your average Expos class, but you learn SO MUCH from Dr. Napier, who's genuinely one of the kindest, funniest, and just best teachers I've had. The technical stuff can be a drag sometimes, but since you have to take Expos anyway, might as well take this one, where you'll actually learn a lot about writing (even if you thought you were a fine writer already).
From what I have heard, this class is definitely one of the better Expos classes. Even if you don't have much prior experience with Jane Austen, it'll be okay and is a great introduction to her works. The main thing to know about this class, which will be true for all Expos classes, is that it is a WRITING class...NOT an English class. So don't expect to discuss the book nearly as much as you would like to, especially with an instructor as awesome as Dr. Napier. That being said, the class overall is great, and Dr. Napier is not only very nice and funny but also really wants you to succeed and improve as much as possible, and the feedback we get on our writing is unlike anything I've ever gotten before. Just start the work early (but even if you don't, you'll be fine, though you will have a hellish few hours/day before drafts/revisions are due), try your hardest, and it'll all be fine. He brings Insomnia cookies on the last day, so if anything, take this class for that.
This class is wonderful –– there is a great class environment and the subject matter is very interesting. No prior knowledge of Austen's works was necessary at all.
