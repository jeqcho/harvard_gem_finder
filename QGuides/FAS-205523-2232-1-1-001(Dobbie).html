Take the course!
Taught at a fairly superficial level. Kind course staff. Light lift in terms of coursework if you know coding and have taken a little econometrics already.
If this is not a degree requirement, you can probably find a better causal inference class.
This is useful refresher course to remind students of the most important assumptions and conceptual frameworks behind leading econometric methods. I would say this is targeted towards intermediate students – not those trying to learn about these methods for the first time, and also not those trying to learn how to apply them in advanced settings.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Causal inference opens up new ways of thinking about social science through rigorous quantitive data analysis. Everyone who wishes to conduct high–quality quantitative research should learn causal inference to get used to thinking and researching in this way. My life has been changed forever by learning the perspectives taught in the class and opened up new ways about thinking about my research.
The class is pretty useful. Very applied (and not very theoretical) and not super quantitative / rigorous. Professor Dobbie is good. Up to date and appropriate for the changing econometric literature.
Don't be afraid to take it! It's not overly difficult and Professor Dobbie is very available and helpful.
