This course is unlike any other that you will take at Harvard. It is fascinating and super engaging, but it is hard and will require a lot of work. I fully believe that it was worth it and every skill that I learned in this course has made me feel more prepared for life after Harvard. Participating in the negotiations was so much fun and writing the strategy and analysis memos were great pre and post negotiation activities. This is one of my favorite courses I've taken here, and will probably be one of yours too, but definitely approach it with the right mindset and be prepared to learn a lot.
If you're an undergraduate at the college, this is one of those classes you remember for the rest of your life. At the very least, that's the case for me. I wouldn't classify it as a class, more like an experience. That experience, however, is incredibly formative and insightful when it comes to anything related to conflict management.
If you are interested in conducting simulated negotiation exercises, this is a fantastic opportunity to do so surrounding relevant geopolitical issues. However, if the historical value is your primary interest, there are better options out there as this class is centered around three negotiation exercises and students are expected to have done the reading about the issues outside of class.
This is by far the best course I took this semester. It is practical, but you also learn so much from a theoretical perspective. I would change nothing about it and I recommend with enthusiasm if you want to 10x your negotiation skills.
Take this class if you want a sort of fun way to deal with negotiation tactics, but it will not actually teach you many new things and act best, will give you a cursory overview of negotiations and post–Soviet conflicts.
This is a fun class and you definitely will learn a lot. Also it is a super low–time commitment during the weeks where is it just class, but the Saturday sessions are a bit long and they are not for everyone.
One of the most unique courses at Harvard. There were probably only 45 minutes of true "lecture" the entire semester yet I learned more in this course than I have in most others. Dr Bell is a genius for how he's set up the exercises where you learn concepts putting them into practice. If you're willing to commit the 3 Saturday, it'll be well worth your while to take this course.
This course was the most practically educational course ive taken at Harvard – I felt that I was learning both negotiation skills, writing skills, as well as people management skills. The course load ebbs and flows, but I would say its most busy at the end of the semester. The course ends all instruction AND assignments about 2 weeks before reading period, a major plus (you will have three Saturdays zapped from ur schedule though). You will be in class with students at GSAS, HKS, from the military, and other schools, and learning from these students was amazing. I will say that due to these demographics, the class tends to be more "real world" than other classes at Harvard – some of these students definitely felt and showed their own self–perceived authority over younger, female members of the class, something that I had yet to experience at college. Despite this, I think that these dynamics reflect ones that await all of us upon graduation, and learning to work around those obstacles was another skill I gained by taking this course.
The course is extremely hands–on and well taught. If you are interested in post–Soviet politics and Eastern Europe and/or considering a job in foreign policy/negotiations, this class is a perfect fit for you. Note that there are three longer Saturday sessions that take place throughout the semester where you do remarkably realistic negotiations exercises on three major geopolitical issues, so be prepared to allocate time for them.

Overall, GOV 1199 honestly inspired me to explore opportunities in the foreign policy sector and, as someone who was quite shy coming in, taught me some valuable life skills about being able to confront the issues that come my way and stick up for myself.
One of the best courses I've ever taken at Harvard. You should absolutely take this class. Negotiation sessions on Saturdays are very enjoyable and fly by. You'll use the negotiation techniques you've developed in this class forever. Class bonds by the end, Nora and Arvid are incredible.
TAKE THIS CLASS! This is hands down the best class I have ever taken at Harvard out of my entire 6 semesters. There are 3 long Saturday sessions of negotiations, and there was genuinely nothing I would rather be doing on a Saturday. I left every session and was so so happy because they made my day. I loved this class. The negotiations were so well organized and built on each other. There are not too many readings which is great because they really let you focus on your negotiation strategy. The writing assignment is a memo where they discourage the academic fluff, which is amazing for applying this to a job. The only downside was that most assignments were due on Fridays 11:59pm which ruined 6 of my Friday nights this semester due to intense procrastination, so please do your Friday assignments on Thursday nights. This class was 60:40 Undergrad: Grad ratio. But the grad students are super nice and are learning just as undergrads are. Do not be afraid to talk up during debates and really take them as an opportunity to work on your debating skills. You will see vast improvements throughout the semester. There are negotiation debriefs that allow you to think about what you did wrong during the debate and apply this to improve your next debate; take these seriously –– they help so much. Overall, best class ever! If you have any doubts, just take it! You will love it!
This course is probably the best one I've taken at Harvard. I don't usually participate much in section and am not comfortable speaking up in general without preparation. The course actually prepared us to negotiate in ways that felt manageable (your negotiation depends on how well you prepared, not how comfortable you are at public speaking). There are no unnecessary readings and lectures are cogent.
