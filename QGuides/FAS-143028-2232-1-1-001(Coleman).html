What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you stay on top of your work, it will be manageable. The theoretical material can be difficult to read, but the class makes it understandable.
Make sure you go to office hours to go over your writing assignment with the TF/Prof beforehand so you're not doing it last minute. Some classes can be a bit boring, especially if you didn't do the readings for that day. Overall, it wasn't my favorite course but it got the job done.
This course is definitely a bit labor–intensive, between reading journal entries due twice a week, long readings twice a week, and three shorter essay assignments throughout the semester, but I definitely emerged from this class with a greater understanding of foundational theories in anthropology and greater insight about how to carefully write about theory and deploy them in practice. This class is a requirement, but you'll definitely come out of it with a better direction of what you're interested in anthropologically.
This course helps to expand your capacity to engage with theory and apply it to wide–ranging events and phenomena. I gained a greater appreciation for how theory intersects with anthropological tools like ethnography.
The course isn't too taxing and the in class discussions are really engaging. Make sure to stay on top of the reading journals though!
