What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're having any doubts about whether you want to continue taking Chinese, take this as a sign that you definitely should!!! I took both 130a and 130b, and the course staff have been phenomenal each semester. I feel that for a relatively small time commitment outside of class (i.e. pretty short assignments, short conversations with teachers from Taiwan), you learn a lot of new vocabulary and grammar structures from this course. My confidence in expressing abstract and complex ideas in Chinese has increased so much this past year, and I remember genuinely looking forward to Chinese class (even in the mornings) every day.
It's a great course if you're dedicated to improving your Chinese. Like any course, the work you put in is what you get out of it. It's definitely more advanced content than the 120 series and even 130a, but if you're consistent with your studying and review it shouldn't be an issue. The course does move at a faster pace and it's easy to be left behind if you're not on top of your work.
I firmly believe the Chinese department is the best language department at Harvard. If you’ve taken other Chinese course at Harvard, then you know what to expect for this class: wonderful and enthusiastic teachers, well structured and thought through lesson plans, and insight into Chinese culture, art, and politics! Of course the time commitment may be a lot with having class five days a week, but it is well worth it. I would not describe it as any more than previous levels. Take this class and watch your Chinese improve, while also meeting some of the best, nicest, and most committed teaching staff around!!
This is a great class. Super fun and the teachers really care about students understanding of Chinese.
Although the 55 new vocab words a week is tough and not all of the words and sentence patterns are the most practical, this class is ultimately a great class due to the excellent teaching staff and Chinese department curriculum. You will emerge from this class a better reader, writer, and speaker of the Chinese language.
This class is absolutely amazing. The 130 series has probably been the courses I have enjoyed the most during my time here at Harvard. The teachers are committed to their students like I have rarely seen and are extremely supportive in light of the intrinsic difficulties of the language. I feel I can confidently express myself about societal issues that impact modern China, and the world.
