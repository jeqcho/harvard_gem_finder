This class is an incredible introduction to sociology, especially if you are already set on concentrating. I felt like I learned so much and will enter each interaction from now on with a new level of empathy. A gem! TAKE THIS COURSE :)
Having taken both this class and Sociology 1000, I would caution students against considering this course to be an "intro to Sociology" course. Do not expect to talk about social theory or anything of this nature in this course—Sociology 1046 is all about real–world outcomes, like how race, sexuality, gender, etc. can impact health outcomes. If you are REALLY interested in how access to resources (dictated by race, neighborhood, etc) impacts health, I recommend this course, as it touches on all of this extensively, but it pretty much only tackles this issue—nothing else in the broader sociological field. If you're into quantitative Sociology, definitely consider this course.
Take this course! It is really interesting if you want to learn more about disparities and inequalities in healthcare. Really important for premed students and highly recommend as one of the foundational courses for GHHP. The readings are manageable and low stress. The weekly discussion posts are about what you read for the week, which are also super chill. The lectures were very interesting and summarizes the readings for you. Pay attention to the lecture summaries of the readings because the exams are reading–focused, but not hard as long as you know the big takeaways and key points from the readings and examples. I suggest keeping up with the readings so you've read the readings at least once. Section will help you with the harder readings and break them down more, but as long as you study the key points of the readings that he summarizes in the lecture slides, you should be fine – know the readings by author though because it helps with putting a name to the argument, which they really emphasize. Jason was a great lecturer and Rachel was an amazing TF – they are so kind and understanding and really helpful. The class overall just felt really chill and you can get a good grade!!

What you learn has so many important implications that reach even further than simply health or health disparities (but it definitely does deepen your understanding for those topics). It is an awesome introduction to sociology and would highly recommend anyone thinking about sociology or medicine or just wanting to understand the world they live in a bit better.
Take this class if you can!! Both professor Jason and the TF, Rachel, are great! The lectures and sections were extremely engaging. The midterms and final are also very manageable as long as you make sure you understand all the important concepts discussed. Overall, you will learn a lot. I highly recommend it!

This course is truly amazing. I actually read basically all of the readings for this course; some were a little dense and I'd skim, but they were genuinely all so fascinating and pressing. Jason and Rachel are the most caring individuals, and I really felt encouraged to explore difficult topics like the ones discussed in class in a safe + nurturing environment. I took this course to count for the recommended premed sociology + as a foundational GHHP requirement, and I 10000% recommend this to anyone considering it! The readings aren't too bad to get through, and the weekly reading responses force you to stay accountable for getting through everything; section is the best time with Rachel's fun icebreakers + open discussion, and I really loved coming to lecture every week. The only thing is that the lecture hall is really comfortable and around noon nap time, so I would get a little sleepy in lecture, so would recommend bringing snacks!

The exams are a little scary at first, all short answer and do require that you actually do the readings + pay attention in lecture/section, so don't let it creep up on you. Definitely go to office hours to get to know Jason, he's the coolest + a great conversationalist, you'll never run out of things to say!
