This is a cool, relatively easy class, and you will learn a lot about the Celts of antiquity and in modern times. Definitely worth taking!
This class is about the Celts, but it also very much about constructing ethnicities and identities. Through the Celts, you learn how complicated the process of identifying the Celts becomes. You learn about the history of the Celts, their subjugation, their revival, and everything in between. If you are interested in understanding what makes people people, this class is a great introduction in how to study people.
Put in the work and you will get a lot out of the course.
take it, it's quite interesting, and could be applicable/relatable to your own life. It's enjoyable, and won't be the class you'll be stressing over
I loved this class! It will change how you look at identity, not only for Celtic people but for other groups as well. It may even change how you think about your own identity :)
Lectures aren't the most engaging and reading can be a bit dry, but overall this is a very low workload class (only a total of 4–5 graded assignments) on a pretty interesting topic. Assignments like the Celtic words project and Iron Age artifacts were much more interesting than your normal essay. If you are looking for a low workload gened, I would recommend this course
Lectures are so dry but sections are great. Don't expect to get much feedback, even if you ask for it.
An excellent HSI GenEd. Not a lot of work at all, but very interesting material. Some interest in Celtic studies, ethnography, or history in general would be helpful to staying engaged –– otherwise just come in with an open mind.
This is a great class to take to learn about the celts specifically and identity more broadly. It covers a range of time periods and fields, and is therefore great at improving interdisciplinary skills.
The content of course (i.e. readings) a be a little dry at times, but it's a good way to satisfy the HSI requirement. Prof. McKenna is wonderful and you can tell a lot of care has gone into crafting the syllabus. Most of the activities/assignments are pretty fun, too! (e.g. museum outing, Celtic word project, Celtic artifact project, ethnonym project) I definitely learned a lot
