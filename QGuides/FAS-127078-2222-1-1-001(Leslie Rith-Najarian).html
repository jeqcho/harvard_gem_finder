Starts easy then gets rapidly harder, very harsh grading
You have to take this class anyway if you're a psych concentrator, so try to take this class before any higher level psych classes. It'll be much easier to understand the higher classes once you take 1900
Your experience in this class will likely depend a lot on your previous experience with, aptitude for, and interest in psychology research. I came into this class with experience from other courses in creating and running psych experiments, so this class was basically a review. On the one hand, it was pretty easy; on the other hand, it felt like a waste of my time. I don't think many students really loved this course, but depending on your previous experience, you might like this course more or less. The positive of this course is that you'll receive a lot of specific feedback on your work, which you won't necessarily find in upper level classes.
The subject of the class can be a bit boring but I think the teaching staff try their best to make the course engaging.
This course covers A LOT. But this course is set up in a way that allows you to understand a wide range of content within the time scope of a semester. Overall I felt like this course was more like an "empty" requirement course than an actual beneficial course.
This course very works heavy, it only shows having 1 lecture and 1 section a week but on top of that you have to watch a 1–2 hour lecture before class and many readings.
This class is perfect and the teaching team is truly amazing. While the final paper might seem daunting, everything is broken down over the course of the semester and you have so much of your paper written before it even comes time for you to write your draft. I found this class quite time–consuming and difficult, and although the content was interesting at times, it wasn't enjoyable due to how stressful it was. Grading was also quite harsh. I felt there was also little guidance in terms of assignments.
I really did not enjoy this course and felt it was unnecessarily stressful with a lot of busywork. On top of class time, there are lectures, readings, homework assignments and group projects. And weekly quizzes. And I did not feel particularly inspired by any of it. It is one of those courses that you kind of just have to get through. Make sure you find a good partner or group at the beginning of the semester when you have to group/pair up because you will be working with those people a lot for the rest of the semester. The project at the end of the semester involves R so it is helpful if you have some background in stats but I guess not necessary. I felt like this course "holds your hand" at all the wrong moments and then throws you in the deep end at all the wrong moments as well. Creating a simple and straightforward study for the final project is also really crucial.
