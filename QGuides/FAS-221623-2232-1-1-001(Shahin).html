This is a fantastic course. Prof. Sharon is very knowledgeable with a lot of anecdotes, but the best part by far is the fact that students present a case about a new country each week, and it is usually the country that they are from because the class is so diverse.
This course would have been great if it were more inclusive of ethnic minorities in the Middle East. Groups like Amazigh and Kurdish communities were briefly mentioned; however, when it came to students like myself advocating for greater conversation about these groups, there was pushback and even disrespect. There was definitely emphasis mainly on Arab and Turkish communities, which are important to learn about, but do not represent the entirety of the "Middle East."
This course was excellent thanks to Professor Shahin who kept the students engaged and interested throughout the semester.

It is a good class. Though background knowledge is helpful, it is overall excellent.
This class is incredible – Professor Shahin is so knowledgeable and kind and willing to discuss difficult topics, and you really learn so much about the Middle East both in the specifics of all countries and very applicable broad themes.

The professor is very flexible and kind.
