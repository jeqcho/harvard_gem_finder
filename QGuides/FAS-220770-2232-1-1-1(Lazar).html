Comments
This class is very accessible to students from all disciplines, so don't shy away if you're not studying MBB. While it may help to have some background in neuroscience or psychology for the readings, it certainly isn't necessary. Broadly, you should take the class if you're interested in virtue, wellness, and/or improving how you look at and live life. This is one of the rare courses at Harvard that will give you the space to do this and to do it well! The course tends to attract students who are interested in deep reflection and genuine growth (another rare thing at Harvard), so you will have ample motivation to become a better person over the semester. As a bonus, the teaching staff is wonderful and extremely caring. Take this class!
This is a good class. It needs to be more organized for next year (syllabus, paper examples, etc.), but overall the content and conversations were amazing. I genuinely enjoyed reading the papers and talking with my classmates. I do think it really depends on your classmates. I'm grateful our class was very engaged, but if no one takes initiative, it might be difficult.
I can not recommend this course enough. I loved it so much. Sara and Michael are phenomenal instructors and genuinely fantastic human beings
THIS COURSE WAS ONE OF MY FAVORITE CLASSES AT HARVARD. You learn so much about yourself and your fellow classmates and have really enlightening discussions about virtues. I will warn that class communication surrounding assignments and deadlines could definitely be improved and I often did not know where my grade stood throughout the semester. Still would highly recommend!
Take it if you want a reflective space on campus!!! Michael is a godsend, and the assignments are doable. You will come out of this a better person if you actually dedicate yourself to reflection!
TAKE THIS CLASS. Michael is an AMAZING professor. Take whatever course he's teaching, even if it's not Virtue Science. Dr. Lazar is also extremely kind. Both Michael and Sara not only want you to succeed as a student, but they also genuinely care that you're mentally well and that you leave Harvard being a good and kind person. As for the class itself, the material is extremely interesting. There's no other class at Harvard like it. You learn so much about the science of compassion, kindness, justice, etc., and the philosophy and psychology of those virtues too. You will not regret this class AT ALL. TAKE IT.
This class made me a better person! Everyone should take it!
