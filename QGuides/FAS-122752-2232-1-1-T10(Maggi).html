What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
When you are picking the courses for the EC970 lottery, I would highly recommend two things: 1) selecting a topic you are interested in, as you will be spending a considerable amount of time reading research papers and 2) if you get a chance, choose Andrés as one of your top choices! Andrés is really passionate about urban economics and his enthusiasm is contagious. He genuinely cares about his students' learning process and I found him to be the most supportive professor I had during my time at Harvard. He was always available to provide guidance and support outside of class, whether it be for Stata problem sets or the final research paper. The class discussions were engaging, and as an introverted person, I felt comfortable speaking up in class. Finally, although the course load may seem intimidating when you initially read the syllabus, Andrés makes it really manageable by spreading out assignments well and in a way that encourages you to get ahead on your research paper. All of the assignments build on the final research paper and are helpful in the whole learning process. Without a doubt, Economics of Cities was my favorite course at Harvard and I highly recommend it if you have the opportunity to take it!

Take Cities with Andrés! It was my favorite class of the semester and one of the best in the econ department. Andrés is extremely kind and patient and really wants you to understand big themes in urban economics and be able to apply your knowledge of economics and econometrics to really important papers in the urban economics literature. I highly recommend even if you have had no prior exposure to urban economics or pre–established interest
I enjoyed this course! I'd highly recommend Andrés' tutorial.
Economics of Cities with Andres is an amazing 970! Definitely rank it very highly, especially if you're interested in urban economics. All of the 970s are a lot of work (reading a paper for each class, writing a paper every week, doing a Stata pset every other week), but Andres makes this one feel much more manageable. The papers we read are incredibly interesting, and I now look at cities in a different way. The class is definitely a bit daunting at first, but Andres makes it very approachable by breaking down the papers into their different components and by clearly showing how the assignments build on each other. He is also very accessible outside of class, and meets with every student individually multiple times, especially during the final research paper process.
