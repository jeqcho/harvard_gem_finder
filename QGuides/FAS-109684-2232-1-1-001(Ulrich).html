Please take this class. It may be on the more difficult side and require more work/effort compared to other ESE classes, but you learn SO MUCH. Patrick is an amazing professor who is so passionate about this topic and is really accessible if you need help. Prepare in advance for the ICEs and you should be fine. This is the ESE class where I feel like I learned and retained the most information due to its intimate style and Patrick's lecture format.
This is a great class and I definitely recommend taking it! Patrick is a great professor and you will learn so much from taking this course! There is a good deal of math involved, which I did not think I would like, but you can see the reasoning and practical application for everything you do in the class; I can genuinely see myself using anything I learned in this class frequently. Because of this, I actually really enjoyed learning the math behind aquatic pollution. This course covers a wide variety of things related to aquatic pollution in great depth, so you come away feeling that you know much more about the course material and can see your progress with every problem set. Patrick gives great feedback and is a big help on assignments, so go to him with your questions about the material!
If you are at all interested in water quality engineering, or you want to explore a new, interesting topic, this course is a wonderful one to take. Homework can be difficult and takes some time, but Patrick is willing to help whenever needed, and lectures give a good foundation towards solving the problems. Patrick is an incredibly organized instructor and generates great enthusiasm for the course overall, and I would strongly recommend taking this course.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Patrick is amazing and you learn so much in such little time. This is be far the most organized, detailed and interesting ESE class I have taken.
