This class was really interesting. It was less discussion–based than I was expecting (Professor Wood talks for a decent portion of the class), which was disappointing to me but may be favorable to others. I was also expecting the consciousness theme of the class to be more about close reading (i.e., understanding the mechanisms by which writers represent consciousness), but it was more about understanding the history of consciousness in literature (i.e., how it changed over time, how different authors compare to each other). In general, we talked less about consciousness than I was expecting. Still, I really enjoyed all the books we read. And as a non–English major who hasn't taken an English class in years, I felt like the course was still very accessible to me. The assignments were very flexible and Professor Wood is super helpful in helping you develop your ideas. TL;DR: I'd recommend this class to anyone looking for an easy/approachable class with a good curriculum and manageable assignments.
I would tell future students to take this class! It was intriguing and a great excuse to read some amazing texts. I will note that the grade largely depended on two essays with no rubrics, so if you want better control over your grades, the class may provide some anxiety. Professor Wood is very kind and will help you think through your ideas, though.
Extremely fun class. Discussions won't be as rigorous as you might be used to, but still incredibly insightful and fun.
this class is amazing! professor wood is so knowledgable about all the texts we read, and its a pleasure just to hear him talk about them!
It's fantastic! Professor Wood is a delightful person who does a great job at keeping us engaged.
It is rare to find a course so rich in substance and with such little flab. Professor Wood is an outstanding teacher–––he is patient, encouraging, and down–to–earth. One thing that Harvard students instinctively do when asked their thoughts on a book is to try to make "subtle" points and delve into esoteric analysis. But Professor Wood simply desired to hear our true, unfiltered impressions of the novels we were reading; at one point, he told a silent classroom, "you don't feel you have to be literary in your answers–––just what did you think?"

Professor Wood also has an infectious enthusiasm for literature that made intimidating or difficult novels so much more fascinating. As a class, we avoided the common tendency to jump straight to "smart" insights; rather, we went through passages we found beautiful, ironic, or particularly amusing. Notably, Professor Wood spent a decent portion of our classes reading out loud. But far from being a pitfall, his reading passages helped me (and other students, I am sure) to better appreciate the various details and colors in the texts. Professor Wood also has a wonderful reading voice! I strongly recommend all students who want to read good novels take this course.
Prof Wood is a treasure and an incredibly brilliant person. He has weekly notes to take away from each novel, and he is incredibly interesting to listen to. Low work, low stress so far! However, lots of reading excerpts out loud
