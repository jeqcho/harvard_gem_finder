This class was informative, but also just plain interesting. I had fun discussing the medieval texts and how people had used them to create their own work in thought provoking or maybe silly ways. The assignments were different from each other and offered the opportunity to express different skills, and were well suited to the course.
Professor Wilson and Andrew are great instructors, really accessible to students. Class has a big discussion component even in lecture, but it’s not stressful or super high stakes. As someone who doesn’t speak much in lecture classes, I always felt comfortable speaking, and it seemed like most of my peers feel the same. Really much more engaging than normal lecture classes. Work is not hard if you’re paying attention, going to section and office hours. As someone who isn’t a humanities concentrator, I feel that I had ample support, and by the time assignments came due, I had plenty to say about the material.
I enjoyed this class! I never would've thought medieval literature and fanfiction could mix, but this class showed me that not only do they mix, they also mix to create some pretty great and hilarious products. Professor Wilson is super funny and very responsive to students. She always brings such great energy to class and gets everyone excited (and laughing) about whatever it is she is teaching that day. Take this class even if you're not an English concentrator and just enjoy the fun!
A great course that will change how you think about fanfiction, creative adaptation, and loving a text. Definitely take it— and choose the creative final if you have the option. (:
This is a really fun class that combines medieval literature and the examination of fanfiction as a medium throughout history. The assignments are mainly in the form of reflections and readings, which were all really manageable and honestly very fun. Even if you don't feel particularly enthusiastic about medieval literature, there will be things you find interesting in this class, and Prof. Wilson and the TF engaged in a lot of insightful discussion about what constitutes fanfiction, why it exists, etc. I particularly enjoyed watching the A24 adaptation of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight, and the creative option for the final project is also a lot of fun. Overall, would highly recommend –– especially as someone who majors in STEM and hasn't really taken English classes here before!
This course will make you interested in medieval literature! It's both fun and informative, the teaching is great, and you get to do a lot of creative assignments if that's your thing. It's especially engaging if you're at all interested in fan culture. I didn't think I would ever enjoy reading Chaucer, but here we are! *
I think this is a great class. It was a very accessible introduction to medieval texts. Professor Wilson is an engaging lecturer, and the size was just right to have good discussion.
Be ready to change your preconceptions of what "fanfiction" and "medieval literature" are. Try to do all of the readings because they tend not to be very long and are all really interesting. Engage as much as you can with the class and you will get a lot out of it.
The title already says it all: such a fresh and fun perspective on medieval literature that I think really lends itself to the young English student who reads classics but also pop culture, fantasy, and YA. And the class itself delivers! Prof. Wilson does a masterful job at bringing her own super insightful ideas while also listening and pulling from what students have to say. The course is both rigorous and lighthearted. For me it was just the perfect fit; one of the highlights of my week. Would recommend to anyone interested in English lit, contemporary fiction + fantasy, and storytelling :).
Took this as an English requirement, but this class BLEW me away and ended up being one of my favorite classes ever in my four years at Harvard. Professor Wilson is exceptionally knowledgeable about medieval literature AND fanfiction theory – what a killer combo. I never enjoyed studying medieval literature before this class. Take it!!! You won't regret it!!
This course is great at breaking down any preconceived notions of what you think Medieval Literature will be. The two medieval texts we read were so funny, vulgar and genuinely enjoyable, which I was not expecting. It really opens your essays to what the history of literature has been. I personally was worried about fulfilling this concentration requirement (pre–1700s lit), but this class was definitely the best class to do that with
I did not think that I would enjoy medieval literature, but it is quite interesting. It is not as scholarly and inaccessible as I initially thought.
