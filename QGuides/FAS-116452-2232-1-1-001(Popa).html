Best class I have ever taken at Harvard so far! Prof. Popa is by far the best at giving lectures, going at the right pace to keep you interested but not blown away by the material. The material itself is very interesting and much more deep than usual iterations of the class. We starting by learning about curves to get intuition, and then learned about varieties and went through Hartshorne's chapter 1. Near the end, we pivoted to covering a lot of material in Harris' textbook such as incidence correspondences, tangent spaces, and tangent cones. Would recommmend if Prof. Popa teaches this class again, or any other classes he teaches in general! This class has changed my life and made me consider (for a very long time) a potential career in algebraic geometry.
Professor Popa is a brilliant instructor. Be prepared for a very challenging course both on a conceptual level and in terms of time commitment, but the lectures were always very engaging and Professor Popa was always willing to meet and help explain things. The course assistants Hahn and Eliot are among the best the Math Department has to offer and always gave very extensive feedback on assignments and were willing to help us catch up whenever things fell behind. The material itself I believe is quite different from how the course was taught before, but which made for a very interesting learning experience. I would highly recommend any course with Professor Popa, but be prepared to put in a lot of hours and carry out some independent reading or research outside of class. In the end, though, I think it was very rewarding. It has been among, if not the, best math courses I have taken at Harvard.
This is truly a great course — one of the best math courses I've taken at Harvard. Prof. Popa is a great lecturer and the class gives a great introduction to the subject. However, as a warning: algebraic geometry is a difficult subject and the problem sets were hard. Prof. Popa covered a lot of material, more than in the previous iteration at least. I found this course to be harder than 55, 114, and 129. Part of this might also have been that I did not know very many people in the class, and I tried to do most of the problem sets alone, but I also think algebraic geometry is just hard. Also, algebraic geometry requires a significant amount of commutative algebra, so you should be warned if you haven't taken 221.
Prof. Popa and this class were both wonderful!!
I think everyone knows this, but Popa's a great lecturer. Plus he's just very friendly and funny, so definitely take this class if he teaches it again! I think problem sets were sometimes a bit on the hard side, but also I wasn't the most studious student so ymmv I guess.
