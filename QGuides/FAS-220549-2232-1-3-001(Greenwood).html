If this class meets again at 9am, please don't let that dissuade you. It is a gift to take this course and although yes the trek can be daunting there has never been a day where I did not leave this course illumniated. The course has fantastic readings and even better lectures from Prof. Greenwood. The course is well structured and has plenty of resources to acclimate to the writing even if you are unfamiliar.
Take this course! It's a fair amount of reading and work, but the literature are important works that students should read, and the lectures are great. Prof. Greenwood is a wonderful instructor who cares about her students.
Emily is a PHENOMENAL interpreter, interlocutor, and teacher. She is one of the most generous professors I have ever had in terms of the time, support, and encouragement she offers. She models such a respectful relationship to her students; she learned our names at the start of the semester, thanked us for our input, and very thoughtfully addressed even the more challenging comments made in class. The subject matter was illuminating. The profound and longtime engagements of Black adapters with the Classics brought spheres that are often presented as separate or at odds rightfully together with a richness and specificity that was absolutely dazzling to me in my first foray into Classical reception, and that was accessible. The session about theater, civic responsibility, and participation came just days after the public release of body camera footage of police violence against Tyre Nichols and was one of the most provocative and thoughtful classroom sessions I have ever had. I will be thinking about this class for a long time, and it was the highlight of my semester.
