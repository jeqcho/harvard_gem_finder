Really interesting topic and great course. The instructors were great and lectures definitely improved over the course of the semester. You learn very applicable skills and practice applying them with code and implementations. Weekly problem sets are pretty fair, though some require going to office hours to figure out what is required.
Take this course! Differential privacy was not a field that I was super familiar with before taking this class, but this course offered a perspective from both the research side and the industry side that highlighted the importance of this field. Salil, James, and Wanrong were fantastic instructors, and I have no doubt that they will only improve on the already excellent problem sets and lectures that they presented this past year in the next iteration of the class. This class offered a breath of different ideas, drawn from statistics, theoretical computer science, programming, and law.
take it if you're interested in coding privacy– no other course like this at Harvard. gets a bit repetitive at times but worth taking to learn more about the field!
Excellent course –– highly recommend. The three instructors (+ many TFs) provide excellent support throughout the course and are very accessible. The lectures are excellent, but be prepared to do readings before every class, as it is mandatory and 1/3 of the course grade. If you are at all interested in privacy, or just looking for a very well taught and organized CS elective, I highly recommend!
Interesting class with excellent teachers and TFs
Office hours are helpful for homework, and you learn a lot of cool technologies about DP. The course staff are very supportive.
Highly recommend this course! Great profs and teaching staff with material that is well balanced between theory/application/law/ethics.
CS 208 was wonderful – the kind and knowledgeable course staff introduced us to the realm of differential privacy through a mix of theory, real–world examples/case studies, and implementation. This course was not overly theoretical (like other Harvard CS courses) but offered formalism enough to the point where I was interested in learning more. The final project was a rewarding experience, and class community was great :)

Wanrong and James are also amazing and contribute with new perspectives on the material. The psets are reasonable, but start early.
