What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Definitely try your best to get a section that discusses something you are genuinely interested in! It makes such a huge difference, especially given that your grade focuses around a research topic and paper/data collection that relates to your chosen topic.
Take the seminar with Professor Ziblatt if you can!! I looked forward to this class every week––the readings about democracy are engaging; Ziblatt provides extensive feedback and makes himself very available to help students (he is a very understanding and compassionate professor in general); you will learn how to write a Gov–style paper in manageable steps; and the 6 lectures cover a fascinating array of topics.
It is a great class that will teach you how to write a political science paper properly without assuming any existing knowledge. The tutorial you get sorted into really defines your experience, so hope that you get your top choice!
This class is awesome! Professor Ziblatt is very intelligent, and he's an enthusiastic professor. He provides amazing feedback that will make you a better writer, and he's always available to talk about your writing.
This class did an excellent job teaching students how to write a formal research paper as prospective social scientists. I appreciated that the course empowered students to work toward a final research paper in increments over the course of the semester, as this allowed students to solicit and incorporate feedback in an effective way. Another strength of the course consisted of its guest lecturers, as each speaker presented a fascinating perspective of political science as a discipline.

An important piece of advice that I will give to future students is to ATTEND OFFICE HOURS AS MUCH AS YOU CAN in advance of paper deadlines, including and especially the first one. You will do well in the course by receiving and incorporating as MUCH feedback/advice from your section head as possible. Seriously, trust me on this. You will not regret it!
