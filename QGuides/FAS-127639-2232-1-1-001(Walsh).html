ES227 is a very good class, but it is incredibly challenging and time consuming. The teaching staff has very high expectations, which definitely helps the team work hard and produce a good product, but you will find yourself obsessing over what the teaching staff wants rather than what your stakeholder is most interested in. Be aware that ES227 has more class time associated with it than ES96 (MW 1.15 hour class, F 2.45 hour lab, weekly 1 hour meeting with the teaching staff, weekly or biweekly meeting with your stakeholder). If you are mechanical engineering, keep in mind that the projects may be more electrical focused, so be prepared to be unfamiliar with a lot of the design work you are doing. Overall I am really glad I took this class, but I'm really not sure if it was worth the constant stress and frustration over this semester. Finally, I cannot emphasize enough that you should not take this course at the same time as ES128.
This class is one of the top three classes I have taken at Harvard. If you have the chance, definitely go with this class over ES96. It will be more work than ES96 but you get so much more out of it, especially with designing and executing solutions.
If you are looking at this class you are possibly a junior trying to find an alternative to ES96. Thats what I was. This course will give you everything that 96 might lack – you will become very familiar with the problem solving process, you will have the opportunity to make something tangible to fill a niche in an industry, you will learn how to consult with your client during the problem solving process, it can be very fulfilling!

However. If you have any concerns about time management, or are looking for a lighter/somewhat easy class, I do not recommend 227. Looking at you, engineers taking hard classes, student athletes, people who want space to devote more time to things outside of school. This class is a huge undertaking – there is always something that can be worked on, a process that needs to be optimized, better diagrams that should be made, meetings with stakeholders that should be scheduled. I spent so much time working on this project outside of class, and it was exhausting. We started early and definitely tried to stay on top of things, but eventually it landslides. All of this to say, it is a huge time commitment! And it isn't just busy work – a lot of times since you are trying to develop a new product, you have no references for how to do something so you need to be meeting with teaching staff, grad students, other professors, ect to figure out how to make something.

And be prepared to feel like crap! You have one semester to build a whole project, and it will not be perfect or even possibly functional at the end of the semester. This will feel crappy.

Basically, it gives you a really awesome opportunity to do something really cool, but it is also a HUGE time drain.
This class felt like twice as much work as ES 96, but you get to make something at a high level so there are real concrete, tangible outcomes.
This class is ton of stress and work but there is a lot you can learn from it.
This course is crazy crazy crazy difficult and time consuming, but you will be working on something that is actually a real no–joke engineering project. Don't take this class with any other tough ones.
This course is a substantial time commitment. You have lecture twice a week, a 2.5 hour lab once a week and an hour–long staff meeting with the teaching staff and your group once a week. On top of that, there are 6 assignments toward the beginning of the semester, and you are expected to be working on your project with your team outside of class hours. The amount of time per week spent on this class is about 10–20 hours. Additionally, since the whole semester is group work, it is definitely important to have reliable teammates.
