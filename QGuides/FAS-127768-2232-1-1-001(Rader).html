What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class provides a lot of practice in statistical writing and presenting. The feedback is a little slow though and there is a lack of grade transparency. You definitely have the opportunity to explore a range of statistical topics, but this class does not help with the process of finding an advisor to work with.
While this course's mission was all about receiving feedback and iterating on progress to improve at researching and presenting topics in statistics, the sheer lack of feedback in the 9 person class made this mission impossible. We did not receive feedback on many of the assignments and presentations we gave, and that made it difficult to know how to improve or continue developing skills. The class provided a good amount of freedom in exploring topics in statistics that are interesting to you, but the quick turnarounds and 9pm due dates often made this experience less enjoyable. It was somewhat frustrating that the course was flexible most of the time, but then had incredibly quick deadlines expecting us to make a lot of progress. The final project does help with thesising/deciding if you want to, but it is just 1 of the 3 projects during the semester (so not recommended you take the course just for the sake of finding a thesis idea).
