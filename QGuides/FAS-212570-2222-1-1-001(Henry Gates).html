What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is one of the greatest courses I have taken at Harvard.

As an economics concentrator, I have never taken an English class (let alone one about the Harlem Renaissance!), and enrolled in this class last–minute on a whim. I am extremely glad I made this decision, as English 276LR taught me so much.

The greatest strength of the course, without a doubt, is Professor Gates himself. He is not only a wonderful facilitator of every class (and has plenty of amazing stories to share from his illustrious career), but he is also very accessible to all his students (and gets to know them very well!).

Georgia, the course's TF, is also an absolute gem. She is extremely helpful toward students' every need, and does an outstanding job guiding the class behind the scenes.

A third strength of the course is its reading material. The articles, essays, books, and visuals we embrace not only allow us to embrace the African American literary tradition, they also shed light upon struggles and challenges endured by black America as early as the Harlem Renaissance, and as late as today.

Perhaps the most underrated benefit of the class was its participants. Every Tuesday gathering in the Hutchins Center allowed for incredibly nuanced conversation among the diverse set of students hailing from different backgrounds, and left us feeling enriched with broadened perspectives.

I cannot recommend this class highly enough!
Please take this course now, bucket list Harvard course. Professor Gates is amazing—lives up to the hype and more. Plus, he brings free copies of new books to class and really makes time for you. He even grades papers personally and provides detailed feedback. No English background required if you're willing to do the work. Take this course now! Seriously, best course I've taken in all 4 years of college.
Really great class! I went in without any previous experience in grad–level english classes, or really any english classes beyond freshman year expos, and I definitely feel like I got a ton out of the course + I never felt overwhelmed or out of place in class
This class provides an opportunity to gain knowledge that is not typically afforded in early education. It's important to come prepared to challenge notions and perceptions of African Americans and what contributions they/we have made to society and the world. It supplements false narratives of history that need to be addressed and replaced in order to showcase the rich history of America beyond the status quo.
