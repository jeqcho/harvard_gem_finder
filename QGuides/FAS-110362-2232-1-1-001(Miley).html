What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Be prepared to work a lot and dont be married to your first idea
Highly recommend the course if you're looking for a creative outlet and want to improve as an architect, and you have the time for late night model assembly! It's quite a bit of work but as someone who enjoys working with my hands, it's really fun and fulfilling, and you'll grow a lot and get a taste for more realistic architectural design. Be upfront with the TFs if something comes up and you can't get the deliverables in; they're very willing to work with you.
the last assignment takes like half the semester
Another wonderful studio, though very different than 96A. Pace in the beginning feels a lot slower than 96A and is also a lot more unstructured –– 96A requires you to develop a pattern and continuing iterating off of that, whereas 96B really allows you to do anything in the first few months. Final project is a lot more detail oriented than in 96A, really requiring you to consider the interior layout of a space and include functional spaces (like bathrooms, stairs, etc...) that were not really considered in 96A. Course definitely ramps out for final assignment, as they push you early to put out process models and pace of course at end is definitely more intense than 96A, though you are prepared for it! Ultimately, up to you on how much you want to push and iterate.
This is a very time consuming class, so I would not take this class just for fun. However, after taking HAA96a, it is much easier to know how to properly manage your time for an architecture studio course. The TFs are also extremely nice and helpful and will provide you with plenty of support if you ask them for it.
Take the course only if you are very interested in Architecture as a field of study and are willing to put in a good amount of time into projects. As you are likely looking into this after taking HAA 96A, I would suggest to continue through the architecture track and take this course.
