This is, without a doubt, the best EXPOS section there is. Don't even look for other sections; this is THE ONE. Even if you're not interested in immigration, you will be after one reading or a single discussion in class. There are a few readings per week, but they are typically short, no more than 1–hour weekly max, and a small discussion post on the readings (15 min max). The best part of this class are the in–class discussions. They are very intellectual, thoughtful, and intriguing. Every class, there is a discussion, which sometimes turns into debate (those are the best days). Dr. Routon is very sweet, kind, and considering of her students. She gives extensions when needed, while meet outside of class, and is willing to do whatever to help ease your stress from the workload. The class is a gem because of her.
Overall, this Expos was fantastic! I had many friends who had terrible experiences, and they always told me they wished they took this Expos instead. Dr. Routon was fantastic, very kind and understanding, and always provided really helpful feedback on essays. Additionally, the discussions that we had i class were always thought–provoking and interesting. It's a shame that Dr. Routon is leaving after this year. As a result, I am not sure if this Expos will ever run again, or if it does, who will be the instructor in future years. My overall advice for picking a good Expos is: DO YOUR RESEARCH! Pick a course that got the best reviews on the Q guide, not which subject sounds most interesting. Everyone I know that had a good experience in this writing requirement picked their Expos very carefully based on reviews of the class and the teacher.
Worth taking. Engaging. Nice environment to learn from your peers.
