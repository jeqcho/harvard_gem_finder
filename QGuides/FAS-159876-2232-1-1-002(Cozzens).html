What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Despite this course being a challenge in the second semester, I would recommend this because it establishes a baseline understanding of Arabic and the grammatical rules anyone can detect with this knowledge. Very useful, and Ustaadh Richard is a great professor!
I strongly recommend taking this course! Harvard's Arabic program is rigorous, and your Arabic will improve quickly simply by being present in the classroom and doing the homework!
1) Professor can be rude. I get students coming in late is not ideal, but nagging them all class about it is not a welcoming learning environment anyone wants to be in. Attitude really needs to change because the behavior with students is unacceptable at times. Throwing a marker at student, for example, was crossing a line.
2) Too many assignments. Hard to keep track of all of these "smaller pieces" and easy to mess up your grade just because there are so many moving bits you can lose track of. There should be more "big assignments" that are longer term and less assignments overall.
3) Lecture is not engaging and does not review course material effectively. The disconnect between the textbook, lecture and quizzes is large. Also, does not utilize more innovative technology or resources out there. I was really bored, it just wasn't fun.
4) Feedback not helpful sometimes.
5) Hard to access office hours w/course staff sometimes––I recommend calendly or something.
6) The class felt speaking heavy over writing heavy––there was a lot more speaking practice than writing practice.
7) In–class activities did not capture different levels of learning. The amount of time given for writing practice in class was sometimes not enough for some to complete their work. Lack of in–class resources other than the professor himself to assist struggles also did not help.
8) Professor hovers too much over your in–class activity, you can't take a breath without him watching you in class, it's too suffocating. You aren't really allowed an opportunity to be in charge of your own in–class learning experience.
This is an excellent course, and puts into practice everything you've learned from Arabic AA. Ustaadh Richard is a fantastic teacher, and takes personal care to ensure that every student masters the material.
