What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Wonderful course and very accessible brilliant professor
TAKE THIS CLASS. If you are at all interested in learning about Dante’s Divine Comedy then this is the best course to do it. The discussions are open and the class will pretty much go anywhere that the students want it to. Professor Camozzi Pistoja is also the BEST person to teach this course because he is deeply involved in research on the comedy and also trusts the students to pick topics that they are interested in. You are also not required to write any papers in this class which is a big plus for those who love to discuss literature but don’t necessarily love writing about it. Also given the nature of the comedy it is helpful to know Italian but absolutely not required.
This course explores great poetry but remains very accessible even to those who might not consider poetry or literature one of their strengths. Three aspects of the Commedia to which to look forward: (1) The pilgrim's spiritual journey, (2) Dante's nearly incredible and long–undiscovered structural details, and (3) linguistic and metrical features.
This is an amazing course that will offer plentiful space for reflection and efflorescence.
This is an excellent course! Professor Pistoja is one of the best seminar leaders I've had. He is extremely generous and attentive, is an electrifying lecturer, and helps everyone feel a live and vital connection to the text.
This class is the most interesting class I've had at Harvard.
Ambrogio is so, so sweet and will meet with you at least 5–6 times during the semester to get to know you but also to help with your final project. He is so kind and so smart and will be inspired by whatever you find interesting in the text. I'd say, take the class just to develop a relationship with this special man. Also because you will read the Divine Comedy, which is an incredible work. Don't be scared off by not knowing Italian –– I didn't know Italian, and it was never an issue. Also, the workload for this class is reallllyy minimal :)
This course was a really relaxed way to look at The Divine Comedy, but you have to be prepared to actually read the material and put in that time to reflect on it; if you do the bare minimum, you might still feel like you have very little overall understanding of Dante. Some of the ideas we discussed were interesting, but class could be a bit long and disorganized. That being said, Prof. C–P really wants to get to know students and work with them, unlike most other professors, and the final project is a way to look at something in a less stressful way than, say, a rigid essay.
While you might not have prematurely wanted to descend into Hell before, to journey down into the Inferno and then up to Paradise through Purgatory with Professor Camozzi Pistoja is the experience of an academic lifetime. The text is, of course, marvelous, but just as thrilling are the innumerable mysteries and symmetries and patterns hidden before your very eyes in each of the lines of the Commedia that Professor Camozzi Pistoja will reveal to you every class. Do your readings carefully––they are never excessively long, and re–read parts you find especially delightful. The more you invest in the text, the more exciting class discussions will be (ours were unfailingly superb). I feel truly lucky to have been able to take this class.
