What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
BEST CLASS EVER !! Honestly this class is just so unique that I don't even know where to start but basically you write songs every few weeks and share/perform them with your community cohort. The songs are based on a whole variety of spiritual and psychological needs that the class brainstorms and it's super cool to see how we can use the creative and emotional aspects of music and songwriting to address them. This is powerful stuff that will change your perspective on music forever! And you get to meet super cool people and just jam what more do I even need to say ,,,,,
Take it! You will learn so much from Prof. Spalding and your incredibly talented peers.
Don't be afraid to apply if you don't come from a strictly singer–songwriter (or even vocal) background –– music here is defined expansively and capaciously, and Professor Spalding is easily one of the most life–affirming and supportive and mind/heart–opening people not only on this campus but (I think :) ) in this world.
This course created a class environment unlike anything else I've seen at Harvard. It felt intimate and like a place we could safely share our music. I think we could spend more time on workshopping our songs and learning how to better write our music –– sometimes it felt much more like show and tell in the sense that the actual writing part we did 100% on our own. Overall, this course expanded my sense of what music can be and made me more open to creating new kinds of music –– I started improvising on the piano, incorporating the audience into my songs, etc.
This class was incredible, not only because Professor Spalding curated a welcoming and artistically inspiring space, but also because you will be able to meet and collaborate with amazing and talented classmates. Our after class jams taught me so much about collaborative music–making and showed me the way we can curate spaces in which everyone feels comfortable being an active participant.
