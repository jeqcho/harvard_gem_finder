This class is hard. It's the intro class for the History concentration, so there are a lot of historical reading, writing, analysis, and research skills that will be covered. It will make you a better writer, but it's a grind for a lot of the semester. Especially the second half, when you start working towards the final research paper, there's pretty much something big due every week. It's manageable to do within the given time frames, but don't underestimate how much time the assignments will take you. Best advice would be to start the assignments as early as you can, even if it is just getting some ideas down to get your thoughts going. I do think that peoples' experiences varied a lot between class sections, but that is not really something you can control. Just stay on top of the work and do your best. Overall, the difficulty of the class should not deter you from considering history as a concentration!
