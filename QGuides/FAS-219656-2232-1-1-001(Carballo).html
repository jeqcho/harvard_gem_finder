This GENED is an archaeology class masking as a GENED. If you are interested in excavations, the rise of civilization in Mexico, and pottery, you would enjoy this class. However, if you thought that you would be learning about food and how the cuisine that we have eaten over time reveals things about our culture and ourselves, you would be mistaken. The lectures were, honestly, very very boring. The lectures would cover some facts about the time period of Mexico that was being discussed that week that probably could have been a single PDF handed out to everyone in the class, yet the reading assignments were often incredibly dense, archaeological texts and scholarship with terms and concepts that I had no prior clue how to understand. However, the sections were relatively fun (we got to explore museums and interact directly with archives of pottery from the time of the Aztecs), and the assignments are very easy + the final paper topic is entirely up to your discretion, which was nice. Thus, I would take this class if I just needed an easy class but didn't care about whether or not the material was truly interesting or not.
This course definitely has a strong focus on archaeology, which means we do a lot of work looking at a lot of pottery and ceramics!
