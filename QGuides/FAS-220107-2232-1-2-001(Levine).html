Take this class if you like horror, but not just horror movies – the history of performing horror and performance theory are discussed and can be difficult to understand to beginners. It's not just for people who like the movie...!
The course material is very interesting and allows you to get experimental theatre experience. I think the space is very welcoming and gives a lot of personal freedom, but I found it difficult to gauge how I was doing in the class.
How many other times will you get to make fake blood for your dramatic final performance? Come on, this class is great!
Syllabus gets pretty fuzzy, so I'll just say exactly what this version of the class was. The structure seems like it might change if the class is offered again, so take with a grain of salt:

–– First few weeks of the class were half English, half TDM. We read/watched The Exorcist and did presentations on certain aspects of it, and we would also have a speaker come most weeks to teach us something about theater, usually in a hands–on way.

–– Last few weeks of the class were based around performances. We were put in small groups to make mini theatrical pieces that related to the exorcist in some way. After the first one, groups were shuffled and we made a new piece. Over the last month, we would perform this new piece, get comments for how to improve it, and make those improvements for the following week until the final class.

For people thinking about taking this class, I'd say that the first half is generally very good. Second half is much more of a toss–up, depending on how OK you are with making a theatrical piece with varying levels of guidance and then performing it several times and working with the same small group of people over the course of a month.
