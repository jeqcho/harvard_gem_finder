Comments
Do not go into this class expecting a seminar style discussion. Instead expect a broad overview of topics rather than specificity!
This is a great class. Professor Rivera is an incredible professor and Steve is a great TF. The final project is a great opportunity to learn more about a place in detail and explore different methods of research and writing.
Absolutely take this class! It has probably been one of the best, if not the best, course I've taken at Harvard so far. Professor Rivera is incredible –– so kind and so smart. I loved just sitting in class and listening to her speak. I even enjoyed listening to all of my classmates speak. Because it's technically a grad school class, the people in the class came from all over –– div school, various other grad schools, Nieman fellows, other college students. Everyone had such different backgrounds and perspectives, which contributed to fascinating course discussions. Also, it was clear that pretty much everyone consistently did the reading, which I can't say is true in undergrad seminars. It was really refreshing to sit in class and have truly productive and impactful conversations. Sometimes, I didn't even want to say anything at all –– I just wanted to listen to the wonderful things everyone else had to say. The readings are chosen intentionally, and they are all super interesting. If you have an interest in environment, catastrophe/revelation, and coloniality, I absolutely recommend taking this course. The research project was definitely a daunting assignment. It's a long paper, and it requires work throughout the semester, something I struggle with, especially when taking pset classes concurrently. But, Steve, the TF, was so supportive throughout the entire process. And, if you put some work in, you will create something that you are proud of, which is very rewarding. I'm actually really glad that I had the opportunity to write such a long research paper. At the end of the semester, we did a symposium where everyone presented for a few minutes on their projects. It was incredible to hear the rich variety of topics and styles, and I'm really grateful that I got to learn from so many people in this course. I truly can't recommend this class enough –– if you are thinking of taking it, do it!!
