This class is one of my favorite Harvard classes of all time. Tom is an amazing instructor and he tries his best to make the class as engaging as possible. Reading a book a week can feel really daunting so make sure to manage your time appropriately so you don't fall behind. The overall class feels like a book club which makes the experience so much fun. Would definitely recommend this course!
If you can take any course with Tom, I'd highly (HIGHLY) encourage you to do so – he is so kind, so enthusiastic, and such a wonderful discussion moderator and person. I don't think this course would be the same without him. The ethnographies were interesting to discuss and critique, and the opportunity to chat with authors was quite cool
Highly recommend this course, Professor Wooten is an enthusiastic and skilled teacher and you will learn a lot about the practice modern ethnography
I cannot recommend this class and Tom Wooten enough. Whether you are a sociologist or are just interested in ethnography and what the study of people can reveal about ourselves and society, this is a must–take class. Discussion was rich, allowing students not only to deepen their own thinking but also to learn about and become friends with one another in the process. The structure of assignments around relatively low–lift (but important) weekly discussion posts allowed more time to be dedicated to close reading, which only upped engagement and made discussion more interesting as a result.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Seriously you will not regret it. One book a week seems intense but it's quite manageable and the works are usually very interesting. You will learn so much about ethnography and the world in general. Tom is great at facilitating discussion and I felt I really got to know the people in the seminar by the end. We even had a little party after our last class :))
Take it! Ethnography as a whole is something that I now feel really strong about students having exposure to. It's really exciting to see how the things we learn in our Sociology classes can both be applied to the real world and thrown out the window when you engage in such meaningful, committed research. It was a great opportunity to explore niche topics while also learning a ton from your classmates!
Tom is an amazing professor – possibly the best I've ever had. Take this course if you're willing to read a book a week because it can definitely pile up and this is not a class you want to slack off in – the best of the course comes from participating and understanding what's going on in discussion. I highly, highly recommend it. You'll learn so much about research and ethnography and so many interesting topics like surveillance, sex work, houselessness, partying, etc. it's just a great time. Take it with your friends!!
This course really opened my eyes to the different kinds of inequality and injustices that influence different kinds of societies, local and far. Tom does an amazing job at facilitating discussions and asking thought–provoking questions. I learned so much from him as well as the other students in the class; I could tell everyone was really interested in the ethnographies we read and what other people had to say. It was so cool to chat with the actual authors of these ethnographies and offer them feedback since some of the books weren't published yet. I read so many ethnography books in this class that I probably would not have read otherwise and even though it was a lot of reading (a book a week), I truly enjoyed this class and could not recommend it enough!

But I loved each and every ethnography so much, and I genuinely took so much away from the course. This course honestly inspired me to pursue ethnographic research. Tom is such an amazing instructor, and he's super chill and comfortable to talk towhich I appreciate a lot! Also, the discussions in this class were so productive, and I learned so much from everyone.
