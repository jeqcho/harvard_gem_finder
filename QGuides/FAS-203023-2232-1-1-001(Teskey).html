Comments
This class gives you the time and support to unpack and understand an intimidating text, which really helped me gain confidence as a reader. Professor Teskey is so kind and also an extremely interesting lecturer so class was always a pleasure. The reading load is completely manageable compared to other English classes I've taken, and so are the assignments. Overall a very solid English class.
As Prof. Teskey would say of our class, "You're doing something most people haven't and won't ever do, that is, read Paradise Lost." A course that will change your views of human behavior, relationships with others and yourself, politics, gender, ALL OF THE ABOVE AND MORE. Take it.
Paradise Lost is probably one of the most meaningful books I have ever read, and that is because of this class. Milton's poetry is beyond gorgeous, and the intense questions of theology, compassion, mortality, and sin are timeless. I say this having not practiced any religion seriously in college. While the lectures aren't always the most structured and sometimes leave something to be desired, the course staff does an incredible job of conveying their enthusiasm for Paradise Lost which goes a long way. A class that is incredibly valuable if you are willing to let it change you.
Take this class!
Great straightforward class – you read one book and write two papers. There is a memorization component which takes longer than expected but ended up being very fulfilling. Think deeply about the human condition without extra random assignments. Low workload, particularly if you're good at writing papers :)
This is a poem you should read and understand. Even if you don't agree with it, it will help you discover yourself.
This class is a really awesome class. It's tough, but it's a good tough. This is one of the classes that most made me feel like a real Harvard student. Professor Teskey is a super kind and approachable person, and the class can really be for anyone.
TAKE THIS CLASS!
You have to put a lot of time and effort into your writings/projects/essays – it is an English course, after all! Don't blow off these assignments, as they make up nearly all of the final grade. That being said, this is a great course that I would recommend to anyone!
