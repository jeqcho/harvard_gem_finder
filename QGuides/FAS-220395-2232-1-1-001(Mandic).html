This class is very theory–based and focuses heavily on very specific situations written by the authors. For example, in the gender week, you don't learn about general gender in sociology but read specific readings about women in guerilla camps or women taking sick days off from work. If you take this class expecting to learn general sociology topics and talk about the modern world, then this class would not be good for you.

This class is really good if you are thinking of going into sociology, just be ready for heavy reading and quizzes each class.
This course provides a solid introduction to some of the most important theories and concepts in sociology. The weekly quizzes aren't too bad (though they are weighted pretty heavily in the aggregate), and the midterm exam/final paper combination is helpful.
Please consider another sociology course first before enrolling in this one. I had taken an advanced sociology course before taking this one and I did better overall, learned more and enjoyed the course more.

Unfortunately the course is just a lot of work for a singular intro class. Every single week there are at least 3–4 readings that are mandatory because they are discussed in the mandatory section. The entire course revolves around the readings and because of this there is a quiz at the end of every class, making up 25% of your overall grade. These quizzes are insanely frustrating because they are meant to trick you, and even if you completely understand the overall meaning of a reading, you can still do poorly on the readings.

A good thing about this course is the teaching team, they are all so nice and put so much effort into the workings of this class and caring for the well–being of students. Danilo is honestly so passionate about sociology that makes his lectures really engaging.
This class is great if you're looking for a "What is Sociology" type class. Stay on top of the respective readings (at least get the main idea of all of them) before each lecture, and find people to talk about the readings with. Mandic's lecture style is really engaging and the topics themselves are really interesting. Most of the readings are broadly interesting probably for most people, with a couple more rigorous ones being harder to understand.
