love this class, Prof. Siegel is such a character in the best way possible, intimidating to speak in it at first when you're a first–year, but overall it is so good
I hoped that this course would be an interesting intersection of three passions of mine: philosophy, psychology, and political science. Unfortunately, it was elementary and disorganized musings about political emotions, completely uninformed by any expert scholarship in either of the three fields. Lectures were disorganized and difficult to follow. Sometimes handouts were provided, and if they were, they were just the text that the professor was saying, which was helpful when you inevitably lose track of Professor Siegel's very fast and difficult–to–follow talking. Even though there were very few assignments, I received absolutely no feedback on any of them (I'm writing this 2 days before the end of classes). So that was frustrating. If you're looking at this class, I suggest you just take a philosophy class, a government class, or a psychology class. You'll actually learn something substantive there.
This is a really nice interdisciplinary course. Though it's labeled as an upper–level undergrad & grad seminar in the Phil department, it's very reasonable in terms of workload and the classroom environment is not intimidating at all. Prof Siegel does a great job of empowering students to feel comfortable sharing their ideas, even half–baked ones. The course is definitely more political psychology than it is philosophy, but analytic philosophy does weave throughout. It would be a benefit to have some philosophy background to get a fuller analytic experience, but I don't think it's actually required to understand the course contents. I thought a lot of the topics we covered (authoritarian personality, civic friendship, schadenfreude) were incredibly interesting and new (to me, at least). The pacing was also very nice. Highly recommend this class!
If you're interested in politics/psychology/philosophy, you must take this course. It will explain why our society is so divided today.
This class was all–around exceptional. I looked forward to the class each week and two hours always went by quickly.
– Professor Siegel is an outstanding professor, now one of my top favorites at Harvard, who enriched our learnings with excellent readings while moderating rich conversations in class. I feel fortunate to have been Professor Siegel's student, a top researcher in her field, and a phenomenal teacher with a rare mix of compassion and benevolence of presence for her students.
– Professor Siegel generously prepared helpful handouts for us for most classes. The readings to do before class also allowed us to focus on engaging in class and actively learn from it.
– Every student in the class has been detrimental to my learning: our discussions were always very lively, and each contribution was significant.The class was an excellent mix of learning from academic papers and from my classmates, who all had insightful comments in class. It was never boring!
Prepare for some intense readings but done be afraid to reach out to the professor! She cares a lot and is very helpful
Take it. If you keep up with reading, it is rewarding, and Prof. Siegel asks you to do a fair, but not overbearing number of assignments.
This class is great! Balance of work is ideal and the two essays made a lot of sense. Great discussions, interesting content, and great structure!
