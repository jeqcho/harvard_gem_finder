I would say that this is a class where the content is fascinating, though it definitely could use a bit of an organizational overhaul. It is a decent amount of work in terms of readings that sometimes can get a bit repetitive and dense.
I loved this class, especially the lectures which exposed us to a diverse array of stories from the history of Latin America. Weld is very easy to listen to and understand. I took it as a freshman and the difficulty level not bad at all – definitely appropriate for anyone who is interested in LATAM, not just history concentrators! I'm personally recommending this class to many of my friends and would take it again if I could!
This class is absolutely crucial to understanding Latin America. It is necessary to understanding that part of the world and challenged my understanding of that region and even the United States. I absolutely recommend it and see it as necessary. However, a lot of weight is put on only a midterm, final paper, and final exam. Also I was not the biggest fan of section. That should not be a deterrent though from taking this.
The content of this class was super interesting. Professor Weld definitely has her own view point on the material which comes across in lectures/section, but it was super interesting content nonetheless. Readings are a lot, but mostly interesting. Be mindful that the class grade is just the midterm, final, and research paper. You will have both a final and research paper at roughly the same time! The research paper is also independent; there's no draft or comments or anything like that.
You really learn a lot about LatAm's history, culture, and struggles — and a lot about US history too that isn't always told or said outright. The lectures contain a lot of information and sometimes you can't go super in depth into a topic, which can be frustrated, but you'll always feel like you're coming away from lecture with something. The class has a moderate amount of work (readings, a midterm) and can get a little overwhelming towards the end, but usually the focus is just on learning. (Also, section doesn't make or break the class but I would check out the TF before taking.)
Take this class!!! But be prepared for a challenge. Professor Weld is incredible and supportive. The level of understanding we gained on Latin America was unequaled. Everybody should take this course, but prepare for keeping track of a lot of information. Professor Weld assigns a lot of readings, but every single one has a purpose to helping grow our understanding of the history. I would completely recommend this course.
For any future students, do not stress about the number of readings or the few assignments that are technically assigned. Enjoy the content that the course provides because everything in the course is very interesting to learn and how it is taught is very engaging.
This class was a good survey course! The exams are difficult. The Professor speaks too fast and doesn't have slides with words besides a few names which can make the class hard to approach if you do not speak Spanish. I think the readings are too long and we cannot have very good discussions if the TF is not prepared.
This is a solid course if you're interested in Latin American history, need to do your distribution requirement, aren't a History concentrator, are a History concentrator etc. I took it for my History concentration international history requirement as a sophomore and liked it but it was probably my least favorite history class I've taken so far, partly because it's an intro and intros are always more broad/similar vibes to HS history courses with big overviews overtime. The course is really really broad – it is technically Modern Latin American history, but you focus a good amount on the colonial period and 19th century history, so it's really a massive overview. This means we didn't talk about some countries, talked about some a lot more than others, focused primarily on political and economic history, and were focused mostly on drawing connections across the region. It's definitely flawed for this reason, but it's an accessible intro course. Work load wise I was almost never able to do the main 50 pages of reading but the only readings you needed for sections were the primary source docs which were usually a total of 20 pages which wasn't too bad. The midterm was tough because you're really pressed for time, but the final was doable because you got a full 3 hours with a pretty similar test structure.

Prof. Weld is the highlight of this course – I honestly would take it just to take a class with her if you're on the fence. She is a really cool prof and great lecturer who had me engaged almost every class and is insanely knowledgable. Lucas is honestly a pretty meh TF in my opinion (sections were just kinda bland, not a ton of discussion), but he is nice to meet one–on–one with if you need help with the paper.

Overall – I don't regret my choice to take this class, but it's not totally perfect. I heard in the past it was better when it was a bit more focused (it used to be Latin American and its history with the U.S.) and with a different TF, but even if they changed nothing with the course I still think it's very valuable – I learned a TON, got to take a class with Prof. Weld, and the research paper (although its a painful 10 pg of research for those of us burnt out from writing papers) is a really cool way to learn more in depth about anything you're interested in.
This is a brilliant survey class, and I would highly recommend. I wish all history lectures were basically like this one. Weld is a fantastic teacher and lecturer, and the readings are almost all fascinating. Like any survey class, it can feel rushed and like drinking from a firehouse (and occasionally Professor Weld can make it seem very much like a story of only clear cut good guys and bad guys), but as long as you recognize that, you will learn SO much.

It's not crazy hard either, but it still has serious expectations (150ish pages of reading / week, though you can get by on occasion doing the 10–15 pages of primary sources, 20 pages of textbook, plus skimming the articles). The midterm and exam weren't too bad, but they do take time to study for, and the research paper was difficult, but I learned so much from mine that it was totally worth it.
