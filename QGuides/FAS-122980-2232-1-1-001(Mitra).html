What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class if you are at all considering thesis–ing in WGS or if you want a supportive mentor in doing an independent WGS research project! I am leaving the semester with the conclusion that I will likely not thesis (just because I want more breadth not depth from my senior year, and a thesis will take away from that experience of exploring more), but I am not AT ALL regretful of completing the WGS junior tutorial because it helped me learn 1) how to conduct independent WGS work 2) was an opportunity to just read a lot about a topic i really care so much about 3) Learned that I don't want to thesis in the meantime. Even if I don't thesis this was a chance to do like a mini thesis (think like one chapter's worth of work) and if that's all you want but don't want to commit to a thesis, this was still a solid v chill way to experience that process!
It's not overwhelming at all considering the scope of the final paper and the whole teaching team is really understanding and available, so don't be scared!
Select a topic that is meaningful to you and the paper will be so much fun to write! It can also be helpful to choose something related to your thesis to determine whether or not you could see yourself writing one about that topic. Take advantage of the opportunity to choose your own idea and spend a full semester workshopping it!
I think extensive background in WGS or a humanities / social science based coursework is necessary. I am a WGS concentrator taking this course for thesis preparation, as most are.

The course was difficult in terms of writing a 20 page original research paper. However, we had a lot of structure with writing workshops, guest speakers, visits to Schlessinger library, visits to art exhibits, and regular discussion sections.

I felt like Professor Mitra and my tutor Sana made this course so accessible! I felt like my writing skills improved greatly in this class and I would recommend it if youâ€™re concentrating in WGS â€” whether or not you intend to write a senior thesis.
