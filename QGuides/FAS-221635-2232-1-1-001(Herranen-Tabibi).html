What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I think I summarized the main ideas in my first answer as well as my previous answer, but: I would recommend this course to any student with an interest in not only the Arctic but also, in general, anthropology, engaged scholarship, and ethical research theories and practices. This course gave me the opportunity to engage with issues of critical contemporary concern, both in and outside the classroom, and always in community with both my peers and numerous brilliant researchers and community members. (I actively looked forward to this class every week, and each meeting ended too soon!)
This class has a significant amount of reading material and the work with the community partner organizations is substantial. That being said, I'd highly recommend taking this class if you can fit it into your schedule because it teaches about science and climate change in a way that will add a lot to your existing perspective. The field trip is great, Anniki is amazing, and the course speakers are very interesting people.
This is a really excellent course to take if you know nothing about the Arctic. Your perspective will change and you will learn a lot if you put in the work. Also, the Engaged Scholarship projects are actually helpful to the partner organizations you're working with so it's definitely fulfilling. Annikki and Chiara are the nicest people and will give you great feedback on your work. I would definitely recommend taking this class if you can!
