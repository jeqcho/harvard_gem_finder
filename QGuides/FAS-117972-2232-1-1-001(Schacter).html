What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I highly recommended this class on memories for those with an interest in the subject. Despite not being overly challenging, the class offers a wealth of knowledge on the topic. The class takes on an interactive approach, favoring open discussions over traditional lectures, which makes the learning experience more enjoyable. Additionally, the resources used in the class are engaging, providing ample examples and research studies, rather than relying on dense scientific terminology that can be difficult to comprehend.
take this class– it's super interesting and Dr Schacter is so informative and nice
Take this class if you're interested in learning more about memory – both how it fails and how it works in general. You don't really need any background, though having an interest in psychology and understanding some basic concepts (say from AP Psychology) can enhance your experience. Professor Schacter is a great professor, and due to his many years of experience in the field of memory research he knows a LOT about the topic. The main book we read (The Seven Sins of Memory) is a book designed for popular reading, so it's enjoyable while presenting a lot of interesting psychological results. The two papers you write are pretty open–ended and a good opportunity to deep dive into a topic in memory you're interested in learning more about. Overall, a great freshman seminar!
Professor Schacter is a really great professor and so knowledgeable about memory. The discussions can get a little boring at times because they are two hours, and sometimes it feels like they're a little too long. That being said, the vast majority of the time I was very entertained and learned a lot. The work for this course is super manageable most of the time. There are two papers to write, one 6 pages, and the second is 10 pages. They're also research papers, so the research also takes a fairly long time. So unless you're working on these papers, it's just reading a chapter of the book and writing a short discussion post every week.
Professor Schacter is a great Professor – he literally wrote the book that you will be using as a "textbook" for this class. He is incredibly knowledgable and very understanding too. However, I would say that you should only take this class if you're genuinely interested in the field of Psychology – there will be a lot of reading papers, and both the midterm and final papers are pretty heavy for a Freshman Seminar. Don't take this class "just for fun" – if you take it seriously there is a lot you can get out from it.
Super interesting, but slightly repetitive on a class–to–class basis
I would highly recommend this class, especially if you are interested in concentrating in psychology or just have an interest in learning more about how the human mind works in regards to memory. Professor Schacter is extremely knowledgeable about the topic, and has the ability to explain tough concepts in a way that are digestible. The workload throughout the semester is extremely manageable, and I also think the major assignments (midterm paper/final paper) were a fun and interesting way to explore a small topic in memory that you find interest in. Thank you, Professor Schacter, for a great semester!
