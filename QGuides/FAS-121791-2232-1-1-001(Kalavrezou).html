What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class if you have any interest in Roman history of the Christian religion. You'll learn quite a bit.
The syllabus makes this seem more like a history class, but it is definitely an art history class. Take this if you are more interested in art, ideally already with an understanding of the history, than if you want to learn the basics of late antique history.
The grading of the midterm is completely arbitrary. The prof told us she was disappointed with our performance on it and graded us all extremely harshly even though I know my classmates and I spent an outrageous amt of time preparing for it and were very confident even right after the test. She did not give clear instructions for the exam beforehand and then got upset when we answered questions the way we were instructed to on the sheet. Such a disappointing and cruel introductory class to HAA. Made me lose all passion for Roman art.
This class provides a solid introduction to late Roman art and Byzantine art, and although it is has been taught in the past, I did not find it very well organized. It's difficult to follow along in the class, so if you're looking for an easy class about art history but are not very drawn to the content, it may be difficult. I had no art history background and found it rather difficult.
Do not take.
