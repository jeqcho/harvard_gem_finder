TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Soroka is amazing! Trust me, take it!
Super interesting content. I really really appreciate how much effort Prof Soroka put into this course to select interesting documentaries, readings, and materials for this class. His lectures were always like a Ted talk. I'd just walk in, keep my computer and everything else aside, and tune in to his spiel. He covers some theoretical basis for propaganda but then goes into a lot of adjacent history and real world examples. All of them together tell a very interesting story and make you realize how much propaganda there really is out there. I started asking more questions about media I consume since taking this class. It truly was a delightful experience. I didn't want to skip a single lecture and actually really looked forward to them.
This class is very history based and has several entire lectures about history (Iran, Ukraine, WWII, etc) without really mentioning propaganda. I really enjoyed the assignments in this course, but the readings were very dense and the lecture was relatively surface level. I know it's the first year they offer it so I hope they change it up a bit for the next semester because the essays and content were really interesting.
I would recommend this class! Dr. Soroka and TF Dr. Nikolova are both great people who teach the subject in an engaging way. This class has genuinely cool information that I was excited to learn about at every lecture. It is also very contemporary in that we discuss examples of propaganda in current events
This class is super interesting, especially the lectures. I found myself looking forward to every lecture because of how fascinating they are, and Dr. Soroka is an incredibly lecturer. The topics covered are very pertinent to real–world issues, and Dr. Soroka does a wonderful job of tying in current events with the topics that he's covering in class. Workload is relatively light; there are only two major assignments and the weekly readings. Take this course if you can!
