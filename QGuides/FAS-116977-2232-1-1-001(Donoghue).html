This class builds upon grammatical skills learned in the first semester and culminates in an extensive final project.
Do the translations!
If you prefer a self–teaching approach to learning, this course is well–suited for you. It primarily revolves around completing extensive translation assignments throughout the semester, with the added benefit of receiving answers during class. However, if you are seeking an instructor–guided format with lectures and sections that focus on teaching grammar, vocabulary, and improving your proficiency in Old English, this class may not meet your expectations.
This class will almost certainly feel frustratingly overwhelming while in the thick of it, but also will feel incredibly rewarding and gratifying when you get to the end. Spending time on translations and making sure you understand how the texts work not just what the modern English looks like will make the process much smoother throughout the semester.
This course is quite disorganized. Only take it if Seif is NOT the TF.
WELCOME TO THE THUNDERDOME!!

In all seriousness, I enjoyed this class a great deal. If you liked high school Latin, you'll love this. Prof. D. and Seif are both really smart, and have plentiful office hours such that you can really access them whenever you need.

This is a hard class; learning a language is never easy, and the translations pick up this semester, both in terms of length and quality. Definitely work hard, because the final project is tough (fair, but tough). There are lots of resources to help you in this class, and (perhaps I'm biased based off of this year's experience) the students in it are super nice.

Overall, recommend!
