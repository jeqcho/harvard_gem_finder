This class is so unique and a lot of fun – it opened my eyes to the distinct roles we give children in society (and made me realize that we've come to naturalize "children" as a concept). There is a lot of room for discussion and research in this course.
This was a fun and interesting class. Michael is great and makes the class very engaging.
This course may be reading heavy, but I feel that analyzing and discussing each of the readings was incredibly beneficial to my understanding of general wgs viewpoints and the ways in which history has an impact on the literature we read. I also feel that I much better understand the socialization of children and the lack of agency children possess, which is the first step in fostering societal change.
Loved this course! Very interesting material and conversations throughout the semester, especially with the student–led presentations. Definitely reading–heavy some weeks though. The workload was manageable, but most importantly, I felt like they were all relevant and engaging; it was really nice going to the Children's Museum and doing my own archival work in Schlesinger. Professor Bronski and Kirin were both extremely kind, knowledgeable, and accessible. I loved learning from them and being in this class this semester.
This is a great course if you're willing to commit to doing the readings. Prof. Bronski is also just an excellent person who cares so much about your experience as a student.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Whether you’re a WGS concentrator or are looking for a course to fulfill the arts distribution this class is amazing! I learned so much about childhood and walked out of it looking at the books I indulged in as a child differently.
Really great class! Quite a bit of reading each week, but the learning that you do will be primarily through engaged class discussions. A really great class to take if you're interested in a historical lens to talking about children and children's books.
This class was interesting! It was the first time Michael has taught it, so there was some room for improvement, but I'm sure it'll get better! I definitely gained a richer perspective on childhood and the ways that we treat children. It gave me a new lens to see children's movies, books, and entertainment through.
