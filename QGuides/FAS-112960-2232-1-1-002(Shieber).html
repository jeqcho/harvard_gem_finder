Be careful about taking this class! It's extremely trial by fire: you're basically going to have to learn everything by yourself from a textbook the first time around by doing readings twice a week and then going to lab and trying out what you just learned the day before in a practical setting. Most of the TFs are really sweet and helpful, but I think your experience can really vary depending on which TF you have. You have to put a lot of work into this class (at least I did) but there are a lot of office hours. That being said, the office hours are really chaotic and stressful (not the TFs fault, just nature of CS office hours): the queue is super long, and since a lot of it requires looking at code, it moves really slow so you have to be on top of things and get there early or wait a really long time before you can ask your question. Ed is also always a good resources, but be patient because the TF s have a lot of questions to respond to and I'm sure things are super overwhelming. This class is definitely really hard if you aren't a naturally talented genius programmer but I think I came out of it with the ability to learn a programming language with a totally different way of thinking and be more flexible in the way I think about designing and implementing my programs. There are also no lectures and the exams are hard and have really low grades (although they're curved). I would definitely think long and hard about whether you really need to take this class, but I think you can come out of it with a lot of tools and a new way of thinking, although along the way you might scream and cry and be incredibly stressed. Also this class takes up a lot of time (in my opinion, as someone who has done no competitive programming and didn't program at all before CS50) so don't take it with other really hard classes if you're like me.
