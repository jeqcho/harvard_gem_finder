What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class!! The small student to teacher ratio is valuable because you learn so much from both Prof. Dillon but also your fellow classmates. The readings may be intense at times, but you take away a lot of valuable knowledge about East Asia and urban studies from this course. Assignments are manageable as long as you manage your time well. Prof. Dillon is also very understanding!
Take the course! Dr.Dillon is an incredible academic with a depth of knowledge on the topic. There are so few courses that focus on cities much less cities in East Asia and I appreciated her ability to share current discourse and push her students to explore topics they find interesting.
If you have an interest in Asia, this class is great. Very comprehensive overview of the major cities in Asia, draws in a lot of perspectives from economic to cultural. Professor Dillon is very knowledgeable and good at facilitating discussion.
This class is a fascinating way to learn more about governance and globalization in East Asia. The readings are manageable and interesting; the class is discussion–based and engaging; and you receive tons of feedback on written assignments. The final paper was daunting to me (personally), but it was broken down into manageable pieces. For example, we submitted and received feedback on our literature reviews before writing the rest of the paper, which was super helpful. Professor Dillon is incredibly supportive, compassionate, understanding, and flexible (which I really appreciated this semester, given some health issues). She makes herself available outside of class, so you can always get help when you need it. Highly recommend this class! And taking other classes with Professor Dillon.
This is a great class if you're interested in learning about the tangible effects of globalisation on cityscapes in East Asia, or if you're interested in learning about how different cities confront problems to do with preservation, or migration, or others like this. If you're passionate about one particular city or one particular region, and want to learn more about it, you can as well through the final paper, though you may want to consider taking another more specific class instead.
This class will cover a pretty wide range of materials and offer an in–depth introduction to four important East Asian global cities, each with their own distinct character: Tokyo, Shanghai, Manila, and Hong Kong. The class is more about urban planning than I originally anticipated, but it's an interesting and worthwhile class nonetheless. If you take the class, you will learn something from Professor Dillon and from your classmates and you will improve your writing skills.
Be prepared to learn a lot of information!
