This is a first–year oriented class, but the readings can be difficult and the writing assignments are just a lot. The prof is very nice. It's more of an intro to humanities at Harvard than a HistLit class, but that means at times surface–level treatments of disciplinary differences. Come in prepared with ideas of what you want to write about, or it will be a challenge. Go to office hours to figure out how they want you to structure things – the style of writing expected (closer to creative nonfiction?) is very different than in other courses.
TAKE IT!!!! If you are at all interested in nationhood, art, music, history, the construction of society, politics, film, or anything that falls under the label “culture” take it!!
This class is super interesting. If you are considering American Studies or Hist Lit more generally, it is a great class to take. It, in a lot of ways, is expos for American Studies, but the skills are extremely transferable. It makes it clear what American Studies at Harvard will be, and it is also a great interdisciplinary class that will expose you to a lot of different concentrations and professors.
Take this course! I absolutely loved it. I think Prof. Deloria is a great professor – he's deeply enthusiastic, engaging, and passionate about the course material. Beyond that, he's just a great person. Dylan was a great TF as well. I think the course materials are excellent, and I loved the readings and guest speakers we had. It truly was a great opportunity to sample a range of different perspectives and disciplines with the humanities and social sciences, which makes it quite a unique course. I would absolutely recommend taking it!
It's a super fun class and you get to do a lot of different things within it that you really don't get to do in any other class (make screenprints, write songs, etc). The assignments are also very fun and I'm genuinely proud of what I've written because you have a lot of freedom in choosing what you want to write about.
This course is INCREDIBLE!!! If you are even remotely interested in American Studies, TAKE IT!!! You will hear from guest speakers every week who will lay out a rich foundation of how American Studies is relevant in a wide variety of disciplines. Any lecture with the course professor is especially good: Professor Deloria is an extremely engaging lecturer. A lot of lectures will involve participation, so they don't get boring! Conversations in lecture are rich and provocative. I cannot recommend this course highly enough!
TAKE THIS COURSE. I don't want it to become so big that it loses the community feel lol but also this class is great and you should take it if you're interested in an interdisciplinary approach to history and thinking about America. Prof. Deloria is amazing and everyone should take a course by him if you get the chance. Class was almost always fun and new ––– tons of guest lecturers, which was awesome to get to know different professors and hear about fields adjacent to/a part of American Studies. One day we screen printed our own version of the American flag in the Carpenter Center to learn about repetition and recreation. Another day we wrote songs in class and Prof. Deloria brought his guitar in to play behind our lyrics/melody. Another day we went to Cambridge Commons and the cemetery across the street to make observations about the environment and infer about its history. Basically if you value a variety of genuinely thoughtful lectures and activities, then you will love this course. I haven't even touched on the writing ––– Prof. Deloria and Dylan do a great job at explaining the style of writing they expect for an American Studies course (an almost New Yorker–style creative nonfiction), by breaking down the elements and having us read tons of examples. The class had a great community, thanks to Prof. Deloria who cultivated it by talking to each student before class. It was definitely my favorite class out of my first–year here.
