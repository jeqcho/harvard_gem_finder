What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you’re at all interested in the class, take it. I LOVED Michael Nathanson, and I think he did an amazing job leading the class. He (and the CAs) are accessible. The course is super collaborative and genuinely so much fun (especially if you take it with a friend). Three PSets a week is high volume, but the workload is definitely manageable (each pset only takes 45 – 90 minutes).
I didn't take calculus in high school, I found the course to be difficult but manageable. I was at MQC for every Pset and had office hours almost every week. If you can, take it with Michael Nathanson. He is the best! His office hours are very helpful and he encouraged me to take problems one step at a time. The Psets are completion based but make sure you understand the concepts so you aren't rushing to his office at the last minutes – Michael might get a bit frustrated at you for not coming earlier. I spent a lot of time for this course. It definitely was my heaviest course load this semester, but the TF and CAs were really kind so getting through it was a lot better.
This class has a mastery–based grading system, so you'll have more than one opportunity to prove that you are familiar with a concept (for example, using linear approximation). The multiple opportunities to prove mastery or proficiency make the class less stressful assessment–wise. If you lack a background in Calculus from highschool, you'll likely need to spend more time learning the concepts/doing the p–sets because they're unfamiliar concepts, but it for sure isn't the case that a good grade is out of reach. Once again, the mastery–based grading system makes a good grade very feasible for this class in my opinion!
I took Math 1A off cycle (my freshman spring) and can't emphasize how much of a difference this made. The course is way less crowded, you get almost a personalized math question center experience (I became friends with the course assistants), office hours are productive and the professor and TF get to know you and your math weaknesses/strengths so well. I heard that Fall 1A can be packed and it is harder to use the support network, so I'd tell you to keep that in mind. Having said that, this class is not easy. It may be way easier for those who took calculus in high school (2/3 of this class tbh), but if you didn't then you might struggle like I did (it had been over a year since my last math class in HS). But struggling doesn't mean failing, and I learned so much with this class –– at certain points I was actually excited about math even though I never saw myself as a math person. Still, the amount of time you spend on this can be intense depending on your background. So make sure you find pset buddies and use the teaching staff to help you (they rotate every semester but Spring 2023 Math 1A had such a great staff). Anyways, if you need to take math you need to, and depending on how much work you're willing to put in, this class will work out even if you were placed in Ma/Mb.
It helps having a background in calculus, but it is not absolutely necessary. I would highly recommend brushing up on concepts like trigonometry, logarithms, the unit circle, trig identities, and etc. I think I was able to do very well because I took AP Calc AB in high school. Even though I had some calculus background, this class challenged me. The structure of the class is very different from my high school. In my high school, the teacher would lecture the whole period, we would do some practice problems, and do homework. Here, there is more emphasis on trying to piece together concepts on your own and understand math. Looking back, I feel like I was just applying formulas and methods, but here, I have to think about what we are doing conceptually, which has helped tremendously in retaining the math in my head. That being said, even though I did have an advantage by taking some calc in HS, I was always at MQC the whole time and it really took a while for me to get used to the style of math. Biggest advice is to stay focused, get help(!!!!!! go to Office Hours, go to MQC, work with others on the PSET), and be open to a different style of learning math.
** if you need to fill your quantitative requirement, this is the way to go. Nathanson is a joy and the tfs are so helpful. PSETs are managable.
This course was really fun and interesting. I would very much recommend for anyone who was placed in Math 1A to take it in the Spring for a small course and very individualized attention.
I would tell future students to take this class only if you are confident in your calculus experience. Maybe you don't need calculus experience, but taking it without any exp can be scary. I took this class without any experience in calculus and let me tell you I struggled immensely. For beginners, don't be scared to ask questions and talk to others. It is easy to make friends and no questions are dumb. Attend MQC every night and it does save lives. I would not have finished my pset if I didn't go to MQC. Definitely look up youtube videos to aid you in this like Organic Chemistry tutor or 3Brown1Blue. Youtube saves lives. One thing that I do have to say the structure of class can be questionable because they put you to work in small groups. Other than that, take the course because it is rewarding, especially for those with not that much math experience.

Amazing class, I'd recommend taking it off–cycle. Professor Nathanson is great and MQC is extremely helpful! The workload is heavy, but there is flexibility for some deadlines and the professor is very understanding. The opportunity to reassess learning objectives from previous tests is life saving!
