This was probably one of my favourite (if not *the * favourite) course I've taken at Harvard. I love studying languages, and being able to take a class explicitly dedicated to the way that translations and languages shape literature was such an amazing experience. The great readings on some theoretical and linguistic aspects of translation were super illuminating for my own understanding, and it was neat to be able to apply these to comparing actual translated famous works every week. The relatively small class size made discussions very engaging, insightful, and educational every day, and Luke and Yang were so helpful in guiding discussions, providing feedback on assignments, and generally being extremely personable, accessible, and understanding resources for all of us throughout the semester. I also adored the translation assignments; it was super fulfilling for me to be able to apply these concepts of translating literature in such a hands–on way, being involved in the translation process from start to finish to create a nice end product.

The only concern I had about the class at all was simply that we had very little feedback directly in terms of grades. We got comments/suggestions on our assignments extremely quickly, which was incredibly appreciated and helpful, but no 'grades' per se throughout the semester, so I actually have very little idea of how my final grade is going to look when it comes out in the next few days.
Be prepared to engage with translation in a different manner than in a language–learning class, as this class interrogates the theory and ethical ramifications behind translation choices instead of "literal" translation. Class time is spent mostly in full class discussions, which is especially engaging when you take the time to thoroughly digest the theory–based assigned readings. Take a look through the syllabus to see if a language you know is being discussed in class, as having your own insight into the original language and sharing your thoughts on the selected translators' translation choices is incredibly fun to discuss in class and in the weekly reading responses.

I recommend fulfilling the independent lecture requirement earlier in the semester, as that's when the Boston University translation lectures are held and attending a lecture in–person is much more rewarding than watching one online.
This class is pretty chill and very interesting, especially if you are interested in world literature. I would recommend knowing another language coming in (it makes your life a lot easier)! Only thing I would say is that the grading rubric is unclear, so you truly have no idea of your progress other than qualitative feedback from the TF.
This is an incredible class if you are interested in the material! Luke is a top–tier facilitator of discussion, so be prepared for interactive class sessions. The assignments are relatively light and fun if you know another language to do translations from; there is a good amount of reading but it is fairly interesting.
One of the best courses I have taken at Harvard! The prof is extremely knowledgeable and genuinely a great guy and the course reflects that. I would especially recommend it to those who are not multilingual.
