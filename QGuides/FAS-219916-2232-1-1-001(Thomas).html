I would not recommend this class to future students. It was always a toss up whether Professor Thomas would be in the mood to answer questions or if he would respond with sarcasm and shut down honest student inquiries and questions. Some of his comments in class were targeted at specific students in a way that I think was meant to be funny or lighten the mood, but did not land and I did not appreciate them, as they were not always kind. There was significant work required outside of class, numerous weekly assignments, and the general vibe was off. It honestly took a toll on my mental health.
Don't take this class if you want a challenging ESPP course. Apart from the hours of busy work each week, it doesn't teach you to think critically.
I think this course has the potential to be really great, but currently, the workload is kind of unrealistic. I am not averse to hard work, but this course really took up all my time this semester; if you have a busy semester in any way, or anticipate that your other classes will be time–consuming or challenging, I would not take this course.
You have to work really hard for this class, and you will have to spend long hours on multiple assignments every week. Pop quizzes are also pretty stressful. Take this class if you are genuinely interested in the course material and motivated enough to spend long hours researching and writing.
Do not take this class unless you've read the syllabus and it has a seemingly manageable workload! If you truly enjoy this subject matter it can be worth it, but unless drastic changes are made, expect to put in significant hours on this class.
Looking back on the semester, I feel really fulfilled by the work that I produced. The term project is definitely a highlight– especially in connecting with actual NGOs operating in the country that you are studying. That said, this class is consistently a lot of work with sizable assignments due every week. Be prepared to put in the effort if you are expecting to get an A. Don't let this be a deterrent, though. I do think it was worth it and would still take it if I knew how much work it would be when I initially enrolled.
Take this class! It may be a lot of work, and it may be difficult at times, but it is honestly the class that has had the greatest impact on me. the professor cares about the material and the students, first class I had the professor not only knew everyones name but different details and things that they liked as well. fantastic professor with great material. The term project may be a lot of work, but it is really fulfilling to be able to work with a developing country and interview different people in the country as well! 10/10 and would definitely recommend it!
Do not take this class unless water resources in developing countries is your main passion. There was way too much work and the professor kept saying "it's easy" when this was the most work I have ever had for a class at Harvard. The work takes away from the content and you get burnt out. If it is restructured next year maybe I would recommend it because Ken was a great lecturer and we had cool guest speakers but right now the work to benefit ratio is not worth it. Some people might think it is worth it for the trip, but if you don't go on the trip it's not.
Dr. Ken is a great lecturer and it's incredibly easy to sit through his lectures and learn from them. He is passionate about the material and it is obvious, and he's very understanding.
