Speaking as a senior, this class was one of the highlights of my college career, and it has changed my priorities after college. I wish I had taken it earlier. Professor Fisher is one of the best lecturers at Harvard. He is keenly aware of modern literary trends and the social history of the United States in the 20th century. If you can keep up with the weekly readings, which are admittedly lengthy, then the class is infinitely rewarding; however, the lectures and sections alone are extremely informative without having completed all of the reading. The assignments are graded generously with emphasis on writing improvement relative to each student's beginning skill level. I walked away with a deeper understanding of both myself and American literature––I cannot recommend this class highly enough.
Professor Fisher is a very passionate lecturer, and the section leaders are knowledgeable and do a great job facilitating discussion. The reading list includes some true American classics, like Nabokov's Lolita and Faulkner's The Sound and the Fury. However, note that at least in the 2022 spring semester, the reading load was extremely heavy – think one novel a week, essentially HUM10 level if not more – and it's very difficult to stay engaged if you fall behind on the readings. I think 80% of students fell behind by the end of the semester. Many students have recommended that they decrease the reading load, so if you notice that on this year's syllabus – for example, taking two weeks over the Sound and the Fury or Invisible Man – you shouldn't hesitate to take the course! If the reading list remains as long, just make sure you have the appropriate priorities if you want to get the most out of this class.
I really enjoyed all of the readings in this class. There are classic books that everyone has heard of, but then lesser known books that are still important to the study of American literature. However, if you don't learn well in a lecture format, I would keep that in mind before enrolling. The readings are substantive, but I really enjoyed discussions during section.

However, the book list is really interesting, and I was able to get through the course just fine without reading a few of the books. I highly highly recommend this course, and was always raving about how interesting the material was to my roommates throughout the semester, so even if you're really new to the subject or humanities definitely try this course out!
