I would tell future students that this is an excellent choice of a class. Your experience in a course isn't only your investment and interest, but also the instructor's investment and interest. Getting to know your instructor is extremely helpful in making you and the instructor a living, breathing human to each other and helps the instructor know how to support you.
Overall I was extremely happy with this expos section. Prof O'Connor is very approachable, encouraging, and knowledgeable. Translation proved to be a broad topic that allowed plenty of room for pursuing all sorts of interests throughout the semester. The workload was very manageable, although I would spend significant chunks of time before each draft was due. Even though expos is primarily about developing writing skills, I feel that I came out with more knowledge about translators' considerations, theories on translation, and especially the relationship between culture and literature. I would highly recommend it if you have any interest in fiction and language!
This is the best expos, hands down. I haven't heard of another expos that people really enjoyed, but I loved this class. If you're into literature and looking for a chilled expos I recommend this class unreservedly. The grading is also very fair in this class and the assignments are fairly straightforward to complete. All in all, gem of a class.
