What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you don't have an interest in Shakespeare already (I didn't) this course might be a little hard to enjoy at the beginning. This course also seems to have much more work than the other Expos sections. However, the essays, since they are mostly driven by personal research questions, tend to feel very engaging and rewarding, and Wilson provides a lots of useful feedback to help you improve as a writer. If you have a very busy schedule and are looking for a light workload class, this class isn't for you, but if you are looking for an Expos class which will help you best develop as a writer, take this class!
I would tell future students that this class is not as daunting as it seems! Just write what works and take advantage of the Writing Center when you need it (I definitely did and it helped out a lot).
Don't listen to the rumors about Why Shakespeare – other Expos classes are so much harder. In Why Shakespeare, you have so much guidance and freedom to express your thoughts through Shakespeare and it's amazing! Please take this course.
Instructor is great! Do take this class if you'd like a glimpse into how literary scholarship in general (and Shakespeare scholarship in particular) while also fulfilling your EXPOS requirement. Workload seems to be comparatively higher than some other sections, but probably still worth it if you're interested in the subject. You also get a high degree of academic freedom in the class.
This class is a lot of work. But if you put the time into it, you will get so much out of it. Your writing will improve immensely, and you may develop arguments you never thought you could come up with. The most important part of the writing process is the response papers. Make sure you give them the time they require, and ask questions early on. It will make your draft much more effective, your conference much more helpful, and your revision much easier. And go to the writing center! I went for all of my papers, and I always got pointed advice. If nothing else, the writing center will give you peace of mind before you submit your essay. I definitely enjoyed this class, and while Expos may seem intimidating, it's very rewarding.
I would say, be prepared to work. Wilson gives quite a bit of work to do outside of class...lots of assignments that prep you for the bigger essays. However, I think that these assignments are worth it because they make writing the bigger essays very quick and give you a blueprint for what you want to do. I would say that you should take this course if you're interested in Shakespeare and literature, or in watching Shakespeare adaptations. If you just want an essay Expos, though, don't take this course.
TAKE THIS if you love writing! It'll be a challenge and stretch you but worth it. I was not a huge Shakespeare fan but really enjoyed this. I learned so much and know I am a much stronger writer.
This class was life–changing for my writing skills. Although I'm a STEM concentrator, I have always enjoyed writing and expressing fun curiosities with evidence, and this class showed me how to do so in a way that made my writing – for the first time – feel truly meaningful. I believe the work was reasonable, and the feedback was excellent! I absolutely loved this class.
