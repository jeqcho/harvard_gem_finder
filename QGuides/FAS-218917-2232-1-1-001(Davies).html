A fantastic introduction to education and education politics. Sparked my interest. I am now even considering to take a master degree in education.

Also, a class that considers both practical and theoretical aspects. You will be required to write an op–ed which you have the opportunity to publish, to contribute. Moreover, professor and teaching fellows are really helpful in the process. They talked me hand by hand about what I should do. It is actually the first time that I have learned about publication.

Overall, fantastic experience please just take the course and enjoy it!
If you are on the fence about this class because it's a graduate course, TAKE IT! It is genuinely life changing because it covers so many different parts of the world, so you're bound to learn something.
To me, this class definitely seemed to be much more politics–focused rather than education–focused. While that made me like this course a bit less than if the balance had been more on education, I did really learn a lot and I think that those interested in international politics would find this class especially interesting.
Take this course if you want an overview of politics and education. Also, if you want a chiller mixed class.
PLEASE take this class, even if you're not an education secondary! Being in this environment feels like the kind of classroom experience you wanted when you first were accepted to Harvard. Everyone participates in discussion, regardless if you're a grad or undergrad student. The conversation is so interesting due to the diverse backgrounds of the students. Emmerich and the TFs are amazing. The Op–ed assignment was enjoyable. I cannot speak highly enough of this course.
Take this class!!!!!!! The course staff is absolutely amazing and the curriculum is incredibly thought provoking and nuanced.
