What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I really enjoyed my experience, but this is a class where you get in what you put in. I'd recommend a mentor with a pre–approved IRB and focusing on a project you're actually interested in. If you have no biomechanics experience, that's 100% fine, any info you'd need can come from attending a few HEB lectures for another class or simply reading a few chapters of a biomechanics book. Grades come from the end of semester presentation and paper, as well as you're individual research performance aka did you do the research. Presentations were nerve–wracking walking in but were pretty chill overall
Biomechanics concept will help every student who wish to study how and why some species were well adapted to a different environment
Take this class if you are at all interested in biological research of any kind!! You get to work on a biomechanics–related project (new territory for me, a mostly–cell–biologist) as a real member of the lab, get involved in the data collection and analysis process, and present findings at the end of the semester (like a real scientist! lol). The troubleshooting process is really rewarding, and you finish the semester feeling like you've truly learned useful, applicable skills. Also a great class for building relationships with more seasoned mentors. Most of the work tends to ramp up toward the end of the semester, when data collection comes to a close and you're analyzing your data and writing up the results, but it's no more taxing than your average class during finals season. No exams, either (always a plus). Ali is amazing and super funny. 110% recommend HEB 1210.
