Best class I have taken. Cannot think of a complaint. Should be a required course––valuable historical information, McGirr was awesome.
While I probably got an A– in the course, awaiting the final, which, of course, never feels great, as a senior taking the class as an elective, the GPA hit seems fully worthwhile. It was my first challenging history class, and it really broadened my view of historical arguments and American history. Having taken the class, I feel prepared and inspired to read further about American history on my own, as if I have some key parts of the puzzle that I can build out myself over the years. Lecture with Professor McGirr was quite sensational: Every other sentence felt like, and often was, the conclusion to a prize–wining history book. Sometimes, I'd write my papers, and then go back and review my class notes, and realize I might not have actually said *that* much that was new. Maybe that's why I didn't do the best; however, it definitely feels worthwhile, at least in the moment, for now. We have the privilege of living in a beautiful, amazing country, and I'm grateful to have had the chance to learn about it from such a master in lecture and such a high, high calibre of teaching staff in section.
It's an interesting class, even if you already know a lot about American history
