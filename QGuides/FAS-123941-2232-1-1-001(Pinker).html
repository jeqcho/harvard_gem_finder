Psych 1 has a reputation, and to me it certainly lived up to the name.

The course was incredibly interesting, insightful, and open to beginners – however, you must be prepared to put in the work. Pinker presents ideas that are well described and sometimes controversial/speculative, all of which sparked critical thought nevertheless. The combined workload with the readings, mini essays, and paper was quite heavy. The midterm was extremely challenging as it tested for specific details while we were expected to study for almost 10 chapters. It is worth noting that everything in this class is graded on a curve, and exams only contain lecture content (so attendance is crucial).

On a day to day basis, the lectures are very fun to watch but not as interactive as some other courses. You have opportunities to earn extra credit by writing papers connecting topics to the real world, which is very rewarding. My experience in this class was greatly enhanced by taking it with friends with whom I'd discuss the concepts, and I would recommend anyone else to do the same. I am extremely glad I took this class, but I also feel like I deserve an "I survived Psych 1" t shirt.
