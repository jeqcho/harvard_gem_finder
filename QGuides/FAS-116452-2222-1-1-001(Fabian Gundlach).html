Definitely take this class! The material is really interesting, and Fabian is an excellent teacher. While this class is definitely not easy, it's challenging in a good way, so you really grow as a mathematician throughout the semester.
Take this course! Algebraic geometry is a cool topic and Fabian is a really good teacher. Just beware of difficult problem sets (the CAs and Fabian were all really helpful though) – they really make you think, but occasionally that one homework problem really should have been a bonus.
This class is difficult but very rewarding.
Take this class! It's a little difficult toward the middle to tell why certain constructions are helpful, but it's very satisfying once it starts to come together.
Although in the end, I was less excited about algebraic geometry than before taking the class, I think this is more of a product of my interests than how the course was run. The course is excellent –– the lectures are standard with a bit messy handwriting (Gundlach), but they are organized extremely well with many illuminating examples. Problem sets are also of a very accessible level. As suggested from the title, a lot of algebra is done in the class, so at least for me it gave insight on how much I actually enjoy doing pure algebra.
This is a nice class, good intro to classical algebraic geometry. Expect a lot of algebra, and less geometry. More algebra than something like algebraic topology. While it's not a prerequisite, I would say it would be helpful to be confident in the fruits and veggies of Math 131 Topology and basic algebra stuff about rings / fields.
This class is one of the best 100–level math courses offered at Harvard. The material is very unique and challenging. The problems often have a geometric interpretation yet are usually understood in an abstract/algebraic context. You need to have exposure to rings and fields before this class otherwise the material will be very off–putting.
The material in this class can be difficult and require a decent amount of time outside the class to understand what is taught in lecture, but learning the material is rewarding, and the psets are fun! Also, the grading in this class is very lenient.
I'm sure that this can be a great course, depending on who teaches it. The material can be very interesting at times, and otherwise it is still quite good. However, as with any 100–level class in the department, there is the possibility of some lectures turning into dry sequences of statements and proofs for more–or–less–obvious facts. After emerging from the painful maze of technicalities (the extent of which is the instructor's choice), the resulting theorems can be quite nice. It just takes a bit of patience.
I found this course to be really good. Fabian is a good lecturer, he always presents only what is essential to the course: somehow every result proved in class made its way into being necessary for some homework problem. I found homework and exams problems to be very interesting, it's been a while since I've enjoyed solving math homework. That being said, definitely take algebraic geometry with Fabian!
