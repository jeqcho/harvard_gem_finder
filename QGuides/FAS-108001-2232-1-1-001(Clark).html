What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
As always, any class that Suzie Clark teaches is worth taking, without exception. I learned a tremendous amount in this class – every single reading and topic was both new and interesting, and also situated to be canonical within the history of music theory. As a result of it I feel that I have a comprehensive and nuanced understanding of some of the most crucial concepts in music theory – the circle of fifths, the structure of the diatonic scale, the relative closeness of different chords, keys, and scales, and the role of Schenker in music history. There was a lot of reading, but the class ended with presentations rather than an essay, which was cool and also created a more reasonable workload for my semester. The discussions in the class were great and I would highly recommend the course to anyone who's curious.
Take this class if you are interested in the history of music theory! Really interesting survey class covering many different time periods and class discussions were always fascinating.
I truly believe that any student interested in music theory (or, to be frank, music at a theoretical level) in general needs to take a class with Suzie Clark. She is everything a music student could want — knowledgeable in the field, well–respected the field, enthusiastic, kind, graceful, and fosters a fantastic class community. I feel so lucky to have taken this class — which connected music theory to so many things that I hadn't an inkling of before. I would drop anything to take another of Suzie's classes again, and I would recommend the same for you.
Take any course with Prof. Clark!! This course was very manageable but intellectually stimulating unlike any other Harvard class. Music theory may seem like a niche topic when in fact, it is highly relevant in understanding broader history and the humanities. If you want a course that engages multiple disciplines in a cogent and understandable way, take this!!
This class is excellent for anyone with any interest in music theory and music history. It provides a good, panoramic view of the development of Western Music Theory.
