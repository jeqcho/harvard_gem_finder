Could not recommend this course more. I loved it so much, for anyone with any personal experience with a diaspora this class really gave me the language to think about my own identity more! We read so many amazing books, and the class was very discussion–based, everyone in the class clearly cared a lot about the subjects. The level of work is extremely fair as well.
Take this class if you're at all interested in literature and its relation to diaspora, migration, and trauma! Keep in mind that it's a discussion–based class that lasts for two hours, so you have to be ready to read and maybe debate some small nuances, but I think the works that we read were honestly so enlightening and eye–opening that even if class discussion can sometimes seem repetitive, if you hang in there I think the class comes to some important conclusions on not easily answerable questions. Also, a huge shoutout to the teaching staff, especially TF Yang, who are just always so kind and supportive.
This course was a lot of reading, but you don’t have to have read the book extremely thoroughly. Just enough to contribute to the conversation and enjoy it. If you’re thinking of taking the course, do it!
Great class! Professor Thornber and Yang create a really great learning community and the discussions are relatively low pressure yet stimulating. You will learn a ton from the readings, and my favorite part was how this class pushed me to reflect more on my own life and background.
TAKE THE COURSE! TO THE CART! CHOP CHOP!
Really lively, encouraging class with a very wholesome and supportive atmosphere: take the class!
Read through the syllabus closely and make sure that you are able to take on the reading load, it can be quite heavy if you have other courses that have a lot of reading as well. Obviously, aside from the discussion post, there isn't a direct way to check back on if you've done it, but without reading the text I can imagine the discussion and course experience are a lot less enjoyable. Other than that, this is a really great open–armed class. Professor Thornber is incredibly knowledgeable and cares a lot about the enjoyment and learning of her students. If you are interested in migrations, diaspora, and love to read, this would be a great fit. But those must be a prerequisite.
This is a very typical discussion–based class.
