The lecture series is really good! Attendance was spotty but I got a lot out of attending the lectures or watching the recordings. The research component of the class requires a lot of research independence and assumes coding background, etc., but was really worthwhile.
Take this class if you want a survey of topics in AI and computational neuroscience through high–quality lectures, and if you have a personal ML project you'd like to earn class credit by undertaking.
You will get out of this course what you put in––there is a great degree of independence involved in implementing your project, and you can make it as challenging as you want. The same is true of lectures as well since attendance is not mandatory. Working on a stimulating project can expand your skillset as an engineer, and listening carefully to the lectures will change the way you think about AI. This is a rare gem of a course, but only if you take the time to sink your teeth into it!
This is a great course to work on a machine learning project that you're interested in!

There are a few assignments, and they are a bit confusing so beware of that, it may take 5–10 hours maybe 3 times over the early part of the semester, and the project is the bulk of the rest of the work.
This class is essentially an independent research project with guest lectures. Some of the lectures were more interesting than others, and I often found that many of them went over my head. They aren't necessarily relevant for the final project, but it is a great way to get a sense of the kinds of research that is happening in the field. For the project, you really should have some coding background. I had never worked with neural networks before, and that was a learning curve enough for me even with a solid Python background. It's a great opportunity to pursue an independent project to put on your resume with the support of Professor Kreiman and the TFs. This class is really what you make of it though – it definitely can be as complicated as you'd like. And it's a nice exercise in research design and report writing. Just be prepared to be working on the project *very* independently.
