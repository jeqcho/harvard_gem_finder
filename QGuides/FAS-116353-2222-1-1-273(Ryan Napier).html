Austen and Us, with Dr. Napier, was by far my favorite class of the semester –– and of my freshman year more broadly. Dr. Napier is an amazing teacher –– he's critical but in a helpful way, attentive, and patient; it's clear to me how much time he spends on this class, whether it's coming up with workshops and lessons or providing feedback on our drafts and in office hours. I honestly couldn't recommend this class enough. If you're at all interested in Jane Austen or enjoy reading and watching movies, this is a wonderful expos class to take.
Take this class! Dr. Napier is a tough grader, but he gives incredible feedback and will really transform your writing to get it in ship shape
Austen and Us is really one of the better expos sections imo! I was in a different section last fall before I ended up dropping it, and it was pain in the ass because it didn't really teach much about writing but expected you to turn in drafts and essays. On the other hand, Dr Napier was really helpful with teaching us about academic writing that isn't limited to Austen related, and the final research paper topic is pretty flexible too. I think I really grew as a writer taking this course, and it actually got me to enjoy close reading and making arguments.
This course was fun, engaging, and I could not recommend it enough! Jane Austen may not seem relatable or interesting, but I promise there's so much humor and interesting comment that keeps her work relevant to this day. The second unit is focused on movies and the third on any Austen topic you like, so you don't even have to keep working with Austen's books if you don't want to. There's something for everyone, from politics to romance to literature to history (well, maybe not STEM). One of the great aspects of this course is that it actually does what Expos is supposed to do: it teaches you how to write! You will go over the fundamentals of writing a college essay beyond the 5 paragraph structure. Every assignment has a purpose and will help you hone skills in close reading, analysis, and research. If you invest time into the class, you will definitely progress as a writer regardless of your prior writing abilities. Plus, Dr. Napier is an amazing preceptor and truly cares about feedback and your progress in the class. He's funny, smart, engaging, and super accommodating. The seminar was always a fun experience, and you'll get to know all the other students in the classroom. Expos has a scary atmosphere around it, but I think this course is a great way to fulfill that requirement.
This class is taught very well and you learn SO much about writing essays (Dr. Napier structures his lectures very well and is super clear/helpful about telling you the things that make up a good essay). However, it is difficult – you'll struggle a lot but it will end up in improved writing.
TLDR: Take this class if you actually want to improve your writing.

Harvard is funny sometimes in that we choose to attend one of the most prestigious institutions of learning in the world and then are surprised when a class actually poses to be a challenge. This class is not easy, but neither is anything that has a fruitful reward –– and this class most certainly does. If you put in the work, you will watch your writing improve. Dr. Napier is very qualified, very thorough, and is willing to help you in any way possible to improve your writing.
The course is good if you are very interested in Jane Austen, however the grading is harsh and requires a lot of time in revision and office hours.
If you like Jane Austen in any form, or are interested in Regency England fiction, this class' focus is great! Also, Dr. Napier is a super engaging teacher who actually teaches you how to write well and explains all the components of a good essay in detail. If you are insecure about your writing abilities, this Expos is for you because he gives the best feedback, information, and is extremely understanding. The only assignments you have to do are your papers, everything else is not graded, which is also really nice.
Many people complain about expos, saying that it is a lot of work and that they use all–nighters to get everything done by a deadline, but this is absolutely not necessary. Expos, especially Austen and Us, is not a class to be feared: the workload is manageable if you do not procrastinate and consistently stay on top of it. If you come to class with enthusiasm, you'll learn a lot about writing, and you'll leave the class with improved writing skill. Don't be afraid to start over your essay on a new document when you're revising.
