This is an amazing and fairly easy GENED that fulfills the Science & Technology in Society requirement! If you have any interest in weather/environmental science, or a fascination with any type of natural disaster, this course will be pretty enthralling, as it was for me. As a primarily arts and humanities person taking this course, I was able to follow it quite well with only a little confusion around some of the equations. My best piece of advice is to complete as much of the labs as you can during section time; it's so helpful to be able to ask a classmate or TF a question as you struggle through some of the technicalities of using GIS software. Brendan is a really great and amusing teacher who lectures in an engaging way and jokes around a lot. Overall, definitely recommend!
This is a great course with a lot of interesting content! Know that it requires a two hour lab section every week which can be time–consuming. But overall, a highly informative course and I feel like I learned quite a bit from it. Brendan is an excellent professor and the teaching staff is responsive and helpful.
Take this class if you are even remotely intrigued by natural disasters. It will teach you all kinds of things about the physics of natural disasters, and will also teach you important things about how we can be conscious and vigilant about the hazards associated with natural disasters. This is super important, as you will see in the class, there is a lot of negligence in governments around the world in preparing for natural disasters. Taking this class will make you more aware, and will hopefully allow you to share with others how important the work of Earth scientists is, and make some real change in the world!

Natural Disasters is an excellent choice for the science GENED requirement. This class is a gem, but it is also not a pointless gem. You will learn a ton about the science behind Natural Disasters, and the workload is very manageable. You can definitely get an A in this course with conscientious effort. There is a Midterm, Final, and 7 lab assignments (roughly one weekly). The keys to success in this course are 1) Go to class–lectures are not recorded & slides are sparse on words (Brendan is also clearly at the top of the Earthquake Prediction Field, so he usually has something insightful beyond the basic lecture to offer), 2)take lab assignments seriously (they are ~35% of the grade and you should get a very high average on them that will make the Midterm & Final less stressful w/ this cushion), and 3)put in some solid effort studying for midterm & final (note that the final is cumulative, so do not forget to actually seriously study the midterm stuff as well for that final).
This class has genuinely interesting course material. Lab is a bit of a time commitment, but if you use your time diligently you don't have to do much or any homework outside of class or lab.
