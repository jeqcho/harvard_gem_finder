Chinese class this whole semester has been, categorically, my favorite class to go to. Going to Vanserg every day, even though the course can be difficult, was always a highlight and I can't express how grateful I am to have gotten to know my teachers and classmates so well. We are truly a family. My Chinese abilities have improved so much this semester ––– it's quite remarkable ––– and the class is designed to help you learn much more about Chinese culture, history, and politics to a much deeper level than you've probably ever learned before. That being said, Chinese class is quite a commitment and you need to be excited and passionate about learning Chinese to enjoy this class. There are A LOT of assignments in this class that can be time–consuming. In my opinion, taking this class was really worth it, but it's really a judgment call you should make for yourself and what your courseload looks like this semester.
This class moves incredibly quickly compared to Chinese Bx and uses grammar structures that aren't used day to day (in the household with family, for example). Much harder compared to Chinese Bx, but definitely worth taking. We discuss a lot of important issues that may sound intimidating (public policy, discrimination, Chinese poetry, Chinese history, minorities, etc.) and we do learn vocab but the emphasis is still mostly on using grammar structures to discuss these issues on a surface/general level.
Take this class if you're willing to put in 9 hours/week and you WANT TO LEARN CHINESE!! This class is VERY fulfilling, ONLY IF YOU PUT IN THE WORK! If you are too busy, you will fall behind VERY quickly and you will struggle.
This class had the best students I have made during freshman year and I really enjoyed showing up to class. Honestly, this was my favorite class. The homework may be a bit time–consuming, but it is worth it. The teachers are all very nice and for this class, you only have to have good listening ability (parents spoke Chinese to you growing up). Even though Chinese skill level varies, as long as you know how to listen mostly, you will do fine. Learning to read and write will not be too bad, especially coming from Chinese Bx. You get into more formal writing and I loved the small class size. Take this class!!
TAKE THIS COURSE, especially if you did Chinese BX. I took BX, and found 123xb was where I started seeing massive improvements in my Chinese. Even though my primary goal was to improve my Chinese to near–fluent level, an extra bonus that I really didn't expect was the breadth and complexity of the topics we discussed, even including things like AI, economics, specific aspects of Chinese culture etc. Also, the community is fantastic. If you're scared about difficulty/grading, note that I believe they slightly changed the course this year (spring 2023) compared to 2022 – it used to be 20 grammar structures a week – but this year it was exactly the same format as BX i.e. 12 grammar structures and ~80–100 vocab a week. I was also initially unsure about taking 123xb due to difficulty/time/grading, but having done the course, it's really no different to BX, and if you're taking this to fulfill your language req, take this course right after BX when what you learned last semester is fresh in your mind. Time commitment per week for me: 2 hrs learning vocab, 1 hr prep for twice weekly quizzes, 3 hrs homework, 1 hr projects/miscellaneous things.
Absolutely take this course!! I had the best teachers and made close friends through this class.
Even though I've already satisfied my language requirement, I'm really glad I took this class as a way to not only improve my Chinese but also to meet new friends :) even though we had class every day it was still the class I was the most engaged in, and I feel like I got so much out of it by practicing vocab and grammar structures on a consistent daily basis. It's pretty easy to lose the vocab recognition progress though, so it's important to keep reviewing the terms.
If you are someone without a super–strong Chinese background, expect to be challenged. This course is a much larger jump from Chinese BX in course content, vocabulary, and grammar, but don't let that intimidate you from choosing this class. From the constant challenge of class and sections, you will notice enormous progress in your Chinese, even if you don't see it at first. From the teachers to the students, making connections with everyone in the class has made it all worth it. I highly recommend taking this class, you won't regret it!
If you are already looking at the q guide for this class, TAKE IT. It's been one of my most enjoyable classes here – the teachers really make the class feel warm and welcoming, and the community in this class is unparalleled. The class definitely moves fast, and you will have to put in the work to keep up, but the amount you learn in such a short time period is actually incredible. I initially only took Chinese here because they had a heritage learners track and out of mild interest, but it's been one of the best decisions I've made here, and I'm now planning to pursue a citation in it. On a more practical note, take this class if you have already taken BX or if you have a decent background in reading and writing Chinese. If you're still unsure if this class level is right for you, feel free to contact the teaching staff! They're genuinely the nicest people ever and are more than willing to help you figure out the best track for you.
Fast–paced, but so worth it if you're trying to build a solid foundation in Chinese.
