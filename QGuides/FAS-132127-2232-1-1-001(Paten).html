I loved this class. Dr. Paten is one of the best professors I've had here. He structured the class incredibly well, had great slide decks, always responded quickly and clearly to emails, and genuinely cared about the success of all his students. Since he's leaving, however, I don't know how much I can speak to this class for future semesters. The content is quite challenging, but I think helpful to overall understanding of statistics in a scientific context.
Professor Paten is amazing. The course is very enjoyable especially if you take it with people you know. Learning statistics fundamentals and applying it through MATLAB made it much less intimidating. I could see the applications of what we did throughout the whole course and am excited to use what we learned in lab and future projects. The readings are helpful to do before class so you can remain on top of things but if you miss it, you can recover and catch up (try not to miss it and let things pile up though). I found the exams and final project to be more than fair and Prof Paten is very understanding and approachable (and funny ngl). Class format is nice since you review the material then work on practice problems with whoever is at your table.
This class was really great, especially because of the Professor Jeff Paten. The class sets you up to succeed and doesn't try to trick you. I paid attention in class, did some of the psets, and then spent 5 hours total going through the slides the night before our first midterm exam and almost got a perfect score. Its a light load and a really good intro do stats with (mostly) good concrete examples. Most classes at Harvard try to teach you by introducing concepts through Psets and this class is not one of them, the prof teaches you something and then the Pset gives you practice on exactly that. Its very predictable and I felt supported the whole time
As someone who has had no background in statistics (did not take AP stats, last time I worked with mean, median, mode was in elementary school...), this course was quite difficult. The flipped classroom learning did not work for me as the textbook reading was incredibly tough to digest. The entire course there seemed to be this overarching theme amongst all of the course staff that this material shouldn't be too hard to get but that was just not the case for me, so this atmosphere also made it pretty tough to get the help I needed as far as understanding goes. It all ended up working out in the end but if you have no statistics experience, expect to put in more hours than the average student in the class if you want to do well and walk away from the class with some actual level of understanding of statistics.
