This course is incredibly engaging and will be valuable to students with an interest in history, law, or both. Professor Herzog is someone you can learn a great deal from, and she fosters a very positive classroom environment that appreciates your questions and perspectives. You will be tested in your ability to make connections, question the material you read, and form your own opinion on prominent debates, but you are certain to become a better reader and thinker because of it. Do not let your lack of experience with the subject or the fact that you may share a space with Law School students intimidate you––there is a wealth of knowledge here that is yours for the taking.
This is a great, very straightforward course where you will gain a solid background on new developments in the history of European law each week, supplemented by many primary sources that help characterize the legal, social, political, and religious tensions underlying each development.
