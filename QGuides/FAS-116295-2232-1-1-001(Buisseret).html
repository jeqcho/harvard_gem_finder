What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Very good course. Perhaps not as useful if you don't want to 100% use formal models on your own work. If you took 2005 and want to get more exposure to cutting–edge research in the field, this is a great class.
I took this class as an applied math/gov undergrad, and if you're in a similar position to me, I wouldn't be too scared of this class being a graduate–level course. The math background helps a lot to understand the models, and I found a surprising amount of overlap at times between things I learned in other math and statistics courses at Harvard with solution concepts for some of the models we studied.
If you enjoyed 2005, you should heavily consider taking this courseâ€”it was extremely rewarding to see how we used the foundation built in 2005 to read papers and tackle more nuanced models with interesting implications. I also left the course with very concrete work to show for my effort (a referee report and research note) and with the ability to understand formal theory papers at greater depths than I ever thought I would!
Do the readings during the second half of the semester, in which Prof. Buisseret presents existing papers during lectures. It is easy to zone out if you haven't done the readings.
