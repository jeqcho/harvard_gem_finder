I would strongly recommend this course for anyone interested in global health equity, social justice, or social medicine. Learning about Paul Farmer and his impact on the world is not only deeply inspirational but also challenges you to think critically about the existing world order.
Take the course! it is a fantastic overview/ critical analysis of Paul Farmer's scholarship, with many anecdotes from the professor.
Take this course! Arthur is retiring soon, and he is an incredible professor to be taught by during your time at Harvard.
The 1 book a week requirement may seem scary but it's not too bad!
I highly recommend taking this course if you have any interest in the field of medical anthropology and social medicine and global health! Dr. Kleinman is incredibly knowledgeable about the field of medical anthropology (he was pivotal in establishing its modern foundations and has significantly advanced through his decades of work) and he adds unique context to the works we read. As the primary purpose of this class is to read medical anthropology and discuss it, I highly recommend doing the readings. Paul Farmer was quite a vivid and talented writer and if you take the time to read his work, you will enjoy it. I did not come into this course pre–med but my exposure to the clinical work that Farmer conducted was quite inspiring. If you are a pre–med, I definitely recommend taking this course as it will push you to think about what it means to provide care.
This course is definitely interesting and allows you to gain a really thorough understanding of Paul Farmer and his social theories. The readings were LONG and often took significant time to complete, but were interesting. The course discussion can get a little boring/repetitive and so it is important you are willing to contribute to discussion. Overall, I would certainly recommend a medical anthro class with Professor Kleinman because he has so much to offer, but beware of the time commitment.
