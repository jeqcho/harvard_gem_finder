Terrific class! One of my favorite courses ever. All three professors are phenomenal. Absolutely recommend if you're interested in the history of life on Earth or geology.
Take this class. It's a small class with basically no work at all and it's very interesting. However, since there is very little work, it's important to keep up with the material or else it will sneak up for exams. The field trip is super cool too.
It’s a really fun course! Definitely ask for help and feedback when you want it, and the profs and tfs will be happy to oblige. You have to do some triage with respect to the information you’re receiving because there’s a ton of it. But definitely take it! Changed the way I look at the planet’s history, which is very cool
This class is incredible!!! Nadja, Dave, and Javier do a really good job alternating lectures based on their area of expertise and are able to teach these concepts so well that you don't necessarily need much background in either EPS/geology or biology to get a lot out of this class! The labs are where you get to look at a ton of cool rocks and fossils and talk about what we can learn from them, plus there's a really great field trip where you get to see real fossils and rock outcrops in the field! The only grades are from the labs (which you basically finish during the ~2hr lab each week) from a midterm and final exam, and from two ~3–4 page papers that aren't too difficult. This class is obviously perfect for people interested in the overlap between geology and biology, but it's also a great 50–level course for people interested more in either subject!
