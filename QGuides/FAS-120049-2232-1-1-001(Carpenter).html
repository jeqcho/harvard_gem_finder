I highly suggest taking this Gen Ed if you're doing anything relating to social studies, Gov, etc. It's basically a primer in liberal political theory, and has the most essential readings for that field, which you may otherwise skip. The class is not difficult, and you get out of it what you put it – it is probably possible to get an A and do very little of the reading, but I'm serious – you're selling yourself short if you do that. Professor Carpenter is an excellent professor, and the teaching team (Daniel and Willie) are awesome TFs, very responsive and reasonable people with a deep knowledge of the subject. Probably the single best Gen Ed I ended up taking, and one of the best Gov courses I ended up taking (did I mention it gives you Gov credit???).

Take it! Especially if you've considered switching to Government but don't want to become a gov kid, this course is a great way to have your cake and eat it too.
This course is heavy on readings, but they are quality readings. I would recommend taking this class if you are a Gov concentrator or just looking to learn more about the history of government. In any case, Prof. Daniel Carpenter is an absolute wonder and brings color to the otherwise monotonous readings.
If you feel estranged from American politics system but ill–equipped to criticize it or discuss fixes, take this class. You will learn about the origins of republican government and how it compared to other forms of government with examples such as Rome, medieval Europe, and the American founding. You will read and discuss arguments from all sides of history. The class delivers on these promises and neither glosses over nor fully delves deeply into the histories of slavery and subjugation that parallel the development of "small–r" republican principles. If you are looking for a class that focuses mostly on the moral failings in the history of America or other successful republics instead of their properties, evolution, and impacts on one another, this is probably not it.

You should take this course! The hour expectation/week was very reasonable in my experience. I wish I spent more time reading the assigned readings to get more out of it myself, but lecture was always interesting. Start on the papers early and participate in class. No real warnings about this class, I thought it was fair and interesting through and through.
Be prepared for many readings and lots of breakout activities. It can be a little anxiety–inducing at first, but generally, the course is very welcoming and helpful.

You'll also be able to think normatively about government in America. The essays are not that hard, lectures are interesting, and the readings are great.
