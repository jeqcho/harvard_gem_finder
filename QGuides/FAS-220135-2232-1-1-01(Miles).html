This is a really interesting course if you are interested in Black or native studies. The readings are all really interesting and insightful, and I loved the opportunity to do a creative and meaningful project at the end of the course. Professor Miles is SO knowledgeable, and I really appreciated getting the chance to learn from her. I also learned so much from the grad students also enrolled in the course, and it would not have been as enlightening without them. Marley and Professor Miles also organized lots of guest lecturers and site visits, and this really enhanced the course. I would recommend if your interests align with the subject material! I just suggest that you engage with the course. A lot of the people were interested in the topic but didn't bring much energy into the class, and that kind of brought the vibe down at times. I think the course would have been a bit better if everyone was energetic and excited about the material rather than just kind of interested.
Tiya Miles is a truly incredible professor, taking any class with her is a once in a lifetime experience and I would strongly recommend this course in particular!
Please take this course if it is being offered again. This is such a rare course that I am so lucky to have had the chance to take. The assignments are very manageable and Professor Miles is very available to all students. This course exceeded every expectation (which were very high) for a Harvard and/or Mindich course. Every class was thrilling, full of adventures, challenging, tough, critical, and communal. The pedagogy of this class and Professor Miles and Marley's (our amazing TF) is one that is hopeful, serious, and passionate. I have felt the gravity of the issues we discuss in our past and we spent time on so many different nuanced aspects of this land's recent political histories.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!!! Prof Miles and Marley are amazing! The workload is super manageable and the readings are fascinating. I loved being able to explore this topic this semester.
Take the class! One of the best I’ve taken here. You learn a lot of things, and also how to think about them – something few courses put together so well. And it’s great to have the opportunity to learn from Prof. Miles
Take this class! It's a robust introductory course to an American history you need to know. Professor Miles is so wonderful and generates really thoughtful enthusiasm from everyone.
This class provides a great introduction to Afro–Indigenous history.
Take this course! Professor Miles is great, and Marley is an awesome TF.
This class is really interesting and there is a lot to learn from Professor Miles. She is very kind and really cares about her students. This class is not easy and requires a background in writing historical arguments.
This course is super interesting and important and Professor Miles is truly one of the more passionate and inspiring teachers I have ever had. It's not a terrible amount of work at all, but it is deeply rewarding and made me view the world and my place within it differently, especially as a biracial person who has struggled with his identity in the past.
I would tell future students to approach this class with an open mind and willingness to learn in alternative educational settings –– it can be a little intimidating to be in a class that has a more unorthodox take to the traditional lecture, but to keep in mind that this class is designed to allow you to grow in ways that other traditional lectures cannot foster.
Professor Miles is an absolute genius and also one of the kindest professors you will ever have. This is such a thoughtful, empathetic course which made the experience great. However, do not expect invigorating lectures where you are taking notes as fast as you can; this is an entirely different pedagogy that takes some getting used to. This class focuses on people and identities that have been intentionally and systematically erased from institutional history. This means that rather than lectures full of "facts," this class is more concerned with stories, people, and holding space for individual accounts and emotions. Yes, this meant that class was sometimes not as exciting as professors whose every lecture is like a TED talk, but I believe it was a super valuable change of pace that requires active participation (choosing to dig deeper and be thoughtful) than passive learning through transcribing what the professor says. At any rate, this class deals with extremely challenging topics, and I think Professor Miles' approach was the most humane I could imagine. This is definitely not for everyone, but if you appreciate less traditional methods of history and the chance to produce something creative as a final project, then you should take this class!
