Please take the class if you love movies
This is an excellent class that offers a wide breadth of perspectives on Hindi cinema. Richard ji's instruction is excellent, the readings he assigns are compelling (though sometimes long), and his feedback on assignments is unmatched. The kinds of movies you watch vary so widely, from gangster movies to comedies to period pieces set in a precolonial courtesan's world. I learned enormously from the course, and the TF Akhil was incredibly friendly, insightful, and helpful throughout the semester.
This class can be a lot of work because you have to watch a movie and write an essay every week
Take this class. If you are at all interested in Bollywood, take it.

The class was extremely unique –– coming into the class, I wasn't sure what to expect. I thought we'd be watching Bollywood movies and doing a bit of "analysis" of them. Boy, did I underestimate what "analysis" really meant. We REALLY dived into the films, dissecting particular scenes and characters within the films, as well as events and situations outside of the film. The class was extremely engaging. Richard ji asks really intriguing questions, which at first seem ridiculous. As the course of the semester went on though, we realized just how deep we can look into these films.

I learned a lot about film analysis, but also about Indian history. This class takes "killing two birds with one stone" to a whole new level. The class literally kills like six birds with half a stone.
