Strongly recommend taking this course to get better at circuit analysis and design.
Take the class!! Although I feel that there were moments where I would've appreciated going more in depth into certain topologies and designing, overall the support is unmatched and I believe it'll be a bit restructured to make this better.
The teaching fellow, Nestor, was a life saver in this class. He is a phenomenal TF. Very quick to reply to pset questions and is super knowledgeable. This class was very interesting but also disorganized. It should get better over time though, as the course staff makes adjustments.
This is a great follow up to ES 152 for those who were interested in further studying analog or mixed circuits, and is really your only option at Harvard. While I personally found the overview part of the course more engaging than the in depth amplifier design, both were good experiences that helped further my understanding of circuits and their applications. This course is not for everybody, but if you enjoyed ES 152 and want more circuits in you life that don't think of transistors as just switches, this one is for you. Gu is a great professor and, with time, I hope some of this year's inconveniences with grading and pacing can be resolved. This is not an easy class, but it is a rewarding one.
If you are an engineering major at all interested in analog (and mixed–signal) circuit design, TAKE THIS COURSE!!! (after ES152 of course) Gu (and Nestor) do a wonderful job teaching and they are very relatable, friendly people.

It was a little disorganized, but next year it's likely going to be a better course. Gu himself asked us for feedback on lecture organization.
ES154 is a great course to take after ES152 for EE concentrators! You really solidify a lot of concepts covered in ES152 and learn how to use tools like Cadence. The final project is a blast – you get to build your own OpAmp! Be prepared to spend a lot of time on this class though and you will have to work a lot with other people in the class to complete PSets. It definitely ties the community together.
This class in the end really helped in reinforcing circuit knowledge learned in ES152 as well as in understanding the design and implementation of transistor–analog circuits. It was definitely a difficult course: lectures and assignments can be overwhelming at times with the amount of content covered, but office hours really helped clarify what concepts were important to know.
Have fun, but prepare for long nights with your fellow classmates finishing psets
