If you like plants, like lab research, like looking at stuff under a microscope, and are interested in speciation, this course is perfect for you! The topic is admittedly really specific, but if you're like me and just really, really like cutting up plants and looking at pollen and that sort of thing, then you're going to have a good time. Extra bonus if you're taking ls1b after or alongside this course for premed or a concentration or whatever else, one of the topics in there is also on the same speciation biology you learn about in this freshman seminar, so you get a head start on learning that stuff too! Robin and Grace are awesome course leads, they make the class so much more fun and it's just overall a good time if you're into what this course is about.
Also, be prepared for some long weekly uber rides to the Arboretum, the course is off–campus so it can take upwards of half an hour each way to get to and from the lab where the course is.
TAKE THIS FRESHMAN SEMINAR!!!! Robin and Grace (the teaching staff) are some of the most kind, funny, and thoughtful individuals I've ever met at Harvard. During every class, they took the time to ask us about our personal lives outside of the class, and they were genuinely invested in us. If you ever needed an extension on anything, they would give it to you in a heartbeat. They brought us snacks every week for the class and were able to foster a wonderful little freshman seminar environment.

The course structure was very simple and digestible! Essentially, there are only 3 assignments and a final presentation, but they are all very low stakes not only because it's SAT/UNSAT, but also because Robin and Grace just want to see effort put into our work. The class is designed to be an introduction to scientific experimentation and plant reproduction/speciation, not anything more rigorous. Essentially, there are 3 units for different ways of speciation, and there are 3 groups that design and lead an experiment for each unit and it culminates in a final presentation. You get PLENTY of feedback and time to ask questions along the way, so it is definitely a very good, low–stakes environment!

Aside from that, one of the coolest things about this class is that the class takes place off campus in a Harvard arboretum research facility! You literally take free Ubers every week to and from this really cool, cabin–like research facility and just vibe. Nothing but vibes. The only caveat is that it's kind of far away, so you will spend a decent amount of transportation time depending on how bad the traffic is. Just a good thing to know in advance for scheduling purposes!
I loved this seminar and I think anyone who takes it will love it too! Robin and Grace are SO kind and genuinely caring people. The content of the course is interesting, and you don't have to have any background knowledge to fully embrace the course. Studying specific species of flowers (we looked at phloxes) allowed us to research things that LITERALLY had never been studied before––and Robin and Grace were happy to facilitate and support our learning in every way they could. They even brought snacks to every class! Not to mention the Arboretum is an amazing facility with state–of–the–art equipment. Be aware––the Arboretum is 30–40 minutes away (they give free Ubers), so be prepared for a bit of a (worthwhile) commute.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Or, any freshman seminar that Robin leads. She is an incredible professor! In this class, we got to meet once a week to learn about speciation in flowers, and eventually design and carry out our own experiments to understand reproductive isolation. This hands on research was an incredible opportunity, and Robin and Grace (an amazing grad student in her lab) were there every step of the way to help us learn more about plants and experimental design. As a STEM major, I really loved this class; additionally, many of my peers were not interested in STEM and still had an amazing time. I learned a lot from this class, yet it was also a very light workload, which was a great stress reliever. We had to read 6 papers and write 3 short responses throughout the semester (each response took <2 hours), as well as lead 1 experiment and write up our results for a final presentation – that's it! This was one of my favorite classes of freshman year –– definitely take it!! :)
Take this course! I cannot recommend it with more enthusiasm!!! If you are considering a freshman seminar, take this class!
