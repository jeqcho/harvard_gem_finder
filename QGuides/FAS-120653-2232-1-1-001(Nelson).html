What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I highly recommend taking this class for those who want to gain a solid understanding of how prominent thinkers have thought about property over time. Prof Nelson gives engaging lectures and does a great job of tying in thinkers from different periods. As long as you're doing the readings and refreshing yourself on the previous thinkers we've covered, you'll do fine on the exams.
This is a fantastically designed course which will acquaint you with the property–related views of many of the major thinkers in the Western tradition. It's very satisfying that the syllabus moves chronologically as it allows you to witness how authors responded to the very texts and concepts that you yourself read about earlier in the course. Astonishingly articulate and learned, Prof. Nelson is an unusually engaging lecturer—his lectures are wonderfully crafted and very easy to follow. A very enjoyable way to start the day! Fascinatingly, however, this course—at least when I took it—seemed to attract an unusual quantity of Gov concentrators who openly deride political theory, scorn the thinkers on the syllabus, and make their displeasure known. Section, as a consequence, is by far the weakest component of this course, a rather grating hour of inarticulate monologues about why Marx, Rawls, and the entire natural law tradition are dumb, wrong, and useless. This, of course, is no fault of the instructors. The course is great; the company is not.
This is such an amazing course. If you are interested in political theory I highly recommend.
I absolutely loved this course. It offers a great survey of some of the most important works in political theory. All the texts were very well chosen and they worked extremely well together, many building upon and responding to similar arguments. Professor Nelson is also an excellent professor, who demonstrates both passion and knowledge of the field through his well organized and engaging lectures that explain the texts and their ramifications very clearly. I also enjoyed the discussion section component of the course. While discussion sections usually feel unnecessary, I actually gained a lot of interesting insights from them, especially because of our TF Wells and the engagement of my other classmates. I felt like the small size of the class really helped in this regard. I highly recommend to anyone interested in political theory or the question of property more specifically.
This is likely the best theory class you will find if you are in search of a relevant theory class.
Create a schedule to do the readings, because it's very easy to get sidetracked with so few assignments.
Take this class!!! Take it. Engaging and interesting. ***
This class is very enjoyable if you are interested in government and want to dive deep into some of history's greatest thinkers. However, this course may be daunting at times if you are behind in the lectures and/or readings. If you are looking to concentrate in Government, this class is a perfect way to test the concentration out and see if it is the type of class and material that you would be interested in.
Content was extremely interesting, but the readings were way too long.
