Take this course. It isn't that hard, and you'll learn loads about math. Accessible for most backgrounds and allows you to explore lots of complex concepts. I cannot recommend it enough. You will get so much guidance throughout the year.
21A is a very fun math class, but it requires a lot of work! With 3 psets a week, my mind was constantly on 21A. However, this is very helpful when exam time comes around, as I felt that I did not have to cram.
This course stills follows a pretty tight structure, which is helpful for the transition to high school. Section sizes are small and the PSETs feel like normal homework. This class is well–taught, but sometimes section moves pretty slow. If you have already taken multiv, this is a good refresher, but it might be interesting to try a proof–based class like 22b.
Math21a is a very comprehensive and satisfying coverage of multivariable calculus for its applications. If you are diligent in keeping up with the psets, it is hard to not succeed in the course. However, if you are interested in more theoretical applications of the material, I would consider Math22b.
This is a good multivariable calculus course; the small section format is great for collaboration and enables learning with peers and with direct feedback from one's TF and CAs. The problem sets are also quite manageable.
