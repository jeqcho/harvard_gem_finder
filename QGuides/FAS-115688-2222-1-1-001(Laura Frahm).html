This class is challenging but rewarding; you get as little or as much as you want out of it, though I will admit it was definitely a bit more time–consuming than I expected it to be at the beginning.
In the end, I'm glad I took this course to gain knowledge of film history, but I definitely didn't love it. It's not just famous modern films, we spent weeks watching silent movies that were so hard to connect with and pay attention to (I will admit I fell asleep during most of the screenings). The lectures are really difficult to connect with as well. The course took up so much time from my schedule (2 lectures + a 3 hour screening + section) and the only part I consistently enjoyed was section (you could honestly skip every lecture and get about the same experience). So not the worst choice for an AFVS lecture class, but definitely not the best.
I think that I'd let students know that it's a really interesting class if you're truly interested in learning more on the topic. I think a number of my peers took the class with a passive interest in film. In the first few weeks, with a focus on unfamiliar silent film movements and theories, I think some of them lost interest in the course, as it wasn't necessarily what they were looking for. Their loss of interest in the course, however, not only affected them but also negatively affected their classmates like me as their mood towards the class could easily rub off on me. In short, I think this class is really beneficial if you get into it, but I think it's important for students to try out the first couple weeks of the course to know if it would fit them or not.
Although it seems like this course is a big time commitment (with the lectures, screening, and outside readings/projects) I do feel as though I gained valuable insight regarding the world of film which is applicable not only in a film setting but in other aspects of life. I learned how to be a more critical thinker and to push the rationale behind director's purposeful choices. This course is also very political, and delves into past social movements and how it impacts art. Definitely worthwhile!
