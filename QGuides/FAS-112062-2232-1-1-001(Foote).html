A couple of things:

(Note that these only apply if you are taking 1010b with Prof. Foote)

1. Don't be discouraged by the fact that there are three classes a week instead of two. Each lecture is around an hour (instead of 75 mins). Attendance is optional and simultaneous enrollment is allowed.

2. No psets, but there are weekly quizzes on Canvas. Usually 10–20 multiple choice questions, but he gives you three hours. So there's essentially no time constraint. You could potentially look at the questions, learn the material and answer them. (But be sure to study well before the exams.) But if you have learned the material beforehand, you will be done in less than ten minutes.

3. Sections are optional (I never went to one), but the section problems are really helpful. Be sure to go over them before the midterms and final.

4. Lecture notes are usually sufficient. I stopped reading the textbook after a while.

5. All of this course is multiple choice. The final, which had 100 multiple choice questions, counts for 50 percent of the overall grade. The midterms had 36 multiple choice each. So the exams are pretty high–stakes.

6. Roughly the top 40 percent of the class get an A/A–, with a roughly equal number of A's and A–'s. So only the top 20 percent will get an A (unless of course Chris Foote changes the policy).
