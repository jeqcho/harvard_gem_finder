It is a lot of work. The essays are graded toughly and it demands a lot of time and effort. The readings are mostly interesting and the class discussions are enjoyable. Definitely a hard expos class but not too bad.
I've heard that there is a general consensus that this is a "hard" expos section (in terms of the grading), but the class itself is pretty entertaining. The assigned readings are interesting and quite short. Adam is also a great preceptor –– he provides excellent feedback and knows how to facilitate polite discussions.
This Expos class is not the easiest. It requires more work than others, and the preceptor grades more harshly than other Expos classes that my friends took. That said, I think it does a good job of preparing you to write at Harvard through a "trial by fire" (perhaps appropriate for a class about Hell). If you don't want to spend a lot of time on Expos, don't take this course.
The other reviews I saw for this course were honestly misleading. The preceptor isn't harsh. He's nice, and thoughtful in his comments, and he's an amazing resource to talk to and guide you through your writing. Not to mention the subject matter of this section is extremely interesting. You'll love it.
If you are even slightly interested in the idea of hell take this class.
Take this section. If you're like me, just remember that you only need to write three essays. Plan to start them early, as I did, but actually execute on those plans instead of twiddling your thumbs as weeks turn to days and hours.
Definitely take this section! The readings and class discussions are very fun and insightful. Adam is an amazing teacher. He is kind, thoughtful, and encouraging. You will learn a lot of great specific writing tips from him, and he will always respond to your writing in a very thoughtful and helpful way. I became a much stronger writer thanks to this class.
I would recommend this class (The Underworld) to students who really enjoyed their high school English classes. The discussions are thought–provoking and just like a seminar.
