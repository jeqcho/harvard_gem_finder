The highlight of this class was the case discussions and guest speakers. Every class is engaging and energetic. Josh is a wonderful discussion facilitator. The guest speakers were diverse and super cool to meet. Go to every class! It’s worth it!
Very useful class if you want to see what an MBA would look like. Josh Lerner is a very engaging professor and you have guest speakers in many of the classes, which helps you gain a wide range of perspectives. Josh is also readily available whenever you need him. He helped me an incredible amount with my research paper, even providing me with data on the topic I wanted to research and taking the time to sit down with me and go through my regression models. I strongly recommend this course.
I can't recommend this course highly enough! Because undergrads can't cross–register at the Business School, Prof. Lerner has taken his first–year MBA course and brought it to the College for us – it's a really rare opportunity that you should take advantage of if you have any interest in entrepreneurship at all. As a CS concentrator who's always been interested in innovation but was always intimidated by the business–y venture capital buzzwords, this course gave me a great introduction to how the innovation ecosystem really works, through a combination of inspirational case sessions and informational classes on the basics of venture capital, IPOs, business plans, and other relevant topics.
Definitely take this class if you are interested in entrepreneurship and want to work through specific business issues. Professor Lerner is the sweetest ever, and he also is such an incredible professor who stimulated interesting discussions. You get to experience the case method from an MBA professor, and it's really so exciting. We also got to meet founders, VC partners, PE investors, etc etc. who shared their experiences. Overall, 10/10 super enjoyable class and not stressful work at all!!

It is absolutely awesome! Professor Lerner is amazing and if you have the opportunity to take a class with him, you absolutely should. This course did not even feel like a "class" because it was so interesting and cool.
I would say to take this class. it was so fun and Josh is a great and enthusiastic lecturer. you learn a lot from the case studies and from your peers and it truly has that Harvard MBA class style written all over it. You meet some really cool people and just generally learn what it takes to be successful with your startup. The materials for the class (in terms of case studies) are very applicable to the business school model – however the way the class is taught is not. The problem with the case study method for undergraduates is that no one actually knows that much about business, and so it is often the blind leading the blind. I felt as if Prof Lerner could have done a better job of guiding the discussion towards some general teaching points that he wanted to make. Instead the class just felt like a free for all of students just giving points, without Lerner actually teaching us. There were some lessons which were lectures, and they were very enjoyable, I just wish that during the case study lessons, Lerner would also make more of an effort to teach.
This class is basically an introductory HBS course (taught by HBS faculty) that was approved for undergrads, so if you're interested in entrepreneurship, definitely take advantage of it. The course is new, so some of the section structures were a bit disorganized, but Josh's enthusiasm and knowledge more than make up for it. It's amazing how someone with a background as impressive as his can be so humble and accessible, and that translates into his teaching and the way he connects with students. Overall I learned a lot in the class and got some great mentorship out of it, too!
Take your time and complete all the readings. Having a good understanding of each case may spark ideas you didn't know you had during the discussion in class.
This is one of the most disappointing courses I have taken in my four years at Harvard. First of all, beware that the course materials are expensive like $100+. I feel like I invested in this course because it was such a unique offering, but it was just so terrible.
Firstly, the cases may be fun to read, but I left every class feeling like we just rehashed the details of the case. We never learned an approach to solving problems or models to judge situations. We never really delved into finance. I constantly felt like I was missing something – why did I leave every class feeling like we got no where? It's also because they year I took it there was the worst of Harvard entrepeneurship bros taking the course.
Secondly, even though I didn't like how we went through cases, I really like Professor Lerner's disposition and teaching style – it's not pretentious and genuinely consider's every student's response.
The only redeeming part of the course was getting to meet VC professionals and founders from the cases we were reading/ in the industry. TBH if I just audited the course it would have been better.
