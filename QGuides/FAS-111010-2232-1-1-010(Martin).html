Math 1b is a standard calculus course. Nothing to write home about. I really believe that taking Math 1a prior is helpful. Math 1a sets up Math 1b, however, I do know students that went straight to 1b and did good. Learning math at Harvard is generally a learning curve.
It's a hard class, but if you pay attention in class and use the worksheets and MQC on the psets it's manageable. It's important to understand the topic if you want to do well and it's not really a class you can coast through without doing work
This class has a lot of support via MQC and exam preparation tools and all PSets have answer keys after to help you review your mistakes. However, teaching is really just dependent on who your TF is.
If you attend class most of the time and put effort into psets you will do fine! When you get stuck on a topic you can get great support from the CAs which helps tremendously as well.

This was actually a pretty rewarding experience. Sure, three psets per week is pretty brutal, but the wonderful CAs at math question center and the instructors running office hours are always very helpful. Classes are interactive, so it is easy to stay engaged. The teaching staff is wonderful, and the exams are pretty fair. Overall, it is a grind but I would still recommend this class.
