This class is absolutely worth it to take a class with Jay, he truly knows so much about the subject and there are so many cool things to learn. It's really interesting to see how so many languages that you might think aren't related to each other have so many connections. Oisin is a great, understanding TF. The problem sets can be a little subjective at times, but overall are pretty managable. Study well for the final exam: though 24 hours seems like a lot, it goes by fast!
This is an interesting and well taught class, and Professor Jasanoff is obviously a giant in this specific field of Indo–European historical linguistics. Lectures are generally interesting, although quite technical with regard to sound change. Jay does a great job of relating things back to examples that have survived into the modern English language, and you can learn a lot about why certain things in Modern English are the way they are! It's also a fun class in general if you're interested in Indo–European stuff; one of my favorite parts of the class was seeing the huge language family trees in the slides That being said, this is fully a non–trivial and non–introductory course, and in my opinion is much harder than the other Ling 10x classes I've taken (which I wasn't necessarily expecting), and the final was also pretty hard. So if you're willing to take a challenging course with a pretty hard final exam, go for it, but I would not recommend it as an introductory course (despite the fact it has no prereqs)

I loved this class so much!!!! It was so so interesting learning about PIE and how branches of Indo–European evolved, and almost every lecture I was fascinated by something new. Prof. Jasanoff's lectures are wonderful –– he is one of the experts in the field on this topic, and presents the material in such an engaging manner, sprinkled in with geography tidbits and historical/cultural information. Ois√≠n's section was also super helpful. My perspective is so much richer now that I've taken this –– it offers so many interesting explanations for phenomena I took for granted!

The class is pretty fast–paced and does build on itself, and the material is pretty dense and non–intuitive, so go to lecture and section! You can get by without it for doing the psets, but it'll help a lot for the final and lecture is great regardless so it's definitely worth putting the time in. The psets are unironically fun to do –– they're graded check+/check/check– so as not to be stressful. The final exam is an interesting but incredibly intense 24–hour takehome, and you really need to have understood the material to do a comprehensive job.

Overall I think if you are interested in historical linguistics/PIE then this is a great way to learn about it! Genuinely one of the most fun classes I've taken here.
