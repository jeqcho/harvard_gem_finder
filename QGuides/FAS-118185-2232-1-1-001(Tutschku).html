What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
don't underestimate the time commitment
One of the best experiences I've had from a class here at Harvard. As a student who has taken multiple courses in music tech before this class, this course stands out in its focus to organized, effective and multi–dimensional classroom. By multi–dimensional, I mean the course structure that makes the student train their ears to appreciate the aesthetics, open their mind to learn the creative process, and practice their hands to master the production technology. Even if the student entered the course with no preparation and with no idea what to expect, they will see their growth in the world of electronic music.
Go out of your way to give other people detailed feedback. This will help you notice things in your own compositions and improve both your listening and composition skills. Also, don't do the assignments one day before they are due; reserve some time in your week to do the listening assignments and compositions.
This is the best class I’ve ever taken! Hans and Elena are extremely encouraging and supportive. I’ve learned so much from them throughout this semester and would highly recommend this course to anyone who is even remotely interested in electronic music or music in general. I was scared that I didn’t know enough to take this class, but that definitely was not the case. The 2.5 hour class may seem daunting, but the time always passed so quickly and I looked forward to going to class and hearing everyone’s work each week. It was also so so cool to listen to everyone’s final pieces on the hydra system in concert and I had a lot of fun working on my own piece.
Take this class. Hans is an amazing professor and every assignment is meaningful and leads to you developing skills to make abstract electronic music and express yourself through music in general. Highly recommend.
