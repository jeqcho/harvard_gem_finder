Highly recommend this class. Professor Engert is cool. Coding assignments are super easy. Readings are straightforward. Definitely worth it to take this class just for the digital assignments, which are really satisfying and an informed look into what current neuroscience research and analysis may look like. This class also helped me learn how to read better. One thing is that it's not entirely clear how the class is graded (I have no clue what to expect on the final), and the reading quizzes can be really difficult. Grading on these depends a lot on your section, and I got the feeling that sections were really different according to who you got as section leader.
Definitely take this class. It is an absolute gem and you will learn a lot! Engert makes sure that you know you are not supposed to worry about your grades in this class and that he merely wants you to focus entirely on the content. The matlab psets are pretty straight forward and there is also help for them. you DONT NEED a programming background! So please dont let that shoo you away. The final exam was a little unguided because there were no practice exams or anything like that, but they say they grade it leniently so it should be fine. Choose a fun project for the final project, choose something you have wanted to explore, that will make the work and research you do 10 times more fun. Overall, PLEASE TAKE!

Otherwise, start early on the Digital Assignments and don't try to finish them in one go – it rarely works

Take this course if you are a neuro major for sure. It is supposedly a gem, but I am not sure how accurate that is... It was challenging, sometimes in a fruitful way, sometimes not, but I suppose the weekly course work was not too heavy and kind of gem–like. If you are not a neuro major (like myself) you might struggle to grasp some of the larger concepts and the aura of the work you are supposed to be producing on projects, quizzes, and exams. Nonetheless, I learned a ton! Lecture was super fun and the readings incredibly interesting!
While this class does cover a lot of interesting material, I don't think it was structured in way that best suited my learning. Florian's lecture were engaging, but that material was not reinforced that well in section. The weekly reading quizzes were not kind of annoying. It's a fun and interesting class and the workload is quite manageable. I don't think there's much general theory or overall structure, but the lectures and concepts are taught through a lot of (interesting) examples. Professor and teaching staff are very knowledgeable.
I would tell future students that the material in this class is very interesting and there is a strong focus on animal models, experimental design, research papers, and MATLAB data analysis.
Prof. Engert is such an interesting person. I was never once bored in this class when he was lecturing. As someone without an extensive coding background, I can attest that the MATLAB assignments are great. So so much better than CS50, and it almost rekindled my interest in CS. The applications are extremely fascinating and make you understand how all the neural circuits we learn through behavior can be encoded mathematically. A great follow–up to Neuro80. One thing though is that the final exam was very difficult in terms of the level of detail you have to recall.
