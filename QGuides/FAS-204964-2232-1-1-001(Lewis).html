This is a fantastic course for CS majors or those with heavy experience in CS stuff. If you're getting a secondary, you won't fully understand most things and could be dead weight to your group's discussions; so I recommend taking some other class.
Professor Lewis is an amazing lecturer and would be a shame if you took it half–heartedly. You get so much out of class if you commit and he always eats lunch with students afterwards. He is amazing and give him a shot***
At the beginning of this semester, Harry showed a chart saying that last year, 60% of people got A's, 30% got A–'s, and 10% got less than that. He then said that all the people that put negative comments on the Q the previous year must have been people who got A–'s and thought they should have gotten A's. He might well have been right, and make of that what you will, since in most classes people aren't as bitter about grades.

In the end, I didn't enjoy this class. For starters, there is an implicit deal: you basically don't need to do the readings––you can spend maybe 20 minutes per class on the diary entries––and as long as you show up to class and do the video project, you will pass. For some people, this is valuable in itself, in which case the class will be worth taking. Because participation is not graded (besides attendance), the class discussions can be quite a slog, with seemingly nobody having done the readings. Harry's lectures are great, but they only last about 1/3 of the class, leaving the final 2/3s for discussions full of awkward silences and misguided comments by people who have barely done the readings.

Then there is the video project, which counts for 100% of your grade (conditional on attending all classes) and that you get two tries at. As you quickly learn, the most important thing is to be persuasive and to do as little technical explanation as possible. From talking to other students, it seems that the less technical topic, the more one is likely to be successful. Some people will spend a couple of hours on this and get an A, and others will struggle for weeks and not get an A. It's hard to tell in advance what bucket you'll fall into.
The discussion component of the class is fun and a nice way to meet new people. The video project being your entire grade is pretty daunting, but there's opportunity for help/revision. Just follow their rubric and it'll be fine.
I would take this class to fulfill the secondary requirement for CS if you really need one senior spring, but I think you'll probably feel like you learned more elsewhere. This course is geared and gives more to people who are concentrating in CS, not secondaries.
A great way to knock off a secondary/concentration requirement. Read the papers to get something out of the course, otherwise you'll cruise and leave not much better than you came in.
***Great course to take as a senior to really cap off a CS concentration or secondary. A very reasonable amount of work, and a really great way to put your CS education into perspective. Don't be afraid to take this class, all the students and teaching staff are super accommodating if you don't understand something!
This is a special class. The guest speakers alone make it worthwhile – this year, FOUR Turing award winners spoke with the class via Zoom (these are people who had foundational roles in creating the internet, programming languages, and more). The video project was a bit of a pain, but the only other work you'll need to do for the course is a few readings per class.

I feel as though the CS concentration does not necessarily cover all areas of CS. For instance, I have not taken an ML course here and had never heard about error–correction codes prior to this course. However, I now feel as though I have a general understanding of all the fundamental areas of computer science. I have also had the chance to converse with several people in my concentration who share my interests and new ones, which has felt like a personally enriching and inspiring experience. I would definitely recommend this course to anyone who studies CS and would especially recommend it to people with a strong background in CS. The papers in this class and their history are very cool and worth exploring! You'll undoubtedly walk away from this class with more CS knowledge and a stronger connection to the people in the CS department.
