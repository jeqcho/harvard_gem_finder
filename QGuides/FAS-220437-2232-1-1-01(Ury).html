What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If one day Scott Ury decides to come visit Harvard for another semester, you take this class. The weekly workload is manageable, but make sure that you actually do the readings. You entirely get out of this class what you put into it, and it is really important that you've done the readings in order to both participate in and learn from the weekly discussions. The final paper is on the longer side so don't procrastinate it, but you're given so much freedom with it that you can ensure it's a topic that interests you.
This is a great class if you're interested at all in Jewish history or politics. While this class is not too difficult or time–consuming, I would recommend that students have taken at least one history class at college before taking this seminar because Professor Ury expects some proficiency in understanding historical academia and being able to write an analytical historical essay. The seminar was always a joy to attend and super interesting. Definitely do the readings before class because you can't get away with not knowing them.
Such an interesting class with a phenomenal instructor. Some of the most interesting readings and discussions I've had at Harvard. take it if you have the chance!
