I highly recommend this course. It is a fair amount of reading but completely manageable. My entire perspective changed through the readings and in–class discussions. I absolutely loved this seminar and learned so much. Please take this course!
You learn a lot about democracy and education, to the point where you can even feel a bit disillusioned, but in a good way. By realizing the extent of the problem of our system, it also inspires you to be a vector of change, and overall truly inspiring.
Take time to engage with the readings as much as you can, because they are quite rich.
I would recommend this course. You will read a great variety of interesting texts.
embrace it be an active participant
This course is wonderful for anyone interested in education, social justice, philosophy, and politics. It encourages you to think deeply about the role of education in society and in terms of content, it fulfilled and transcended my expectations. It does a good job of introducing the a very wide breadth of theory on the topic and opening you up to further questioning. As far as freshman seminars go, I would say this is one of the more demanding in terms of work and assignments—a lot of reading, response papers, and presentations, which is something to keep in mind—though all of it is quite fascinating.
