If you're looking at this class, then odds are that it's either a concentration requirement or a prerequisite for something else like the premed track. Here's the rundown, but be aware that the class is going to be taught by a different professor next year so I don't know how much of this will still apply.

Lectures: Decent to good, it really depends on the lecturer so it may be different, but they weren't bad. Dr. Berry and Dr. Sabeti each had their styles of lecturing that were both more engaging than just sitting in a hall listening to someone talk for an hour, which was nice. They put up these questions which you just had to answer for attendance, but other than that, they're a good way to get the general idea of what you're going to be learning in any given week, with some examples sprinkled in. Pay attention to the examples that they give, a fair amount of this class is (kinda surprisingly) based in some math and statistics, so seeing problems being worked through will come in handy on the PIEs(more on those later).

Lecture notes: There are these notes published weekly alongside the lecture slides, READ THEM. Go through, take notes, make sure you understand everything in the lecture NOTES. Anything in there is fair game, and sometimes there are details that aren't in the slides, so if you want to do well, use them. Heck, you could probably get through the class on those alone, as long as you also make sure you know how to do some simple stats calculations.
Lab: So once a week, you go into a lab room, do an experiment or part of an experiment, clean up, fill out some info for a lab report, and repeat. That part's pretty fun if you're into biology, what sometimes sucked is when we had a multi–week experiment where something went wrong in the beginning and we had to use sample data for the whole thing, but yeah. If you're coming from ls1a, be warned that there are also a few lab papers you'll have to write throughout the semester, where you have to find scientific articles and then do a write–up on a specific gene or bacteria. It's very formulaic, they tell you what to include though, so it's not hard just time–consuming.

Exams: Good news, ls1b doesn't have midterms. Bad news, it has these things called PIEs instead. Instead of 1–2 midterms, you have 5 min–exams that are 40 minutes each, and you get to drop the lowest one. I'm not sure what they were like in past years, but coming from someone who did pretty well on them, they did NOT calibrate the difficulty correctly. Half the class barely passed the first one, and they weren't much better moving forward. You're expected to write way too much for the time they give you, and any short–response question is graded on whether you include 1 or 2 specific details about the question topic, so you end up just writing everything you know in the box and hoping for the best. They let you take a single page of notes into each one too, but there isn't time to really use it, and some questions are oddly specific so you still have to write mostly everything on it in order to answer those questions. Be prepared if you're going to take this class. The final exam was pretty fair though, so credit where credit is due.

Homework: Classic PSET course, not much different from ls1a's PSETs if you did those. Get a weekly document, answer questions, go to office hours, work with other people, etc. Definitely get a good PSET group together if you're not already planning on taking this class with other people you know. Halfway through the semester, they reduced the number of PSETs, but that just meant they took half of the remaining PSETs and merged them with the other half, so each one was twice as long instead. In terms of difficulty, very hit or miss, some would be pretty straightforward and then others would take hours to figure out.

So...yeah, that's about it. The content was actually pretty interesting, there's even a little bit of disease genetics and virology if that's what you're into (I was, which made it fun), and overall it wasn't bad – though, having friends also taking it and being able to study together and all that definitely helped.
