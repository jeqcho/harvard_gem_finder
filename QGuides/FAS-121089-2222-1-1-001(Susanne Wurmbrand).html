This is a great course. It is well–organized and the material is interesting. It can be challenging at times, but the textbook is a helpful resource and you can work with classmates on the problem sets. I would recommend taking one of the introductory linguistics courses first.
A great class to introduce the world of syntax and X–bar theory to those who aren't familiar with it and are interested. I used it as one of my linguistics classes to fulfill my secondary, and I really enjoyed it. Concepts are applied VERY well in homework assignments, exams are fair, and in–class and study materials are abundant. I didn't really read the textbook, though I will say it does help in understanding concepts for the first time and/or exploring them more deeply. One thing though –– if you don't have experience with syntax (like me), things will definitely not make much sense in lecture or section the first time around, but working through the homework really solidifies understanding. Overall a great class on syntax, definitely gets tougher as it goes, but I really feel that oddly enough, come exam time, things actually start making sense as you look back through all your notes.
If you take this class, spend some time learning the parts of speech and the vocabulary for basic grammar. It will help you immensely if you go in knowing all of this so you don't have to spend time trying to catch up just to understand the terminology.
This is a tough class. The material often doesn't make sense, and once you learn one thing, the next week you have to change basically everything that you just learned. I wouldn't recommend this class for someone who wants to try a linguistics class to see what it is like, there are plenty of classes in the department that are both more interesting and easier to understand. It's helpful to make friends to do psets with. The class is hard but the midterm and final weren't too bad!
It's definitely complicated, but Deniz as a TF makes it extremely manageable
The course is designed to walk you through the basics and complexities of syntax. Theoretical linguistics is a niche subject and should be considered if you have a great interest in abstract linguistic topics.
The problem with syntax (as with many linguistics courses) is that the theory is updated and corrected each week, so old information quickly stops being useful. This is a very disorienting pedagogical approach. When combined with the professor's, TF's, and textbook's differing conventions/expectations, it made it very difficult for me to know what to do. I felt that section could have been used to review the material rather than introducing new ways of thinking about the material. As one of the non–concentrators in the class, I almost felt like the section was not designed for me at all.
This is a challenging but informative class.
An excellent class if you're looking to learn more about syntax.
This class was okay. One of those cases of a terrific professor’s being assigned to a fairly dull curriculum but making the most of it. The material was a bit dry at times, but this truly wasn’t her fault. Having Professor Wurmbrand for LING 102 was like eating a meal cooked by a world–class chef but made using somewhat lousy ingredients.

The pedagogy was so powerful and well thought out. The homework was very useful to do and drew upon everything we learned, brought it all together — very well designed. I definitely liked the assignments from the first half a bit better, but that’s just because I didn’t enjoy the material from the second half of the course. Again, not the professor’s fault. Additionally, the exams were really interesting. A challenge, in the best way possible.

Talk to the DUS about using morphology for this core requirement, but if you definitely have to take this class, take it with Professor Wurmbrand. She’s amazing! Gave us a real glimpse into how scientists build models, find flaws in those models, and then improve them. Also, she and the TF were transparent with us about the flaws in the models we were learning, about how the field is very much a work in progress, which I loved.

So the course was really as good as a syntax course was going to get. Again, if you’re going to take syntax, try to take it with Professor Wurmbrand. She’s so good!

This class was definitely a highlight of the linguistics department, and I recommend that you give it a go if you can. It makes a good fifth class, and the grading is extremely kind too (we're talking most of the class getting 100% on the midterm and final).
Have fun with this class and you will get tired of drawing trees
