What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is basically a reading group, but with a final project at the end. Indeed, this means that you get as much out of the class as the effort you put into reading and understanding the papers. So, I strongly suggest you to carefully read them, and engage in discussions.
This course is one of my favorite taken at Harvard. You read a wide array of papers and present on two of them. During class, you hear the presentations and discuss the papers, and it is really helpful to hear everybody's insight. In addition, the professor comments on the writing of the paper, which is really interesting to hear.
Stat 293 is a great course. The teaching staff, led by Jose and Larry, are fantastic – they take great care to walk through the difficult material and are very patient and supportive. The set of papers is very interesting and fundamental for a strong understanding of research and applications of causal inference and experimental design methods. The course itself is also quite flexible in terms of assignments, which were all helpful in building understanding of the material. I would strongly recommend the course!
This is the course for you if you are interested in causal inference. Some prior exposure to causal inference would be good, but not mandatory. Jose is a brilliant professor who is incredibly knowledgeable and passionate about the course subject. Larry is a great TF. You won't regret taking the course.
