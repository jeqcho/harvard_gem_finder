This class has a fairly heavy reading load, but the readings are pretty interesting. Professor Harrisson is very kind and cares a lot about this topic! You will learn how to think about how subjective the surveys are that create a lot of the facts we recognize as true about our world, and you will definitely gain the skills to create your own! The content is not always the most interesting, but I think it could pair well with a thesis or if you know you want to do survey research, or if you are just already interested in the topic! A pretty easy way to fulfill your QRD, especially if taken pass/fail, because there are a fair amount of assignments.
I can tell that Prof. Harris is a very sweet man. However, I felt the lectures were a bit disorganized/sporadic. I got confused on what to write notes on and it felt that sometimes the lectures didn't really address the readings for that week. Section usually makes more sense and was more helpful in this respect. A lot of the class content felt somewhat like common sense, but it really gave you a lot of the terminology to describe the issues that could arise as well as pinpoint other issues you would've have thought of if you were creating a survey. I liked the class overall and I thought it was helpful.

If youre a humanities concentrator looking to get your QRD out of the way, this is a great additive course to do it with since it will be helpful in your thesis/final papers, etc. Very aimed towards social science majors as well. **
This course has demanding assignments that require you to stay on top of the material. But if you do, it really is manageable and interesting.
If you're interested in survey research methods or need to fulfill your Quantitative Reasoning requirement, I would strongly encourage you to take this class. The coursework is informative and engaging, yet not too overwhelming. Additionally, the instructors are truly exceptional. Their passion for survey research shines through in their teaching, and it's evident that they are dedicated to helping their students succeed.
