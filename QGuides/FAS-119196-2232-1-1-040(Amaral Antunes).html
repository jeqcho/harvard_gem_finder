This class is honestly amazing. It's the right amount of challenging, fun, and enriching, although I would clarify that your experience depends HEAVILY on the quality of your TF and the people in your section. I had an amazing group of people and one of the best TFs (shoutout to Jonier) so I was spoiled; I'm fairly certain the experience wasn't as good for some people in other sections.
Regardless, though, the class is an amazing one. The lessons are structured very well, and MQC is built to help you. The TFs and even course heads are very accessible if needed, and the CAs were basically in your shoes a year ago so they're chill and empathetic.
The two hardest parts of the class are definitely visualizing in 3 dimensions and being able to conceptualize vectors. If you're able to do both of these things well, you're set.

Also, a background in physics is CLUTCH for anything to do with vectors. Didn't know this coming in but I was very blessed.

Not much else to say, but if you're looking for a moderate level math but don't want to make math the focus of your semester, this is a fabulous class to take.
This class will hold you accountable through frequent psets and a fast pace, but if you buy in it will be a rewarding experience.
