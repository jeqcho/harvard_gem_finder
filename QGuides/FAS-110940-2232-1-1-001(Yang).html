Consistent practice is key to success in this course. Set aside time (especially before dictations) to review the material and practice applying it to new scenarios. It is an every day commitment, so make sure you really want to learn the language before signing up! That being said, it really is worth the commitment if you are interested. My language skills have improved dramatically, and the class is SO much fun. I've also become close friends with some of my classmates and gotten to know the instructors, who are the best!
120B is definitely a step up from 120A! You definitely need to dedicate more time to learning and reviewing old and new grammar before quizzes and tests.
If you are at all interested in learning Chiense, take! this! class! It is by no means easy (very demanding work and time wise) but you learn so much from such an amazing teaching staff that it is absolutely worth it. Definitely reinvigorated my love for the language!
This course is a great intermediate course for anyone interested in one day attaining fluency. Study for tests and you'll be fine.
the chinese department does a phenomenal job of teaching its students, and this course is no exception. i took more out of 1 year of 120a/b than i did in 3 years of high school chinese. that being said, this is a challenging course, and you should be prepared for the mile–long trek to vanserg every day. the class quizlet really helps when studying for dictations, and it is best to do a little bit of studying every day (even just 15 mins!) instead of trying to cram. and definitely make use of the extra credit opportunities –– they don't involve a lot of work for how much they can contribute to your grade.
TAKE CHINESE!!! This is truly one of the best departments at this entire school. The teachers are some of the best people you'll ever meet, and they care so deeply about their students. It's so evident that they don't want you to fail, they want you to succeed, and they dedicate their entire selves to your learning. They want to you to care, and it works!! If you're thinking about taking Chinese, seriously do it, you won't regret it.
Sure, you can get through Chinese 120b smoothly. I never really devoted more than 4 hours of work maximum outside of class and individual sessions. But, put in more time and effort (and it doesn't have to be too much, 1–2 hours is probably fine), and what you gain, in knowledge, culture, and community, is TREMENDOUS. Get to know the teachersthey are amazing, especially Guo Laoshi. Get to know your classmatessome of my close friends I found through Chinese and I have a core study group still going from freshman year. Engage with the culture, and you learn so much about people and life. 10/10 would recommend!
Make sure to start your homework assignments and review quiz studying early–––honestly even half an hour a day spent on Chinese homework/studying (outside of class) is very helpful to your language learning and retention of vocabulary.
