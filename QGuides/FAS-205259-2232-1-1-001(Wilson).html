What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Absolutely take this class. I got so much joy out of studying the history of 20th century physics with Prof. Wilson. This is his area of expertise and he communicates the material with enthusiasm and deep knowledge. The time commitment is minimal and the intellectual enrichment is significant.
This is a great class. The topics are fascinating, and you don't need any specific background to learn a lot. Do the readings as thoroughly as possible; it'll only make your reading responses and experience in discussions better. Prof. Wilson is super nice and enthusiastic about the material.
One of my favorite Harvard classes ever! Such an interesting and informative class that manages to be intellectually challenging but relatively low stress. One of the most reasonable syllabi (in terms of assignments and weekly reading load) that presents important ideas and developments in physics in the 20th century while weaving in important social and political developments, highlighting the mutually influential relationship between both spheres. No previous knowledge of physics or history of science required. Professor Wilson is also super approachable and a great person to speak to if interested in studying the history of physics, or the history of science more generally.
