What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You will enjoy the readings in this course. They are well selected, and they offer a glimpse into photography as an art, which is a really important thing to consider.
This is a great course if you want to read about photography, and talk about its relationship to literature. A lot of the readings are dense, so be prepared to spend a fair amount of time on them if you want to fully grasp them. Class discussions are very focused on understanding what each author has to say about photography, more so than looking at or analyzing photographs.
Take this class! Especially if you are a photographer, this is the class for you. The class is perfectly structured, with engaging readings, well–spaced essays, smaller "pastiche" writing assignments, and a few really cool museum visits. The class discussions are superb, and M Rodriguez does a fantastic job of lightly facilitating them but leaving a lot of space for students to talk about what they want. This has been one of my favorite classes at Harvard. I think one of the key reasons is that it wasn't so much a french class as it was a class taught in french. It's a fantastic class at the core, and the ability to take it in french only makes it better.
I can't wait to take another class with Professor Rodriguez! He was so engaging and fostered enthusiastic discussion in every class.
Take this class if you want to explore the complex role of photography in our lives while also improving your French skills. This course is pretty writing–heavy, but you will learn a lot from it!
