What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Great instructor and materials just unclear assignments and a bit disorganized. But extremely kind and intelligent Professor and cool stories
I would say to take it! My biggest advice is to just have fun. If you keep in mind how fun it is that we are talking about trolls, culture, witchcraft, spirits, spells, and stories that range thousands of years, you'll feel fortunate that you get to dedicate a semester to this topic with one of the leading experts in the field. I felt so incredibly lucky for this experience. Mindset is important, because you could easily become stressed and overwhelmed if you try to intensely and perfectly keep track of names, places, unfamiliar language, and belief systems. That being said, take it seriously as Steve expects you to be fully prepared and to work through the materials making connections and thoughtfully exploring them with purpose. Take risks, make mistakes, and open yourself for seeing things in new ways.
take this course if you are interested but be prepared to read nearly 200 pages weekly. I loved it despite being a slow reader!
This class is a great example of how folklore and mythology can be important to other disciplines (ex. Scandinavian studies).
