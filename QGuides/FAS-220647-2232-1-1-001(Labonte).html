Incredible class. Dr. LaBonte cares so much about the course and her students, and the TFs are excellent. The course has a manageable workload with readings that are short enough for you to actually do, and the assignments are structured to keep you accountable and engaged. One of the better histsci classes I've taken.
I thought the readings were an appropriate length. However, the weekly response papers are a silent killer. You'd think writing 600 words would be relatively easy, but forcing yourself to write 600 words week after week for 12 weeks starts to take a toll and gets really old really quickly. To be frank, the amount of writing started to make me question why I wanted to do Histsci. Other than the response papers, lecture was enjoyable and the podcast assignments were all pretty manageable. By the time finals weeks rolls around, you'll be most of the way done with the podcast. That being said, if you truly enjoy writing and are interested in the material, then this course is great for you.
I would recommend this course if you have any interest in biotech and how different life sciences technologies have developed over time or bioethics and equity in healthcare.
Great class and not much work! Highly recommend!
If you have the chance take this class!!! Hands down the best class I have taken at this school!

Professor Labonte was one of the best teachers I've had at Harvard. Her course was an absolute diamond — the readings were interesting and very accessible, she was kind, and she included so many engaging activities in the lectures. Often, in addition to pair–and–shares, Professor Labonte would include activities where she'd hand out a picture of a graph from part of a crucial research paper in the field and we'd try to determine what the paper was about, or what the figure meant. She always walked around the class to listen in and ask questions to the students who were discussing the paper in groups. Furthermore, her lectures were connected to the readings, but weren't repetitive. I always felt like I was learning something new! At some point, I stopped taking notes in the class, just because I felt like it was so interesting and so engaging that I didn't need the notes to be able to remember all of it. Professor Labonte also emphasized that she was very interested in meeting with students, and meeting with her was always really interesting! During every class, she'd frequently mention that she often "debated" what to put into the lectures, just because there was so much interesting material to share! It was clear that she cared deeply about the course and was very well prepared to teach it. One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard, by far.
TAKE. THIS. CLASS. I have never met a Professor at Harvard who truly cares for her students as much as Michelle Labonte. She does everything in her power to support you, and truly values student feedback. She is a 12/10 lecturer, and manages to make every single class amazingly interesting. If you have the opportunity with Professor Labonte (and specifically this one), DO IT!

This class is INTERESTING. Seriously, I'm MCB, not even HistSci, and I loved this class's material, progression, and curriculum. At the same time, the HistSci concentrators I knew in the class were some of the most active, so it must have been very engaging on multiple fronts, even with my limited academic perspective. The class talks about major events/discoveries/observations/advances I was aware of from an MCB perspective but focuses on the historical and thereby political and/or cultural events that made the history we talk about through the course significant, connected, and relevant to the modern day.

If that doesn't sound interesting to you, you're in the wrong place. If that sounds even remotely interesting to you, this should be on the top of your potential class list at the very least, if you didn't already enroll before finishing this review.
