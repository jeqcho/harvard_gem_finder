I don't feel like I got a lot out of this class because I took it as a fourth pset class, which I DO NOT recommend. It's not an easy class by a long shot, which I thought it would be based off of the Q guides from the year before. Markus is a great guy, and if you can make his office hours he's good about answering questions and clearing things up, but his lectures are hard to follow and his psets are SO LONG. I don't think I would take this class if I could redo this semester. But if you can make the time, I think you'll get a lot more out of it. There are things in this class that deal with quantum electrodynamics, which is important in higher–level physics classes. You could do a QFT treatment of optics!!! So in summary, this class is not easy, not very well organized, but you might get something out of it?
Some of the psets felt more like math than physics, but overall it was really cool to learn about lasers and optics. Recommended.
175 is a great class with prof. Greiner, he is a wonderful lecturer and a great professor as well. The material gets interesting in the second half of the term, so expect less "Modern Optics" than the title suggests. PSets are pretty short and well–connected to the class. I think one can take 175 without 15c if they had previous wave/ray optics experience.
This is an amazing course. The material is really cool and useful, and Markus is a fantastic lecturer. My only slight criticism is that the lecture notes are quite haphazard and lectures aren't recorded, so it's very hard to catch up if one lecture goes over your head or you have to miss class once.
Most of the content in this course is very interesting and important to know if you are interested in AMO and Professor Greiner has a lot of very useful intuition that he conveys in his lectures. The only downside is that the course is a bit disorganized and there are not a lot of opportunities for learning the material beyond lecture (since the class doesn't follow a particular textbook), especially for the content that doesn't appear in the problem sets.
This is a good class to take if you are thinking of doing anything experimental in the sciences. It will teach you all the fundamentals, and also give you a chance to think about all the applications of lasers. It is a fun time and not too stressful.
This is a fantastic class! If you have taken 15c or have significant experience with waves/optics, I would definitely recommend it. Sepehr and Professor Greiner both gave good explanations for concepts, were available outside of class for office hours or help, and took the time to get too know every student. The demos were also a lot of fun!
