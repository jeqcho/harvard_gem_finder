What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The assignments need a lot of effort to solve, so better start earlier. Also, textbooks are strongly recommended to consult when doing homework. This is very helpful in building the connection between assignments and lectures.
I took this course to satisfy my 15c requirement and would recommend to that end. The content of the course was surprisingly interesting to me. Lars' lectures are a bit dry but they present the content very clearly. Lars is very sweet and accommodating. The problem sets had some interesting problems but also often had ambiguous wording that required going to office hours to clarify. This was frustrating, though the TF was usually accommodating.
If this course is offered like it was this year, know that it is AT 9 AM IN THE QUAD. Let this sink in because it is not trivial. Maybe you can do it on the first few weeks of the semester. But near the second half? That's a struggle my friend. Aside from that, Lars lectures in a fast–paced, ultra–monotonic way that puts you to sleep faster than relaxing lo–fi bedtime sounds will. And there are no lecture notes but the psets rely heavily on things said in lecture, so be prepared to write down everything mentioned in lecture in full detail. Aside from that, this class is basically the only one in the university that teaches fluids at all, and it does give an excellent, rigorous treatment of it as long as you are able to withstand the early morning and the dull lecturing. I had an excellent time in the course and am happy to recommend it to anyone who wants to learn fluids really well and is a morning person.
