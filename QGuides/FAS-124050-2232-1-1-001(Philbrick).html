This course often had time–consuming readings that could take hours to complete if done thoroughly. It is possible to get them done sooner though, if you read efficiently. When you find your groove, the class is not terrible, and provided a nice break in my schedule.
If you have space in your schedule, take this class (especially if you have interest in Ancient Rome)! It's very comprehensive and the workload isn't too much!
This class is entirely dependent on the teacher, although it's a great survey of Ancient Rome.
I took this class as a STEM concentrator, and I really enjoyed it. If you work hard on the readings and essays, you can do well! The course content is engaging if you have any interest in Roman history.
This class was interesting, and, at the end of the day, I am glad that I now have a basic understanding of Roman history, as a Classics concentrator. However, I felt that the course could be learned solely from reading the textbook. The lecture generally covered content from the textbook readings, meaning that one was, at least partially, redundant. The textbook was quite comprehensive, but there is always room for an actual professor to engage beyond the source material, and I'm not sure that I felt that that was done. I also think that the quizzes, especially at the start, were quite punitive and difficult, although the course staff adapted well. Dr. Philbrick is lovely and quite understanding, and the assignments that she created were interesting and engaging, beyond the weekly reading quizzes. Connor is also a wonderful TF and did a great job at supporting students and creating discussion. Nevertheless, the actual format of the class is not super conducive to either learning or engagement.
This was one of my favorite courses at Harvard. It provides an excellent survey of all of Roman history, including the culture and societal structures. It was the first humanities class I've taken at Harvard, and I greatly appreciated not just the typical study of history in terms of battles and conquests, but the analysis of architecture and societal structure as well. Highly recommend to anyone interested in learning more about the ancient world (I took this as an elective pass/fail –– makes it much less stressful and more fun!).
It's a really comprehensive course on Roman history.
This course is a pleasant introduction to the Roman world and I would advise that even if you have a slight interest in it to take this class. You really need to keep up with the textbook on a weekly basis and make sure to take lots of notes in lectures, especially about images of locations or items because those are heavily assessed in the midterm and final. There are two long–term projects that should be an easy way to engage with course material and earn some points for the class.*
Great class. 100% recommend. A lot of reading, but in my opinion, it’s worth it
It’s a required course, but if you’re looking for something interesting, I would still take it. It was a little bland at times, but that doesn’t mean it was a bad course or the teachers were bad. In terms of required courses, this isn’t too bad at all.
This is a very interesting and engaging introduction to the Roman World. Be warned – there is a fair amount of reading that you will be quizzed on, so you have to do the readings every week!
If you are interested in the course topic, then take this course. It was interesting, and as long as you are ok with a few weekly quizzes with more obscure questions you will enjoy the course overall.
For classics majors, especially those with a preference for ancient Rome like myself, you really can't go wrong with this course. It's an extremely solid overview with some creative assignments that will encourage you to branch out and think about the field in new ways. The fact that the course is open to non–concentrators makes the dynamic of the course interesting, and there's definitely less of a sense of community than other concentration tutorials, but the content and teaching are great and there's truly not much to complain about at all.
**Take it if you're looking for a chill fourth class in the spring. If you're just curious about Rome—as in, not a concentrator in the classics—you'll come away knowing a lot about trends in Roman history, and this could definitely be helpful if you're taking more history classes. It's a great class!**
