What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
By all means take it. The material is both sophisticated but also accessible, and most of all manages to balance historical detail with the broad scope necessary to understand the dynamics of economic history. Mike is also amazing and very knowledgeable. The class runs smoothly and is always engaging.
Take this class! This felt like the culmination of the discussions I've been having in all of my social science classes, with an even more critical edge. The interdisciplinary nature of the work makes it incredibly illuminating and satisfying.
Dr.O'Sullivan is a gem, as is this class! One of the best–run seminars I've taken at Harvard, and one of the best instructors I've had. If you have any interest in comparative global history or economic history and are willing to do the readings (it's worth it), 100% take this class
This course was a great look at economic history, and dealt lots with historiography, which I found really interesting — thinking about how historians have interpreted economic changes through history. Dr. O'Sullivan is also an excellent instructor; he brings passion, enthusiasm, and a really incredible dedication to helping students develop their ideas and writing capacities. I'd recommend this course to anyone interested in economics, history, or both!
As a graduating senior, I can genuinely say that this course and this professor (Mike O'Sullivan) are truly unique within all Harvard classes. This will be a particularly good course for you if you are interested in the intersections of: economics, history, development, growth, fragmentation, state formation, state capacity, imperialism, neo–colonialism, hegemonic powers. I think it gave me a much more thorough understanding of how we got to where we are today, and how we can potentially change the arc of history through understanding economic growth and how it lead to global inequalities.
If you like economic history and reading dense materials on the field, you will like this course. If you don't like reading, the course may be challenging.
