What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Enjoyable, interesting year–long course that covers all things drugs and drug use disorder. If you need or want to take five courses, this is a great way to get in an extra 4 credits over the year. All major drug categories were covered and basic neuro mechanisms are introduced at the start of the class to ensure students understand later content. Each week, you will read a paper pertaining to the drug covered and respond to it in a discussion post format. The midterm and final project challenge you to create a proposal investigating or treating a drug use disorder and both are great opportunities to explore ideas related to a thesis or outside project that interests you. Overall, this course was a great addition to my course load and, because of the late start time, is unlikely to conflict with other classes that interest you.
This class is very interesting. Dr. Jordan is so sweet and thoughtful and has a genuine interest in her students. It is generally based on research studies, and that is where most of the discussion comes from, but this is a controversial and provoking topic, and the students in my class were very respectful of that. I learned so much about the way drugs work in our brains, the history behind the drugs, and the tools science has to understand them in laboratories. It is very manageable with other classes, and you have the opportunity to dive deeper into what you are interested in through the midterm and final!
This course is phenomenal. Chloe likely won't be teaching it in future years, but if she does continue then you're incredibly lucky. She does such a wonderful job facilitating conversation and interest in the subject matter. The paper readings are very manageable – it'd be useful to come into this class knowing at least how to read a paper, but if you aren't there yet then you'll learn in a very low–stakes setting. This course has been one of the best, if not the best, courses I have taken at Harvard because it effectively taught me how to critically engage with science while simultaneously distilling a lot of knowledge on different drugs of abuse.
One of the best courses I have taken at Harvard! Dr. Jordan is an AMAZING professor! She is compassionate, easy to understand, and truly does her best to make the class accessible to everyone. She always asks for feedback and actually applies it. First semester was great, but after making modifications, second semester was awesome!
Take it! Especially if you are interested in learning neuroscience through a topic with strong public health relevance. This is my favorite neuroscience course thus far, and I truly cannot stop raving about how wonderful the experience was.
This course will have a different teacher for you guys than when I took it, but assuming the course stays more or less the same, I would say just take it!! The subject matter is so so interesting because you get to learn about how different drugs from nicotine to ayahuasca affect the brain. The workload is so light with just one paper and a few discussion board questions required weekly. Midterm and final paper assignments are very manageable. Definitely lives up to the "half class" per semester idea. Overall, it's a low stress way to fulfill an upper level neuro requirement and I can't think of a reason not to take it!
