A great class to take – maybe not if you want to focus on one individual project, but you'll work on something different each week, or every two weeks. Building from light meters and viewfinders to still photography, the Bolex, then video. The highlight for me was the visiting filmmakers, we had amazing visits from alumni and filmmakers who came to show their work and discuss with us
TAKE THIS CLASS
if you're interested in learning anything about filmmaking, you will come away from this class with so many skills — how to use film & digital cameras, how to compose and light an image (with studio or natural lighting), how to build a MODEL, how to edit, how to color correct, etc. etc.
but more than that, you will learn so much about yourself and your practice. Joana is brilliant at giving individualized advice and taking students seriously for their work in the most productive ways. I've always wanted to be a filmmaker, but she was the first professor I have had that took me seriously for that — both challenging me in all of the right ways and also inspiring me to do that. In this sense, this course is inspiring. AND it gives you time to just create so much material!!! and what more could you ask for. take it!
Take this class!! Joana Pimenta is a wonderful instructor and all the assignments will push you to be creative.
It is a great class, a good balance between theoretical discussions and practical work.
This is really an amazing class. The teaching staff is brilliant and really help you to hone your own specific style as a filmmaker. This is one of my favorite AFVS classes so far.
If you have the opportunity to take this class, do it. Ask questions, pay attention to feedback, and challenge yourself to be better. Everyone I took this class with started as an amateur and all were able to show off and uncover their visions and talents. Put in the work and you won't regret it.
The assignments and subjects covered are diverse and open ended enough that you’d find your thing. Photo sequences, 35mm film, handheld camera etc. Although it is a class focused on cinematography, you end up learning about and discussing other areas like directing or sound as you’re working on your projects individually. It doesn’t lean heavily to one type of filmmaking, and you’ll likely see works from very different backgrounds. The instructor’s bias is quite obvious though, but you’ll also learn to just accept her as a person and filter out that bias from feedbacks you receive as well.
