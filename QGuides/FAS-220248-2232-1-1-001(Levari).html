What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class! Tanya is wonderful—she provides great feedback and knows how to foster meaningful discussion. The policy discussions and debates every other week are a really exciting way to apply the material in a new/hands–on way.
Absolutely loved this class! It was exciting to see an education class offered in the Psych department, and the class did not disappoint. You get to learn all about the science behind educational practices –– what really works, and what doesn't? It was refreshing to be in a class full of students interested in education, with a few having actually taught before, or wanting to go into teaching, because I feel like that can be pretty rare at Harvard. Tanya is a great professor –– super approachable, cares a lot about her students, and gives great feedback.
This is the first course I have seen during my time at Harvard that has combined psychology and education, which are my two primary fields of study. So, if you have any interest in the intersection between these two fields, TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Levari is wonderful, and her course thoughtfully combines research which has theoretical implications and research which has practical applications –– a balance I rarely find in my classes here. Through her course, I have learned so much about myself as a student and as a future educator.
If you are interested to be a teacher or working in education–related jobs, this course will be helpful. It is also very useful if you want to pursue research–related work or a Ph.D. degree because you get to read and debate about a lot of research papers.
