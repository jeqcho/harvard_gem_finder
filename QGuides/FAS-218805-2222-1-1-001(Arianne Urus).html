thoroughly enjoyed this class. The readings were interesting, prof. urus was great, and I learned a lot. really enjoyed learning more about the idea of “ways of knowing” and how that applies to our modern conception of science and religion. super fascinating content.
Definitely take this class if you are interested in the subject material even a little bit. It will change the way you look at the world and is also a really valuable course to take if you don't have prior experience in history and literature.
This class offers an interesting window into studying the witch hunt in early modern Europe and in the Americas. There is also a lot of interesting information on colonization and enslavement. The workload of this course does wildly fluctuate from week to week; sometimes you're reading a few short articles and sometimes you have a 400 page book.
Please take it. It was interesting and I had great conversations within it.
Whatever you think you know about witchcraft and magic will absolutely be challenged by this course, and you will be better for it. There are so many interesting perspectives and ideas to read, but be careful about what you take for granted––there is always some hidden motivation or inconsistency that you should be cognizant of. This class is also by no means a breeze. It will challenge you as a writer, reader, and thinker, since an important portion of your grade is also participation, so falling behind on the readings is not an option. There are new and unfamiliar ideas that you may come across, but enter this class with an open mind and resilient attitude, and you will succeed. Professor Urus is someone very understanding and knowledgeable, and I encourage making time to meet with her to attain your maximum potential.
It's simply amazing. There was not one day that I went into this class and did not enjoy it.
