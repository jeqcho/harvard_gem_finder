What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is by far the best course I have taken in my life. If you are genuinely interested in the content, the hours you have to put in don't matter because you learn so much. Don't take this course if you can't put maximum effort into it. Professor Onuf and Gordon–Reed are incredible legal scholars, and it is worth every second of work to be able to engage in discussions with them. This course is not to be missed.
I learned a lot about British influence on American constitutional law. This is an Atlantic course and a course focused on elites, so keep that in mind. It is a great course for what it is. But I would recommend it for people interested in how politicians and elites created constitutions and law. Also a lot of discussion on present politics, which could be interesting to some but less appealing to others.
If you want to read a 200–400 page book a week and then have the class have a discussion about it that you might not understand go ahead.
Like most history courses, the reading list is quite dense, but you can get a ton out of them and the seminars. Overall, it's a great course that will push you to think about "the big picture."
