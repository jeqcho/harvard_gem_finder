What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Translating World Literature is one of my favorite classes at Harvard. Luke is a wonderful professor and I always really looked forward to coming to class. The readings and discussions we had were super interesting and I got so much out of them. The assignments were very creative, fun, and challenging and I thoroughly enjoyed getting to write my own translations of poetry and literature that appealed to me. If you know another language, I highly recommend taking this class. Your view of language and translation will definitely change for the better!
This class is a great introduction to various famous texts in a number of different foreign and ancient languages as well as learning about translation as an art form! Highly recommend, especially for any Hist & Lit concentrators trying to complete their language requirement with very limited knowledge of other languages.
What a wonderful class! I loved reading texts from lots of different literary traditions (Proust, Chekhov's plays, Dante's Inferno, Chinese and Japanese poetry, etc.) and interesting essays about translation. This was just such an enjoyable class overall, and I felt I learned a lot without busywork or pain.
It might seem daunting, but it's one of the most interesting language courses at Harvard
You are going to have so much fun learning and studying classics in different languages! Professor Leafgren is a really encouraging professor that creates a classroom environment that is super welcoming to everyone! You don't need to have any translating experience to be a part of class. The assignments are tailored to your knowledge and personal interests so they're pretty interesting to do. You won't regret it at all!!! I really loved the course.
This is a great course both to survey translation theory and to practice translation in a second language – well worth your time!
