What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
* You should take this class if you are taking some other time–consuming classes, but want another class that is quantitative and is maybe something you've never looked at before mathematically. If you've never done any structured research, it can be a little difficult –– you don't know exactly what kind of study is sufficient for the project, but from what the TF's were saying it seems like there is some lenience there (hopefully). The research project is like 80 percent of your grade so don't procrastinate (the check–ins are helpful for that reason). Also don't even think about doing the project by yourself lol, the days before reading period were a grind even with two project partners.
Super easy research seminar. I study math, but a significant portion of the class did not. This was kind of surprising since the class is supposed to be a graduate math class. Because most of the class did not have a pure mathematics background, very little time was spent on the mathematics of evolutionary dynamics. Rather, it was mostly powerpoint slides of paper results.
Take this course if you're interested in mathematical biology and are looking for a low stakes course to do more in–depth research in a topic of your choosing. It's quite a chill class with low workload and interesting lectures, so would highly recommend!
This class is relatively low stress and give lots of freedom to students in developing their own model and work on a project individually or with group members.
This is a super chill class that does not have weekly homework or any exams. The only graded parts are a final project proposal due in April and a final project due in May. It is a graduate math class that is also suitable for being a fifth class. You may learn some coding skills and get to know some interesting people in grad schools like the business school and the med school in the final project.

This is not a class without its downsides. I don't think I learned that much from the class nor would I retain much, but it was fun hearing some speakers talking about what makes them passionate and Nowak is an important figure in mathematical biology. If you want to take a class to learn something deeply and have a strong class community, this might not be for you; but if you want an enjoyable survey class with low stress and is a grad math class, this is a great class to take.

Overall I enjoyed the class and am grateful for meeting my final project teammates that I may otherwise have not met, though I do have ambivalent feelings about this class and would not recommend it to everyone.
