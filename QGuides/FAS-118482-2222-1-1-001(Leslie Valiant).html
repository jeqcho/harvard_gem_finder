What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I took the course to get out of taking cs124 and it was such a great alternative (takes so much less time and still learn about proof writing and combo topics). The psets are not bad at all but bc it's a small class there were only two OH sessions available per week.
If you're interested in graph theory, it's not a bad class to take, but know that the lectures are *super* dry. Really lean into section and the TFs for support, since Prof Valiant doesn't really engage with students.
I'm really glad I took this instead of CS124.
This course is not for everyone. But if it is for you, it is extremely rewarding. You have to go to lecture. If you think that you are the type of student who might end up not attending lecture, don't take the course. The PSets are hard and take a while; they are always 5 problems, many of which are proof–based. (If you haven't done proofs before, this is not the class to learn how to do them.) The professor is very good, in a weird way. If you knew nothing about math and just listened to him, you would think the class is boring and slow. But really, lectures are fantastic if you are sufficiently engaged. His pace is perfect; not too slow, but not so fast. Such that you have enough time to take notes and think about the material to ask questions. And the topics are interesting, if you like math even just a little. Everyone I know liked the class; it's more the details around it that we didn't like. Of course, waking up early (it was at 9 am this year) is hard. And there were not enough TFs to help with the PSets, given how hard they were. Also, it is definitely not Applied, so I don't know why it is categorized as an Applied Math class. The topics are theoretical, and the fact that we are proving stuff confirms that.
For me, this was an extremely difficult class. Even though I was very interested in the material, I could not really make much sense of it, which was frustrating given that I diligently attended every lecture and office hours. I believe there was a disconnect because what I actually understood seems to be far less than what the professor expected me to know already, which meant that I was usually quite confused during lecture. Nevertheless, I enjoyed the challenge, and always derived a rewarding feeling of satisfaction from finishing each pset. I was able to get through the content by relying heavily on office hours.
