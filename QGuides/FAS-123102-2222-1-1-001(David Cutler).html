It's a lot of work in the beginning to brainstorm and craft your proposal. Waiting until the last minute makes things chaotic.
Take if you need a class that isn't too much work in order to balance out a rough schedule.
I think that this course is a really worthwhile space to potentially research and write a piece of work you can be super super proud of. I think that it's really cool that the finished product of this class is a real world policy recommendation based ostensibly off of the best and most recent research on a subject area. I wish that I had been better able to take advantage of this class's framework for putting together a body of work like that.
Do NOT procrastinate <3. Write something you are already passionate and knowledgeable about or use this as an opportunity to learn about something new. I did the latter, and it was a really great experience!
I loved this class. It took a lot of work, but I was really proud of my final paper and I really cared about my topic. Definitely choose a topic that is important to you or you might lose steam over the course of the semester. By the end, I probably had read over 100 sources about my topic so that is hard to do if you apathetic towards your topic. Zotero is your best friend.
You should only take this class if you need the GHHP research requirement. If you do, then this class is not too bad. It's taught in sections and my section was great. Just be prepared to write a 20–25 page paper, and be prepared to have some weeks with 10+ hours of work scattered throughout the semester.
try to take this GHHP secondary requirement earlier if you can (not your senior spring), writing the paper felt like pulling out teeth.
This course is required for the GHHP secondary, but a good TF can make it pretty enjoyable (I was in Jason's section, and he was awesome). You'll learn about research methods, which you may or may not be familiar with, depending on your academic discipline. Regardless, it's good stuff to know, even if it isn't the most exciting topic. The course culminates in a 20–25 (max 30) page paper, which the course prepares students well for through a series of lead–up assignments, including a pretty substantial literature review assignment that gets quite a few pages done before finals period.
Do not take this class if you can avoid it.
Likely if you are reading this it's because you have to take this course. I would only recommend not taking this course if you are self–motivated and will not wait until the last minute to write your term paper if you do a 91r (unlikely if you are a second–semester senior). That said this course can be a drag but choose an interesting topic and try to get a friend in your section.
This course is fine to fill the GHHP research requirement, and is interesting if you enjoy your topic. Pick a topic that interests you, and starting gathering literature as early as possible!
For any students with a secondary in GHHP, this is a relatively painless way to fulfill the research requirement. It's a very easy class if you keep up with the assignments. Make sure to pick a topic for your policy proposal that you really love and want to work on for the whole semester since everything you do revolves around that one topic. The sections are relatively boring, especially at the start of the semester, since they teach very basic skills like writing and citing, but they get better, and the peer feedback component is relatively enjoyable and helpful.
It's a good class. Everything is structured so that you slowly chip away at what eventually will be your final paper. Although I do imagine your experience depends on your TF.
This class is very challenging –– in a good way. it allows you to really dive deep into a topic you are passionate about and to approach it in a critical way. You can be creative –– and you also get to think and consider all of the moving parts of a problem. You will be able to produce a paper and policy proposals that you would be proud of. I would also say that this course is NOT accommodating. It runs on a 'strict' schedule. So please take this class If you know you can hit the deadlines. (I think they're flexible with senior theses). They only seem to care about a timeline, not student wellbeing.
