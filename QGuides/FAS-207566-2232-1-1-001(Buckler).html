Take this class to make reading fun again!!! Professor Buckler cares about every single student so much and that is so rare at this school. As a STEM concentrator, I would usually hesitate to take a class like this because I'm not great at writing essays, but this class is very accessible even if you don't have a lot of background reading/writing about literature. The lectures are engaging and help make the readings interesting. The syllabus has a lot of variety, so something for everyone.
Take this class if you want to do more English but maybe feel scared about the discipline
This is a great introduction to Russian/Slavic literature and art that you can take regardless of prior experience with literature classes. The required reading is not strenuous, and the focus on assignments and projects takes an edge off while reading. The course content is very varied and all are engaging in different ways, and the lecturer is fantastic.
I would recommend this class to anyone who is interested in Russian literature or even Russian culture and history. It is not a difficult class in terms of workload, but you will gain a lot of knowledge about the body of Russian literature from that time period and also an appreciation for the works. Also, if you are learning Russian or already know Russian, there were special optional Russian translation sections that were very fun and can be a great additional learning tool.
I loved this class! Professor Buckler is super knowledgeable and her passion for the material is evident in her lectures and careful course planning. I was so glad I could learn from Professor Buckler's expertise in the field and I really appreciated such a broad exposure to Russian literature. I took the War & Peace course she offered in Fall 2022 and loved that course as well. This class is easier in the sense that you aren't reading one book the whole semester and if you fall behind one week it's not too much of an issue because next week you're likely reading something new. I wasn't a huge fan of the push for more technology/media based assignments as some of them were in a group work format and that didn't work out well for most people. I think the traditional discussion post assignments would have been better. I really enjoyed the class and it was one of my favorites of the semester. I'm looking forward to taking more courses in the Slavic Department in the future.
Great class with a manageable workload. Professor Buckler is wonderful
This is an amazing class! Professor Buckler is great at getting students to interact with the material and almost all the material you will read/watch is interesting.
If you have any interest in Slavic literature/culture or even just reading/literature in general, this is a great course for you. You'll read some masterpieces and also spend time with ballet and opera. It's a great mix of classics from Russian culture. The reading load per week is very manageable, and you'll find it interesting. There are no essays, unless that's what you choose to do as your final project. There are only a mix of weekly discussion posts and other more creative assignments like video annotations, scrollytelling, and video essays. It's a good mix of different media based assignments that gives you the chance to be creative and approach the material in a different way. Prof. Buckler and Joanna are excellent, and if you have any knowledge of Russian, Prof. Buckler also does an optional Russian section where you check out the original Russian versions of the week's reading, which I loved. I highly recommend overall.
I would tell them that the readings are worth doing, but that the teaching team could work harder at making this class more engaging.
If you're looking for a broad and well–done introduction to Russian literature (and there is so much there) this class is great! Prof Buckler is a great professor and a wonderful human, and the class is very well done.

This course is designed in such a way that students may focus on the rich course materials, particularly the readings and the visual media including Russian ballets as well as films. Professor Buckler takes time to learn every student's name which has become rare in other courses and demonstrates how much she cares about her students. Professor Buckler is also one of the most compassionate professors I have met; she creates an environment of discussion that is inclusive of all students, even those who do not normally voice their opinions. I learned about multiple Russian authors that I will continue to read on my own. I am grateful to have taken this course as an elective as it was very enjoyable.
This course is such an incredible look into a specific period of Russian culture and the context behind it – it's both enjoyable and deeply rewarding without being too much of a challenge. The teaching and lectures are fantastic, and the readings are genuinely fascinating; I actually looked forward to doing the readings every week. This is a great course for everyone, whether you're a concentrator or have never taken a humanities course before. *
This class allows you to explore a really cool variety of Russian art and literature, and it does so in a manageable way. Some of the assignments and readings are more tedious than others but I never felt that it was overly taxing. Professor Buckler does a great job providing context and insights about the material, and I feel like I really learned a lot about an area of the world/literature I knew very little about.
If you are interested in Russian literature, TAKE THIS CLASS! Prof. Julie Buckler is amazing –– the works she has chosen provide a very broad (but also deep enough to encourage further interest) view of the art of many of the Russian Greats –– and she does the a great job providing the context needed in order to read them most efficiently. The assignments are light and allow everyone to discuss the respective work through whatever lens they find most suitable. There are weeks when the reading is not too small of an amount, but it is absolutely worth it. The class is very fulfilling: it feels like a deep breath of fresh air compared to other Harvard classes. One of my favorites this semester.

Professor Buckler is clearly very passionate and knowledgeable about what she teaches and every lecture was interesting to attend. The class definitely has some work (like the readings and discussion posts each week), but it is very manageable so you don't have to be stressed out by this class. I learned a lot about Russian history, literature, and culture, and the professor does a great job at discussing so many different aspects of this diverse empire and not just assigning the most conventional or famous authors.
