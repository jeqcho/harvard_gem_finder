Too hard :(
I have some mixed feelings about this class. On the one hand, Professor Gordon is truly such a great teacher. His lectures are really well organized (he has a handout every lecture with an outline of the lecture which helps you follow along) and really clear (no small feat given the difficulty of the readings). You come away from each lecture with a clear idea of what you are supposed to have learned, and you come away from the class with a clear understanding of the arc of Hegel and Marx's work and the major themes and ideas that you should take away from the class. The essays are extremely manageable, and you get good feedback on them. Section discussions can be enlightening and help you understand major questions and ideas better. If you take this class you will become a much better reader, potentially a better writer, and you will have a much much better understanding of Hegel and Marx's ideas and how they fit into a particular kind of thought.

That being said, I do want to warn about a few things. For one, the readings are hard. It may take you like five minutes to read a single page of Hegel at times (if not longer). At times you might feel too stupid for this class. You will probably be very confused at times. But going through this is precisely the process that will make you learn a lot in this class. If you don't want to put in the time for that, though, this class is probably not for you. I will also say that the readings are almost exactly ordered to go from the hardest reading to the easiest reading, so do keep that in mind if the first few weeks are hard. (The Science of Logic is practically unintelligible, Phenomenology of Spirit is not much better, but after that the readings get easier and you get better at them/ get a feel for it. And Marx is not too hard to read, though you still have read attentively.)

The other thing is the people in this class. The class was mostly white people and mostly white men. I don't know if that was just a quirk of this year, but my section was about 2/3 white men ––– there were only a few women/ POC. There were also some people in the class who had a lot of experience reading theory/ philosophy, and had already read these works in particular, which was kind of intimidating at first in section (some of them made smart, insightful comments and some of them just tried to show off). However, I would say that for the most part section discussions were really interesting, fulfilling, and accessible (Hegel is hard and so he is a great equalizer).

So, if you feel intimidated in the first few weeks of class that is totally valid, but chances are you can handle this class! Personally I am really glad that I stuck with the course. But do be aware that at times the vibes might be bad.
I highly recommend this class to anyone interested in learning about Hegel and Marx. In my opinion, no serious harvard student can graduate without taking a class on marxism: this is that class. Professor Gordon has encyclopedic knowledge of the material and is a great lecturer. He also has great taste in music and great fashion sense (don't be late to class like I was to get the full experience!). Though I will say I cannot agree wholeheartedly with his reading of Heidegger, and I wish we would spend more time on how marxism evolved (Adorno, Althusser, etc.).
This course is an excellent opportunity to begin or continue reading Hegel and Marx. Hegel especially is a difficult fellow and his writing can be obscure and difficult to follow. But from the dialectical mist Hegel concocts, Professor Gordon helps guide the class through the difficulties in the most exciting way possible. Even the hardest–to–follow texts become a source of joy and pleasure and every lecture an event you look forward to. A wonderful class!
