What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class was fun and engaging. The seminar is interactive, as you learn about math and physics through puzzles. The weekly puzzles are fun and not a lot of work.
This class is very interesting, and if you are interested in math and physics you really could not choose a better course. It was fun, the professor is a great orator, you get to solve puzzles in class, and you learn a lot of really cool concepts. Before this course, I hadn't heard a lot about what is going on in modern physics or anything about the history of physics really. Not only did we learn about that, but the delivery is so engaging that even for topics that seem way too advanced to comprehend you can learn a little something.
Take this class! It's fascinating, very little work, and a lot of fun!
This is an excellent seminar for those interested in math and physics! Professor Vafa is a great lecturer, and many topics are introduced in this class that students can then further explore on their own time if they are interested.
You should only take this class if you have a very strong background and interest in both math and physics. I thought I did, but often found myself lost in lecture and not getting the most out of it. It is an easy class for sure, Prof. Vafa brings snacks for everyone and the PSets are short and graded on completion. Still, I don't think it is worth using a spot up on your schedule unless you can get the most out of the content, which means having a lot of experience in math and physics.
This is an amazing class to get an informal glimpse into modern physics. Professor Vafa is an amazing lecturer, and the puzzles are fun and approachable. Although there are weekly psets, the workload is very light. Do not expect a lot of formalism, however; this is not a careful treatment of the material, which is good since fully understanding most of it would likely require a graduate degree.
Awesome class, great lectures. Really stimulating content, but not the best class for the community/connection aspect.
Great class to take if you want an overview of the broad concepts of theoretical physics. Taking this class changed my view of the relationship between math and physics drastically, and made me realise just how fundamental both are to each other. The lecturer is really really really smart, and he does a good job explaining very difficult and esoteric math and physics concepts without the advanced math, which is a pretty difficult thing to do in my opinion. I was left with a bad feeling after every class, simply because a lot of the concepts and examples shared in class was done so in a sort of "hand wavy" manner without a lot of the mathematical and physical justification. This is understandable, because a lot of the concepts really are quite advanced and require a lot of background to fully justify and understand, but it left me wanting to learn more nonetheless, which in my opinion is pretty much the aim of the class. Take this class not if you want to fully understand the material (because you won't), but take this class if you want to take a sneak peek into a lot of areas of theoretical physics and math, and to see what interests you.
