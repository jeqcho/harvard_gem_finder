It counts for 2 semesters of organic chemistry and one semester of biochem combined into only 2 semesters. CHEM 17 is where most of the heavy work is done (e.g. learning the basics of aldol, retroaldol, Claisen condensations, nucleophilicity and electrophilicity, iminium condensation, hemiacetal/ketal formation, orbitals). CHEM 27 just takes that knowledge and applies it to biology. Learning the amino acids isn't bad at all. The mechanisms are also simple to remember after enough practice.

It will help understand why and how reactions happen, as well as the requirements for E1cb, decarboxylation, and more. The slides at the end of lectures with the diagrams on enzymes, what Km is, what the properties of inhibitors are, and what happens in the electron transport chain of metabolism, for instance, will show up on sets and exams.
