Comments
This class was great, and exactly what it was described to be on the previous course evaluations / canvas / my.harvard. However, my general advice for expos would be to really spend a good amount of time researching the class. Just because the class has a cool name or you like the topic doesn't mean you will enjoy the class. Check how many papers there are due (and what length), is there a final project, do you find the readings interesting, does the class take a philosophical or practical approach to whatever topic it concerns etc.
Depending on the section, expos can actually be enjoyable. However, depending on where you started in terms of writing ability, the degrees to which this course will actually help you can vary greatly.
This class provides a refreshing take on Orwell and is well worth taking. It is provides a lot of new insight into 1984 and shows a lot more connections to the real world than you would think. A very fair workload that is enjoyable to do.
Since Expos is a tough class, choosing an interesting topic to explore and discuss is very helpful. Therefore, I would highly recommend this course.
This is a great course and I'm really happy that I chose to take it. No previous knowledge or experience with 1984 is needed, and even if you have read the book before you will find it super interesting. The book is an absolute masterpiece, the discussions in class are always great, the professor is super nice and gives clear and helpful feedback. Assignments are clear but also very open, which means that you get to focus on and write about something that you personally find particularly interesting. I was worried that the class would be extremely difficult and complicated, but I had a really good experience. All expos classes are challenging but it becomes easier to handle when you find a course you enjoy and especially when the professor provides helpful advice and guidance throughout each step of the course.
I would tell them that they should only take the course if they are prepared to be scared about the reality of our world!
I definitely recommend the 1984 expos. The topics covered are very interesting, and if you have any interest in dystopian literature, politics, and/or their intersection, taking this class will likely make you forget that expos is a requirement and rather feel as if you are taking an elective. The workload is manageable, and Dr. Cole makes a big effort to make sure he gives all of his students individual attention.
Take this class for the chill workload, it really helped me get through my freshman spring with a tougher course load. Prof. Cole is a very smart scholar in political science and I think he has some quite interesting insight to offer about Orwell and political science in general. A really interesting class overall, but I wish there were more chances for feedback on your writing in class – it doesn't feel like you are there to learn, but rather get a grade and get over with the Expos requirement. Expos is a pain, but I think this section is the most bearable of them all. I really had fun with the topic. Also, be aware that this is a section more on the literary side. There is a chance that the classmates will be lovers of humanities: probably proficient at writing and already really good at analysis, having crazy ideas while you blankly stare at the reading asking yourself if there is a chance you're illiterate. Don't get discouraged, but take this into account when choosing which section would be your best choice.
