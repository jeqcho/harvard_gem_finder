Mansfield's final course was just as thought provoking and interesting as expected. The visiting lecturers each had their own style but all contributed to the central themes of the course.
I felt lucky to be in attendance at the lectures and section. There are only three papers required for this course, so plenty of time to interact with the material. Great choice for the Gov theory subfield
The four professors really made this course unique. Each professor gave a different perspective on the course materials they also were extremely well communicating and really clear in their ideas. I really enjoyed Professor Muirhead’s enthusiasm. The section discussions were very meaningful. Nathan was an incredible section leader he allowed for us to share our ideas, he was also extremely helpful in putting my ideas into a coherent argument. Nathan was really easy to reach out. I met with him for office hours and he really helped me better understand course materials. Nathan was very prepared to give in–depth advice. One of the best TF’s I have had.
An amazing class, I hope they can even approximate it after Mansfield is gone.
This class was good, specifically the section and the lectures with Professor Mansfield (Harvard is losing one of its best professors with his retirement). However, there were some serious issues with the class, despite the pros. There were three guest professors in addition to Professor Mansfield, all of them with their very distinct styles of lecturing (which is not necessarily bad) and grading requirements. With Professor Mansfield and the TF, that means there were five different graders, and you had no idea who would be grading your essay. Their feedback often focused on very different things and they all had different expectations and guidelines. This resulted in a very disjointed, confusing process for grades and trying to write essays to appeal to different people was very frustrating. Despite this, I'm still glad I took the class because Professor Mansfield is a great lecturer (although, again, because there were three random professors, he wasn't able to lecture as much as he would have otherwise) and section tried to make up for the class's faults.
Great course, would recommend it.
If this class ever runs again, TAKE IT.
Unfortunately Professor Mansfield is leaving but the course curriculum is exceptional regardless.
Great course. Unfortunately, Professor Mansfield is retiring and the visiting professors likely won't be back. Three papers and attendance make up the grade so very very manageable. Highly recommend.
