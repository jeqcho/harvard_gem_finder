What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Definitely stay on top of the readings each week. Even if you can't get around to reading the entire thing, definitely look at each one before lecture. Professor Cratty never assigns readings that are pointless so they all came in handy for the assignments and exams. In addition, they help your understanding of the lectures.
This is a really interesting class that covers a ton of material! The lectures are fascinating and the assignments, while sometimes more on the creative side, are fun.
This course is what an older brand of Harvard professor might have called a "meat and potatoes" course: a survey that covers much ground on a traditional field, complete with a sit–down exam. (Contrast this with, say, a course on a more narrow field in history.) There are not many such courses around, and they are difficult to teach: an instructor must have a great deal of knowledge ready, even given the aid of lecture notes. Dr. Cratty was an excellent lecturer, covering important moments in a span of British history with a lot of them. The course's assignment structure was interestingly varied and fair, though rigorous. Readings were selected well and balanced nicely between "history" reading and primary source or even literary reading.
The Anglophile's dream. A grand tour through the most exciting time in British history. Prepare for adventure. Expect the unexpected.
It is a great introductory course for people interested in studying history. The subject matter was specific enough that somebody with a genuine curiosity in the subject would find all lectures really engaging and would appreciate Professor Cratty's enthusiasm in the subject and willingness to answer questions. The essays/projects of the class were also genuinely fun to complete as they were not simply conventional essays.
I would let future students know that they should definitely take this course if they are interested in this culture and time period. I really would not be able to rave about Professor Cratty enough. I would, however, warn them on the weakness of the course due to the teaching style of the TF and section.
Dr. Cratty is a good lecturer; interesting anecdotes about personalities and religion in early modern Britain. Readings and assignments are reasonable and not extensive. Dr. Cratty is very interested in the theological divisions and debates in early modern Britain, so prepare to spend a lot of time throughout the course looking at Catholic/Anglican teachings, as well as various religious sects (Puritans/Ranters/etc.). Would recommend this course for those looking to knock out the History concentration pre–1750 requirement.
This is a wonderful course that is well–taught by Dr. Cratty. The readings are very reasonable in length and always interesting. Dr. Cratty succeeds in tying the readings together into the lectures and bringing enthusiasm to an under–appreciated subject in the History Department. Little academic preparation is needed for this course. I would highly recommend it to concentrators and non–concentrators, alike.
This a good class if the subject matter interests you. Assignments are very reasonable and course staff are helpful. Anyone can take this course and do well, even without any history background.
