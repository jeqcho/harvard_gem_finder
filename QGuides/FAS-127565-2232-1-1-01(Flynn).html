Disparity in undergrad vs grad participation
The class content is interesting, but don't expect to leave the class feeling like you gained a deep understanding of RNA biology. The professor tells us himself that this is a very broad field that is always changing, so this class gives more of a brief sampling of various topics across the field and does not go in–depth into any particular aspect. There is no expectation to memorize any of the information (there are no exams), and problem sets tend to focus on understanding experimental techniques. This year, most of the students were graduate students, so prepare for the class discussions to be very research–focused. As a whole, this is a worthwhile class without a big time commitment, and you will definitely become more familiar with experimental techniques in RNA biology.
I think this course is great for students interested in RNA biology (specifically). As the materials are research–paper heavy (that is, scientific literature versus textbook excerpts), I would perhaps caution students who are not invested in biological research. Nevertheless, being exposed to such content and having the opportunity to discuss and present the content of the papers helped me to grow academically, and I have developed skills that I otherwise would have not developed in a standard biology course.
Take the class if you're interested in RNA research, Prof. Flynn is super nice and responsive. It's simply an invaluable experience to just chat about random research questions and ideas with him.
Undergrads, be wary of taking this course. The grad students that take this course tend to be incredibly passionate about RNA biology and know so much that it means that they are often the only ones able to engage in discussion. The psets are pretty difficult to do, as there are no written materials for you to reference. Unless you magically remember everything, you have to go back and listen to recordings 5–6 times to try to understand.

Only take this class if you have a GENUINE and (lowkey) extremely niche interest in RNA biology.
Definitely take the course if you are even remotely interested in RNA. Prof. Flynn is a fantastic lecturer and you'll learn a ton.
It is very easy to get nothing out of this class, i.e. to zone out completely, haberdash the psets, and eke out a final project. It's an all–or–nothing class so if you don't want to waste your time, be prepared to have an interest in RNA that can overcome an uncharismatic (though trying–his–best) lecturer.
If you want to learn more about RNA, take this class. Ryan is an extremely passionate lecturer.
