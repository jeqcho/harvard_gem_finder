What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are interested in decarbonization this class will give you a good overview of all of the issues and tools involved in that task. The class will, however, go into more depth then you will be likely to retain, or need to in order to succeed in the context of this course.
Really what you make it. The final paper was a highlight of the course—taking many of the concepts and putting them together in exploring a topic of interest deeper. Lectures were also very informative and had a good balance between reviewing the chapter and covering things that may have been more difficult. Prof. Aziz also did a great job of tailoring the lectures to the class' level of prior understanding with specific material and based on questions that came up on Perusall. Homeworks were fair and did serve to reinforce/apply the lecture & readings content. Overall an excellent class!
This class provides a good overview of the state of energy technology today and looks at both scientific and economic aspects of implementing and changing current practices.
This course is fascinating and very doable as an undergraduate student, but you should be prepared to spend a lot of time on it (especially if you don't have significant background in economics).
Only take this course if you're already really interested in the subject and are sure that you're going to put the work in to get value out of it. Nothing about this class will draw you in, so you have to make sure you're intrinsically motivated. If you are, you can learn a lot.
I LOVED this course. Easily my favorite class at Harvard. Professor Aziz is writing his textbook over the next year which is why it isn't available in 2024. I spent a lot of time reading the textbook because it was so so interesting and applicable to my interest in energy and climate. This class is very STEM–focused and is great at applying physics, thermodynamics, chemistry, engineering, economics, and more to the energy sector. You don't need experience in all of these fields but it will make the course easier. The final project is a great way to explore something that caught your interest. It was my first real research paper and I really enjoyed it. I would 10/10 recommend this class. The p–sets are bi–weekly. You can put in as much time as you want and will get out of it as much as you put in.
