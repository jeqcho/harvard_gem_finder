Awesome course! Shai is so engaging and kind! The TFs are so helpful too! It is a hard course, but if you put the time in you will get a lot out of it. (p.s. I recommend the TF Zehra–– she is amazing, seriously a genius, and her powerpoints are great)
Honestly, I was scared of taking this class and thought it would be excruciating, however it ended up being my favorite class this semester. Don't get me wrong, this class is definitely challenging. There's a lot of readings and it can be dense at times but there are so many resources out there to make the concepts and topics easier to understand. Also, Shai and the TFs are always available and would be happy to clear up any tough topics. The class is super rewarding and you'll with such a great understanding of sociological theory, both from the fathers of sociology and previously ignored theorists, such as POC and women. The second half focused on the forgotten theorists so I really appreciate the amount of time we dedicated to these individuals. Also, the midterm and final paper allow for much freedom, you can write about anything which makes the process so much better. Also, the sections are super engaging which was great. Zehra was an amazing TF and if you are lucky enough to get her you are set. She cares so much about our success and will go above and beyond to help us. Shai is also an amazing prof and cares so much too, he has a good sense of humor which I appreciate. Overall this class was great but make sure you have a lighter workload to make room for this class.

Be prepared to read a lot (AND TO READ EARLY; DO NOT SAVE IT FOR THE NIGHT BEFORE) and to think critically about the material. Attend office hours and participate in both lecture and section. Pick group members who will actually do their part of the group project well and on time (there is a group project component where you will have to read a contemporary sociological work and present on it). Start working on your essays early!
This class is quite a bit of work in terms of a good amount of reading every week and weekly discussions posts and it can be tough to get engaged if theory isn't really your thing which it wasn't much for me. There's also a group project on a book which can be a lot depending on the group you have though the books are generally pretty interesting. The teaching staff is great and always helpful on assignments so everything is pretty manageable.
Be prepared to take care of your mental, when discussing serious issues trigger warnings are not provided which can be extremely damaging at times.
However much reading you're expecting to be assigned in this course, it's unfortunately probably more :( But the course itself is definitely manageable (the secret is that you don't need to read EVERY SINGLE WORD which is harder at least for me to remember) and the workload is more than reasonable. I think you will feel it was intellectually worthwhile by the end––as long as you are somewhat interested in the social determinants of the world's problems, you'll find something of interest in this course I think.
It is a hard class. It is a doable class, but it is hard. I would be prepared to take a lighter load in other classes if possible. The TF's will cold–call, at least in the first few weeks of sections definitely. The feedback I received for the midterm essay was the harshest I have ever received in my life. It was valid, but framed very harshly. Overall, it's not the worst requirement out there at Harvard, but definitely for Sociology. Best of luck
A great course – helped solidify my choice to concentrate in sociology!
I learned so much from SOC97! Even though this is required for soc concentrators, I truly believe that I learned so much from the sophomore tutorial. Shai is an amazing lecturer and he is truly passionate about social theory and deconstructing the sociological canon. I had Zehra as my TF and she was amazing as well. I learned so much within section and even though it was 2 hours long, she always made sure to make it engaging for us and was so encouraging!

Zehra is amazing, such a compassionate section leader and made sections very low–stress. Lectures are cool too, especially incorporating cool videos to connect dense theory to real life.
