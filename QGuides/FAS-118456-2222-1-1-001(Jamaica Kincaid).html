What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course, I think, corrected a path I risked getting on with regards to my writing. I love to write, it's my natural way of processing life, but doing so in a Harvard context makes it easy to turn writing into yet another thing that can impress people, or give you a sense of your worth, or become a "career". Taking this class reminded me that that's not what writing is, and unsurprisingly I now feel much more like myself when writing. In that spirit, perhaps the most valuable thing I learned from Professor Kincaid was to learn to pursue that which is "useless" or of no immediate, apparent benefit in a utilitarian sense –– a 'useless' article about Saturn's rings, books about plants, memoirs of mountaineering expeditions, collections of Artemisia Gentileschi's paintings, and of course conversations with other people... These discoveries make both writing & living much more worth it.
If you're hoping for a structured class where you can workshop something you've already written, this may not be it. But this course is a really rich, wonderful opportunity to listen to and learn from Professor Kincaid and classmates alike. I learned so much from and felt really inspired by my classmates' writing ––– Professor Kincaid invited us to engage with profound, personal topics, and everyone rose to the challenge.
This class offers an amazing opportunity to write and share your writing. Everybody in the workshop will offer you insightful feedback, and Professor Kincaid will be a source of never–ending inspiration to you.
This is an incredible opportunity to sit with Professor Kincaid and some of the coolest people at this university and talk about writing for three hours a week – a must–take class. Maybe one of the best I've ever been in.
