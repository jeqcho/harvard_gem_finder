What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
As with all Gov seminars, it is highly dependent on the TF section lead. Harry Oppenheimer was a great TF for this course, really chill, but also super knowledgeable. Apparently they standardize grading across the entire Gov 97 course, but grading doesn't seem to be very easy. A–, B+ seems to be the normal range of grades. If you get the Technology/Disruption in IR one, the readings are moderately interesting, a few highlight ones, but also some rather drab ones. It is focused on technology, but it isn't really cutting edge, like AI for example. Political science moves a little slower, so take that into account for your expectations of topics. The course is mostly discussion based so make sure to do most of the readings in advance to get value out of the course.
I *really loved this class. I know it is required for gov majors, so I wish you all luck. It is a lot of work, so please keep that in mind when choosing your other classes for this semester.
The course heads tried to make grading and everything else as fair as possible, but the structure of this course does not allow for it to be 100% fair. Differences between deadlines (some sections given extensions), feedback, and the timeliness of grades greatly differed. It is also very difficult to be engaged in a course in which you are not interested in the topic. One section aligned with my interests, and unfortunately, I was not placed in that section. My section was fine, but the topic was boring and made it hard for me to be passionate about my paper. My section peers were all heavily interested in the topic and had prior exposure, which made things further unengaging. I had little background in the topic, making it hard to speak in the section.
Really helps if you pick a topic you have a deep interest in. Makes readings much more exciting, and the discussion during seminar better.
