What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is on an interesting topic and the professor is extremely knowledgeable. The class this year was disorganized and that made assignments confusing. It is also hard to know how you are doing because we receive minimal feedback on the presentations and there is just one final policy memo. All the work is in groups.
It's an interesting seminar about democratic backsliding in Eastern and Central Europe that is focused on finding solutions and strategies to tackle this trend. I liked that the final paper was a group project, so it's not very stressful. I recommend it to people who want to learn more about this region and are interested in ways to strengthen democracy around the world. We also had a few guest speakers who were very insightful!
The course makes you think practically about the problems and situations on the ground. I learned a lot about the limited role of international institutions, how civil society actually plays a larger role than I imagined and how important the organization of the opposition is. Please do not take this class unless you have some background in the region and are willing to think critically on the issues on the ground.
