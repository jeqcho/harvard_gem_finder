This class is full of extremely talented musicians and coaches– the level of playing is quite professional. That said, please try to audition if you're interested. I would recommend forming a chamber group when signing up for the course, so you can spend the semester working with them and performing a piece at the end. Apart from practicing individually and rehearsing as a group, the coachings are only one hour per week, so it is possible to take this as a fifth class.
If you have classical music training and want to make music with friends, take this class! It takes some more time than you expect (rehearsing outside of coaching, practicing by yourself), but it's worth it. It's great to get coaching from excellent musicians and collaborate with friends at Harvard.
Best course at Harvard, hands down. Kathy and the Parker Quartet are always such joys to work with, and I learn SO much every semester I take this class. It's the best thing I've chosen to do at this school and I highly, highly encourage it for any classical musician with an appreciation for chamber music.
The BEST chamber music experience at Harvard, hands down. I have found some of my closest friends at Harvard through this class and making music together. The level of playing and coaching is truly extraordinary, and the Parker Quartet and Kathy are some of the most insightful, yet understanding faculty on this campus. I highly encourage anyone who is even slightly interested in making chamber music at Harvard to take this course. I've taken it every semester that I could while at Harvard and I have no regrets.
This is overall a great course, and especially as a light fifth course if you want more music in your life. My only critique is with the audition and placing process for this class. Unfortunately, if you have to audition for the course (i.e. don’t get a free pass through Harvard/NEC), and don’t have a pre–formed group, whether you get in or not can be pretty arbitrary – be aware that pre–formed groups (of whatever standard) have priority. Additionally, although I understand why it’s difficult for the instructors to form groups in a way that makes everyone happy, there is no guarantee that you will be placed in a group of similar standard (which could make the course somewhat unfulfilling). Apart from this, it’s genuinely an amazing class if you are with a compatible group – I feel that for this kind of chamber music class, it might be more about how much you learn (and of course the instruction is fantastic) than the group you are with.
Definitely take this class if you are a seasoned string musician!
Amazing amazing course, the Parker Quartet and Kathy are amazing mentors!
As a graduating senior, I can comfortably say there are few things at Harvard I will miss as much as this class. It has been one of the greatest joys to make music alongside friends and under the incisive yet always warmly encouraging tutelage of the Parker Quartet. Take advantage of the gift that this class is while you can! It's a privilege to have this structured space to take musical risks and express yourself—and it really brings out the best in everyone involved.
Great opportunity to continue chamber music studies for those who have substantial experience in it! Performances are very rewarding and the Parker Quartet is very supportive and insightful in feedback.
