This course is an excellent alternative to more traditional metabolism classes that require you to memorize pathways and regurgitate them on exams. If you want to learn how to think critically about metabolism and how it relates to human health, you should absolutely take this course. Also, the patient experiences are absolutely amazing and changed my perspective on the connections between basic science and medicine. If you're wondering if you have the necessary prior experience to take this course, you should know that a strong background in cellular biology is essential to understanding the content. Organic chemistry will be helpful but is usually not required. Overall, I'm very glad I took this course and it was a great experience that was not time–consuming but still made me a more effective and knowledgeable scientist and researcher.

Great class in terms of depth and breadth of metabolism––you will learn plenty and be exposed to a variety of pathology–related metabolism research (from that on obesity to cancer to immunomodulation, e.g.). BUT be prepared to be a very active student in and outside of class––be ready to commit 3–6 hours weekly outside of class to truly internalize the material (whether studying the material or working on a case response, which have often required extensive literature searches), to frequently discuss in–class activities every day of class (which will shape 35% of your final grade), and to spend 2–3 weeks learning about a metabolic pathway of your choice for your student presentation. If you do not do these things, the class can easily be a GPA dropper. So, the class is both fascinating and effortful––important to keep in mind as you maximize your learning opportunities as a student, balance this class with others, and strive to protect/maintain a particular GPA.
As Dr. Michel will say, you will get out of this class what you put in. There is a lot of really interesting content in this class and you will learn a lot! That being said, this class can be very overwhelming at times. It will end up taking up more time than you think, so be prepared for that.
This is a course where you get out what you put in. It's difficult to do poorly in this class, but in order to truly understand the material and make the most of it, you will need to spend quite a bit of time outside of class engaging with lecture content, reading the related literature papers etc. on your own. Also there's a very supportive teaching staff!
I would make them aware of the high workload required as early as possible. Overall the teaching staff is coming from a good place and trying to help out, even if I think they have became a bit too overbearing.
This class is about preparation. The further ahead you begin to prepare things like presentations and case studies, the better off you'll be.
Metabolism is extremely fascinating! This course will equip you with a strong foundation in various aspects of metabolism and help you understand the many different roles of metabolic processes in both health and diseases. BCMP234 will most importantly inspire you to think beyond current basic science research, but the clinical and translational aspect of metabolism research and what that means for patients. At the end of this course, you will have a solid understanding of Metabolism, the complexity of metabolic diseases, and be able to start thinking about the many remaining questions within Metabolism research and how to approach them!
