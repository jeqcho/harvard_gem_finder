What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Pay attention in class because although there are office hours, there aren't really resources outside of this.
This class covers a super cool array of topics – mostly whatever Maurice Smith is interested in. Professor Smith is super smart and it's really cool to learn from him. The class is super disorganized and assignments get graded extremely late (last week of classes) months after being turned in, instructions for presentations and other assignments were released super late and were unclear, class often started 10 minutes late and ended 20 minutes late, etc. However, this class is still worth taking. It's what a freshman seminar should be – a Professor talking about all the topics he's interested in with assignments once every two weeks.
Take this class during your sophomore or junior year. I took this class as a freshman, and although the content was very very VERY interesting, I found myself needing to learn some fundamental topics (i.e. linear algebra and physics topics) that I wouldn't have had to if I took the class as an upperclassman. Other than that, I would recommend this class to anyone interested in the brain and/or how human movement works. We basically tackled the idea of movement from 4 or 5 different angles (ranging from machine learning and convolutional neural networks to the physics behind joint mechanics) and each angle was very interesting. I couldn't recommend Professor Smith enough.
Stay on top of homework assignments from an early time. Attempt all problems before going to office hours.
Go to lecture and take detailed notes. Everything is explained in great detail, but he will usually only say it once, so pay attention. Honestly, this class is really cool and you will learn a lot if you engage with it.
