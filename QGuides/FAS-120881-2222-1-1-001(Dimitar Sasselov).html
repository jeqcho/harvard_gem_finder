This class is incredible!!! Dimitar is a great lecturer and the subject material is really interesting even for people who aren't interested in life sciences, geology, astronomy, or astrophysics. While you could probably get through the class and do pretty well without going to lecture, it's definitely worth going!!! You'll get so much out of this class as long as you participate – part of what has inspired ideas for my senior thesis was working through the final project for this class, which involves presenting on a topic of your choosing and writing a 10–page paper on it. No exams – just 5 psets that each take ~2hrs to do, section participation, and working on the final project make up your grade. Section is technically 2 hours long, but you normally can finish the work in an hour and leave.
I think this class does a good job of giving students broad knowledge of astronomy, biology, and chemistry. Beyond this, however, the execution of the class is not great. Just when a topic gets interesting, the class moves on past it and starts anew. The homework is more confusing than anything, and the section assignments often feel like busy work. This class does NOT need to have a two–hour weekly section. If you are looking for an easy GENED, this class is probably for you. If you want to learn a lot about astronomy, I would choose another course.

Pretty simple gened, would recommend.
