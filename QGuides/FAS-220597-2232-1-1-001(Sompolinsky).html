Comments
As an applied math student who know almost nothing about physics (until now, I still do not have an intuition on phys terms like entropy and glassiness but only their math definitions), this course is still friendly to me – I can understand course materials and complete assignments on my own. 5 star recommended if someone's interested in learning neural nets from a different perspective.
If you are interested in the physics of disordered systems or spin glass techniques used in stat/ml fields, this course is a must–take as the instructor is one of the founding figures of the course.
One of the coolest (but more difficult) classes I've taken! The course moves very quickly, and you need to review the notes / do some extra reading to get everything. The course builds a lot of theoretical machinery, which all comes together very beautifully in the study of neural networks at the end of the course. On the problem sets you get a lot of practice with replica calculations in different random systems, and I think I got a lot faster and more confident over the course of the semester. Haim is a great professor and it's always fun to go to lecture.
This class has proven to be quite difficult. the course expects students to already know a lot of materials and familiarity with math and the content. Would definitely prepare a lot before entering the class.
This is a tough course, but a lot of effort is put into it and the TFs and professor are often available in class, section, and on slack for questions and support. The professor is intimidating to ask questions to, but he cares about students coming out of the course with an understanding of the material.
It’s very challenging but an opportunity to learn from a world expert in both spin glass statistical mechanics and neurophysics
I was really inspired by the interdisciplinary nature of this course. I enjoyed learning about how replica theory can push our ability to use statistical mechanics to solve the phase diagram of a variety of physical systems. I also appreciated the incorporation of linear algebra problems in random matrix theory. This class left me with a strong appreciation for biophysics and I hope to continue exploring stat mech in the context of non–equilibrium systems in my future career.

This is a difficult class which requires significant math prep: complex analysis, advanced linear, dynamical systems, differential equations. The lecture notes are helpful but could be more thorough in terms of explanations for particular calculations.
