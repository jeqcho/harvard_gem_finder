What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class was very hard for me even as a social studies person. The class always felt really rushed especially when talking to other students in different EXPOS classes. There is also very little flexibility in terms of extensions which is very ironic considering the class is all about how workers are exploited and capitalism, etc. There are no extensions whatsoever and just an overall pessimistic attitude towards giving extra time to complete an assignment since the dates are given in advance. Other expos classes were much more flexible so this excuse didn't make sense to me. The material is interesting though, but you have to have some background in sociology/Marx. Unit 2 was so hard, I really did not like going to class because even though I would pay attention to everything Professor Meyer was throwing at us I could never follow along. She is very enthusiastic and knowledgeable, I bet her other class is much more captivating, but I don't think this topic is suitable for an Expos class. She does give good feedback but I would increase office hours and appointment times. 15 mins is not enough for a conference. I would be cautious in ranking this class. I did learn a lot but at times it was frustrating as a student of color because I felt like the discussions lacked a racial/income angle.
The content is very interesting and engaging, which I understand to be rather rare for an expos class. Definitely consider ranking this one high if you're broadly interested in the social sciences!
this class is super well taught! professor meyer is super passionate about sociology and a wonderful resource both in and outside of the classroom. you'll learn a lot about how to write as well as some interesting sociology :)
I definitely recommend this class if you are interested in the sociology of work! I came in with a lot of questions about labor unions, social movements, as well as a. keen interest in sustainable development. The class reading are unexpected and lead you down interesting questions. The topic is difficult so expect that you will be challenged in thinking through it. As an instructor, Rachel is very accessible and knowledgeable so you can learn a lot about this topic from her. I always looked forward to discussing the readings.
Best expos class ever! Interesting topics, and the preceptor is so helpful throughout the writing process.
this class was really interesting and unlike other EXPOS classes that I've heard about, i do think that it improved my own writing and analytical skills. you can also tell that rachel is very invested in the subject matter and her passion shines through. sometimes, she can be a little condescending during conferences or class –– though i do not think this is intentional. it was just annoying at times
I would highly recommend, you get a lot out of it even if you're just taking it as a requirement to satisfy.
If you are interested in sociology as a field this is an excellent option for an expos class.
