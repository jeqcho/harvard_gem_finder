What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

(1) If this is your first math class at Harvard: get an iPad and download GoodNotes! This will be super convenient and helpful for studying/reviewing material later and save you so much time.

(2) Do not make the mistake of studying for an exam by simply looking over worksheets/skimming solutions. TRY SOLVING THEM YOURSELF! This will allow you to realize what you're iffy about and will go so far with providing you a conceptual understanding of the topics.

(3) GO TO OFFICE HOURS OR MQC––– do Psets there, it will save you so much time, or ask questions about concepts

(4) There are LOTS of formulas, have formula sheet with you

(5) Make concept maps or have your TFs make concept maps for you – they don't draw connections between concepts they teach, even though this is super important
If you're a Math–lover, take it! It's a really good foundation for upper–level classes, and you'll have fun in the process.
This class must be prioritized in your schedule. There are three PSETs a week, which for the majority of them you must attend office hours or the MQC. With that being said, the CAs and TFs are amazing, and they are always willing to help you out. But do not expect this class to be something you can put off to the side because if you fall behind it will be difficult to catch up. Overall, a solid calculus class which you should take if its part of your requirements.
Great class! I loved it and learned so much. There is so much support that as long as you are able to put some work in and aren't afraid to go to office hours/MQC, you will do well. My only caution is that the midterm grading feels nitpicky at times, and it feels like you can lose a lot of points for what seem like relatively small errors. Overall, though, I think the grading is fair and that this is a really fun class.
It's multivariable calculus, not much needs to be said beyond that. I didn't have a particularly special experience but I found the course to be fairly easy and informative, and the assignments are all very reasonable.
