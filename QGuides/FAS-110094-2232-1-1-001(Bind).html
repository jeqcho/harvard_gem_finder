What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
A background in basic stats could be helpful, but the class isn't really orientated around pure statistics, it's more about applying statistical concepts to R. You do very little computations by hand, you learn to code them in R instead. Office hours are extremely helpful for the psets (I think some of the psets would have been impossible to do without going to office hours). But overall, this was an extremely fun class, and I don't usually describe classes as being "fun". Professor Marie Bind is very nice and good at explaining concepts. I recommend taking this class as long as you understand that you'll have to put some work in to be successful, but it's nothing ridiculous. I'd say it was similar in effort to math 1a, but the psets and exams were much less difficult.
I would like to tell future students that this class is certainly not easy, especially if you are not necessarily a coding person (as I would consider myself). However, after finishing this course, I feel like I am a better person and a better science scholar; further, the teaching staff is amazing and truly want you to succeed, and the office hours are life changing! Although this course isn't easy, it is certainly worth it.
If you are interested in statistics and life sciences, and are even thinking about taking this course, do it! Dr. Bind is incredible and the TFs are very supportive. You will work hard, but will learn so much that it is totally worth the weekly problem set grind. I enjoyed this class so much and has been such an incredible experience with this course.
Marie and Julie are both super nice. Go to office hours if you have any questions! It's not as bad if you're willing to put in the work and learn the concepts.
the course staff are so helpful and willing to work with you, even when you are behind. I learned a lot about coding, statistics, and experimental design in general
This class desperately needs structure. Toward the end of the semester, we started learning things that seemed like what we learned at the beginning. Also, the lack of office hours is frustrating. They do have a slack Q and A but I personally dont think it makes up for it and sometimes the answers to questions on slack wouldnt be helpful at all. However, you do learn to code in R and a little bit about statistics too. If this had a solid structure, it could be a ten, but currently its a 5 (on a good day).
I would recommend not taking this course unless it is absolutely required to. The amount of work is not worth the added nuance of learning about statistics through the life sciences and there are so many other ways to fill a basic statistics requirement.
You will need to put effort into this class if you want to do well. Go to lectures, attend section, and ask questions when needed because the coding and conceptual topics can be quite confusing at times. The faculty are all super helpful so don't be afraid to reach out.
Take it if you want a real intro to Statistics that won't kill you and has lots of good support! 10/10 for me!
