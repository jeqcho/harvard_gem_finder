This is one of my favorite classes I've taken during my time here. I'm very much not pursuing a career/concentration in humanities/politics and actually considered ranking a more STEM–y expos section higher — but I'm super glad I ended up being placed in this course. Prof. Gilbert is super enthusiastic, passionate, and committed to having an open dialogue in the class; he's a really, really excellent teacher. I learned a lot; I challenged myself as a writer and as a person, as did a lot of my classmates. I've also been able to meet so many cool people through the class and had some interesting discussions outside of class since the material is that compelling. Would definitely recommend taking it :)
This class is certainly not the easiest expos class out there, but if you do the readings and engage in the discussions, you will learn a lot and become a better writer along the way too
EXPOS does get a lot of flak but it really is a mixed bag. Don’t let others’ stories and experiences cause you to fear this class. How you’ll feel about and ultimately perform in the class will depend on two things; your preceptor and your attitude. Preceptors are a mixed bag so I’d say your attitude is the most important. And your attitude will heavily hinge on how invested/ interested in the subject material. So when you’re ranking your choices don’t just rank them blindly or put them because your friends are taking them (it’s a lottery anyway), but make sure to rank the ones you’re genuinely interested in at the top. It makes a difference. Take some time to read the descriptions on the EXPOS website. I loved this particular EXPOS (Culture and Politics of Inequality) because I hit the jackpot with it: enthusiastic preceptor, interesting readings, and I was super interested in the material. Experiences will vary but you have a chance to affect yours.
You'd be an idiot to not add this class to your lottery application.
