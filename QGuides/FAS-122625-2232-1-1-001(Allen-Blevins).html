I would tell future HEB 97 students to really take time to engage with all of our weekly readings, as yourself meaningful questions about the material to bring to class, and come prepared to discuss and learn about new and exciting topics in human evolution. If you are even remotely considering HEB as a concentration, take this course! There are not many sophomore tutorials left like this in other departments, so it is truly a privilege we get to enjoy!
Stay on track with your readings and start writing your essays early. Stick to the rubric, and you'll be fine. This was a really interesting class, don't let the fact that there's so few points in the gradebook scare you, they're really consistent with grading and once you finish your first paper everything becomes much better!
Such a fantastic course. Cary is an amazing professor, so knowledgeable on the topic and also facilitates great discussion in such a small class. We have 4 essays throughout the semester and while these are stressful, the grading is tough but very reasonable and the feedback on the essays is helpful, in–depth, and exactly what is needed to continuously improve the quality of the essays. The material overall is actually THE most interesting stuff I have ever learned, maybe this is just because I already liked evolutionary bio but I truly think the info we learn is applicable in a ton of different ways, I retained so much of it, and I understand fully how important it is to learn about how humans evolved in order to efficiently progress.
