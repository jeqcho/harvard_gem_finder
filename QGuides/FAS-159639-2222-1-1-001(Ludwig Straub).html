What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Learn the computational methods as well as possible – the skills are generally useful.
This is an intense course ranging different topics. If you like macro, you definetly should take it. Especially the first part taught by Ludwig, which gives you a mix of contemporaneus economics history and tools for modeling in macro.
It will be a lot of work but you will learn how to tell the non–economists in your life what is going on in the world. The professors really care.
The new iteration of the class with Ludwig, Adrian, and Xavier moved very quickly and covered a lot of material. I appreciated that this class provides a modern update on the material and we engage with models/research that is at the frontier of the field.
This course is definitely accessible to an undergraduate (like me) with lots of math/stats background and Econ 1011A. It was certainly challenging, but I found it so interesting and rewarding to learn macro at this level. The work was worth it.
Hard class, lots of topics, but mostly rewarding
