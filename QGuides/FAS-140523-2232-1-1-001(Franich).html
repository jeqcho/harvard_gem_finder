Great class. A wonderful addition to the Linguistics department as this subject matter isn't taught in the department. It's niche so your classmates should be interested in the material which makes for great class discussions. And it's just enjoyable.
This course deals with classic and contemporary content pertaining to the evolution of sociolinguistics, often using modern examples. This makes the course engaging. Professor Franich is caring and openminded. This makes the class well worth taking alone.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! The subject was super interesting – if you are interested in sociology, linguistics, or both, you will definitely enjoy it. We read papers each week and discussed them in class, with themes of identity, sexuality/queerness, race, and place recurring throughout the semester. I'm so glad I took this course!*
This is a great introduction into sociolinguistics, both for concentrators and for people who are just interested in the topic from a personal or intellectual perspective. I would recommend starting the final paper early, which will allow you to keep the workload very manageable throughout the semester. Katie is a wonderful instructor who will support you in your interests, whatever they may be. I'd highly recommend anyone who is even remotely interested in linguistics or sociology to take this class!
Sociolinguistics is awesome! It really feels like a culmination of the many fields of linguistics (or an introduction to them). It touches on all the different elements of language and how they may relate to social realities. This is a fun class, lots of interesting papers, and not too much work
TAKE THIS CLASS! Even if you aren't a linguistics concentrator. Prof. Franich is wonderful and the discussion was always interesting, with students being encouraged to bring in their own personal experiences in an incredibly welcoming learning environment. The workload is very fair and allows you the freedom to engage with the course material at your own pace.
TAKE THIS CLASS! BEST LING CLASS AT HARVARD AND MOST FUN. Whether you're a concentrator, tired of the endless classical/formal linguistics classes, or a prospective learner, looking for a light introductory course, this class is for you. Prof Franich is amazing, Yvette is here to help, and you will NOT regret it!!! Can't recommend enough
