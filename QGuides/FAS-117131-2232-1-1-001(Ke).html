What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course focuses primarily on a few basic model structures; while taught very well, I walked out of the class feeling that I had only really learned 2–3 core model types throughout the course of the semester.
In the first half of the course, you learn a lot of useful foundational models for time series data. In the second half of the course, you learn about various (often obscure) extensions of those methods that apply to specific scenarios. I found the second half overly complicated and lacking in intuition.
The subject's interesting; though the instructor may not be the best speaker. The slides were a bit unorganized (questions do not come with answers), so you really have to be present and take very good notes for every lecture.
The course is amazing! Tracy taught almost everything you want to know about time series model. For example, in the course, we have learnt ARMA, ARIMA, GARCH, HMM and so on. They are all really useful in academic research and practical applications(e.g. stock returns prediction). Besides, this course makes a great balance between the math(theoretical) and practical using. You can enjoy both the beauty of the math behind the models, and you can also learn how to actual use them! Moreover, for the students who major in finance, this course may help you understand better about Capital Market related models(CAPM,FF3,FF5). And for the students who major in economics, this course may be an extension of econometrics. Time Series will be really helpful for research and practical applications.
