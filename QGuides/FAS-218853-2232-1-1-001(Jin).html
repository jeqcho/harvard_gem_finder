What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Meng is a great instructor, both in helping to create a very constructive and creative learning and writing environment as well as genuinely helping in the writing of a particular piece. You don't need to be daunted coming in, as long as you're willing to take writing and the class seriously. Some things may seem funny or unusual, but if you embrace the weird–ness then you get the most out of the class. Be ready to spend a lot of time on your final piece. I think the greatest benefit to this class truly is Meng's feedback, and the more prepared you are going into her meetings as well as into workshop, the more you'll get out of learning to be a better writer.
I'm sorry you won't be able to take this class because it was amazing. If you can see the syllabus, I'd recommend reading the books for this class and to write and share delights with a group of friends.
I am a graduating senior and this is in my top 3 courses I have taken at Harvard. I went into this course with *zero* creative writing experience and felt completely comfortable and supported; Meng fosters a warm, playful, and welcoming creative community within the class. In addition, the workshop process for this class gives you more agency over what feedback you receive / how it is received, which makes the process less intimidating and more collaborative. The readings are enjoyable, thought–provoking, and perspective–shifting. I am leaving this course with a different understanding of delight / grief / suffering / meaning in life, a more delightful experience of my daily life, and a newfound realization that I actually enjoy creative writing. Meng is a great teacher and extremely kind. Take this course!
This is one of the best classes I ever took at Harvard. Meng is so insightful and encouraging, and the relationship you form with the other students in the class is amazing. I loved the course's emphasis on thinking from the perspectives of non–human beings, and how much of the class was focused not on what we would normally think of as the "writing process" (as in, putting words down on a page) but instead on going outside and spending time in nature and with each other, learning to slow down, notice, and listen to the world around us. Genuinely a life–changing class tbh.
This class was amazing and probably one of the most memorable classes I've ever taken. Readings were impeccable and there was a comfortability in the classroom setting that encouraged me to contribute to classroom discussion.
There will not be future students of this class, I don't think. But, "let yourselves be led. Don't resist."
