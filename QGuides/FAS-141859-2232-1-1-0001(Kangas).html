You have to be ready to speak, and it's intimidating at times, especially when you're uncertain about the readings, but the classroom environment that Professor Kangas creates is really nice and welcoming, mainly because he's a really nice guy and makes a lot of dad jokes and quirky jokes about drugs!! Definitely recommend––just make sure to sleep the night before or you will suffer since it's a super long class!

Take this course for the Neuro requirement
This class is great! The reading workload varies but I would expect to budget at least 7 hours on readings and notes –– I would recommend looking back at the email Dr. K sends to know where to focus on and write down questions you have. I would also familarize yourself with scientific writing.
This class is really awesome if you are at all interested in pharmacology/drugs/drugs of abuse/addiction/behaviorism/neuroscience. This is probably the single most engaging class I've taken in my 3 years at Harvard and the one in which I learned the most about neuroscience and scientific research (despite being a neuro concentrator done with all concentration requirements). The only assignments in the entire class are weekly readings and a final paper. Be warned though, the readings are pretty lengthy and you actually do have to do them pretty closely. The entire 3 hr class is a discussion seminar where participation is weighted heavily and there are occasionally pop quizzes on the reading. Also, you have literally no idea what your grade is going to be throughout the entire semester (the final paper is worth 50%). So if you don't do well with that, this might not be the class for you. I would still really recommend this class if you are interested in the topics and it's really unlike any other class I've ever taken at Harvard.
Just take this class seriously hahaha
Take this class if you like neuro and are interested in translating animal research to clinical populations! It was SUPER interesting and pretty much mainly discussion based. Behavioral pharmacology is basically all about how different drugs impact individuals differently based on different contexts (in simplified sense). The papers each week cover a wide range of topics, and youre really able to dive deep in discussion to think critically about the papers and generate a larger discussion with really nice guidance from Dr. Kangas. Would highly recommend!!
This is definitely a course where you get out of it the effort you put in. The readings can be a lot at times and the quizzes can be a little hard, especially if you might not have a lot of experience reading research papers. However, I learned a lot from the course and would definitely recommend it to anyone wanting to learn more about pharmacology and participate in discourse.
This course is extremely interesting, but represents a significant amount of work. Be ready to read 5–7 papers a week, multiple times (as there are pop quizzes for which you need to be quite familiar with the text). That being said, the course is exceptionally informative and Dr. Kangas obviously loves what he studies. Though one of the more stressful courses I have taken at the college, I highly recommend the course if you have the time and energy to invest in it.
I would tell students that it can be a little long at times, but the material will really challenge you to go deeper with what you know about neuroscience from classes at Harvard. It is very interesting, and it is important you take the time to read the readings –– it will be worth it. Ask questions, and try to connect it to what you have learned in the past.
This course is an amazing introduction to the field of behavioral pharmacology. Although some of the readings are lengthy (and they might seem numerous), you quickly get used to the pace of 6ish paper readings per week. Plus, they are really interesting, and it is truly to your benefit to read them so you can comfortably participate during class and excel at the pop quizzes. Overall, I think this is an amazing course that is definitely worth taking if you are at all interested in pharmacology or even just in the field of psychology. I definitely recommend it.
