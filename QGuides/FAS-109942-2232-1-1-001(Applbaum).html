If you don't have much experience reading analytic philosophy, there may be a bit of a learning curve, but it is so worth it. This class tends to attract students with a variety of intellectual interests who are interested in asking and answering very difficult ethical questions. It is one of the few undergrad classes at Harvard where every single student is genuinely interested in learning for the sake of learning, so it's a unique and exciting opportunity. That said, the class is highly rewarding but also intellectually rigorous. To get the most out of it, you will need lots of time to think! Since the bulk of the assignments are due Sunday through Tuesday, be careful of frontloading other obligations that might prevent you from taking the time to seriously engage with the readings, assignments, and discussions.

Although class meets for nearly three hours each week, the time will pass quickly with Arthur's dynamic teaching (which includes a suite of long–running dad jokes) and discussion facilitation. Plus, JP is an incredibly kind and knowledgeable TF who is always willing to help (and who gives feedback at an uncanny speed). Take this class for the challenge and the community, among the class itself and with the Safra Center at large.

Absolutely take this course. This has been my favorite course at Harvard so far. Not only will you get the chance to enjoy fascinating lectures and discussions with really smart, thoughtful students, but you will get a chance to interact with the Safra Center, which is an incredible community and center at Harvard. How many people get the chance to interact with leading scholars, professors, and students at the front edge of ethics in such an intimate setting? The course is truly delightful. Readings are engaging and apply directly to day–to–day life. Their direct relevancy to our lives makes class discussions important and meaningful (and more lively as a result). But much of the liveliness of the course can be attributed to both Arthur and JP, who are some of my favorite teaching staff at the university. They are not only brilliant and brilliant to listen to, but generous with their time and attention. You are in great hands in this course and will inevitably have an amazing experience.
Applying for the fellowship and taking the course has easily been one of the best decisions I've made as a Harvard student. I have never been at a seminar table with as high a concentration of incredible, thoughtful, kind students. Arthur is an absolute gem on this campus –– he makes the 3–hour class fly by in an instant and has a lot of passion for the fellowship as a whole. JP is the absolute sweetest. The readings and material are (almost) always fascinating. The food is pretty nice. I really couldn't recommend the fellowship more highly and I definitely recommend taking advantage of all the cool events you get invited to as a fellow!!
No background is needed for this class, but interest + intrinsic motivation will go a long way! It also has some of the besttttt people on campus. Absolutely take this if you have the time to budget.
The class is great, but don't expect a traditional discussion seminar (even though the content of the course really lends itself to such). Discussion really isn't encouraged; most of the time in seminar is Arthur lecturing, which is very edifying but not really seminar–like. The topics are fascinating and, if you are interested in ethics or politics, I would encourage you to apply for the fellowship and take the course.
