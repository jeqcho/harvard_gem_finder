EXCELLENT CLASS! The teaching staff as a whole (including the TFs) is the best I've had in any class I've taken here so far. In terms of background, you need to be very comfortable with linear algebra, including with complex matrices (terms like Hermitian, unitary, spectral gap, eigendecomposition, SVD, tensor product, etc. should not scare you, although I definitely wasn't familiar with all those terms coming in but was able to catch up). You also need Stat 110, CS 121, and CS 124. In addition, Physics 143a and perhaps Stat 171 will be helpful but aren't super necessary. There were certainly several lectures I felt very lost during but rewatching after, working through the scribe notes, and asking questions during OH to clear things up was usually sufficient. The final project was so much fun to work on and you get to read papers that are very recent. Lots of people (including myself) who take it are undergrads and it's 100% worth it.
The class is a very good introduction to Quantum Information and requires only some linear algebra. Prof. Anurag is super nice and although this was his first time teaching, he put a lot of effort and improved noticebly. The course covers a wide range of topics, so you get a change to see a bit of everything, and then focus on a favorite one for the final project. The homeworks are extremely well designed, which is maybe why they only have a couple of problems each. The notes for the class are taken by scribes, but they are so detailed it is amazing. If you have any sort of experience with quantum physics, you would find this course very enjoyable and not too hard.
Honestly, I was on the fence about taking this class at the beginning of the semester, with this being a new class and having a new professor. I'm so glad I ended up taking it though because I actually really enjoyed this class. The time commitment was fairly chill and Anurag is a very kind professor and always trying to make sure that everyone is understanding the content as much as possible. I never really imagined myself taking a quantum computing class and made this decision on a whim, but the content was really cool and I can really say that I learned so much taking this class. My one issue with the class is probably that it felt like there were more underlying prereqs to the class than expected. While the only prereqs were CS124 and Stat110, it felt as if we zoomed through the physics background that you needed to learn about and I had to spend a bit of time outside class just trying to understand all the notation and all the other basics. Once I got past this part, it was fairly smooth sailing so I would still recommend this class to anyone who has a strong linear algebra and CS background.
