I'd recommend taking this course in a non–Zoom format; that would have notably changed the value many students would have gained, I believe.

Otherwise, you really get out of the course what you put in. If you want to skim the papers and grok a little bit of the lectures, you can. But if you want to do the readings in–depth, go to office hours, and participate in class, you'll get *way* more out of it.

Also, Kominers is incredible. Highly recommend everyone form a relationship with him, if you are lucky enough.
This class is life–changing! Not only will you learn so many cool market design ideas and proofs, but Scott is also such a great professor to talk to outside of class, and he cares so much about all of his students.
When I started college, I decided that my priority would be to learn and pick up a lot of different ways to think about and problem solve the world. I got hooked onto this class the first few weeks, when I saw how mathematics was being used to address equity issues in public schools. It's so valuable to have your foot in the doorway that opens to the perspective that Market Design offers.

My only regret is that, after a rough semester and many other commitments, I did not engage with this material and with Scott as much as I would have liked to. Scott was right from day one –– you get what you put in.
Amazing class!! Scott is great and the readings are super interesting, don't forget to apply in advance and check Scott's website for info if you're interested.
Absolutely stellar course with a top–notch lecturer and fascinating economic ideas; Scott is a genius and so approachable and friendly. The class is as much work as you want to put in (and the more you put in, the more you get out), which made it an incredibly enjoyable experience. The one reservation I would have for recommending this course is that it was taught over Zoom this semester; I did not think this long virtual seminar format worked for me or most of my classmates. It was very difficult to pay attention for such a long period of time and keep track of the many complicated concepts.
ECON 2099 is super interesting and very reasonable workload–wise. Scott works hard to make this a chill, graduate topics course that encourages personal exploration. His comments on paper milestones are helpful. Comfort with math will be very, very helpful. The coffee chats were fascinating and I really like the wide breadth of topics chosen for the final project. Definitely walked away with a lot of new ideas (if not new skills) and a great experience.
I thought this course was okay — you will get out what you put in and its pretty chill, but not life changing.
If you feel like you might have even a passing interest in a mathematical and application–driven approach to both traditional and very atypical economic thinking, this class is absolutely a must–take. Scott is an incredible professor who cares deeply and is passionate enough to rap to the class, and without even having to stress about grades or assignments (there's just one flexible final project), you'll leave with both a deep understanding of market design concepts and a desire to use your newfound research chops for good.
This is the best elective in the economics department in my opinion!
Take this course! It is definitely on the Harvard must do list! Scott is absolutely amazing (as a teacher and as a person), the guided discussions of papers are great, the topics are both rigorous and meaningful (with cool applications to current events such as the pandemic, and modern issues like inequality and cryptocurrency), and the project is a great opportunity to explore a topic that you care about with great mentorship from Scott. Definitely highly, highly, recommend.
This class is very interesting, Scott keeps things lively and exciting, and workload is pretty much as little or as much as you’d like. The final project is fun, it is open ended and you have the chance to be very creative!
This course is an absolute must–take if you are interested at the intersection of economics and math/CS. Definitely apply!
Try to take this class in person if you can! Scott is a great professor and has a lot of cool insights to offer, and everyone can learn from him. Just wish I was able to talk to other students rather than a long Zoom session.
This is an incredible class – how much you get out of it really depends on how much effort you put in. While there are only 1–2 required readings for class discussion each week, I appreciated the extensive reading list for each topic that allows students to take a deeper dive into topics that they find the most interesting. Scott's enthusiasm for market design is contagious, even on Zoom (which didn't take away from the experience at all)! He is really approachable and genuine in providing feedback to help you meaningfully refine your project proposal, and generally a lot of fun to chat to, so sign up to as many discussions/chats/office hours as you can.
If you get the chance to take this class, take it!! Scott is an amazing professor, so knowledgeable about so many topics (market design and beyond), makes himself available, and truly cares about his students.
