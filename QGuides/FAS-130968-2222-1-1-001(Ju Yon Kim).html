Emily Sun is the best! If she's TFing again, a huge plus — great at leading sections, really helpful and kind. Professor Kim is so brilliant and lectures excellently. The course material is a great selection, and teaching staff cares a lot about making the course accessible to students with any level of background in English.
Great course! Take it if you're interested in the horror genre (or just want to branch out)!
This class was online in spring 2022, so the class felt very disjointed. There was a lot of reading, so be prepared for that, but the texts were interesting and varied, and the writing assignments weren't too bad. The final project was actually kind of fun. Overall good experience, even as a non–humanities student.
This is a very well–taught course that approaches the history of American literature in an engaging and diverse way, and touches on themes –– fear, violence, marginalization, grief, loneliness –– that are useful for every person to think about, regardless of whether you are an English concentrator or not.
This is a really interesting class, Professor Kim is great, and Emily is such a wonderful TF. It is a lot of reading and can move fast paced sometimes, but it is all very interesting.
The topics in this course are very interesting, and the feedback on your writing is pretty good. I highly recommend if you are looking to satisfy your Arts and Humanities Divisional Distribution.
I would definitely recommend this class strongly. While you do read a lot of literature over the semester, the short stories in the beginning of the semester are definitely geared to bring you up to speed. And even if you don't read every page of the novels assigned, you can get by by reading chunks of the stories . The creative project also was much fun, and I definitely encourage future students to take advantage of the creative aspect and stay away from writing analytical/comparative papers.
If you're looking for an appropriately rigorous while still extremely accessible course in the humanities, this is a great choice. I recommend with enthusiasm to those looking to make a start in the humanities. I do think it is a bit redundant or even too "easy" for a well–seasoned humanist taking it for a requirement, and find myself a little dissatisfied at the close of my Migrations requirement, it was regardlessly an excellent course.
I wanted to like this course more than I did. The class felt uneven, going from "classics" of American horror (Poe, Chesnutt, James, etc.) to contemporary works of horror in a way that did not feel like it wished especially to unite the two or to discuss the development of the genre. Professor Kim is very nice and puts time into the class (including getting to know students and their work), which was appreciated very much. And Emily was a wonderful TF. But that doesn't save the class from feeling like an incomplete overview of –– I apologize to horror fans –– a genre I have not yet met in a way that would make me consider it consequential.
This class was amazing! Professor Kim was a very knowledgable and engaging lecturer. The readings were all very interesting, although at some points the pace of the readings could be intense. Even so, it was worth it.
