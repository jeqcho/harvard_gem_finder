I think that, for an expos class, this is a great course. Through the nature of expos, you will probably not completely enjoy your time since your writing will be broken down and built back up in a way that you might not necessarily agree with, but I think that this class did a good job of making the students make the most out of this experience through the interesting discussions, fascinating topics, and, overall, process of improving one's writing. For these reasons, I would recommend the course. Nevertheless, be forewarned that, to get an A on your essays, you will have to subject yourself to Jen's very specific style of writing, such as the introduction of personal pronouns, the "roadmap paragraph," which states exactly what you will discuss in the essay, and having to do things such as not being allowed to include a counterargument or a conclusion in your first essay since the class hadn't yet covered these aspects of essays.

Again, however, I think that this class was definitely a net positive, and, if you have the faintest interest in philosophy, definitely consider taking this expos class!
If you're interested in philosophy, take this course. This course is meant for people who are actually interested in the work they're doing. Reading requires close analysis and discussions need participation. That said, you have actually to care about the coursework to do this. Jen is an amazing preceptor who gives a lot, so that same expectation is there for you. Please take the course though even if you have a slight interest in asking yourself big questions, it can generally make an impact on you. Also, the reading checks probably seem really bad, but I found they get you to read and make the course smoother in the long term.
I would tell future student to actually read the course reading before they take the class. It can be a lot but as long as you start early, best believe that it is very possible to complete the reading.
As an expos requirement, it's quite a fun one! The readings will be enjoyable if you really spend some time thinking about them (or e.g. even discussing them with your friends), which is great. Readings are relatively limited in length (total readings are not much), but may be dense and take a few read–throughs.

Great, beginner–friendly intro friendship and philosophy. If you are looking for a formal introduction to philosophy broadly, I would reccomend just taking that instead. However, if you think a lot about friendship in general, I think this is a great expos to potentially take.
It's a great class that's really interesting if you're interested in philosophy or just friendships in general. Even if you have no experience in philosophy, it's a great intro into that realm. The professor is very nice and helpful, but she expects a high level of effort and energy to be put into the class and the assignments.
Now, you only take one Expos 20 class, so I can't directly compare this one to any of the others, but even so I feel confident in stating that this is one of the best (if not the best) Expos class. Coming from someone who could only be described as a very STEM person who went into this with no philosophy background, Philosophy of Friendship ended up being incredibly interesting and fun, partly because what you learn here can be applied to pretty much anyone. You learn to write, sure, but also end up getting to see your relationships with other people through different perspectives, and that's pretty cool when you're first coming to college where there's a bunch of new and interesting people to get to know and make friends with.
As for the course load/difficulty, what you put in to this class is absolutely what you get out of it. You could theoretically get away with doing the bare minimum, but I highly recommend putting in the effort, since you WILL be rewarded for it, and like I said the topic is also just really cool to engage with. The weekly schedule is a philosophy paper to read before each class, which doesn't take too long, and then a class that's divided between discussing philosophical ideas as a group + going over writing skills. Jen is a fair grader but also super nice, she gives really good feedback on essay drafts so be sure to read those if you end up taking this expos. Seriously though, great class, definitely one to put in your expos lottery list if you think the topic is even a little interesting.
