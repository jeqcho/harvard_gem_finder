Comments
This is a great class to get an overview of the world of drug development and delivery, and provides a good opportunity to go deeply into an area of the field you're interested in.
Lectures are boring because of the format – Samir (Head Instructor) just walks back and forth and lectures for 1h 15min. However, he is very insightful and knows a lot about the drug delivery field. Try this course out for a week and see if you like it. One thing that was nice about this is you learn about different methods in the drug delivery field and apply those by coming up with your own drug delivery system, you write a grant proposal.
This is a great course to take to understand the basics of and the evolution of the field of drug delivery. The lecturing style makes it easy to grasp concepts. There is plenty of room for discussion regarding related topics. The course load is not heavy, and the deliverables include weekly presentation evaluations and a final grant proposal. This will also introduce students to an R21 style grant and the process involved in developing one.
Prof. Mitragotri is an amazing professor who truly cares about his students. He's open to any question and very available after class as well. He truly wants his students to succeed.
This is an amazing class. I strongly recommend it. You'll learn so much cool stuff!
Take this class!! Favorite class potentially ever! Prof Mitragotri is a leader in his field and his lectures are quite interesting. Lectures don't go too in depth, but you come out feeling like you have a pretty comprehensive overview of drug delivery concepts. I also recommend going to office hours just to ask questions to Prof Mitragotri for the sake of interest. You get out what you put in––it's easy to fall into the habit of skipping class, but then you won't get as much out of it. Also, stay on top of the grant proposal assignment because it's difficult to finish last minute, especially if you don't have grant proposal writing experience.
This course requires minimal work. Lectures are not the most engaging but provide a decent overview of the field. Weekly student presentations on products on the market were super interesting and well done. We submitted subsections of the grant proposal every 1–2 weeks to get feedback, which made the final submission much easier.
This course is absolutely fascinating and Mitragotri is a great lecturer and incredibly experienced. Would definitely recommend.
