What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Incredible course. Loved the intimate nature of the class which provided us with opportunities to be vulnerable & test and develop our writing skills with the constant reinforcement of our peers. The workshop setting is absolutely vital to this sort of creative exploration, and my writing benefitted from being able to workshop my peers' stories in small groups with highly analytical, literary–minded peers (workshop is great at helping you to develop tough skin and the ability to be vulnerable with your writing). Take this class if you LOVE to write and are SERIOUS about improving your writing. Truly the light of my semester!
Take it if you have the fortune to!!! (Though I think Gish is "retiring" –– hopefully not for long! ;)
Definitely take this class, it's one of the best ones I ever took at Harvard. You learn a lot from the instructor and your peers. Not only will you write better by the end but you'll also be a more critical reader and learn to provide useful feedback to others. Because the theme of this course has a focus on non–Western traditions, you'll learn to bend Western rules of writing and better understand how Western techniques have been employed so far.
Be prepared to put in time and effort into the readings and the workshop. This class is what you make of it. It can be just another class if you let it be, but if you really dive in it can be transformative for you as a writer.
I highly recommend this course to anyone with a sincere interest in writing fiction. The feedback from your professor on multiple short stories, plus the ability to critique the work of your peers, is invaluable experience.
Take the course! I really can't imagine someone regretting this course, and think that the subject matter and growth that you'll have as a writer will be phenomenal!
This workshop was incredibly helpful with regard to teaching certain plot and style techniques. You should be prepared to read and write extremely quickly, as short stories are not turned in until two–and–a–half days prior to when they are discussed.
Wonderful class! Gish is one of the more straightforward professors I've had (in giving feedback/saying what works and what doesn't work), which I found was really helpful in editing. Super kind and accessible too, and is always willing to give life/writerly advice. The quality of the discussion depends on who's in the class.
