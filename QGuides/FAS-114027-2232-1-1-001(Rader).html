I was hesitant about taking Stat 104 because of the bad q–reports in past years, but hear me out, it's a good class! Lectures are recorded so you don't have to go to a single lecture if you don't want to, Professor Rader is a great lecturer and teaches stat very well, and the pset (due once every 1.5 weeks) is not bad if you take diligent notes in lecture. However, I'd HIGHLY recommend you have some sort of coding knowledge beforehand, otherwise I'd imagine the R–component of the course (which is kinda the whole thing) would be very difficult. I took CS50, but you could probably get by with even less as long as you know very basic coding principles (variable types, etc.). All in all, it gives you a nice introduction to regression and probability and I'd recommend to anyone interested in statistics.
