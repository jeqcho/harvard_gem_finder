This class is not that difficult and probably less work than other premed bio classes, but it is so extremely frustrating. Biology lectures were so boring and disorganized. Why are they entirely given in analogy? Just describe the cell directly. Microscopy lectures were a little more interesting. Quizzes were not helpful for learning and unrelated to the lectures sometimes. Expectations for lab reports and independent studies were extremely unclear and they took an insane amount of time with no guidance from the course staff. They gave us no information on how to prepare samples or take the images and we guessed based on online guides incorrectly and had to spend many hours doing it again. You probably will learn very little as most of the lectures are just on actin and very boring. Also, there is nothing to check if you recall them at all. The lectures are unrelated to the lab work and the quizzes can be Googled. Overall, super disorganized and irritating, but still relatively easy.
A 10/10 MCB class. It’s lotteried but take it if you can. It’s a lot of fun and the course staff are amazing.
This class is normally interesting and very manageable, and then when the final project hits you realize you got straight up jebaited. We literally received no instructions on sample preparation. We then had to hella grind and figure things out ourselves, wasting literally two appointments just figuring out how things work following some very vague protocol we found online, which turned out to be wrong but we were so desperate and it was our only information. Normally this just seems like an L+course diff but what got us coping and seething was how we were given the WRONG information about one of our samples, and we then had points deducted because of this. Got me literally malding over such a cringe move from the course staff. This was absolutely troll since when we asked for clarification our message was IGNORED. All of this could have been avoided if the class just was better organized and we actually got a slightly more reasonable amount of instruction.
Fun class if you like actin, microscopes, and history.
Take this class!! One of the best ways to fulfill your pre–med requirements!!! Professor Garner and Lichtman are very passionate professors, and from my experience, Professor Garner is always willing to help out, whether that be for class material or microscope troubleshooting!! You really get to learn how to master using the microscope and the microscopic world is just so fascinating!!
Please take this course if you're interested in microscopy and seeing cool images. The independent projects are great ways to get creative with microscope imaging.
If you have space, take this course: it is one of the coolest classes I have taken at Harvard so far. Its hands on approach rekindled my love for research, and there is a big emphasis on tinkering and technical problem solving, which I haven't felt in any other course and is so helpful when it comes to actual lab work. The class lab takes a while (especially at the beginning) but it is so worth it, especially if you are going into bio: the experience and understanding of microscopes you get is very useful for any kind of imaging you might do in the future.
This class is very interesting – especially if you want to work with microscopes in the future! I really had a lot of fun with the lectures, and found them to be very engaging. Be sure to begin the independent projects early though and stay on top of the work – it can be very difficult to catch up at the end because taking the images and writing the report is very time consuming. Also for the second independent project, reach out for help early and often – there is very little oversight and it can be difficult to troubleshoot on your own.
This class has a few crunch times throughout the semester with high workload and vague instructions. Make sure you ask for help early and plan ahead. Otherwise, it’s an interesting course on microscopy with a side of actins and microtubules.
This is an amazing course and I definitely recommend it. Go to office hours to talk to the professors! I regret not doing this more. Start your independent projects early so you can image a lot of different samples and pick out the best ones to include in the write up.
This class is great if you learn better without the pressure of exams––we just had lecture quizzes which were not too bad. The freedom to image whatever we wanted to with the microscopes was a very cool aspect as well. Final project is definitely a bit stressful, but as long as you show you put forth your best effort you'll do fine.
This is a great class, pretty easy and you learn a lot about how to use microscopes and how to get better images with them. I knew nothing about microscopes prior to this class and I was totally fine. You put in what you get out, so if you put a lot of effort and try to do more innovative techniques with the projects you will definitely get more out of it. I wish I had had more time to spend on this class honestly.
This class will teach you microscopy as an actual, real, useful skill. I had a background in cell bio but not in hands–on microscopy, and this class totally broke the ice for me. Using light microscopes throughout the semester really gave me a comfortability with them that I will carry on to future lab work, as well as the confidence to troubleshoot as necessary in upcoming projects. The workload is manageable –– no PSETs, just two short online quizzes a week, in–person labs at the beginning of the semester, and two independent projects later on. The independent projects are quite open–ended, and I was able to pick specimens and design experiments/procedures I was actually interested in, as well as use more high–tech scopes in the Harvard Center for Biological Imaging. Overall really cool experience, 10/10 recommend.
