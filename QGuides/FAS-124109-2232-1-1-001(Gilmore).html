In my opinion, this is a must–take class; it's a laidback seminar–style introduction to the many spheres of microbiology across one semester, guided by the expert hands of Harvard/Boston–area microbiologists and leading researchers. It'll help refine your presentation and scientific proposal writing skills along the way, too. Particularly if Sam Wellington Miranda is still involved, it's one of the best classes offered at Harvard.

OEB 290 is a great course that exposes you to many different facets of evolution and ecology in microbiology. It can be extremely low effort, though the time required can scale based on how closely you read the papers (I appreciated the flexibility when certain weeks of the semester became rough). Assignments are structured to be useful for fellowship applications – I'd highly recommend this class for anyone looking to explore this field before grad school fellowship applications.
This course invites different speakers every week to talk about their work or work they find seminal. It's a great survey course about different frontiers in microbiology, though it is not comprehensive nor necessarily deep. What you get out of it is what you put in.
