This class is honestly life changing. Hudson is an incredible instructor and has helped my writing significantly. The engaged scholar portion, where we invited guest speakers was incredibly impactful. Hearing from people who experienced the problem we were discussing put the entire debate into a different lens. My one warning would be that the course is challenging. But that challenge is what makes it particularly special. It's not challenging in a way that there is an enormous workload that is impossible to handle. It is difficult in a technical way, where every piece of the writing has to be precise and well placed, analyzed and supported. This means you learn how to create a well crafted argument with evidence to support. I would highly recommend this course.
Take this course!!!!! As someone who was not very familiar with the topic of mass incarceration, the course gave a very nuanced introduction to the subject that placed students at the center of the debates surrounding mass incarceration. In addition, Hudson pushed and supported us in every aspect of the essay writing, giving us a lot of time and feedback on the first essay to thoroughly develop our arguments and orient readers to our sources (this Expos section has a more relaxed schedule than other sections, since it requires two essays + one short capstone project instead of three essays). Hudson is also kind and caring, creating a welcoming environment in the classroom. The speaker events were also fantastic, completely changing my perspective on the issue and helping me realize how horrific incarceration is.

If you want a course that will improve your writing, improve your understanding of one of the most pressing issues of our time, and have a reasonable schedule that is not as rushed as some other Expos sections, then take this course. You will not regret it. The only critique is that some of the writing lessons will be a bit easy (i.e. transitions and citing sources), but this is helpful in the long run to make sure that every aspect of the essay is as polished as it can be.
Hudson is a great teacher and person and this class is sooo worth it! It is challenging at times but challenging in a way that requires you to critically think in perhaps new ways and not procrastinate too much. I also found that all the other expos classes had earlier deadlines for papers than us because of the different opportunities this class focuses on besides just writing.
If you put in the time and effort, this class will be so interesting. Mass incarceration is a topic that everyone should know about, and Hudson is amazing at talking about it and teaching how to write about it. The class is a fair amount of work, but it was honestly not terrible. I loved the community in the class and the environment.
I'd like to tell future students that you should DEFINITELY take this course as your Expos 20 course. The workload is completely reasonable and manageable, more–so than I've heard from other Expos 20 courses. Dr. Vincent allows you to take time and work on each assignment carefully so that you can succeed in this class. There's also so many resources that can help you succeed in this course. As long as you turn everything in on time, meet with the professor, and follow the academic arguments, you will succeed.
RANK IT #1. Seriously, it is the absolute best Expos I could've asked for. The content is so interesting, and you're really pushed to think critically about the state of affairs we live in today. Hudson is very knowledgeable about the topic and writing, so he gives amazing feedback to make you better. He's also just really nice and understanding. There are also several guest talks with people who have different types of experience with the prison system, so it's very hands on. You will not regret this class, I promise.
This class is amazing! Hudson is very kind and nice, gives constructive feedback, and facilitates discussions really well. The class will provide you with lots of new insights and perspectives on mass incarceration and the prison system in the US. Hudson also invites really awesome and inspirational guest speakers, some of whom experienced mass incarceration firsthand. Their stories are very memorable and will change the way you think. We also had an opportunity to look at old archives, which was really cool. Hudson also provides lots of time for revisions, and gives detailed feedbacks on assignments.
ONE IMPORTANT CAVEAT. I identify myself as neither liberal nor conservative (I agree with each side more on different topics). I am strongly and passionately against mass incarceration and the racism in the prison system today, but I do NOT support complete prison abolition and I do NOT believe prisons are fully racist and does not do society any good. The views represented in this course do not all support complete abolition, but they are all liberal and some of them too much so for me and I sometimes find it awkward to express my views too vocally as I would probably be the only person with a different view (than Hudson or most other people in the class). This may be a reflection of Harvard in general but I do think the course can do better in incorporating conservative views as even if you disagree with them it is just fair to also give them a voice. Even though it is kinda awkward, I do still voice (and sometimes debate) what I believe and so far Hudson or my peers are kind and open and I do not see any negative impact on my grade (have not got final grade when writing this), but I sometimes do feel a bit isolated and during discussions I can quite easily sense Hudson tends to side more with the prison abolition views.
IN CONCLUSION, I rated this course excellent. Would highly recommend this course and I honestly think Hudson is a great instructor. Even if you are like me and not strongly liberal it is still worth taking.
HOWEVER, I am not even conservative and I sometimes feel quite isolated, so if you are a full fledged conservative I would probably not recommend this course unless (as I hope they do, as it is an important topic) they make some adjustments to make the course more balanced and at least give a more fair representation of conservative views.
TAKE THIS CLASS SERIOUSLY. This is not a class where you can or should try to just do the bare minimum. Really think about what you learn in this class and how you can apply it. Personally, I wish I took this class at a time when I was not taking 5 classes so that I could have that extra bit of time to put into my essays.
