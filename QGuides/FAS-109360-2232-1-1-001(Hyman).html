I think that this course is a pretty competent introduction to the field of neuroethics, which is incredibly rich, complex, and increasingly important in our world. The course covers a wide variety of neuroethical issues–– polygenetic risk scores, IVF, cosmetic pharmacology, drug addiction, personhood and autonomy, mental illness, and much more. This "width" in approach does not undermine the depth with which issues are explored. Especially as someone who is going to mainly do humanities/social sciences stuff for the next few years, I feel like I learned so much about neurobiology and have become even more interested in philosophy of mind and questions of agency and the self. However, although this is a Gen Ed, it seems to be taught in a way that assumes students have more background in biology and neuroscience than the prereqs on my.harvard. It's true that a lot of the students were bio and neuro concentrators, but that's no reason to tailor lectures and readings to their level of understanding just to keep them engaged when plenty of other students are just taking this course to see if they might have interest in those fields in the first place.
