This is an amazing class if you think you're interested in data science/you already know that and you wanna expand your skill set. Naijia created a great syllabus that introduced us to rigorous quantitative methods and explained them very clearly. The problem sets were moderately difficult, but the TF and CAs offered us a lot of support. At the end, we had a poster session and we presented our final projects. It was great hearing what my classmates were working on, which often doesn't happen in my other classes.
GOV 50 holds your hand completely through the course and so GOV 51 is more of a switch. GOV 50 used tidyverse while GOV 51 preferred Base R but tidyverse was also acceptable but the readings provided with the textbook were in Base R (the QSS book has both a tidyverse and base R version). I would recommend having a decent grasp on the materials learned from GOV 50 but it is not required as GOV 51 will go over it but it will be helpful to know. The midterm was too too long for the time period allotted (1 hour and 15 min) so I personally didn't finish it and which likely led to a lower grade in the course than what I wanted so I recommend practicing the coding tasks from textbooks and going to office hours when confused but overall I enjoyed the class. I will be taking more Data Analysis/Science related course either way as the class, Naijia & Jeremiah were both great as an instructor and TF.
GOV 51 is a great class! Naijia is such an awesome, patient professor –– she clearly cares a lot about the material and making it accessible to students. You cover a lot in this class –– at times it can feel a little too surface level –– but the benefit is that by the end you have some experience with a wide range of quantitative social science methods. The math is really reasonable –– Naijia and the TA did a great job of explaining the math repeatedly in class, section, and office hours.
It's very different than Gov 50 and more math based. Also the problem sets/exams are more interpretative than Gov 50. I love the teaching staff for this team and they are so patient. There isn't an expectation to master everything, but simply follow along in class and you'll do well.
Class is good, and the professors are very kind and good lecturers. This class is VITAL if you're doing data stuff in your career, academic or otherwise, as the mid–level techniques here are going to be important to go beyond typing in lm() and doing bootstrapping for fun. However, I will note that you should, if at all possible, have some stats knowledge and have taken Gov 50 to be familiar with R. The techniques here assume basic familiarity with either, and while stats knowledge isn't really vital to succeeding in the course, I think you'll get more out of it if you actually understand fundamentally what the techniques are doing beyond just cramming it into your head.

The problem sets are really good, and the midterm is very fair and reasonably graded. I also think that the final project is not that bad. It's not a gimme, but really, do you want this course to be a gimme, or do you wanna learn some stuff?
I LOVE THIS CLASS. It was totally manageable and your grade accurately reflects the work you put in. The teaching staff is also the kindest, most responsive, and intelligent out of like any class I've taken. I wish I could take more classes with Naijia and Jeremiah!
Take this course!!!! Professor Naija is such an amazing teacher, and she really really cares about her students! She is truly one of the best professors I've had at Harvard and is so welcoming and approachable! You will lean so much from this class! The beginning is definitely more difficult because it covers the most important statistical concepts, but they provide a lot of support to make sure students understand it well. The class has three PSETs, which are all very manageable. The final project was so much fun and I loved working with my group! Truly an amazing class! You will not regret taking it!
Before coming into GOV 51, I was set on concentrating in Government under the Data Science track. Now, I am leaving this course as a joint concentrator in Statistics (primary) and Government (Allied). Professor Naijia is just that good!!! I highly recommend going to her office hours for PSET questions or academic advice. She's a great teacher and even better person to meet during your Harvard experience. If you're interested in the quantitative methods of political science, I highly recommend taking this course!
Given previous years responses, it seems like this year the department decided to change Gov51 quite a bit. I will not lie, it is not an easy class, but I believe that given this year was so different from previous years I feel like this class is extremely valuable to anyone wishing to learn more about quantitative social science. All of the course staff were amazing, the class was very informative, and I feel like I have come out of this course with so much knowledge that I would highly recommend this course in the coming years. That being said, I expect they will change some things in the coming year because the transition from Gov50 to this was a little rough (the switch to Base–R instead of tidyverse combined with the intensive statistical teaching was hard to adjust to). Regardless, great class, great staff, I would not hesitate to go back in time and take this course again.
