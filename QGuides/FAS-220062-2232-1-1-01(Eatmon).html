Comments
This is an excellent course. While Professor Eatmon can be strict, it was a good learning environment – she did a good job of making space for everyone to speak. For me personally, having assignments and deadlines clearly laid out was very helpful, but others could find it restricting. A lot of really interesting content is covered and it's an accessible class to those who don't have background in history. The lectures are great and she really cares about her students. Also, Andrew was an amazing TF.
Professor Eatmon's teaching was incredible. It never felt like she was just lecturing rather she was promoting discussions. She exhibited great intellect in the subject matter and always encouraged students to delve deeper. A great professor who I am honored to have met and learn from. Materials were carefully curated to ensure amazing discussions. It was evident that a lot of thought and consideration was placed on the materials. Professor Eatmon gave amazing feedback and even adjusted classes accordingly in accordance to the class' feedback to improve everyone's learning experience. The material and lectures were incredible. I would highly, highly recommend taking this course if you are even interested in this subject.
If this class even slightly interests you, I recommend you take it! Through expertly selected course readings and materials, this course does a masterful job of exposing the trends and patterns that define the Black American experience all the way from the Civil War until the present. As a cherry on top, the professor is an excellent lecturer and is super accommodating!
