Start preparing for exams early
If you have a solid background in French, this is a fun course that will let you practice your language skills a few times a week and watch some good movies. The main drawback here is that the teaching staff is super disorganized.
I honestly really enjoyed the film component of the class despite having no prior film background, and assignments were pretty easy and well–paced. However, it wasn't always clear what/when things were due, which I hope they improve in future iterations of the course.
Take it! You'll improve your French and have fun in the process.
Madame Brisson is a saint
This class feels a lot like learning about movies in French as opposed to learning French through cinema. An interest in analyzing aspects of film along with their intended messages will likely make your life a lot easier taking this course.
The cahier is a little unclear. Make sure to check in multiple places to clarify what your homework is.
The time management and class environment made this class unnecessarily stressful:

The TF forgot that a quiz was coming up; they did not inform the class, so students only had two days to prepare. It had a notable impact on how well people did on the quiz; the Professor had to come in to offer people a chance to retake the quiz.

At one point, some students were struggling to understand what was being said to them in French, and how the TF expected them to respond. After 15 minutes of trying to converse, the TF essentially threw their hands up in frustration and began speaking a bit harshly to the student struggling with the content. The rest of the class sat just watching the back–and–forth, with some people laughing; it was uncomfortable. Basically, it felt like there was a clear division between favorite students and ones that would either receive frustration or laughs (sometimes even from the TF) because they weren't speaking as fluently.

Even the Professor and TF couldn't keep up with the schedule set in the syllabus, and this had a serious impact on grades and learning. The syllabus said that students would get a chance to turn in a second draft of their papers in order to work on what they struggled with and to receive some points back, but the class couldn't manage to get through everything that was originally scheduled; we didn't get a chance to write any second drafts (or work on what we struggled with, or receive points back...) The Professor had to drop an entire quiz because time was running out, which was nice because quizzes aren't fun, but it was still indicative of how poor the time management was.
