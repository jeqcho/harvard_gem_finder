this course is very easy. push yourself beyond what the course demands
Only take this class if you have to for a requirement. The organization of the lectures and sections leaves much to be desired, and the content is not that different to the stuff you have already learnt in Music 51A. If you have to take it for a Music concentration/secondary requirement, then go ahead, but test out if you can.
Solid class. Our final project was awesome––we composed a string quartet and it was played by the Parker Quartet during our last class. Class time could be used much more efficiently and sections could be run much better but the teaching staff is knowledgeable. The small number of students in the class creates a fun atmosphere where your questions can be answered quickly.
** If you're on the edge, take it! It is not a huge commitment, but you will learn a LOT about music, art, and yourself. The TFs are very chill and the assignments don't take a whole lot of time, and you will be close to the people in the class by the end of it. The final project is an amazing experience; you get to hear your own composition be performed by professionals.
Not going to lie, I kind of regretted taking this course. I just don't think it's the right kind of class for me. If you took music 51a, this class is not really any more interesting or fun than that class. If you loved 51a, you will probably love this class, if you didn't I doubt you will really like this class (that was me). If you didn't take 51a and know a decent amount of music theory, I think you should take this course, just to give music theory at Harvard a try. It's not miserable, but it's not amazing. One warning is that the lectures are kind of convoluted. His teaching style isn't the most organized or clear, so I would often times have to try to relearn everything from the handouts he gave us. Another warning is that this is not a composition class. The clear focus is on analyzing music, not writing it. Many of the skills cross over, but just keep in mind that composing is not what the course is meant to teach you. I found this frustrating at times, because I don't like analyzing music, but if you enjoy analyzing music maybe you're chilling. Homework isn't that bad, maybe 2–3 hours a week, plus the two projects. The last project is a three minute composition , which I really think we should spend more time on workshopping at the end of the semester to get those pieces as good as possible, but like I said, this isn't a composition class. This semester the professor tried integrating some concepts that were not just western classical music, which was really cool and I respect the idea, but I don't think it was executed that well, but I bet next time he'll know how to teach it better. Don't let this negative review deter you, just know that it's not for everyone. A lot of people I think really did like this class.
