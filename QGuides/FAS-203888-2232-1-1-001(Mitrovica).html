this is a great intorductory class and very easy for someone with no earth science experience to understand. there are 4 labs and 3 site vistis, as well as an optional field trip.
Jerry is an amazing professor he always motivated his students was understanding of each of their situations always told jokes in class and I have found very few people who care about their students but Jerry is one of those rare gems. He so kind and I truly at Harvard have not found a professor who dedicated as much time and effort to his students like Jerry does. Building up to finals he had 3 days of virtual office hours each 3–4 hours, had an open door every morning before class, often provided students with snacks, and does not expect you to be passionate or care about the subject because he knows most students do it as a requirement. But truly if I had to take this course over again I would only take it with Jerry. He’s a kind man and a fantastic professor.
It was evident that Professor Mitrovica and his teaching team cared deeply about the success of each and every student. They made themselves available to answer every one of our questions: the Prof. held office hours late into the night before exams to ensure that we all felt comfortable with the material, the TFs were happy to dive deeper into the material one on one during section, and they were all responsive over email and after lectures. Lectures were FUN... the Prof. mixed interesting lecture material with hilarious anecdotes to create an exciting learning experience that I looked forward to every week. The course surveyed a long list of very relevant topics, ancient and modern. I look at the world around me differently now, and am more comfortable engaging in conversations about climate change. Professor Mitrovica encouraged us, challenged us, welcomed us.... he became a friend. He practically begged us to come introduce ourselves at his office hours... which were held at all hours (i.e., an open door policy). I've heard that he won't be teaching the course again... he's a big reason why it was my favorite so far at Harvard, but it was definitely worth taking for the material alone.
Jerry is a wonderful person who really makes an effort to get to know students. There aren't many assignments in the course, which is a plus. The downside is that exams (especially the final) make up a large portion of your grade, and it's difficult to study because of the amount of content covered throughout the semester.
Jerry is a very kind professor who is dedicated to helping students succeed in this class. As a humanities concentrator, I was hesitant at the beginning, but Jerry did a great job at presenting the content in a way that allowed me to perform well in lab/tests. He does go off into tangents sometimes, but that's just part of the charm of the class!
This is a great class! You will learn the basics of earth science in a fun and engaging way, no background in EPS is necessary. Would highly recommend!
The best class I've taken so far!!!! This class was an absolute joy. I loved the content even though most of it was brand new to me, Jerry is so passionate about the topic and very patient in answering all of our questions, and I loved all the assignments I had to do for the class. It wasn't very time consuming either, and while you could probably get through it by doing the minimum, I think Jerry's enthusiasm about the material really inspires you to try hard and do well. Overall I loved this class and will definitely be taking more EPSCI classes in the future. If you need a fourth class like I did, take this!
