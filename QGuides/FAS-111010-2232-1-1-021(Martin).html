Even having had integral calculus before, I was a bit nervous when I enrolled in Math 1b, as I had the impression that it was infamous for its difficulty. However, once I got into it, I did not find the course to be significantly more difficult than any other, and it was definitely comparable to Math 1a. Perhaps I just think this because I love math, but although the course certainly challenged me, it did so in all the right ways, and I did not feel as if I spent more time or energy on it than I should have. Even when things did get a bit more difficult, office hours and the MQC were always there. In short, there is no reason to be afraid of this course. The teaching faculty are there to help you succeed, and you will likely gain a fresh appreciation for mathematics through the course, even if you never take another math class.
Math 1b is a good course, the TFs are available to help you in and outside of class.
If you've taken Math 1a before, it won't be that much of an adjustment – still 3 psets a week and pretty much the same amount of work except now you have two big midterms + a cumulative final exam. By the time you get to the final, you'll realize you don't remember how to do anything you learned before, so make sure you study ahead of time. Other than that, it's your average Harvard math class.

You wont regret taking this class, and may even discover that you actually do like math!
