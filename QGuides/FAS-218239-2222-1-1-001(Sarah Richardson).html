Have fun in the course! This felt like a poorly taught intro to history of science and intro to WGS class rolled into one. Syllabus is outdated and very Euro/Anglo–centric, professor is unreceptive to constructive feedback about the course materials, and assignments are mostly tedious busywork. Take HS100 for a much better introduction on how to think about history critically and check WGS course syllabi for something more diverse and updated for a much better experience.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Richardson is absolutely brilliant, knowledgeable, thoughtful, and dedicated. She is genuinely one of the best, if not the best, professors I have ever had, and I say that as a graduating senior. Her lectures are so eye–opening and accessible to all backgrounds, and at every lecture I was blown away by what I learned. She teaches so eloquently and clearly, and my attention was hooked at every lecture. Please take this class regardless of your background and identity, especially men–– all of the male students thoroughly enjoyed the lectures and information as well and the teaching team is so welcoming and knowledgeable. In fact, I highly recommended this class to all of my male STEM friends because the history of gender and science is very applicable to them as well. The assignments were graded very reasonably and sections were great. Taking this class was honestly the best decision of my senior spring because I got to learn so much about the history of gender and science in a very low–stress, accommodating environment. After taking this class, I now am most likely going to continue studying the social sciences in the future. PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!
Fairly chill gened. Lectures are interesting, though sections can be a bit boring (but that's not really the fault of the course design or the TF imo –– I think generally gened section discussions have low–energy because many students are only in the class to fulfill a gened requirement, not because they are passionate about the course content). Reading can be a lot for a gened, but you'll be fine if you skip/skim some of them. Grading on assignments is generous.
This is a fairly easy class but only if you help yourself. You only have to complete 2 papers and theyre not too difficult but make sure to start on time because theres a lot of finicky bits you have to include. Youll have to attend course events which are interesting but somewhat annoying because they take up extra time outside of section and lecture. Because this course also counts for concentration credit, I thought the grading was a little harsher than that of other geneds, so keep that in mind when deciding how much effort to put into your assignments. The final project is a lot of work but the presentation fair makes it all seem worth, super cool to see what everyone comes up with! All around great class to take, especially if youre a woman concentrating in STEM, but be prepared to do a bit more work than in a gem. Again, this class isnt that hard, Im just lazy and so are most people
