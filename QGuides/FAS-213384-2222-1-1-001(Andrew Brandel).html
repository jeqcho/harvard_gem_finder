It is important to meet with the teaching staff earlier in the semester, so that they can help you figure out what you are passionate about! They are there to guide you as you navigate your thesis journey!
I think the course was a great way to learn about different social sciences and the way knowledge is produced — and as such has made me a more critical reader and consumer of knowledge. However, if you really want to learn about one method (and how to do it well), I would recommend a methods course in a different discipline, especially if you are thinking about doing something quantitative.
If you've taken a Social Studies Junior Tutorial or another class where you had to write a research paper and think critically about methods, this class was very repetitive and intuitive.
This is a really great methods course that actually helps you get ready for your thesis! The teaching staff is great and the topics covered are super varied, they don't just cover the typical things, so also super interesting! This course did actually make me change up my plans for what methods to use, as I was exposed to new methods and found different ones fit my thesis! Would definitely recommend!
Social Studies 50 doesn't have a great reputation amongst concentrators, mostly because it's supposed to be a bit unhelpful. This course wasn't like that! Andrew is a great professor and really worked to make this course more interesting for the students. The readings are fantastic and the mini–assignments are actually super helpful. My advice: don't write this course off, regardless of what you've heard – I actually found it one of my favorite classes this semester. You can learn a lot from it, if you're willing to put in some effort.
Think deeply about what methods you’re interested in before the course — that will help you stay rooted as you explore the different methods you’re taught. The teaching staff are there to help you with not only the course materials but also with the other aspects of thesising or Social Studies life, such as finding an advisor or searching for grants. Be grateful for them!
Andrew makes the class enjoyable even though most people take this just as a Social Studies requirement.
Be sure to do the readings and think more about what you want to do with your research. Get the mini assignments out of the way early so you don't regret it later in the semester but also do not do anything that isn't relevant to your research.
This class can be very easy and boring if you want it to be. However, it can also be extremely informative and challenge your perceptions of research and academia, if you choose it to be. I would recommend the latter and also starting assignments ahead of time.
A pretty chill class that gives you a very broad overview of many methods. Felt like it focused a lot more on certain fields than others (ethnography, anthropology, etc.), but still a useful class.
Be sure to take this class during your junior year, not any earlier. The class is oriented around helping you develop your senior thesis. If you are a sophomore and you are not planning your senior thesis, you will not get nearly as much out of this course. I took this course my junior year, and it was really helpful as I prepare for summer research.
Unless this course changes substantially next year or you actually care about the material, don't worry about the readings.
If you want to write a thesis that does any sort of quantitative analysis, take econometrics instead.
Try not to make the work busy work, instead, actually complete the assignments in a way that contributes to your thesis
I would tell them to take this for SOC methodology course!
