I took this class with Professor Mansfield the last time he taught it. I know he teaches it in a very different way from other professors, so I am not sure how much that experience will help decide whether to take it with another professor. That being said, I can say a couple of general thoughts. It is A LOT of reading. It helps to be a fast reader, which I am not, so do keep that in mind. Of course, you can definitely survive without doing all the readings; I definitely did. But you do have to put in effort to do most of the readings for at least 3 of the thinkers, to be successful in essays and the final test (for which you should really do as much of the readings as possible). Go to lecture, take good notes. Go to section, participate and ask questions. All of that will help, even though it is not strictly necessary. If you take this as an elective, try to get the most out of it. That means being engaged. The essays are essentially just about summarizing some idea of one of the thinkers you read, adding your own spin on it. They are not argumentative; they are mostly expository. The final test is very hard but definitely doable if you do as many of the readings as possible.
