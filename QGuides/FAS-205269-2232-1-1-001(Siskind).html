What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
A wonderful course, which will radically change the ways you read and think about migration issues.
If you are at all interested in the role that the humanities and art can play in the global field of migration, this is the course for you. Homi and Mariano are such great professors who lead you through a staggering range of fascinating materials. Some of the reading is quite challenging (stylistically, thematically, or emotionally) but the resulting discussions are always edifying and so much fun.
The material is interesting and the course is informative, but it's a bit difficult to receive feedback during the course and engage in a class discussion. The class was far too large for the room, and sometimes it felt difficult to contribute to the discussion as there were not many openings offered by the professors. A lot of the time it felt like the discussions were conducted between the professors with some openings for students to chime in here and there.
I would tell them to read everything
Be ready to read a lot, but the course materials are incredibly illuminating and were selected with the utmost care.
