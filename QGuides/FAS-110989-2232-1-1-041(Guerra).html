This is an INCREDIBLE math class. I loved this course so, so much more than 21a, but I acknowledge that I was an anomaly in this assessment. I always found it useful to have background when reading Q–reports, so this is my math history: I hated math in high school and never thought I was good at it. I took Alg2 freshman year, Precalc sophomore year, BC junior year, and linear + multi senior year. Harvard fall I took 21a, and then in the spring I took 21b. Math21b is a truly incredible course. It is so much more interesting than Math21a, and although it may be a bit harder, it is so much more rewarding. This class will be a grind â€” I regularly took 3–4 hours per pset, and there are 3 psets per week. In addition, there are weekly 1–hour quiz sessions. The midterm practices are seemingly impossible, but the actual exams are easier. The final was really quite difficult and similar in difficulty to the practice exams. Despite all this, though, I highly recommend this class! You'll likely have to take it anyway if you're AM or CS, but I'd recommend taking this over 22a. It's taught in section, and you get practice with computational skill and theoretical proofs.
So I am a FGLI student that went into this class with a Math 1b background. The psets are heavier than 1b and the m series for sure, but they are not impossible if you start them right after class. This class has a LOT of content so there really was never time to revisit concepts from classes prior unless there was an intentional connection, so if you are lost you should spend time with it immediately (go to quiz session, watch the pre–work video, read the handout/textbook reading). I have never struggled in math and passed calculus with flying colors, and I could pass my past math exams without studying (psets and going to class were enough), BUT THIS CLASS was where I met my match. I wouldn't say it is hard in terms of being overworked but studying for this class is completely different than studying for calculus, because I'm being so serious I have studied like I never have before and I nor any of my friends really saw their effort translate into their performance on exams. I failed both midterms, and even though I studied exponentially harder for the second one, i literally did worse. The exams are extremely different from the material you see in class, but you are not necessarily being tested on something you weren't taught. Only someone who truly understands conceptually is able to come up with the solutions. What's worse is that there is limited time on exams, so honestly there are problems that I know I would've figured out had I been given more time. I would say course staff is available for sure though and amazing (which is weird because idk most times at harvard when ur struggling its not you its the instructor). I would say that instructors, at least jill, teach you like a college student and don't necessarily say things in the most simplest terms, so you are definitely challenged with understanding jargon and notation and stuffs. Overall, I really liked this class and I would recommend linear algebra broadly because I strongly appreciate its applications. I have never appreciated math the way I have appreciate linear algebra, and even though this was a challenge, I would do it again. I would say If i could do it again I might actually watch the pre–work videos and do more readings throughout, perhaps then I would have built enough intuition to do well on exams. I would have also started going to quiz sessions earlier on ( i strongly recommend these). Go to quiz sessions because those drops add up, AND it is a super efficient way to revisit material you need help on. Trust me it is a blessing. Anyway good luck! Esp to the fyreflies <3. A note about pre–reqs: The only thing need from 1b is integration by parts, a basic understanding of series (none of the geometric stuff ust be able to know what it means at a high level lol), and derivatives obviously. Also, know your limits! But yea good luck fyre flies!
I'm a computer science concentrator and I took both 21a and 21b. Although I did enjoy both classes (21b more than 21a), I really don't think 21a is necessary for 21b. However, if you took/take 21a first, it will be easier for you to understand some concepts in 21b like the ones that involve partial derivatives and integrals.

21b really builds a lot on itself, more than 21a did which was more about knowing how to do mathematical calculations in my opinion. 21b is more about knowing how to apply past concepts to other concepts to problem solve, which I think requires a bit more intuition than other math courses. However, you will be provided with a lot of support in office hours and through your TF, so you shouldn't worry about it. P–sets are pretty long, and 3 a week, so make sure you make time for these. It took me from 2 to 5 hours to complete each p–set. The two midterms are tough, but if you do plenty of practice exams and go over your notes (take notes, it's very helpful!) then you will be completely fine.
