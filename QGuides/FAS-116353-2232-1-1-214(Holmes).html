If you're at all interested, rank Climate Fictions highly! It was my first choice and did not disappoint. I found the readings and course content both manageable and interesting, and Eliza is absolutely amazing. Writing doesn't come super naturally to me, so this class was one of my more time–consuming this semester (6–7+ hours during essay weeks)––but I don't think it would have been less so in any other Expos class. If you are at all able, go to office hours! They really helped me get a better sense of what was expected for the assignment and how to organize my thoughts, especially since the pace of Expos in general tends to be faster than high school writing classes.
Take Climate Fictions!! Eliza is wonderful, kind, and provides really helpful feedback on your drafts so I always knew how to improve my final draft. Especially if you're interested in environmentalism and climate change, the readings are so interesting and widened my understanding of climate art a lot. I think the professor can make a huge difference in your Expos experience, and I would totally recommend taking Climate Fictions with Eliza. It was my favorite class this semester.
Overall, I think this is one of the better Expos sections to take. Eliza is very chill and nice to talk to, and if you're at all interested in climate change (it doesn't have to be a super strong interest), learning about climate fiction is really cool. Class discussions were probably as fun as they could be. I'm not professing to have particularly enjoyed Expos, and it's not very nice that it's a requirement, but once you accept that, Climate Fictions was not too bad.
This class will force you to rethink your writing style and spend some time workshopping drafts and arguments, but you will come out on the other side as a much better writer.
I would totally recommend this expos for any students that are passionate about the environment and climate change! It really changed my perspective on the state of the climate and climate art. I am not really an English/writing person, but I want to pursue conservation/bio in the future, and I was still able to write about topics that I was truly passionate about.
This class is really interesting and Eliza is a great teacher for expos, but just get ready for some readings that may be a bit boring – they are still really helpful for understanding the course.
Eliza is so lovely and I really enjoyed my experience in this class
Eliza is a great teacher and awesome person, and as far as expos classes go, this one is very good. Would absolutely recommend.
