*** TAKE THIS CLASS AND TAKE IT TWICE. *** This course not only offers a deep dive into inhibition in the brain and GABA–mediated phenomena, but will provide students with essential skills for moving forward in academia whether to medical school, industry, or otherwise. You will leave this course obsessed with inhibition – I even chose to include aligned research in my senior thesis because of how much I loved and learned from this tutorial. Saad is an incredible and kind instructor with undeniable expertise who is always glad to provide guidance and flexibility while serving as an encyclopedia of neuroscientific knowledge. This course will allow students to build skills in reviewing literature, critiquing and "killing" papers through peer review, paper writing, presenting, writing figure legends, and more in a relatively low stakes course structure. Other neuro tutorials won't be nearly as awesome; assignments are LOW STRESS, very spaced out, and not time consuming aside from one longer paper the entire year. Best course I have taken at Harvard – don't miss out on this one!
Best class I have taken at Harvard hands down. Saad, the course head is incredible, supportive, kind and a great resource inside and outside of the class. The class has a perfect balance of learning the fundamentals of inhibition in the brain and reading scientific papers. It is also not much work but you learn so much!!
This was one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. Saad is both incredibly knowledgeable and a fantastic teacher, which makes each class fun but also intellectually rewarding. The workload is not tedious but does demand that you think deeply about the subject. Due to the small nature of the class, we all became close as a group as well. Of all the neuroscience courses at Harvard, this one taught me the most about fundamental neuroscience concepts, diseases, and how to read papers in general.
