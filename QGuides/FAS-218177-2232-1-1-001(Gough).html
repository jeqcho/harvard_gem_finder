What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You should absolutely take this class! It is one of the most theoretically provocative courses being offered.
Overall, I learned a lot in this class and it was very cool to interface with work that we were learning about in person each week. I gained substantial knowledge about 20th century weaving and textiles within the Modernist realm, and I will certainly take that with me! My reservations about this course relate to Professor Gough. She loves the sound of her own voice, and she loves interrupting students when they speak. Rarely did an individual get a full comment out without being bulldozed by Gough, sometimes being discounted or immediately undermined. This did not foster an easy, flowing conversation or group dynamic. In addition, Gough made us do weekly 2–page reading responses that purely *summarized* the readings (often over 100 pages), rather than just 'responses', with no breaks throughout the whole semester. This class will ask you for a good amount of time each week, so take that into consideration.
A very rewarding class that gives you a good foundational understanding of many modernist, female artists that are often overlooked. Professor Gough gives you very thorough feedback and spaces out the work for the final paper in a very manageable way. I loved seeing the artworks in person each week and Professor Gough also is a wonderfully clear and articulate lecturer.
This course requires care and attention, as Prof. Gough is smart and tough–––she expects you to care about these subjects as much as herself and the other students. I recommend looking out for your favorite styles or artists in local museums for your final paper early.
