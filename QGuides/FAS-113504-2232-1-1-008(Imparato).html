What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are taking GOV 97, definitely rank Yi Ning's section first. She ran an excellent class that hits everything you want from a gov course: genuinely interesting readings and discussion, freedom in your research with very useful and actionable feedback, and no busy work. Everything had purpose and was useful. Yi Ning is a phenomenal teacher and section leader. You will learn so much and enjoy the process!!
Take the Southeast Asia and Postcolonial Theory section if it's offered!
This class is a requirement for gov concentrators. It is not too bad but it is not the easiest class ever, so perhaps factor that in when balancing with other courses.
This is a required class so you don't really have a choice about taking it but sections do influence your experience. Try to learn as much as you can about the different section leaders and their syllabi ahead of time. My biggest struggle in my section was the sheer about of reading. In terms of assignments, my section leader was very tough but she also gave good feedback.
Your experience in a Gov 97 will definitely depend on whose tutorial you are in and what the topic is. I personally really enjoyed both my tutorial leader and the topic we were reading about,. As a whole, the sophomore tutorial structure is a little odd and unhelpful, in terms of developing your skills as a political thinker. The lectures felt underwhelming at times. While I understand the need to mandate attendance and have some form of accountability, the response papers felt unnecessary, especially given that the topics weren't necessarily essential to an understanding of political science. I think they would've been better off either using them to demonstrate skills and topics that are important to gov or pitch it as a research symposium where we can hear about the interesting work being done in the field.
I would advise starting the readings early and regularly consulting with your TF on the paper! Also office hours before a section were a great help in making sure you were prepared to discuss the content.
