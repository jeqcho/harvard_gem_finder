Class is well run with lots of support resources. It is fair amount of work but you'll learn so much. And nothing feels like busy work. All good stuff to know. Go to MQC and office hours.
This is a very interesting class and will be very engaging but the 3 psets a week can be tough to manage if you fall behind at all.
This class is terrible. Exams are so difficult. Best of luck.
What most people say about this class is true: the material is not trivial, 3 psets a week is brutal, and the tests are pretty hard. That being said, if you are able to stay on top of the psets (start them the day they are assigned), it is manageable and teaches you a lot of math! It was rewarding to look back at how much I learned and how much more comfortable I feel with matrices. Overall, it is just a grind that you have to get through, but it definitely has not been the worst or hardest class I've taken; it can just get annoying.
I would say that this class is very challenging, so prepare to do a lot of work for it. I would also say that the hardest part for me was the exams, so I would recommend preparing for those very thoroughly.
Math 21b is quite difficult, as it requires making connections between topics that don't appear on the surface. In other words, it requires grinding. Lots of it. If you do that, it is like any other math course at Harvard.
If you are interested in learning linear algebra, I'd most likely take 22a because linear algebra is best taught through proofs. However, 21b is also an excellent class that isn't as tough, so I'd take this if you need to take a linear algebra class.
It’s a lot of work but very worth it!
Amazing class, take it!
This class has very difficult material, but what makes it such an impossible class is that the workload is unfeasible! They expect you to turn in three PSETs a week with no late hours, then all 35 PSETs only make up a small fraction of your final grade. This class is unreasonable in all aspects, also expecting attendance three times a week, while only making up a tiny part of your grade. While the CAs were amazing and supportive and kind, it does not feel like higher course staff goes out of their way to support students or actively listen to students’. concerns over difficulty and rigor of the course load.
Go to MQC and stay on top of your psets! Otherwise, you'll fall behind.

I would highly recommend the course! It may be a bit more work than Math 22 given its three psets a week, but it is definitely manageable and taught very well. It will give you a very solid foundation. While it does not do proofs like 22, from what I have heard it goes into a few extra topics that 22 does not cover or at least goes into more depth on some things.
This class feels like the definition of "what was taught in class vs what was on the test" except relating to the psets. Even if you understand a concept fairly well the psets will be out to get you and you'll probably find yourself in office hours or mqc whether you like it or not.
