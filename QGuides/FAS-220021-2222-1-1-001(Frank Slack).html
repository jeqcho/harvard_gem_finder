I would tell other students that there will be weeks you are super into the papers and other weeks maybe not, however, you will get something out of the class and will become skilled in reading literature.
This class will only be helpful if you are willing to participate in the group discussion!
This class is helpful for learning foundational research in the field of molecular biology and for learning to analyze papers more deeply.
As long as you read the papers before class, you will learn a lot from faculty discussions. Don't be afraid to ask questions about parts of the papers that confused you during these discussions – their purpose is to help you.
For future students, I highly recommend reading the papers twice. Don't try to understand every single detail the first time, because there are so many outdated techniques and hand–wavy arguments that it is very difficult to get everything the first time through. Skim once, wait a day, then go to the figures and make sure you can explain at least a few of them. Then you'll be ready for discussing with the faculty.
