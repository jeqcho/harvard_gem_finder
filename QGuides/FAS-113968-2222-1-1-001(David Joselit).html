I think it's a great introduction to AFVS, but it should be taken alongside another class that is more to do with the particular area of focus you have chosen. There is no studio component to this class, and more than anything it's an intro class about theory.
Do the readings, its worth it and go to office hours to get applicable discussions
Only take this class if you have to. The professor is great and the survey lectures are helpful, but the class is not well structured in terms of developing your practice.
If you care about learning about art history take an actual art history class. You won't learn accurate information about underrepresented histories such as the women and people of color during surrealism– i.e. Frida Kahlo, Valentine Hugo, etc. The professor actually said "they were't there" during that time of history when a student specifically asked about it. Expect to be berated with art that objectifies women and is then discussed as how it's wrong that this was the thinking at the time or how women used it to empower themselves in later time periods. Would have rather not taken this class but it's required. I didn't speak my opinion to avoid retaliation. The professor immediately overwhelms students with a very long, fast–paced speech if they question his methods.

On the plus side the assignments are easy and I stopped doing the readings after lost respect for the course. The choice of readings themselves is terrible as well. Don't take the class seriously if Joselit is teaching again.
While the lectures cover interesting material, this is not a good course if you are primarily a filmmaker. It is, however, a required course for the concentration.
you have to take it for department requirement. you will hate it, but it’s 2.5 hours a week of low effort pain.
I was really dissatisfied with this course as the sophomore tutorial for AFVS. I thought too much of the emphasis was on lecture and theory and really dense readings that could be summed up by secondary sources instead of dense primary sources—I just want to learn more about art, not read and analyze text like an English or history class. The assignments were cool but super infrequent and super vague, and while some people did a really cool job when we shared, some people didn't buy in as much which was less enjoyable. I was also really dissatisfied that so much of this content was focused on studio art and essentially way less or even nothing on film and photography and the visual studies element of AFVS, and because of that I felt like my studies were less represented in the class. It also had way too much lecture and less discussion among smaller groups so I feel like I got to know other people in the class less than I would have liked as fellow concentrators. Professor Joselit was very nice but I thought his background as more of a historian and curator and not a maker showed a little too much in the content of the course.
