What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is one of the most incredible courses I've taken. You learn so much very quickly; I think in one week of this course I learned more than I did a semester in high school or even in my history course at Harvard I took prior. There are a lot of readings and if you don't have knowledge already on Soviet history, expect that this class will consume a lot of your life. I spent almost all of Friday–Sunday just focused on this course and really understanding the readings with a lot of annotations. The weekly one page papers are really fun to write and allow you to connect your personal interests to the topics. If you take this course, be prepared for an active discussion in the class with a lot of counter factuals and thinking on your feet. The readings are a lot––200–300 pages of very dense sources, about 7 primary sources and 7 secondary source excerpts each week.

You cover a lot of material across a lot of lenses, but all of it is focused on a single policy of the Soviet government––the nationalities policy. You will thoroughly understand this policy, but it is a singular policy, so I would recommend this class if you are the person who likes to go into depth into one topic. Every reading came back to this policy. However, you will cover political, literary, cultural, linguistic, art history lenses of this policy so you will learn to speak to a lot of disciplines.

There is a 5 page book report and oral presentation which gives a lot of good practice in verbal communication. Professor Martin has vey high expectations that he will hold you to. It was roughly half undergrad/grad. He reads your work very very carefully and provides specific feedback that helps you grow as a historian, writer, and presenter, but as such, be prepared to put a lot into this class, as he will expect that and demand that, but you will get a lot out. There is a roughly 15 page paper for a final which is very flexible and illustrates how you will get to pursue your interests in this class. You will speak with him 1:1 and he is very responsive to make sure that you are set up for success and have enough access to resources. but all of these require a lot of proactivity and initiative! I even emailed him about something he casually mentioned in class and he gave me databases and resources to explore on my own. This class requires you to have a genuine enthusiasm and interest to drive that proactivity!!

In short, I highly recommend this class, but you have to have a strong love of the subject matter and learning. Professor Martin is my favorite professor so far and puts a lot into this class and will ensure you grow as a scholar. I am very grateful for every moment of this class which I will treasure and I expect this class to have formed some of the most special moments of my undergraduate career.
Good class to take if interested in the intersection of nationality and the Soviet Union.
This is a great class if you have an interest in the inner workings of the Soviet Union and want to learn more – Professor Martin is pretty much unrivaled in the field and can help in any way. However, dont take the class if you already have a busy schedule – there are ALOT of readings thatll take a significant chunk of time.
