This course challenges your assumptions about natural climate solutions, so if you take it be prepared to reconsider your prior thinking. If you're able to come in with an open mind, there's lots to learn. The course illuminates many of the flaws and challenges of natural climate solutions, so the general tone is more negative than positive and you'll likely need to grapple with the concepts to find your own silver lining.
I would not recommend this class unless you are very interested in natural climate solutions. The readings and discussions were overall interesting, but in my opinion, Dan Schrag's negative view of NCS was stifling. I heard from peers that they also felt that the class environment revolved around Dan's viewpoints and left little room for disagreement. Also, there is very little feedback in the course.
Missy Holbrook seems like a great professor, though. I wish she was more involved in teaching the course.
I think things ended up being fine, but I personally did not enjoy this course.
The discussions were great and in general really made me think more in depth about how to read scientific studies critically. If you want to learn how to read a scientific article critically and not except what it says at face value and instead question everything, the assumptions made, who sponsored the research, etc this class is super helpful. I am not ESPP and I still feel like the skills I learned are going to help me in other classes. That being said, you really get out what you put in to the class. You can get away with skimming the readings but then you risk getting called out in the discussion for a stupid opinion. Very cool thought provoking small sized class, so it felt like we were all in it together. Prepare to have your thoughts and assumptions pushed by Schrag, he really pushes you to think critically and defend your statements and I really loved having him as a professor.
I would say the overall discourse of NCS being "feasible or a fantasy" was definitely on the fantasy side of things. I rarely left the class feeling hopeful about future climate mitigation strategies incorporating NCS. I would not consider this course to be intersectional; when students attempted to bring up intersectional topics, the conversations were almost always shifted or blatantly ignored. If you are looking for a class to better your critical analysis of scientific research papers, I would softly recommend this seminar. Otherwise, another seminar might be best. The class has a very light workload and doesn't require any background. I would recommend taking any class taught by Dr. Holbrook, though!
Really interesting class with phenomnal course direction and classroom leading. Shrag and Missy act as great resources for you to get a lot out of the course – phenomenal people.
Missy and Dan are very open to discussion and this is a class to learn and challenge pre–concieved notions you might have had about climate change and nature based climate solutions. I recommend this class if you're interested in terrestrial carbon uptake and it also gives you a good basis in carbon offsets.
This class is super informative but as most classes centered around environmental issues it can leave you with a heavy and depressed feeling after class. This is because of the severity of the issues and the discussions focused on all of the issues within the solutions. The class itself is very informative and a way to connect with like–minded individuals and very knowledgable professors.
Daniel Schrag gives terrible feedback and is generally mean–spirited. I would have enjoyed this class with just Holbrook, but Daniel truly made it a boring class
This is a great class to expose you to some potential solutions to climate change and analyze the practicality of them.
This class will really help you think about the policy and actions that must be taken to combat climate change! It is great to be able to discuss these topics with your peers and can give you a totally new perspective.
