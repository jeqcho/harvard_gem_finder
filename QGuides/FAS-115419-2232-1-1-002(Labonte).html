This can serve as a fifth class. It's a lot of busy work and time in class but it's manageable work! Do not stress.
There is a limit to how good a sophomore tutorial can be, and this one is ok. It's not too much work or too hard, and during the working like a historian weeks you often don't have to go to class which is really nice. The sharing sessions are really chill, as are the presentations. The written assignments can be graded pretty strictly but my TF gave really good (actionable!) feedback which I felt like improved my writing/ understanding of history over the course of the semester. I do kind of wish I emerged from the tutorial understanding the major works/ ideas in hist sci better, but the course is not really trying to do that. Don't expect this course to be the highlight of your semester or anything, but it's not bad either.
This course was not my favorite, but it is important for the History of Science concentration. I recommend spending time on finding research topics that interest you in order to make the assignments engaging, as they can feel repetitive at times.
Overall, this course is fine. There are some interesting case studies we will explore and the final prospectus will allow us to delve deeply into a topic of interest. I'm not sure how much my writing abilities improved through this class.

This is an absolutely wonderful class!!! It truly helps you gain an appreciation for the complexities of the history of science and better understand your roles within it. My peers and I were able to not only develop our skills as historians of science, but employ them to explore nuanced issues that continue to affect us to this day. I greatly enjoyed the various spotlights, assignments, and faculty panels––you'll have a lot of fun in this class!
