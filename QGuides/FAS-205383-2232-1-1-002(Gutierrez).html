Great, interesting class. I loved Adri! There are so many assignments and papers tho
This class is amazing! It is really fun, completely manageable, and you talk about real–world stuff. Much of the reading and conversations are about viewing public health through a social and cultural lens, and the assignments and conversations really allow you to get creative. I'd recommend this class with enthusiasm –– it's not too challenging, really easy to balance, and so fun!
What can I say... more law students need to be working on their Spanish. They just need to take this class. Probably the big reason they aren't doing that is law school and legal culture, but it might also make a difference if the cross enrollment process was easier –– the first time I cross–registered for Spanish at FAS, it was difficult to figure out what deadlines the various institutions wanted me to hit, or whether or not I had maybe already missed them, and there were an astounding number of errors in the published instructions for taking placement tests, picking up textbooks, etc. (The things that the various registrars are telling cross–registrants may not be apparent to you because things seems to work so differently on the department level. I was very surprised when I got to class and discovered that I could have just walked in for the first time without having done any of these things!)
Great way to practice Spanish. Generally, people at this level are no longer taking Spanish classes just to fulfill the language requirement, which is great. I speak Spanish a little bit at home and it was great to be around other people who are speaking the language pretty well
