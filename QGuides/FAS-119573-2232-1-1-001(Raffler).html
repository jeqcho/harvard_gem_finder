This was my favorite class I took in my first year in the PhD. I learned a lot about substantive topics but also about how to approach research questions, think about big ideas, and come up with research designs. Definitely recommend for anyone with some interest in PE or comparative politics.
This is an excellent introductory course covering both theory and research applications at the intersection of politics and economic concerns. I highly recommend it for graduate students interested in political economy and comparative politics.
A very fun class with a lot of reading. Be prepared to chat and have a good time all around
Pia and Torben are wonderful wonderful scholars and teachers.
Torben is the very best in the field of the comparative political economy of advanced democracies. But he also knows everything about developing countries and autocracies. For anyone who has any interest in political economy, you definitely want to take a class with Torben to learn from his way of thinking about and studying political economy.
Pia is the very best in the field of the political economy of development. She is the kind of scholar that you want to become ––– she is a true thinker. Like Torben, although they both work on their specialized fields, they both know a lot different contexts and topics.
Thank you both so much!
