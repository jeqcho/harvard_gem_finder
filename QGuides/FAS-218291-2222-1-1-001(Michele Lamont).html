What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Hopefully Prof. Lamont makes things more collaborative/discussion oriented, bc there's definitely a lot of potential here. I enjoyed myself in the class (but may have been amongst the minority in that regard). The material and readings are very interesting and Professor Lamont obviously knows her stuff, but just wish there had been a little more flexibility/latitude in the class in general.
This course can be really interesting if you're engaged in the material and are interested in applying it to real world contexts. However, the class was somewhere in between a seminar and a small lecture, so it made it was easy to become disengaged from class conversations, leading to some unproductive classes. The assignments are definitely manageable and are relatively easy if you have been paying attention and doing the readings.
Not all of the greatest scholars are the greatest teachers. This class is compelling on some ways: the readings are great (though perhaps too academic), the ideas themselves thought–provoking, and your peers will likely help you work through and discuss the concepts. However, the structure of the class means it isn't great; it is both too academic and disjointed to be an effective intro class, but also too surface–level to be great for those with a strong background as well. As for Lamont, she improved in teaching over the course of the semester, but it's clear she is used to teaching grad students and the main class never really felt like it got there; lectures in particular did not do a great job explaining concepts in clear terms with helpful examples, but rather felt like a list of different related ideas and the books about those ideas. Lamont is also very experienced in the academic institution, which means that certain topics and ideas (there was a lot of backlash towards her handling of the Comaroff letter and her teaching style in general) may cause tension.
I would recommend steering clear of this class and this professor.
