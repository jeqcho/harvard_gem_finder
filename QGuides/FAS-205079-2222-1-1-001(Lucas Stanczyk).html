Besides making a strong case for the urgency of climate change, this course introduced a host of new concepts to me of which I had either no previous knowledge or a very surface–level understanding. The units on intergenerational justice, individual–level abatement, and the treatment of developing/poor countries were particularly striking. Climate change is the greatest threat to the future of human wellbeing and this course gives you the tools to grapple with the nuances of the threat and debunk denialism.

You don't need any prior knowledge of environmental science to do well in this course, except for perhaps some knowledge of microeconomics.

That's not to say that the readings were bad –– many were interesting for other reasons, and a good number were interesting philosophy –– e.g., anything by Derek Parfit.
