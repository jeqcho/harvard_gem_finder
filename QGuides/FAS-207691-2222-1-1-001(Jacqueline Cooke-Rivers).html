What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are interested in critical discussion or writing, this class of for you! If you are interested in Black America, this class is for you! It will not be easy –– and you may leave the class with a more critical, nuanced perspective than you once had, but you will really learn. I got to really engage and become friends with the class members and Dr. Rivers over the semester. This class really created a learning community I have hardly experienced at Harvard –– and I am a rising senior.
I would tell students to come into this class ready to welcome the reading and literature with open arms – it's a great way to get a lot out of the course.
This class was interesting and class discussions were usually engaging. This class is very reading intensive and the grading can be a little harsh.
It is difficult to meet expectations for assignments. You have to read and synthesize everything given to you.
This class was extremely engaging and provided a survey of the sociology of Black America, commencing with slavery all the way up to the Black Lives Matter Movement.
Definitely a lot of work, strict grading at the start of the year, need to do readings to stay on top of class –– but overall one of my favorite classes. really pushes you to think critically about issues within Black communities, and is a great overview of the major historical points of Black history. Rivers is a really great professor, will help you grow as a thinker, and really cares about her students
