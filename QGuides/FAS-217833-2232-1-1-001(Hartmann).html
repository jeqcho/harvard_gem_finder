Take this class. It is a really nice introduction to marine bio, and it gives you a good overview.
Take it, but understand your grade is dependent on your TF. Professor Hartmann is one of the nicest professors at Harvard – genuinely so passionate about marine bio and so willing to help students. The class did get a bit overwhelming toward the end with heavy writing assignments (which wasn't the case in years prior...seems there is an attempt to make this class less of a gem, which I cannot comprehend why considering low stress has helped with better information retention). If you are at all interested in marine biology, take this course. It does a great job of covering a variety of issues facing our oceans all while discussing the various ecosystems that have marine life. The weekly quizzes you get to take with your group are not difficult. This class has minimal outside–of–class work required. If you have taken more advanced marine biology courses this course may seem a little broad and lacking the intense detail covered in other courses, but it is still beneficial and covers information not talked about in other classes.

OEB 60 with Prof. Hartmann is an absolute GEM in the goldmine that is OEB. he does a fantastic job of making upper–level course material accessible to non–OEB students, and focuses a lot of the class on the impacts of climate change and general marine ecology. Great way to balance out a semester with more time–consuming courses. Going to lecture is worth your while because they weren't recorded and the material is just taught in a great way with tons of guest speakers, and most of the projects and papers give you the choice to find a topic that you're really passionate about. Grading isn't harsh at all. The chill final paper and no final exam are a HUGE bonus. TFs are also super helpful, and prof Hartmann is a genuinely kind and caring individual that cares about spreading his passion on coral reef conservation and sharing a lot of his own experiences from working in the field. Please take this course if you get the opportunity!

If you have any interest in learning about the ocean, its inhabitants, or human impact on marine life, TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Such an amazing course, the assignments are all super fun and not too difficult, and Professor Hartmann is so friendly and accessible. There are a lot of diverse components to the grading (participation, group quizzes, one exam, papers, video project, in–class debate, etc.) so you never have to stress about any one assignment. The class has a pretty hefty collaborative component (weekly quizzes with a group of ~5 people + peer editing, debates, and mini projects with that same group), which I found very fun and engaging but might not be for everyone if you don't like group work.

Note that this class is very much "you get out what you put it" – can totally be a gem if you want to put in minimal effort, but if you engage with the material and your group members, you'll have a much more enriching (and fun) experience. Overall 10/10 you should take this course. Genuinely enjoyed going to class for every lecture.
