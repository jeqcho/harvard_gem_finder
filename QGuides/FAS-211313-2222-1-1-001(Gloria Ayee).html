This class requires self–direction. If you want to learn and are sincerely interested in the topic, this class requires you to spend a lot of time thinking about and discussing the readings/films on your own time as class will likely be unsatisfactory.
It's a chill class and you get out of it what you put in
I was really excited about the subject material of this class, but was disappointed by the execution. Although I learned a lot from the readings and assigned films –– and now watch movies with a more more critical and analytical eye, which I think is really helpful –– lectures often felt very surface–level and repetitious, and didn't usually give me a deeper understanding of the course content. The work level is very manageable.
I highly recommend this class! The teaching staff is attentive and clear, and fosters lively intellectual discussion. As an international student, I feel I understand much more not only about implicit racial meanings in American film, and the relationship between film, public opinion, and major historical events, but also in my home country, where a critical framework is rarely used to approach questions of racial representations in media despite those being very laden with ideological content.
Take this class! You will watch great movies and become a more informed and empathetic citizen.
I really enjoyed this class (one of the best I've taken here) and also being able to talk about the different films we watched!
take this class!!! the discussion is so enriching and the material is very important & applicable. you will learn so much about others/the world and how to talk about important topics
This class is intro level "what is racism?" The films we look at are all very basic and you have probably seen them. Despite the fact that the course requirements are easy, the expectations are high and it is graded very strictly. If you are looking for a gem, this is not the course. It is only useful if you are not a person of color and have no experience talking about racial issues but want to learn how.
A great space to think about race while also feeling safe in your ability to participate or just listen. Also has great readings about race in film.
Definitely take this course if you can get in. Professor Ayee is insightful and thought–provoking, and seeing Black women representation in Harvard's faculty was so impactful for me. The content of the course is riveting and eye–opening. Would recommend with 5 stars.
If you're passionate about the topic then this class is for you. Really do everything you can to make your reflection papers good because they are graded harshly (a little more harsh than I would have liked). Class is easy and discussion is interesting but sometimes I felt intimidated to raise my hand.
This course is very introductory and needs to be restructured to encourage more critical reflection. It often got boring/repetitive on the professor's part and left me feeling wanting on learning skills to analyze films as well as in just actual prompting to analyze films.
This class offers an interesting way of learning about America's history of race through film. I would suggest it more for people who are interested in the government/sociopolitical side of the course, as I did not find the film portion of the course as engaging.
I'm conflicted about this class. The movies are interesting and the professor is well–meaning and well organized, but meeting once a week is not enough to get the most from these films. We barely saw the TF, though his comments were insightful. Feedback was uneven and grading was slightly unpredictable.
While it can be an easier class, you must remember that in addition to the movies, you need to do the readings as well. That can be time–consuming come time to discuss in class or when writing papers. I did not love the structure of the class. It's pretty big and there's one group discussion that you are expected to participate in.
