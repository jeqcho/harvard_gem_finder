This course is very fundamental to one's learning of math, and the Rudin textbook is extremely valuable. I highly recommend buying the hard copy. That being said, Math 112 is a very hard course to tackle completely on your own, so be sure to have at least one friend in the class with whom you can collaborate on psets and don't be afraid to go to office hours.
This course is extremely rewarding. The lectures have an elegance and beauty that you will not have seen before if you have not taken a pure math course in the past. Myrto, though unfortunately she is leaving Harvard, was a fantastic lecturer and Professor. This class is incredibly doable even for non math people, and I am walking out of it with a greater confidence in my mathematical abilities that I can apply to other fields.

You know what you're getting: it's a good course, as you just learn exactly the first 7 full chapters of Rudin's real analysis textbook (look at some of the exercises in those chapters and see if it's something you want to be able to do after a semester of work).

Problem sets are pretty difficult (as in all other pure math courses), probably 6–12 hours a week, and a lot of time on midterms and finals weeks.
Take it to improve your proof skills.
As with all math classes, this is highly instructor–dependent, so make sure you ask around about your year's professor. However, I really enjoyed the content itself. I took Math 22b and thought I might enjoy a real analysis class, and I was correct.
