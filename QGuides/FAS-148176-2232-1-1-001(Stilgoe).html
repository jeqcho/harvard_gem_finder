If you want to be challenged, not in terms of workload but in terms of curiosity, take this class. This class is what I thought all Harvard courses would be like. It is exploratory, interesting, and studying with Stilgoe is an incredible experience. I cannot recommend this class (or his other courses) enough. It has shaped the way I view all other courses and the world. Definitely only take if you don't have a 10:30, as attending office hours are as (if not more) fascinating than the class itself.
This is the best course I have ever taken in my life. Professor Stilgoe embodies all the qualities you would want in not just a consummate intellectual, but in a life teacher: his insights, always grounded in reality, go against the grain of ideology and restore confidence in the human gifts of observation and intuition. His lectures are extraordinary (this is the only 9AM class that I have ever been to consistently; he doesn't even check attendance, but I show up anyway, no matter how late I've stayed up the prior night) and his assignments are always thought–provoking. Take this course if you would like to understand modernization – and, more broadly, life itself in a chaotic time. Truly life–changing.
PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE !!!! stilgoe is wonderful. he has so much wisdom on how the world works and really challenges you to think for your self and form your own opinions.
I said a version of this before but I think it's worth repeating for future students. this is a really complicated course and I have difficulty marking it as net good or net bad. I learned so much in it, I will remember it forever (and for what it's worth, it's also quite gemmy). Would also flag that the more you put into it, the more you will get out of it. You don't have to show up to every lecture or read every book, but if you do, you will learn so much more. Ultimately, though, this course is its professor, not its assignments or materials.

In that sense, the weaknesses of the course are also its strengths, I think. Prof Stilgoe is SO smart & provocative & at times quite headstrong in a way that really makes you challenge your thinking but also at times can feel like he is being provocative for the sake of being provocative. There's nothing wrong with this because it forces you to think more deeply, but when it intersects with questions of identity/diverse perspectives, it can feel problematic. (For example, him facetiously taking on a sexist or racist perspective in order to make a point about gender or race. He's being facetious, but to be the minority in the room bearing the brunt of that rhetorical point can feel burdensome and deeply frustrating.)

At the same time, I think sometimes when I learned the most it was because I had to recognize how genuinely pissed off he was making me and then ask why. I guess bottom line is that this won't be an easy class emotionally/psychologically (especially if you're a woman, which I was) but you will learn more than you'll learn in most other Harvard classes. And it will be a fairly easy one in terms of assignments he doesn't ask a lot.

I would say take it but take it with a grain of salt!! And get out of it everything that you can including knowledge and including connections. Prof Stilgoe WANTS to help you later in life. He wants to form personal relationships with his students. Make the most of this!
FANTASTIC CLASS!!! This was my favorite class in my entire Harvard experience
TAKE THIS CLASS. I stumbled upon this class as a senior looking to fill the A&H distribution requirement, and it turned out to be my favorite class ever. Professor Stilgoe's lectures are really engaging and I found myself regularly pulling up early to this 9am just to hear more stories from Stilgoe before class. If you take this, be sure to visit office hours too and get to know Stilgoe. He gives you a lot of flexibility in choosing what you want to write about for the papers in the class, so they are really enjoyable to write as well. This definitely was a highlight of my senior spring.
This class is so amazing – definitely take it! Stilgoe may at first seem to have some controversial opinions, but when you listen closely, with an open mind you will find that perhaps they are not as straightforward as they originally seem. Take time to invest in this course, because what you put in is what you will get out – and if you put in effort, you will learn so much and be so grateful for some of your takeaways. This course was maybe one of my favorite courses in my time at harvard and I came away with a new appreciation for so many various things about history/advertisements/books/harvard etc.
This course is excellent if you are interested in American history and/or visual representations of the United States during the period listed in the course title.
Professor Stilgoe has a way of observing the world that he offers to you as a gift. I would describe his lectures as tangential epicycles tangents leading to tangents that revolve around a central focus. This is the course's strength as well as its weakness. What you find after reading his written work is that the approach fares better in print. But this is not to deny Professor Stilgoe's charm, wit, and humor. Ultimately, in signing up for this course, you are signing up for Stilgoe nothing more, nothing less. But that is enough to fill up several courses, severalfold.
Any course with Professor Stilgoe is an experience to be cherished and remembered. Prof. Stilgoe makes himself available for office hours every week before and after class, which you should take advantage of. The material is interesting and extremely related to the present. Prof. Stilgoe makes a tacit and strong argument for why history matters in the present. Prof. Stilgoe's pedagogy is also unlike any other professor. In a time when we are often handheld in classes, especially in the humanities, Stilgoe empowers students to make choices and pursue their interests within the material.
