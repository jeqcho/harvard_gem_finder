I have very mixed feelings about this class. On the positive side, I loved Professor Suo – he is very engaging and makes a point to teach entertaining lectures (his stories always make me crack up). He's great about answering questions during lecture, and I think he is a very fun person overall. I did feel that I learned some useful diff eq tricks, and I've already applied some these tricks in my other classes (physics, astronomy, even research).
HOWEVER– A few of the quizzes (and a lot of the final) seemed very heavy on derivations, which seemed a little outside of the context of this course. In addition, many of the psets seemed a little disjointed from the material covered in lecture and section. Finally, I was really frustrated by the amount of content that was either only covered in section (but not in lecture) or just not covered at all, especially since those topics appeared on the quizzes and final. The quality of the sections are VERY dependent on which TF you get assigned to; I got unlucky, and I ended up having to read the section notes and teach it to myself. Also, be warned that this course is very heavy on physics applications, so if you're not a fan of physics, I do not recommend this course. Otherwise, if you like physics and are willing to sometimes pick up some slack on material not covered in class, this is a pretty easy and moderate workload class that teaches you some useful tricks.

This course was refreshing! I've always loved math prior to enrolling in college––taking this course reminded me of that, even as a non–math concentrator. This is my first applied math course at Harvard, and I could not be more excited to continue taking AM courses for my secondary. Professor Suo always came to lecture with great enthusiasm and passion for the subject, which I in turn felt while learning in class. I always looked forward to lecture. One of the things I loved most about this course was its particular pedagogy: it focused primarily on ensuring we learned and grew comfortable with applying mathematical relationships to real world examples. While we reviewed history and some theory, the problems and exams themselves emphasized application. Furthermore, I felt that all the quizzes were very representative of our understanding of the subject. Modeled closely after lecture and homework problems, the exams were never an unpleasant surprise and I felt that I not only could prepare well for them, but that I also learned a great deal while preparing for them. Overall, this course has one of the strongest teaching structures of all the courses I've taken thus far here at Harvard. I also really appreciated the Slack platform––it allowed for easy and quick communication with both the teach staff and with other students. Highly recommend this course!
