This course was one of the most unique courses I’ve taken. It’s a great elective course that isn’t much work and has really interesting lectures and readings.
If you are hopeful about learning about witchcraft, be aware that the class primarily focuses on what the Catholic Church deemed as witchcraft, not the actual practice of witchcraft. The history is entirely focused on Western Europe and Salem with one lecture on the Azande people in Africa. It is hardly encompassing. In addition, the class excessively focused on the devil and how women who were "witches" had alleged relationships with the devil. So, if you are interested in that version of witchcraft and why women were persecuted and murdered take this course. I took issue with the midterm being asked to be 10 pages double–spaced solely because they were afraid of students using chatGPT. None of the prompts warranted such a long response. The final project is easy because you can choose any topic, and the final exam is easy enough to study for, but don't expect any feedback from the midterm to help you study. There was literally no feedback given throughout the entire course. In addition, the TF answered no emails and was minimal help.

It's a really interesting class that I recommend that you take. Be prepared for some heavy readings and surprising topics, but other than that, it's really cool
This is an excellent class if you are interested in witchcraft, folklore, and/or history, European or otherwise. Although the focus is on Europe, you have the opportunity to make the final project very personal, engaging, and creative, and have the liberty of picking almost any topic tangentially related to magic or witchcraft. The readings are interesting, if a bit dense at times, and some weeks have a lot to cover, so learning how to skim is a key skill you will develop during this course. Steve Mitchell is very friendly and approachable, so if you have any questions at all or want feedback on your project ideas, don't hesitate to contact him. As of writing this comment, we have still not received our midterm grades, so I have no idea what grade I can expect from this class. However, the TF warned us the grading criteria would be quite harsh. Therefore, do not assume that you will get an A just for turning in your paper on time. Engagement is highly valued, and attendance is mandatory, unless you want to fail the class. However, the class is overall really fun, and I highly recommend it.
Spend time with Steve outside of class if you can, he is a wealth of knowledge
This class was a great introduction to witchcraft! I honestly didn't know anything about the topic before entering, but the material was engaging and the assignments were relevant. Stephen was a great lecturer and Ameerah led some great section discussions. As someone who does not have a concentration or secondary in Folklore and Mythology, this was a very approachable and interesting class***.
