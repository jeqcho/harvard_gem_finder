Comments
This is definitely not the easiest expos/has a fair amount of work, but if you are interested in literature at all, you will enjoy this class! It is challenging but fun, and totally possible to do well in.
Dr. Napier is the best preceptor in the expos program. Emma is an excellent text. You will not regret taking this for your expos. If you are initially turned off by its supposed focus on social justice–y issues, do not be. You may write about whatever you find interesting in the text.
This is truly an AMAZING Expos! If you know you like Jane Austen (or think there's a chance you'll like her novels), TAKE IT! (That is, give yourself the best chance of taking it and rank it first. Pro tip: if there are two sections, rank them both first and second if you can – that's what I did – to increase your chances of getting in.)
This class was absolutely wonderful, I honestly can't praise it enough! I was a little scared of Expos before taking it, and thought Expos might just be something I have to endure on my way to a STEM major, but from the very first class, this course quickly became one of the most enjoyable and instructive ones I have ever taken. (Although, admittedly, I might have been slightly biased as I was already getting excited ahead of the first class for analyzing Jane Austen :))
Dr. Napier is a fantastic instructor, he will provide you with plenty of examples and sample essays and clearly explain every step of the writing process to you. What stood out to me the most, perhaps, was how much time he was willing to devote to our learning, always agreeing to set up additional office hours to provide individual help. Also, his comments are incredibly helpful (and I know others share my opinion). Whenever I felt stuck with my essay, I knew that talking to Dr. Napier would guide me in the right direction. I was honestly always amazed how quickly he understood the gist of what my issue was and how helpful the solutions he offered were.
In short, unless you know you absolutely do not want to read anything by Austen or Austen–related (but then you probably wouldn't be reading the Q–guide for this class), I sincerely, absolutely recommend this course! (Keep in mind, this is coming from a STEM major – this class is not just for English concentrators! Our group was a good mix of prospective STEM and Humanities/Social Sciences majors.)
If you're hesitating between this class and another Expos, pick this one (or another one that Dr. Napier teaches if things change at some point – perhaps you're reading this a few years after I wrote this!) – it will be one of the best decisions you'll make in your first year at Harvard!
Expos is a very variable experience, but this is definitely the expos to choose. Dr. Napier is excellent–he gives incredible feedback and he actually teaches you how to write. The class is definitely not easy, but it will make you a stronger writer, and a good grade is very doable to achieve. I am so happy with my expos experience and I think that I definitely picked the best expos out of all my friends.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! Whether you love Jane Austen or not, Dr. Napier is an excellent and patient professor that will prepare you for future writing assignments.
The class is very good, but it's not easy compared to other expos. Most of the class is english students. It's a great experience and you will grow, but it is definitely not an easy A. Dr. Napier is AMAZING though!
Really great class, and since you narrowly focus on Emma the scope is quite nice. Very good class if you like austen, and you definitely will have to like Austen. I felt that the grading was fair, and you don't have to worry about stuff like only one person gets an A. If you just consistently do class assignments(just reading examples) and attend writing conferences to iron out your essays/ideas, you'll be fine. Emphasis on going to writing conferences when he offers them–– its the best way to figure out if there are any flaws in your argument that you don't realize but he definitely will.
