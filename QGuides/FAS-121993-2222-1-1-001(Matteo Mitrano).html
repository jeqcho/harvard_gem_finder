What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class teaches you how to think and work like an experimental physicist! You get a lot of hands–on experience. The course staff (Professor Hoffman, Professor Mitrano, Joe, and Jieping) are really wonderful and very helpful.
Unless you're excited about this course ahead of time, RUN AWAY. There are alternative ways to fulfill the requirement, and your best option is to go for those. This class has been one of my least favorite in four years here, and definitely the worst within the Physics department. Everything breaks when you touch it, or even if you don't, and at most one person will know how to fix it. When that person is busy fixing everything for everyone else, good luck waiting around. Perhaps someone else will try their hand at a repair, and will end up making it way worse. The expectations for assignments, and therefore the justification of your grades, will be opaque (that is, if you even get a grade on your assignment). Raising any concerns will result in the staff expressing their empathy, along with making exactly zero changes. Take this nightmare of a class, and you will end up reconsidering your choice of concentration.
Make sure you have the same academic goals with your partner. Also ask questions everyone in class including faculty, staff and students are happy to help.
Most weeks you have little to no work outside of class, but when a paper is due be prepared to grind a lot outside of class. If you can get through the long class times you'll be fine.
Spend lots of time
This course is a great course for students wanting to strengthen their laboratory and research skills through experiments. But make no mistake, this is an advanced course, and you will probably spend countless hours trying to fix certain parts of the experiment that go wrong, reading and re–reading lab material, and writing the research paper. Joe and Jieping were wonderful lab assistants, but they have to help out with 6 different experiments, and could only designate a small time to help out, so the rest is up to you to figure out. This class will be stressful at times, but you really get out the amount of work you put in, and you will learn a lot.
It’s challenging. Expect a lot of independent work. Also theory you won’t fully understand and scientific methods not fully explained. Rely on Joe for help, he is a blessing.
