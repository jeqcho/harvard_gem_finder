What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Koss is very passionate about political parties in East Asia as this forms the crux of his main research – if your senior thesis involves East Asian politics in any shape or form, this class is a must! He is helpful in recommending sources to read/materials to look out for.
If you are interested in East Asia or political parties, definitely take this class! It is interesting and engaging and Professor Koss is a wonderful teacher. You will learn a lot from him and his expertise and also from the other students in the class. The work is totally manageable and graded fairly and the class discussions are educational and exciting. I highly recommend you take this class!
If you are an undergraduate, be prepared to do a lot of reading. Many of the college students were not used to the amount of reading, nor were they accustomed to writing a paper that is fifteen–ish pages long. These are skills that you can acquire through practice but just be advised.
This class provides a great overall understanding of the political landscape of East Asia as well as some of the important history as to why it is the way it is. Professor Koss is truly a great source of knowledge and always has a reasonable argument or logic behind his discussions. This year I felt the course could have covered a few less countries in East Asia as I would have preferred some more depth in the major East Asian countries but this is something Professor Koss is actively working to change next year. If you are a prospective East Asian studies concentrator or secondary field concentrator, such as myself, this was a great course for expanding breadth of knowledge and unlocking areas of future interest.
Really enjoyed taking this course. Prof. Koss is amazing! He is super knowledgable on the topic matter, and is also very accessible. He is always willing to help and I really appreciated his guidance. Loved this course a lot!
Excellent Professor, interesting but difficult assigned readings
I hesitate to recommend this class – there are some cool readings and the professor is very enthusiastic and well informed. I did however feel that the lack of structure, the size of the readings, and only having one class a week made it easy to lose track of core concepts. The difference in experience levels between the students (grads and undergrads) was both a boon and a bane, as basic concepts would sometimes go unexplained. I think with some improvements, and the introduction of a section/TF, this course could become a lot more. Check for if a section component is listed before adding to your crimson cart.
I would highly recommend this course to anyone interested in applying and testing political science theories in an East Asian context. You will learn a lot about the two major political parties in China and Japan and this course will provide a very different perspective from how political parties operate in the American context.
While it does cover many different East Asian countries, it is not designed to be an introduction to East Asia. It really is focused on political parties.
This class is a wonderful way to learn more about the CCP and the LDP, and to gain a better broad understanding of the political trends across East Asia and parts of Southeast Asia. In its current iteration, it's definitely a course that goes more in depth into a few case studies rather than one that gives a broad and sweeping discussion: definitely take it if the former is what you're looking for, and still consider even if you're already familiar with the subject matter!
Highly recommend. Professor Koss is one of the best professors Ive had here so far. He is insightful, organised, knowledgeable, accessible, accommodating, and clearly passionate about the course material. Workload with the final paper and other assignments can be tedious but if you are interested in the class this work is engaging. Lectures are always enjoyable with good balance between Professor lecture and student participation.
