I loved this class! Professor Lauder is an excellent professor and the best professor I’ve had at Harvard. Even those who don’t know a lot about fish can learn so much during this class.

Most of the class is straightforward and it’s relatively easy to do well, BUT the tests are memorization–heavy. I wish there had been more “application” of concepts learned instead of rote memorization, but I also get that phylogenies and anatomy are just…things you have to memorize and can’t “apply.”

Labs were super fun, and viewing the specimen in the museum’s collection during class was a once–in–a–lifetime opportunity. The TFs and Professor Lauder were so nice and clearly wanted you to do well and understand the concepts. Definitely take this class if you have any open slots in your schedule!
Take this course!!! Lauder generates so much enthusiasm for the material and you'll learn so much in a fun, low stress environment. There's a great mix of fish phylogeny, physiology, evolution, and ecology. The labs are really fun and chill (my favorite lab section at Harvard) and you get to go on a field trip to the aquarium! The workload is super reasonable and the lectures are really engaging. Highly recommend, even if you're only semi–interested in fish.
Pretty good course, TFs are great and Professor Lauder is really cool. Some of the topics are a bit dry and the tests are a lot of material to remember (lots of diagrams too). Overall, solid OEB course with some interesting material, taught well.
TAKE THIS CLASS. No problem sets, just short readings, short lab assignments, and very reasonable exams. I wasn't even that interested in fish and was just looking for a final OEB elective and WOW I'm so glad I chose this one. Professor Lauder is so enthusiastic and explains topics so clearly that I just remembered them without really trying (which made my studying a lot easier). Exams are just memorization. Labs are super cool and what you'd expect: dissections, getting familiar with different groups of fish.
This class will transform how you view fish. Ever wonder how some fish can use bioluminescence to their advantage? Take this class and you'll learn not just that but much more!
Had to take as a requirement and wasn't all too keen to begin with, but have really enjoyed it. Professor and teaching staff were fantastic. Start learning your phylogenies sooner rather than later.
This is an amazing course. HIGHLY recommend, only offered every other year, so if your a junior and you might be interested in taking it, definitely petition for it. Lauder is a fantastic, engaging lecturer and there are only a few, quite interesting labs. The tests are primarily based on lecture slides, so don't worry too much about keeping up 100% with the textbook readings, but they are pretty useful in the beginning. If you are interested in fish at all DEFINITELY try to take this class, but it is very niche and exclusively on fish, so be prepared for that.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!!! If you are at all interested in fish, please just take the class. It is so amazing, and the teaching staff is ridiculously helpful. They bring out specimens during lectures so you can see the species and structures being discussed. The lecture slides are posted before class begins so you can follow along, and the lecture slides themselves have a ton of information on them for you to study throughout the course. Lab is a ton of fun, and you get to look at specimens from the museum and you get to dissect a fish. This was one of my favorite classes I have ever taken.
This course is phenomenal, honestly no regrets taking it. The course description does not do the course itself justice–it may seem like a little silly fish course at first, but Lauder and the teaching staff are so cool and just really passionate about fish! They also don't just spend the entire semester looking at cool fish (though we did do that too, lol) but rather they tie it in with the greater marine ecosystem as well as present–day issues, such as invasive species in the Caribbean or the effects of climate change and the commercialization of fishing on fish diversity and marine life. I found the exams to be straightforward, though it was definitely a learning curve as this was my first–ever OEB course so I wasn't sure how tests would go. As long as you take notes, engage in the class during lecture time, and have a passion for learning about cool fish, this will certainly be the course for you.
This class is really fun and interesting. I'd recommend creating flashcards of the different fishes you see for the purposes of doing well on the fish identification portions of the exams.
TAKE THIS CLASS if you have even the slightest interest in fish. You learn so much information and Professor Lauder and the TFs are truly incredible. It is far and away my favorite course I've taken at Harvard. The lectures are engaging and the teaching team brings physical specimens into each class to examine whilst discussing them. The labs are fun and the field trips allow you to get your hands dirty and involved. All that being said it is not a cakewalk of a course, it requires a significant amount of memorization, and staying engaged in lectures and readings is necessary. The diagrams drawn in lecture should alleviate the stress of the sheer volume of information, and if not anyone on the teaching team can explain it to you. If you're lucky enough to take this course, enjoy it!

Passing around the preserved fish really helped me see what we were learning about rather than them just being abstract concepts. All of the assignments are fair and I can't complain about anything. Highly recommend this class.

Lauder cares for his students and does everything he can to ensure students are enthusiastic about fish (its very easy to be enthusiastic about fish after taking this class 10/10 recommend). Exams can be challenging – so do not neglect the details! (Memorization will be important.)

As someone who never was too interested in fish, I loved this class and have a completely new perspective on fish because of it. As a pre–med student this class was super relevant and helped enrich my understanding of anatomy and physiology enormously, even though we were examining fish instead of humans directly. I also really enjoyed the evolutionary and ecological aspects of the course material, and the course fostered a great balance of different fields of study. Professor Lauder is incredibly knowledgable, engaging, and approachable and makes learning very fun. You'll find yourself telling your friends cool fish facts almost every day. I highly recommend this course to anyone interested in marine biology, or just biology and anatomy in general!
This course is a relatively easy but comprehensive introduction to fish biology. The lectures primarily cover 1) the evolutionary relationships between different clades/groups of fish and what the phylogenetic tree for fishes looks like, and 2) the physiological mechanisms underlying how fishes swim, sense their environment, and feed. The content is mostly memorization–based.
TAKE THIS CLASS if you can and if you are offered a spot! This was probably my favorite class that I have taken at Harvard so far! On different occasions, I took some interesting facts from the class and shared them with people outside of the class! The exams are fair, and the lectures are very interesting and engaging, especially if you like biology. As I read before taking this class, even if you don't know much about fishes, I think you will indeed enjoy this class.
