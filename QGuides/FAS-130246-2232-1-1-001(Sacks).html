What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you need an English class for a pre–med req, or if you just want a gemmy English class, take this one! It's a super light workload (you don't really have to do all the readings each week) and Professor Sacks is great! He's very well–informed about poetry and such an expert in the field and definitely someone to take the opportunity to learn from!
I took this course to fulfill the premed writing requirement and because I love poetry and this was perfect! We get to read so many poems (do know that you will not find all of them interesting or engaging by any means but you will be excited by at least one poem) and I discovered so many poets that I can now call my favorites that I never would have known about before! This class meets once a week for two hours (or at least it did for Spring 2023) and it is on Zoom, which means that you have a bit of flexibility for where you take the course. That does mean it can get boring at times when the professor is lecturing but generally it is entirely interesting what Professor Sacks is saying. He encourages students to meet with him and I highly recommend doing so! Adam is also a phenomenal TF. Literally, the best teacher.
Prof. Sacks will help you see poetry in a new way. A wonderful class that combines breadth and depth of reading in poetry to give you an incredible foundation in lyric poets.

Only flag is if the class is still on Zoom (as it was in spring '23) – if this is not your cup of tea, that's something to be aware of.
This class is wonderful! Professor Sacks is so knowledgeable, and it is clear how passionate he is about poetry. Lectures are a bit long, so it can be hard to stay focused the whole time, but I think coming in with the approach of getting what you can out of it is great. Even if you listened to lecture for 5 minutes you would get something great out of it, as Professor Sacks is always saying such wise things. Assignments are very straightforward, and as long as you complete them you will do great in this class.
I would recommend this class with enthusiasm for those new to poetry, or those who want to gain a deeper and more sophisticated understanding of lyric. Peter is extremely knowledgeable and kind. Adam is extremely enthusiastic and responsive. Together, they made an ideal teaching team. You also learn a lot from those in the seminar with you, which ranged from beginners in poetry to graduate students studying a specific lyricist.
This class was a joy! Assignments are very reasonable, lectures are great, and you will learn a ton.
I would tell future students to be prepared to read a lot of material, but the experience is worth it if you want a community to experience the beauty of poetry with. It truly depends on what you are hoping to achieve with this experience.
