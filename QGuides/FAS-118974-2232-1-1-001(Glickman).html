Mark is a fantastic professor who is incredibly knowledgeable about the material and cares about the successes of his students. Recommend taking this class with friends or finding a study group
*For most stat dept. classes, the lecture material seemingly has nothing to do with the pset questions, so it's extremely refreshing that everything covered in lecture appears on the pset. One of the better classes in the stat department, though this class does takes 3 months to explain three functions in R, so it might feel a little slow paced and in the weeds.
This class is absolutely worth taking! Professor Glickman is an incredible teacher and lecturer and makes the material easy to understand. The tools learned in this course are incredibly practical. Definitely the best stat course I've taken at Harvard!
Awesome stat class because its interesting and doesn't have a ton of busy work like stat 139. This could be a great one to take p/f if you don't want the stress senior spring. Glickman is the man.
Take this course! Mark is great: nice, funny, an engaging lecturer, extremely organized and passionate about this class. The material is very relevant, and you cover a ton in just a few months. You'll be challenged the perfect amount: it's not totally trivial, but you won't be pulling all–nighters studying and doing homework. The problem sets are long, but you have two weeks and can usually get through them ok with the course notes + questions on Ed.

The one comment I'll make: when I was reading course evaluations before taking this course, they all emphasized how strict the grading can be on homeworks and exams, and to make sure not to forget to "hold other variables constant" lest you lose a few points. This was at the top of my mind all semester, and it should be for you if you take this class!

But seriously, awesome class, every Stat concentrator should take it. You might have a bad subconscious association because of Stat 139, but this is way, way better.
