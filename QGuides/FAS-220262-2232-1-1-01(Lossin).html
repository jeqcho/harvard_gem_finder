What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great opportunity to learn about labor history!! Some of the readings and themes you explore in this class will shift the frame of how you see history. There are a few flaws with this course in its first year, mostly that discussion in class was staggered and that the class didn't do a thorough overview of some of the labor history "basics," but I still highly recommend checking out this course!
This class is great, a rare gem of a history seminar in that it is actually interesting in what it teaches and how it's taught. You'll learn a lot about working class history that will change how you think about this time period of US history. Truly fantastic readings, though there are quite a bit of them, and the final paper can creep up on you, but is definitely worth the work you put into it.
This is a great class if you are interested in labor history. It is well–balanced for intersectionality as well. However, you probably need some background in US labor history or you may find yourself a bit out of the loop during class discussions.
A great overview of labor movements, and what different movements even mean. There was also a lot of important theory about the history we read, which really brought the level of the course up.
This is an excellent course that you really should take if you have even a passing interest in modern American history. You'll learn a lot, read a lot of inspiring radical texts, and gain a much more nuanced and developed for the way that power––of all different sorts––is motivated in the workplace. The readings are manageable, if a little long sometimes, the assignments are spread out through the whole semester and are thus well paced, and Prof. Lossin and Ione are both incredibly kind, passionate, and knowledgeable people who you'd be lucky to learn from.
