Great class! Fun to watch and think about Latin American movies as a group
This class is really fun, but does take up a surprising amount of time. We'd watch two films a week for most of the weeks; with films sitting at 1.5–2 hours, I'd usually end up spending five hours weekly just watching films, taking meaningful notes, and transcribing them. Discussion and the expectations for discussion could also get quite intense, considering this was an elective.
If you like film and are from Latin America, you should take this class. It is a little time consuming because you have to watch a movie for every class, which can sometimes be long, plus do a reading for each, so in the end it can add up. Also, some of the movies are a little boring and even difficult to understand, but they help you dissect them in class. Despite this, there aren’t too many assignments and the midterm and final are very manageable. And both the professor and TF are very nice! Overall, I recommend it, especially if you have some connection to Latin America.
You should take this class if you are interested in the progression and diversity of Latin American cinema, but don’t expect to watch the biggest regional blockbusters or an easy ride. This is a serious analytical course with a high workload (two films plus two readings per week).
It was one of my favorite courses.
