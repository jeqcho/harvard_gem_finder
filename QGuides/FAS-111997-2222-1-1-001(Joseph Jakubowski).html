What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Joe is a great person with a genuine interest in the material, which makes classes and discussions more interesting. The readings can get a bit tedious, though you get more out of the class and the discussions when you do the readings.
I would only take this class if you are passionate about learning more about the cognition side of music. While the studies conducted have to do with music, much of the content is science–focused. Of course, that is the purpose of the class, but you should be more curious about how the brain works and what techniques are out there for taking on these scientific approaches.
Music 194R was a rewarding class – enjoyed exploring and reflecting upon current frontiers in music cognition research. Dr. Jakubowski is wonderful – highly passionate about course material, and super caring towards students. Would recommend this course to anyone even remotely interested in the way cognitive science and music interact – you don't need background in either music or neuroscience to learn a lot from this class!
Honestly, this class will probably be better in future semesters, but I felt that this iteration of the class suffered from discussions that were filled with awkward silences, and discussions that didn't go beyond summarizing the results/findings of the papers. The workload is a little rough (5 assignments that are around 3 pages each, plus a final 3000 word essay/presentation), and the readings definitely could be streamlined a bit.
I would definitely not recommend this course, as it neglects any seminar–style discussion. As much as I enjoyed them, the guest lectures aren't worth taking the course either.
I think the topic of the class is interesting, which is enough to recommend taking the class if you are also interested. However, because if the class has no prerequisites, it is likely that this can be the first "seminar" class for a lot of the students which means that there will be a lot of recap rather than discussion in class. I felt like the most interesting parts of the class were the readings and the guest lectures, rather than the normal class discussions.
