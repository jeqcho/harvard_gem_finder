What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class did a great job of breaking down the barriers I felt between me and public speaking. I was able to institute the lessons I learned in circumstances variable as club speeches, campaigns, casual toasts, and in job interviews.
Take it!!
There is always room to grow in public speaking. Don't be afraid to experiment with different aspects of speaking and step outside your comfort zone. You get out of this course what you put in.
If you're interested in improving your public speaking (whatever level of experience you are coming from) you should definitely take expos 40. I've had a bit of public speaking experience, and I was definitely pushed out of my comfort zone, mainly with the improv exercises, which are fun, challenging, and low stakes. The main speech assignments aren't super time consuming, and I think there are only like 5 throughout the semester. Honestly the toughest part of this course was the 9am wakeup and that it was 2 hours in class twice a week, but the workload is pretty minimal (pretty much just writing and practicing the speech the day or two before...you're supposed to work on them for longer but I procrastinate). Terry is a great instructor and very caring and supportive no matter what level of experience you have. He lowkey gives politician energy which is funny lol (he used to be a politician) but he is such a good public speaker so he's great to learn from. You get to know a cool group of people cus its a small class and its interactive which is so fun. Honestly 9.5/10 experience !
Felt like most of the class ended up being memorizing and rehearsing speeches, which I think diminished how much I took away from the class regarding my impromptu public speaking skills. Terry was solid, and really enjoyed the group of people we had in our section.
This is a special class. Your speaking comfort and skill will get way better no matter your starting point. The class gets close quickly which makes speaking a lot more comfortable. Great atmosphere and new group of friends.
Take the course!!! Day–to–day stress level will definitely vary based on your preceptor, but everyone grows from having a course dedicated solely to practicing speech skills. It's a space to try new things and learn something from each attempt. Terry's section was definitely a bit more high pressure (practicing speeches in a bunch of different formats, like impromptu speeches where you don't know the prompt until right before you go up, presenting a problem/solution for Harvard to the President of the College, giving a speech to your graduating class, etc.), but I really feel prepared to give speeches and present my ideas orally after having taken this course. Also a lot of fun! We played improv games and silly warm–up exercises. The relationships in the class felt a bit like how expos 20 was where people actually got to know each other a bit from the speeches and from spending time together in a small class.
