If you are serious about building a venture and want the support of Harvard behind you, then please take this class. I learned so so much and met wonderful students and guest lecturers. This was a wonderful class to end my undergraduate career!
I expected this class to help me either 1) refine my idea or 2) pitch to investors, and we didn't really do either. It was pretty chill and interesting speakers (some HBS professors and other professionals), but it wasn't exactly what I was expecting in terms of moving my venture forward.
I think this class has really fundamental strengths and weaknesses.

Strengths:
1) Students can apply (and get) $2k of funding for their ventures mid–semester.
2) The teaching staff is very nice and understanding!
3) You get assigned a really cool real–world mentor outside of the class.
4) There is a photoshoot day with a professional photographer.
5) There is a useful pitch practice day in front of startups folks.
6) There is not too much work but the assignments help you think about your venture.
7) There is a lot of flexibility for you to work the lecture.

Weaknesses:
1) The course is almost exclusively guest lectures. Many of the guest speakers are not your typical Silicon Valley startup folks. As such, many of the lectures don't help with building the nuts and bolts of your venture. There are no tutorials about raising money, sales strategies, etc. Most of the lectures are about soft skills and didn't feel relevant.
2) The lecture is a 3 hour session and rarely with any interactive components. Given that I did not find the lectures to be very relevant to building a venture, the class time felt to be a weakness of the course.
This was one of my favorite classes at Harvard. This is probably a course I would recommend to anyone. It had great guest speakers and provides funding for your venture.
