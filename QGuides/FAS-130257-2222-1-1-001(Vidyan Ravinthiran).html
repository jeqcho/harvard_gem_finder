What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a really interesting course if a bit reading–intensive. I really enjoyed it, though, and if you want to learn more about epic literature then I highly recommend.
Be prepared to do a lot of work if you want to take this class. The readings are great and the professors do a great job of unpacking them. But it's a lot of work, almost an unreasonable I'd argue (3 papers, 1 final project presentation, 1 final exam, and 300–400 pages of reading a week that's tough).
The course itself made me far more interested in many of the works we read – classical works like Aeneid and Paradise Lost that I didn't think were interesting were spotlighted and taught in such wonderful depths by the two professors. That said, the grading in this class is very harsh + overall the course has a much higher workload than all other courses I've taken in the English department. Lastly, the course uses Star Wars in its title, but do know that the course does not talk about any contemporary epics until the very last lecture (Star Wars is mentioned for 10 minutes).
This class is an absolutely fantastic exploration of the genre of epic. Whether you are a student of the classics or a STEM concentrator that just love Percy Jackson, this class created an extremely accessible environment to analyze and discuss some of the seminal pieces of world literature. There is a substantial amount of reading that should not be underestimated, but the value that comes from class discussion and the writing assignments (as well as the feedback on the writing assignments) is well worth the struggle.
I took this class without having read any of the texts previously; it is doable, but I imagine the readings took me a little longer because I had to do a pass for understanding and a pass for analysis. My writing skills certainly improved through the feedback I received on assignments, and I am a more critical reader now than I was at the beginning of the course. I'd recommend the class for anyone who wants to read some great texts under the guidance of excellent instructors.
