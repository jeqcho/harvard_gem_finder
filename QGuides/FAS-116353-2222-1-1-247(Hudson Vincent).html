This class is challenging but very rewarding. Hudson is an exceptionally kind, generous, and effective preceptor and is always willing to help. He does a create job of creating a sense of community by forming small research groups to provide feedback and having members of the community speak about what they're doing to end mass incarceration. If you are willing to put in time and effort, you will become a much stronger writer and learn to contribute original arguments to complex and important topics. I highly recommend this class, especially if you are even remotely interested in criminal justice, activism, and community–based work.
Absolutely amazing class! It’s so so interesting and also fun. I believe this may be the best expos class.
Be ready to be challenged! You will receive great feedback and opportunities to learn so much, so the rest is up to how much effort you put in to learn about the topic, think critically about your own perspective and biases, and grow as a writer. The community Hudson creates in this course is amazing though so you will have support along the way.
Take this class!! The course material is really engaging and you learn so much about mass incarceration, while simultaneously improving your writing. Hudson is a really engaging teacher and gives great feedback on your writing. He is also super accessible, supportive, and genuinely wants to see you succeed in the class.
This is one of the best Expos classes you can take. I was originally looking to take the easiest Expos possible, just to meet the requirement. I got put in this class after ranking it 4th on my preferences, but I'm so glad that I did. Hudson is so supportive and truly cares for his students. I feel that I really did improve my writing, and I fundamentally changed the way I approach writing. I found this class fairly difficult, especially since writing is by no means a strength of mine. But I would certainly recommend this class if you're looking to improve your writing and learn about an important and presing issue.
Take this class!!!!! It has truly changed the way I see the world. Best class I've taken at Harvard. Hudson is the most incredible teacher.
Expos has been an obnoxious academic experience, which has made my life miserable and my stress levels to reach all–time highs. However, I think Prisons is one of the best sections, as the preceptor is very excited for the material, which is relevant and interesting. He tries to help everyone write better and he does a good job at that.
Expos 20 with Dr. Hudson Vincent was one of my favorite classes this semester. His commitment to seeing us thoughtfully engage with mass incarceration is noticeable from the very first day. The topic is something he is very passionate and considerate about, so even if you do not know a lot about mass incarceration, the prison industrial complex (PIC), existing conversations about prison abolition and prison reform, etc., you will definitely come out with a more grounded perspective on the ongoing state of the criminal legal system in America. I myself am a pre–med and the course/its teaching rivaled some of my other classes in terms of relevance/engagement. Although it is not the “easiest” Expos subject to take given that the topic itself is extremely serious and requires your full attention/sincere engagement with it, it will more than likely be one of the most rewarding classes you will take at Harvard. Don’t be afraid to ask questions, thoughtfully complete all the response papers, thoroughly read/annotate the texts, interact with the class visitors (they are all AMAZING), and listen to Dr. Vincent’s advice, and you will be more than fine!
This is the expos course to take. Hudson is genuinely goated –– so kind and accommodating and at the same time so insightful and instructive. If you're not into large reading assignments –– this is the course for you (we read probably four 15–page excerpts). If you're not into large writing assignments –– this is the course for you (only TWO essays when almost all the other expos class are three). If you've ever thought "hey maybe this prison thing doesn't make much sense" but haven't explored further –– this is the course for you. It not only gave me the tools to understand how we got where we are today, but most importantly, how we can make a difference –– something I haven't found at any other Harvard course I've taken! The one thing I wish is that we could've talked about the material itself more, but I guess that's the nature of expos. Let's be real, expos is painful, but this course will make it as painless as possible!
Take this class if the topic really interests you! I really enjoyed it, and while it was hard at times, it was fun.
