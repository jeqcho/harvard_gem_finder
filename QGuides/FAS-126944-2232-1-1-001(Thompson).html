This course offers a good overview of genomics research techniques and analysis. It doesn't go as in depth as a more specialized class would, but you'll definitely learn a lot about a lot of different things.
I would not recommend this class for non–computational students. It was really difficult to complete the homework sets as the lecture content did not prepare us for doing the homework. You need to have a solid understanding of python.
Excellent class and primer on genomics. Highly recommend for anyone in biological sciences.
I do not recommend this class to students who lack a strong computational background or who do not plan to pursue bioinformatics in their future research/profession. I do not think that this course is appropriate to students outside of a data science graduate program (unless the student has conducted rigorous computational work in their own research). Do not expect the instructors in this course to teach you code, and do not expect the TAs to be particularly helpful during office hours (most of my questions were answered ambiguously by TAs due to the collaboration policy of the class).

I also strongly disagree with the collaboration policy in this class. Be aware that you are not allowed to share code or collaborate with others on homework assignments, which makes completion of these assignments very, very challenging. In my own experience, some of my most meaningful learning experiences have occurred in collaborative classroom environments – this course does NOT permit that.
You will learn a lot, but this class is a lot of hard work. The problem sets will take you the full two weeks and an office hours visit or two.
Having a strong understanding of coding is crucial for this class.
