This is a great class to take if you are looking for a STEM class to pick up as a non STEM student, you learn a ton in one semester, it's not an easy A but its not terribly difficult, the work is back loaded so just keep an eye on that but otherwise a pretty light workload, objectively one of the most fun ways to fill a STEM requirement and the field trips were super fun
Solid class. Work load not bad. But I think it's getting revamped so ignore this comment.
This course is deceptively interesting. It gets very frustrating later on due to its structure.
Ben and Paul are great! They are great lecturers and really care about making the course better. This is a great introduction to ecology in general, and I feel like I learned a lot. The assignments were very sparse during most of the semester, with a lot of things due at the end, so beware of that, but I would recommend this class!
A very cool class to take if you have any interest/curiosity in the natural world ... the professors + TF's are very approachable and very willing to discuss their research

Would definitely recommend this course
This class covers a great breadth and depth of materials, and there are fun field trips. However, there are several assignments that pile up at the end of the semester right before the final. I highly recommend being in touch with your TFs if you are confused about any assignments. I wished for a bit more organization in the class to plan for assignments, field trips, and exams.
Honestly one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard! An incredibly enthusiastic and talented teaching staff that teach the class in a very approachable way. If you have any interest in ecology and general wildlife biology that Harvard doesn't offer very many classes in, this class is a great intro.
Take this class! If you are at all interested in ecology or nature, you will probably really enjoy this class. The professors are extremely knowledgeable and enthusiastic about the material, and they generate a lot of enthusiasm for the course.
I highly recommend taking this class if you're curious about ecology or just want an impression of all the different fields within ecology that are available. The teaching staff is very accessible and you can tell they're super eager to get more people interested in ecology. Super interesting material with an emphasis on real–world applications. Not too much work, and the psets are reasonable. Again, highly recommend!!
I would recommend this class because I find the content fascinating and I enjoyed the TFs. However, I was really sad to see how disorganized the structure and schedule of assignments was and didn't feel like the professors worked with the students to help ensure their understanding.
