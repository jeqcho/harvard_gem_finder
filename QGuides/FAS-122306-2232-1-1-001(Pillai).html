I took this class because I had a decent amount of statistics background and would be interning in quantitative finance, but knew literally nothing about finance. I thought it would help me gain some familiarity with working with stock data and help me get a better sense of what the market "does." This did not happen. This course at its core remains relatively heavy on theory, which means if you've taken 210/212, >80% of the course will be nothing new. That said, I think the current version of 212 does not discuss the idea of quadratic variation, which yields a more intuitive understanding of Ito's formula, and the ideas behind options pricing are interesting, so if you take this course you'll at least come away with that.
The lectures are a bit all over the place
This class was re–introduced after a hiatus of 5? 6? years, so it was basically like taking a new class. Logistically, the course was not very organised. The syllabus was rather bare boned, there were little to no course materials, and there was no outline of what lectures were meant to cover. Grading weights were a bit unclear since changes were made (and almost all such changes or other important announcements, e.g. about exams, were made orally with no communication on Canvas). So it was very difficult to figure out what was going on in that aspect.

In terms of course material, I'm not really sure what I learned this semester. There were some interesting content on martingales and Brownian motions, but it seemed rather unmotivated and disconnected to each other and other more–applied financial concepts. Homeworks were generally longer than needed, and grading was slow so we didn't get useful feedback until rather later in the course. Also the homework problems were deliberately ambiguous (per the course staff) to encourage "creative thinking", whatever that means.

Exams, somehow, were the best part of the course, because they were unambiguous, not too long, directly connected topically to the course material, and sufficiently difficult to test our grasp of the material.

Overall, I would recommend not taking this course until some more reorganisation and structure is introduced.
