Comments
This class was incredible and the academic highlight of the semester. Darcy is the most generous instructor, your peers will all grow to support and lift up each other thanks to the environment he fosters, and you'll learn so much about yourself and your voice in the process.
Amazing writing course, even for beginners. Was my first humanities course at Harvard, and I'm so glad I took it. Creative nonfiction is a great way to reflect on your life, and you learn so much from your peers. Plus, Darcy is a gem.
I really loved this class, not least because of Darcy's excellent feedback and facilitation, but this workshop (compared to one other I've taken, Science Journalism) was extremely interactive. We read and critiqued SO MUCH of our peers writing and critiqued and got so much feedback in return. It's really lovely to hear so much of your peers' lives, stories, and inner worlds.
Regardless of your concentration, if you're interested in becoming a better writer, TAKE THIS CLASS. It's really rare at Harvard to get real, detailed, critical feedback on your writing –– even in something like expos, professors aren't generally reading line–by–line for the effectiveness of the craft of a sentence, or singling out moments where the authorial voice falters. This class gives you that feedback, and also challenges you to give that feedback yourself, and in the process, turns you into a better writer and a better reader. Nonfiction might sound dry, but this class dispels that idea immediately. Each week you read a different fantastic published piece of creative nonfiction, which are each very different, but equally engaging. Even if you have very little experience with creative writing, I'd highly recommend at least applying to this class –– after taking it, I feel like the scope of the things I have the ability to write about has widened drastically.
Take this class!! The course is a wonderful introduction to writing creative nonfiction. The example pieces are amazing and Professor Frey is great. Lots of fun, and a great opportunity to grow as a writer :)
It is one of the best classes I took in my four years of graduate education at Harvard. Darcy is an incredibly intelligent and engaged and sensitive professor and the reading materials are wonderful, as is the writing and community that emerge out of the workshop.
This was life–changing. This course made me a better writer, a better reader, and genuinely so much more passionate about my writing and the experience of writing in general. The course materials expose you to a variety of different styles and Darcy is the most wonderful professor. He genuinely encourages and takes an interest in your writing, which creates an amazingly supportive environment. Being in a classroom with other equally passionate writers also gives you such energy to write and want to think about writing. I would say take this course if your schedule allows you to, it made every week so much more fun amidst the other classes I was taking.
