This class is incredibly fun and engaging. Every week is a new project for you to grapple with (using prisms to study light, making a telescope and looking at the night sky, growing a plant, making a motor) and think critically about how scientific discoveries changed how people understood and thought about the world. Also helps build a basic intuition of phenomena across chemistry, physics, and biology.
Super fun class, you definitely get a lot of hands–on experience for the assignments. Definitely one of the most unique classes I've taken at Harvard!
Engagement with Professor is unparalleled. Team exercises are fun. The problem sets and experiments are fascinating. Best course at Harvard. You won't regret it.
I genuinely doubt I will ever take a gened as interesting, rewarding, and /fun/ as this one. If you are interested in history of science, tinkering with home–made telegraph machines, and holding a mock Nobel appeals hearing, you will love this class.
This class is a lot of fun and is very different from any other class I've ever taken. If you enjoy doing low difficulty hands–on projects and want a break from lectures, this class is for you. As someone who didn't really love science going in, even I found the concepts we learned about to be fascinating and I have walked away with a much greater appreciation for science and the people we learned about.
The best teaching staff, who can also cook amazing bbq :)
This was a very fun and relaxed course! The Max and Phil are great and generate a lot of enthusiasm for the subject matter. I would highly recommend!
