What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a great course that can be tough at times but like most statistics courses at Harvard, it is ultimately very satisfying. Alex is a great lecturer and assignments are due every other week which is helpful given the material. Spatial statistics is an exciting field and there are some interesting applications that help you build decision–making skills when it comes to analysis.
Alex Young is a great lecturer and professor! The work all feels very purposeful. There are many things to help you figure things out, including lecture notes, vignettes, and numerical examples. I would recommend against trying to take this course asynchronously, as some concepts are not intuitive and probably require follow–up questions in lecture. I was in this course because of an interest in infectious disease epidemiology, and I feel like I better understand some of the ways to analyze, for example, location–aggregated data (lattice/areal processes). If you're more interest in ID Epi dynamics, though, I would recommend a different course.
This course felt a little niche but does include topics that are more applicable in general (e.g., Gaussian processes for machine learning). The first pset was super bashy on the math but the rest got better, and the psets basically alternate between theory psets (doing math) and computation psets (writing R code to analyze data). The final is take–home and was very reasonable and not difficult at all. Alex is a great lecturer and explains the math and rigor behind the various topics in a way that is easy to understand. Overall, an excellent class to take, if not a little niche.
Take this course! One of the better 'upper–level' statistics courses for undergraduates. Alex is excellent: organized and communicative, effective lectures, clearly cares about his students. David (TF) was great also, although I think he is graduating.

If you really enjoyed the probability stuff you learned in 110, this is a great opportunity to apply it in another field (you'll go a lot deeper with Poisson processes). I enjoyed 171 because it gave the 'time' component of probability; 141 gives the 'space' component.

The course structure is very pleasing as a student. Homeworks are 50% of the grade and, while some of the theory can be challenging, there's plenty of support. The final is a week–long take home, so you never feel the pressure of having to cram / study. The R toolset is pretty cool as well. All in all, take this course and any other that Alex is offering!
Take it. This has been one of my favorite courses thus far. Learn a lot without being overwhelmed.
Take this class as a relatively chill statistics elective! Alex is great and you'll learn some cool material and think about probability/inference techniques that extend 110/111/139 material into the realm of spatial data.
The theory part sometimes feels more like learning new terms which are just old concepts from previous classes renamed in the spatial statistics context. The applications you learn are helpful but are limited in scale and impact.
