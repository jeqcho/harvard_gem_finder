This is a very interesting but also very challenging course. Salil is a wonderful professor and the lecture notes and support are great. But this is very much a challenging grad course that brings you to the fore front of spectral graph theory. So, if you're taking this as an undergrad be ready for very challenging problem sets and make sure you have good pset group to tackle them with. I was overwhelmed by the difficulty and thus didn't take as much away from this class as I might have from an undergrad level graph theory class.
You should be confident in your linear algebra skills to do well in this class! I was really rusty at the start of the semester, and that hurt me a lot (lol) when it came time to do PSets, or even understand what was going on during lecture. Definitely make sure you've taken MATH 21/22 at least, STAT 110/111, CS 120/121 and 124; these are pretty foundational. Take advantage of TF sections and OH; use those to get as much of the PSet done as possible. Take with friends, or make friends! A very collaboratory class. My first grad course and it was definitely super tough, but you gain a lot of academic confidence :) Plus, no exams and just a final project are a godsend!
This is very tough class, but also extremely well taught and the material is fascinating. I would absolutely recommend it. The homeworks are hard and take time but there is a lot of support available.
It is a very challenging course both in terms of the content and the difficulty of the problem sets. It gets more and more difficult towards the end. However, if you are willing to dedicate sufficient time (approximately 20 hours per week in my case), you'll learn a lot from the course. It is well–structured, starting with fundamental concepts and gradually delving into more advanced topics. Also, Salil is an amazing instructor, and it makes a huge difference for the class. He provides clear intuition for everything, and you can feel that he invests a lot of time in teaching the course.
Interesting and challenging material, progression of topics builds on past ideas. Before–class readings were helpful for understanding and Prof. Vadhan's responses to comments were very helpful! Overall, Prof. Vadhan is a great lecturer, very engaging and explains complex concepts well, kind and great about answering questions. Feedback on the project was really helpful and it was cool to see everyone else's projects. Do note that this is a challenging course, probably the hardest CS course I have taken at Harvard and is definitely a graduate–level course that requires focus, time, and effort. Make sure you have a strong understanding of linear algebra. At times pre–lecture reading was a little confusing because explanations skipped steps, but the teamwork of the class pointing out questions on Perusall and the course staff answering questions helped bridge this gap.
Be prepared to spend a lot of time in this course, but you will learn a lot! Salil is a great lecturer and cares a lot about his students' learning sections/OHs are a lifesaver and really help you understand the material!
This class is amazing. The lectures are very well organized and structured. The problem sets are incredibly challenging, but definitely worth the effort.
This course was SO DIFFICULT but SO WORTH IT. Prof. Vadhan is amazing – he cares a ton for his students, breaks down complicated ideas into intuitive chunks, and gets students genuinely excited about course concepts. The biweekly psets are difficult, but much bearable if you 1) start early, 2) bounce ideas with your peers, and 3) make good use of office hours. If you enjoy theory (and enjoyed 124, 121, etc.), then definitely consider this course!
For those that like theoretical CS, discrete structures, and clever proofs, this is the course for you!
Salil is great and cares about the class/material. He also was very responsive to feedback throughout the semester in making the psets shorter/easier. Highly recommend, particularly if you're very interested in spectral graph theory!
This is a hard class, but the material is really cool (and more concrete than many other theory classes). The problem sets are very well designed and biweekly, but they do take time.
This course is really great if you're into theoretical CS – Salil is a wonderful teacher/lecturer, and both he and the TFs were extremely helpful resources. The psets were a lot of fun but very challenging, and it's hard to get them done without going to office hours. Overall, would highly recommend this course!
The derivations can be dry/unintuitive sometimes, but the overall story is pretty satisfying to learn about. It is fascinating how graph–theoretic properties can be deduced from looking at eigenvalues of matrices associated with the graph.
I was routinely frustrated by the way the class was taught and would advise against taking it unless there are improvements made. There were too many times during lectures when we were asked Can anyone define this term for us?" or What should be the next step?, after which we would wait until someone finds then reads the answer out loud from lecture notes we were required to pre–read before class. I found this to be an ineffective use of lecturing time and not the best way to measure or encourage student participation. Adding to that, I did not agree with the course's approach of covering a lot of ground by giving lectures that superficially cover the material with complete proofs given very rarely. To be fair, we were told that we could look into additional readings for more details, but I believe this excuse can be made for any class. However, the material itself can be cool and Prof. Vadhan is a good speaker, so make what you would like of this comment.
