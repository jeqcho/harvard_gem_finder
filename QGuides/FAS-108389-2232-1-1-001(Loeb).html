Avi is an incredible teacher, and it was an honor to learn from him as an admirer of his work. His lectures had a good mix of technicality and discussion — and he made the math material accessible for students who didn’t have as much experience before / weren’t STEM concentrators. I loved his tangents, his profession of his dream to die in space and become a historic meteorite in another world, his candid discussions of what mattered most for human progress and human’s foolish history of precluding progress through politics. He inspired me to develop a more sharp, critical view of the world and scientific advancement. This is something I will carry with me.

Morgan is awesome. His lectures / activities for the second half were always illuminating, and he clearly cared about everyone in the class. He was always open to questions, no matter how small. I think he was instrumental in setting the tone for the course, and creating a community among the seven of us that was both serious about the work we were doing and compassionate. His passion for astrophysics is infectious.

Courseload: more than most freshman seminars. Heavy technical reading, psets every week, and a final paper you have to write while also completing psets.
This class is too much work for a freshman seminar. Prof. Loeb is amazing and the class sections are super fun, but there are weekly PSETS that take almost as much time as a regular PSET class, weekly readings and a research paper at the end. If the syllabus changes, and its less work, definitely worth to take it, otherwise you will not enjoy it as much beacause of the workload with your other classes.
Take it! Avi is a rockstar, the literal best in astronomy. This class made me want to concentrate or do a secondary in Astrophysics. I learned so much about the Universe, but especially about life and science. My main takeaway was knowing to never stop being curious and questioning things, both as a scientist and as a human! Avi´s insights are sensational, and Morgan made us feel very comfortable with each other and with asking any question we wanted. We also appeared in a documentary about Avi´s research, which was super cool!
The class is super interesting and the lectures are incredibly engaging, but this is NOT a normal 0–1 hrs/week freshman seminar. Take the class if you're even remotely interested in astrophysics because Avi and Morgan are both great, but be prepared to do real work in this class.
This freshman seminar is such an amazing opportunity to learn from a world–leading expert in a small group setting, and you should absolutely take it. It does require some background in either physics or math, so be wary if you don’t have any background with that, but otherwise, I would 100% recommend it to anyone. Definitely one of the best experiences of my freshman year, the professor and TF are both wonderful people, and I learnt so much.
This is a great course if you are interested in astrophysics or anything to do with space. Avi is a great person to meet, and he is so caring about his students. His passion for the field also really shines through his work and personality.
