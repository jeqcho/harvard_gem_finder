This course is extremely well taught and structured. Lectures are interesting and move at a good pace. Dr. Morin is a great lecturer who has an incredible grasp of all the material (he has literally written all the textbooks for this class). Further, Dr. Morin also runs useful office hours and gives clear explanations. He's also just a very approachable and enthusiastic teacher.

The course presents interesting material with fairly relevant applications and teaches fundamental mechanics comprehensively. With only two lectures a week, this class does, however, move quickly and condense a lot of material into a relatively short span. Some physics background (AP Physics C, for instance, covers much of the same materialthough in less depth) is recommended. It is possible to do well in this class with no prior physics experience, as the class starts from scratch and covers all the topics from an introductory standpoint, but this will require a large time commitment to learn and review all material thoroughly. The single pset each week is a manageable length; definitely try to start early, but there are plenty of resources to help you through it from office hours to other students to physics night. This class also has a lab and a section component. Sections will provide helpful walkthroughs of problems much like those on the psets but can sometimes feel a little disorganized. Labs are chill (I would recommend the 9am lab if it's offered) and provide useful data analysis skills. The labs require coding in Python. Although you can definitely get by without any CS background, it does help a lot if you want a productive understanding of the underlying syntax and functions that are taking place.

Overall, I'd enthusiastically recommend this course to anyone with an interest in physics mechanics who has some prior exposure and wants a more comprehensive background!
