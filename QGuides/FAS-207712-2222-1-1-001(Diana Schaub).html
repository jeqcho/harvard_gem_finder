What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
For me and a number of other students that I have talked with, this course has been the best, by a wide margin, that we have taken this semester, if not all time at Harvard. Professor Schaub is an extremely engaging and enthusiastic lecturer and led very interesting discussions on everything from Lincoln's early life to his thoughts on slavery, to his writing style itself. Be ready to read Lincoln's speeches and to be impressed and amazed by his clarity of thought. In American history courses, it's always in a historical perspective, but in this course, we went through the years like we were living in them. I joined this course because I thought it extremely unique to spend an entire semester learning and studying one singular American leader, and I certainly was not disappointed.
Professor Schuab is an incredible guest professor, and I sincerely hope she returns to Harvard to teach other courses. One of the most knowledgeable Lincoln scholars in the world. Lincoln is a fascinating statesman, and studying his thought will help you understand the biggest problems of politics in general. A really interestingly and well–organized course; you read what Lincoln read as his development, then what he wrote. Doing a comprehensive study of a single thinker is a really valuable experience that all students of politics, history, and philosophy should do.
Excellent course that I highly recommend
Highly recommend the class. This course helped me understand and shape my political views, and taught me how to communicate them to others in the most effective way possible.
If you have the opportunity to take this class, you absolutely should, regardless of concentration. It is quite accessible, requiring fairly small amounts of reading per class, yet helps explore the best of American democratic thought from its greatest statesman. Professor Schaub is truly an expert in his thought and work, and does a great job encouraging and directing discussion in class.
This is one of, if not the best class I have taken at Harvard. Anyone even considering taking it should jump in. I sincerely hope that Harvard brings back Professor Schaub, as she puts a ton of work into the course, clearly loves and knows the material, and wants to get to know every student. She is a better professor than many I have had at Harvard, and she really makes you fall in love with the readings. She stirs conversations along well and gives excellent feedback on essays. Please use this class as an opportunity to think deeply about a central figure in American history and life. You will get to do it under one of the best.
