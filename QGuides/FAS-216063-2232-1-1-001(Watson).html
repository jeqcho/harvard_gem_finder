I highly recommend this class to anyone who is interested in English and honing their writing. Academically, this course will change your understanding of how form and genre shapes meaning in literature and art in general, allowing you to tap into a hidden wealth of meaning in all manner of works. More importantly in some sense, however, this class will inevitably deepen your own appreciation of literature and poetry as a whole. I already loved English, but even I will walk away more passionate than before. Language tastes sweeter on my tongue than ever, stories are more meaningful and memorable, and the world is generally more beautiful.
Mixed feelings on this course. Prof Watson and my TF were both amazing resources—they’re very knowledgeable about the material and really want to see you succeed. However, the class moves through each reading very fast and spends a lot of in–class time going over history rather than looking at the works themselves, which makes each reading feel rushed and underutilized. The creative assignments are fun, but the grading on other essays is unnecessarily harsh. If you’re not an English concentrator/secondary taking it for the requirement, I wouldn’t go out of your way to take this course.

Although this class is required for all english concentrators and secondary students, it is a great class. I was a bit wary heading into the class because I saw a lot of unknown texts on the syllabus but they were all super interesting and super fun. Plus, it's great that there's a lot of variety because on the off–chance that you don't like one of them, you'll only spend a week talking about that specific text and you can choose not to write about it on your essays. Professor Watson's lectures are interesting and offer a lot of good, formal context for the work you're analyzing, but section is really the place where the class shines, as you get to do some close readings with the class and your TF. The grading I thought was very fair and considerate, and there were a lot of opportunities to get more points. This is a great class and you'll have lots of fun; just make sure you do the readings and participate actively in class to get the most out of it.
English 20 is great! You will need to read thoughtfully and work hard on essays, but the course provides a lot of room to grow and you don't need to be an expert in English already to succeed.
