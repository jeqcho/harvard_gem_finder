In terms of positives, the course content is interesting and varied. It covers a lot of ground, and you get to learn about a ton of different subfields in linguistics. Also, the workload is very reasonable: one problem set and discussion board post roughly every two weeks, and a take–home final exam. Lastly, Adam is a good lecturer. He's an engaging speaker and is clearly passionate about the subject. Sometimes it felt like the lectures were repetitive and simple concepts were over–explained, but it's also an introductory course so some of that is probably inevitable.

The main limitation of the class is that it's not very organized. For example, assignments were often released or cancelled randomly. Adam would promise that an assignment would be released on a certain day and it would only be released several days later. Some of the assignments were unclear or contained errors that the teaching staff had to later correct. Grading was relatively slow, particularly in the second half of the course, and it felt very arbitrary sometimes.
