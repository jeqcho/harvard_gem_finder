What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Immersive research experience with tons of guidance if you ask for it. Try to choose a project you know some background for, but really not too much prior knowledge is expected *at the beginning*. However, there is a steep learning curve and you're expected to keep up. Especially in the first 2/3 of the class I felt like it was very straightforward and fun. When the final paper and poster assignments started, I wasn't expecting the grading to be so harsh when there is a grade boundary set so high. It became stressful at the end because of that but otherwise it's a fantastic class.
Make your lab group your friends. You'll have an awful time if you're just all pursuing the same project without communicating personally. The thing that made this class so wonderful was that by the end, it didn't feel like work. It was just spending time with friends who wanted to work at a shared interest. The point of this class for you should be to bond with the people you're working with as more than just classmates.
The work outside the class is light until you have a research paper to write at the end of the semester. This is relatively difficult to do as a group, but definitely rewarding. Your lab experience will vary greatly depending on what group you’re in; at times lab can be exhausting and/or frustrating. Overall I would recommend this class if you want to learn a lot about lab technique and get to know your lab group really well.
This has been one of the most valuable courses I have taken in college and has prepared me for a real world science experience better than any other course I have taken.
This class is actually soooooo good. Absolutely fell in love with the teaching staff and am not kidding that this is my favorite class so far at Harvard. Be ready to block off 10 hours a week though!
You have to take this class for chem– from what I have heard about 165, this seems much better, but I am unsure compared to chem 145. It is so much work, probably 8–10 hours outside of the 10 hours of lab time. There are problem sets, papers, posters, presentations, and science advocacy assignments. They were all extremely useful and helped me grow a lot as a scientist, though! Also, Heidi is one of the best professors I've ever had, very understanding and gives great feedback. Be prepared for an insane workload, but the grading is not harsh, at least and you will learn so so much!
Take this class with your friends and try to get in the same group, it makes the experience a lot more fun. Start on any writing submissions earlier and email them to Heidi for early feedback so you don't get blindsided by the submission grades. As long as you stay organized and keep up to date with assignments/the notebook it's a very enjoyable class.
This class is pretty fun if you take it with your friends. All the TFs are very friendly and knowledgeable. Heidi and the TFs are responsive and accommodating. They give a lot of thorough feedback on assignments and are quick to respond to emails/questions. The only down–side is that the class is 10 hours/week, and there are assignments that do require a decent amount of time outside of class. Be prepared to dedicate a good chunk of your week to this class. Heidi is also a pretty strict grader.
Being in lab for 5 hours at a time is sometimes rough but this class is extremely valuable. It does a great job at introducing students to research
