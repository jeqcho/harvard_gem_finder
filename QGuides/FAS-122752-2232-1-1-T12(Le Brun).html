For me, reading is not my strong suit so this class was difficult. Dedicate some good time to focus on your readings and problem sets and writing assignments cause they pile up a ton.
This is a great class which provides a basic introduction to different areas of substantive and procedural law and shows how we can view the law through an economic perspective. Ken is very knowledgeable about the law and such a great resource when it comes to sourcing ideas and data for the final paper.
Professor Felter is the best! His section is challenging but well–worth the challenge; the readings are enriching and the discussion is engaging
If you are an economics student who is interested in going to law school, I would highly recommend this class. It is a great introduction to the major themes in law and Professor Felter does a great job teaching and explaining the material.
This seminar was incredible and I would definitely recommend it! The readings can be a little long, but honestly, I found them manageable and mostly super interesting. Professor Felter does an amazing job encouraging discussion and engagement with the material in section without doing any sort of cold–calling or awkward pauses in discussion. He is also just very accessible, kind, and truly interested in your development! It's one of the only tutorials with two sections for a reason!!!!!
Professor Felter is one of the most caring, nice, and genuine professors that I've had at my time in Harvard. Don't be mistaken, this course is definitely an incredible amount of work and normally we were doing about ~80 pages of reading a week, sometimes more, sometimes less but I'd say on average it was about that much. However, with that said, coming into this class, I was not much of a reader but coming out of it, I've genuinely improved my ability to quickly skim and pull out the most important parts from readings (which is a super great skill in my opinion to have). Professor Felter cares so incredibly much about ensuring that you succeed –– he leaves a large breadth of comments on your assignments that makes it very very clear on how you can improve for your next one. I've never had a professor be so thoughtful and engaging in our comments. He is extremely accessible outside of class, scheduling office hours 1–on–1 with you around your schedule and it was wonderful getting to know him. He is a super cool guy overall –– a lot of amazing stories from when he was a lawyer and through law school. Overall, I think this course is a pretty good introduction to what *might* be expected in a law school class and I think it genuinely helped convince me that I for sure want to go to law school because I was on the fence when I had taken this class about it. Additionally, even though professor Felter cold calls at times, what I appreciated was he never made you feel dumb or stupid or isolated if you didn't know the answer. He genuinely just wants to lead a nice and productive conversation during class and it's a much more enjoyable experience when everyone in the section has done the readings and is able to engage. I'd for sure be ready to put a good ~10 hours into the course each week, at minimum, if you want to get a good grade and really engage with the content. Normally the readings took me around ~4 hours/week (probably a little more towards the beginning of the semester) to really dig into them and understand them (especially when we had to do our assignments based on the readings) and another good ~6–8 hours each week working on the assignments which again, professor Felter spends an incredible amount of time and care with grading them so I'd for sure spend a good amount of time and care in writing them. Overall, such an incredible course and easily one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard despite the fact that it was definitely a heavier load than normal classes.
Be very sure that you want to take on the amount of work this class presents. This class will take up a significant portion of your time so be sure to take other courses that aren't as demanding. The coding part of the course was fine (only 4 PSETs) but I didn't find it necessarily helpful for my own final project. If you have significant interest in the topic of law and economics, then you will find this class very interesting and will be able to participate a lot.
