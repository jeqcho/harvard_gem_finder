What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This has the potential to be a great class if you don't slack off. Susan is so wise and gives amazing lectures, the readings are excellent, and feedback from peers and Susan is always thoughtful and nuanced. However, much of the quality of the class hinges on how engaged the students are in discussing readings, and how honest students are willing to be when giving other students feedback on their work. Please be engaged and don't be afraid to give useful feedback, beyond flattery, on other people's work! In any case, if you're an aspiring journalist or you just want an opportunity to improve your writing, this is an incredible class to take!
Susan is an amazing professor. She taught me so much about ledes, structure, and language without making it feel like I was ever being lectured. Like her writing, her teaching avoids abstraction, instead drawing on concrete examples and metaphors. The images of "ledes" as opening scenes of movies and the diver searching for the "wreck, not the story of the wreck" will stick with me.

My classmates were incredible — brilliant, thoughtful, genuine... the lowest ego group of people I've ever been with in a class at Harvard. (It was a class of all women; I wonder if that had something to do with it.) Everyone is kind, but not in a low–effort "your piece is amazing!" type of way. People will comment on how your piece resonated with them and point out specific details that they remembered, which ends up being much more meaningful (also, very heartwarming!) Every week, I looked forward to reading others' blog posts and reading comments on mine.

Take this class if you can — it was one of my favorite courses in college, and I have a feeling it will be for you too :)
I think this course made me a better writer. I was forced to write short blogs weekly, and Susan gave extensive written comments on every piece of work. I felt challenged to improve in a way that I didn't in another creative writing course I took in college. Susan is an incredible journalist with great advice, and she cares deeply about students' improvement as writers. She is really organized and devoted to the course. I only wish I had taken it a different semester, because I sometimes felt overwhelmed with deadlines (especially the various chunks of the final paper—lede, first chunk, first draft, etc.), which wasn't at all Susan's fault, but for future students, it might be worth considering what semester you will have time to give the course the attention it deserves. I think if I had taken it earlier in college, I might have been able to make it my focus of the semester. Regardless, the course is definitely worth taking, but will take time and effort. I feel really lucky to have been part of it! The weekly blogs also facilitate a warm classroom environment and closeness I hadn't experienced in a college class before.
Take this class, but take it seriously. If you put in the work for a great final story, the payoff will be incredible; if you put it off, you'll just end up feeling guilty. I kind of wish I hadn't taken this course as a second–semester senior, because I didn't have it in me to give this course everything it deserved. Make sure you have the time and energy to make it a priority every single week!
Love love love this class! Absolutely a must–take
I have never taken a better course at Harvard. I learned how to write, how to think, how to report, and how to grapple with the biggest questions of our time in a narrative longform style. The ability to learn from someone as amazing as Susan is a huge privilege, too. Where else could one receive feedback from such an accomplished writer for one's amateur work? The lessons I learned in CWWR are lessons I will carry with me for the rest of my career and life.
It’s amazing!! Take!!
