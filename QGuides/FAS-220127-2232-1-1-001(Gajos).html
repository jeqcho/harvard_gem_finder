TAKE THIS CLASS! 100%. This is one of the best classes I've ever taken and it truly transformed my approach to making impactful technology. Krzysztof is an incredible professor who is extremely engaging and thoughtful and is always willing to help. I have gotten so much out of this class and I think there isn't a single person who would not benefit from it. It forces you to think critically throughout, and I think the way the material is presented makes it very easy to absorb. The most important thing I learned is that if you want to make impactful technology there is just no way to do that without involving the societal component throughout your process, and there are specific existing ways to do that that we learn in the class. I think society would be much better off if more people took this class. The group of students who take it is also wonderful and you learn a lot from your peers. The assignments are also great, especially the final project, during which you can get really deep into a sociotechnical system and learn so much! You will do yourself so many favors by taking this class, and more than any other I think what you learn will stay with you afterwards.
Come into this class with intellectual humility and be willing to have some of your assumptions and values challenges. Also come prepared to have productive conversation with a diverse and amazing community of people! This may or may not be a transformative experience for you, but it definitely was for me, in the best possible ways.
Take it!!!!! It's so important we have all sorts of different backgrounds represented in this discourse, so regardless of your background, there is something very valuable for you!
TAKE TAKE TAKE this class if you can. I've never seen anything like this offered before and if you're remotely interested in designing sustainable solutions through a technology lens this is it.
First year offering the class, Prof. Gajos put a lot of effort into taking feedback from the students and being adaptable.
One of the most thought–provoking classes I've taken at Harvard. As someone who has had a few years of professional working experience as a designer, this class made me question my design processes and think critically about sociotechnical systems and unintended consequences, and how we can be more cognizant of addressing inequities, accessibility, and unconscious biases throughout the design process. This class isn't just for people interested in design – it's for everyone who creates for others, be it educators, engineers, scientists, doctors, etc. This class was a safe space to explore different perspectives and Krzysztof really encouraged us to explore challenging questions that often didn't have an answer, and that was completely okay. He was also extremely open to feedback and demonstrated a lot of humility that I hope to embody as well. I'd recommend this class to anyone in a heartbeat.
This class was actually one of my favorite CS classes at Harvard. Krzysztof is an amazing professor, and one of the kindest. I also feel like this class is extremely important for most CS concentrators to take because it teaches you about the societal implications of the existing technology and how to design projects that take into account impacts on different communities and unintended consequences to the best of your ability –– something that every person going into tech should be mindful of as they enter industry. 100% recommend to anyone interested in the class content!
This was such a transformative class, and honestly, it is a must–take for anyone involved in creating/designing, or doing anything tangential to technology in the future. You learn so much about the human aspects of creating technology –– from its conception to its implications, and Prof. Gajos does such a great job at embodying humility and facilitating astute, enriching discussions about what we hope to take away from the course.
Super good way to get a chance to think about aspects of technical work that you don't really usually get to explore. Gajos is a wonderful conversation facilitator and I loved meeting different people from different disciplines of work and different Harvard schools.
This is one of the best classes I have ever taken, and I am confident that you would feel the same!
