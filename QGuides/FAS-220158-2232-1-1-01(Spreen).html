What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I absolutely loved this course. Although it was my first history class in college, I found the lectures, readings, and sections extremely interesting and engaging.

Professor Spreen was an awesome lecturer. He presented post–war German history in a way that was historical mindful, but always with a unique and eye–opening lens towards current events. He was also very interactive and organized in terms of clearly laying out what information to prioritize for assessments.

The readings were manageable and very applicable for the key concepts of the course. The lectures also happened to be quite small (6–10 people), allowing each student to get to know the material and professor in a way that was unique from other humanities courses. Additionally, although the quizzes seemed daunting before I enrolled in the class, they were extremely manageable as long as you payed attention to lecture.

Overall, I would absolutely recommend this class for anyone with even a slight interest in modern Western history.
I would highly recommend this course. There is a moderate amount of reading, but the expectations are stated from the beginning and there is no effort on the teaching staff to “pull fast ones” on you.
Great class that teaches you a lot about not just German history but Cold War and Post–War history in general. Class materials are really diverse (movies, music, primary sources, secondary sources) and the topics covered have many diverse perspectives. Plus there was only one paper and a couple straightforward exams. The subject matter is always interesting, and the many perspectives that we had (including a focus on historical historiography) in the class will definitely inform how I understand other eras that I study in future courses.
If you are interested in German history at all, take this course!! You will learn so much more than just the politics after WW2 including women's rights, conceptions of race, and debates about historical memory. You must be willing to put in the work, but it is worth it!
