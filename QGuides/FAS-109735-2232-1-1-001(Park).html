What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I learned so much! I've always been interested in the country and associated foreign policies around North Korea, and this course was really helpful in diving deep into how our current politicians, business interests, and other actors are consuming/making decisions on North Korea. It is definitely not a history course, and 50% of the time we talked about structures/frameworks of analysis, *how* to ask questions, lots of prompting us to give compelling commentary/presentations. Dr. Park comes from a lot of experiences, including consulting background, which is definitely felt. It's a very peer–like environment, where all comments and discussion points are fair to raise and be responded to, which was really cool to experience. Not too much work aside from readings and short comments on canvas each week (and if you find them interesting they won't be arduous), one mini–presentation halfway through the course (which is pretty helpful actually for starting the final paper early) and then of course the final paper. This might just be a Gov department–wide thing but there is a bit less support for finishing the final paper and the research has to be done on your own in order to finish it in time.
Even if you are not interested in North Korea, this is an excellent way to train your critical thinking skills with an sincerely empathetic and incredibly supportive instructor.
The course will challenge you but in a way that truly excites you in the developments of your personal growth and understanding of broader foreign policy issues. North Korea is an ever–evolving, ever–present threat, and having someone like Dr. Park distill those issues into accessible lessons is an invaluable, fulfilling experience. A wonderful class and a truly exceptional professor!
Again, if you do nothing else in your time here, take a class with Dr. Park. My experience in this class has been nothing short of life changing, and I truly mean that. Not only do I feel amazingly prepared to enter any topical discussion on the past, present, and future of North Korea, international security, business and the private sector, cybercrime and the blockchain, and the many other topics we covered, but you also learn fundamental skills that I have never been taught anywhere else that changed the way I approach every aspect of academia, careers, and interactions with peers.
Take it! It’s hard but you will learn so many important aspects if you think you may work in the public sector but also nuggets on how to present in the private sector.
This class is very interesting and allows you insight on a topic that is not really found many places else.
Take this class. It was certainly a highlight of all the classes I've taken so far here at Harvard **
The skills you take away from this class are second to none. They can be applied to various domains in and out of the classroom.
