What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Very good class on energy and future of renewable energies. Professor Hau is very good, very knowledgeable and willing to help students. She cares a lot and will even hold extra office hours to help if you need it.
This class is an excellent class for anyone looking to do a deep dive into the physics behind solar cells and energy storage. The course material is very interesting and Professor Hau teaches everything in an intuitive and simple to understand way. The workload is also fairly low and the final presentation/paper doesn't feel like a ton of work because you can probably choose a topic that's really interesting to you.
This class goes into a lot of depth for semiconductors and photosynthesis. The first half mostly focuses on semiconductors, which is the more interesting half, however, the lectures can be dry. There's not much work that needs to be done outside of class because psets are assigned approximately every 2 weeks. The final project is a bit of a pain to do because you need to deliver a 30 minute presentation during reading period, and write a 6 page single spaced paper as well. Overall, it's not too much work, but it can be a boring class
