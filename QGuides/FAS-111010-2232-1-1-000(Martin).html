I took Math 1A before Math 1B and felt well prepared for the course. The course is less generous than Math 1A in terms of grading (no re–tests) but is well structured in terms of psets, exams, and practice materials. You'll do alright for the exams if you can get good practice with the practice materials. Amadeus was an excellent lecturer, class follows a similar structure to Math 1A where they have you practice problems during class except I felt there was more instruction this time around (which I appreciated). It's kind of difficult to gauge your grade in the class throughout the course as the staff assign letter grades based on your scores at the end, and you only get a little blurb after each exam telling you which range of scores is good, satisfactory, and unsatisfactory. Other than that, solid math course and really does build your calculus knowledge.
