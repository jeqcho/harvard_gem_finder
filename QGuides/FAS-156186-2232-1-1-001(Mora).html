This class was an excellent introduction to philosophy, especially philosophy of law. Emilio and Ryan made a fantastic effort to be accommodating and provide support on the content/assignments, and everything was very thoughtfully planned. I would recommend, though definitely recommend doing all the readings and putting effort into the writing assignments.
You must do the readings
The scope of topics is fantastic and provides a great introduction to many key principles in the philosophy of law. The readings are excellent. Lectures are great – Mora is a wonderful lecturer – though they may feel a little dry at times as they recapitulate the main points of the readings.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Ryan, the section leader, is phenomenal, and very funny. Professor Mora is very kind and gives excellent feedback. Lectures are a little dull at times, but the course content itself is engaging.
This was my first philosophy class, and it was such a wonderful introduction to philosophy reading and writing. Emilio's lecture notes are extremely clear and his lectures follow them very closely. The readings are really interesting and flow naturally throughout the class, and I really appreciated the teaching staff's efforts to figure out students' interests and tailor the readings to them. Ryan's sections were also just so fun, and though he did a good job of keeping us on track he is also very fun and welcomed brief humorous diversions. I had a really fun time working on the assignment as well, which scale up naturally over the semester cumulating in a ~10–page paper on essentially whatever you want in the class.
If you are interested in law, take this course. Moreover, the texts become easier to understand with more practice. Emilio is also very helpful in making sure that all can understand the content.
Great class. Prof. Mora is a great lecturer. The workload is pretty manageable. One "short" paper (1800–2200 words) and one "long" paper (2300–2800 words). There are lots of OH for help with the papers. The readings are very interesting and cover a wide range of topics. Grading seemed pretty reasonable so far.
This class is extremely informative and engaging. Taking the class will change your entire perception of the legal system and your understanding of the legal obligations of society. The one caveat I had with this class was my unfamiliarity with lecture style teaching that did not employ the use of any white boards, technology, or presentations as the professor lectured. There were handouts to use in class but no visual aids to follow–along with. As long as you are comfortable with that teaching style, then this class is well–worth it and everyone should take it.
The only thing I would say is to do your best to keep up with the class readings.
This class is great! Make sure you do the readings – the material is much more interesting if you’ve read the authors we discuss in lecture. The readings are the main course material and the lectures just explain them more in depth.
take this class if you are interested in legal theory (not law school or real world law) and want an introductory course to philosophy
