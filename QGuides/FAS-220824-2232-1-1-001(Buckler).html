What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is lovely! It gave me an amazing first overview into 20th century modern literature, and has gotten me excited about reading more works by these authors in the future. Professor Buckler is great, and it's a really collaborative format in that the students can suggest readings and guide where discussions go/what sorts of assignments we do. I had a lot of fun.
Great class, worth taking and learning more about Russian literature
Definitely worth taking. The minimum requirement for the course is third year Russian, but I think fourth year would be more appropriate. The texts can be quite difficult and time consuming if you find yourself looking up every second or third word. You'll get out what you put in––can definitely coast through (esp if you're a heritage speaker) if you really need to.
If you want a real Russian literature course, but you're not a literature PhD or native Russian speaker, this is for you. These classes were often the highlight of my week.
I would definitely take this course if you're looking to simultaneously work on your Russian reading skills/vocab command and expand your understanding of major authors/works/literary concepts. I would say that the combo of Russian readings and English assignments/discussion makes this one of the most efficient ways to simultaneously progress those goals. (I would note that your Russian listening comprehension, grammar, and speaking skills probably won't be helped by this course.)
