Take this expos! People say expos is kind of a drag, but almost everyone in my class said this was one of their favorite classes this year. Dr. Quigley is warm and informative, making class a place to ask questions, connect with students, and dive into difficult topics. This course is the perfect mix of science, history, and English, as we often write with and read more scientific styles, while discussing many social and political aspects—I went in with little scientific background and was totally fine! The grading is overall quite fair; in conferenced she helped your craft the essay so that each paragraph has a specific goal toward your argument—as long as you create a coherent and interesting thesis, you’ll be fine! I learned so much about the history and contemporary perspectives of psychology and gender, questioning how diagnosis are influenced by a bias and capitalist society and questioning why we assume sex is a binary. Seriously, I highly recommend this course… I think everyone should take it!
