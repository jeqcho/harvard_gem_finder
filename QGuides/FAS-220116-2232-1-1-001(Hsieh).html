Great class! Perfect as a capstone, reflection–oriented course for senior spring.
One thing that we think about a lot once we enter college is our career––what should I do after college? What do I need to do now to make the best out of my future? What are my goals? This class will give you the opportunity to think about this question concretely. Weekly readings will give you a glimpse into the exemplary/cautionary works, lives, and purposes of other people. During in–class discussions, you’ll get to hear other students’ thoughts on work, which will give you a more well–rounded perspective on your career. I think taking the time to talk about these types of things is important, and my perspective about how to choose my job has transformed throughout the course of the semester.

This class is also a gem!

There is not much work but a really good opportunity to think deeply about what you value in your work and personal life. In addition, Professor Hsieh opts for real–life cases instead of dense philosophical readings, which I think is a positive. In the current form of the class, we didn't spend too much time explicitly mapping out our own career choices. So, definitely do not expect to have figured out your career by the end. Rather, the cases more liberally talk about the meaning of work in our own lives and others. I would say definitely take this course if you think a fair amount about your own career and want to experience a really engaging case study–styled course!
