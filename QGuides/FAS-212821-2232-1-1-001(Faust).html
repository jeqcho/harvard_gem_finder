One of the best classes I ever took here. Professor Faust is a legend and she shows how well she knows the material and teaching in this. Met so many amazing guests who gave us great insights. Peers like to talk a lot and that can be daunting but you can learn a lot from them and the material.
Without a doubt, this course is one of the greatest I have ever enrolled in at Harvard. There are four main strengths that come to mind.

The greatest strength of HL–90DR was Professor Faust herself. She is an extremely personable, thoughtful, and intelligent professor, and she guided every class conversation in a productive way. Professor Faust made herself very accessible through office hours, and was always willing to provide feedback over email as well. Plus, she has a fascinating collection of stories to share!

A second strength of the course consisted of its students, who hailed from a diverse array of "Harvard backgrounds". This made student contributions interesting and eye–opening, and made the class even more enjoyable.

A third strength of the course was its syllabus: we covered a very broad and interesting collection of speeches in the course. The opportunity to do so helped us discern nuances between different "genres" of speeches, and to better appreciate oratory as an art.

A final strength of the course consisted of its guest speakers. We had the chance to interact with multiple individuals who have an intimate relationship with speech–writing and oratory, and this experience allowed us to learn about public speaking in a more engaging fashion.
This was the best course I have taken at Harvard. Professor Faust is an incredible instructor, from her deep understanding of American history and public speaking to her keen interest in how her students are doing both inside and outside of the classroom. While this course provides you with a comprehensive overview of American oratory, it ultimately challenges you to reflect on and improve your individual public speaking. I can not recommend it enough and feel fortunate to have had the opportunity to take it while it was offered.

This was a result of many things: Professor Faust's incredible knowledge and leadership, my classmates' preparation and thoughtfulness, and the fact that speeches provide an absolutely wonderful venue for the sorts of analysis we were performing. Speeches were a great way to explore history — and also a fascinating way to dive deeply into questions about performance and persuasion. Such a tremendous course!!
