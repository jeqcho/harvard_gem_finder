Unfortunately, this is the last year Wastelands will be available, but if you are reading this because you are considering taking Collier's new Expos class, TAKE IT. Collier is one of the best instructors I met in my first year, and the amount of enthusiasm he generates for the topic matter is unmatched. He is very accessible both inside and outside of class, and he will always be happy to chat with you and give advice about how to make your writing better. I would whole–heartedly recommend this class to everyone, including STEM majors like me; no matter your background or interests, you will find this class enjoyable.
Take this class! Wastelands is really great – you learn how to analyze fiction and nonfiction texts, and the workload is not bad at all compared to other expos sections.
Great expos, definitely (***). Professor Brown is very kind and understanding. Excellent essay feedback. Great office hour conversations about improving your essay or just about life. Not a lot of work at all during the weeks. Class often ends early.
Dr. Brown is an incredibly kind, empathetic person that encourages you to take what you've learned in his class and apply it to writing assignments in other classes. He takes a genuine interest in what students have to say, the topics they want to write about, and where they want to take their studies/lives beyond Expos.

Each unit is clearly defined in what it's trying to teach you. Listen carefully to his directions on your first draft and in your conference, and you'll succeed.
This is a good class, as Collier is a really great teacher. His essay assignments are reasonable and he gives very good feedback. He is very passionate about what he teaches.
It's not a very reading heavy class, but the feedback might be a little confusing, and the preceptor is super busy so sometimes he might not be able to meet unless it's like a required conference.
Although it probably won't be offered again, Wastelands was one of the best classes I've taken. The documentaries we watch are very interesting and inspiring, the readings of the short stories are interesting, and Dr. Brown is extremely supportive and encourages discussion in classes. The essays are very reasonable and allow for a lot of freedom to what we want to write, and the office hours and conference times are amazing. Dr. Brown is truly a joy and I would love to take more of his classes in the future!***
Professor Brown is incredibly nice and approachable, and usually very willing to help. With that being said, not all of the content is interesting. Furthermore, I wish I was able to actually learn how to write and what is expected of me. I got some helpful tips, but a lot of times I felt very lost. With that being said, the workload is manageable and some of the texts were incredibly fascinating. For an expos class, I would recommend it.
Unfortunately, this is the last year Collier is teaching wastelands – but he'll be doing a different Expos next year! RANK HIM HIGHLY YOU WILL NOT REGRET IT
