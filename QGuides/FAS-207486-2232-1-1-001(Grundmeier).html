This class is great. Moderate–to–high workload (9–12hr/wk if you want to really do the PSETs well and deeply understand the material), but it creates such a good community that PSETting feels like hanging out with friends during office hours (because it kind of is). This also does not sacrifice a rigorous treatment of the material at hand, and I definitely feel prepared to take higher upper–level math classes (112,122, etc) both from a mathematical preparation and proof–writing skill point of view. PSETs are also interesting and explore cool cracks in the definition that may otherwise slip through (why does Clairaut's Theorem only hold for C^2 functions? Are there functions that are not integrable but still have a definite integral?), and office hours are spread out appropriately throughout the week (shoutout Alice and Anamaria for the GOAT conjoined office hours on Sunday afternoons).

Grading is pretty chill. 60% of your grade is determined by 3 midterms, which were fair in difficulty and you were given problems to adequately practice on, so no worries there. Another 20% is homework, 5% is computational exercises, and the final 15% is the final project that we have in lieu of a final exam (and it is so much more fun and takes so much pressure off of finals week, 100% recommended) where you can explore whatever you want provided it has rigorous treatment of multivariable calculus and algebra.

DEFINITELY shop this class, and I really hope the next professor is good, because they will have big, big shoes to fill.
