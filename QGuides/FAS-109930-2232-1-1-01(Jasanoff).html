HIST 97 is not easy, but is certainly effective and valuable to concentrators. Learning to write effectively and how to use primary and secondary sources is crucial to diving into any historical knowledge. The readings can be dense, but the writing assignments build on one another. By the time you reach the final paper you are fully prepared. The TFs in this course are an excellent resource for your writing and to bounce ideas off of. They want to see you succeed.
This is a great Hist 97! Prof Jasanoff is very knowledgeable and accomplished in her field, and can definitely seem intimidating, but genuinely cares about pedagogy and wants the students grow as writers. Be prepared for lots of writing, but if you start early and communicate with your tutors, you should be all good! Don't expect to learn much imperial history content (the course is much more about thinking/writing about imperial history rather than facts), but nevertheless was quite interesting.
