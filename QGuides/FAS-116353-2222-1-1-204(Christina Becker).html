I don't think this course is being offered next semester, but I would cautiously recommend this course to future potential students. Work is pretty light for the first month or so, but then once you pick your exhibit and start writing it gets difficult. Most ppl stick with the same exhibit from the beginning, which is difficult because if you find your evidence doesn't end up supporting your claim, you have to do some major readjustments. But also don't recommend chnaging your exhibit bc that's even more work. I love Professor Becker and class is fun––we jump from exhibit to exhibit just talking about what we see and building off of each other. One warning: the professor only has one hour for office hours every week, and the 15 min slots fill up fast. You only meet with her once one–on–one throughout the course.
This expos is not the most interesting one, but for people interested in museums it is a good choice. Not a huge workload compared to others, and the topic is also somewhat interesting.
Don't get caught up in the museum aspect of the class. It is merely a topic which defines the parameters of the class, but you should focus on writing and analysis, rather than the museums themselves, history, etc. While analyzing museums themselves will seem superficial and uninteresting, delving deeper into the mechanisms of writing and going through the process is truly what you should take out of the class (not what you learn about a museum, its exhibits, their framing, lighting, etc.)
Doctor Becker is an incredible professor. She is very passionate and loves what she teaches
This was a great class, and it was a great way to write and learn about art, art history, and architecture. I really enjoyed the research assignments, where I was given the freedom to write about topics that interested me. Choosing a museum I liked from the start definitely helped too.
The workload is definitely manageable, but it felt difficult to master the art of reading museums in a span of a couple weeks before the first essay was due. If you are really interested in museums, however, I would recommend taking the class.
This class was great! The content was really interesting and Dr. Becker was a great preceptor. Not having to write a 3rd paper is definitely a huge advantage. Overall a great Expos course and I would definitely recommend it.
EXPOS in general is honestly about forming a cohesive enough argument to make it through the class.
A great choice for expos!! Great professor, interesting readings, engaging discussions.
