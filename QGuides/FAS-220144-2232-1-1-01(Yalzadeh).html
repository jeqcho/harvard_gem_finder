What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course was an incredible dive into United States racial history and its ties to empire. The readings were thoughtful and eye–opening, discussion was always fruitful, and I was challenged to view our country with a more critical lens that I ever have before. Take it if you can!
Definitely take this course. You learn so much about the United States involvement in other countries – particularly in Latin America.
This course has some interesting readings but I didn't get as much out of it as I hoped to. I felt that class discussion focused far too much on extrapolating sprawling, abstract ideas about racism and colonialism that weren't very grounded in the specifics of the history we were reading at any particular time—this had the effect of dulling the fruitfulness of conversations. I wish we had spent time examining early racial identity formation in the U.S. and gotten more perspectives from other historical scholars in our readings. This class also suffers from too much busy work as it stands—particularly with excessive, repetitive weekly zine journals (which were often on zines that were unrelated to course content and that we wouldn't discuss in class) and the lengthy weekly annotations, which often felt contrived/lent itself to us rehashing the same ideas each week.
Definitely take the class! You will learn so incredibly much and the final zine project is a great opportunity to explore a subject of your interest in depth in a way that reaches beyond the constraints of typical final papers.
