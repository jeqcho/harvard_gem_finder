This class has a phenomenal reading list, but could have been organized better. Terry would assign us excellent readings, but then allow students to fully direct the flow of the conversation for the full two hours. The pacing often suffered because of this. There were times when we would read a really interesting book or paper, but never discuss it in class because the conversation never came around to it. Often we’d have five/six readings but only discuss one or two of them—making it hard to motivate myself to finish all the readings. It also felt like we often spent lots of time on minor details, so that the core ideas of a reading were never discussed.

We got weekly emails with our reading assignments, but they were sometimes confusing or poorly worded.

On the other hand, Terry is an amazingly thoughtful, committed teacher who really cares about his students and has a deep knowledge of the subject matter. The go–with–the–flow design of the class did allow us students a lot of chances to discuss and debate with one another, which is always fun.
It is a great class if you are interested in law, but you want to be certain that is an interest because it is very legal heavy. If you have some background in legal writing/reading that is much more helpful.

It isn't purely about the law, rather about the implications of the law on various social issues. If that appeals to you, take the class. If you are taking this as an introduction to law–school type courses, choose something else. Be willing to make the most of it, as your papers can be about anything, not just readings from the course.
TAKE THIS TUTORIAL if you have any interest in law and human rights. Terry is absolutely wonderful. Incredibly friendly, understanding, and kind, he was always accessible outside of class, ready to strike up conversation after class ended, and great at guiding discussion while still giving us students the most agency in directing it. Only a midterm and final essay, which is a lot less work than many other tutorials, and the readings were truly fascinating. If you enjoy legal debate, court cases, and general pondering of how the law relates and affects each one of us differently, you will enjoy this tutorial.
The 15 page midterm essay is unrelated to the final essay and you receive the prompt ~1 week in advance.
Terry is a wonderful teacher! I highly recommend taking this course.
