If you are interested in constitutional law, you like to read dense material consistently, and you want a professor who goes above and beyond for his students, TAKE THIS CLASS IMMEDIATELY. I think Ross is the core and shining star of this course. You can see the effort he puts into making this class FOR his students. He tells us time and time again that he immediately changes his course curriculum based on the needs and desires of us, his students. He's so willing to listen to any accommodations you may need or concerns you have about a paper. You're stressed and confused and experiencing writer's block? He'll work with you to help you through that. He has been SO patient and kind and understanding throughout my writing journey in Expos that I genuinely don't think I would have gotten through this often difficult course (mostly due to the course material itself being quite difficult and dense) without him. All my thanks to Ross – he's awesome. Now, take this course.
This is my favorite class from freshman year!! It is SO interesting and Ross is such an amazing professor. That being said, it is definitely one of the harder expos sections, and reading heavy at the beginning (it gets better as the semester progresses). The readings are mostly Supreme Court decisions, which are super interesting, but also definitely not for everybody. If you are willing to accept the challenge that this class will bring, it will be so worth it and you will get so much out of it.
Take this class!!
This class is different from other expos' in that is ONLY reads and uses supreme court cases as texts for analysis, including for your papers (except for the final research paper). So if you really want a wide variety of legal literature, maybe this is not as appealing. The topics are also somewhat limited, so its not a broad survey course of the all the major supreme court cases, so that you can focus in on dissecting a few. Most of the branching out into different persepctives relies heavily on the class discussion, which then can be hit or miss depending on your section I think. Mine was good, and felt really welcoming so I did enjoy that! Ross is also just a really understanding guy and is very willing to meet you where you're at and work with you.
Take this class, especially if you're considering law school! It might be somewhat challenging but I grew as a writer and developed a deeper understanding of the law.
I was on the fence about taking this class since I have taken a similar class where I had to read court decisions. However, I am glad I ended up ranking this class as my first choice. This class allows you to further explore America's history in conjunction with the Supreme Court's holdings through the lens of personhood. The topic itself was immensely interesting and worth your consideration if you are inclined to learn more about hidden classism and racism is hidden within American history. On one hand, it deals with dense text that can make you cross–eyed after a while but on another hand, you can better learn about what it means to be a person philosophically, psychologically, biologically, and of course, constitutionally. An added bonus is that you then remember each case by name.

The class did not focus as much on teaching you how to write as much as I thought it would. However, the discussions that arose from the class were some of the best discussions I have ever had. The professor grades fairly.
As a STEM student, this writing class about constitutional law opened my eyes to how interesting the Constitution can be and how exciting it is to see my writing progress throughout the semester. Ross is a great instructor – he provides great feedback (through written feedback and conferences) and is even willing to meet with his students outside of class time to work with them further on their essays. He also leads the discussions in a way so that students can think more deeply about the subject, which is also helpful when brainstorming ideas for essays. Highly recommend this Expos class!
Take this course! It is a little bit difficult but Dr. Martin is a wonderful professor and you will truly learn a lot about writing.
I would tell future students to take this class because they get to read about supreme court cases that have impacted the history of the United States. However, for your essays, you can write about issues relating to the court cases that have the greatest impact on your life and what you are passionate about.
I LOVE THIS CLASS! I LOVE ROSS! The class might be a little challenging, but it is very, very rewarding. Though the essays you are writing and cases you are reading might seem intimidating at first, Ross is so so willing to help at any point of your learning and this makes all the difference. I also think reading and analyzing cases is just a great way to learn how to write, because the cases themselves are very heavily based on logic just like expos essays and good essays in general are. The course was such a good training for my writing and helped me so much in bridging from high school writing to college writing. It is valuable to my learning in all logic–based and/or writing–based courses and topics. It does require time and will to think hard, but I think this is what a good class should be like. PLEASE TAKE THE COURSE. IT'S SO PRECIOUS. I LOVE IT.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! It is absolutely amazing. Definitely my favorite class this semester by far. It was also my most difficult class this semester, but it was 100% worth it and totally rewarding. You get to learn so much about reading and writing for college but in a really interesting way because the class is more uniquely focused on deep discussion. Constitutional law has never been so interesting to you until now!
If you like thinking, court cases, and want to do meaningful assignments, this is the class for you. The beginning is for sure not a walk in the park but it gets much easier as time goes on. I can't speak for other EXPOS classes, but you ACTUALLY become a better writer and wrestle with incredible concepts here. This is the type of Harvard class you dreamed about. + Ross is a kind man.
**AMAZING choice for EXPOS** This course is not for everyone, but if you are even remotely interested in American history, constitutional law, or the debate on abortion, you absolutely should consider ranking this EXPOS highly. Even if you don't think you are interested in these topics, this course is the perfect way to learn more about it. Ross works with each student individually and is readily accessible to ensure that you can succeed. His sense of humor and wit makes class discussions lively and engaging. At the beginning of this course, I wasn't considering going to law school at all, but this course empowered me to consider that as a potential path forward. There are three papers (4–5 pages, 5–6 pages, and 8–10 pages) and weekly readings. The first reading (Dred Scott) is probably the roughest one you'll have to do. This is an EXPOS you'll always look forward to attending.
Do the readings and don't be afraid to ask for help; Dr. Martin is a great professor and will always be willing to help you out in your writing and learning process.

Get excited –– you'll learn a lot.
