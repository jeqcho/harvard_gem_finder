What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Allen is very interesting and so are her ideas! Even if you're not super into political theory — this was my first class in the subfield — you'll take something away from this class. It'll also incentivize you to participate, and you'll realize that you have more to offer to a discussion than you might have expected.
This is a remarkable course that attracts students with a wide range of backgrounds who share a commitment to justice. While the course is policy–heavy, it is one of the few courses at Harvard that will provide insight into the theoretical and philosophical reasons for why and how we should commit to conducting good politics. Take this class if you are interested in public service, political campaigning, or living a good life in general. Under the brilliant tutelage of Danielle Allen, you will walk away from this course with a theoretical toolkit to make better sense of the world and how to improve it.
This course would have been more powerful had it been a section, but the lecture format made it difficult to engage with the material at a more intimate level. I appreciated my peers quite a bit, and wish I could have learned more from them.
This class is an amazing opportunity that allows you to explore a new paradigm of democracy amongst passionate students and policymakers. However, it is a challenging course that can leave you feeling out of place if you do not have a strong foundation in public policy and philosophy (as I did not). If you are willing to go through that and learn things that may seem very out of reach, take a chance and take this amazing course!
Danielle Allen is a genius, and listening to her will be one of the highlights of the class. She'll also bring in a bunch of cool people and readings to discuss. Overall good class.
This is a great introduction to the ethics and values of policymaking.
If you've ever been curious about what it's like to take an HKS course, I highly recommend this one because it's a cool mix between HKS while still having a separate aspect geared towards undergrads which makes it less daunting. Professor Allen is a wonderful lecturer with very moving life experiences so I would tell you to take this class for that reason alone. This class is one of those examples of a course where I thought to myself "Oh, that's why it's so awesome having opportunities like hearing from policy experts every week at a place like Harvard".
Definitely take this class if you want to engage with paradigms of liberalism and how to create progress in a paradigmatic manner. Don't be afraid to engage, please read – it helps with engagement. And take advantage of the opportunity to take a course with adult policymakers, journalists, and activists. It is an enriching experience.
I recommend this class to those in government because it is a good way to get a taste of what policy work is like and learn form grad students just as much as you learn from the professor.
Danielle Allen is an awesome teacher... she is very approachable and encouraging. Her book is fascinating and will change the way you think about democracy. The assignments are manageable in terms of time/load, but we didn't receive feedback on them so I didn't know how I was doing until the final grade came out. Be prepared for a fair amount of reading (you will read Allen's whole book plus excerpts of lots of foundational texts)––– I'm glad that I read them though because I now have a good grasp of basic theories related to political economy (Constant, Rawls, Keynes, etc.). The course sessions were long this year (2.45 hrs), so it was sometimes hard to stay focused throughout. But we got a break in the middle and Allen does her best to keep it exciting. I definitely recommend this course because the issue of democracy will be front and center in the coming years––– you will walk away feeling prepared to contribute to conversations about it.
This is a great class if you like applied theory and you want to engage with Danielle Allen, one of the most genius policy thinkers of our time. Pick her brain and get smarter.
If you’re looking for a more engaged theory class this is the one !
This is a really interesting class that will push you to think about philosophy and politics in a new way. Professor Allen leads great sessions. As a non–gov concentrator, I learned a lot.
