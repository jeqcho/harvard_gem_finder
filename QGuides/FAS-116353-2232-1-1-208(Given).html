What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Definitely take this course for your Expos requirement if you have nay interest at all in the history of art. It focuses on discussions about past and present repatriation movements, and the field trips are extremely informative and engaging. The final paper, in which you get the chance to do original research on an item of your choice, is long, but it can be exciting if you do an item with a history that you are interested in. This can make it much easier to write your paper, because there is a level of personal investment, and it becomes a story that you are passionate about telling in your writing.
No matter what expos class you are in, the writing will be painful. Choose a topic that is interesting and engaging, and you will at least have something to write in those painful papers.
This is an art history class! We focus on artifacts, not necessarily "who owns culture"
You need to be prepared to dedicate a certain amount of time reading and researching to make this course worthwhile. Be prepared to spend more time editing papers as well, as Dr. Given really cares about the revision process and how much you are able to edit to create a smooth and effective paper. Overall though I would recommend this course, as I was able to find something that interested me and motivated my drive to continue researching and learning more about artifacts, especially in my research paper.
Although this is a new expos section, I cannot recommend it enough. Prof. Given will work with you if you are struggling with any aspect of the course and I got to critically assess Harvard's role in systems that other classes have you read about. I loved being immersed in museum/library visits and Dr. Given will always relay opportunities to engage with course material in a non–classroom setting by emailing out information on campus events and more. This course also seriously made me consider concentrating in history and/or anthropology.
take this class if you're interested in the topic, the readings and workload during the week isn't bad at all but grading is harsher than other expos classes
This class is so incredibly interesting! There isn't too much work at all, I was never overly stressed about an assignment. And Dr. Given is such a great professor!
Dr. Given is an effective educator and gives helpful feedback. However, I felt that he expected you to figure out some areas for improvement in your writing on your own. For example, he made a comment on my draft and I corrected it. Then on my revision, he made a comment on something that he did not mention on my draft submission. If he had pointed it out earlier, I feel that my grade for my paper could have been better. But again, he is very good at being an educator!
While this is a really interesting topic for Expos, I am uncertain if it is worth the trouble. I enjoyed this class because I love museums and debates with a philosophical background (questions about ownership and ethics), but the grading has been harsher than what I have seen my friends in other expos classes go through. It's a bit unfair, but that's life. Our final research paper is also more complicated than that of other expos classes, as it requires us to choose an object from Harvard's collections and do work that is much more in the weeds than what other classes do. It is not easy by any means, but it certainly is interesting. Take this specific expos only if you find the subject very interesting and are willing to put in some extra work.
If you enjoy visiting museums or examining artefacts at all and want to learn and discuss about the history and meaning of an object and how that fits into (very) current debates about their repatriation (and more!), pick this class! The field trips to museums and discussions with curators are absolutely amazing, the essay topics are diverse and there is a good amount of freedom especially for the final research essay. I also appreciated Dr. Given reading complete drafts and giving extensive feedback both via in–line comments and more general thoughts at the end. Overall it was lots of fun and I loved going to class despite skipping lunch every time. For me, the topic provided a fun theme through which to improve my writing!
