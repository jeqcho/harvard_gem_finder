What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The final weeks of the class are hectic. You have a pset, project, and take–home final.
The course is more of an "advanced probability theory" course than a CS course.
I feel that a student with strong fundamentals in mathematics can take the course, even if they know nothing about programming (which can be picked up along the way).
On the contrary, a student with proficient CS knowledge who doesn't feel confident about mathematics will suffer brutally.
If you find CS theory even vaguely interesting I would strongly recommend this class. Mitzenmacher moves fairly quickly but nothing unmanageable. The topics are well thought out and the book provides an excellent reference – definitely one of the better textbooks I've read. The end of the class can get to be a bit work–heavy if you haven't started the final project early enough.
If you are interested in learning about randomized algorithms and the many different ways to analyze them, then you will enjoy this course's content. However, it is a lot of work. The lectures move very quickly and are quite dense as well, so you will likely have to spend some time after going through them again or sitting with the textbook. The problem sets are similar as well, in that some of the questions will likely require a few hours of thinking before they can be solved. This course is also *very* math heavy, so you should be very comfortable with probability before taking this, comparable to a good understanding of everything in Stat 110. However, Professor Mitzenmacher is very accessible outside of class, and his OHs can be very helpful in relieving some of this work.

Also, be aware that there is both a final exam and a final project, which is different from many other grad–level classes. Both will take a considerable amount of time, especially the final project which should be started at least a month in advance. Don't expect this class to ease out during finals period!
