What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Gov 97 was a good experience. Some people did not enjoy it, this is because it all depends on your section. Make sure you are interested in the topic of your section. The assignments are not bad at all, and they build on each other. After the Lit Review you basically just add 2–4 pages to it for every assignment. I took Drawing Democracy with Chris and it was great. I highly recommend. The guest lecturers are super interesting too!
Gov97 gets a lot of mixed reviews (often depending on your section topic and interest level in research/academia). For me, it was just ok. The week to week work load is light (relatively short readings and discussion post) however, the written assignments are very time consuming, and take a lot of effort to plan effectively. The grading is very tough and unpredictable, and you may find that even your best effort only earns a B. While the rubric can act as a guide, ultimately you're grade is determined in part at the discretion of your TF. Definitely make sure you pick a topic you're really interested in, as all of the assignments in this class culminate in a final paper focused on a tangential topic. Also, all of the weekly readings are focused on this topic (which can quickly become repetitive and feel a bit redundant) The best part of Gov97 is definetly the guest speaker lectures. I learned a lot from these lectures, and the topics and perspectives very quite varied and engaging.
Gov 97 isn't normally an option but I would say it's important to choose a subject matter you are interested in because it makes the readings and paper much more enjoyable! Also, it never hurts to ask your tutors about the grad student TFs when those are released before choosing your section so you know which ones are nice :)
