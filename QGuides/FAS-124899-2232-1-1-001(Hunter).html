What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You don't learn anything that is in any way useful to your research in the next 5–6 years or anything about genetics of 2010 onwards.
This class is going to suck if they don't change it next year. Just show up, get your attendance points, and sit through it. If you like historical science, this is the class for you.
You may not need to read every paper until you know every detail. The 1st priority should be to understand what is being stated in these papers to a reasonable degree based on your time availability. This will help each class function at its full capacity and serve each student. Then, the next priority should be in finding something exciting or debatable about the material. We're here to learn how to be scientists, and that is tiring. However, a scientist should be familiar with science in other fields, even if they aren't particularly interested in it. It keeps the science moving forward. All that being said, take this opportunity to do what will improve you as you start your Ph.D.
