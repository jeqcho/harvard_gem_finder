Take this class.
Depending on your sub–team, the class can be very self–led, which is good for learning how to do things on your own but hard for understanding all the concepts and next steps. Just something to keep in mind! Otherwise, it's a fantastic way to get some research experience, develop a passion or research question you're interested in, and meet incredibly learned people in an area of interest! Angie is absolutely wonderful and very accessible over email (despite being a bit busy), and Alain is incredibly knowledgeable, always available to help out if possible, and very funny!
This course had the potential to be great, but I often felt like I had no idea what I was doing and didn't know how to get on track. This made for a stressful semester.
good way to get into research – depends on which project you are part of
TAKE IT! For most of the semester, it is just about showing up to lab 6 hours and a 2–hour class (which usually ends early). The presentations are very chill, and if your group splits it well then you can possibly only go up once or do small parts. Also, the lab portion is enjoyable, you can do work and talk with your project leader and teammates and get to know them. They are to help you and answer all your questions. Just make sure you work on the paper early since its due sometime during the reading period. But even if you aren't from a science background, I think its very do–able.
It definitely helps if you have research/lab experience because it'll just be easier to understand things. Especially since the course is pretty unstructured and it's just kind of up to you. You do get a mentor to guide you but you kind of have to figure it out yourself sometimes. It does really give you a good exposure to what research/lab work is like. Like different protocols, methods and just how long it can take to perform an experiment etc.
You’ll have to organize most of the stuff with your group by yourself.
If you want to get a feel for what lab is like, this is the class for you! It will expose you to many procedures and teach you also how to create your own experiments and hypotheses.
This is a great course for anyone who wants a guided approach to starting research. It is a very flexible course since you are able to have autonomy on when you want to go into lab.
