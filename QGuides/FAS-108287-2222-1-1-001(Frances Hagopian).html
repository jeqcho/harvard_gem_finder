If you want to write a senior thesis you should definitely take this class. A lot of deadlines for junior summer research etc. are actually quite early in the spring, around spring break, so unless you're immersed in talking/thinking about your thesis it can be easy to miss those deadlines. This class provides structure to be refining your topic and helping you feel so much more prepared for your thesis. And Aaron (if he's still TF–ing) is one of the most helpful people to talk to about turning vague research ideas into real, functional research designs. One of the professors mentioned that this is like where the "AP students" of Gov gather together—so get those AP credits for your thesis!
This is a MUST TAKE for anyone even thinking about writing a Gov Dept. thesis. Incredible course and SO SO SO useful!
Awesome thesis preparation course! At times really boring but worth it to take it
This class is very helpful for people who want to write a thesis. However, it's also very demanding and stressful, because it forces you to make decisions about the thesis very early on, when you are (at least I was) unprepared for it. That being said, I don't think it's worth it to take this very challenging class, unless you're pretty sure you'll be writing a thesis. If you're in that category, you'll definitely have a much better understanding of what that process entails once you take it (BUT it will require a lot of effort).
This class feels more so like a semester of thesis preparation and workshopping than a qualitative methods class.
This class is one of the best ones in the gov department. The teaching staff is wonderful, always available, always building onto your ideas to make them better, and always supportive. Your classmates will become a great support for your first leap toward thesis writing. You will really feel like you learned something at the end of the semester, not just like you learned a lot of readings. I highly recommend this class, the work is very worth it.
TAKE THIS CLASS! If you are passionate about writing a thesis, this course will prepare you well to do so. The teaching team is supportive and knowledgeable, always there to help you build and refine your research.

At times, this class can be stressful but all the work that you're doing is working towards your own thesis project so it feels meaningful. I cannot imagine going into my senior thesis without having the experience of this class. They break down the process for you and make an extremely stressful and elusive thing, like a thesis, more understandable and possible. Professor Hagopian is a sweetheart and tries to get to know and help all her students with their individual topics. She is available to talk to you and can point you in the direction of more information. If you are lucky enough to get Aaron as a tf, you will have a great support system throughout the semester. He is also incredibly helpful and made the class a great time.
Super helpful class if you're planning to write a thesis – a bunch of work for sure, and will often make you consider things at a time that you feel like is too early, but overall it was good to be pushed to think about these challenging questions about my research project. If you plan on writing a thesis, this class will give you a huge leg–up.

This class is a MUST TAKE if you plan to write a thesis in government! Professor Hagopian is amazing and the feedback and planning assistance you get is invaluable. While it requires time and effort, the class is meant to actually help you, not to be ridiculously difficult.
Really good if you want to write a gov thesis and get a head start.
This is an invaluable course for developing a thesis idea, as it effectively serves as a placeholder in your junior spring to allow you the time and space to workshop your ideas. It's a great crash course through all the potential components in a Gov thesis and leaves you feeling confident that you didn't leave any major stone unturned while conceiving of a research design. Class can get dry due to the length of lectures and discussions, though, and the frequent writing assignments are quite stressful each week. My biggest advice if you're taking this class is to start thinking about a thesis topic during the winter break/early on (though it's totally common to change topics during the semester — I did so myself, and might even do it again over the summer) and take advantage of office hours, to which the teaching staff is very generous about dedicating so much time.
This course is going to be challenging for everyone. The weekly readings are an insane amount, not counting your outside research. Plus, the biweekly assignments can feel like they are stacking up rather quickly. The lectures are about 2 hours long and cover an insane amount of content. However, if you put in the work this class will be amazing. It will make you feel prepared for your senior thesis and you will feel better about your research. Not to mention, you get to meet and work with other people who are also considering doing a senior thesis so it makes it feel more manageable. That being said, this isn't a class to slack off in. Do the readings, assignments, and work and you'll get the most out of the class. Also this is the class where you should make going to office hours a priority. Good luck!

Truly, they went through each aspect of the thesis and prospectus in a digestible, weekly format. They also paired it with real–time, department announcements like thesis advisors and grant applications. I wouldn't have applied for a grant or found my thesis advisor without their guidance and notice. TAKE GOV62 IF YOU'RE THINKING OF WRITING A THESIS – it's honestly a really valuable introduction to research questions, planning, and writing I haven't gotten anywhere else in the department.

This course is absolutely essential if you are considering writing a thesis, even if you are planning to use solely quantitative methods.
