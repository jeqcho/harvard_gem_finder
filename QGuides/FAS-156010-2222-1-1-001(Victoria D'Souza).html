Great class overall. Victoria D'Souza is incredibly knowledgable about virus biology and does a good job of presenting the material in lecture. Lectures can be a bit boring at times, but discussion section is always engaging. This is on the lighter side as far as course work goes, only 1 summary of a paper per week + a final presentation at the end of the semester. I entered the class without any particular interest in virology and feel as though I'm leaving with similar interest level for the material. however, the course does effectively push you to develop paper reading skills, critical thinking, and communication.
Make sure you read the papers. You will get out of this class what you put into it, so take advantage of the opportunity to engage with research literature without stress
This is an amazing 'journal club' class where you'll really learn how to read papers (as you do it on a weekly basis) and you are really pushed / challenged to think about the alternatives and the limitations to the paper or future directions. Definitely take this class if you want to become more proficient at reading papers.
Go to lectures & Read papers ahead of time and you'll be fine!
You should totally take this class! It's a really great way to learn a lot of cool new things about viruses and their replication cycle and how they interact with the host without too much effort. Only work is just reading one paper a week and writing a 500 word summary and then participating a couple of times in section, and the final project is super chill and is done before reading week. Victoria's lectures are also engaging and interesting. Highly recommend if you are CPB/MCB/other bio or bio–adjacent concentration and you are looking to fulfill an upper level class requirement, or if you're just looking for a chill class where you can learn some interesting and important things about viruses.
Excellent class for learning generally about viruses without tedious/stressful assignments and exams. Professor D'Souza is really knowledgeable and an excellent lecturer.
Just enjoy and read papers
If you have an interest in HIV or viruses in general and you have a decent background in biology, consider taking this course. It's challenging but not overly difficult or impossible to do well. Keep up with the readings and assignments. Come to class and sessions prepared and ready to actively participate, both asking questions and answering ones posed by the teaching team.
pretty chill class, not a lot of assignments and you learn a lot about virology
If you are truly interested about viruses, take this course!
Low workload but the grading can be kinda sketch
Take this course! It's a low–stress, light workload 100–level MCB class. The workload each week is reading an article about a recent study that discovered something interesting about viruses, writing a summary of the article, and discussing it in a small group. No exams and no psets meant that I could learn for the sake of learning – rare for a Harvard class, especially an MCB class. I came out of this class with a greater appreciation for biology.
The lectures can be a bit hard to follow sometimes but you'll learn a lot from reading papers. Workload is manageable.
Great class to take. Gives you great practice reading scientific literature, and the class is laid–back in the sense that the information is actually enjoyable to learn (you dont have to worry about cramming for any exams; just learn the information because it's actually really interesting).
