Dont take this class if you want to engage deeply with sociology, economics, or network theory. The class may be easy, but its mind bogglingly annoying; youll find yourself working on trivial assignments like making slides, filling out surveys and google forms, diagramming, and filling out collaborative documents that, while changing up the pace of the course, doesnt really further your understanding and just takes a lot of time. I find myself held back by the sheer number of small asssignments that are due throughout the week, and its hard to find the energy to do so many of these tasks that make the class overall feel like a nitpicky gened. I spent more time keeping track of assignments and being asked obvious questions than actually learning new things.

The concepts discussed in class have potentially, and the sociological and economic theory discussed in class can be interesting. Dr Leah is also an enthusiastic and kind professor who truly has a deep understanding of the field and is very engaging to talk to and get to know. The size of the class, however, likely made this class feel too impersonal and trying to make this class accessible to everyone likely made this class more intro than is needed to actually explore the material in depth. The staff overall are kind and the class is made with good intentions, but other classes may be a better option if you want something substantive.
