This is a decent introductory course for Sociology concentrators and you will read many texts that you can take with you to other courses – that being said, expect the discussions to be relatively uncomfortable.
This is an amazing course to take as an intro sociology course. It is small and allows you to build a community with those around you. The assignments aren't too demanding, although you do have to stay on top of the readings, and it is handled fairly and with equality at the center.
Take this class! Very intro level, so the topics discussed are pretty obvious. But conversations are good and interesting
This is a great introduction to sociology option because it exposes you to sociological methods/ the field of sociology through the lens of a topic that is so relevant to so many possible interests and perspectives.
The course is an introduction to Sociology, and I would recommend it to people interested in the discipline. I would not recommend it as an elective, because it is a lot of work and there are other Sociology classes that are more engaging and fun that push the bounds a little bit more.

This course inspired me to think deeply about inequality, where it comes from, and how it is sustained. Especially gender inequality/ family gender roles.
I would like to tell them that the readings are actually fun and enjoyable and Professor Cross is really, really great at facilitating discussions.
TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Cross is an excellent educator & class discussions are interesting/ thought–provoking. I would caution, though, if you do not like reading, this class may not be the best fit.
