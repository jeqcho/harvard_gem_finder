LS1B is an intro course that is required by many concentrations, unfortunately. I walked into this course expecting it to be quite a bit of busywork and nontrivially hand–holdy (due to it being an intro course). But what I did not expect was to spend 10–20 hours a week doing useless busywork.

Besides that, the grading is very messed up due to the following reasons:

1. PIEs are almost half of your grade in the course. They are online and open–note, but there is rampant cheating. This bumps up the cutoffs for grades, making it very difficult to do well in the course, even if you make a few silly mistakes here and there.
2. Everything you turn in, other than the exams, is graded by your TF. Thus, your grade is entirely in the TF's hands. Certain TFs may be very kind and lenient with grading while other TFs count off just because they want to (or on a whim, depending on whether they like you or not). If you have a particularly strict or mean TF, you are already put at a significant disadvantage.

Additionally, your experience in the course is dictated entirely by your 3 hour section (which includes discussion and lab) led by your TF. Otherwise, you do not interact with the course staff at all. If your TF is not good or does not know the material, your experience in the class will be absolutely awful, as was mine.

I have not taken a class that I dislike more than I dislike LS1B. I've never spent more time doing useless busywork, and I've never had such an awful experience with a member of the course staff.
