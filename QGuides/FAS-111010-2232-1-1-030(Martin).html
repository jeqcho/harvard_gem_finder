Do not attempt this class alone. This class can be brutal at times, but having a group of friends to work and study with is CRUCIAL, especially since there are 3 PSets a week. I found this course to be WAY BETTER than MATH 1A. The support you are given by the teaching staff is FAR better and new concepts are explained to students with the expectation that...they are actually new to students. Be sure to look over the worksheet answers as they are pretty helpful. The course is very fast–paced, so I would recommend going to EVERY class and learning as much as you can there so that you don't fall behind. I will say, however, that when it comes to tests, this class shows no mercy. They can be pretty rough. There were times when I felt I was very prepared for exams but was quickly humbled by the type of questions that were asked. Study hard for these.
100x better than 1A by far, psets were much more reasonable and QRD was not as much of a pain either. Please get a section with Fanhui or Amadeus they're amazing TF's who both incredibly kind and explain things very clearly, almost didn't take this class because of 1A and I might be leaving this class an Applied Math Concentrator so highly recommend! (Especially if you've taken Calc AB/BC already)
Be prepared for 3 psets a week
Be prepared to spend time in office hours or MQC. I preferred OH and hardly ever went to MQC because I found that it was less students per staff member and it was still collaborative like MQC without the rush.

The course is not too difficult but it can be time consuming. I often felt I couldn’t do the entire pset without help or like some sort of cue but I wouldn’t say the course is too hard just some questions on psets I needed some guidance. It is about moderate. I spent about 3 hours on each pset on average at the least and so around 10ish hours a week was spent.

I enjoyed the class overall and my section leader, Fanhui. I know some people say this course varies on who your section leader is & I think I believe this is partly true from hearing other students.
This class is so well worth your time and energy. Wow. I didn't realize how poorly Math M was run until I enrolled in this class. In 1b, there are no additional pointless weekly sections (you are only in class for 3 times a week instead of 4), your CAs are very smart and invested in helping you, and the TFs are actually really good at explaining concepts. In 1b there are office hours held in the daytime by the TFs–– go to those to do your psets instead of MQC. Make sure you go RIGHT AFTER CLASS–– the material is on your mind and you won't be overwhelmed with the 3 psets per week. I wasn't used to doing my psets right after class or honestly just anytime before the night before it was due. Make sure you read the Gottlieb textbook. I didn't even look at this until too late, I only watched the recorded videos. That textbook literally has what you need to know and will help you actually understand what's taught in class. The class time is too short and usually rushed, so do the readings, watch the videos etc in time with the class course progression (not all before the test) and you will really understand everything. Be in communication with the TFs. They will help you. Had I taken this earlier, I would have switched from Ec to Applied Math, Stats, CS, or something else that requires more math. Please do not let a terrible experience in Math M prevent you from taking this. It is a very, very well done class. I am so impressed, honestly. And I am never impressed. As someone interested in learning more math but hesitant, please, take this class. Work through the material with the TFs, look at the textbook pages etc.
This course is not an "easy" class, even if you took AP Calculus BC in high school. Three psets a week and three exams is a grind. And, the final exam is pretty difficult. The staff is excellent and they will help as much as they can, but prepared to put a lot of work into the course.
You really need to work hard and put in the hours to do well, but doing so will be rewarding.
This class is not as bad as I anticipated. Attendance is not required for section, but I would HIGHLY recommend that you pretend that it is. MCQ is great and so are the CAs, but I would always prioritize office hours if you can make them. The instructors are tend to be more fluent in the course material and attending OH can feel like an extension of section (or help cover missed content from sections you were absent for). The course staff is really wonderful, and they were always willing to provide help when I went looking for it. No one really takes 1B for fun, but it was sort of fun at times!
For students looking to enroll in Math 1B, I would highly encourage doing prep over Math 1A (Calculus I or AP Calculus AB) prior to starting the semester. There are many skills, especially integration and deriving–wise, that are utilized throughout the course. Thus, it is very helpful to enter the course with all of these skills ready to go.
As always, only take this class if it is explicitly necessary for your concentration. But it is definitely better than Math 1A, and if you have enough calc background on derivatives, limits, and basic integrals, this class is a great way to get out the QRD requirement (even though it is a 3 p–set a week class).
