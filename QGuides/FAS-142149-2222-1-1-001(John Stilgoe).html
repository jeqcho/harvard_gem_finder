I would tell future students to take this class with an open mind if they want to break free from the Harvard echo chamber.
You know those classes that are so cool and so strange that you absolutely have to tell your roommates all about it as soon as you get home every day? Yeah, that's this class. If you enjoy fantasy novels and strange tangents, I cannot recommend this class enough. The term paper not having a rubric is spooky, but Prof Stilgoe genuinely wants you to explore whatever has piqued your interest in the class.
This is one of the best courses you will ever take at Harvard. The content is interesting, the discussions engaging, the final paper brimming with creative potential. The most important facet about this course however, is Professor Stilgoe. He is such a unique and special professor, and it is a shame there are not more like him. the most important aspect of this course is the feedback. Like I told my public speaking course, I have always loved thinking and theorizing academically. I just never believed that A) I was ever any good at writing academically, and B) anyone cared what I had to say because I had often been criticized for "being too ambitious" in my assignments or "not doing what was asked of me" (even if I did more than what was asked). Professor Stilgoe has been one of the ONLY people at this university who not only let me write how and what I wanted, but found things to praise about my thinking. I am not saying this is "excellent" because he simply gave me positive feedback, but because the way he engages with every single student and paper shows so much caring and excitement on his part about what we the students are thinking about. His in–depth feedback was the only way I felt actually spoken to about my writing, and it is single–handedly because of his feedback that I am not only a much better writer and thinker today, but actually believe that I have ideas that are worth something. Take this course, and any other Stilgoe teaches. You will be a much better person for it.
