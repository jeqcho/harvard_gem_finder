What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Start early and communicate deadlines/needs to your mentors and PI. Also, the workshops are not the most helpful, so don't stress about them.
This course, as well as doing a senior thesis in general, is a good way to determine if graduate studies are what you want to pursue. This course is a lot of work and for me, there were times when a lack of course structure and guidance made this course more difficult than was perhaps intended.
If you are willing to sacrifice many late nights in lab and drafting, writing a thesis is an incredibly rewarding experience that allows you to dive into a personalized plan of study in a field of interest.
Echoing what everyone else will say, the thesis is a lot of work. I would say pace yourself, but no one really follows that advice ever unfortunately. One piece of advice is to be more assertive. While you have people in lab who are "above you," treat yourself like a member of a team. That means what you think and say matters. Your mentor wants you to do more experiments last–minute? You're the boss of your project, say you don't have the bandwidth, tell them it's not happening. No one providing feedback? Follow up, CC Dom/Monique in an email so they know you mean business. Take charge and initiate and don't let ANYONE walk over you. This is a learning experience, and no one should be taking away from that. Girlboss that thesis. xoxo. and honestly, we're all going to die one day anyways,,,,, it's not that deep don't put "one more experiment" over your sanity and wellbeing. It's not worth it.
Definitely write a thesis! It was a great experience and I felt supported throughout.
Your experience in MCB99B and writing a thesis overall is very dependent on your project and mentor. Generally, though, I would advise students to wrap up their key experiments by mid February so that they have a dedicated month to write. I would also advise them to lean not only on their direct mentor but also others in their lab to get lots of feedback on your drafts at different stages. Also, do not underestimate how long it can take to make a figure. Lastly, remember that the quality of feedback you receive depends on the quality of your draft, so the earlier you can put together a reasonable draft the better the quality of feedback you can get!
