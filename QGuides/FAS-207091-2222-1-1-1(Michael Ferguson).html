Highly recommend this course! Professor Lazar and Professor Ferguson are both great professors who know how to balance rigor and relaxation with the course content and discussion. I learned so much about neuroscience in this class, but in my opinion, the most important things I learned were an introduction to practices that have helped me in real–life. If you're looking for a chill but rigorous class, or are even remotely interested in meditation or neuro, take this course!
This course is an amazing empirical investigation of the impacts of mindfulness meditation. Dr. Ferguson is more than happy to discuss tangential fields as well such as spirituality, mysticism, psychedelics, and more. This course is great for anyone who has a personal or professional interest in mindfulness' impact on the mind and is searching for an intimate classroom setting that emphasizes vibrant discussion. Dr. Ferguson is truly an amazing professor and I would highly recommend the course.
One of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. Professor Ferguson makes the material super engaging and does a great job connecting philosophy and neuroscience. The is also not time–consuming so definitely take it if you are interested in the content.
Really try to incorperate as much of the meditation practices and styles into your routine as this course provides a great space to try out new things
Get ready to read a lot of scientific papers on mediation and neuroscience. But, in turn, you learn a significant about meditation and the brain.
This course has super interesting material that integrates Buddhist & meditative literature with neuroscience in really cool ways. A lot of the readings are dense and filled with jargon, so be prepared for that, but other than that the workload is very reasonable. Discussions were always interesting and instructors made space for us to take the topics where we wanted in class.
This course was super interesting and significantly changed the way I understand myself and others. I had never been exposed to meditation (and tbh thought it seemed kinda silly) but this class changed my whole perception on the subject. Now, I try to include mindfulness in my everyday life. I am really glad I took this class.
As far as course details, it is primarily discussion–based and is centered around student presentations. There is a final paper and weekly discussion posts due Friday night that can be quite tedious. The readings are kinda intense and take a ridiculously long time. I definitely hope this class scales back on the reading load a bit. Additionally, the deadlines and assignments were super scattered and poorly organized––they kept posting the wrong dates, then changing them last minute. It was quite stressful sometimes. Hopefully they can fix this for next year. Overall it was a very interesting class and I learned a lot. Glad I took it.
I love Ferguson!!! He's amazing. Jump at the chance to take any class with him.
