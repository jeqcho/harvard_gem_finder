What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Stay engaged in class and participate in discussions and you will enjoy the class.
If you plan on concentrating in philosophy, social studies or any humanities discipline that has argumentative writing, you should 100% take this class. Philosophical and argumentative writing at the college level is so so different than any other discipline, and many courses at Harvard do not prepare you to write in this fashion. This class truly teaches you how to write in a way that you have never done before. Jen provided examples to truly help you develop the necessary skills to write.

Beware, however, that this course is not a gem. There are quizzes in each class, which aren't hard, but are a little frustrating because you have to ensure that you are prepared for them. The readings can be very dense. But, the writing is not in itself that hard, but it does require a little more work than another course.

This class is definitely not a guaranteed A, but if you do put in the work, it is very doable. I would 100% recommend this course.
I believe this is one of the best exposes. All the readings are stimulating and make you think. The classes are interesting when we discuss the philosophy when we share our thoughts. It is a great introduction to a field that is most likely new for most people. Since the requirement is mandatory, this not only is useful, but you will learn much more outside of the general writing that is in each class. Perhaps if you are interested in philosophy it would be better to have done Hum 10 however.
Take this class!! Jen is absolutely amazing. She cares about helping you become a better writing, and gives you great feedback to help you improve after each draft. She also grades fairly. The class itself is very interesting, especially if you have any interest at all in philosophy. I enjoyed the readings, and the essays and assignments are spaced out so that it never feels like we have too much work. I feel like my writing has improved significantly since I started this class, both because of learning about structure and style in the class, and also because of Jen's feedback. I would highly recommend this class!
Given that Expos is a requirement, this class is a good pick. As a STEM student, learning the norms of a philosophy paper weren't necessarily applicable to my future courses, so do think about if you might want to take a science–focused Expos if you're in this position. That being said, Jen is a great teacher –– she will help you understand philosophy even if you have never taken a course in it before, and she provides TONS of useful feedback to improve your essays. This class genuinely taught me a lot about writing, and I think it's a solid Expos choice; the workload is not too overwhelming (just the 3 papers, mainly) and we had some great class discussions.
Jen is such a great preceptor!! She genuinely cares about improving your writing and is always there to support you. This class was very different than my previous English classes (in a good way).
I really loved Philosophy of Friendship! Jen, for one, is an absolutely amazing preceptor she is super sweet and considerate, and takes the time to get to know every student individually. I also felt like my writing really improved due to this class; my structure, especially, got way better.

I also really appreciated that a small community formed from my Expos class. Because the ideas we were talking about could be a little more philosophical/sometimes deep, we all became friendly with each other.

Overall, 10/10 recommend. Just be aware that sometimes Jen's comments on your first draft can be quite harsh, but as long as you take time to address the comments and work hard on the revision, you will be okay.
Expos is expos. For someone that loves philosophy, you'll probably really like this class. For someone like me that discovered maybe philosophy isn't their cup of tea, this class will be an hour and fifteen minutes to get through twice a week. In other words, it will be expos. Jen is a wonderful person, with super helpful office hours and essay feedback. The philosophy is interesting, although the basic writing teaching gets pretty old very quickly. It probably won't be the worst class you take, but it certainly wasn't my best this semsester.
For STEM people: Your weekly PSET schedule will seem routine. Expos will throw that out the window. Sometimes it's an hour of reading in one week, sometimes it's eight hours of drafting and revising. Plan for this!!
For people thinking of taking this at 9am: It actually wasn't the worst. Do sleep the night before though.
I would highly encourage future students to take this class. Whether you realize it or not, we all approach college writing with our own rules and biases. Expos helps loosen those rules. To be upfront, the class presents its fair share of challenges. However, five minutes in Professor Nguyen's class shows you how intentional and thoughtful she is with everything she does. If you like philosophy, it's a nice bonus, but she really makes the experience one of growth.
This class challenges you as a writer, but leaves you with a real sense of improvement. Jen is incredible understanding. I will be studying philosophy more at Harvard, and possibly concentrating in it, because I enjoyed this class.
