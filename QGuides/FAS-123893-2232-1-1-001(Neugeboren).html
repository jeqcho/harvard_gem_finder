It is a difficult class, but interesting. Pay attention in lecture because the psets will be based on those, and the lecture slides are not great.
I would recommend taking this course if you are already interested in game theory. Professor Neugeboren keeps students engaged with games where you have the chance to win actual money, and there are plenty of opportunities to meet with both Professor Neugeboren and Mehar. The final and midterm count for a combined 70% of your grade, so plan accordingly. *
.
Game theory is very interesting! Professor Neugeboren and Mehar Jauer (TF) are both very kind and dedicated to the course, and are both very accessible in and outside of class! The lectures were quite interactive and I really enjoyed the opportunity to engage through answering and raising questions in class.
This class is a bit strange because the readings aren't helpful and the lectures are interesting but information relevant to PSET's is scattered between them. Section is amazing though and super helpful. There isn't much materials outside of the slides but the midterm was fine. The final was a lot harder but I'm hoping it will be curved. The in–class games are fun. I would recommend paying attention to lecture, don't worry about taking notes, just absorb major concepts. Section will be more helpful for PSET's but paying consistent attention to topics as they arise is important for the final because they aren't addressed in the PSET's. It's definitely a manageable class but set aside more time than you think for assessment prep.
This is a pretty interesting game theory class with a pretty low workload. If you've seen game theory at the 10a (or 1010a) level, you will be well–equipped to take this, but there will still be enough new stuff to make this class interesting. For example, I haven't been exposed to zero–sum games and prudent strategies at any of my previous classes –– it was interesting to learn about these.
This class is great as an introduction to game theory, or if you're looking for a course that has real world applications and relevance. Prof. Neugenboren gives excellent lectures and the material is both interesting and widely applicable
