This was a really good expos class, especially if you enjoy horror! I have friends who hated their expos classes because the readings were so boring, but this was extremely engaging and the space Mande created in lectures made it feel very easy to exchange ideas. The readings weren't too heavy at all, and die down after the first unit especially to make the class much more manageable. In general, when you're picking expos courses, focus on the topics because at the end of the day, if you don't like the topic being taught you will hate the readings regardless of how short or long they were, and thus hate the class for an entire semester.
Take it! Mande is such a great preceptor and always so enthusiastic about what she teaches (and the topic of horror and its uses is incredibly interesting!!)
Choose a topic that you like rather than a topic that would look good in your transcript. Expos is what you make out of it; you will learn how to write better anyway, so it's better to choose something that seems interesting to you. Give yourself enough time to work on the assignments and ask for feedback from your professor and peers.
