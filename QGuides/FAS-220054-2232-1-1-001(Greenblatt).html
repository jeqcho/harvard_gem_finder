What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

It's a great class. You learn Shakespeare from the world's leading Shakespearean scholar. The lectures are great and very engaging. If you're interested in writing (especially script writing), directing, or even just reading, this is a very worthwhile class.
I am a second semester senior and this was my first English course ––– it made me wish I had been taking more English classes the whole time! Though the course certainly could use some focusing and improved organization, it was a highly enjoyable experience. Discussions are readings were interesting and engaging and this was an intellectually fulfilling experience. I didn't have very much Shakespeare knowledge coming in (outside of general interest) but I finished the course with a very solid overview of six major plays. Assignments are all reasonable and if you're interested in Shakespeare they are even enjoyable. One of my favorite Harvard classes!
This is a wonderful course. Professor Greenblatt is an absolutely phenomenal lecturer, and if you can take a class with him wherein he can talk about his specialty, you should. While I was taking his course at Harvard, my friends taking Shakespeare courses at other universities were being assigned articles written by him. To hear Professor Greenblatt speak about Hamlet for four classes in a row is to understand better both the play's significance and perhaps even one's own place in the world.
If you like Shakespeare but don't know much about him or where his stories originally came from, this is the course for you. Minimal work including reading 6 plays, writing a couple 3–5 page essays, and writing a few 1–3 page dialogues based on Shakespearean characters and styles. Pay attention in lecture, do the reading, and you'll love this course.
If you're interested in theater or English, this is definitely the course for you! And even if you aren't, this is still a wonderfully fun course to take. The assignments are extremely accommodating to those who may have less experience in playwriting or analytical writing, and you learn a lot from the professor and your peers. Minimal effort for maximum reward
This class is a great Shakespeare course with an iconic professor, and the classes are pretty chill and fun. But the reading is super dense and assignments are kind of tough. The lectures can also be kind of graphic.
* Great course if you want something to round out your schedule and you don't mind reading some Shakespeare (which I would recommended). Be honest with yourself – barring some exception, if you aren't going to read the material don't bother taking it. You'll get so much more out of it if you actually read the plays and participate in class and section. That being said it certainly is not a hard course, but it isn't really meant to be. Take if you want to learn more about Shakespeare, because this may be the best course at Harvard (with some exception) to do so.
This course is AMAZING! Professor Greenblatt is so passionate about the material and he shows it. So many of the lectures were absolutely incredible, and made you think deeply about the plays, as he opened them up in new and unexpected ways, which gripped me and astounded me! And Sam is a really wonderful TF who made section a great experience every week! Would 100% recommend this course.
Do not hesitate to take this class. One of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. Professor Greenblatt is an extremely engaging, kind, and genius professor. His insights on Shakespeare are poignant, fascinating, and relevant.
