I personally love the topic of the con, which in my opinion, makes the writing, reading, and content 10x more interesting. Ian also gives back really good and concise feedback, so I'm sure that your writing will definitely improve if you put in the effort and try. He is also super willing to work with you on deadlines and gave me the time to rewrite my draft and submit it for conference, when I heavily disliked what I wrote. Overall, great course!
The papers are challenging but Ian is dope and always there to support you
I honestly think this is one of the best EXPOS classes to take (I have heard horror stories from my other friends). Ian especially is very chill, and the vibes are very much there. There is a decent amount of peer collaboration and discussion. Ian always opens with really funny questions that help you get to know the people in your section which just makes it a more comfortable environment for exploring this style of writing.

As for the actual work, it honestly makes it SO much easier to do the reading. Otherwise you kinda develop an inferiority complex when everyone else has really good and thoughtful comments about the text...

But, Ian gives great feedback and I do honestly feel that I have developed past the five paragraph structure, which I guess is a success. The content of the course is also super interesting –– we literally only read one book. I found cons to be very fascinating, and it is honestly such an interesting idea to touch on cons as extensions of extreme societal pressure. We approach the same text through multiple lenses, and is a very cool way to see how an author's background can drastically change the interpretation of the same facts. Take the course, tbh

I would VERY highly recommend taking this course with Ian.

Ian is an amazing preceptor who never fails to make the class more engaging and exciting. His approachable and conversational teaching style immediately makes everyone feel welcome in his classroom, which made the class a joy to go to every week. This is definitely one of the tougher expos classes though; there is a lot of required material to go through, especially in paper 2, and Ian has pretty high standards when grading that push you to write your absolute best.
