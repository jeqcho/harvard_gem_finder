I would 100% recommend this course to everyone! This course is certainly different from any other Harvard course I have ever taken and imparts lessons you would never learn otherwise. The reason I say this is because this is a course where you learn fully from doing. The instruction in this course is fairly minimal and the lectures are more of a starting point for your imagination rather than a guide for how to approach your assignments. Your actual learning takes place in the studio, where you dedicate hours to a painting to carry out a vision of yours. There is no experience requirement and you will certainly grow your artistic abilities as well as instill confidence in yourself. Please do not be scared of taking an art course! As someone with minimal experience, this course really made me see myself as an artist and encouraged me to pursue art outside of the classroom!
Anyone and everyone even remotely interested in art or painting should take this course if they can. This is not a gem (in order to produce work that you'll be satisfied with, you'll need to put in just a bit of time) but it's also a very reasonable time commitment and extremely rewarding experience. The open–ended structure allows for creativity and independent experimentation. Students are given loose guidelines, taught basic painting principles, and actively encouraged to pursue their personal interests.
This is a great class to almost "force" yourself to have a hobby outside of other classes. It is a bit of a time commitment since you have to do one painting a week, which also requires good time management to make sure everything dries. However, don't let that discourage you. You can put as much or as little effort into it as you want.
It is fun but time demanding
