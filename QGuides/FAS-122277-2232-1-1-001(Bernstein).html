If you need to fulfill one of your GenEd requirements, this is a fine option. Some good things:
– You'll learn so much about different types of theater, how they relate to race/gender/sexuality, how they can create or hinder social change, etc
– You'll watch many performances that are pretty entertaining and fulfilling
– You'll definitely want to go do some performances and make social change using the tools you've learned from this class
Some things to be aware of (a.k.a. things I personally did not like, but presented in a factual way because you may be perfectly fine with them):
– Attendance is required and graded for both lecture and section.
– A lot of lectures involve getting in small groups and discussing things. I found this to be pretty useless and also overwhelming as an autistic person and an immunocompromised person with all my peers coughing in my face.
– Instructor and staff apparently don't practice what they preach, because they don't consistently wear masks in class and somehow are under the illusion that talking into a mic makes covid disappear, so even if they are masked, they take the masks off while talking. This is especially appalling given that this is a class about challenging racism/sexism/homophobia.
– There is surprisingly little about performing disability/disability justice/how ableism intersects with race/gender/sexuality, and I personally think this is problematic because you can't understand the root of many social injustices without talking about ableism. Sort of checks out that the staff don't wear masks though.
– Midterm and final exam are closed–book, hand–written, attendance required/very difficult to get medical exemption. They are also graded very harshly and arbitrarily.
– Performance analysis assignments are DIFFICULT, especially if you've never done performance analysis before. You definitely want to run your thesis by your TF each time, but even then, they'll hit you with feedback you didn't expect because they don't put out a darn rubric for the assignments.
– Course handouts and assignment descriptions are only given in print in class. So much for accessibility.
