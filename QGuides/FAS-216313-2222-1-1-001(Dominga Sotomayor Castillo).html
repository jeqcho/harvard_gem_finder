This course is fantastic and captivating, and the material is nothing short of engrossing. The films are excellent and the material is extremely engaging.
However, if you have AEO accommodations I would just keep in mind that the course staff may not be as accommodating as other courses you've taken. Be very clear about what is required for your grade. If you are ill and have to miss class, be wary of alerting the course staff and keep your dean looped in from the start even if it seems overly cautious. Some courses are accommodating of people with AEO–documented disabilities and some are not.
Great class! Really interesting films, very manageable course load. Lots of super interesting discussions. I highly recommend this class for people interested in film studies and wanting to dip their toes into filmmaking assignments as well.
Haden Guest is one of the best professors you'll encounter at Harvard. Combined with the experience and insight of Dominga and the kindness and support of Ignacio, this class raises the bar for how classes should be taught.
Take this class! If you don't mind heavy discussion I think anyone who takes this class will find it very intellectually stimulating with very low stakes in terms of hard grades.
This class is a good class and not that much work. The films you get to watch are really interesting, but the discussions can be a little boring because they are so long. If you know you love cinema, this is a great class for you to take. If you're unsure, this can still be a good class, but expect to be constantly engaging with content and film rather than learning theory and gaining a general introduction.
TAKE THIS COURSE! Without question, just do it. It's the best seminar I've ever taken at this school, because Haden and Dominga meet you where you are and show you how far you can go: intellectually and historically and in terms of what they'll teach you about film. This class gives you permission to play around with all kinds of artistic forms, and learn all kinds of histories, in a way which is timewise more than manageable, but which allows you to get everything out of it that you could want from a film class.
