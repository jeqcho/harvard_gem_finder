Take this class!! Professor Benenson is amazing and really cares about this class and the material. She is always willing to help out and the papers are super fun to write.
This class is harder than it looks. The subject material is relatively interesting, and the professor is very nice, but understanding the grading is challenging and the quizzes (2 big ones that count for 20% of your grade) are extremely annoying with how picky they are formatted (they are multiple choice but select all of the following that are true).

That being said, 65% of your grade is made up of 5, 2–page papers, which are not that hard to write, but graded a bit harshly. Lots of opportunities to improve your scores on those papers, though, as the professor was quite accommodating.
Definitely take this course! Joyce Benenson is such an amazing professor. She is so encouraging, kind, passionate and will go above and beyond for her students!
Please take this class! You will be doing yourself a favor. The course is very relaxed and enjoyable but also purposeful – I feel like you will start to think about human behaviors in a new light (in relation to our evolutionary history). It's very interesting content, and Professor Joyce Benenson is just amazingly sweet and caring.
Dr. Benenson will always be one of my favorite professors at Harvard. Her enthusiasm for mammalian behavior is incredibly contagious and her care and interest in the lives of her students is always clear. It was difficult not to be excited about this class with a professor like her.
I enjoyed this class and would recommend it to anyone interested in learning about mammals and the identity of humans as mammals. Lectures could be kind of dry sometimes, but this course overall was engaging and EASY. Very much a gem. Barely any readings and the essays are easy if you follow the rubric and guidelines.
Please consider taking this course if you are interested in animal behavior; reconsider taking this course if you value your GPA to a great extent.
