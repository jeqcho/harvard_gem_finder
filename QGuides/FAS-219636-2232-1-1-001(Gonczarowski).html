This is a great course to take if you are interested in learning more EconCS! The course will expose you to many useful proof techniques and interesting research topics. Yannai is also super approachable and friendly, so you should definitely try to talk to him and learn from his deep knowledge of the field.
This is a great class to learn more about Econ–CS. The content is challenging but in–depth, and the class can be very rewarding if you are willing to put in the time. It is a little less structured than something like CS136, but Yannai is an amazing professor so you will receive a lot of support throughout.
Yannai's lectures are great, would recommend
A well–designed and fantastic course. Yannai tells you at the start that his goal is for you to get a general understanding of the landscape of Econ/CS, and he delivers. Not too hard but rewarding and fun. I looked forward to going to lecture and would highly recommend anything Yannai teaches in the future!
You can get a lot out of this course, if you pay attention and engage, and pick an interesting problem for the course project.
Yannai is a sweetie and so was our TF, Jamie. The lectures could feel a little disorganized – it's pretty easy to get lost in the equations – but I liked the syllabus and I liked the philosophy of the course, which is to marry these two seemingly disparate but also related fields of study.
This class will present you with a diversity of mathematical techniques and economics topics which I personally had never heard of elsewhere. Even for the CS student who is not interested in economics itself, some of the more obscure techniques that are mentioned in class in the context of econ applications are themselves worth learning.
There is really a lot of variety in the topics that get covered, so even if some weeks feel harder to grasp, that's okay –– you usually get a fresh start in the next week!
This is a wonderful course to take if you want to learn CS/Econ in the future. Yannai is a great teacher and communicates the difficult ideas very well. He cares a lot about his students and puts a lot of effort into his teaching. This class is also not a lot of work (only 3 psets and a final project) and the workload is very manageable. This class introduces you to a lot of cs/econ research techniques (some of the paper we read were published in the last five years), which I thought was really cool.

I would especially recommend the class to people who are good at proof–based math/have mathematical maturity, have taken ECON 1011a/CS 136 and CS 121/124, and want to do math that is more impactful/relevant to the real world. If you really like problem solving, then you'll like what we cover in this class.
