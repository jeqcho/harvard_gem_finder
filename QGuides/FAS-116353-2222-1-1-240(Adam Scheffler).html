What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This was an amazing Expos class. Although the topic is about the underworld, you spend a lot of time discussing interesting societal problems as well. The first unit can feel a bit dull as you're looking at older texts, but the second and third units are where the class really gets interesting. The readings are both engaging and varied; you go from religious sermons to intellectual analyses of how Hell is used in media to short horror stories. They also aren't too long, and if you enjoy them it doesn't feel like a chore to go through them. Adam is also an amazing preceptor. His comments on your writing really help you improve, and it's clear he wants to help you improve. If you like reading fiction and discussing their larger ideas, you should definitely take this class.
You just kind of have to push through it, we all have to take it, and as someone who HATES writing essays.. I'll just say this class did not make me love it more, but the discussions in class were fun, especially cause it was about hell, which brings in a lot of interesting other topics that are relevant to the real world.
I had a really great time in the Underworld section. The feedback I got on essays was extremely valuable and exactly pointed out the issues that I needed to work on. I can say my argumentative essay writing skills have definitely improved as a result.
The class is fun and the content and discussions are very interesting. The assignments are also fun but can be often difficult. The assignments are also progressive and are meant to help you develop your writing skills.
