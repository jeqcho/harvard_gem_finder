What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course will ask you to thoroughly engage with what it means to be a university student and what it means to be supporting a university, especially with a school like Harvard. At times you will certainly be challenged by the readings and the debates being done in class, but you will come out with a thorough understanding of what you think the university should be shaped like and ready to engage in on going conversations and debates about the role and nature of the university.
This was a really interesting class! I enjoyed the weekly readings, the topics were guided by student preference, and the class discussions were engaging.
Overall, this class is a gem and is fun and interesting to think about. However, the readings are extremely US–focused and tend not to incorporate much diversity from POC and women, which is my only reservation about the course. Assignments are manageable, and since they walk you through the research project throughout the entire semester, it's really not that much work during finals.
I thought this class was going to be interesting and we would talk about the quality of education, but ended up a being a very narrow focused discussion about academic freedom and what freedom specifically professors have in speaking out about modern social issues. This was all very abstract, and to add to it, most readings were solely from white male professors from the 1960s where the most important social issue was the Vietnam War. But most students in the course wanted to learn about today’s issues. The second half of the course gets into case studies which are super interesting (affirmative action, divestment, open admissions, etc). But I wish this part was longer.
Halfway through the class, the weekly assignments on the readings are 500–700 word posts based on the readings. I never understood why we had to write so much all of a sudden, and no specific prompt was really given. I don’t see how the course staff can really spend the time to fairly grade these assignments, and from the student perspective, spend an adequate and meaningful amount of time where 500 words is necessary to make a point. I believe a 200 word response would be sufficient.
Such an interesting class with a very thoughtful selection of readings week to week. The class is engaging with different forms of teaching / small group discussion / activities that make the class familiar with one another.
This was a solid Gened. The final research project was interesting and not too stressful (if your group members also pull their weight and the work is distributed somewhat evenly). Meeting once a week (with 15 min break) and 45–minute sections to work with your group was very chill. There are a LOT of assigned readings, but you don't necessarily have to complete all of them to contribute to in–class discussions. The teaching team – Julie, Zennon, and Orelia – was my favorite part of the course. They were super understanding and supportive, and encouraged us to think critically about the topics we discussed.
Glad I took this course, and would definitely recommend – but take it when you're less busy!
Take this class if you would like to engage deeply with a lot of relevant topics relating to universities! There is a lot of opportunity to learn from your peers and hear diverse perspectives on issues like divestment, public school partnerships, and affirmative action.
Take this course if you are even remotely interested in debating. Even if you’re not good at it, this class will help you with your argumentative skills. Student discussions in this class felt like legislative hearings. Also, attendance is important, it affects your participation. Julie mostly read off of a PowerPoint slideshow, as far as “teaching” goes. Not much input there, but she does play devil’s advocate during class discussions. I guess that’s nice.
Really do the readings, they teach you so much.
Take it!!! If you want to learn about how universities engage with their students and their broader communities, this is the course for you. Also, the final
group project is soooo fun, meaningful, and rewarding! Definitely take this class. There is no time wasted, and you get so much more back than you put in. (Plus, everyone taking the course is likely super cool!)
