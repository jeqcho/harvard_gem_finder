This is an amazing course. Prof. Deloria is an AWESOME professor who is worth hanging onto every word he says –– he is such a smart historian (he is such an important scholar in Native American and Indigenous Studies and does incredible work) and an even better teacher who makes everyone feel welcomed (invites students into the conversation, is very down to earth, is not intimidating to talk to) and is infectiously passionate about what he does. The TFs in this course are great too –– I had Emma for my writing tutorial, and she was incredibly helpful. I felt like writing tutorial was actually very productive and helped me a lot on the revisions, and meeting with her 1:1 in office hours was also great. She is actually just a great person to talk to about history/history grad school and is doing really cool work herself –– all around super cool and kind person. The course is in no way easy though lol ––– there is a LOT of writing...almost a paper or revision every week, there is a lot of reading that you cannot slack on because the 3hr seminar is spent discussing the readings, and the final research paper is a lot of work. But these are reasons why the course is great –– it's a lot of content and you produce a ton of work, but you'll feel proud at the end of it for how much you've improved. There's a lotttt of help along the way, and I definitely encourage meeting with Prof. Deloria in his office hours to discuss your paper if you're ever stuck, because he gives great advice which will not only be useful for your paper but will also be those useful tidbits how to write/do history that are worth storing away as you go throughout other classes at Harvard. The 5pm Sunday deadline was brutal for me though lol so hopefully they change that. You get a 24 hr extension bank which is good but I still left almost every paper for the Saturday before and that was pretty rough. Overall really loved this course though.
Before entering, know what you want your research project will be
If you have to take HIST97, take Indigenous History. Even though the topic felt overwhelming at first, Professor Deloria made us all feel comfortable. TFs were also excellent. There is much to be said about the issues with HIST97, but this course made it bearable (although it will be stressful no matter who you take it with).
I would recommend it over other H97 sections. Professor Deloria is an excellent teacher.
Deloria is an incredible teacher and this course was a great introduction to the field of indigenous studies and an even better opportunity to improve writing skills.
This course is a fantastic way to fulfill the Hist 97 requirement: Professor Deloria is thoughtful and encouraging, and both the TFs are wonderful and engage with students on whichever level they need. Writing the final paper is an incredibly rewarding process, but start the research early!
Would definitely recommend choosing this 97. Professor Deloria is awesome and the content is really interesting. At times you will be suffering with the workload & frustrated with the course structure but that would happen in all of the 97s.
The Indigenous History tutorial was great for building writing skills for history papers. Also, being a very structured class with frequent deadlines, I definitely learned a lot of helpful scheduling skills for being a better history concentrator. I do think that, for me, this class definitely leaned towards the writing side over the Indigenous History side. But I also think this was balanced out by the time I put in on my own to my personal subject. The freedom to choose pretty much any topic that interests me was incredible. All the papers build off each other towards the final, so you really get the reward of working on a single topic and project for a majority of the semester. Class culture. was also great. Professor Deloria and the TFs gave me a great new perspective on the field of history and its importance.
