An absolute gem! Some weeks there are two assigned films, so you'll spend 2 hours outside of class watching that. This semester we only needed to attend 6 screenings and write 6 postings (out of ~13 weeks) which was very convenient for midterms/finals period. As such, attend the screenings and do the postings EARLY in the semester because they are EASIER and take LESS TIME. Analytical postings (two required) take <.5–1 hours but creative postings (i.e. video essays, making a film) take much longer (2–5 hours) but are more fun. I highly recommend doing creative postings as well as a creative final project because you've already written plenty of academic papers and film–making is a great alternative to essays for conveying information. Shout out to the Bok Center workshops, they are very helpful, have free food, and count towards your screenings! Absolutely take this class. Professor Li is so knowledgeable and sweet and the breadth of the coursework is astounding. As someone who knew very little about cinema and East Asian culture, I was worried I wouldn't have enough context to fully participate and understand what was going on, but that was not at all the case. The workload is totally manageable and even enjoyable, with 1–2 movies assigned to watch each week on your own (or at the class screenings), short 1–2 sentence responses to those movies, 6–8 weekly postings (creative or analytical) over the course of the semester, a short midterm paper, and a really fun creative final project (or essay, but I really recommend doing a project). The movies are all quite entertaining, and watching and discussing them with classmates can be a really fun experience.
