Take this course if you can! It has the best work–content balance I may have ever seen in a course, though you don't have to do much work outside of class and lab, you still learn so much. John Shaw is an excellent professor, and lecture pacing is very good. The TFs Robert and Kristen were a joy. Robert was my TF and he is so energetic he can make even the most painful labs tolerable. Overall one of the better courses I have taken here, would definitely recommend. Also just a heads up: be willing to sell out for the bidding exercise, there seems to be more money in hydrocarbons and natural gas than renewables sadly :(
I really, really highly recommend this class to anyone interested in energy or climate. It taught me a TON about the technical aspects of coal and oil/gas production in the first half of the course, much more than I've learned in the many other climate–related classes I've taken (which tend to just reiterate facts about the negative effects of carbon emissions and the importance of policy). The second half comprehensively covered different kinds of renewable energy systems and helped me understand the technical, not just political, steps necessary to achieve decarbonization. The field trip is fantastically fun and a very rare opportunity to see i.e. a shale gas drilling rig or a coal mine, things which are almost impossible to see on your own. John is deeply knowledgeable and very interesting to talk to, and both TFs were absolutely fantastic (Robert was easily the best TF I've had in my time at Harvard) – encouraging, patient, and clearly super enthusiastic about the subject. Fantastic class overall and very worth taking!
EPS/ESE 109 is really a great course. I am studying ESE with an interest in energy and I learned SO much through this course and found it very interesting. This course explores energy sources through a geology–perspective. The field trip at the end of the semester is so so fun. You visit coal mines, wind power plants, and more which translates everything that you have been learning in class to real life. This class is a great way to get to know students that are interested in climate, resources, energy, and related subjects. Also, there is so little work and the TFs are the BEST. 10/10 recommend.
Take it!!! This class is incredible for anyone interested in environmental science and both the professor and the tfs are amazing. I would 1000% recommend to anyone interested, the workload is totally manageable and you learn so many cool things
This is probably my favorite course that I have taken at Harvard! You really learn the material well and get to use great software, and it is not an overwhelming amount of work. The material is great to know and every lecture is taught wonderfully.
Pay attention in lectures and review the notes and concepts throughout the semester so that you dont have to try and cram the week before the midterm and/or final. Its a ton of information so its good to start studying early to make the exams a bit easier for you. And make a class group chat early on its good to have friends to collaborate with and its good to get to know the people who are taking this class.
You should definitely take this class!! It is super interesting and I learned sooo much from it. Because there is so much covered, the tests can feel intimidating, but through lecture, labs, and field trips, you become an expert in the topics so that the tests shouldn't be too daunting. The field trips are super fun and a big perk of the class! Another perk was the bidding assignments in which we earned literal money! Also, the TFs were some of the best TFs I've ever had at Harvard. If Robert and Kristen TF this course again, you're in luck. In general, this class did a great job combining geology, politics, economics, and modeling to address environmental issues. Before taking this class, environmental problems and solutions felt relatively black and white, but this course really showed me all of the gray areas. You will really learn about the full complexity around our natural resources.
you will need to memorize a LOT A LOT of info, but if Robert is the TF its worth it and the field trip is super fun.
