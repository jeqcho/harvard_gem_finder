Very time–consuming class, with three psets / week and three classes / week. While the math is not quantitatively difficult, the concepts are quite abstract. Make sure to find a pset group early and study hard for midterms/exams.
This course is excellent, probably the best class I have taken so far at Harvard. You learn a lot of interesting/useful math (the differential equations portion of this class should not be overlooked). The workload is a slight step up from 21a, but it is still pretty manageable (stay on top of your 3 psets a week). The material itself is more abstract than 21a and totally different than calculus. If you are thinking of taking this course, I highly recommend it: it's well organized, not too overwhelming, and you learn a lot.

Would highly recommend, although it's lots of work and grading is difficult. Your class experience can depend a lot on what TF you get, but it made me enjoy learning math. Section is engaging/collaborative, met lots of cool people and had a good time
This class is awesome! Granted, it is definitely difficult and will require a significant amount of work per week. Exams are challenging and there isn't much partial credit, so definitely be extremely careful on assessments. Despite how tough this class can be, linear algebra is a super interesting subject and sections can be a lot of fun as you and your classmates work through the worksheets. Content can be difficult especially near the end of the course with differential equations/Fourier series, but it is so rewarding to watch your comprehension and grasp of linear algebra grow over time. Have fun and stay diligent!
Definitely more computationally heavy of a class than the other series (no proofs, etc). Lots of psets, but very worthwhile to dip your toes into linalg.
When people tell you this course is hard, believe them. It's not something you can wishy washy your way through. If you want to gain a full understanding of the material (as you should, especially because things build on each other heavily in this course), you must do the readings and watch the videos they provide, go to MQC/office hours, and study early for that test. Just by doing the readings and watching the videos, you are doing yourself a huge favor for understanding things in class/in general (you'll have to study less for the exam!). This class will challenge you if you haven't been pushed hard yet, and will likely still challenge you if you have.
