What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Expos 20 is a 20 vehicle flaming wreckage on the Massachusetts Turnpike. It is the worst course at Harvard, sustained only because it is a requirement, and no matter who you are you will almost certainly hate it. It is very possible that it will make you hate Harvard as a whole, and potentially hate yourself too. Expos teaches you nothing useful about writing, it only makes you jump through the arbitrary hoops that the writing program has laid out. The assignments bear no resemblance to any sort of academic writing that you might actually do, unless you are an English concentrator. The first two papers are literary analysis papers, while the third is a research paper in which you do no research, instead you simply compile other people's ideas into a trite synthesis of their research that you must then claim contains your own original contribution. This class in no way achieves its goal of improving people's writing.

That being said, since you are forced to take it, this is among the less bad sections to be in. Eliza is a really nice person, and a fair grader. While Expos teaches you absolutely nothing useful about writing, some of the readings in this section are moderately interesting. If you are sufficiently skilled at compartmentalizing your pain, might even momentarily enjoy some of the readings or discussions before returning to the untreated sewage waterboarding that is the Expos 20 material and assignment structure.
So many of my friends complained so heavy about their expos, but this course was very good! I enjoyed Eliza thoroughly as a teacher, while the content was always quite interesting to me. I also learned so much about how to write.
Get started on assignments early and ask plenty of questions.
Take this class for expos! Dr. Holmes is an amazing professor, and will always work through any questions or difficulties you may have. There were some really interesting readings in the course, and if you are interested in climate change, I would definitely recommend this expos!
I loved this expos class. I think it was incredibly helpful in developing a routine for writing for me that really made me craft effective arguments. I know my strengths and weaknesses in writing and how to approach them.
