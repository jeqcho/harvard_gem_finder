What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class!!! My gov 97 section (taught by Casey Petroff) was a low–stress, yet very informative course
You gotta take it. I beg you to turn in the stuff on time, just throw some stuff on there, it's fine. You will probably not get yeeted for being a little late, but one of the big parts of this course is that you have to learn to like, take a big project, and break it into little pieces. Like, come up with a research topic, then ask the TF, then you kinda look into it a bit, and turns out the data is screwed, so you go and dive around some more and change your inquiry a bit, etc. That process should take place over like, a number of weeks, not in one rapid mess. That's what this course will ideally teach you.
I learned a lot more about how there is an inherent tension between science and policymakers, and the development of both of these facets over time. I also learned a lot more about the idea of social science research in general, and how political science as a profession and academic community works. I think these discussions were really salient and will probably stick with me.
Choose a section topic you think you'll be genuinely interested in! Your final paper has to relate to the section topic to an extent, so if you choose a topic you hate, it'll be a long semester. If you like your topic and your section leader, this class will be amazing!
