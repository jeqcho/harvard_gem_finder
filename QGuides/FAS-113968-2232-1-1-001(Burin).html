This course is a great way to explore new mediums of art outside your comfort zone.
this course is required for AFVS concentrators so there's no getting out of it, but hopefully they design a better version of this class in future years. to get the most out of the course they currently offer, its important to know that this class is completely unstructured, so its up to you to design your own project, seek out feedback, and evaluate yourself. this class is a great opportunity to get to know people in the afvs department since you're all in the same section, but you definitely have to be proactive about it because there aren't many structured opportunities to share your work with the class and ask for feedback –– its mostly individual work time. so, definitely ask people about their projects and express interest! the one thing i got out of this class was knowing how cool and talented my peers in the afvs department are, across all our various artistic disciplines. hopefully you can make new friends out of this class, be inspired by people's work, and even find new collaborators!
This class is extremely frustrating. It changes year to year depending on the instructor so it's hard to say what the class will be like in the future. As for this year, the class is only designed for a small population of Studio AFVS concentrators. We had group critiques and studio time that was useless for any Film Studies or Film Production concentrators. There wasn't a syllabus and if you asked for any clarification from TFs they would get upset that you brought it up. I genuinely had no clue what we were doing the entire semester and ended up skipping a lot of class because when I did show up we would just sit there for 2 hours and "work on our projects," of which were never properly defined. I can see students who can lean into the lack of structure enjoying this course, but as someone who isn't like that, I really dreaded going to the class every week. I think the course staff was experimenting with an unconventional structure but missed the mark pretty badly.
Do not be afraid to try something completely different for your project than you are used to
The best part about this course is talking to Katarina and the TFs. I would encourage new students to take as much advantage of them as possible because they know a lot and can help you a lot, even if/especially if you kind of have no idea what your next steps are
Take as a fifth class! Unless they ramp up the workload in the future.
I'm not sure what the next iteration of this course will look like, but I'd tell any future student taking it to take each day as it comes.
If you would like to get something done in the class, you have a lot of support from the instructors but you really have to create your own structure. Lovely artistic experiments though
it's a sophomore requirement so you have to take it — I do hope the format changes and become a little more structured but if it's not I would tell students to just put in what is helpful for them and that's what they will get out! it's very self–guided and I struggled with that (but maybe that's more on me); if you take initiative, I think you can do cool things with the course
