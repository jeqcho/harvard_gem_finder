This course is very heavy on reading and for the papers, midterm, and final, you really can't get by with just reading an online summary, in my opinion. Certainly, you don't have to read word for word, but actually engaging with the text feels pretty crucial to succeeding in the course. I was hesitant about political theory going into the course, but I do think this course is a great introduction to the field and the assignments are fairly reasonable. This class is what it is. Political theory can be boring but the class explores cool thought experiments and applications to today. Professor Beerbohm tries to make it more interesting and is clearly very passionate about what he studies. You will be fine if you put in a normal amount of work.
This truly is a good introduction course and learning from someone who loves the content as much as Eric makes it so enjoyable!
Very good class that you will learn a lot from. Only meets once a week which is great for scheduling and for the students but not so great for staying on top of the material and topics. Section aspects were really fun and interactive which was helpful in understanding the material.
While this class is front–loaded with reading, it definitely gets less heavy towards the end. The professor also makes lectures very interesting and genuinely cares about the subject; both him and the TF are very available outside of class. I regret not taking more advantage of office hours and just engaging more with the professor, as he is genuinely interested in political theory and getting to know you as well. Overall, I would recommend taking this class as it has a manageable workload, supportive staff, and the material can be interesting when applied to real world scenarios. Even though I am a STEM person and have never even considered political philosophy in my life, I was able to manage and would still say take this class as it will be a good way to fulfill your theory requirement for gov.
This course provides a comprehensive overview of canonical and contemporary political theorists. The readings and lectures are long, but they will provide you with a good foundation if you plan to go into Government. I think taking this course made me realize that Government wasn't for me, but that's not due to any fault in the course itself ––– if you're wavering on Gov, I would take this class to gain some clarity on your decision.
Do the readings!!!! Dont procrastinate themit will seriously help you.
SO MUCH READING, DON'T FALL BEHIND. If you really care about learning the course content, deeply engage with weekly readings. Skipping out on them will significantly degrade your class experience. That being said, if you simply do not like doing heavy political theory readings every week, this class is just not for you. If you do like reading, you'll learn a lot through your own analysis of the texts.
This class is very well–organized, and Professor Beerbohm is a great lecturer. However, for me, this wasn't my favorite Gov class, but that's probably just due to the nature of the course material. I personally don't find political theory super interesting, so the readings and lectures felt very long and dry. I don't think there were any major issues with how the class was taught, though. If you're interested in theory or have to take this class for the requirement, I would recommend it, but don't expect it to be the most exciting Gov class ever.
Don't be afraid of political theory because the support system of the professor and TF are AMAZING!
If you are at all interested in political theory, this is an excellent class to take. Professor Beerbohm is enthusiastic about the material and wants students to succeed. There is significant reading for the course, but overall the work is manageable and the take–home exams prevent it from being too stressful.
Gov 10 is a great class for anyone wanting to learn more about political theory, gov concentrator or not! The class is very interactive, beginner friendly, and encourages you to think for yourself and connect concepts rather than memorizing them. The readings consist largely of primary sources and old texts, which is fascinating but takes a while to get through – I would definitely consider this a reading heavy course. The midterm and final are strenuous, but not unreasonable, and are ultimately fun essays to write if you're interested in the topics. Compared to other classes, I would say that it's a little difficult to know where you stand grade wise in this class until the very end. However, nothing was hard for the sake of being hard and I feel like all evaluations were very fair. I would recommend this course for anyone wanting to think philosophically or sharpen their writing skills!
