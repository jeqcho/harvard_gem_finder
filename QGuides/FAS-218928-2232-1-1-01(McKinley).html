Take this class! It has been one of my favorites at Harvard, and you learn so much. Kara and the teaching staff are all amazing and willing to help with any questions. The workload is not bad, and you walk away have such a good understanding about a part of biology that often is not taught at the undergrad level.
this is an amazing class! the subject matter is really interesting and unique– you won't find any other class at Harvard that's teaching reproductive biology. The teaching staff id also great, you can tell they really care about the well being and thoughts of their students. The assignments weren't bad at all either, and were mainly used as a learning opportunity or us. I liked how we didn't have lecture every class, and instead how sometimes we had guest speakers come and speak about interesting topics.
One of the best courses at Harvard! you will learn so so much about reproductive biology, well beyond just pregnancy. This class is especially wonderful because it links reproductive biology to the current ethical and social implications for reproductive health. You get to hear from expert panelists on topics like intersex health, abortions, surrogacy, and more. The course is not a ton of work and only 2 days a week. Great to take as a senior or if you have a difficult course load. As someone interested in medicine, this class has really encouraged me to pursue a field in the reproductive health realm.
Take this class!!! Every lecture was so interesting and Prof McKinley is such a great teacher and very approachable. The course load is very manageable (no exams, only 3 psets and a final project). This class not only talks about the scientific and medical side of reproductive biology, but also touches upon the ethics of some of these topics which gives a lot of nuance to topics such as intersex surgeries, surrogates and abortions. If you are interested in medicine/gynecology/urology or just want to learn about women's/men's health this class is for you.
TAKE THIS CLASS! It has such important content and it was challenging in the best way. I never felt like I was drowning, but I was constantly learning. The assignments never felt like busy work. I also appreciated the problem sets being worth a lot of our grade because it felt like extended take home exams in place of in class exams. Truly being able to sleep on a problem is so helpful. I also think the content itself is super important and not enough time is spend on reproductive/gonadal biology in general. Further, trans and intersex medicine was a larger theme in the course which I really appreciated. If you're a HDRB, you will be already used to the experimental designs we use, but if you're not you can still take the course because Professor McKinley has a bootcamp where you can learn everything. Overall, Professor McKinley was incredible to learn from. She was acutely aware of the ethically and timely weight of the content and language surrounding it. I felt that the complicated material was explained thoughtfully and this made the lectures easy to follow.
