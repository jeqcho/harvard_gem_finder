PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE. Peter is amazing, and the TFs give amazing feedback. The deep sea is really fascinating, and Peter does a great job–making lecture interesting. Even though there are only 4 essays throughout the course, because of the feedback from each essay I became a better scientific writer. If you aren't familiar with the sciences, Peter does a good job of covering the basics and making sure anyone of any background can succeed in this course. If I could take it again, I would.
This is a great, genuinely interesting, and easy class to fulfill your science distributional requirement. No prerequisite knowledge was necessary nor experience with the sciences. Just a really fun and interesting class to fulfill a pesky requirement.
Like a lot of the best classes, you get out what you put in.
Take this class!! Not only is the content fun and interesting, but the staff are willing to answer lots of questions and are very helpful in their feedback on assignments. You will learn a lot and become a better science writer without being stressed about grades. Also, there are lots of opportunities to connect with staff and learn about their careers. Pete is very knowledgeable and has been working in marine biology for a long time, so he is a great resource if you have questions about marine science, how it is funded, and how it is conducted.
I would rate this class PG for Peter Girguis.

The professor is a very nice guy. The amount of work is not nothing, but it isn't crazy (4 essays + 1 group presentation + 1 individual presentation). In general, it's a pretty good time, and in my opinion the course gets much better as the semester continues.
This class may have some lectures that are a bit hard to pay attention to, but overall this class is a great time. The main professor is great, the assignments are doable, and the class is a very chill environment. Great class too if you want an OEB class that has a bit of history too.
Enjoy!
Ample feedback is provided for papers. The teaching staff wants you to succeed as long as you put forth a bit of effort.
This class is very chill. There are 4 take–home essays throughout the semester so one about every 3 weeks. They required a fair bit of time and research and can be sort of tedious, but all 4 are posted at the beginning of the semester so you have a ton of time to do them if you start them early and are proactive. And even if you aren't, they are completely doable in a day, they'll just take up the majority of that day. You also have one group presentation at some point in the semester where you and your groupmates have to read a scientific paper and then present it to the class. Then there's a final presentation on a topic of your choosing. All in all a really interesting class if you have any interest in marine biology, and not a ton of work.
It is a very exciting and interesting class, with a light workload and a passionate teacher who will help you get closer to the field if you want to. However, it is not too strong on theory and oceanography principles (and it is not the goal of the class) which you may get from other classes.
Very interesting class; the day to day workload is pretty light (assignments are spread out and are not always directly related to concurrent lectures) and the information in each lecture is just so interesting. Definitely take if you have any interest on the topic. Bulk work of many of the assignment is looking for primary sources.
If you want an easy class, take this! No section, only two lectures a week, no weekly assignments, only four essays and two presentations. This is very low key. Most of the time, you don't even need to pay attension in the class honestly. But materials and videos are quite interesting. So, if you are taking hard courses and need an easy one but still want to learn something, I recommend this class!
