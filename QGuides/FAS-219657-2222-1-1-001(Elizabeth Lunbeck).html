PEOPLE OF THE Q GUIDE: congratulations, after hours of scrounging through q–guide reports, you stumble upon this comment and realize: you have found a gem–ed.

This course teaches you about the history of psychotherapy while going in–depth into each specific type of psychotherapy (PPT, CBT, AI –– these were all confusing acronyms to me before taking this course, but I now understand the intricacies of different therapy types and what therapy type might be right if myself or a friend ever needed help navigating the confusing scene of psychotherapy). The section of this course was MY therapy of the weak (obviously joking –– there's no real substitute for psychotherapy of medical needs, but the section of this class helped me relax and reflect on my week). Professor Lunbeck gives extremely interesting lectures and will sometimes stop halfway through the lecture to ask how everyone is doing and if she should continue. There are also weekly, ungraded writing assignments and for the larger essays, you can really write about whatever you want as long as you focus on psychotherapy in some form. Overall, this class has changed the way that I interact with people and the field of psychotherapy.

Seriously, look no further. This class was my favorite class from my entire freshman year.
