excellent class. John Hamilton is a wonderful professor and I would recommend taking a class with him before you graduate.
Professor Hamilton will change the way you feel about learning literature. He teaches in a unique style that emphasizes philosophy, philology, and intellectual history. Rarely will you find someone so knowledgable about Virgil, Poe, Lacan, Bataille, and Heidegger at the same time. Please take this class.
John is wonderful! A wonderful orator, providing engaging discussions! As someone less well versed in literature, he made sure that I felt comfortable in the class and was incredibly welcoming. He also would invite us to interrupt him anytime during his monologue for questions or comments and was always open to them. It is a good sign when I could tell he was genuinely eager for any of us students to jump into the conversation. Indeed he is also very accessible, being very happy to meet with anyone along with holding office hours every week.

He is also just a very friendly guy and can seemingly talk about anything, with expertise in so many areas. He is also very funny and his connections from his own life to the stories we were reading were incredible.

The way that John would analyze these texts was so insightful for me to learn from. I would recommend a class with him to anyone, even if it's not this one.
Take this course if you like to read German! Die Blechtrommel is a fantastic novel.
This course was so fun! This class has really shown the beauty of close reading
This class is AMAZING! The Tin Drum is an incredible novel which would usually be tough for non–German–speaking readers to properly appreciate, but this class makes it accessible, and The Fall has powerful, meaningful messages. More than all that, however, take this course because of Professor Hamilton –– he is an unbelievable lecturer, one whose class discussions always seem to span various themes, disciplines, and moral quandries in an immensely satisfying way. There are very few classes after which I have felt genuine appreciation and gratitude for the amazing faculty members available to us at Harvard –– this is one of them. If you get the opportunity to take this course, you should do so and feel fortunate.
If you really want to engage with literature, John Hamilton and his courses are the way to go. This is not Hum 10, superficially covering a ton of material. Prof. Hamilton puts the "close" in close reading, offering a truly unparalleled chance to ponder the text and give it the attention it deserves.

The class is definitely lecture–heavy but discussion is always welcome. And the lecturing itself is highly engaging. Prof. Hamilton's style is unmistakable and itself a good enough reason to take a class with him.
This is one of the most surprisingly fulfilling courses I've taken at the graduate literature level, across both Harvard and Dartmouth. The professor's knowledge is truly comprehensive, and if you respond well to more lecture–based courses, this is one of the better ones. Although some may feel lecture–styled courses are passe, I generally find I get a lot more out of an organized, coherent presentation than free–form student discussion, so I appreciated this style. There is opportunity for discussion, it just wasn't the main focus of the course.
This is an excellent course for anyone interested in the intersection between comp lit and philosophy. I had a great experience in this class.
