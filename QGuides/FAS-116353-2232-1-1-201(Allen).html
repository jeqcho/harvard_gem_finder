What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course can be quite time–consuming and challenging – set aside enough time in your class schedule for a solid workload. Also, be sure to utilize your preceptor’s office hours, the Writing Center, and for your third essay, Harvard Library resources, as these can make your life much easier.
As a required and bothersome course full of excruciating writing and reading assignments, there's not much to redeem EXPOS overall. But if you choose a course that looks interesting and has a caring and thoughtful instructor, it can be a much more mild experience, as is the case with City Living. Even as someone who wasn't especially interested in the topic of cities, I got a lot out of the course and found new ways to think about the physical world around me and how people function in a space, as well as philosophical concepts and psychological ideas to discuss and think about. Personally, the Unit 1 essay analyzing Maier's photographs was particularly enjoyable for me as a photographer and allowed me to stand on a topic I knew strongly in order to explore the unfamiliar topic of city living. Michael was a thoughtful, understanding, and fun instructor who facilitated really engaging and perspective–shifting discussions with the class. He is helpful and generous with extensions as long as you are upfront and communicative! I recommend choosing an EXPOS course with at least one essay you are excited to write from the get–go.
Please take this class! The readings are fair and most intriguing, and Michael is an amazing professor!
City Living is fantastic. Michael is an attentive, eloquent, and scrupulous professor, but above all, he is a caring person. The sort of person you are lucky to have has a professor here. And the material is lots of fun too, provided that you have any sort of interest in how cities work. Take the class—you won't regret it. : )
Take this course if you want a preceptor who offers incredibly thoughtful and detailed feedback on your work.
Michael is an incredible preceptor who genuinely cares about his students — our discussions in class and the community build through this course made Expos feel more manageable, and I would heavily recommend anyone take this.
Be prepared for a class that could be potentially interesting; yet, at the same time a bit dull. Choose a subject that you like, for you'll be writing three papers about it.
