Taking a class with a professor who is a leader in the field they are teaching about is an incredible experience, and I would highly recommend it if you have the opportunity. Note that this class doesn't seem so strict at the beginning about what prerequisites you need to have, but don't be fooled the prerequisites are important in this class! I think it's misleading that it's registered in the MCB/Neuro department — this class is mostly math/stats/physics.
I would not recommend––probably use your time to take a more instructive course instead of a survey course such as this one. Do not take this course if you are planning to learn anything with sufficient depth.

I enjoyed this course, but it was certainly challenging. Working with partners on the psets is a huge help, and ask for extensions if you need them. The course staff were super accommodating throughout the semester.
This is an amazing class (and an amazing opportunity to be lectured by a very known researcher in this field). However, that means the material is hard and it takes a while to understand it. Take this class with a study buddy if you are an undergrad, but nevertheless I recommend it.
