What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I came into this course with a passing interest in space life sciences, and left with a passion for the field that will drive me in my future research. The material in this course, especially the focus on spaceflight and human health, is something you probably wouldn't find in most universities, let alone with 2 professors as knowledgeable and well–connected as Gary and Vlad. That alone made the class very compelling for me, and if you have even a basic interest in extreme environments and medicine, I'd absolutely recommend taking the course just for those reasons.

That being said, you should know that this course focuses much more on overall physiology than just neuroscience. While this does mean that having a neuro background isn't important to understand the material, you do need a good background in anatomy/physiology to appreciate the material. The lectures, while fascinating, can seem a bit disjointed at times – perhaps this is just because of the very niche nature of extreme medicine, but sometimes I was left wanting a clearer understanding of how each specific extreme environment affects the human body instead of a combination of all environments at the same time. The variance in stress levels I experienced from this course was also really high – from very low during the weeks with just a quiz (which are quite easy), to medium during the week of my presentation, to quite high when having to write a 7000 word term paper during reading period, which was far longer than any term paper I've ever had to write. This wasn't helped by the fact that the course has no TFs or CAs to provide assistance for assignments and that Gary and Vlad provide very little feedback (or even grades!) on completed assignments.

Ultimately, despite the caveats above, I'd still gladly recommend this course to anyone interested in going beyond clinical medicine to learn about environments outside the hospital, especially spaceflight – the material is just that interesting, and the unanswered research questions that compelling.
This course is super interesting material–wise, but has some organizational issues in the syllabus. Readings are not necessary prior to class, but are usually interesting reads to do after class if you want to learn more. Assignments are minimal and this course is low stress. Professors have undeniable expertise and first–hand experience with NASA and offer a lot of interesting stories, while they can be very hard to reach via email.
