This class has singlehandedly been the most rewarding academic experience I've had at Harvard. I loved 21A, and I'm not even a math person. If you put in the effort, you will be fine. Course staff are here for you and so very helpful. Take this class.
3 Psets a week is a lot. Even if you find them to be easy, you can sometimes end up staring at a pset for a while simply because it feels like they are never ending. Midterms are more than fair (generally much easier than course materials leading up to them), and the final is very fair as well. Make sure you get a good TF and have a good study group you can rely on; if not, MQC is an amazing resource!
Take math 21A!! The concepts taught in this course are widely applicable to a variety of other fields. It is rewarding learning the course concepts and very enjoyable too. The TFs and CAs are great and can provide lots of support whenever you might need it. One piece of advice I'd give is that 21A is less about computation than something like high school calc, so just be aware of that and be prepared to become a stronger problem solver. TLDR: Take this class, you won't regret it!
If you're worried about the 3 psets a week, know that it definitely gets manageable––I too was scared but very soon found a community of other 21a students, MQC teachers, and TF office hours. There are so many support systems and I love this class dearly. One of my favorite and most memorable classes by far. With that said, it is definitely not easy. You need to put in the work and it does get easy to fall behind just because of how fast this class goes. I went straight into 21a without doing 1b and it was difficult in the beginning but I soon got the hang of it in the end!
