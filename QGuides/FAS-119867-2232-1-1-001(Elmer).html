What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Lots and lots of Greek, though all the material that Professor Elmer picked out was very fun and good for generals. Gave me a decent background for the context, but we didn't get to translate that much in class.
If you don't have experience reading much poetry, this class will be immensely difficult, but still very much worth it.
This is an incredible class –– if you can manage to take both 112A and 112B as an undergraduate, you should. You'll learn so much, but be prepared to sacrifice a fair amount of sleep for the readings. The selections of text are usually outstanding –– this is basically a greatest hits list class –– but some weeks will be much more challenging than others, so be prepared to spend anywhere from 3 to 8 hours per reading depending on difficulty and your familiarity with the material.
