TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This was my top choice to fulfill my final gened requirement, Ethics and Civics, and I am so glad it worked out that I got to take it. While the workload might look like a lot on paper (daily reading quizzes, weekly discussion posts, a midterm, a final, and a culminating project), it really ended up proving very reasonable. The quizzes were 3 multiple choice questions each, you only have to get 2/3 questions right on at least 2/3 of the quizzes throughout the semester, and their questions are quite manageable (and encourage you to engage with the readings more critically!). Also, your discussion posts each week could literally be a meme if you wanted (for which you can win philosopher finger puppets!), and the project allows you to be creative too and is not super long. Prof. Damrosch is brilliant, super down–to–earth, and just really wholesome. His lectures are relatively slow–paced, and while the reading materials (and by extension the lectures) could get a bit dense during the early modernity section, he always had lots of insightful perspectives that weren't just regurgitating the readings back to you. The last week of lectures was particularly interesting, as we discussed the ongoing Russian invasion of Ukraine through Volodymyr Zelensky's old television show, as well as got to hear from a guest speaker on the issue. He also formed an awesome course staff in Min Ji, Alex, and Lara, so fingers crossed they all stay on the course for next year!
Highly recommended, the materials are interesting and the teaching is memorable. I had no prior experience with many of the things touched on by the class and still got a lot out of it. A significant portion of the class grade is split among the minor things like section participation, attendance, etc. so if you're not the kind of student that always stays on top of those things (like me), DON'T be so fast to sweep those under the bus. 10/10 still.

I was a premed taking this class. I was little anxious coming into the class (actually I was shaking in my boots), but I stuck with it and got an incredibly enriching experience in addition to free finger puppets. To future students, dont be intimidated at all by the texts, the course is very doable if you put some earnest effort and time towards it. Lecture can be a little bit of a strange experience for those more accustomed to science/math lectures; Prof Damrosch speaks a lot and much of the substance is in his words and not the slides. That all being said the lectures themselves felt to go beyond the readings in a sense; you could succeed in the class just listening to them and not taking notes. The biggest help is actively trying to make connections between the texts you read.

Before enrolling in this class, Id recommend looking at the syllabus and bearing in mind the number of readings and discussion post assignments; I took this class with Chem27 and SCRB50 so at times the readings got a little in the way. The work load is fine; just schedule time for your readings and your discussion posts youll breeze through them.

The TFs are all bangers; help them help you especially when it comes to your final projects and exam prep.

If youre a big STEM person, Id recommend taking this class. As a big STEM person myself, this class opened my eyes to extremely important texts and topics. Seeing the comp lit folks and how that department operates. is also quite entertaining. By far the hardest part of the course was trying not to disintegrate when one of the TFs read a selection from the Mandragola aloud in class. Id recommend taking the class just for that one moment.
