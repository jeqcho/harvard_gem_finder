Professor Logevall is great! Super engaging course and good variety in lectures (speed, topic, speakers, etc.).
This class is wonderful! Lectures especially are fantastic. Professor Logevall is a fantastic teacher, and he brings in a bunch of amazing guests. I would guess there were 5–6 guest lectures throughout the semester and they were all really nice. Reading is often fun, although there's a decent amount (anywhere between 100–250 pages a week). Assignments aren't difficult, and the environment is generally laid back. For an undergrad, it can seem a bit intimidating, because the class is filled mostly with Grad students and Fellows. But Professor Logevall does a great job including undergrads in discussions and making them feel welcome. Really, if it seems even slightly interesting, I highly recommend.

Talk to the different students in the class; lots of different opinions / interests. Take advantage of office hours too.
If you are looking for an applied history course or want to learn about how you can apply history in practical terms (e.g. decision–making, policy), take this course! You will learn about how leaders should apply history when making decisions and why history should be taken into account. Don't be discouraged that there will be grad school students in this course! This actually makes for a memorable learning experience! You get to learn from peers who come from different fields/backgrounds. You should also stay up to date with current events (aka read the news).
This class is super interesting! It can feel a little daunting to be surrounded by mostly grad students, but at the same time it's really cool to be exposed to so many varying degrees of expertise. The class is reasonably accessible if you have no background in history or policy. The work itself is reasonable–––weekly briefs engaging with the reading materials, a couple in–class activities, and a final paper. Professor Logevall is an incredible lecturer who is genuinely interested in the material; his lectures alone are worth taking this class.
