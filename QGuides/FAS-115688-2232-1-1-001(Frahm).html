This class is very philosophical. It is not a practical film course, meaning you will not learn about different cinematic techniques and how to use them. If you like discussing the philosophy of what makes something art or cinema, this class is for you. If you do not, do not take this course.

This is the best class that I have had all year. It is incredibly engaging, the community is welcoming, and the work — although there are a lot of readings — can be really fascinating. There are three projects — and the deadlines do sneak up on you! — but they are all really fun and your TFs give fantastic feedback. The sections are wonderful and I urge you to go to them as much as possible. They are helpful with applying the readings to lecture. Professor Frahm also is one of the best lecturers that I have ever had and she really applies the readings to what it is that we talk about in class and watch during film nights.

This was a really calm and nice course! This is not a film class where you watch box office hits, but rather one where you learn to see how film has and does operate as an artform. There are a lot of old films (silent, political films, foreign cinema too), so be prepared for that. I would be patient with the course material, as it can seem dull at first, but I think the lens through which it is approached in this class really animates it.

If you are super interested about the specific material (read the syllabus very closely!) then definitely take this class. It’s way more fun if you come to lecture and do the readings!
This is, somewhat regrettably, the intro class for AFVS. If you're into film studies, you'll probably want/need to take it—it's a very good introduction to the history and critical study of film, and Professor Frahm is an incredibly friendly and devoted instructor. If you're only interested in film, though, and not in really getting to know film studies as a discipline, I would skip it. The reading load is intense and, at least to me, seemed way heavier than it needed to be, especially since there were usually only one or two readings that were really critical to the topic of the week. Two of the projects involve video editing, so if you don't have any prior experience, be prepared to spend some time learning that skill on your own, since there isn't much help provided in class. As always, you get out of the class what you put in, but this class seemed to demand much more effort than most do to be rewarding.
