It is worth taking this class. It is helpful in providing useful details about thermodynamics in the real world, including biology and machinery. Overall a great class that provides lots of information, which may be a lot but it’s worth taking.
I feel like you will make more out of this course if you embrace its participative nature. Find topics within classes that you enjoy the most to make thoughtful and interesting presentations. Go to section often as these will walk you through how to answer the problem sets and will be more targeted towards the homework and midterm.
Enjoy this class, and be prepared to present a lot! I was scared about the presentation component initially, but the whole class becomes so supportive throughout the semester because of this. It is rare that I talk to each member of a class, but I think I have developed a connection with each class member.
Prof Martin really cares about his students, and he makes himself available if you ever have questions. The course is structured well and goes in–depth through different applications of thermodynamic concepts. Great way to fulfill a chemistry requirement.
This class is a bit tedious, but that is the nature of an intro thermo class. Scot really cares about the subject so if you are also engaged he is a really great resource. The emphasis on practical applications and student presentations makes it more interesting. If you have the choice of this over 181 then def take this . The workload is very manageable and overall it feels more like a highschool type course than a college lecture.
This is a great class to take, especially to fulfill a thermodynamics requirement.
