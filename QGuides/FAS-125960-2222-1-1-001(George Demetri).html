This freshman seminar is likely one of the best ones! Dr. Demetri does a great job of keeping the class low–stress while also facilitating engaging discussion about various facets of cancer.
TAKE. THIS. SEMINAR. If you are remotely interested in cancer, medicine, science, pharmaceuticals and drug development, even business administration, or if you're just looking for a neat seminar that's outside of your usual fields of interest, take this seminar. Dr. Demetri is a warm, kind, knowledgeable, passionate doctor and professor with a talent for giving engaging lectures. This seminar was, to me, everything a freshman seminar should be: low commitment, high enrichment. Every week I walked into class knowing I would spend two hours learning fascinating bits of information about all sorts of different topics in an accessible, entertaining fashion. Outside of class, I never felt pressure or stress because of work for the class; in fact, there's barely any. 10/10, would recommend highly to anyone.
First, two hour seminars are a lot to handle for your focus and mental strength so eat before you go, and do your best to sleep enough the day before. This seminar is highly informative and Dr. Demetri brings refreshingly honest stories about cancer research. The lack of class participation in the discussion during the seminar can make the seminar drag out longer because of the silence. There are very few assignments, and take notes if you feel inspired to retain your information, but they are not necessary.
This class is a gem!! Dr. Demetri is such a passionate physician and professor. You will learn so much about cancer, from the basic science to the patient care to public policy and more. We also went on fieldtrips to Dana Farber and a local biotech, which were incredible learning opportunities. The class is very low stress, and allows you to focus on learning rather than the grade. I cannot recommend this seminar enough!
Definitely take this class even if you don't have any background in biology or cancer. You learn so much and the professor provides so many interesting insider stories of what it's like working with the development of cancer treatments and being a doctor in general!
