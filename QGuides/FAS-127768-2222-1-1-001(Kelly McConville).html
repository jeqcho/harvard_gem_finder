What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Great class to ease you into research. The last project is a great opportunity to dive into something you're interested in.
The only problem is that grading is a bit arbitrary (I never had any idea what I would get for any assignment).
If you are interested in scientific research in the future (statistical or otherwise), I would strongly recommend this class. If nothing else, Stat 98 will force you to work on an extended open–ended project, which is a very useful experience. The grading can be very harsh, which is a little demoralizing after multiple revisions and many hours of work, but the course staff are very helpful and the class has a collaborative feel.
If you're considering writing a thesis, this class is the class to take. Would highly recommend it as a stepping stone to senior thesising, and you get a chance to work closely with Kevin and Kelly, which in turn helps you get to know the stats department better. The class is still helpful for those not planning on writing a thesis, but if you have no interest in statistics research and writing a thesis, I would suggest taking a different stats course instead.
This is kind of a weird class, with no set curriculum and driven by a few independent research projects. Definitely not anything like other stat classes. If you are doing summer research, or a thesis, this is a great way to get up to speed in whatever area of statistics you will go into, since there are plenty of structured assignments that involve literature review of your choosing.
Overall, I thought this tutorial was a great course to begin thinking about how to communicate the statistical concepts we've learned so far. The professors are so helpful, approachable, and kind, and made the course such a wonderful learning environment. There are three main assignments that help you develop your writing, presenting, and simulation skills. There are plenty of collaborative sessions throughout the semester for students to read each other's work and give constructive feedback. The only downsides were that sometimes the grading would be unclear/harsh and cause stress among the students. I was also under the impression that the tutorial would also help you find a thesis topic/advisor, which didn't turn out to be true. You do get to know the professors quite well though, giving you more opportunity to ask them about writing a thesis (if that's what you're interested in).
