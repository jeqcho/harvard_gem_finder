What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is great. You will learn far more than you could ever imagine. The instructors go above and beyond to share historical information. You are pushed to understand why specific methods were chosen and the shortcomings of these methods. It is worth your time. But it is a lot of time. You will likely be stressed every week. The pros outweigh the cons.
This course requires a lot of reading and effort, but it will improve your reading comprehension and critical thinking skills. The course provides a very comfortable and supportive class environment.
Take this course if you have always wanted to "read more papers" but never seem to get around to it.
This is an excellent class for anyone remotely interested in gene expression. The course is discussion–based, and assignments are basically just reading papers and preparing to participate in the conversation. You'll cover a broad range of topics; some weeks are more mechanistic, and others are more conceptual, so there's a little bit of something for everyone. All three professors are excellent instructors (and generally very kind people!). Would definitely recommend GEN216. :)
This class was wonderful. I spent time reading scientific papers deeply, and through that experience, I have come to understand genetics from a more inquiry–centered perspective, which has allowed me to appreciate the field even more. This course allows you to have deep, and sometimes existential, discussions about science and genetics, and will definitely help you improve your thinking as a scientist. This class was a very great, very wonderful, experience. I would highly recommend you take this class if you are even remotely interested, just be prepared to read the papers and be ready to have interesting discussions about them. Great, great class!
Fantastic class that everyone interested in gene expression should consider taking! Instructors are incredibly knowledgeable and provide excellent guidance throughout the group discussions, but with a relaxed environment. Will really expand your conceptual understanding of gene expression, and change how you think about experimental design. Topics have a nice breadth, varying from historical papers (like the discovery of mRNA) to recent work in phase separation.
