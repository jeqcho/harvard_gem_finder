Review:
This was, understandably, by far my hardest class. I averaged to about 45 hours of work per week in total for all of my academic work this semester. 34% of the time was spent on this class alone. So, do keep in mind that you will have to spend a LOT of time on the class. You HAVE to manage your time well. Do not make the same mistake as me and take 5 classes during your CS124 semester. It was a stressful semester to say the least. I recommend taking as many easy classes to go with this class so you have more time for this class.

I would also not recommend taking this class freshman year if you do not believe in yourself a lot. Remember, I didn't have any of Stat 110, CS61, cs120, cs20, etc. This will act as a multiplier on how difficult the class is. Do NOT try to prove yourself through this class as this class will defeat you. You being at Harvard or you being one of the most clever students even within the Harvard cohort doesn't mean anything for this class. You will struggle a lot. Get used to it. Psychologically preparing yourself for the incoming doom is important for having a healthy experience with this class as having high expectations will actually cause you more harm than good.

For assignments, make EXTENSIVE use of Office hours and make sure to attend at least two per week. I would not recommend going to class if you are not already in the SEC for something else. Watch at 2x speed and learn more. Definitely watch and digest MIT 6.042 and 6.006 before taking this class if you can since they will help a lot. Your grade will ultimately be determined by the two exams. This is because Office hours make Psets and Progsets essentially easy. The median for the second progset, for example, was an astounding 100/100. The median! You must differenciate yourself through the exams. According to an Ed post by the professor, the first 19–21 percent get an A. Assume that at least 70% of the class is gunning for that top 20 percent. You will most likely not be in that cohort. The next 19–21 get an A–, what I am currently aiming for. The median on the midterm was a 38.5/75. Mean: 40.43, SD 12.55 and the max was a frustrating 75. Assuming a normal distribution, my score would land me at the 70th percentile by my calculations. Good for an A–, but not ideal.

i have a lot of thoughts about this class, so buckle in. for full transparency, i am going to start with the negatives, so i guess skip ahead if you're looking for a more positive look on the class, because there are positives. first of all, this class is really poorly designed. lectures are oftentimes confusing and not structured very well, and one thing that i could not get past is that adam and madhu's handwriting is both really terrible. i cannot read their handwriting. and when they erase the board they use this tiny little eraser that just smears all of the chalk around, and so when they write stuff over the erased stuff, it's even harder to see. this is such an avoidable thing and it made parts of the class way more annoying than it needed to be. i digress. alongside the not–great lectures, the psets were usually just annoying for the sake of being annoying. they were long as hell and just had obnoxiously difficult questions. this class could definitely teach as much as it does and accomplish the same amount of learning for its students even if the psets were slightly shorter. because my pset buddies and i were spending twenty+ hours on each pset and going to essentially every office hours offered. on the same note of office hours, they were always overcrowded and as a result, the teaching fellows physically could not get through all of the students in the two hours per office hours. perhaps this just means that they need to offer more office hours at the same time so students are forced to go to one over the other, but again, making psets slightly nicer would be helpful as well.

on to the positives. one of the biggest positives is that despite their terrible handwriting, adam and madhu are both really incredible people. i freaked out about midway through the semester and ended up talking to adam after class about how much i was #struggling, and he and i ended up sitting down together for twenty minutes and talking through study strategies and how to deal with the fact that this class was my fourth lecture of the day. he also met with me the next day via zoom to work through my most recently graded pset and chatted about how to improve my proof rigor and how i could improve my psets in the future. with that, he also sent me a follow–up email after the midterm to check in with me, which was much appreciated and something he absolutely didn't have to do, but chose to anyways. also, even though the teaching fellows are usually swamped with the amount of people in their office hours, they're always kind and willing to re–explain things when they don't make sense. the patel fellow is also wonderful, and also, the teaching fellows are super flexible and a lot of them made review videos for the midterm, which, again, they totally did not have to do but chose to anyways. so, the people affiliated with the course are fantastic. one last note for this paragraph is that you do end up learning SO MUCH. the material is really fun and it's cool to see your algorithm brain growing over the course of the term, and even if questions on the pset or whatever don't make sense upon first glance, working to make them make sense is really rewarding.

one of the other biggest positives for this course for me was my pset group. my three–person pset group was my saving grace in the course. for study strats, the three of us evenly divided the pset and were assigned questions and then re–taught them to each other – and although if we were a bigger group this could have been more efficient, the three of us worked really well together and indeed, that strategy worked really well for us. nonetheless, my two pset buddies and i became pretty close friends and would literally have to set timers for work and 'hangout time' because we would just talk for hours. i would literally die for the two of them. i suppose what i'm trying to say with this stupid paragraph is that the students in this course are also always and always the best people ever. whether that's in office hours and you and someone else end up trading solutions or whatever, or whether that's your ragtag pset group you randomly end up in, the people are so kind. and, maybe, you'll luck out as much as i did and end up in the best pset group ever (love you forever dan).

tl;dr this class is kind of bad sometimes but the people and material do, in some sense or another, make up for it. <3 if you end up taking this, best of luck! you're going to do great.
Whatever you read in the Q guides, it's true. I usually think the Q guide for CS50 and 51 are extremely exaggerated and take way less time they the average says, but this class is no joke. Be prepared to spending 15–20 hours a week on psets, and oh my god, the tests are very very difficult. You'll walk out feeling horrible about it, but so does everyone else (if thats any consolation). The curve won't be as nice as a class like Stat 110, but there still is a curve. Despite all this you will learn a lot, but you have to stay on top of work. Don't get behind on lectures, read over the course notes, watch youtube videos, read over ED posts, and go to section if you need help with the material you learn that week or else you will be lost on the psets. You will also need to go to office hours to do these pset, as some of the solutions are insanely complicated and others are simple, but incredibly clever. That being said, I think this class and CS51 is some of the best prep you will get for technical SWE interviews. You learn so much about algorithms, data structures, their runtimes, and even though there are only 3 programming psets, it makes you a better thinker and coder. This class will probably the hardest you ever take, but it will be worth it if you put in the work
