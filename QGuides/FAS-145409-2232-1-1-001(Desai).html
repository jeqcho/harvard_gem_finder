Great class for an introduction, but must be self–motivated to learn.
This is a chill upper level oeb course that lets you explore more quantitative biology in a lower stress way (psets completion based). Desai is also a really interesting lecturer. However, the pset reviews felt like a waste of time and the course is very unstructured which can make it difficult to learn/retain information.
For undergrads, I think this course is a lot like LS1B but with Stat 110 + more depth. They very casually will throw in distributions like the Poisson, Exponential, NBin, Binomial, Geometric, Gaussian, and kind of expect you to know and be comfortable with those + using their PMFs/CDFs right away, so for people without that exposure, it can feel a bit like using mystery formulas. I think that some of the math gets really complicated with partial differential equations + everything, but they mostly abstract it away, so you don't really have to be able to derive anything. I think the first exam was fair + very similar to the psets, and the second exam was much more difficult (at least the first question was much more difficult) as the content covered was also a bit more involved mathematically + statistically. Definitely try to understand the psets, whether before or after they are due, and pay attention in lecture!! I would always give myself a solid amount of time to really think + check + work through the exams, so even though they are take–home + open–book, definitely take them seriously because your grade is basically determined by these two exams.
This is definitely a challenging class, but because the psets are completion graded and the exams are take home, it's a little easier to focus on learning rather than stressing about grades. The professors are also very approachable and seem eager to talk to students. I would definitely suggest taking a stats course before taking this class, even if it's not listed as a prerequisite.
Read the textbook!
The teaching staff is so sweet and helpful, and the class is really interesting and low–stress
This is a great AM Bio class. The teaching staff is kind.
