What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Workload is heavy so make sure you have time in your schedule for multiple written assignments each week.
Fun class and definitely worth it if you're planning to write a thesis! Practicing the process of a long term project was really helpful. Course content is a good overview of water topics but not very in depth. ESE classes would be a better way to engage with numbers/real environmental science processes.
I would only take this class if you are ready to dedicate a lot of time to writing and researching. This class can be a lot of work at times (the final project is a cumulation of 5 separate 10 page assignments), however it is rewarding once you see your final paper come together and receive feedback from local NGOs and academics on your hard work. The small class size also allows you to get to know everyone, and Ken makes an effort to make it a fun and friendly environment. Go on the spring break trip if you can, it definitely made the course and workload worthwhile and allowed me to better understand what we had learned in lecture and section.
I cannot recommend this class enough. Ken is an amazing instructor who cares deeply about the intellectual progress of his students, and this course is strong on practical experience that gives you a sense of what it's really like to consult or work in the water world. Just remember the quizzes!
Ken is a very knowledgeable professor!! This class was a lot of work, but it's rewarding to have a final project that comprises of the work you have done throughout the semester. The work is spaced evenly so that it's not too much by the final – but you will be writing ~ 10 pages/wk throughout the semester. Take the class if you can go on the trip!!
This class was really fun because of the field trip and the focus on specific logistics in developing countries. However, it was a lot more work than advertised, and it could be very difficult to understand what the actual expectations are for grades.
