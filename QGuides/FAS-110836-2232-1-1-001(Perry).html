What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Prof. Perry is a legend for a reason; this is a fantastic course and she is an expert at leading discussions and helping students grasp the tools and power (and limitations) of political science as a discipline. She assigns one (generally relatively recent) book on China and allows students to pick from a list of other non–China classics in the field on the topic. The juxtaposition of the two books was generally thought–provoking and helpful, and also meant that I both learned more about the field and had some agency in picking my weekly readings. (It did also mean that the course was a lot of work, but I think it's absolutely worth your while to read both books .) Overall, I gained a deeper understanding of important topics in comparative politics and in the study of China.
If you have a chance to take this class, you probably should. Me, personally? Well, I certainly would not pass up on this one.
As many have said prior, this course in incredibly rigorous yet well worth it, if one has the time and ability to put in the work. The China–specific readings cover a wide range of topics, and the open nature of the selected "non–China" readings allows students of political science who are looking to brush up or expand their understanding of key literature an opportunity to do so.
I would recommend students who are interested in Chinese politics and want to do some research in this field to take this class. It will be a very rewarding experience.
