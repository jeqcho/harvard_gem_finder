I highly recommend this course if you are interested in improving your Spanish–speaking skills and gaining an understanding of the public health problems facing many Latin American countries. Professor Gutiérrez is excellent!
It is a super relaxed class with generally fun assignments. Profe Gutierrez is awesome.
Spanish 61PH is a great class for everyone, especially premeds. Profesora Gutierrez is super helpful and knows a lot about other ways to stay involved with Spanish in the Boston community, beyond her class. It is a very low–stress class and the main focus is improving your Spanish. As with any class, you get out what you put in, so I would definitely encourage speaking a lot in class to get the most practice and speaking feedback.
This is an amazing class!!!! Profe is really caring, enthusiastic, and passionate about this course. The readings and materials are all very interesting. & worth the read! This class is also a very nice way to balance a tougher work load. Very low–stress class. I definitely recommend!
This course is phenomenal and Professor Gutierrez is amazing! Take this course if you have an interest in global health and want to engage this interest as it pertains to Latin American countries.
Another great Spanish dept. course. All of class you’re either discussing the readings/prep content, or listening to another student give s presentation. The readings aren’t easy, but they aren’t too long and bring a lot of interesting discussion in class. Prof. Gutierrez is super understanding and flexible.
This class is amazing if you have any interest in public health and want to get a feel of the Spanish vocab for topics like tuberculosis, depression, etc. It felt like a normal public health class just conducted in Spanish which was amazing. Adriana is an amazing professor and truly wants to see you succeed. Assignments are very low stress and deadlines are flexible.
Take this course! And take 61n! They've been my two absolute favorites at Harvard. The readings are from so many different authors and so interesting, and none of the assignments are too hard, although they let you think more deeply about the readings. And sections are so engaging and produce awesome discussions.
