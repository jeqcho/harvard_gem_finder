This course was incredibly challenging in material and was the hardest course I've ever taken. However, I met the challenge (with a lot of struggle) and learned a lot in the process. The grading was lenient and flexible enough that even though I was struggling I still did well, and I probably should have been less stressed about it than I ended up being because of that.
This course provided a good overview of the field of organometallic chemistry, and it was also a good course for experiencing what types of research projects are done in the field through the proposal project. I learned a lot of fundamental chemistry in this course as well as some specific reactions and formalisms, and it certainly made me more interested in catalytic processes in chemistry. The class is fairly straightforward (as a chemistry undergraduate) if you have some experience with organic chemistry, and no part of the class was ever too stressful or high stakes (even when the material gets challenging, there is plenty of ways to learn it over time and the class is never a rush). Overall, it was a really strong class and I hope to see it offered to future students (graduate and undergraduate).
The class itself is very fair and Richard is a great lecturer. You get out of this class what you put into it, so make sure to habitually review the lecture notes and not put off studying just because quiz retakes are available.
Take this course! It's a great class at an appropriate rigor for graduate and undergraduate students alike. It's interesting and you will have a greater appreciation for organometallic and inorganic chemistry.
TAKE THIS CLASS! You learn so much but it is also a lower–stakes environment. Richard is knowledgeable without being pretentious, which is such a rare find in chem. This class was great and I took away so much and learned so much information. I literally cannot recommend it highly enough, especially if you want to know more about organometallics. It is a thorough expansion of Chem 40 that is manageable and well thought out.
I learned so much in this class because it was quite different chemistry from standard organic chemistry. It's definitely the single class that I learned the most information in and it made me more interested in organometallic chemistry. The professor and teaching staff was so nice and willing to answer any question you have. Really good at teaching the topics in a way that's easy to understand even if you had no knowledge of organometallic. Professor Liu is also really responsive to feedback as after the midterm evaluations, he made minor adjustments to the class to address people's feedback. The assignments for the class were really manageable and the professor is lenient and understanding if you needed extensions. This class taught me more chemistry than all of the previous chemistry courses I've taken and the way that things are taught, I'm actually retaining the information unlike some of my other courses, where I forget everything after. Definitely take the class if you can.
If you’re an organic chemist, you need to take this course. Organotransition metal chemistry is so adjacent to modern organic research that it is essentially the same field. Prof Liu does a great job of exposing you to the fundamentals and advanced concepts, and after taking this course you will have a much stronger grasp of the roles of metals in organic chemistry.
Lectures were very difficult to follow. However, I'm sure Richard would aim to improve this class for next year. There are no psets, but this made grasping the content quite difficult. My recommendation would be to give out short psets every week covering the most important concepts.
I would encourage students to take this class, especially if they are interested in organometallics but feel as though there is a lot of literature out there but few resources that are streamlined, organized, and not overwhelming.
A nice class to learn about organometallic chemistry beyond Chem 30/40. You'll learn a lot of different reactions. Richard's helpful and genuinely cares about his students, so I recommend going to his office hours!
The class is a great opportunity to learn in a relatively stress–free manner about an interesting area of chemistry that is often not covered to the extent it should in any other course.
I think Chem 153 was an interesting class and I have learned a lot. It has been the first time in many years that it was offered so it definitely was not perfect. However, I am confident it will get better in the years to come. The quizzes/exams are very fair and Richard is very happy to answer all your questions.
Chill class. Each week is a quiz that you can retake as many times as you want, meaning it's extremely flexible, and I could easily catch up whenever I fell behind. The project is treated very considerably, and the final is very similar to the weekly quizzes. Instructor super chill. Definitely recommend if you're into exploring organic chemistry + transition metals at all.
This class is an excellent upper level chemistry class. You'll learn a lot about metals and organometallic reactions. Quizzes are fair and can be retaken so you really learn the concepts from each week. The course staff is very friendly and are excellent at explaining things.
I think you get out of this course what you put in. For a lot of the lectures I felt very out of my depth, but it's a graduate–level course at the end of the day, and by the end of the class I felt like I had a much better command of the material than on any given day of the lecture. The quiz retakes helped reinforce a lot of the learning, so be sure to do those.
