What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this course! Asakura–sensei is an amazing instructor.
Reflecting on my last Japanese (language) course at Harvard, I can confidently say that I would take this course again should I have the opportunity. This course is much less concrete language learning, and leans heavily into strengthening your ability to articulate yourself and your ideas in Japanese. Most of the people in this course had near–native language ability, making it easy to learn off your peers and their knowledge. Good blend of undergrads and grad students, and due to the small class size, we all became friendly.
This course is a natural extension of all of the other Japanese courses.
JPN 150 in general has a lesser emphasis on kanji and memorizing specific kanji and tango. You get to spend more time reading and dissecting texts, and then having deeper discussions in class about those texts, which made this year feel more like a really good section: deep and meaningful discussions on assigned readings where you get out how much you put in.
Overall, I'm really sad that this is the last year of Japanese offered, because I've gained so much throughout my journey of learning Japanese b/c the Japanese language department invests a lot in making you more proficient in Japanese and able to understand the culture and language at an advanced level.
I highly recommend taking this course for anyone who is interested in engaging in advanced discussions about social/political issues as well as works of literature in Japanese.
This is it –– the final frontier. This is the highest level of Japanese offered at the greatest school in the world.

To be honest, if there is one thing that all 150B students know, it is that the 'real' language–learning journey starts AFTER this class. This class is merely the turnstile that you have to pass through in order to start your adventure in earnest. To truly become a sage in the field of Japanese Studies, you have to ascend to a point where you no longer have an explicit 'need' for teachers.

So, if you like REALLY like learning Japanese, try making it to the point where you get to write a comment like this one!
