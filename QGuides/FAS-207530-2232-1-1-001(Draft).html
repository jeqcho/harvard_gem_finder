What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Neuro115 is definitely a challenging course, but it is rewarding!
This is a really cool class if you enjoyed the first half of neuro80 about ion channels etc. It just a deeper dive into that and the pace of the class is really good and manageable. You will have time to fully understand most of the concepts and in lab you actually get to patch clamp neurons and record from them which is very exciting. You can record action potentials and actually measure currents through K and Na channels. It's also actually collecting data and you'll learn some useful lab skills.
Furthermore, the teaching staff is really nice and excited/passionate about the material. I had a really good time in this course and have a better appreciation of how plasticity and ion channel characteristic can lead to different types of action potentials and synaptic responses and how that can have impact on behavior and firing patterns. Really cool class, def recommend.
Highly recommend this class! You will learn how to patch clamp in lab, which is a very useful skill to have if you are interested in neuro research! The concepts covered in lecture felt like a deep dive of what we learned in neuro 80 and are very relevant and important to learn. It may be tough to understand lecture at times, since it tends to be fast paced. But don’t worry – lots of help is available and the teaching staff want to help you succeed!
Take this class over other foundationals! It builds on Neuro 80 pretty well and Ryan and Katie are very nice course instructors who are always willing to help. In addition, the lab component was pretty fun!
This class is awesome! Lab is so cool (sometimes disappointing if things don't go as planned but when they do it's so exciting!).
The best neuroscience class I've ever taken! I could not be happier that I chose this course. lab is fantastic, lectures are clear and accessible and thorough, course benefits from both breadth and depth. assignments are all very reasonable and help you solidify your understanding.
If you have taken Neuro 80 and would like to dive deeper into some of the things discussed in that course, especially things related to neurotransmitters, receptors, ion channels, and action potentials, take this course! Not to mention, half of the course is taught by professor Ryan Draft and the second half by professor Katie Quast, so you will have a stacked teaching staff!
