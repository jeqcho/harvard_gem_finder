Comments
This was my dream class! I have studied issues in K–12 American education before, and I was eager to study it through a philosophical lens, thinking about what justice looks like for disadvantaged communities, and this course met all my expectations. I learned something new and always had something to reflect on after every discussion. Every student in the class built on each other's ideas, and Gina always helped keep our discussions engaging and on–topic. The critical papers also helped guide my own thinking, and led to my reflecting heavily on my own positions, and they helped me grasp a better understanding on where I stand on the topics, and what I can do to counteract educational issues affecting my community and communities like mine.
This class was fantastic. The workload isn't overbearing for an advanced philosophy class and the readings are digestible and interesting to engage with. Seminar discussions themselves were great as well, as Professor Schouten does a fantastic job facilitating and making sure everyone is heard in the conversation
I adored this class. If you have any interest in education, I'd definitely suggest this course! I learned a lot and had a great time.
Professor Schouten is excellent at facilitating discussion and assigns readings that are interesting, accessible, and illuminate the precise questions of justice that you would expect coming into a course entitled "Educational Justice" and then some. She clearly puts a tremendous amount of thought into every aspect of this class, and I imagine that the results speak for themselves. I recommend this course very highly, whether you are a philosophy concentrator or have never taken a philosophy course before. This was my first "real" philosophy course and I now plan on concentrating in the field, in no small part because of how much of a positive experience I had in class/with Professor Schouten.
I absolutely loved this class, by far one of the best I have taken at Harvard. Professor Schouten is an amazing teacher and facilitates an effective and fruitful section. She is incredibly attentive and accessible for help. The assignments are a bit difficult, but you'll definitely be able to see yourself make progress throughout the semester, and there are many opportunities to improve your grade. Those with a philosophy background will probably be a bit better prepared, but really anyone from and background could be successful in this course as long as they put in the effort. The readings and podcasts were quite interesting (although a bit difficult to follow at first). The class group project was quite involved, but so much fun, and I greatly enjoyed by time learning about and working with my group members. I thought I knew a lot about education, but this class introduced me to a wide array of issues and really challenged by understanding and perspectives. If you are at all interested in education, definitely lottery for this class!
