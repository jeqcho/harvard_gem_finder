This course is a relatively low–stress way to gain a broad I introductory understanding of climate change. I think it would be particularly useful for students with minimal Python experience who want to improve their skills.
Eli is a fantastic and kind teacher, and always looking to chat with students and answer questions.

That being said, the psets are a COMPLETE waste of time, and you won't come out of the class with a much better understanding of global warming science. If you don't care about learning the material, and want to breeze through some psets, then this course is for you.
If you have experience with python and are vaguely interested in environmental matters – take this course!! The content is super interesting and the lectures are too. Don't be afraid of the 2hr45min commitment, its only once a week! Psets are sometimes complicated but just go to homework help hours, the TFs are really helpful but in general the Psets are very doable on your own. Eli is a great lecturer and makes everyone feel welcome to ask any questions.
It is a decent course within EPS and good for the ESPP Secondary. If you have Python experience the class is manageable, but will require going to office hours. The TFs are very helpful and often you could complete the whole weekly coding notebook in 1 hour in office hours. You also can complete more than 50% of notebook in class, reducing homework. The 3 hour class is a little long, but the coding and breaks make it manageable. I'd take the class and recommend it, but don't expect it to be a complete gem. But, if you like coding and don't mind a longer class and can go to office hours or spend some time attempting to read the Professor's mind, this is a good class for you.
The course was very informative and did transform the way I view climate sciences! The teaching staff if great. The math requirements were honestly not that important. On total, there was maybe 4–5 questions over the course of the semester that having a background in higher math is needed, but not for anything else. The python can be learned fairly easily even without a background. Starting the psets in class is very helpful for this.
Take it! But be ready to put in a lot of work to really understand the coding portion
I highly recommend this course. Eli is a great teacher, and it really does give you a good introduction to key issues in climate science. As someone with minimal scientific background, the course was challenging, but in a productive and exciting way. The course staff go above and beyond to make sure everybody is comfortable, and the textbook is an excellent resource.
This class is pretty manageable once you get a weekly flow down. The three hours is sometimes long but you can usually get a decent amount of the pset done, especially if you work with other people and ask the coaches for help. I would definitely recommend to fill a requirement and is semi interesting as an elective, but does feel a bit repetitive because the structure is the same every week.
This class counts as a breadth class for the AM concentration. If you have a heavy workload, I highly recommend this class. There are no exams and the assignments are relatively straightforward.
