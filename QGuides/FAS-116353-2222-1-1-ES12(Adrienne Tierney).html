What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class might be stressful but at the end you will see the change.
Take this class! You will learn a lot about the writing process and about yourself as a writer. The course does a wonderful job with breaking down the steps and supporting you throughout the pre–draft, draft, and revision stages. I've been implementing the writing strategies I've learned in this course into my other courses, and I feel more confident in my writing approach now.
Take this class if you know you'll benefit from more hands–on support.
Expos Studio 20 is a course I would recommend taking if you don't quite feel that Expos 10 covered areas of writing that you still need to work on. Also, the topic is quite an effective topic (topic is successful life) in that it is interesting for most as it is what many of us hope to have a successful life in some shape or form. The class also teaches how to read articles more effectively and how to go about writing a research paper. I also found the class helped with navigating Harvard's library.
Before you decide on an expos course make sure you find a topic that is interesting to you. Just because you begin in Expos Studio 10 does not mean you have to enroll in Expos Studio 20. This course is very very good if you want to learn about academic and occupational success.
It is very important to take the rough drafts seriously. They can become the building blocks of your final product.
