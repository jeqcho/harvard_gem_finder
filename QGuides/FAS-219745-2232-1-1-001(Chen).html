This course did a good review of convex optimization and prepared us to dive more into the subject. A lot of the content is theoretical tho, so if you are not that into math and just want to learn about ML algorithms, this course might not be for you since some of the content does not have direct applications in ML. However, knowing convex optimization is quite useful in many fields such as engineering, CS, econ, and this course definitely teaches you new concepts you probably didn't learn in other classes. If you want a deeper understanding of ML (or any other area that uses optimization), this course is for you.

However, I must note that the content sometimes felt unmotivated and dry. This did not affect my experience with the course, but I hope they improve on that. The psets were sometimes challenging, but not in a bad way. I feel like I gained a much deeper understanding and appreciation of the material as I did the psets (psets definitely not tedious like 124). Overall, I recommend this class!
CS 128 is kind of an odd course, but overall it was really great and I would definitely recommend it if you're interested in ML or applied math. On the pro side, the workload is quite reasonable, with only 5 homeworks over the course of the semester and 2 midterm exams, the assignments are at a reasonable difficulty, the content can be fundamental depending on your interests, and Yiling is great. On the con side, my main issue was that as a new course, there would sometimes be typos on the homeworks which would make them time consuming to start early (though I'm assuming this will get polished by the next time its offered). I also found the course too slow for my liking and would have preferred if it covered more topics with more breadth.

The course is mostly theoretical (we don't end up doing that much coding), and it's in an odd spot where it sometimes tries to be more theoretical but can't since it doesn't assume the necessary math background, which I found slightly disappointing. That said, I thought it's still a pretty great course.
This is a new course, so things were being made while we were taking it, but I think it's under constant improvement. Convex optimization is a good thing to learn about formally
Definitely take this course! Prof. Chen and Ali are amazing! This is a manageable course where you will learn a lot of super useful things from.
If you are a junior who have already taken many CS classes, such as machine learning and stat 111, then this is the course for you. It teaches the math behind machine learning and is very helpful for AI research. But be prepared to do many things on your own, Ali was a great TF but most psets were from outside the scope of the course so be prepared to get confused. Most people in the class are also grad students or seniors and juniors so this is not the class to take as a sophomore.
This is a great and chill CS theory class! Take if you are interested in optimization, especially if you like math (this used to be an AM class).
This was the first running of this class as CS 128, and it definitely had a lot of wrinkles. But I believe that future editions of it will run much more smoothly.

Yiling is an excellent lecturer and teaches quite well. This class has two midterms but no final exam, which is nice during finals period. The main issue with this class is that each pset had multiple typos, and in addition, there were often things which were very difficult to prove which we were sporadically told to handwave, which made expectations quite unclear. Overall, the psets need to be written much more clearly, and I hope that in future years they are. Overall, I think this is a great third theory course to take, and while this year had some issues, I think Yiling is great and in the future things will run much better!
This course is quite advanced for an undergraduate course — much more than I had anticipated. For one, the biggest surprise that I had with this course was the large amount of mathematical background necessary for the course. It is not REQUIRED, but I felt that I was at an extreme disadvantage by not having an advanced math background compared to others in the course. Even though I had taken linear algebra in the past, not having taken it recently was quite a disadvantage.

In general, I would note that the amount of content covered in this course is much more than most classes I have ever taken at Harvard, even within the CS department. It was overwhelming at times to keep up with the material; every second of the lecture was important, and remembering all the content was often overwhelming and difficult.

Problem sets are manageable, but the content is difficult. With the help of office hours, they are definitely doable. I would note that the exams are very difficult and, in my honest opinion, not a great test of understanding. That being said, putting in all your effort is very fruitful. Although I did not perform very well in exams, the teaching staff acknowledged extra effort put in throughout the course (such as in assignments), and I felt that my final grade was a good reflection of the amount of energy and work I put into the course.
Please consider taking this class. This is most definitely not an easy class, but I don't think it should be. The reason why it was such a nice class in my opinion is that it was a great way to combine Math, CS, and Statistics throughout the material, which is mainly the focus of my degree. The support that we received from Prof. Chen and Ali was out of this world really, for the psets, exams and understanding the material overall, which is the most difficult part of this course. If you want to do anything related to ML, AI, intersection between CS & Econ, Data Science, then this course is for you and you should take it for sure (ESPECIALLY when it's taught by Prof. Chen who is an amazing lecturer). The concepts are beautiful if you deeply think about them and although it might seem scary in the first few weeks given the amount of math you need to know, please don't give up (I was about to, but staying in the class, asking for help and really trying to understand the material was probably the best decision this semester). The class was sometimes disorganised and a bit chaotic (especially with the pset content), but this was the first offering and they are committed to really improving it in the future years. Take it, I promiss you won't regret it.
This has the potential to become a great course but there were some clear hiccups this year since it was the first iteration of the class. I think once the psets are calibrated and typos/mistakes are fixed, what will remain is a great instructor who's extremely accessible, kind, and delivers clear lectures, and an interesting subject matter. Great option if you're looking for an optimization course.
