To be completely honest, the first thing I did after my first section in this class was fill out a petition to switch to another Expos class. My petition was denied (clearly), and while my Expos experience has been incredibly difficult, I absolutely don't regret having continued with this class. This class is accessible to anyone, regardless of their past experience in science or writing, and Emilie is very supportive of students' diverse perspectives. She is also a very lenient and flexible teacher when it comes to deadlines and providing feedback, but be sure to reinforce strict boundaries and deadlines for yourself if you end up taking this course; write as much as possible for your "drafts" and take every opportunity to have your work reviewed in class. You'll also have to be diligent about completing readings in a timely manner—some of them can be a bit dense—but in general this was a very manageable class (as Expos sections go)!
Frontiers of Biomedicine is a genuinely enjoyable expos class! You hear a lot about how hit or miss expos can be, but this section is definitely a hit. Emilie successfully facilitates engaging, in–depth discussions about the material. Because the material is so interesting, the essays are pretty enjoyable to write. We cover so much analysis in class that finding a topic was never particularly difficult. I found the grading on the assignments pretty kind, but full of useful feedback. All in all, this expos allows you to learn about incredibly pertinent and fascinating topics in modern biomedicine as well as learn how to improve your writing. Definitely do recommend!
Although this class is advertised as a STEM class, it involves a lot of dense reading in a short period of time. It doesn't really dive into research papers as much as you'd expect (although there are usually a couple in every unit), but you will have to learn to either block out a lot of time to read ~50 pages during the days that you don't meet or learn how to skim really well. Most of class time is spent on discussing the articles that you read, but they were really helpful in learning more about each article and finding viewpoints that I wouldn't have found without the in–class discussions. Overall though, it is a great class to take and definitely would recommend it, even if you're not a STEM person!
This course incited tremendous growth for me. It is my favorite class at Harvard thus far and when I speak to my classmates they remark to me the same thing, "People think it's so weird that I love my expos!" No doubt, if you want a fun and engaging expos experience, you have to take it. This class had, in my section, lots of people from LS50 and Math 55 and I think that really improves the course. They put a lot into the class and come to writing with a new perspective. Additionally, getting to meet people in those communities is extremely rewarding. I think everyone in this class really is fun to meet and engage with. Preceptor Raymer is a great. She's super accommodating and makes the class fun and enjoyable. All in all, I really enjoyed and appreciated the course. Take it! SERIOUSLY
I LOVE THIS CLASS!! I learned so much in this Expos 20 Frontiers of Biomedicine with Emilie. She is an absolutely wonderful preceptor who is kind and attentive. The grading is fair, as long as you put in the work and ask Emilie for help you will get a good grade. The classmates in this Expos are also very loving, brave, deep thinkers who make this class a truly special learning experience.

I have grown a lot both as a writer and as a human being. I learned more about the nuances of writing an argumentative essay, both in a descriptive and a prescriptive style. I think I have become more empathetic as a person and the diversity of the class really helped me appreciate thinking in new perspectives. The class consists of a few LS50 kids, a few Math 55ers, and other students in different disciplines, but most importantly in class discussions are always respectful while being challenging. I want to say thank you to everyone who made this class so unforgettable.

Overall this class is an absolutely joyful ride and I would highly recommend it to anyone who stumbles upon this humble review of mine.
More than writing effectively, the course taught me to think clearly and deeply about what I read, say, and convey! I learnt to see beyond the ideas explicitly stated in papers, pushing myself to look for more. Further, I learn to come up with interesting ideas combining all the different papers I read, trying to come up with something new already not addressed before. For me, this was not just another writing class, it was a full experience of discovering the world through discussions with peers, readings of novels and scientific papers, and thinking through writing! For sure take it, Professor Raymer makes it so worthwhile!

Incredible course if you're at all interested in bioethics! Readings are curated expertly to provide a diversity of opinion on each issue, and everything is genuinely interesting to consider and discuss. Emilie is a fantastic facilitator and incredibly kind and understanding!
Emilie is the sweetest, most understanding, and supportive instructor!! Talking to some of my friends in other expos's I think this one had a stronger focus on the actual topic and content of the course (ethics of genetic engineering, hospice/anti–aging, last one's your choice) rather than just how to write, if that's something that you're looking for. The content itself was also super cool and very thought–provoking, overall a great class!

Assignments are challenging, but insightful, allowing for great improvement as a writer and a thinker.
