What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is similar to 21a in structure but the content is definitely harder. The psets took longer to do (1.5–3 hours), but there were still 3/week. Because there is so much arithmetic/simple algebra, it is really easy to make small calculation mistakes that mess up your work, so I would recommend doing psets with a partner to minimize mistakes made and maximize points. Most of what you learn makes sense conceptually (a lot of it is algebra you could teach a smart middle schooler), but it will be applied in ways that make it a lot more difficult. The tests are harder than 21a tests, but they are easier than the practice exams, so if you prepare with those you will walk in much more confident. Definitely attend the quiz sessions the CAs host each week. not only can you get pset drops out of them, but they prepare you for the true/false questions on every exam and teach you how to do different types of problems that also might show up on the exam.
I probably would not take this as an elective math course, but if you need to take it for your concentration, just do it, you will make it through this class and learn a lot throughout the way.
Here's the deal: you're probably taking this class because you have to, so just take it. Get it out of the way. The material really isn't bad at all, its definitely not, say, a Stat110 (aka, super conceptually difficult). The issue with the class is in the time commitment, because (as I'm sure you've heard) 3 p sets a week just kills you. But you're allowed to collaborate on them so take advantage of that (even with this, budget like 2–3hrs per p set). Long story short this is a grinder class; don't take it when your schedule is super busy because it will just really annoy you (because it is not hard enough to be a priority, it will be like a fly always buzzing in your ear).

TLDR take it because you have to, its not hard, but it is a lot of grind work, so plan schedule accordingly.
Psets weren't as bad as people made them out to be – yes, there are a few that will take more than three hours to complete, but a lot of them took under 2 hours for me, and I'm likely on the lower end of the spectrum of aptitude for math students. Exams, however, were way harder than I expected. Much harder than they were in 21a. Do not underestimate the exams in this class, they are unforgiving, graded harshly, and not similar to the way they were in 21a.
You should only take it if you need to for a concentration or secondary, be sure that you're interested in doing what you need it for; This class is only a means to an end, not for the things itself (they teach 0 applications)
Math21b is a very difficult course. Some students might like this class more than Math21a but Math21a has a more solid foundation and it is easy to figure stuff out on your own, you cannot do the same for 21b. This course is taught in sections creating an uneven playing field among the students enrolled in the course. Your TF can make or break your experience in this class, and by a chain reaction, the grade you get in the class. Students in one section could feel more confident on the content compared to students in another section. I often found myself learning stuff that another TF taught their students days before a midterm or learning about a "shortcut" for a problem from a friend in another section. Overall, if you want to do well in this class you better hope to get a good TF or spend countless hours studying for the midterms for a chance at a good grade in the class. I highly recommend attending MQC the same day you are taught a lesson as less students attend and you are able to receive more personal help.
