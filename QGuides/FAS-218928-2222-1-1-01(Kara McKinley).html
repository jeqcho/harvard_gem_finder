TAKE THIS CLASS! It's a little more effort than a lot of other upper–level SCRB classes out there, but the effort is 100% worth it for some of the most interesting topics in regeneration and development that you will ever think about at Harvard.
I would definitely recommend it. Kara was super approachable and a great human being to get to know. The class was incredibly interesting and doesn't just touch on biology topics, but you also talk about the ethics of the things going on in the field which is not something that many classes at Harvard do from my experience.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Although there is a lot of content, the topics are super interesting and there are also no exams so the pressure to learn for grades is not there. Instead you can actually learn for the sake of learning and this made the class really enjoyable! Kara is such a good lecturer she gets through like 80 slides within the class time and makes sure you understand all of it. you won't regret it!!
This class will afford you a low–stakes opportunity to learn a lot of material without having to be stressed about exams. This was the first year the class was offered, but the lectures, problem sets and assignments were really well designed, and I actually got a lot out of all of them. I think the ethical discussions were particularly enriching because the whole class got to learn about things that aren't commonly mentioned in mainstream media, and the resulting conversations were interesting and got everyone in the class to participate.
Buy in and enjoy every lecture. You won’t always be tested on things that you learn but that’s the beauty of it. You just get to sit there and learn and it’s so interesting.
SCRB135 is one of the most transformative experiences I have had at Harvard. There are no other opportunities to learn about reproductive biology in a STEM context at Harvard. Kara is a very engaging lecturer and an extremely dedicated teacher – she made this mammoth task of learning an entirely unfamiliar branch of biology into a fun, and interesting semester – while providing ample opportunity for discussion on the class content and her academic/professional experiences (she is a great mentor/advocate for women in STEM academia!).

There is a LOT of content in this class, but there are no exams so you can always reference notes as you consolidate materials. The lift is pretty small week–to–week and the final project builds itself over the final weeks of the course. There are no waves of work, it's a very stable load throughout the semester (less than 5 hours always and rarely more than 2). If you can take the class, definitely do!
Take this class!! Dr. McKinley is so passionate and knowledgeable about the content, and connects the science to everyday life. Because it is a new class, the teaching team is super receptive to feedback and the course is somewhat flexible to what students want. There are only a few assignments that are pretty easy (meant to be useful and interesting rather than a test), so you can get away with doing very little, but you certainly get out what you put in.
While the content was not easy, I felt that this class was very manageable. Kara and Anusha did an incredible job of explaining things and helping outside of class if needed. I absolutely loved the format of the class and would highly recommend to anyone interested in SCRB. Kara and Anusha really made this class what it was and I highly recommend this class. As a graduating Senior, this class is definitely on my list of top 3 favourite classes at Harvard!
This course is awesome. Kara cares so much about the students taking the class and she really wants to make sure that everyone is understanding what is going on. This course focuses not only on reproductive biology, but it also gives you tools to become better at designing experiments and hypotheses. Overall, even if reproductive biology isn't something you are super interested in, you come away having learned so much. That all being said, the content is super cool. You learn so much that isn't taught in other classes at Harvard. This class does a great job at tying together both the scientific side of science as well as the ethical side of science. Overall, this was an amazing class, and I strongly recommend taking it.
