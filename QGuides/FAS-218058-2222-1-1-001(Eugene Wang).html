What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you want to learn how to learn and be involved in a highly enthusiastic and passionate class community, this is a wonderful course to take! All work is self/peer–directed, and students are encouraged to truly think outside the box in creative ways you rarely find at Harvard. That said, this is a somewhat time–intensive course given the amount of reading/hypothesizing needed to produce new theories and conclusions.
This class is engaging and interested but tons of work for an art history class!
I did not expect to learn this much, to acquire this much knowledge about early Chinese art but I am so glad I did!!!
Get excited but also be prepared to work!
The class is taught in a really great and unique way––I felt like I was really doing investigative research and contributing to the field, rather than purely learning/regurgitating content. Taught us how to research.
It is really student–driven, so in that sense, it gives you the power to shape your own learning experience, which is great. But that also means that you have more responsibility, so if you fall behind, it's hard to catch up. It also might get chaotic and uncertain throughout the semester, but it ended up tying together really nicely.
