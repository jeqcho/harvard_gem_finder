This class, as a requirement for a lot of concentrations, is what it should be. It's not necessarily riveting and the workshops kind of end up being a waste of time, but if you put in some effort, you'll learn the content well enough to succeed in the course. 21a is not full of thinking math, you can plug and chug your way to an A, but you need to make sure that your fundamentals are down pat. Since the course moves relatively quickly, if one topic passes by without learning it, it makes the rest of the course much more difficult. Do all the PSets and take a look at the solutions for the problems in class every day, they'll prove helpful not just for the PSets, but will be vital for understanding!
Please take this class!!! I used to dislike math, but then I took math1b and that turned my life around when it comes to math. So, I ended up taking math21a as well. If you enjoyed math1b, you will enjoy math21a. 3 psets a week and a lot of attention from CAs and TFs. The material is difficult but it all makes sense in the end. You just need to trust the process. Practice midterms are a lot harder than the actual exams, so if you go through most of the content, you will be set!
The quality of your TF will determine how much fun you have here. Sung Gi is the absolute GOAT and I highly recommend him as a TF if you can make his section time. The exams can be challenging, but they're fair. Your experience in this course will be heavily dependent on the TF you have––I've heard some TFs are bad enough that teaching yourself the material was more beneficial than going to section. So, keep that in mind in the first few weeks.
And for those of you wondering: yes, this class really is better than Math 1B in almost every conceivable way. It can be harder depending on what type of person you are (I personally found the material easier, but that may just be me), but it is a better class overall. This class is also very applicable to most STEM fields. hell, even if you aren't a STEM concentrator, this class is still great.
Definitely go to office hours and MQC! The course material will be strange in the beginning but it gets better as more computations are introduced. Also start studying for midterms and finals very earlier as there is a lot of material covered. Finding a good Pset group will be the best thing to help with the class.
INCREDIBLE INCREDIBLE INCREDIBLE! Math 21a was the highlight of my semester. (This is coming from someone who absolutely dreaded her Math 22a class in the fall). Math 21a has SOOOOO much support – left and right, front and back, at any corner you go. The section style of this class is what makes learning so easy. I learn better with a smaller class size, more opportunity for collaboration, and more face–to–face time with my instructor. There will be 3 Course Assistants in your section all the time, who are eager to help you out. The CAs are also VERY smart.

You only need a decent understanding of high school calculus to do well in this course. They really build you from the ground up and are with you every step of the way. Just don't be afraid to ask questions.

Math 21a is so well–organized, and I truly made the best decision switching from the 22 series to the 21 series.
