I think the biggest strength of the course was definitely the chance to see shows. Getting to see such a vast array of different works and then discussing each show in such detail really helped me gain a better sense of what to look for in every show I watch. The class itself was a bit disorganized, and I wish there could've been some more concrete take–aways from each class. However, the atmosphere was always extremely encouraging, and the professors weren't afraid to introduce controversial topics for discussions among students, which helped me learn a lot!
great class for the most part! lots of shows seen for free, LROD is an actual blessing, Debra is very knowledgeable
Make friends with everyone in the class to maximize your fun. New York is a great time to do this!
Also be sure to actually do the readings. Section gets very in–depth on them! And make sure to put effort and thought into your blog posts ahead of time. There is no such thing as a throwaway assignment in this class. Best of luck!
Eh! The course is fine. It is not the most enjoyable experience but it was not as painful as others had told me.
Debra's teaching style is... interesting, as in there were awkward pauses after a question was asked because we tried to figure out what exactly she was asking. However, the people you meet in sophomore tutorial will be your peers for the rest of your time in TDM and I do feel like the fact that we all saw the same theater and traveled together helped to strengthen that bond. The performances that I saw and enjoyed, I will talk about for the rest of my life—same goes for the one's I saw and absolutely hated. You will come out far more fluent in the world of theater criticism.
