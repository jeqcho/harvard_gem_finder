What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a fantastic survey class to information studies –– Professor Csiszar really knows what he's talking about, the readings were interesting, and our class discussions were never boring. Some of the units included: journalism, the state and surveillance, information theory, hidden labor in the technology sector, platforms, algorithms, disinformation, and genomics. Each week brought something new, so even if you're not sure that all the topics interest you, the next week will be totally different (and keep an open mind! You may like something totally unexpected). The class is also fairly low time commitment –– just weekly discussion posts and three short (3–6 page) papers. Highly recommend!
This class is awesome. Professor Csiszar is a great teacher
This is an amazing class, and Prof. Csiszar is a one–of–a–kind teacher. The material is wildly relevant and the course discussions are super engaging. If you like small group discussion, this is the class for you – so much room to get passionate about an exciting topic.
This is an amazing class! Take it.
This class is great so take it if you can! Professor Csiszar is really passionate about what he teaches and puts together super interesting discussions!
This is a small discussion class with about 16 students; as such, your time in the class will depend very heavily on *who* is choosing to take the class at the same time. I really recommend that you take the class with a friend so that at least you can ensure that someone is on the same page as you. In any case, if you're looking for a gem, this is it, but it can actually be a super rigorous class if you want that to be your goal.

Professor Csizar is actually one of the kindest, sweetest, most wonderful professors you could ever imagine. He creates excellent materials for class and puts a lot of thought and care into the curriculum. Unfortunately, I think he is a bit too nice sometimes to moderate the discussion in a way that keeps it most productive / interesting for everyone in the class (the class is sometimes a bit prone to hijacking by "section kids.")

The assignments are great 600, 1000, and 1500 word papers that are graded more so for your thoughts on the topic than any type of specific topical knowledge. In that way, you can pursue something really interesting even if the class discussions are a bit subpar. I've heard that these vary from year to year, so just get a vibe check on your first session!
