What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class was truly the "what is money and why does it matter" class that Harvard was missing. Professor Krampf was great, but any instructor equally passionate about the topic and equally willing to break down tough finance concepts for students would make this course a big win. I honestly can't imagine having gone through college without taking a course like this: it was truly a great academic experience. The work load was manageable and the style of the final project made it much more fun than most research papers as long as you follow the guidelines closely, really try to ask for and understand the Professor's feedback, and do everything EARLY rather than later.
If you want to take a course on global finance that covers immense amounts of history and lots of different theories, this class is for you! The course provides a fantastic overview of international macro and explains many of the major historical events that have occurred in the global economy. Highly recommend!
I would say to take this class if you want to go super deep into finance and political economics. This class is a bit more rigorous and theory–heavy than the intro courses on the topic offered elsewhere. You should have some sort of background in IR, and political economics would be helpful.
