What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is hard and substantial amount of practice is required. You need to spend more time to get a solid understanding about different statistical theories and applications.
Useful/foundational statistical inference course. As a core course, it's and nice probability practice (and obviously necessary for the quals).
Keep up with the material and make sure you understand each slide, try completing assignments on your own before reaching out for solutions and take advantage of office hours!
This class will probably be more difficult than BIOSTAT 230. Unless you already have ample statistics background, be prepared for long, challenging homework problems and incredibly tough exams that are impossible to finish. I would recommend reading the relevant chapters of Casella and Berger and spending 1–2 hours BEFORE each class reading the slides and taking notes to actually understand what will be presented during the lecture. Also, attend as many office hours as possible, and study as if for an exam throughout the semester – not just in the week leading up to the midterm/final.

Needless to say, I wouldn't recommend taking this course if not required. On the plus side, by the end you will have thoroughly mastered the material needed for the Biostatistics PhD qualifying exam!
