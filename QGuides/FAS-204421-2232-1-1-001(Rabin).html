Comments
This course is very disorganized and you never really know what to expect. The professor will say one thing and do another, and the TF makes little effort to stay on track. That said, if you go to office hours to do the problem sets, the course assignments aren't terrible. But the structure and consistent lack of direction or expectations make it a little frustrating. This is a shame because, on paper, the course seems awesome, as it is taught by a very famous professor in a small setting while covering interesting subject matter.
This course is interesting, but be prepared to spend time at office hours for all of the psets. Work with the other students in the class––best way to get through it! Rabin has done a lot of extremely interesting studies and is an interesting person, but the classes can get long at times. Make sure to grab a coffee and figure out ways to stay focused throughout class.
The lecture and discussion content in class is interesting, and so is the pset content, the problem is that they’re two very different things. The course generally is not super well organized, and the psets (primarily the first 2) were pretty unrelated to the material covered in class and it didn’t feel like there was adequate preparation. The pset questions also felt convoluted and difficult to interpret at times. In the weeks between psets (which can vary unpredictably) the course is very chill, but when the psets are assigned the workload spikes drastically. Overall the professor is very nice and knows a lot, and the material is interesting, I just think it could be executed better. If you have a strong statistics background you’ll be fine, otherwise I would have some reservations if the course is structured the same way it was this semester.
Professor Rabin is very knowledgeable in the topic but does not teach the material well. Sitting in a very small classroom for 2 hours and listening to a lecture the entire time is not conducive to learning. This course would have so much potential had it been taught as more of a seminar–style discussion–based course.
