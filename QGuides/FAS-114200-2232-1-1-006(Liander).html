What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a wonderful introduction to the four countries that we examined throughout the semester. The instructors have taken the time to ensure that the course is accessible even for those that do not have any previous experience with the material. It is also a great opportunity to work on and learn the Spanish language and get comfortable with different colloquial expressions.
This class is very manageable and the teachers really care about the subject and helping you learn it to the fullest.
The workload of this course is very manageable. Most weeks you either read something or look at art/listen to a song/watch a movie, but you don't have homework to turn in. There are 4 written assignments and 4 exams. The exams felt easier than Spanish 20 and the written assignments were fairly straightforward. The readings were more advanced than those in Spanish 20 but doable if you spent some time on them. If you're a grad student considering taking this language course, I'd say it's very doable next to your research!
I would highly encourage students to take this class as a good intermediate/advanced class. The class moves at a fast pace however, you will be prepared for that pace if you have the necessary knowledge about Spanish grammar and have taken previous Spanish classes. I also think your experience in the class is contingent on the section instructor you have and also the people within the class.
I would tell future students that this class is very good for a low–stress, but informative course that helps fulfill the language requirement.
