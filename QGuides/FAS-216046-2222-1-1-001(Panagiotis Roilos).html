What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class takes a very rigorous approach to a number of texts that examine notions of reality and "hyperreality." It is in a seminar format, so student participation and discussion is imperative.
This is absolutely a class you should take. Not only did the readings and discussions introduce an entirely new perspective through which I view the world, but it is incredibly manageable in terms of coursework and commitment. Just by showing up and participating you are completing a large part of the course. By the time you write the final paper, there is more than enough content to discuss. I highly highly recommend!
Take this course if you have taken similar courses in the past on this topic or are prepared to do background research on your own; not a lot of learning but, instead, discussion with the assumption that you already have knowledge on these concepts/theorists/topics
Professor Roilos is a gem of an instructor who would make any class fantastic!
Whether you study philosophy or literature, this course will challenge you. The professor is highly knowledgeable, flexible in his thinking, and quick to make connections between assigned readings and other traditions and phenomena that may arise in discussion. While the week–to–week nature of the course is loose in its conversational approach to discussion, the course's aim never strays from the main point at hand. If you are curious to learn about hyperreality and the replacement of the real with the simulacrum, this course is for you.
This class will give you what you put in. It's very free form, but it has a great set of materials and Professor Roilos is amazing.
