What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The course isn't always clearly structured and classes can sometimes drag on, but Prof. Abramson is a pretty amazing resource. So many stories about journalism and her comments on your drafts are invaluable. The course gave me the opportunity to produce my favorite writing of the year and develop my skills at weaving narrative and factual reporting. I felt like I was also able to develop my personal journalistic voice.
If you like writing or are even semi interested in journalism– take it! Work is so manageable, especially with only one class meeting per week, and you can tailor the timing of projects. Jill is so flexible and helpful, and will help you pursue any topic you might be interested in!
This is a cool course if you want to be exposed to journalism overall but don't expect direct instruction on "how to be a journalist." What you get out of this course is really what you put in. it is very unstructured at time but there is also flexibility which is nice
I loved it!
This course will provide you with great hands–on experience and a knowledge about the history of journalism as an ever–changing field.
