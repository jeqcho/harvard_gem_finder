What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a fun course that proves several fundamental results of analytic number theory that make the subject so appealing to the general mathematical audience (e.g. the prime number theorem, Dirichlet's theorem on primes in arithmetic progressions, etc.). I would recommend taking this course both if you are a student interested in (analytic) number theory and if just want to explore analytic number theory as a student of mathematics primarily interested in some other subfield.
Be sure you want to take this course. I took it because it fit my schedule and seemed cool, and I was unprepared for how intense it was going to be. I greatly enjoyed what I could understand, but I struggled hard, and if you aren't very sure you're already really good at complex analysis then you will too. But what do you expect? It's a graduate level course in analytic number theory. It's supposed to be hard. If it were easy, everyone would do it.
Fabian's 229 was amazing. He didn't follow any text, but despite that, the class was really well designed!! The material flowed really intuitively, he structures his lectures in subparts and numbers everything, so your notes are always really organized and easy to look back at. He's really enthusiastic, and the topic is really interesting. This is one of the few analysis classes at Harvard, but the quality doesn't suffer at all from the deficit.
Fabian is an excellent professor and material is quite interesting (though sometimes bashy, which should be fine), with many fun results
Take only if you like both analysis and number theory
