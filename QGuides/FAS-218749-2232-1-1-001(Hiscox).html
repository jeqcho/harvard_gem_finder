This course teaches you deeply about how to conduct a randomized controlled trial, and the various considerations and concerns that come with it. I appreciate the fact that the course is very much grounded in reality, and considers the real–world implications of implementing such trials, including the problems that come with working with a community partner! It is not just the theoretical idea of a perfect treatment/control group, which is what you might be exposed to in another econ class on causality. I recommend that you take this course if you want to learn more about how causal research is conducted in the wild. Plus, Prof Hiscox is at the forefront of the field when it comes to the implementation of RCTs, and he has a cool accent.
