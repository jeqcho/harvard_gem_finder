This is a great class. A great way to understand the theory behind FEA and learn how to use it in practical situations. The class is a lot of work and it takes some time getting used to the problem set format, but once you do catch stride, it is a very interesting and enjoyable experience.
This course broke me. It taught me a lot, like a huge amount. But its simply too much work. Each pset has 3 parts which are effectively a whole Pset of another class's worth of work. No matter when I started the Pset I always felt behind and struggled to finish by the deadline. I would recommend if you dont mind putting in the hours and grinding, but I took two ES classes and an AM class alongside this class (with some other personal health issues I had to deal with that were unique to this semester) and I've actually never been more stressed in my life. Katia is GREAT, and Im really glad I've developed a relationship with her. But I still think this course, especially on the Matlab and Abaqus side, could use a change to limit the amount of work. I think you can teach the same amount they did with less struggle by being more clear with directions and fundamentals of these programs. If I were only dealing with this class, I would love to delve into the errors and learn all about what is going on, but people only have limited time in a day and cannot do that. Like I said, I've learned a ton and feel like an Abaqus weapon now, but this could've been much less of a struggle than it was. Spending between 10 (rarely) and 25 hours (more often than not) on a pset is defeating and crippling. I had to put so many of the other things going on in my life on hold to sit there and debug a tiny little issue that could've been addressed in one sentence in the directions and I'm hopeful the teaching staff will make this more smooth in the future.
This is literally the BEST course I have ever taken at Harvard. Not only was the class taught by a bada** female engineer (very rare to have that in academia), the teaching staff were the most supportive. As a woman in engineering, the fact that the teaching staff was majority women had such a substantial effect on me during the semester. I felt seen and respected by all teaching staff, and I truly learned so much. This class really challenged me, but I am so grateful for it. This class is a lot of work, but it is sooo worth it! My advice would be to attend as many office hours as you can and start on the assignments early! The problem sets take many hours each week, so it's necessary to plan your work schedule around office hours so you can ask substantial questions rather than surface level "how do I start?" questions. I seriously cannot say enough positive things about this class. Katia really is such a great person and teacher, and she really wants all of her students to succeed.
This class is good but it's just hard. Some weeks you contemplate why you took this class. But it is a good class and helped me understand everything in 120 a little better as well
Super useful! Lectures are extremely helpful, start the homework early, and try to challenge yourself with the abaqus.
If you're looking at the average time commitment for this class and thinking "that's not going to me me" yes it is. The only way I can explain this class is unrelentless. Not only is this class an incredible time commitment, but it is also incredibly difficult. You can spend twenty hours straight on a PSET, go to every set of office hours, and still not get a perfect score. If you don't feel confident in your MATLAB skills proceed with caution– it's doable, you'll just struggle quite a bit more than your peers in the beginning. You will learn a ton from this class, but it will be an emotional journey of lots of failed Abaqus simulations and struggling on PSETS. Finally, I cannot emphasize enough that you should not take this class at the same time as ES227.
They are like tough coaches. Hard on you at times but its for your own good, you come out a boss.
Hardest class I've taken and probably hardest class I will take. It will challenge you so much! Be prepared to have this class take up most of the time you dedicate to classes. That being said, it is such a great course!! Psets are hard, but really fun and you will learn a lot from them. Very relevant and useful content so definitely worth the struggle. And plus, Katia and the whole teaching staff are amazing so you will have tons of support.
Highly applicable to any student in mechanical, aerospace or biomedical engineering disciplines
This course is very challenging, but incredibly worth the time and effort it took me. It was by far my most time consuming class, but I enjoyed the grind of this class, as it taught me skills and knowledge of a software that is highly applicable to industry. I truly believe it is the best engineering class Harvard offers. Do be wary of the demand of this course. It requires working knowledge of matrices and vector calculus, and massive amount of programming, both in MATLAB and Python. It is certainly possible to succeed without such experience, but be prepared to suffer for the first half of the course.
