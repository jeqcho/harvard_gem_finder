Tl;dr I loved the class but I recommend you take CS61 instead.

Let me explain. Yes, I loved this class but I think that its mediocre reputation is warranted. First of all, the class' name is misleading. Yes, the class does cover abstraction and design but that is not what the entire class is about. Instead, the class is a proper Programming 2 class. A more in–depth look at programming as a whole which includes abstraction and design. I would say the first 1/3 of the class is dedicated almost entirely to abstraction and design but after that, you go into topics that are certainly applicable to software engineering, but they are not solely in the interest of teaching abstraction and design! Another thing that pains me (as well as most of my classmates) is the language that the class is taught in—Ocaml.

Ocaml is seldom used in industry and the professor will spend the first lecture telling you that it does not matter what language the class is taught in because it's a class on abstraction and design and NOT a class on Ocaml. In my opinion, that claim is simply not true. Like I said, the class does cover design but it is by no means the main focus of the course. In addition, to say that the class is not a class on Ocaml feels a little disingenuous when a good chunk of the exams require knowledge of very specific and niche details about Ocaml and do not focus on design. Yeah, exams for a class that's on abstraction and design and NOT Ocaml focuses on Ocaml and not as much on abstraction and design... that's a little ridiculous. In addition, the class focuses way too much on "semantics." Semantics are basically the way the computer evaluates your code and its presence is pervasive in the final part of the course. It accounts for a considerable portion of the second midterm and is the sole focus of the final project. I mention this because while semantics can be useful in laying down the foundations for understanding how to program, I think it was overemphasized in this course and so did my classmates.

Now, I have spent most of this review tearing CS51 apart and yet I claim that I loved the class. That is true, and it is because CS51 was almost exactly what I wanted—a deeper dive into programming. I only started programming last semester in CS50 and wanted to take another class dedicated solely to programming. So logically, I took CS51 and got just that, a comprehensive look at programming as a whole sprinkled in with a good chunk of abstraction and design. However, if you are looking for a class to fulfill the Programming 2 requirement, a class that will actually cover abstraction, design, and a useful language, then this is not the class for you. If those are your only motivations for taking this class then take CS61 instead.

However, if you just want a class that will build off the programming knowledge from CS50, then feel free to take this class. CS51 is hard, but it is also fun if you take it for the right reasons.
