This course changed my thinking in so many ways. I don't know if I should attribute this to the teaching or rather the readings themselves, but I can conclude the class is worth taking if you're interested in Philosophy of Mind.
Take this class if you are already interested in how the mind works. It takes some motivation to get through some of the readings.
This class is a good balance to others in the philosophy department – it's very standardized, uses a textbook, and consistent in assignments. Good class to take earlier in the concentration or senior year in addition to a thesis. Lectures can drag, so be sure to have specific questions or ideas you're interested in from the readings to discuss. The Canvas posts are very doable if you actually read the texts. And Cheryl is very nice! (though very busy so not always the most accessible)
This is an excellent course! The readings are all very interesting, and lectures thoroughly walk through the arguments so everything makes sense. The papers also give you ample opportunity to explore your own lines of thought and form interesting objections. Overall, I highly recommend this course.
Poster course for "interesting, but not engaging." Super cool subject matter, lots of fun conversations with other students after class, but I found it quite hard to stay mentally checked in during lecture.
I highly recommend this class not just if you are interested in philosophy, but even if you're interested in psychology, neuroscience, or computer science. We discuss very interesting topics in philosophy of mind like consciousness, intentionality, and the mind–body problem. It is also really nice that we only write 3 papers throughout the whole semester with no exams and our TF even gave us 2 free extensions on our papers. Professor Chen is a great lecturer because she describes concepts in the textbook very clearly and provides many examples to solidify our understanding. This is the best philosophy class I have taken so far after taking 3! I promise you will enjoy this class if you are interested in any of these topics and you enjoy writing over exams.
The class is interesting, but you have to be willing to engage critically with the material. It's hard to tell exactly how beneficial the class is without putting a lot of energy into truly grasping the content of each lecture and applying your learnings to the papers. I would highly recommend the class if you want to engage with the works of Descartes, Hilary Putnam, J.J.C. Smart, and John Searle (among many others).
I think it would be helpful if this were not your first philosophy class.
I took this course as my first philosophy class, and it was really cool! The content is interesting, but does get a bit dense toward the end. Nevertheless, I have come to think about more interesting questions about the mind, especially as a neuo concentrator. Not a ton of work, basically three 6–page essays and very quick bi–weekly exercises for readings. I recommend this class.
