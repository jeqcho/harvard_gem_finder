This is an interesting course about refugees and humanitarian crises, which is not a commonly taught topic here.
This class is incredibly interesting and lectures are engaging! However, there is a significant amount of work. The work goes quickly if you're interested in the topic and none of the assignments are too difficult, but definitely make sure you care about the content of the class––it's not a class you should take just to fill a requirement.
Courses like GHHP 70 are the reason why Harvard is the best university in the world. Dr. Kayden and Dr. VanRooyen are quite literally the best of the best in the humanitarian industry and it still baffles me that they take time out of their very busy schedules (just running emergency medicine departments at MGH & BWH!) to teach undergrads. And they always manage to still come in enthusiastic and ready to really work with students. I feel beyond grateful to have been able to learn from them, and if you can get the opportunity, regardless of whether you are premed, gov, global health policy, interested in humanitarian work, or not, you should absolutely take it.

If that is not enough to convince you, the topics in this course are so vital for every student to know/understand. Millions around the world die every year and even more are displaced from their homes in crises like the ones we discuss in this course and everyone can have a hand in helping these people. This class will rekindle your fire to do something good with what you have learned at Harvard, and as a graduating senior this was especially true.
This class is great! You get what you put in, so if you're enthusiastic and want to learn about the topics you'll definitely have a great time. Also Dr. Van Rooyen and Dr. Hayden are the best.
I loved this course!!! If you have any interest whatsoever in humanitarian work I strongly recommend taking it. You will gain so much insight into what the field is actually like and you'll have the opportunity to speak to aid workers who have really worked out in the field. It's an extremely well rounded course and it's really manageable in terms of workload.
This was my favorite class of the semester. The teachers are passionate, the content is extremly engaging, and if you put in the effort, you learn a lot. The only draw back is that the mid–term and final are group projects, which sucked. I felt like the grading wasn't representitive of what I knew in the class and felt like I could have done better on my own.
The week–by–week material is very manageable and the class starts from basic concepts, while building to cover a lot of historical background and a lot of material about how humanitarian responses work in the present day. You'll definitely learn a lot about the role of the UN and NGOs in responding to crises, so if that is something you're even remotely curious about, this would be a good class for you! While the weekly assignments are totally reasonable, the midterm and final projects were pretty stressful at times. Dr. Kayden and Dr. Van Rooyen are really nice and funny, and they're ultimately great instructors with really incredible experiences.
It is transformative and extremely enlightening. This class not only helped me grasp the severity of events going on around the world but also helped me learn skills to provide aid in affected areas as a humanitarian worker.
This class is AMAZING. The professors are so excited to teach and so knowledgeable. You get to learn through a variety of topics– case studies, studying laws/conventions, and in–person practice scenarios. I cannot suggest taking this class enough.
I found this class to be one of the most interesting courses at Harvard! You truly get out of it what you put in, and I was very excited to o the readings. The case studies are especially interesting, and the course staff is very kind. Do come in understanding though that the presentations and paper require a lot of effort, and the course heads are picky on the grading, but the course is not stressful. Also, the simulation was amazing! Be cognizant of how close it is to the exam period though
ABSOLUTELY TAKE THIS CLASS. I learned so much while also never feeling too much pressure with my assignments.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! Dr VanRooyen and Dr Kayden are some of the best lecturers I've had at this school – though the lectures are 2 hours long, I was genuinely never bored during a single lecture. Weekly assignments are very digestible and don't take long to complete. For the midterm and final group project, they really emphasize visuals (use slidecarnival for your slides), good presentation (practice public speaking with your group), and specificity (better to be overspecific when doing your analysis!!) – if you abide by these three tenets, you'll do great. Overall, course was one of the best I've taken at Harvard!
