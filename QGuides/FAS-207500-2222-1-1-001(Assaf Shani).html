Comments
Take this class! If you have confidence in your mathematical abilities, I would highly recommend this class to balance out a heavy workload. The material is interesting, and there's no pressure!
A great introduction to the material. The psets are helpful and Prof. Shani is very accessible outside of class.
This is not a commonly taught topic as far as math classes go, so I'm sure you will learn a lot and have fun too! Would be helpful to have some combinatorics background, in particular graph theory, game theory, and Ramsey theory. There will also be references made to classical results in algebra, such as properties of the natural numbers, vector spaces, and group theory.
This is a great class if you want to explore a niche field of pure mathematics in a low–pressure setting; there are no exams, the problem set resubmission policy is very generous, and Professor Shani is very open to providing help on the psets. However, I would advise against taking this class as your first exposure to formal proofs. The concepts in this class are very abstract and formal, so some mathematical maturity will help.
Take this class for sure if Assaf is teaching it. He does such a good job delivering complex concepts in a simple way – I went to office hours very often and he was extremely patient, clear, and helpful. He also lets you resubmit problem sets but that doesn't mean it's a low–effort class (in fact I'd say the converse is true). The class encouraged learning in every sense, since the homework policy involved a lot of feedback and acting on that feedback.
Assaf is is WONDERFUL professor. He is extremely patient and helpful at office hours and offers to meet one on one whenever you need personalized help. I will be taking more of his classes in the future.
