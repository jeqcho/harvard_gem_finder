What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is an incredible chance to read and explore documentary poetry of all kinds, and you will come away with many concrete skills and modes of analysis. It can be tough to write a poem every single week but having some source material to start from can also be useful. The class is quite abstract at times, but it really pushes you.
Take this class! If you consider yourself a poet or not, your mind and poetic sensibilities will expand greatly.
Wonderful workshop experience with Professor Smith. Her feedback and especially her takes on the excellent assigned readings are thoughtful, insightful and deeply socially conscious. Pretty busy semester as we wrote a poem every week, workshopped 6 people a week (you write a poem every week even if you’re not up for workshop, but Professor Smith writes comments on all of them every week), and wrote two primary reader letters for peers each week, plus keeping up with assigned readings, which we discussed for the first half of the 3 hour workshop. As a result you get a ton of experience reading and writing poetry but it can be a bit draining and the 15 minute slots for workshopping each person felt quick (I’m sure this could change in later years or even within a semester with student feedback). But because Professor Smith wrote feedback every week and you wrote and received two full feedback letters every week, you end up with like a dozen heavily feedbacked poems, half which were discussed in class, which is awesome. Overall a great course to explore documentary poetry, and the assignments are varied and you can write whatever you want (she doesn’t expect you to follow the assignment prompts exactly if you don’t want to). And of course such a pleasure just to hear Professor Smith talk about poetry and life, as with all these workshops, do be sure to read her work if you aren’t already familiar and to talk to her about her writing and experiences in office hours!
Prepare for pretty high creative output. This course can be taxing depending on how deeply you choose to engage with the readings & strive to improve your own documentary practice. Make sure you go to Prof. Smith's office hours and in general communicate with her about where you're at.
