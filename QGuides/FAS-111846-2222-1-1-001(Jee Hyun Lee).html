What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
130b is a huge jump from 130xa in terms of amount of work, but not in terms of difficulty. I'm not sure how it will be next year, but this year the lesson homeworks were shorter than 130xa, but the textbook homeworks required more work and there were video speaking homeworks to go with each speaking session. There is also a reading presentation that is longer than the final presentation. Given all that, the homeworks all had a purpose and really made me practice Korean every day. The final essay topic options are also generally drier than 130a options as they are supposed to be more advanced topics. Lee and Kwark SSNs were really amazing as always and were a joy to be taught by. There were many opportunities to practice speaking and to hear others speak which I thought was great. After 130xa/a there is still a lot to learn in terms of grammar structures and vocab, so 130b is definitely worth it if you have the time.
Prepare for a lot of assignments and a significant time requirement.
Korean 130B is a fantastic class! I learnt a lot – I finally felt like I could leave the level of being conversational and talking about personal matters only and could finally begin discussing bigger more objective topics like what was happening in the news. The complexity of ideas has a significant jump from 130a so make sure there is at least one topic that you feel particularly passionate about and that you feel comfortable in re: vocab. Study hard and study regularly! Do a lot of speaking and listening practice. 130b is a big jump from 130a but it will all pay off if you work hard!
