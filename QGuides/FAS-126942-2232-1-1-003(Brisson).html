What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
My TF Atim was very flexible with deadlines, which was really great and appreciated during a hard semester. While I enjoyed the flexibility this class gave me, I feel that I did not learn much beyond reviewing the passe composee, PQP, imparfait, and the subjunctif. The entire class surrounds social media and podcast making, and assignments are a mix of essays, podcasts, and a few tests. No vocab or grammar quizzes.
The French department (and I assume other Harvard language departments) labors under the misapprehension that the best way to impart knowledge to their students is to incorporate novel technologies that have absolutely nothing to do with French. Similar to the VR requirements in French 11, which accomplished very little other than taking time away from the study of the French language, the podcasts in French 30 presented no real platform for language learning. This course would benefit from more structure and less technology. Teach us the grammar and the vocabulary, and let us practice speaking. None of these things require technology.
