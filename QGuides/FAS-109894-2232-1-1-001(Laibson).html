Econ 10b is a great introduction to macroeconomics. After taking the course, you will have a solid understanding of how the economy works. For instance, you will understand why inflation is very high right now, and you will understand why fighting the inflation resulted in the failure of Silicon Valley Bank. Having some basic knowledge about these topics is important even if you don't end up studying economics. As a computer science concentrator, I am very grateful I took this course.

If you're making a comparison to Econ 10a, you should know that Econ 10b is much more interesting. The workload is about the same, but the topics are much more relevant to everyday life.

Econ 10b involves many different assignments, and sometimes it becomes hard to keep track of them. Make sure to read the syllabus carefully. It is easy to forget about a deadline.

Problem sets are not very hard and require at most a couple of hours. They are due Monday and are perfectly doable over the weekend, but it might be harder to get TF help if you start late.

Unit review program is a joke, but unfortunately it's mandatory. You have to take a mini–test every few weeks. These are pass/fail, and it's pretty hard to fail if you keep track of the material at least a little.

The essay assignment feels very unnecessary. If you do a reasonable job, you're pretty much guaranteed >90% on the essay, so no need to put too much effort into it.

You will see that there is a debate on the syllabus, but don't worry too much about it, it's completion–based.

The exams are actually fairly tricky. Do NOT be tricked by doing well on problem sets, you still need to study before an exam. The midterm and the final together are 55% of the grade, so it's easy for your grade to drop if you don't do well on the exams. (The syllabus says they might curve the grades, but this never happens in practice, the grade cut–offs in the syllabus are real.) The biggest help for an exam is doing the practice exams. Some questions are even repeated, so make sure to do practice exams AND review the solutions.

Overall, despite its quirks, I can recommend Econ 10b to non–economics concentrators who are looking to get a basic understanding of what economics is all about.

The lectures are very clear and the section component of the class is fantastic. I would highly recommend this class, especially if you didn't take Ec10a (as I hadn't beforehand). There are a few key things to do if you want to succeed in this class. First, get a good TF because they teach your section and grade all of your assignments (including the essay). if you start off with a brutal TF, just ask to switch. Second, go to office hours to check the PSET before it is due. Often, the answer key has nuanced explanations that you would never be able to figure out on your own, so to get 100% on all problem sets, office hours is crucial. Finally, study really hard for the exams, no matter how confident you are! They are worth a lot (especially the final), and it is very easy to feel like you can use common sense, but that is not a good strategy. Study hard and you will do well!
