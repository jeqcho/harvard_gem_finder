What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class was a chill math class, there's only one pset a week and the exams are pretty short and straight forward. I would take this class over classes that have 3 psets a week
Small class size facilitates more focused learning and sections are helpful, although the professor can go beyond time somewhat often. I would recommend online videos to supplement learning, and this is not because of the class itself, but because MV Calc is a generally difficult topic to get into without visual reinforcement.
This class was essentially a classic multivariable course, and seemingly had no relation to AM22a. However, this is not necessarily a bad thing. 22B covers some of the most important topics necessary for higher math classes, and helps fill in some of the holes created by the AM22a curriculum. The small class is extremely helpful in terms of giving you the best opportunity to learn the material. Meanwhile, although the MATLAB use was minimal, being necessary for no problem sets or assignments, the midterm and final exams were extremely light and reasonable.

In terms of the lectures, Dina might be one of the most passionate teachers I have ever seen in my life. Unfortunately, that extreme enthusiasm rarely translated to the class. Similar to 22a, most classes were entirely lecture–based, and could often run on for 20–30 extra minutes. Nevertheless, I truly enjoyed having Dina as a teacher. She truly cared about the success of each of her students and sought to get us passionate about the material.

Overall, I would still recommend taking 22b compared to other multivariable alternatives, especially for those who have taken 22a. I feel like taking both 22a and 22b have given me a leg up in terms of learning the entire scopes of linear and multi, as well as additional useful info.
I would definitely recommend AM22 over Math 21. It's 1 reasonable homework per week (instead of 3) and the exams were super fair– easy if you studied. Dina is extremely dedicated to teaching and you can tell she really cares about making sure we all truly understand the material. Perhaps because of that, she can come across as a tough professor sometimes, but is really kind and caring as a person. Edgar, the TF this semester, was amazing as well– super laidback and funny, and definitely improved my course experience. Since our class was very small (another perk of AM22 imo), both Dina and Edgar were very responsive to any confusions or concerns we voiced (whether about the course material, homework deadlines, or even when we wanted to take the midterm exam); this level of understanding from the course staff made such a huge difference for my mental health this semester :)
If you're looking to extend your thinking beyond the material, please please take normal 22b. If you want to take a class with your friends that covers this curriculum, please just take 21a. This class has barely any people (it's a totally fine class) but you will meet more friends and have more support (this class didn't have office hours really) in any other math class.
