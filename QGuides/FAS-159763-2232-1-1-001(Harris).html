Although they say no pre–reqs, if you only have 21a/b or equiv, this will be very hard
This is a fun class at the beginning with IMO / quant–interview–question–vibe psets. It gets pretty serious after that tho, with number theory (prime numbers and cryptography), generating functions, and probability. Python experience virtually required, Stat110/probability highly useful, experience with number theory/high level maths also helpful. But TFs and prof very helpful and accommodating to students.
It is a great class for those who are familiar with and interested in approaching various mathematical topics through the lens of problem solving. The course material is very broad, and so if you are not as interested in one area of the course it will not be dwelt on for long. Great opportunity to gain exposure and see the applicability of the pure math work you've been doing (if you are a math major). The course also includes the writing of an original math paper, which is perfect for the expository paper requirement (again for math majors). Very relaxed and fun environment, and the professor/TFs are remarkably helpful!
This is a great class if you're looking to add a math course that's less work than usual to your schedule. Problem sets were manageable, CAs were great, etc

The assignments from class had absolutely no relation to the psets given to us. Most psets took me 30 hours and I have taken extremely difficult advanced math courses, it's just that this course jumped around from topic to topic of random difficulty levels. I was hoping for a fun environment to spend time with other students and solve interesting math problems however, this was just unreasonable, and I didn't even learn anything in the end.
First, this class is taught by a grad student, I never met or interacted with Professor Harris who is listed as the instructor in my.harvard. That said, I think this class is a lot of fun! It has 9 psets where you can drop two, and you learn a lot of interesting topics. There is lots of support and problem sets are generally only a few problems. Overall, if you're looking for a math elective where you do brain teasers (like quant finance interviews), modular arithmetic, error correcting codes and some clever probability problems, go for it! I think you learn a lot of interesting topics that aren't taught elsewhere, and it's low commitment.
The course name is a misnomer. The assignments are graduate level mathematics that are not taught in lecture and which you are somehow expected to know how to rederive without even being told the basic vocabulary. The problems are also not even applicable to the "world" in any sense. The few problems which are not graduate mathematics are basic on tricks and gimmicks that work only in very specific situations which the course staff will keep from you.
A fun class to take as an elective, and rewarding if you are willing to put in the time. Probem sets are not trivial, but all doable if you go to office hours (the instructor and CAs this year were all particularly helpful and understanding!)
The first pset was deceptive in that the rest will be a lot more mathematically involved. However, the psets aren't anything crazy as long as you go to office hours to get the solutions. The teaching team was super chill with deadlines and extensions. I didn't love that class time was spent working through a set of problems, but the teaching team would walk around and help students, which I liked.**
Take this for a chill problem solving class! The problems are fun and its not a stressful class.
This class is pretty much a guaranteed A because the grading was very lenient, but the homework problems (75% of the grade) were so hard I could never do almost any of them without office hours. Could be pretty frustrating that the homework problems were usually nothing like class problems, so Id always feel unprepared for homework, so definitely go to office hours, the CAs are very helpful and will walk you through problems. There were flexible homework deadlines which is really nice, but the assignments were quite difficult. Not really math puzzles like advertised. Also no course head, just a TF and CAs
