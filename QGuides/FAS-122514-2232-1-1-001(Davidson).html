I would recommend for every student to take this course! Entering Harvard, you probably had the standard English, history, biology, chemistry, and math classes back in high school. You may have a general idea about what field you want to concentrate in, but taking the initiative to take a class in a field you've never encountered before will be incredibly rewarding. You don't need any particular background to take this course, as Dr. Davidson and the teaching fellows make sure to teach you everything you need to succeed in lecture and in section. You will cover each unit in linguistics (morphology, syntax, semantics, phonetics, etc.), but the course focuses on establishing a foundation upon which you can take more linguistics classes or learn interesting concepts that you can apply in various contexts (computer science, English literature, neuroscience, etc.). The grading is fair and prompt, and you get useful feedback about all mistakes that you might make on problem sets. There aren't exams in this course; rather, there are two "in class" psets; as the course syllabus allows dropping of one pset grade, this means that you have more flexibility in how you learn and apply the material in the pseudo in class exams. The projects (idiolect interview and teaching video) have rubrics that clearly explain what is expected from students to succeed and get a 20/20. If you double check that you are fulfilling rubric requirements and actually showed some effort in your project with how you tried to apply the class concepts, you will be successful on your assignments. The teaching staff are also fair and try to make time to meet with you in case you have any challenges or want to speak to them outside of their regular office hour slot. I would suggest trying to get to know them and going to office hours! Its a great way to meet and talk to other peers interested in learning what linguistics is even about.
