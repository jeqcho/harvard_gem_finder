This is a GREAT course if you want to gain an introduction to many of the major east asian religions. There is a focus on the historical concepts that formed these religions and assignments are not overly time–intensive and very reasonably graded. I had no EASTD experience prior to this and really enjoyed the course, but might be less interesting to you if you have some prior knowledge on the topic since it seemed like we covered very introductory topics. Overall a great course and a gem!
This course suits you very well if you are curious and you will stay curious after you took it. Highly recommend!
It's not a very demanding course, and Professor Robson is great. Readings are good, and though I would've liked slightly more structure I still enjoyed it.
This class was so interesting and well taught. Professor Robson is so knowledgeable and passionate about the material, and class was always engaging. There is also basically no work or stress––you need to have been in class in order to be able to do the take home quizzes, but that's about it. Highly reccomend!
Take this class if you are at all interested in East Asian Religions! The course presents a riveting narrative of Buddhism, Daoism, and Shintoism through China and Japan. Professor Robson is very knowledgable and passionate about the subject, making the lectures enjoyable.
This is a great survey course which gives you a strong foundation in each of the religious traditions covered. Professor Robson's lectures are always very enjoyable, and he is an incredibly knowledgeable instructor whose teachings go beyond what you would normally learn or read about (e.g., a main theme was deconstructing the term "religion" for the East Asian religious traditions). Work load is not heavy but the reading selections are great, so I would highly recommend doing them.
If you are interested in deepening your understanding of East Asian religions, this is a fairly low–commitment and interesting course. The lectures are interesting. You can get by without doing too much of the readings if you are particularly busy on any given week. The assignments (two quizzes and a paper) are very manageable and reasonable. For me, this course was particularly impactful in terms of expanding my conception of what "religion" is beyond assumptions I had inherited from Protestant Christianity, deepening my understanding of religious traditions underpinning meditation practices I had engaged in for years, and providing me with new frameworks through which to reckon with some of the big questions of life (suffering, meaning, the nature of reality). Although some lectures fell flat / section wasn't great, those overarching takeaways have made this course satisfying and thought–provoking for me.
TAKE THIS CLASS! No background required. One of the only intro classes I have taken here that is actually an intro class. You meet really cool people. I sat next to a monk taking the class all the time. Robson is a very engaging lecturer and has a true passion for the class. I am premed and had a very intense courseload and was easily able to manage the class. Readings help understanding, but are actually not necessary at all as long as you attend lecture. Section is very chill, mandatory but no attendance taken, and you dont have to speak at all if you dont want to. There are 2 take home quizes with multiple choice, short answer, and a short essay question. Very easy if you have been attending lecture and graded easily, I got a 95 or higher on mine. The final is just a 7–10 page essay and you have a lot of time to do it.
Take it! A wonderful class.
