This is an amazing class, but it definitely requires a good amount of work to do well. Find a study group early on and you'll be set.
Be prepared to spend much more time than you anticipate for this course. With a MWF schedule, lab component, 3 exams, lab report, and weekly problem sets this course will take up a significant chunk of time. The course material is well thought out and comprehensive but also means you learn quite a lot of material often at quite a fast pace. Finally, this is not a course that rewards you for choosing one of the harder intermediately biology options, meaning despite all the material and components of the course, grading can be stringent at times and exams can be both too long for the allotted time and too difficult, even though the course staff tries their best to support you. If you're truly interested in physical biochemistry, feel free to take it, otherwise this class likely isn't for you.
This is a great course. If you plan on studying structural biology, biochemistry, or biophysics this course is a must. The teaching staff are absolutely amazing, both in terms of their teaching and their support. The course covers a lot of useful content and is rigorous enough to challenge you to internalize the concepts and understand them well.

That being said, it is a difficult course with a big workload. I would not recommend taking this course alongside several other heavy courses. So keep this in mind. Nevertheless, it's still an amazing course for students interested in the material.
This course is definitely challenging. If you're interested in biophysics or structural biology, you should definitely take this class. If you liked LS1A, MCB 65 is like LS1A on steroids. Keep your schedule balanced so you have time to dedicate to it.
