Start the homework as soon as you can! Form a study group as soon as the first homework drops because in this class you can definitely tough out on your own, but it will be much more enjoyable with others.
Why you should take this class: All the amazing aspects of Chinese Bx carry over to this course, including: a small class size, dedicated feedback from teachers, an engaging textbook, and a gradual improvement of your Chinese language skills that is reflected in your phone calls to your parents ;) However, two additional things I found valuable in 123xb: 1) A deeper dive into Chinese culture, including Chinese history dating back to the Three Kingdoms to current Chinese government policies, the perception of marginalized groups in China, and popular phone apps used in China. 2) Engaging projects, including a poetry recitation competition with an audience that filled an entire Science Center Hall and a letter–writing to someone close to you who speaks Chinese. Would highly recommend taking immediately after Bx because the grammar structures learned in Bx are built upon in 123xb.

Workload: It was a bit more work than Bx for me (8 hours outside of class compared to 6 hours), but I feel it was necessary in order to really master the complex material that was being taught. Prepare to spend ~2 hours more per week outside of class than you spent in Bx.

Suggestion to future students: Learn to use Anki. Spaced–repetition helps so much so you're not just cramming vocab before quizzes, but can retain vocab permanently.

Chinese 123XB is one of the most enjoyable classes that I have taken at Harvard thus far. Regardless of whether you are taking this class as a continuation from Chinese XB, or as a brand new incoming student, it is very easy to fit in and find your place in the course. The instructors all do an outstanding job of making the class very engaging and have also arranged a great set of topics. The course discusses multiple socially relevant and timely topics, which make what you learn very applicable to everyday life (especially vocabulary–wise).
