Professor Abi–Rached is one of the most insightful professors at Harvard. I took one of her fall term courses and decided to take another one in the spring — and was not disappointed. I love that her lectures build upon what we learned from the readings and cover a wide swathe of material. She also included many different media in her presentations, including art and films. "Mental Health in Crisis" is a great course because it also exposes you to a wide variety of issues that are all tied to mental health, including colonialism, the opioid crisis, Covid–19, and the traditional idea of "trauma neuroses" and nervous diseases. Generally, the readings are interesting, though some texts are difficult to parse through. Abi–Rached is a great lecturer, too. One downside of the course is that about half of the lectures are given by guest lecturers — some are great, others are alright. If you have a prior background in psychopathology, some of the guest lecturers might be a little bit irritating (for just reciting DSM diagnoses or things you've already learned in the course). But, overall, the class is good.
