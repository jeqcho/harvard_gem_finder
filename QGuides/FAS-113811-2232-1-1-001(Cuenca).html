What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Boring lectures. Confusing material. Inaccessible teaching team. Unfair exams. Take Stat110 instead.
This is an interesting class and you will learn a good amount about the theoretical underpinnings of probability (probability spaces, random variables and events, and so on). However, we don't go super in depth on anything, and nothing we learned felt that advanced. If you've done some probability or abstract math before, this class won't be too hard. Sometimes the lectures got behind schedule and we'd have psets that covered stuff we hadn't learned yet, which was annoying. Overall, a decent class, I learned a good amount, just not that exciting.
This class is deceptively hard. The first unit up to the first midterm is very intuitive and is probability that you have likely been exposed to before like coin flips and dice rolls and it's a fun and challenging course at the beginning. But then the second half of the course becomes instantly more confusing and difficult to visualize and connect to and because of that it was much harder to keep up with. The exams in the class don't feel like they are a full demonstration of all that you know because they were only 4 questions long and 1 hour so it would only cover 4 or 5 topics as opposed to 3 or 4 chapters worth of material that was gone over in class. Also, the small size of the class is not very helpful. I would say if you're deciding between this class or Stat 110 (which I was), I would take Stat 110. But, if you're taking this class after Stat 110 for a more theoretical and rigorous approach, I would say it's worth it, just get ready to very heavily rely on the textbook. There were times I felt that the lectures weren't even necessary because all the material was well articulated in the textbook and the lectures just muddied up the topics in my opinion.
I would like to tell future students that it's a valuable course with incredibly use and insight that I would happily recommend.
The grading is a bit weird, but I enjoyed learning about probability theory from a more mathematical perspective.
