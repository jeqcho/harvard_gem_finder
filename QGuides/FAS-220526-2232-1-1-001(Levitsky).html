Comments
If you are interested in a class that is entirely focused on learning interesting material from readings and then repeating that exact same material in the class, then this is a course for you. If you want robust student discussion and to learn from the professor/students, then you should find another class.
This class is a dream for anyone interested in comparative politics. You get to sit around a table for two hours a day and talk poli–sci with super knowledgable classmates and a world–renowned professor. As someone without the greatest Gov background, it was tough, but it also taught me so much so quickly.
I loved this class. Levitsky is amazing and I loved writing the final paper. This is a class where you actually have to do the readings because of its small class size and the seminar being structured where Levitsky walks through the concepts in each reading one by one. I loved that it forced you to do the readings. However, I thought that the seminar part of the course could have connected concepts from reading slightly more and made you build upon concepts. Therefore, I did not get much out of the seminar portion when I did the readings in depth because it was just reviewing the concepts of the readings which felt a little elementary. But the reading list was spectacular, and I really enjoyed the topic. Also Levitsky is a character with so much knowledge. His expertise cannot be understated. Go to his office hours; he's a great guy to talk to.
