This is an amazing class that I would recommend to all Native, Indigenous, and Pacific Islander students. This is by far the most enriching and fulfilling class that I have taken here at Harvard. I would also recommend this class to those who are working on solidarity for Indigenous folk! This is a great class to hold space and hear from Indigenous voices.
really interesting and enjoyable class, professor is passionate, kind, learned a lot!
Take this course! There are not many courses that focus on Indigenous perspectives, and specifically Indigenous feminist perspectives. No matter your background, this is such an important class to take to expand your knowledge and appreciation of Indigenous–led environmental movements, as well as to challenge your thinking about the history of settler colonialism and the present challenges Indigenous people face.
Absolutely take this class. The workload is totally manageable and the classroom environment is memorable, welcoming, and friendly. I loved every time we could meet and I will hold this experience close to heart as I move forward in life.
This course was incredible! Every week we looked at a different recent feminist Indigenous environmental justice movement. The material was manageable— Professor Hogue assigned amazing readings, as well as a broad and exciting array of music videos, art, poetry, short films, etc. Throughout the class, we were attentive to the categories of gender and sexuality, which I really appreciated. Professor Hogue did such a beautiful job creating a space in which people felt safe enough to have rigorous, risky, and deep conversations each week. We also got to hear from many absolutely incredible guest speakers— Indigenous women activists, artists, thinkers; settler allies supporting essential decolonial work. Everybody should take this class, particularly those with commitments to environmental justice, decolonization, thinking beyond white feminisms, building beyond racial/carceral capitalism/settler colonialism (which are commitments everyone should have!).
Wonderful, wonderful class!
Have an open heart and a curious mind.
