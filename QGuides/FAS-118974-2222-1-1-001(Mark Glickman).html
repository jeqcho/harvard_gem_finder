Comments
I feel much more comfortable reasoning about non–normal data than I did before. I think this material is worth dwelling on and really letting it sink it. I appreciated learning about all of the less–exotic–but–more–interpretable alternatives to machine learning models, and I feel like having these models in my back pocket will help me work with all kinds of data throughout my career.
Take this class for your concentration requirements. I took it because it seemed the "least painful" of the next level stat courses, but I ended up loving it almost as much as any stat course I've taken yet.
– Take this class with a group of friends; otherwise, you might find yourself unnecessarily worrying about losing a few points here and there off your work. The material is not bad, but the nitpicking part about it makes the class less appealing overall.
STAT149 is an excellent class, and I would highly recommend you take it. Professor Glickman is an amazing teacher: he explains concepts very clearly, he responds to emails/questions promptly, his slides/notes were helpful, and he clearly cares a lot about the course. Grades/feedback on assignments were always released quickly, and I thought the marking was strict but fair. The course involves quite a bit of work, but I always felt like there was more than enough time to get the assignments done as they were well spaced out: problem sets were only due every two weeks and Professor Glickman would always release them early, plus there was ample time to prepare for exams and complete the project. However, the biggest pro of this course is that its content is extremely useful. We covered so many different types of models, which is refreshing after most of the mandatory stat classes up until this point have focused on linear regression.
Stat 149 is a very fun and chill course that provides a soft introduction to a broader set of models. WAY better than Stat 139 and a GREAT course to take as a senior. Mark knows that people do not want to work that hard so he makes the assignments focus on content rather than making them seem like busy work as Kevin does in Stat 139. The tests are reasonable as is the project.
You should take this after 139 if you're interested in extending your modeling capabilities. I was skeptical at first, and it seemed like a low work stat class that could pad my concentration, but in reality it was fun, interesting, applicable, and REASONABLE. Six psets, two midterms (final was heavily focused on second half), and the grading was fair. Would be useful to take with a friend.
This class isn't particularly hard. However, it is graded pretty difficultly. The teaching staff is very particular in their solutions so while the concepts are not extremely difficult, it is graded quite harshly. Would recommend, however, if you need a stat elective.
The class is very organized and the instructor is very clear in explaining concepts. The psets are super helpful and not very hard, but the exams are quite challenging.
This course is fantastic – I would highly highly recommend it if you're a stat concentrator or have taken the pre–requisites! The course materials are very well–developed, and Mark is a great instructor. The workload is incredibly manageable, and the material is very useful if you want to do any sort of modeling in the future but don't want to be constrained by the assumptions of ordinary least–squares regression. The problem sets are honestly kind of fun, and the final project was very interesting for me.
The content of this course is good, such as the lecture notes and provided R code. Beyond the content, this course does not provide much value, and the structure of the course is frustrating. For example, the bi–weekly homework assignments are not very difficult, but they take time to complete and are only worth 15% of the course grade. Meanwhile, the two 90–minute exams are quite different from the homework assignments, and they are worth 60% of the course grade.
