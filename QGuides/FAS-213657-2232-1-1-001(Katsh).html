This course was absolutely phenomenal. It definitely solidified my decision to go to law school, so if you're a tentative pre–law student, I highly recommend taking this course. The theory lens is great for the issues you will consider –– from prostitution to polygamy to prescription drugs. You might be skeptical at first, but I highly recommend you dive headfirst into the more "philosophy–y" papers you read at the beginning of the year. Those provide a really great framework for considering the rest of the course. There were also some phenomenal readings later in the course about neuroscience, determinism, and free will –– woah, were those world–shifting!

The leeway you get on the final paper is also great: you can literally write about *anything* you want, as long as you can connect it to the course material. It's awesome!

Gabe is also a phenomenal (I'm using that word too much!) lecturer. He's great at guiding discussion without dominating it, and you really get to see your classmates shine. Honestly, that was my favorite part of the course – getting to know the folks in the class and seeing their thoughts. It's one of those classes where everyone in the class gets the chance to dazzle, and it's really awesome to see.

Overall: Take this class! You will have a phenomenal time.
Do the readings! Go to office hours! You will learn so so much!
