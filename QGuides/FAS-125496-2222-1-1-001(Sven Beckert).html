American Capitalism is an okay Gen Ed. It's easy, but I didn't find it to be the most engaging or stimulating class. I definitely had a lot of high expectations coming into the course after reading some of the previous Q Guides (and given the topic of the course itself), but often left feeling like there was no overarching framework or structure to the course. I personally felt like a lot of the readings weren't the most valuable, and many of the section discussions, which were occasionally interesting, lacked structure. There is a good amount to like about the course, but there is still quite a bit of room for improvement.
This class is perfect for students who enjoy history and can use it for either a GenEd or History requirement. The lectures were very compact, yet still effective in getting through the history of American capitalism, given by a real expert in the field. The material allows one to grapple with important questions about the development of and relationship between social, political, and economic systems, all of which heavily impact each other. Readings are not bad, and just giving them a skim will be enough to contribute to insightful sections. The assignments (midterm exam, two short essays, and one long essay) were not bad at all either.
If you are looking for a course that you will remember for the rest of your life, take American Capitalism with Sven Beckert. The discussions you have in this course with your classmates will be some of the best in your time here. Very rarely in your life will you get the opportunity to talk with people from such different backgrounds about a topic that is so pervasive. The material will paint capitalism in a most negative light, but this is because the course is intended to complement teachings of American capitalism in modern high schools and the (likely) positive preconceptions that most Americans have regarding the topic. All opinions are welcome so long as they can be supported by historical evidence or strong rationality. This is the ideal Harvard course, and the staff should be immensely proud.
