Pretty easy class
Absolute Gem of a course that is actually interesting. Professor Nagy and Nick were awesome and extremely accessible
This is a great class. The Tain is a really interesting story, and Professor Nagy does a great job explaining the intricacies behind word choice and symbols that might otherwise go unnoticed. Nick was a fantastic section leader – I actually looked forward section each week. Other than reading, the workload is three relatively short writing assignments.
It is a good way to knock out requirements but you won't really learn about Celtic culture.
This course provides an in–depth exploration of early Irish heroic tropes. I appreciated the attention to the role (often unconventional) of women in these sagas usually centered around male heroic figures. I learned much about what can or cannot be assumed about recorded oral narratives and/or the cultures that generated them, and recommend this course to anyone interested in Irish literature and/or oral narrative cultures.
Nagy is a cool professor, very accessible, kind, flexible. He's obviously probably one of the most knowledgeable people around on the material. Lectures can be dry, but if you do the reading, they can be very informative. The department is small, and these classes are often quite small as well. You get to know everyone well, so it's easy to get help if you want to make sure you're on track.
If you need a low key arts and humanities course this is a good option. You have short, short readings each week that you aren't constantly quizzed on (but worth it to read so when the assignments show up you're not a fish out of water).

Midterm is two 500 word essays on the book which are relatively easy if you've paid any attention. "reading" paper requires a bit of work, but if you have all the sources it's really not that much. Final was three 750–1000 word essays that were simple and not bad. Grading is fair, but it's hard to get below an A/A– if you just keep up with the book every week. Was a perfect fifth class for me personally.
This is an easy class to misjudge. Yes it is somewhat of a gem, but don't let that think you that you won't have to keep up with the reading or not go to lecture. The writing assignments are easy, but only if you keep up with the readings. Not time consuming until it comes time to do the actual assignments.
Definitely take this class. Not only is it an easy A but it's a lot of fun and the course material is actually interesting. Nagy is an amazing professor and he is very accommodating. The assignments are easy and don't take too long, even the final paper and take home final. Take this class.
