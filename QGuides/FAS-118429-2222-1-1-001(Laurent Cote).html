Easy class
Really interesting course material; though presented somewhat confusingly at times. Nice and challenging PSETs but not overly laborious. Exams felt a bit hit–or–miss as far as content goes. However, Laurent is a very fair guy and wants you to succeed.
I would only take this course if you've taken a course in real analysis before. Group theory also would be useful. Overall, I enjoyed the course a lot.
Laurent is great! Only complaint would be that I wasted a decent number of hours in my life due to mistakes in the problem statements in the homeworks.
This was Laurent Cote's first time teaching, so there were a small but nontrivial number of typos in assignments and exams were not very well written. That said, I think he is a good instructor and that the course is good. I have full confidence that in a second iteration of the course, these details will be ironed out and the course will be much better.

One recommendation I have (especially if the course is offered at 9 am again) is to attend lecture! This course is not so hard, but it is not one which can be learned just by reading notes online (which can be a bit hard to read). If you have trouble waking up or just generally do not attend lecture, I would advise you choose a different course. But the course is very good and different from just about any other math course at Harvard. It is an excellent way to fulfill the Math requirement for those who want something a bit harder than Math 112 but probably not quite as hard as Math 114.
