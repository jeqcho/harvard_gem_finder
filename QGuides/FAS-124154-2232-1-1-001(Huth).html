15c was great for the first 2/3 of the year; lectures were fairly clear (and recorded) and I was able to do all the PSets on my own in a day or two. However, near the end of the course it really dropped off. The last PSet was extra long and if I didn't drop it I would've had to spend an insane amount of time on it. I still recommend taking the course versus skipping as it helped me understand waves and differential equations better. Also, if you're thinking about taking this and 143a at the same time, DO IT! I did and it was such a good decision (143a actually helped me with 15c at times, and vice–versa).
It was an interesting course about waves that covers a wide variety of topics. It was pretty manageable for the most part, and the demos were entertaining. I feel that the lectures were a little chaotic at times, so I really learned the most from the PSETs and the lecture notes. I was a little annoyed that the Final Exam was really only on the last four weeks of the class because we were told it was cumulative, but it wasn't particularly hard I just didn't study that material as closely as I should have. Overall, the class is not bad as a req. It was pretty helpful for 143a, sometimes I wish I took it before 143a.

Also, a small number of the homework problems seemed confusingly unrelated to what we discussed in lecture.
15C with Huth is a good course, but gets a bit disorganized towards the end. It's not that challenging though, and the material is pretty cool.

This is a really solid class. Of the 15a/b/c series, this is probably the most useful material for your future physics career.
