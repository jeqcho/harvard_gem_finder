Comments
It is an informative class and covers a wide array of topics related to east Asia such as International Relations, Economics and history.
Take this course if you are interested in East Asia and/or global trade! The more you're interested, the more you're able to take away from this course.
GENED 1119 is one of those GENED courses that is disguised as a general education course but in reality is very much a GOV class. The work associated with this class can be challenging for someone not familiar with political economy or international relations, so know that going in. While Christina Davis is an excellent lecturer and the TF staff did their best, the expectations for this course are higher than where they should be at for a GENED course. I very much enjoyed the course, but I did put in the time to do the reading and the like. The more time you put into this course, the better it comes out. In short, take GENED 1119 if you are interested and passionate about the topic and are willing to do the work; the course is one of the more demanding general education courses on offer.
Would recommend this class if you are very interested in the political economy in East Asia and the WTO. If you more want to learn about the culture of East Asia or the history/economics of the East Asian Growth Miracle, this class does not go very in–depth into those subjects. Many of the methods to grow a country you learn in this class also go directly against traditional economic principles so make sure to discuss with someone from economics to get the alternative perspective if you do decide to attend.
This course would be very helpful if one wants to understand international politics but do not expect the course to be extremely comprehensive.
If you are interested in East Asia (particularly China) in contemporary world economic system or want to learn about the East Asian Miracle, this is the course to take!
You will learn a lot from this class, but it's easy to fall behind and not go to lectures after the quiz. Definitely try to stay on top of your stuff. Ask for help early
If you are interested in trade policy, economics, and East Asian politics I would highly recommend this class. The lectures can be a bit dry and the readings can be a lot, but the content is very interesting and Professor Davis is incredibly knowledgeable.
Do not take if you have a busy schedule! You won't be able to enjoy the interesting materials in this class because you won't have the time to manage it.
Lots of readings. Cool assignments. Just be prepared to put in the work but also expect the instructors to understand that!
The readings are extremely extremely extremely lengthy for a GenEd, and it is not clear which readings you are expected to know for section. The TF will ask questions about the readings you didn't know you were expected to read. Also, the lectures are quite dry.
this gened is a lot of work, but it's honestly worth it. doing the readings makes class infinitely better (and the readings are often REALLY interesting, albeit quite long). utilize your TF for the papers!!
A good class that double counts in the gov department. Feels a lot like a gov class, which is reflected in the work type and length. However, the content is interesting, with lots of good historical angles. Prof. Davis is compelling
The assignments, especially the final paper, were far to restrictive. Rather than letting us explore the course content in ways that interest us, the assignment forced us to write about topics we might not care about due to its complexity and its specificity.

The content is not as interesting as it could be and skews VERY far to the left in terms of economic study. Nearly all of the reading assignments push for governmental control rather than free market control. I understand the course is about East Asia but showing all sides of the problem would be more beneficial and honest.
very interesting topics. lots of readings for a gened, but i have a hunch this might change in future iterations of the course. Austin gave really, really detailed feedback on the policy paper and led effective sections
