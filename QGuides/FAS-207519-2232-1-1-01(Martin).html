What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You will develop as a writer during this course. You will experience significant highs and lows as you go through the process of reading, research, and writing that is not comparable to other concentrations (I would know, I'm a joint concentrator)! Give it your all and you will definitely see the benefits.
You will get a lot out of this course in terms of how to be an effective writer in history. It's a lot of work and the turn around is really fast in terms of turning in papers basically every week, but the feedback you get is super valuable and you come out of the course as a 100% better writer than you were. You also learn a lot about interacting with primary and secondary sources, making original arguments, and making historiographical arguments.
The tutotial is interesting, but little guidance is actually given on how to write and improve essays. You are kind of thrown into the dark and told to figure out how to write for what your TF is looking for. The grading isn't super hard and the actual discussions can be super super interesting. Overall not a terrible class at all just needs work on the structure.
I would tell future students to take this course, especially if Professor Martin is teaching it.
This class was great. It raised really important questions about the role of history in academia as well as questions about morality and international diplomacy. Of course, there is a lot of writing but it pays off. You will see significant improvements in your quality of history writing. Be prepared to class having done the readings and wanting to engage, as it is crucial to doing well and learning. The tutorials are great and allow you to read what others are writing as well as receive great feedback. You really do get what you put in with this class.
Make sure to do the readings and participate in lecture to get the most out of this course.
I'll be frank: this course is required, so you don't have much of a choice...

That said, the professor and teaching team were excellent, and the readings were generally interesting. Discussions, unfortunately, dragged on (largely because many students didn't do the readings and fudged their way through the THREE–HOUR discussions), and tutorials were less than helpful. My writing did improve over the course of the class, but primarily because of my TF's written feedback and the many opportunities we had to practice writing.
