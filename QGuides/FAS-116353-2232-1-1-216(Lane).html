If you have done any writing before, take Hum 10.
One (unsolicited) piece of advice is to make sure that you’re using Dr. Lane as a resource! She’s extremely helpful and was always happy to answer my questions on Slack outside of scheduled classes and office hours. Especially with the first drafts, it’s often really easy to not really figure out how to structure your essays but after talking to her my next steps in setting up my papers became much more clear.

That being said, definitely take this Expos. The grading definitely feels a bit harsh at times, but the reading list is honestly really cool. Pretty much every time I was reading something for this class, my friends who were taking Expos simultaneously (in other sections) would always be like “I can’t believe you’re reading that, I have to read [insert boring text name] instead.” So, you’ll never be bored at least. Definitely only recommend if you’re into science fiction though — if you are, you’ll love it and if you’re not, you probably won’t.
Take this Expos. I'm not a big writer, so I can't say I loved this class, but I definitely didn't mind it, and every time I spoke to someone in a different expos, I was so happy to be in Dr. Lane's class. I found her feedback very clear, which helped me edit my essays to meet her expectations. There is not much reading, so the workload was very manageable. The content is really interesting, and the final essay gives you a lot of flexibility to talk about pretty much whatever you want.
I loved this course, very well–done EXPOS class. You essentially learn about to what extent can we trust technology and how it has been discussed in various aspects of society (environment, media, ethics, and more). Here's my feedback:
– Workload varies throughout the semester. Mostly just ~2 hour readings each week. The most work–intensive parts of the class are when you're first writing your essay. Budget a good amount of time. I can't really put a number on it, but ~4–5 hours for a draft I think was roughly my amount, broken into bits.
– Look on and off your essay after reading it. Look at it and edit it for an hour, then wait a day and then look back at it to allow your mind a break and reset.
– Essay feedback was great. Don't necessarily expect stellar grades for your 1st essay– you'll learn how to WRITE (not just the 5 paragraph style high school essay, but actually persuasive, natural academic papers) and honestly it made me such a great writer. I turned out fine in the end.
– Go to the writing center if you want, or go to her office hours, or get external peer review help. At least one of these three will help you with your final revision.
TAKE THIS CLASS !! Professor Lane is amazing and is very reasonable and clear with her expectations. The material is very interesting, and you'll love this Expos.
If you're looking for an easy expos, this one is it. Not super learn y though.
