This course was great — Dr. Hooven led great discussions in class and provided really useful lecture videos to study the concepts every week. It struck a great balance between teaching the science while also spending a lot of time discussing its implications in the world. I'd definitely recommend it to anyone interested in biology, evolution, or even just human behavior.
If Dr. Hooven isn't teaching this class, then I have no idea how it will go. I also cannot say anything with regards to the lecture format, except to say that I am 90% certain I would not have enjoyed the course half as much if it hadn't been a seminar. If this is taught as a seminar in the future, my advice would just be to make a schedule for yourself to stay on top of the work. Since seminars only meet weekly, it can be tempting to put off watching a video or reading an article or chapter, but this is the kind of material that you really need to digest over the course of a few days to fully understand, so it's best to space it out. And make sure you take notes while watching videos.
In seminar form, this class was one of the best. As a non–bio senior this was one of my favorites in my whole time here.
