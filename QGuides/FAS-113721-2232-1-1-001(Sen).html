If you are interested in Markov chains or the idea of stochastic processes in any way, take this class! Professor Sen is an amazing professor, and he is able to break down and explain all the concepts in a very intuitive fashion. Although some of the concepts can get challenging, the lectures are not too fast, and Subha is always willing to stop to take questions and explain things further. The course also does not require too much time. The problem sets are very manageable, with a good blend of computation, actual thinking, and implementing simulations in R. Grading is a little rough, with 75% coming from tests, but the problem sets are fairly representative of the midterms and there is a little bit of curving applied. Overall, if you took Stat 110 and were interested in the Markov chain portion of the class, take 171.

This class was probably the most interesting class I took this semester— if you liked Stat 110, you'll probably like this class. Subha is an amazing lecturer, the textbook is very clear, and you'll be doing a lot of the type of problem solving that made 110 fun.

That being said, this class is 75% exams. If you're not a great test taker and/or don't have a lot of time to dedicate to this class, doing poorly on the tests will really drag down your overall grade. In addition, the problems on exams were not always weighted in a way that made sense and the final was much harder than the two midterms.

Nevertheless, I highly recommend this class if you like probability— just make sure to give it the time it deserves.
