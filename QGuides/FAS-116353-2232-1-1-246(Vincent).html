Hudson is a really caring and engaging instructor. He obviously is very passionate about this subject, which makes learning from him a really great opportunity. He gives great feedback, and he's very open to the many different opinions and perspectives of his students (which was something I appreciated a lot). The class structure is organized and clear as well.

This EXPOS shouldn't be considered easy. It's challenging and rigorous. But its students are pushed hard in order to help them become better writers, and I'm very glad I took this class.
This course will not only allow you to grow so much as a writer and critical thinker but will engage you with an issue that is the most pressing problem society faces. The readings and speakers will provide you with crucial knowledge to become very educated on mass incarceration and its complexities.
This is a good option for Expos, Hudson is great instructor and really nice person! To summarize the syllabus, the first essay is a comparative analysis essay of two pre–selected sources, the second is a research essay of a topic of your choice, and the third assignment is a short capstone project (letter to congressman, a Crimson article, etc.) based on your research essay. ***Most important thing when choosing an Expos class is to pick a topic you are genuinely interested in, like really!!*** Having some background knowledge about the subject is also really helpful.
Definitely take this class if you are interested in our country's prison system. You definitely do a lot of research so you really learn so much. This class is challenging and time consuming like any other expos class, but it is worth it in the end. The essays were a bit challenging but very rewarding. I recommend taking this class. Hudson is an amazing Professor and works with you to get the best out of you.
Take this class if you want to become a better writer and thinker! Hands down my favorite class at Harvard so far, I can't recommend it enough. If you want to not only learn about oppressive structures in America but understand how to dismantle them through writing, this class is perfect for you. I came in as a pre–med student and will likely leave as a government/pre–law student.
This definitely the most rewarding and powerful class I have taken this year. The readings are pretty interesting and the capstone project is as creative and powerful as you make it. No one comes into this course as great writer but you will definitely leave a better one. Hudson is a great professor that facilitated great discussions and lessons. The only thing I will say is that it is somewhat graded harshly and you may not get the grade you want on your first essay but he does prepare you to do better on the rest of the assignments so as long as you put in the effort you will succeed.
This course was absolutely amazing. Every time we met for class, my worldview shifted a bit, and I came out of the classroom just a bit more aware of the issues that surround us. This class was my favorite all semester, and truly changed the way I approach not just writing, but thinking about society. I definitely recommend this class, and Hudson is a fantastic and patient teacher! Just make sure you always have a first draft to submit for feedback, because he gives feedback only once on your essay draft before you have to submit the final product, so don't miss that window. Definitely do all the readings and additional work, because it is imperative in order to contribute to and understand the course material. Also, start your essays well in advance so you can go to the Writing Center or consult your peers for feedback.
This course requires a high workload, but this expos class is actually interesting and worth taking even without previous interest in this topic.

We learned how to cite in MLA format, and other structural writing techniques to make you a better writer. Hudson always said that writing is an art and is an important way to express yourself and create change in the world. Although it is a heavy topic, this class will definitely inspire you to make change in this world.

This is great for people who are interested in social justice. Be sure you read AND UNDERSTAND the books; you really can't get away with not doing it (at least for 2 of the 3). This is a very rewarding class. Very worth it.
