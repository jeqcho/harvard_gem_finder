This course was genuinely one of the best I've taken at Harvard. I learned so much about our world and the underlying causes of human suffering. The students who take this class are really motivated and passionate about this work, so you will find yourself in a great community led by experienced professionals in the field of humanitarian response.

GHHP70 is a really interesting course that tackles humanitarian relief from both the legal and medical aspects. It also goes into depth on historical context and prominent case studies. The workload is very manageable, and the teaching staff is amazing! Definitely worth taking during your time at Harvard.
Grading is super harsh without warning, and you don't get to choose your project group. The class has super interesting material and discussions, but the grading took away from how enjoyable it was to learn.
This is a fantastic class, which lives up to its hype. Dr. Kayden and Dr. VanRooyen have built out a seamless and fascinating course syllabus, which which will allow you to engage deeply with some of recent history's most seminal events from a humanitarian standpoint.
This is one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard; the professors are incredibly eloquent, thoughtful, and clear leaders in the field and have invested immense energy in creating a course that effectively prepares you for global health and humanitarianism. The readings are all great, and the assignments are manageable and often quite engaging.
This course was excellent, and there's no better way to get an understanding of the humanitarian sphere at the College than this course. The instructors ask thought–provoking questions in lecture, and the course is very well structured. The entire teaching staff was also very kind, and they made themselves available despite their busy schedules.
Take this class! Savor every moment, the assignments, the short responses, the in–class discussion, the group projects. Truly have never taken a class that has made me relearn and regain my appreciation for learning. Shows what a difference good teaching (Heba, Brinkley, Dr. Van Rooyen, and Dr Kayden) can have on a student!
Take it!! It is a wonderful course with an even better teaching staff. You will learn a lot!
Best class I've taken at Harvard. You come away from this class much more open–minded about the messiness of humanitarian situations, and also the importance of crisis response––in everything from disaster evaluation and risk assessment to coupling interventions with longer–term development and facilitating collaboration across sectors. This class pushes you to think like a problem–solver and a leader, and throughout offers opportunities for teamwork, feedback, speaking, and learning generally. Profs. Kayden and VanRooyen will be among the most eloquent and engaging lecturers you'll ever encounter, and the course material and case studies are very informative and thoughtfully compiled. Sections offer opportunities for interactive discussion and review, and the group assignments truly encourage you to collaborate with your team and learn from each other. You emerge from this class more thoughtful, compassionate, and humbled. A wonderful class; could not recommend it highly enough.
This is overall a great class! The course staff, as well as the majority of the students, care so much about the subject. The case studies were the highlight of the class. Sometimes, it felt like we were given mostly busy work (there are three weekly assignments that I didn't feel like I gained particularly much from), but I never felt stressed or overwhelmed by the volume of work. Some of the overarching themes (e.g. poverty exacerbates vulnerability to crises) seemed relatively straightforward, and spending a lot of time on these seemed to take away from the more interesting simulations or case studies.
