Fun and low stakes class to learn about architectural forms from all around the world (neat overview of buildings and histories you probably wouldn't see elsewhere). That being said, each week is a different professor so the teaching style and expectations can be inconsistent. Honestly a hit or miss with the quality of lectures. But section is very engaging, and exams are just rote memorization which is not hard. Be sure to memorize not just the IDs for the weekly buildings but also the random buildings mentioned in section!
This is a good introduction to landmarks: you cover a lot of landmarks, which is great if you're looking for breadth. I personally enjoyed the two–lecture rotation, but if you're looking for a deep study of a certain landmark, this is not the course for you. Some of the speakers are fantastic, but note that other lecturers are much more dry. Overall, this class is definitely worth shopping, and I'd take it again.
Take this course if you are at all interested in architecture! Having a keen interest in it helps a lot, otherwise you'll just feel like it is a burden. The course is so amazing if you come in with an open mind.
The lectures are boring. The section is particularly bad , and not just my own from what I've heard. It is not the section leader's fault, but the questions are just not engaging and the way it is structured is dreadfully boring. Also, there is an absurd amount of memorization for this class's final and midterm – it is unclear why we have to memorize over 300 specific dates, names, and definitions.
Take this class and be sure to go to the section. The guest lectures (almost all classes are guest lectures) are either great or terrible. The assignments are short and fun to work on.
I really enjoyed HAA as a survey course in history of architecture in my senior spring. You get to learn basic terminology and cover a wide number of landmarks, many of which are fascinating and beautiful (e.g., Ise, Chartres, Seattle Public Library, Ani Cathedral). The course is not terribly difficult and is highly accessible. Esesua is an amazing TF.
Great class and lots of very cool lectures. You come out with a greater appreciation for architecture. I took this pass fail and would have ended up getting an A, but honestly, I would do pass fail again because you end up enjoying the material a lot more without feeling the pressure of having to memorize all the landmarks. Great senior class to take since you can skip the midterm as well!
This course is very manageable and a perfect class to take your senior year. The content is fairly interesting and you don't need any background in architecture.
Great class for those interested in architecture. Dull for those who are not... but overall gem. Light work load and fair exams.
Take this class!! It is so chill and the lectures are actually so interesting. You get a new professor every week to teach about a different landmark, so you'll never really get bored. The only 2 assignments are very chill and short papers. Really cool class, everyone should take it.
Great class that serves as a good starting point for HAA. The lecture is generally interesting and engaging with buildings that are for the most part, important to know about. Section and assignments are low–stress, educational, and fun.**

Lectures vary week to week –– understandably, given a class where lecturers change weekly –– but you'll start to understand more about architecture. You probably won't come away with many life–changing insights, but it might be a good way to see if HAA is for you.
Come to sections with enthusiasm
