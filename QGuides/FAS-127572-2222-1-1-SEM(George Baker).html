I would say that paying attention in class and doing the readings, being active in discussions will pay off the most.
This is the most important class about climate change and renewable energy offered at Harvard. If you have any interest in decarbonization take this class.
I would absolutely take this class, regardless of concentration. I concentrate in biology and had no reason to take this class other than wanting to know more about clean energy, and it ended up being one of the most engaging and interesting classes I've taken at Harvard. I particularly enjoyed that our discussions were centered around technology, economics and many other aspects of clean energy, as this provided a clear picture of the benefits and pitfalls of different clean energy solutions.
The weekly assignments were a pain and were graded, which I disliked immensely, but the information from the class is important and valuable to learn and the final project is interesting.
This class is a great introduction of specific renewable energy sources and their financial feasibility. However, it is A LOT of lecturing and not a lot of discussion. If you're looking for a more seminar, small group discussion type class– do not take this class.
Take the course if you are interested in renewable energy. Especially if you are not ESPP, it is a great way to learn cool facts.
Great content, worth it if it aligns with your future ambitions.
This is an excellent course. George Baker is smart, experienced, and a great / engaging lecturer. This course is a deep dive into the technologies that will begin to combat climate change. Extremely relevant today.
