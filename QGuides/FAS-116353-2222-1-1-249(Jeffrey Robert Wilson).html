What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is very enjoyable and facilitates a lot of great discussions. You will learn a lot about your own writing style and how the writing process works (especially in regard to revision).
This class is a TON of work. There were numerous points where it seems that the presumption of the course is that you have no plans to do anything other than Expos over the weekend. I suppose that’s fine if you care a lot about Shakespeare, and Expos is your #1 priority, but for me it meant that I handed in a lot of work that I wasn’t really proud of and that I learned very little from completing.

This course spends a lot more time talking about how to write than anything else. This is a writing course after all, and if what you’re looking for is a way to understand very well what a specific person thinks of writing, then this is a good course. Dr Wilson is good at explaining his thought process behind writing and breaking it down into chunks. However, if you are already a fairly experienced writer and are not very interested in the writing class, this may not be the best fit for you. If you’re looking to take a course in order to talk and think about Shakespeare, I think you are probably better off taking a course in the English department.
This course is one of the more demanding Expos courses: a lot more response papers and a lot of reading. But Wilson was a great teacher, the grading was pretty forgiving, and Shakespeare is a great Expos topic.
This is a hard class but if you work hard and pay attention you will get a lot out of it. Also the difficulty of the class doesn't come from without but more from within. This class is challenging because the professor does a great job pushing you to create your best work and craft coherent and informative essays.
