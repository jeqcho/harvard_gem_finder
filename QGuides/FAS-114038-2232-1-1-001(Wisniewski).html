The CompLit sophomore tutorial was an absolutely phenomenal introduction to the concentration. We were introduced to key topics in CompLit (e.g., travel writing, translation, etc.) through a great selection of readings from different languages in translation (and always making use of the combined language skills of students and instructors). The instructors are such knowledgeable and kind people who created a very welcoming and enjoyable environment for the seminar discussions — I have been in many humanities sections with a rather intimidating environment for participation. I also liked that the seminars were always led and structured well with different exercises, rather than just having an open discussion about the readings. Do be prepared for a lot of reading and writing, but it always pays off. The writing assignments also teach you different valuable skills (close reading, translation, research). By the end of the course, you will have a strong foundation in important areas of studying literature.
