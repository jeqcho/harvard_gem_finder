Comments
This class is super fun to take! It requires more outside work than typical Spanish classes at Harvard due to more compositions and memorizing lines for the in–class plays, but it is worth it. The professors are so helpful and patient in learning Spanish with you. The readings and content are also very fun to read (family drama, past lives, etc.). I highly recommend it.
I loved Spanish 50! Cristina was my TF and she was very kind, supportive, and enthusiastic about the subject material. Wish we got to do more acting! That was the fun part of the class.
Super fun, creative way of bettering your Spanish. Very interesting course material and great for bettering your oral skills in the language.
Don’t be afraid to perform in from of the class! Everyone is in the same boat and no one is judgmental about acting skills. A step up in workload compared to Spanish 40, but it isn’t necessarily more difficult, just more work
This class is an awesome one. As long as you genuinely try your hardest and do your best, you don't need to worry at all—you definitely get out what you put in, so while you could slack and get by, it is so worth it to actually try. I would say that if you are shy about speaking Spanish, either don't take this course or get over it, because it is so worthwhile to improve your Spanish in this class. The workload is very light, so it is good to take if you have other classes that are harder (and the homework is all completion–based). It is also a great class to take that fosters an excellent class environment, since we're all forced to get close and work together, which is nice. Just put yourself out there, and you will love this class!
