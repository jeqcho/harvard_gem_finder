TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This was such a great course! Very well organized and was an extremely comprehensive survey course on Japanese architecture. Prof. Lippit is an amazing lecturer and always takes time to get to know everyone. The weekly response papers are not too bad and there is just one midterm (classic HAA memorization style) and a take–home final exam. But note that lecture participation matters to your overall grade and they are not recorded. Other than that, this is one of the best classes I have taken so far at Harvard.
I recently had the privilege of attending a course on Japanese architecture, instructed by the erudite Harvard professor Yukio Lippit. The course delved into the intricate details and historical contexts of iconic structures such as the Ise Shrine, Katsura Villa, and Himeji Castle.

Professor Lippit's eloquent presentational style is both engaging and informative. He adeptly elucidates complex architectural concepts, historical underpinnings, and cultural subtleties, creating a vivid tapestry of pre–modern Japanese architecture. His evident passion for the subject matter infuses each lecture, fostering a stimulating learning environment.

The course content offers a comprehensive examination of pre–modern Japanese architecture. Students probe the cultural, religious, and aesthetic foundations of these renowned structures, gaining valuable insights into their significance and the mastery of their design

A distinguishing feature of the course is the thoughtful selection of field trips orchestrated by Professor Lippit. Students are afforded the opportunity to examine two local architectural gems: the Machiya at Boston Children's Museum and the Harvard teahouse. These visits serve to contextualize and reinforce the knowledge acquired in the classroom, allowing students to appreciate the craftsmanship and architectural nuances firsthand.

In summation, this course presents an enlightening and enriching foray into the world of Japanese design, history, and culture. Through his refined presentational skills, well–structured content, and astute selection of field trips, this course stands as an invaluable experience for those interested in architecture and Japanese design.
