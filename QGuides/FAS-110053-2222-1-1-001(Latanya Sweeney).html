It's a good class and Prof. Sweeney is a great and phenomenal professor, but this class uses such recent events that the assignments sometimes feel not well thought out and take up a LOT of time and feel quite vague which means you don't understand what you need to do.
Professor Sweeney is an amazing professor. You will learn so much in this class.
This class can be very hit or miss on a class–to–class scale, but is very rewarding overall. Highly recommend you put more effort in in order to get more effort out.
Professor Sweeney and her awesome stories/experience are reason enough to take this course, in my opinion. The assignments/materials are a little rough around the edges (grading a bit arbitrary but not too harsh overall, not a lot of materials in general, assignments occasionally frustrating) but the course overall was still a lot of fun, and I'm glad I took it. I feel like I understand what tech science is, major goals/problems/stories, and feel excited for the role I can play in it in the future. Getting to interact a lot with classmates via group activities/projects was also cool.
The value you get out of the class really depends on what you make of it. This may be true for education in general, but I think it applies to this class more than to any other class I have taken at Harvard. In many of the classes we had I felt that the input I got from the class itself was scarce. Where I learned the most was when collaborating with other students on some of the assignments. (You can work on most assignments on your own or in groups of up to three people). For one of them, I worked with an experienced consultant who took the HKS version of the class. Seeing how differently he approached the problem at hand was a great experience. For the other class, I worked with a college student with a CS background, whose R–skills added incredible value to our final project and clearly showed me where I (with my Econ major) need to improve. Apparently, the grading is harder if you work in groups but I would say (though I don't know my final grade yet) that this is totally worth it, especially if you work in groups of two.

Prof. Sweeney also has interesting ideas for research during the summer – do make use of this opportunity if you can and if you like the topic of the class.

I think the class has huge potential. The hybrid HKS–college structure is a fantastic idea. Prof. Sweeney, as I mentioned earlier, is very inspiring.
Also with the current structure of the class, you do get something out of the class, if you make an effort and take the assignments seriously.
If they do improve the structure and increase the "content–load" for this class, this could actually become an excellent class one day.
You must take this class. Not easy but the lessons, experiences, and take–aways are invaluable. Recommend for any non–concentrator as well. Its worth the work and the final project is awesome
This is a great class, and Professor Sweeney/the teaching staff are wonderful. You will explore important technology–society clashes facing our world today, primarily by doing brief independent research in each of the 3–4 units. The work gets more and more unstructured over the semester, which gives you the leeway to be creative and hone your own skills. It's not a ton of work, but it is super interesting. Do be prepared, though, for the class to be, at times, a bit disorganized. But that doesn't take away from the great content.
Great class, assignments are very interesting but can sometimes take a lot longer than you expect/are told
I highly recommend this course. Dr. Sweeney is still my most favorite professor at Harvard. Between her humor and anecdotes, she truly makes you feel invested and empowered in the work you are doing. The course assignments also are interesting, and although laborious at times, still feel like independent research projects where you have creative direction.
