Take this if you're looking for a relatively lighter workload physics class which will still teach you useful and relevant physics concepts! This is great to take as a senior to ground all the physics you've have seen so far. Morin is a fantastic lecturer, and TF Soumya was also an incredible section leader as well. The course topics are super interesting! We covered cool concepts like the physics of flight, nuclear power, cosmology, and so much more! The materials are also of high quality, with very detailed lecture notes and pset solutions. Workload is not easy, but it is still very manageable for a physics class, and very worth it considering what you'll learn. You'll leave much more capable of doing fun back of the envelope style calculations on literally anything.
This is a fantastic physics class! A refreshing contrast to other classes which force you to do a lot of the teaching yourself, this class actually provided us with everything we needed to succeed, and I felt the course staff really cared about supporting us. There was room to explore many interesting topics, including our own ideas in the presentation. David and Soumya are both really fantastic. I'm really glad I got to take this class.
This is a great class and possibly the easiest physics class at Harvard. I recommend taking it as an upperclassman so that you can fully appreciate it. It would be difficult but possible to take this class without physics experience.
This class isn't a lot of work, but it's really well taught and covers an interesting selection of topics. If you don't like some topic, the good thing is that the class will move on to something else in the next class or so. It does, however, spend around four lectures on cosmology, but apart from that nothing lasts more than one or two lectures. There are ten psets (you get a drop), a midterm, a presentation, and a final exam. The exam problems are very similar to the homework, and the presentation is pretty chill. The problem sets often had interesting problems (including some classics) and were not very difficult (but that of course would depend on your physics —and math — background).
This is a good physics class. The lectures are interesting and the material is rather here and there, but otherwise covers fun physics topics. The presentations are honestly chill and the psets ar e it too bad if you go to office hours.
If you are a MechE trying to avoid taking another chem course for the pesky requirements, take this instead! It is not only very interesting, it is also a fairly light workload. If you have a strong physics background, this class will be a breeze. If not, you will have to look at the lecture notes a bit closer, but even then, it will be relatively easy since Professor Morin has stellar notes that align perfectly with the assignments. The key to doing good on the psets is to simply go to OHs and the occasional section.
This is a great course and I'd highly recommend it for anyone generally interested in physics or other stem fields. You get a lot of general exposure to a lot of topics. The psets were a little long sometimes but very doable, especially if you go to office hours.
This is genuinely such an excellent course; likely my favorite course I've ever taken in the physics department (as a physics concentrator). It's essentially just Prof. Morin giving a bunch of lectures on random physics topics he finds interesting –– you'll learn a lot about a lot of different things, and you'll have a blast doing it. Professor Morin is a great lecturer and all around a really fun person. Very much recommend this class.
I'm writing this for the other MechE's who might be looking at this class to fulfill the general science requirement. Do it! It's not easy, but it's very fair, and way more interesting and applicable than the freshman science courses that are your other options. It's also not nearly as hard as the core engineering courses. Professor Morin is a really fun, interesting and thoughtful teacher, and you can tell this course has been refined to work very smoothly. My only regret is being to burnt out of a senior to fully take advantage of it. But very glad I enrolled!
Take this class! Pretty chill – just two exams. The material is really fun and honestly seems really informational in terms of just being a citizen of the world (i.e., subjects like planes, human energy, global warming), in that it gives you a lot to talk about. Morin is fantastic as a lecturer – covers topics in a clear way. All the homeworks are honestly really fun. Exams are fair. Soumya (the TF) is awesome – extremely nice and knowledgeable, really cares about the students.
You should definitely take this class! It's an interesting physics class that's low workload.
Amazing course and professor! Take this class if you are a physics concentrator!
This is a fun class. It is interesting and the derivations are explained very well. You can tell David Morin had a lot of fun designing the class, and this shows itself in the creativity of the derivations and variety of topics. Some of the psets are more difficult than others–– a couple are very easy, a couple are pretty involved, but none of them are more stressful than a pset from another standard pset class. All in all, not a gem but less work than other physics classes, and it is definitely a fun and interesting time.
