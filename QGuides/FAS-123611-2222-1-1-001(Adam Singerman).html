What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are interested at all in how linguistics is done practically, take this course. Even if your interests are in theory or (for example) in historical linguistics, which might seem unrelated to fieldwork, learning about a language from the ground up is fun, exciting, and valuable, and I would not pass up the opportunity.
This course has the potential to be an amazing experience, but how much you learn depends heavily on the instructor.
Take this class. Take it multiple times, if you want. The language changes every time. The linguistic consultants are amazing and so is the professor. Get this experience in while you can!
The course may be improved with more structure, I would be hesitant to recommend the course as it is currently formulated if it were not a requirement.
Field methods is extremely valuable, in terms learning to collect data in an unbiased way, and in terms of simply interpersonal skills of interacting with language consultants. Unfortunately, depending on the instructor, you may or may not learn these things from this class.
