Try to think about what interests you about every unit because this will be helpful for developing a research question for the final prospectus.
This course is a wonderful requirement for Hist Sci. It is not too intense in the workload, just be sure to look ahead for the writing assignments so they don't sneak up on you. It does not feature lectures, but is student–led. However, the presentation you will lead, your section classmates will help you with, so it is not hard.
I had to take this class, but I hated it until half way through the semester. In the end I loved it though,
The teaching staff is composed of truly kind, brilliant people who are always willing to talk –– never be shy to discuss anything with them.
This is a great intro course: you'll get a chance to work with archives, use oral histories, analyze a film, and participate in a lot of interactive assignments that test your skills as a historian–in–making! Really a lot more chill than I thought, the sections were very interesting, and the assignments were actually enjoyable/worthwhile. You will come out with a set of new skills that you didn't realize were so within reach!
This is a fantastic introduction to History of Science, both in the sort of work scholars in the field conduct as well as the actual technical nitty–gritty involved in engaging in critical research processes. Prof. Harrington really cares about the sophomores, is such a cool person herself, and has really figured out how to make this class great. The rest of the teaching staff as well was excellent and they were all always willing to answer any questions about the course or the department more generally.
Might be required but still pretty fun overall! Chill, relaxed style and interesting conversations
This course is fantastic — the readings are interesting, Professor Harrington is a star, and the teaching time is generous and extremely engaging.
Even the weeks that I didn't think I would find interesting ended up being really engaging. I loved seeing how the different section's presentations at sharing sessions fit together and built off of each other.
This course is, unfortunately, necessary for history of science. You'll have to take it so be prepared for lots of spur–of–the–moment, outside of class time events that are required. Be prepared to have to shuffle around your schedule frequently.
This course is what you make of it. You will leave learning a lot if you put in the effort
