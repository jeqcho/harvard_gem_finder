Description:

The course offers some basic tools of conducting, such as score study, understanding of the musical text and style, gestural communication and clarity, how to approach the challenges of rehearsing. Each student will receive weekly podium time, and will also learn by observing while playing/singing in the ensemble. Repertoire from the symphonic and operatic canon will be chosen based on the individual needs and interests of the selected students, as well as on the availability of players/singers. A possible focus on Italian opera may be added if enough singers are enrolled. All interested students are encouraged to send an email to the course head or the TA before the beginning of the semester, and to attend the first class meeting. The target number of students is 6-10. No audition required. The selection of the applicants, if necessary, will be made based on experience and music background. Since a conducting class is largely an individual training course, no general syllabus will be provided at this time. A more detailed weekly plan, tailored to the students selected, will be posted after the first few meetings.

Topic:

Intensive Conducting

Course Notes:

The course is open to advanced conductors, singers, and pianists. See below the specific requirements. For all participants – advanced musicianship. For conductors – experience in conducting, some abilities in piano recommended (limited admission). For singers – participants should be engaged in classical vocal studies and preferably have some experience with Italian operatic arias. For pianists – advanced skills, some experience accompanying singers recommended.
