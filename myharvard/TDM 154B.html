Description:

Intended as a follow-up to TDM 154 Designing With Light, this course will teach a standardized process for creating and executing a lighting design for live performance, from concept through completion, with an emphasis on artistic and technical collaboration. Fixture photometrics and drafting section analysis will provide a framework for light plot construction. Students will learn to use industry-standard Vectorworks and Lightwright software to generate design documents. Other topics may include theatrical venues and their interconnected systems, lighting system design, design considerations for unusual spaces, intermediate programming on the ETC EOS console family, and the role of the design assistant. Visits to theatrical venues and productions outside of Harvard will be required.

Course Notes:

While prior enrollment of Designing with Light (TDM 154) is not required in order to take this class, it is encouraged. If you have not taken that course, please contact the instructor and share any relevant interest or experience.
