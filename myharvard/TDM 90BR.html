Description:

Don't miss your chance to join us in bringing "Iphigenia and Other Daughters" to life. Are you ready to dive into the enchanting world of theater while exploring a play that shattered conventions nearly three decades ago? Join us for an exciting journey in "Iphigenia and Other 2023 Daughters"! This isn't just your average theatre class; it's a thrilling fusion of classic drama and modern-day perspective.

In this course, we'll put the spotlight on the captivating and powerful "Iphigenia and Other Daughters" by Ellen McLaughlin, written in 1995 from a liberated feminist lens. Fast forward to 2023, and society's notions of gender, sexuality, femininity, and masculinity have evolved even further. Gender and sexuality are no longer limited to a binary, and this production will create a welcoming space for women, non-gender-conforming, and femme-identifying artists.

We'll embrace McLaughlin's vision and, without altering the text, transform this masterpiece to resonate with a contemporary audience. Together, we'll explore what aspects of the play stand the test of time and where we can breathe fresh life into the story.

But most importantly, we'll have a blast!

This course centers on the rehearsal and production of the play, with students required to attend a set number of rehearsal hours outside of class time. Because of this, there's no need for additional written assignments or homework. You'll build a strong foundation for working with classic texts, hone your acting skills through practical application in the rehearsal room, and learn new approaches and techniques. Not to mention, you'll refine your rehearsal room etiquette.

Finally, you'll have the unique opportunity to be directed by one of the leading directors in the field while working alongside a professional design team and stage management crew.

Don't miss your chance to join us in bringing "Iphigenia and Other Daughters" to life. Let's celebrate the evolution of art, honor the legacy of the past, and create a production that's both revolutionary and, most importantly, incredibly fun! It is important to note that the text will not be modified so students should be prepared to engage with conversations that come up during the creation of the piece. Please read the text to make sure that themes and ideas resonate with you!
