Description:

This course leads students through a new play development process, culminating in a fully rehearsed and designed production of "The Singularity Play" by Jay Stull, directed by Keenan Tyler Oliphant. The course is primarily focused on performance with a full production component, and seeks serious actors with intrepid ambition. Students with interests in playwriting, directing, or theatrical design are also welcome, although enrollment in the course will necessarily involve performance of some kind. Questions guiding our explorations in performance are invited by the preoccupations of the play itself and the director's vision for the production: what is the performance of consciousness? Of embodied existence? Of proscribed gender? How does a virtual world (a Metaverse) affect behavior in the actual world? What constitutes the boundary of human-ness and how does proximity to that boundary affect behavior, performance, and power?

Class Notes:

TDM 90BR is required of concentrators and open to other students involved in the TDM spring production (as actors or apprentices on the production team). Participation is limited to students involved with the production, either as performers or as members of the production team. 

Enrollment in the course will be via audition. 

Students should first acquire and read the script of The Singularity Play by emailing Madi Fabber at madisonfabber[at]g.harvard.edu. 

Auditions will be held remotely, via Zoom, on Wednesday, January 11th (2pm-4pm ET), Thursday, January 12th (4pm-6pm ET), and Tuesday, January 17th (2pm-4pm ET). Students may schedule a 20-minute audition slot on these dates by using this link: calendly.com/thesingularityplay. Students interested in the class but unavailable to audition any of those days should contact Jay or Keenan directly via email to schedule an audition time, pending their availability.

The audition will consist of a conversation about your experience performing, your experiences in theater, your questions about the play and the production, and your interests in themes of performance and embodiment/disembodiment. 

And, although no significant experience in performing is a prerequisite to enrollment, we’d certainly like to get a sense of your facility with the script. As such, students who audition will prepare the role of either GREG or LAUREN from Act One of the script, beginning on the top of page 47 with LAUREN’s line, “But that feels completely against what would normally happen” and ending on the bottom of page 49 after GREG’s line, “It was a bug, Lauren. It wasn’t an evolution. It was an error in the math.” 

Enrollment in the course will be indicated no later than Wednesday, January 18th through MyHarvard.
