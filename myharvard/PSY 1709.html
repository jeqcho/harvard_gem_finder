Description:

While one person prefers a large social gathering, another favors coffee with a friend. Where one person completes tasks a week in advance, another waits until the last minute. Simply put, no two people are exactly alike. To understand the many ways in which we differ as individuals, this course will survey prominent theories and contemporary empirical research in personality psychology. With an emphasis on deepening our own self-knowledge, we will discuss how psychologists study and differentiate stable traits from more transitory states and how personality develops and changes across the lifespan (e.g., temperament, attachment, etc.).  We will incorporate perspectives from work on motivation, culture, cognition, and psychopathology to broaden our understanding of individual differences. Throughout this course, we will evaluate theoretical perspectives in the context of current empirical data and take deep dives into methodology to expand our understanding of how individual differences are assessed and quantified.
