Description:

Through the lens of social justice issues in France and the Francophone world, this class will focus on language as a means of civic engagement. You will interrogate topics such as colonialism, islamophobia, immigration, and sexism by studying a range of creative, analytical, and polemical texts, images, and film. This course builds on the communicative competence acquired in French 40, with a particular emphasis on developing your writing and speaking proficiencies through creative and analytical projects such as description, portrait, and polemical assignments. This course will also include an intercultural exchange with the local Francophone community in addition to online partner conversations with students at the Kedge Business school in France.

Course Notes:

Conducted in French. Students must complete online information survey on Canvas before registering for the course. May not be taken Pass/Fail, but may be taken Sat/Unsat by GSAS students. Not open to auditors.

Recommended Prep:

Prerequisite: French 40; a score of 721-750 on the SAT II test or the Harvard Placement test.
