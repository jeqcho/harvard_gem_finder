Description:

The goals of this class are to teach students the skills essential for coming up with new scientific ideas, and communicating those ideas effectively with others through both writing and presentation. Skills necessary for ideation include scientific topic exploration and refinement, generating testable hypotheses, and developing aims. In parallel, students will learn how to communicate those ideas by finding the essential story, giving and receiving critical feedback, and preparing effective slides and proposals.

Specifically, this class is designed to:

  • Teach scientific ideation and communication as systematic processes
  • Provide practice in writing and presenting science
  • Find each individual's on-stage persona through improve exercises
  • Cultivate a culture that embraces and nurtures diverse voices, styles and backgrounds.
  • Increase comfort with controlling conversations & presentations while addressing interruptions and questions

Approach ideation with confidence and a positive attitude
