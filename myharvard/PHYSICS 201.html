Description:

This course covers methods for analyzing experimental data. Students will learn a Bayesian framework for statistical inference, modern computational methods such as Markov-chain Monte Carlo techniques, and the application to problems in particle physics, biophysics, condensed matter, applied physics, astrophysics, and other fields. The course emphasizes an intuitive, principled approach to data analysis and will involve discussions of ethics and reproducible research.

Course Notes:

This course is suitable for students with limited or rusty programming skills. Students with more advanced programming skills may wish to take APMTH 207 or ENG-SCI 255.

Recommended Prep:

Research experience commensurate with that of a first-year (or higher) PhD student in experimental physics. Students without such experimental experience must have taken Physics 191 or Physics 247 (or equivalent) first.
